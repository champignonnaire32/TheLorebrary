### [7]: Rakian Aphorism
Eleven-year-old Sheeana lies on a sand dune on the planet Rakis and listens to an approaching sandworm. She remembers how a giant worm destroyed her village and swallowed her father three years ago. During that attack, Sheeana ran toward the monster and climbed onto its back in a fit of rage. The worm surprisingly obeyed her commands and carried her to the moisture-rich city of Keen. Upon arrival, Sheeana confronted the worm at the city's gates and shouted at its gaping mouth.

Two priests discovered Sheeana on the sand and bowed to her, calling her the "Child of Shai-hulud." Sheeana performed the "Dance of Propitiation," which the priests interpreted as a sign of divine forgiveness. They took her to the temple complex where senior priests debated whether she should be punished or revered. High Priest Hedley Tuek decided to test the girl's abilities in the open desert away from the public. The priests used a thumper, a rhythmic mechanical device, to summon two smaller worms to Sheeana's location.

Sheeana stood her ground and commanded the worms to leave when they approached her too closely. The worms obeyed her instructions and retreated into the desert, leaving the watching priests in awe. The Bene Gesserit embassy in Keen immediately sent a report of these events to their headquarters. Sheeana was returned to the city and began a new life under the careful observation of the priesthood. The Sisterhood realized that the miracle they had long expected had finally manifested in this young girl.
