### 1: Chapter 1
Paul Muad’Dib and his sister Alia rule an empire built on a twelve-year religious Jihad. Paul possesses Mentat computation abilities and prana-bindu muscle control taught by his mother, the Lady Jessica. As the Kwisatz Haderach, Paul sees many places at once through his oracular powers. He controls the spice melange, a geriatric drug that allows Guild Steersmen to navigate space and provides Reverend Mothers their vision. Without this spice, the Imperial citizens would die from addictive withdrawal.

Historical accounts suggest that Paul’s supreme power contains an inherent failure due to the nature of prophecy. Conspirators including the Spacing Guild, the Bene Gesserit, and the Bene Tleilax plot against the Emperor. The Bene Tleilax are amoral scientists who utilize Face-Dancers, shapeshifting mimics, to perform their work. These plotters intend to use a Dune Tarot to cloud Paul’s prophetic vision and introduce a ghola to his household. A ghola is a being brought back from the dead in axolotl tanks and retrained for specific tasks.

This specific ghola is Duncan Idaho, the Atreides lieutenant who died saving Paul years ago. The Qizarate cabal, led by Korba the Panegyrist, intends to turn Paul into a martyr. Their plan involves framing Chani, Paul’s Fremen concubine, for the Emperor’s downfall. Historians argue that Paul’s defeat is caused by the lethal certainty of his own predictions. The dedication to the Muad’Dib Concordance frames the story as a tragedy for all mankind.

### 2: Chapter 2
Scytale, a Face Dancer, meets with fellow co-conspirators on the planet Wallach IX. Edric, a Guild Steersman, floats in a transparent tank filled with orange melange gas and appears as a humanoid with finned feet. Reverend Mother Gaius Helen Mohiam and Princess Irulan join the discussion inside a dome designed to shield them from Paul’s oracular sight. Scytale proposes using a "psychic poison" rather than a frontal attack to avoid creating a martyr of the Emperor. The Face Dancer chooses a round-faced, bloated appearance to invite his companions to underestimate his abilities.

Irulan confesses that she has been secretly administering a contraceptive to Chani to prevent Paul from having an heir. The conspirators decide to gift Paul a Tleilaxu ghola made from the flesh of his deceased friend, Duncan Idaho. This ghola, named Hayt, was recovered as a corpse and restored in axolotl tanks by the Bene Tleilax. Edric explains that the Guild purchased the ghola to act as a distraction and a psychological weapon against Paul. Scytale reveals that the Tleilaxu once created their own Kwisatz Haderach who committed suicide rather than face his own nature.

Hayt is also a Mentat, a human computer, which Scytale believes will attract and distract Paul’s sister, Alia. The Face Dancer shifts his appearance into a round-faced man with epicanthic folds to demonstrate his powers to Irulan. Scytale asserts that the Qizarate religious arm can be turned against Paul through the manipulation of belief. The conspirators plan to annex less powerful groups in the name of progress while Paul’s reign collapses from within. Edric notes that the Guild's spice stockpiles are finite and that they must break Paul’s monopoly on melange.

### 3: Chapter 3
Paul Atreides returns to his Keep in Arrakeen after walking the streets anonymously in a stillsuit. He removes the gear, which recycles body moisture for survival in the desert, and reflects on the burden of his Imperial power. Chani enters their bedchamber to serve spice-coffee from a silver pot once owned by a man Paul killed in single combat. A poison-snooper, a device with mechanical arms that detects toxins, monitors the food they prepare to eat. Paul notes that Chani remains unmarked by the years despite their stressful life at the center of a galactic Jihad.

Chani asks Paul to consider letting Princess Irulan have his child to stabilize the political situation and secure an heir. Paul refuses, stating that a child would give Irulan too much power and that he swore never to take her to bed. He realizes Chani is making a Fremen sacrifice because she has not yet produced an heir for him. Paul explains that his oracular vision, which he compares to water draining through his fingers, makes the future increasingly obscure. He describes the Jihad as a "terrible purpose" that he wishes to end, even if it means sacrificing his own godhead.

Chani reports that the Fremen tribes have revived ancient rites and blood sacrifices to summon Muad’Dib back to the desert. She mentions a giant sandworm over one hundred meters long seen near the Shield Wall, interpreted as a spiritual omen. Paul experiences a demanding memory called "adab" and recalls an early vision of a funeral procession in the desert. He fears that by following the lure of the oracle, he has trapped himself in a single-track "spider-future." The couple decides they must eventually return to Sietch Tabr, a traditional Fremen rock-settlement, to escape the city's intrigues.

### 4: Chapter 4
Scytale travels to a suburb of Arrakeen to meet Farok, a retired Bashar of the Ninth Legion. The Face Dancer wears a flat-featured face and identifies himself by the name Zaal. Farok’s son sits nearby playing semuta music, a drug-induced melody, on a stringed instrument called a rebec. The son lost his eyes to a stone burner, an atomic weapon that emits J-rays and causes permanent blindness, during the conquest of Naraj. Farok admits he longs for the old Fremen ways before the Atreides brought wealth and water to the planet.

Farok describes his first encounter with a sea on the world of Enfeil, an experience that healed him of his desire for Jihad. He explains that the Imperial Keep contains trees from many worlds and a stunted sandworm kept in a water moat. Scytale realizes the blind youth is using the music to transmit a distrans message into the Face Dancer’s neural system. This hidden data contains the names of every conspirator and contact phrase needed to undermine the Emperor’s rule. Otheym’s daughter, a young Fremen woman, appears from a back room in a state of narcotic ecstasy caused by the semuta.

Scytale kills Farok and his blind son by firing poisoned needles from his sleeve into their necks. The Face Dancer immediately shifts his physical appearance and voice to mimic the dead Farok. He convinces Otheym’s daughter to leave the house with him by claiming her lover needs to rest. Scytale intends to use the woman as a tool to gain access to the Emperor’s inner circle. The corpses are left in the house as Scytale departs to begin the next phase of the conspiracy.

### 5: Chapter 5
The Imperial Council meets in Arrakeen with Paul, Alia, Stilgar, Chani, Irulan, and the priest Korba in attendance. Korba leads the pilgrims in prayer from the balcony while the Council discusses the Tupile Treaty regarding sanctuary worlds. The Spacing Guild demands Paul sign the treaty without revealing the exact coordinates of the Tupile Entente locations. Paul decides to sign the treaty to keep the Guild off balance and maintain the fragile peace between the Great Houses. He explains to Stilgar that prescience is not a natural law but a harmonious act that avoids cause and effect.

The Council reviews reports of the Ixian Confederacy demanding a constitution to limit Paul’s absolute Imperial will. Paul rejects the idea of a constitution, labeling it the ultimate tyranny that removes individual dignity and mobilized social power. He orders that his decision be recorded as an Order in Council rather than a legislative act. Reports indicate that the deposed Emperor Shaddam IV is performing landing maneuvers with a single legion on Salusa Secundus. Paul instructs Irulan to warn her father that such actions make him a target for the more aggressive Jihad commanders.

Irulan formally proposes herself as the mother of the Imperial heir to ensure the continuation of the Atreides bloodline. Paul publicly rejects her proposal, citing her lack of love and her primary loyalty to the Bene Gesserit Sisterhood. Chani admits that she has sought both medical and spiritual help to conceive a child but has remained unsuccessful. Paul grants permission for the Spacing Guild to establish a formal embassy on Arrakis headed by a Steersman. Irulan realizes that Paul and the Steersman will be mutually blind to each other’s futures, allowing the conspiracy to remain hidden.
