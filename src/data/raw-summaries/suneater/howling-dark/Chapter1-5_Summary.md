### 1: The Red Company
Hadrian Marlowe wakes from a six-year fugue sleep in the medical bay of the *Pharaoh*. Doctor Okoyo monitors his recovery and informs him of the standard date in the year 16219. Hadrian meets with Bassander Lin, a Mandari captain who expresses deep frustration with their decades-long hunt for the Cielcin. Lin reveals that a mercenary named Otavia Corvo has found a lead on an arms dealer named Samir. This Samir reportedly works for a mysterious figure known as The Painted Man.

Hadrian insists that he must lead the away team to the planet Rustam to make contact. He leaves the *Pharaoh* to return to his own ship, the *Balmung*. During the shuttle flight, he observes the massive black scars on the planet's surface where the Cielcin destroyed a human city. Bassander Lin wears the uniform of the Meidua Red Company but remains a loyal Imperial officer at heart. The Cielcin are described as predatory xenobites who raze human colonies and drink of human worlds.

Hadrian’s lictor, Switch, accompanies him on the shuttle and notes the death toll on Rustam reached two million. They dock with the *Balmung*, an ancient Imperial destroyer that serves as the company's flagship. Hadrian reflects on his forty-eight years of service and his ongoing quest to find the mythic location of Vorgossos. He struggles with lingering hallucinations and memories of past violence triggered by the revival process. The chapter ends as the shuttle approaches the *Balmung* while the Cielcin continue their crusade elsewhere in the galaxy.

### 2: Suspended and Undead
Hadrian enters the cold cubiculum on the *Balmung* to inspect eleven Cielcin prisoners kept in cryogenic suspension. He speaks briefly to the frost-covered crèches, promising the Cielcin leader, Tanaran, that they are nearing their destination. Valka Onderra, a Tavrosi researcher with a machine daimon interface in her skull, joins him in the chamber. She expresses doubt about the mission and requests permission to thaw a prisoner for interrogation, which Hadrian denies. They discuss the news of the lead on Rustam and Valka’s frustration with their slow progress.

Hadrian meets his centurions, the grizzled Pallino and the massive Ghen, who report that the captain is waiting. He proceeds to the ventral observatory to meet Captain Jinan Azhar. Jinan Azhar is a high-caste Jaddian soldier with copper skin and an oval face. She and Hadrian share a private embrace and discuss the dossier on the informant Samir. Samir is pictured as a hairless, obese criminal with small eyes and a wet smile.

Jinan confirms that Karim Garone is already on the planet’s surface making arrangements for a meeting. Hadrian confides his self-doubt and the pressure he feels from Lin and Valka regarding the mission’s validity. Jinan encourages him to persist and reaffirms her loyalty to their cause. The two lovers leave the observatory together to spend their final hours before the descent to the planet.

### 3: The Sunken City
Hadrian descends to the planet Rustam accompanied by Switch, Lieutenant Greenlaw, and the dryad pilot Ilex. Ilex is a green-skinned homunculus whose skin is thick with chlorophyll and who feeds on sunlight. They land on the outskirts of Arslan, a city constructed from the hulls of grounded starships and industrial scrap. The air in the city is acrid and oily, belched from the nuclear stardrives that power the settlement. They meet Lieutenant Karim "Crim" Garone, who is disguised as a Jaddian merchant in a red kaftan.

Crim leads the group through the crowded, vapor-filled streets toward a meeting point. They pass holographic advertisements and religious processions as they navigate the ship-built warren. The team enters a jubala den filled with the bitter smoke of drugs and holographic flames. They find Samir sitting in a back booth, appearing even more obese and hairless than his photographs suggested. Hadrian introduces himself as the Commandant of the Red Company and expresses interest in purchasing restricted weapons.

Samir is suspicious of Hadrian’s noble appearance and pale skin, accurately identifying him as an Imperial palatine. The criminal warns them that the Chantry priests maintain a secret influence over the lawless city. Despite his personal distaste for the nobility, Samir agrees to broker a meeting with The Painted Man. The chapter concludes as the group prepares to meet the elusive Extrasolarian arms dealer.

### 4: The Painted Man
Samir leads the team to a stately wooden tea house perched on the edge of a massive starliner. They bypass the main dining area and move to a private portico overlooking the sprawling city of Arslan. There they encounter The Painted Man, a red-haired homunculus with translucent skin and pupil-black eyes. His body is covered in living tattoos of eyes and mouths that move across his flesh. Hadrian requests the location of the hidden planet Vorgossos and a supply of illegal atomic weapons.

The Painted Man mocks Hadrian’s noble heritage and reveals that Vorgossos belongs to a being called "The Undying." The homunculus claims that the planet has no sun and can only be found with its master’s permission. The conversation takes a hostile turn when The Painted Man correctly identifies Hadrian as an Imperial spy. He signals his thugs to seize the group, but a sniper shot from Ghen’s hidden reinforcements kills one guard. Hadrian draws his highmatter sword, a weapon with a phosphorescent liquid crystal blade that cuts through any material.

The tea house patrons suddenly fall silent and reveal themselves to be puppets controlled by The Painted Man. These diners are revealed to be humans modified with wires and internal machinery. The Painted Man leaps into the crowd and uses his thralls to launch a coordinated attack on the Red Company team. Hadrian stands his ground with his glowing blade as the peaceful tea house erupts into a violent battlefield.

### 5: Eyes Like Stars
The Red Company team battles the machine-daimons, who are human corpses reanimated by artificial intelligence and internal wiring. Hadrian cleaves through the attackers with his highmatter sword, noting the white synthetic fluid that leaks from their wounds alongside blood. A Jaddian soldier named Artur is swarmed by the mechanical thralls and killed before Hadrian can reach him. Hadrian becomes pinned beneath a headless machine-man that continues to fight despite its mortal injuries. Centurion Ghen saves Hadrian by destroying the attacker with a high-energy plasma burner.

The Painted Man uses the chaos of the mindless mob to vanish from the tea house entirely. Hadrian manages to stun Samir with a secondary weapon, securing him as a prisoner. The team retreats toward the stairs as more machine-daimons seize the weapons of fallen soldiers. Ilex signals the drop-shuttle for an emergency extraction while Ghen’s snipers provide covering fire from across the street. Hadrian leads the survivors through the burning wreckage of the portico, clearing a path toward the landing zone.

They suffer casualties and fail to capture the primary target, leaving the mission in a state of disarray. Greenlaw openly blames Hadrian for the tactical failure and the loss of their men. The chapter ends as the remaining machine-thralls freeze in place, their glassy eyes watching the team depart. Hadrian realizes he has walked into a trap and that their enemy was prepared for their arrival.

**Sentence Count Check:** 14
