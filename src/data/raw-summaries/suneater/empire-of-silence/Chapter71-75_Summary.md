# Chapter 71-75 Summary

### 71: Inquisition
Hadrian attends a high council meeting in Borosevo to decide the fate of the ten Cielcin captives. Lord Balian Mataro, Knight-Tribune Raine Smythe, and Grand Prior Ligeia Vas preside over the discussion. Hadrian argues that the Cielcin vessel was not a military craft and suggests treating the prisoners honorably to facilitate ransom. Ligeia Vas and the Chantry demand an interrogation to extract tactical information regarding Cielcin fleet movements. The council ignores Hadrian's objections and orders the Cielcin captain, Uvanari, to be turned over to the Chantry.

Hadrian is appointed as the mandatory translator for the interrogation because he is the only person who speaks the language. Knight-Tribune Smythe, a blunt Legion officer, agrees to house the other prisoners in the bastille while isolating the captain. Jaddian emissaries, including Lady Kalima and the swordmaster Sir Olorin Milta, are invited to observe the process. Hadrian realizes his promise of medical aid and safety to Uvanari will be broken by the Chantry's methods. Lord Balian silences Hadrian’s further protests by comparing his demand for obedience to Hadrian's own father.

The chapter concludes with Hadrian being ordered to report to the Chantry for his duties. He reflects on the fact that political atrocities are often committed by quiet men in council chambers. Ligeia Vas smiles at Hadrian’s distress, marking a personal victory over him.

### 72: Pale Blood
Hadrian enters the Chantry's interrogation theater where Uvanari is naked and strapped to an X-shaped cross. Inquisitor Agari presides over the session while two blindfolded cathars prepare surgical implements and sensor tape. Uvanari recognizes Hadrian and accuses him of breaking his promise of sanctuary before the torture begins. The cathars use a push knife to snap off Uvanari’s glassy claws one by one to force a confession. Hadrian realizes he can lie to the Inquisitor because she does not understand the Cielcin language.

Uvanari reveals that the Cielcin came to Emesh "to pray" at the ruins of the Quiet. The captain explains that they jumped into the system blind and did not expect humans to be present. Uvanari states that the Cielcin have similar ruins on their own homeworld, which they also call "Earth." Agari uses a shock-stick to torture the captain even when he provides answers she deems too easy. Uvanari suddenly lunges and bites off two fingers from a cathar’s hand, swallowing them instantly.

Hadrian warns the captain that he will be punished for the attack, but Uvanari remains defiant. The Inquisitor forces Hadrian to ask who the Cielcin serve and where their fleet is currently located. Hadrian experiences a moment of double vision, seeing himself in both the torturer and the victim.

### 73: Ten Thousand Eyes
Weeks of interrogations pass as Hadrian translates for all ten Cielcin prisoners held in the bastille. The Imperial forces confirm that the Cielcin were not an invading force but a minor clerical or scout group. Hadrian remains isolated in his suite under guard, haunted by the screams from the interrogation sessions. Valka Onderra returns from the Calagah dig site and visits Hadrian in his dark, cluttered chambers. She reveals that she has a neural lace, a machine implanted in her skull, which she uses to disable the cameras.

Valka creates a localized power brownout to ensure their conversation is not monitored by the castle's ten thousand eyes. Hadrian tells her about the Cielcin's worship of the Quiet and their search for ancient technology. Sir Olorin Milta interrupts the meeting, bringing a bottle of zvanya, a potent Jaddian orange brandy. Olorin informs Hadrian that the high council has decided to let him speak to Uvanari alone. The Jaddian swordmaster praises Hadrian for convincing the Cielcin to surrender in the first place.

Hadrian reacts with rage and frustration at the mental toll of being the bridge between the torturers and the victims. Olorin notes that Hadrian's actions in the tunnels made the Imperial forces "do better" than usual. Hadrian ultimately agrees to the private meeting, hoping to find a way to end the suffering.

### 74: The Labyrinth
Hadrian enters Uvanari's cell, which is filled with the stench of rotting meat and raw sewage. Uvanari is in a sitting position on an adjustable cross, his right arm flayed from wrist to elbow. Hadrian administers a human painkiller that Imperial doctors believe might work on Cielcin biology. Uvanari calls Hadrian a slave because he works for others, whereas the Cielcin consider themselves "kasamnte" or nothing. The captain reveals that his master is named Aranata Otiolo and that he will never be found.

Uvanari mentions that Tanaran is a "baetan" or root, implying the younger Cielcin has a higher status than a soldier. Hadrian tries to convince Uvanari to cooperate so the Chantry will stop the torture and graft his arm. Uvanari begs Hadrian to kill him, using the term ndaktu to describe a formal mercy killing. The captain admits he made a mistake by surrendering and now believes death is his only remaining option. Hadrian reflects on his own complicity and compares his situation to Theseus walking through a labyrinth.

He realizes that whatever he learns will be twisted by the Imperial authorities for their own ends. Uvanari spits blood at Hadrian’s feet, a gesture Hadrian interprets as a lack of malice. The chapter ends with the captain asking Hadrian directly if he will perform the mercy killing.

### 75: Mercy Is
Hadrian stands on a castle parapet at night, watching the repair ships around the supercarrier Obdurate in orbit. He meets Valka in the gardens and asks her to help him fulfill Uvanari's request for ndaktu. Valka agrees to use her neural lace to create a camera blackout in the bastille for the execution. Hadrian visits the communal cell where the other nine Cielcin are held in metal bars. He informs the prisoners that the Chantry is torturing Uvanari and that the captain has asked for mercy.

The Cielcin react with a ululating wail of grief and fury upon hearing about their captain's condition. Tanaran, the baetan, tells Hadrian that he is responsible for the dishonor and must end it. Valka triggers the power outage, and Hadrian uses the darkness to coordinate a plan with the prisoners. Tanaran explains that it carries a "piece" of the master's authority, functioning as a high-ranking emissary. Hadrian asks if the Cielcin were created by the "Watchers," and Tanaran fiercely confirms they were.

Inquisitor Agari confronts Hadrian about the brownout, but he pretends to be frustrated by the prisoners' lack of cooperation. Hadrian lies to Agari, claiming the Cielcin only told him to go to hell during the blackout. He tells the Inquisitor that Tanaran is a high-ranking nobile to protect the younger Cielcin from further harm. Hadrian leaves the bastille, knowing he has committed himself to the act of killing the captain.

**Sentence Count Check:** 14
