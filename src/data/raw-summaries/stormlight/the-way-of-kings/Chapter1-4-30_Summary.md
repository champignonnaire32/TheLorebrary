### 1-4: RYSN
Rysn and her babsk Vstim arrive in Shinovar to trade with the Shin people. Rysn notes the strange properties of Shin grass, which is green and soft but does not retract or move when stepped upon. She sets up a new fabrial device on a tripod, a large heliodor fixed in metal that pulses with yellow light to warn of approaching people. Vstim explains that Shin warriors are the lowest social class and are traded as slaves using small stones. A group of Shin led by a farmer arrives, riding horses and pulling wagons with large, thick animals.

Vstim trades crates of Soulcast scrap metal, which looks like shell or wood but is solid metal, for Shin chickens and cloth. The Shin farmer, Thresh-son-Esan, expresses disbelief that Vstim wants worthless chickens instead of valuable horses. Vstim reveals that the scrap metal is considered garbage by Thaylen ardents who use it for Soulcasting practice. Thresh mentions a Truthless servant he once traded to Vstim seven years ago but refuses to sell any current soldiers to outsiders. The two merchants conclude the deal by emphasizing the worthlessness of their respective goods as a form of polite haggling.

Vstim gives Rysn a small pot and tells her to cut a sample of the Shin grass and soil to take with them. Rysn uses a knife to harvest the grass, which sits in "soil," a soft, dirty earth that doesn't exist in the rocky East. Vstim instructs her to care for the plant until she no longer finds it odd, claiming this will make her a better merchant. Rysn puts on rugged gloves and rolls up her sleeves to avoid ruining her fashionable blue and green silk dress. The caravan prepares to depart Shinovar to sell the exotic chickens for high prices in the eastern cities.

### 1-5: AXIES THE COLLECTOR
Axies the Collector wakes up naked in a garbage-filled alleyway in the Iriali city of Kasitor. He uses his Siah Aimian ability to sculpt his body and inhibit his sense of smell to block the stench of rotting tubers. His skin features deep blue nails and he casts a shadow that points toward the sun instead of away from it. A beggar demands rent for the alley and accuses Axies of destroying a local temple during a drunken stupor. Axies receives a ratty blanket from the beggar to use as a waist wrap before leaving the alley to avoid public indecency charges.

Axies inspects the notes he has tattooed onto his own skin, which he uses as a notebook because he is frequently robbed of his possessions. He discovers a new entry near his elbow describing "sudspren," which appear as small brown bubbles clinging to objects when a person is severely intoxicated. He travels to the city docks to witness a unique, hundred-foot-tall sea-blue spren named Cusicesh the Protector. Cusicesh surges from the bay at the same time every morning, forming four long arms that rest on golden pedestals. The spren displays a blurred succession of different human faces on its neck while facing toward the Origin in the east.

After the ten-minute display ends, Axies feels a physical sensation of being leeched or drained of energy. A group of street urchins steals his blanket wrap, leaving him naked and attracting the attention of four golden-haired Iriali guards. The guards arrest Axies and lead him toward the city dungeons for his public nudity. Axies does not resist, seeing the incarceration as a new opportunity to search for the elusive "captivityspren." He whistles to himself, satisfied with having observed two different types of spren in a single day.

### 1-6: A WORK OF ART
Szeth-son-son-Vallano crouches on a stone ledge inside Makkek’s gambling den in the Bavland city of Bornwater. He wears a tight black costume and a large black cloak that covers his round Shin eyes and bald head. Makkek uses Szeth as a personal assassin and maintains control over him through the possession of his Oathstone. Szeth leaves the den and travels to the eastern side of town to assassinate a rival gambling lord named Gavashaw. He uses Stormlight drawn from gemstones to Lash himself to walls, allowing him to run vertically and infiltrate Gavashaw’s fortified mansion.

Szeth summons his Shardblade and cuts a circular hole through a stone dome on the mansion's roof to gain entry. He infuses the stone chunk with Stormlight and Lashes it into the sky to distract the guards with a distant crash. Inside the master suite, Szeth discovers that Gavashaw has already been killed and his severed head placed on a table. A masked figure emerging from the shadows reveals that he has also murdered Makkek and now possesses Szeth’s Oathstone. The stranger uses a Soulcaster fabrial to turn a stone wall into smoke, demonstrating his power and resources.

The new master gives Szeth a list containing the names of several high-ranking world leaders, including the King of Jah Keved and six highprinces. The list is written in the warrior's script of Shinovar and includes specific instructions for how each target should be eliminated. Szeth is ordered to use the same overt, high-profile assassination tactics he employed years ago in Alethkar to cause global chaos. The masked figure leaves through the Soulcast opening, leaving Szeth to contemplate the impending war his actions will trigger. Szeth folds the list and prepares to fulfill his new master's demands despite his personal horror at the task.

### 29: ERRORGANCE
Shallan Davar uses a spanreed to communicate with her brother Nan Balat and his betrothed Eylita back in Jah Keved. She explains that Jasnah Kholin keeps her Soulcaster fabrial locked in a safe and wears the key around her neck at night. Nan Balat informs Shallan that their father’s steward Luesh is dead and that mysterious men have arrived demanding the return of the Soulcaster. These men bear tattoos of three diamonds in a specific pattern, matching a pendant found on Luesh’s body. Shallan realizes her family is in immediate danger and that she must successfully steal Jasnah’s working Soulcaster to save them.

Shallan meets with Jasnah in an alcove at the Veil in the Palanaeum to discuss her research on the Alethi monarchy. Jasnah criticizes Shallan's habit of making clever quips without thinking and encourages her to channel her intelligence more effectively. They discuss the murder of King Gavilar and why the Parshendi would have hired the Assassin in White to kill him. Shallan expresses her skepticism of scholars who use "errorgance," a term she invents for those who are overly certain despite lacking facts. Jasnah praises Shallan's improving rhetorical methods but warns her that a woman's mind must be employed with care.

King Taravangian of Kharbranth visits the alcove to eat lunch with Jasnah and Shallan on a high balcony. During the meal, Jasnah and the king debate whether morality and mathematics exist independently of the Almighty's decrees. Shallan attempts to sketch the king but accidentally draws two tall creatures with glass-like cloaks and floating geometric symbols instead of heads. She crumples the drawing in a panic to hide it from Jasnah and claims she simply grew sloppy during the conversation. After the king leaves, Shallan feels sick with guilt over her plan to betray Jasnah's trust and steal the Soulcaster.

### 30: DARKNESS UNSEEN
Kaladin leads Bridge Four through morning exercises in the lumberyard while being mocked by soldiers and carpenters. He has successfully recruited every man in the crew, including the holdout Bisig, by providing extra food and medical care. Gaz the bridge sergeant watches them with his single eye, feeling unsettled by the crew’s newfound discipline and speed. Lamaril, a low-ranking lighteyes, warns Gaz that Kaladin’s initiative is dangerous and could inspire a rebellion among the bridgemen. Lamaril demands that Gaz ensure Kaladin falls during a battle rather than being murdered in the camp to avoid creating a martyr.

Kaladin trains the men to carry the bridge on its side at a slant instead of over their heads. He places the taller men on the outside and shorter men on the inside to distribute the weight among the three rows. Gaz intercepts Kaladin to demand an explanation for this unorthodox and seemingly inefficient carrying method. Kaladin lies to Gaz, claiming the side-carry is simply a way to shift muscles and accommodate a depleted crew after high casualties. Gaz approves of the method, secretly hoping it will lead to a disaster for Bridge Four during their next plateau assault.

The windspren Syl warns Kaladin that Gaz and Lamaril are plotting against him after she observes their dark expressions. Kaladin acknowledges the threat but decides to focus on the immediate survival of his men rather than speculating on lighteyed schemes. He inspects a half-finished bridge made of makam wood and decides to use the bridge itself as a shield against Parshendi arrows. Bridge Four continues to practice the side-carry, with some men like Moash remaining skeptical of Kaladin’s ultimate goals. Kaladin organizes the crew into subsquads led by Rock, Teft, Skar, and Moash to improve their tactical cohesion during the upcoming runs.
