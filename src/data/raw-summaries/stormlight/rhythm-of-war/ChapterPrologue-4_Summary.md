### Prologue: To Pretend

Seven years ago in Kholinar, Navani Kholin manages the frantic preparations for a treaty feast with the Parshendi. She struggles to coordinate the sudden arrival of foreign dignitaries and the large drums requested by the Parshendi. Amidst the chaos, she encounters Aesudan, who is failing to properly entertain the important guests in the gardens. Navani is frustrated to learn that Gavilar has invited the master artifabrian Rushur Kris without informing her. While trying to reach the artifabrian, she is pulled away to resolve a wine shortage caused by Dalinar’s drinking.

Navani later discovers Gavilar holding a secret meeting in her private study with two mysterious western ambassadors. She observes strange gemstones on the table that glow with a reverse violet light, absorbing the surrounding color. One ambassador, a tall Makabaki man with a birthmark named Nale, speaks sharply about travel and the realm of gods. When the men leave, Gavilar treats Navani with cold contempt and refuses to explain the nature of the strange spheres. He insults her scholarly ambitions, labeling her a common whore who only seeks power through the men she marries.

Consumed by fury, Navani paints a "Death" glyphward prayer but immediately feels shame for wishing her husband dead. During the subsequent feast, Gavilar is assassinated by the Parshendi, falling from a high balcony to his death. Navani finds his broken body in the larder and realizes their final conversation was a bitter, irreconcilable argument. She retrieves the empty sphere pouch from his pocket and replaces it with her own gemstones to maintain his public image. Resolute and steady, she assumes control of the chaotic palace to preserve the kingdom and Gavilar's false legacy.

### Chapter 1: Calluses

In the occupied town of Hearthstone, Lirin the surgeon treats a never-ending line of ragged Herdazian refugees. The town is now under the control of the singers, led by a former slave parshwoman named Citylady Abiajan. Lirin works alongside his wife Hesina and the former lighteyes Laral to provide medical aid and clean water. He harbors a deep resentment toward war and heroism, blaming such concepts for the loss of his two sons. Despite his cynicism, Lirin coordinates with Laral and her disgraced husband Roshone to hide a high-value resistance leader.

The hidden figure is Dieno enne Calah, known as the Mink, the legendary general of the fallen Herdazian resistance. Lirin uses a faked medical emergency to pull the Mink and his four guards out of the inspection line. Roshone provides a distraction by acting as a stumbling drunk, entertaining the singer guards while the group moves. The operation is interrupted when a massive Fused with jagged carapace and glowing red eyes arrives in town. This Fused, a member of the ancient enemy race, is searching specifically for a dangerous spy with a slave brand.

Abiajan and her guards are flustered by the presence of the Fused, who demands to inspect every refugee's face. The interpreter explains they seek a tall human with wavy black hair and the shash glyph branded on his forehead. Lirin watches in silent horror as the singer guards approach a cloaked figure at the end of the line. When the hood is thrown back, the man is revealed to be Lirin’s son Kaladin, looking harsh and battle-worn. Kaladin immediately erupts with Stormlight power, ending the ruse and preparing to defend his home from the Fused.

### Chapter 2: Severed Cords

Kaladin summons his honorspren Sylphrena as a silver spear and Lashes himself into the sky to face the Fused. The enemy, a variety of Fused that can teleport as a ribbon of red-violet light, pursues Kaladin relentlessly. The Fused grapples Kaladin and stabs him repeatedly in the neck to sever his spinal cord and force paralysis. Although Kaladin heals the wounds with Stormlight, he struggles against the creature’s expert wrestling and teleportation tactics. He eventually manages to strike the Fused's body with his Shardblade, but the soul escapes as light to find a new shell.

Meanwhile, Shallan Davar operates in the Sadeas warcamp using the persona of a merchant named Chanasha Hasareh. She intentionally broadcasts her disdain for Dalinar Kholin to attract the attention of the Sons of Honor cult. While reviewing accounts in a winehouse, Veil is drugged by the owners and loses consciousness as part of a kidnapping plot. Back in Hearthstone, Kaladin evades the Fused's teleportation bursts by flying at high speeds through the clearing fog. The Fused, wearing a wrap of its own hair for clothing, eventually retreats after warning Kaladin that they will meet again.

Kaladin returns to the town and quickly defeats the remaining singer guards who refuse to retreat peacefully. He reunites with his mother Hesina and meets his young brother Oroden, who is delighted by Syl's playful shapes. Kaladin discovers that the Mink has snuck away from the hiding shed, leaving a dummy of rags in his place. He confronts his father Lirin, who is disappointed that Kaladin has brought further violence and death to Hearthstone. The argument is cut short when a massive flying ship called the Fourth Bridge emerges from the fog to evacuate the town.

### Chapter 3: The Fourth Bridge

Navani Kholin stands on the deck of the Fourth Bridge, a massive wooden platform kept aloft by conjoined fabrials. The vessel uses complex gemstone lattices and aluminum to transfer motion between the ship and a base in Urithiru. Navani takes pride in the engineering feat, which she named in honor of the bridges that originally saved her family. As the ship lands in Hearthstone, Edgedancers glide across the ground to heal sick refugees and organize the boarding process. Dalinar Kholin accompanies Navani, providing a steady leadership presence as the Alethi army prepares for the evacuation.

Dalinar meets Lirin, who is initially unimpressed by the military display and remains critical of the Blackthorn’s past. Lirin suggests the flying ship would be better utilized as a mobile hospital rather than a transport for soldiers. Kaladin reports the encounter with the new Fused and receives a fresh infusion of Stormlight from Dalinar's Bondsmith powers. The Mink reveals himself on the command deck, having successfully evaded the guards to inspect Navani's maps. He remains cynical about Alethi intentions but offers his surviving army's cooperation if the Fourth Bridge rescues his remaining men.

Navani manages the logistics of settling hundreds of Hearthstone townspeople into the ship's lower cargo holds. She continues her research into the broken fabrial systems of Urithiru, hoping to understand the tower's lost Sibling spren. Her observations of Dalinar's ability to touch the spiritual realm provide new data for her scholarly notebooks. The peaceful evacuation is interrupted by a report from the Windrunner Lyn regarding a massive enemy approach. Three full flights of Fused are spotted closing in on the Fourth Bridge, forcing the crew to prepare for immediate aerial combat.

### Chapter 4: Architects of the Future

Shallan wakes up with a sack over her head as she is carried into the deep chasms of the Shattered Plains. She maintains her merchant disguise while Radiant and Veil internally discuss the progress of their infiltration mission. Her captors remove the hood to reveal a group of robed cultists belonging to the secretive Sons of Honor. The group is led by Ulina, a member of Ialai Sadeas's inner circle who seeks to restore the Vorin church and Heralds. They use a specialized fabrial to verify that Shallan is not an illusionist before beginning her formal initiation.

Shallan recognizes the detection fabrial as one she previously sold to the cult at an exorbitant price to gain their trust. She glibly swears false oaths to the Sons of Honor and Queen Ialai, knowing her order allows for such deceptions. The cultists claim they orchestrated the return of the Voidbringers and Radiants to usher in a new era of divine rule. They believe Shallan is a simple merchant and refuse her immediate request to meet with Ialai Sadeas. Veil grows impatient with the slow progress and attempts to bait them with offers of information regarding Dalinar's plans.

The cultists boast that they already have a highly placed informant within Dalinar Kholin’s inner circle at Urithiru. This revelation alarms Shallan, as a mole in the high command could prove catastrophic for the coalition's survival. She realizes that she cannot afford the time required for a slow infiltration through the ranks of the cult. Fearing for the safety of her allies, Shallan decides to abandon her cautious merchant persona to force a confrontation. She stands up and pushes her captors aside, declaring that she is not actually the woman they believe her to be.
