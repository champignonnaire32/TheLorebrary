### 91: Why he Froze
In a flashback to his time in Amaram’s army, a young Kaladin cleans crem deposits off a barrack floor with a chisel. Crem is a hard mineral substance left behind by highstorms that creates uneven layers on stone surfaces. Kaladin is deeply concerned about his brother Tien, who has been assigned as a messenger boy in a different squad. Tukks promises to try and transfer Tien so the brothers can train together in the same unit.

Kaladin admits to Tukks that he did not freeze out of fear for his own safety or life. Tukks encourages Kaladin to focus on his squadmates as a surrogate family to overcome his hesitation to fight. The sergeant believes that caring for others is what makes a soldier sane rather than a callous killer. That evening, the rest of the squad becomes more welcoming to Kaladin because he was officially disciplined for his behavior.

Syl is now human-sized and has physical substance, allowing her to lean her head against Kaladin’s arm. She explains that spren do not need to sleep or eat but require a human bond to remain sentient in the Physical Realm. Kaladin remains haunted by the presence of Moash and the death of Sah during the fall of Kholinar. He resolves to find a weapon and a way to return to Roshar to help Dalinar and the others.
