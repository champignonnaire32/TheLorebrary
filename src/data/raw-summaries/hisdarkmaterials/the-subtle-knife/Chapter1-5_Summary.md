### 1: The Cat and the Hornbeam Trees
Will Parry leaves his mentally ill mother with his old piano teacher, Mrs. Cooper, to protect her from men searching their house. He returns home to find the men breaking in and searches for a green leather writing case containing his father's letters. During the struggle, the family cat Moxie trips one of the intruders, causing him to fall down the stairs and die from a broken neck. Will escapes with the case and travels to Oxford, where he observes a tabby cat disappear into a patch of thin air. He discovers a window between worlds and steps through it into the silent, abandoned city of Cittàgazze.

The city of Cittàgazze is built on a Mediterranean-style coastline with palm trees, stone breakwaters, and elaborate buildings like casinos and hotels. Will explores the empty streets and finds a café where he eats and then bathes in the sea before finding a place to sleep. He encounters Lyra Silvertongue, a girl from another world who is accompanied by a dæmon named Pantalaimon. A dæmon is a physical manifestation of a person's soul that takes animal form and can change shape until the person reaches adulthood. Lyra explains that she is looking for Dust, a mysterious elementary particle that her world's Church fears.

Will and Lyra share a meal of an omelette and baked beans while discussing their different origins and the nature of dæmons. Lyra realizes Will's dæmon is internal and invisible, which is a trait of people from his world. She uses her alethiometer, a golden truth-telling instrument with thirty-six symbols, to determine if Will is a friend or an enemy. The device informs her that Will is a murderer, which she interprets as a sign of his strength and reliability. They agree to help each other, with Will promising to show Lyra how to enter his version of Oxford.

### 2: Among the Witches

She infiltrates a ship belonging to the Magisterium and witnesses Mrs. Coulter arriving to oversee the torture of a captured witch. The Magisterium is the ruling religious body of Lyra's world that seeks to suppress natural impulses and control the population. Serafina uses mental magic to remain unnoticed while she observes a council of clerics discussing a prophecy about Lyra's destiny. The clerics believe Lyra is the "second Eve" who is destined to bring about a new fall of man.

Mrs. Coulter tortures the captured witch by breaking her fingers to extract information about the prophecy and Lyra's true name. Serafina becomes visible and kills the suffering witch with a knife to end her agony before fleeing the ship. She travels to Trollesund to consult with Dr. Lanselius, the witches' consul, who informs her of the Magisterium's massive military mobilization. Lanselius mentions rumors of zombis, warriors who have had their dæmons severed to make them mindless and fearless. Serafina returns to her homeland and convenes a council of witch clans to decide their course of action in the coming conflict. Lee Scoresby, a Texan aeronaut, joins the council and announces his plan to find the explorer Stanislaus Grumman, whom he believes is still alive. Scoresby seeks a mysterious object of protection that Grumman supposedly possesses to give it to Lyra. ### 3: A Children's World
Lyra wakes from a nightmare about the severed head of Stanislaus Grumman and tries to make an omelette in the Cittàgazze kitchen. Will wakes up and they discuss the existence of multiple parallel worlds connected by windows in the air. They meet two children, Angelica and Paolo, who explain that the adults have fled the city to escape Specters. Specters are invisible, malevolent entities that consume the souls of adults, leaving them as mindless, empty husks. Children are immune to Specters until they reach puberty, allowing them to roam the abandoned cities freely.

Will insists that Lyra must disguise herself and wash before they enter his Oxford to avoid attracting unwanted attention. He explains the need for "camouflage" to stay hidden from the authorities and the men who are hunting him. Lyra washes her hair and they find a department store where she picks out a tartan skirt and a green blouse. Will pays for the clothes, surprising Lyra, who is used to the children's lawless behavior in the abandoned city. They find the window in the air near the palm trees and prepare to cross back into Will's world.

Lyra ducks through the window and is immediately struck by a car on the busy Oxford ring road. Will follows her through and tells the witnesses that they are siblings named Mark and Lisa Ransom to hide their identities. They escape the scene and Lyra shows Will the alethiometer, which remains undamaged despite the accident. Will uses his mother's bank card to withdraw money and they take a bus into the center of Oxford. Lyra is overwhelmed by the differences in this version of her city, noting that Jordan College does not exist here.

### 4: Trepanning
Will calls his father's lawyer, Mr. Perkins, and learns that John Parry disappeared on an expedition to Alaska ten years ago. He sends a postcard to his mother to reassure her of his safety before hiding in a library to research his father. Will finds newspaper articles confirming that his father was a Royal Marine and an explorer whose expedition vanished without a trace. He uses a clipboard and paper to pose as a student doing a project to avoid being questioned for truancy. Meanwhile, Lyra explores the city and notices that people in this world have their dæmons hidden inside them.

Lyra visits a museum where she discovers trepanned skulls, which are human remains with holes intentionally bored into the bone. The alethiometer informs her that these skulls belong to ancient sorcerers and are surrounded by significant amounts of Dust. A man named Sir Charles Latrom observes Lyra and strikes up a conversation, offering to introduce her to people interested in her findings. Lyra senses a hidden rot about him, similar to the atmosphere of Iofur Raknison's palace, and politely declines his offer. She consults the alethiometer again and is told that her true task is to help Will find his father.

Lyra follows the alethiometer's instructions to a research unit where she meets Dr. Mary Malone, an experimental theologian. Dr. Malone explains that her team is investigating "Shadows," which are elementary particles of dark matter that exhibit consciousness. These Shadows are identical to the Dust Lyra is searching for and respond specifically to human thought and workmanship. Lyra demonstrates her ability to read the alethiometer, which shocks Dr. Malone by revealing her past as a nun. Dr. Malone agrees to let Lyra test the "Cave," a computer system designed to detect and communicate with these particles.

### 5: The Cave
Lyra uses her mental focus to communicate with the Shadows through the Cave's computer screen, producing vivid patterns of light. She realizes that the Shadows are the same entities that move the needles of her alethiometer. The Shadows confirm through the screen that they are the same as the Dust found in Lyra's world and that they are conscious. Dr. Malone is astonished by the clarity of the response Lyra elicits, which far exceeds anything her team has achieved. Lyra suggests that the computer could be programmed to show symbols like the alethiometer for easier communication.

Will visits the Institute of Archaeology and discovers that his father's expedition was actually seeking a window between worlds in the north. He realizes that the men who broke into his house were likely working for an organization interested in his father's discoveries. Will returns to the window in the air and crosses back into Cittàgazze, where he waits for Lyra to return. He feels a growing sense of responsibility for his father's legacy and the need to protect his mother. Lyra returns to the café in Cittàgazze and tells Will about her meeting with the scientist and the connection between Dust and dark matter.

They decide to stay in the café for the night and plan their next moves in both Oxfords. Lyra tells Will that the alethiometer has commanded her to help him find his father, which Will initially resists. He eventually accepts her help, realizing that the truth-teller might be the only way to locate John Parry. They observe more children entering the city, including a boy named Tullio who appears to be searching for something specific. Will hides the green leather writing case under his bed, and they prepare to return to the museum the next day.
