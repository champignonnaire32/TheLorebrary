### [Book Three - Chapter I]: [The Departure of Boromir]
Aragorn climbs the hill of Amon Hen and sits in the Seat of Seeing but sees nothing clearly. He hears the horn of Boromir blowing from the woods below and runs to find the warrior dying against a tree. Boromir admits he tried to take the Ring from Frodo and confesses that Orcs have captured the Halflings. He dies after Aragorn promises that Minas Tirith will not fall. Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli gather Boromir's body and place it in an Elven boat.

The companions place Boromir's broken horn, sword shards, and the weapons of his enemies into the boat with him. They sing a lament for the fallen man as his vessel drifts over the Falls of Rauros. Aragorn searches the riverbank and finds evidence that Frodo and Sam have taken a boat across the river alone. He decides to leave the Ring-bearer to his own path to avoid abandoning Merry and Pippin to the Orcs. The Three Hunters set off on foot to track the Orc-host into the plains of Rohan.

The hunters identify the tracks of a large company of Orcs moving with great speed. Legolas notes that these Orcs do not belong to Sauron but carry the White Hand of Saruman. Some of the dead Orcs at the battle site also bear a white S-rune on their helms. Aragorn realizes that Saruman has openly entered the war and is seeking the Halflings. The companions run through the night and into the following day across the rocky Emyn Muil. They descend the East Wall of Rohan and begin their long pursuit across the green grasslands.

### [Chapter II]: [The Riders of Rohan]
Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli pursue the Orc-host across the plains of Rohan for three days. They discover a leaf-shaped Elven brooch dropped by Pippin as a sign that he is still alive. The hunters realize the Orcs are running even under the sun, driven by a will that lends them unnatural speed. On the fourth day, Legolas spots a company of one hundred and five horsemen riding back from the north. The horsemen surround the three companions with spears leveled and demand they identify themselves.

Eomer, the Third Marshal of Riddermark, questions the strangers and threatens Gimli for speaking of the Lady Galadriel. Aragorn reveals his true identity as the heir of Isildur and shows the reforged sword Anduril to the Riders. Eomer is moved by the revelation and reports that his company destroyed the Orc-host the previous night. He claims no Halflings were found among the dead and that the bodies were all burned in a great pile. Eomer lends the companions two horses, Hasufel and Arod, on the condition that they return to Edoras.

The three hunters ride to the site of the battle near the eaves of Fangorn Forest. They find the smoking remains of the Orc-pile and a newly raised mound for the fallen Rohirrim. Night falls as they search the area, but they find no definitive sign of the hobbits. While camping under a tree, they see an old man in a wide-brimmed hat and cloak standing at the edge of the firelight. The figure vanishes instantly, and the companions discover that their horses have broken their pickets and fled. Aragorn suspects the visitor was Saruman walking in the woods.

### [Chapter III]: [The Uruk-hai]
Pippin wakes to find himself bound and being carried by Orcs after being knocked unconscious during the battle at Parth Galen. He observes a fierce argument between the Uruk-hai of Isengard, the Orcs of Mordor, and smaller mountain goblins. Ugluk, the leader of the Isengarders, asserts his authority by beheading two rivals and insists they return to Saruman. Pippin manages to cut his wrist-bonds using a jagged knife from a dead Orc during a brief struggle. He uses the opportunity to drop his Elven brooch in the grass as a trail for his friends.

The Orcs force the hobbits to run on their own legs after giving them a burning draught that restores their strength. They are hounded across the plains by whips and the constant threat of the approaching Rohirrim. Grishnakh, a Mordor Orc, attempts to search the hobbits for the Ring but is interrupted by Ugluk. The company reaches the edge of Fangorn Forest just as the Riders of Rohan encircle them on a small hill. During the night, Grishnakh steals the hobbits away from the camp to search them privately for the Ring.

A Rider of Rohan spots Grishnakh in the dark and kills him with a spear as he tries to flee with the captives. Merry and Pippin use the distraction to crawl away into the deep shadows near the river Entwash. They cut their remaining bonds with a discarded Orc-knife and eat some lembas to regain their strength. The hobbits watch from the edge of the woods as the sun rises and the Rohirrim charge the Orc camp. They witness the final destruction of the Orcs and the death of Ugluk at Eomer's hand.

### [Chapter IV]: [Treebeard]
Merry and Pippin escape deep into Fangorn Forest to avoid the battle between the Rohirrim and the Orcs. They drink from the Entwash stream and find the water provides an unusual sense of refreshment and growth. While climbing a rocky shelf to get a view of the land, they encounter a fourteen-foot-tall creature named Treebeard. Treebeard is an Ent, a shepherd of trees with a mossy beard and deep, ancient eyes. He carries the hobbits to his home at Wellinghall, a stone bay at the foot of the mountains.

Treebeard gives the hobbits a draught of Ent-wash that makes them feel taller and more vibrant. He listens to their story and expresses deep concern over Saruman’s treachery and the felling of trees for Isengard’s fires. Treebeard explains that the Ents are few in number because they lost the Entwives long ago and have no children. He tells the story of how the Entwives preferred gardens while the Ents loved the wild woods. The Ents have grown sleepy over the ages, but the news of the war begins to rouse Treebeard's dormant anger.

Treebeard summons a Great Entmoot at Derndingle to decide whether the Ents will go to war. Merry and Pippin spend three days with a "hasty" Ent named Quickbeam while the elders deliberate in their own slow language. On the third day, the Ents reach a decision and march south toward Isengard with a thunderous song. They are transformed from slow, tree-like beings into a terrifying force of destruction capable of splitting stone. Treebeard carries the hobbits on his shoulders as the forest itself seems to follow the Ents to war.

### [Chapter V]: [The White Rider]
Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli search the battlefield at the edge of Fangorn for signs of the hobbits. They find cut cords and a mallorn-leaf, confirming that Merry and Pippin escaped into the forest. While tracking the hobbits inside the woods, they encounter an old man dressed in grey rags. Gimli believes the figure is Saruman and urges his companions to attack before he can cast a spell. The old man reveals himself as Gandalf, returned from death and now robed entirely in white.

Gandalf explains that he fell into the abyss with the Balrog and fought the creature from the foundations of stone to the peak of Zirakzigil. He defeated the Balrog but died himself, only to be sent back to Middle-earth to finish his task. Gwaihir the Windlord rescued him from the mountain top and bore him to Lothlorien for healing. Gandalf informs the hunters that the hobbits are safe with Treebeard and that the Ents are marching on Isengard. He tells them the Ring has passed beyond his help and is currently moving toward Mordor.

The wizard whistles for Shadowfax, the chief of the Mearas, who brings Hasufel and Arod back to the companions. Gandalf mounts Shadowfax and leads the three hunters on a swift ride toward Edoras to meet King Theoden. He explains that Sauron is preparing for a massive assault on Minas Tirith and has released his forces prematurely. Saruman is also double-crossing Sauron while attempting to seize the Ring for himself. The company rides across the plains of Rohan as a great smoke rises in the west from the direction of Isengard.
