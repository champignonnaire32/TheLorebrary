### 16: The Window on the West

Sam accidentally reveals that Boromir tried to take the Ring from Frodo at Parth Galen. Faramir laughs at the irony but reiterates his vow to never claim the artifact for himself. Frodo finally confesses his plan to enter Mordor and destroy the Ring in the fire of Mount Doom.

### 17: The Forbidden Pool
Faramir wakes Frodo in the early morning to show him a creature in the pool below. Gollum, a small and slimy creature with large pale eyes, is diving for fish in the forbidden waters. Anborn, a soldier of Gondor, holds a bow ready to kill the intruder for trespassing. Frodo begs for the creature’s life, explaining that Gollum is his guide and is bound to his mission. Faramir allows Frodo to descend and attempt to capture the creature alive instead of shooting it.

Frodo crawls down to the edge of the pool where Gollum is eating a raw fish. He lures Gollum closer by promising safety and invoking the name of the "Precious." As Gollum approaches, Anborn and other hidden guards jump out and pin him to the ground. They bind Gollum’s hands and place a hood over his head to carry him back to the cave. Gollum screams and spits at Frodo, feeling betrayed by his master's trickery.

Back in the cave, Faramir questions Gollum about his identity and his knowledge of the hidden refuge. Frodo intercedes again and officially takes Gollum under his protection as a servant of the Ring-bearer. Faramir grants Frodo a year of freedom in Gondor and warns him against taking the path to Cirith Ungol. He describes Cirith Ungol as a place of dark terror and ancient evil located above the valley of Minas Morgul. The Captain gives Frodo his blessing and prepares supplies for their departure the next morning.

### 18: Journey to the Cross-roads
Faramir provides the hobbits with new walking staves made of lebethron wood and iron. The company leaves Henneth Annûn blindfolded once more before parting ways with the men of Gondor. Frodo, Sam, and Gollum travel south through the woodlands of Ithilien under a darkening sky. Gollum urges them to move faster as the air becomes heavy and the silence of the woods grows deep. They walk for two days, covering several leagues while avoiding the main roads and open spaces.

The travelers reach the end of the forest and see the road leading from the Tower of the Moon. Gollum identifies the distant ruins as Osgiliath and the polluted stream as the Morgulduin. They hide in a large holm-oak tree to rest as a great blackness begins to consume the stars. The next day brings a dead brown twilight instead of a normal sunrise due to the fumes of Mordor. A low rumbling sound like thunder or drums begins to shake the ground beneath their feet.

Gollum leads them to a great ring of ancient, broken trees where four roads meet. In the center sits a headless stone statue of a king that has been defaced with Orc graffiti. A crude stone head with a single red eye has been placed on the statue’s neck as a mockery. Frodo notices the original kingly head lying nearby, crowned with a trailing plant of white flowers. The sun sets behind the mountains, casting a brief light on the ruins before total darkness falls.

### 19: The Stairs of Cirith Ungol
The travelers climb a steep road toward the valley of Minas Morgul, the city of the Ringwraiths. The city glows with a pale, rotting light that looks like a corpse-fire and smells of decay. A white bridge spans a steaming river lined with luminous, malformed flowers that give off a charnel scent. Frodo feels a sudden compulsion to run toward the city gates but Sam catches him and pulls him back. They find a narrow path hidden in the stone wall that bypasses the bridge and leads up the mountainside.

A massive red flash from Mount Doom lights up the sky and a great crack of thunder shakes the earth. Minas Morgul answers with blue lightning and a piercing screech from the city gates. An army of black-clad soldiers and horsemen marches out of the city led by the Witch-king of Angmar. Frodo reaches for the Ring but instead grips the Phial of Galadriel to resist the Wraith-lord's presence. The Witch-king pauses at the bridge, sensing a power nearby, before leading his host toward the war in the West.

After the army passes, Gollum leads the hobbits to a sheer rock face containing the first of two long stairways. They climb the "Straight Stair," a vertical path of narrow and broken steps cut directly into the cliff. At the top, they enter a dark passage that leads to the "Winding Stair" on the upper slopes of the Ephel Dúath. They rest in a crevice where Sam and Frodo discuss whether their adventures will ever be turned into songs or stories. Gollum sneaks away while they sleep, returning later to find Sam cradling Frodo's head in his lap.

### 20: Shelob’s Lair
Gollum leads the hobbits to a massive cave entrance that reeks of filth and unnameable waste. They enter the tunnel of Torech Ungol, which is filled with an impenetrable and stagnant darkness. The air is heavy and they feel long, sticky tentacles or growths brushing against their heads. Sam and Frodo hold hands to stay together as the tunnel forks into multiple passages. Gollum disappears into the gloom, leaving the hobbits alone in the middle of the lightless labyrinth.

A bubbling hiss and a gurgling noise echo through the tunnel as a massive presence approaches from the shadows. Frodo uses the Phial of Galadriel to create a brilliant silver light that reveals Shelob, a monstrous spider-like creature. Shelob is the last child of Ungoliant, an ancient evil that has lived in the mountains since before the time of Sauron. The light of the Phial and the blade of Sting temporarily drive the creature back into her den. Frodo uses Sting to cut through a thick, rope-like web that blocks the exit of the tunnel.

Frodo runs out of the tunnel toward the pass of Cirith Ungol, thinking they have escaped. Shelob emerges from another exit and leaps behind Frodo while he is distracted by the sight of the pass. Gollum attacks Sam from behind, pinning his arms and trying to choke him to death. Sam fights back with his staff and sword, causing Gollum to flee into the darkness. Sam turns to find that Shelob has already reached Frodo and is hovering over his body.
