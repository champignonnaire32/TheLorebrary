### 16: Book II - Chapter 4: A Journey in the Dark

Wargs are large, intelligent wolves serving Sauron that hunt in packs and speak their own language. During the night, Legolas kills a great wolf-chieftain with an arrow, and Gandalf uses a fire spell to ignite the trees and repel the pack. ### 18: Book II - Chapter 6: Lothlórien

Gimli visits the Mirrormere to see the reflection of Durin’s Crown before the group follows the Silverlode river. Aragorn uses the healing herb athelas to treat Sam’s scalp wound and discovers Frodo’s Mithril armor. The group reaches the Nimrodel stream, where Legolas sings a song about an Elven-maid and an ancient King named Amroth. Legolas climbs a mallorn tree—a massive tree with silver bark and golden leaves—and is confronted by hidden Silvan Elves.

During the night, Frodo sees a small, dark figure with pale eyes sniffing at the base of the tree before it vanishes. ### 19: Book II - Chapter 7: The Mirror of Galadriel
The Company enters the city of Caras Galadhon and climbs a broad ladder to a high hall built into a giant mallorn. They meet Celeborn, Lord of the Galadhrim, and Galadriel, the Lady of Lórien, who appear tall, grave, and beautiful. Celeborn expresses grief over the fall of Gandalf and initially blames the Dwarves for stirring up the Balrog in Moria. Galadriel rebukes Celeborn’s harshness and speaks kindly to Gimli, who is moved to praise her beauty above all jewels. The Lady uses her power to search the hearts of the companions, offering each a choice to turn aside from the Quest.

The Company rests for several days in a pavilion while Elves sing laments for Mithrandir, the Elvish name for Gandalf. Legolas and Gimli become close friends, spending their time exploring the woods together. Galadriel leads Frodo and Sam to a hidden garden containing a silver basin on a pedestal. ### 20: Book II - Chapter 8: Farewell to Lórien
Celeborn summons the Company and offers them small, light boats to travel down the Great River Anduin. Aragorn accepts the gift, as it delays the difficult decision of whether to head toward Minas Tirith or Mordor. The Elves provide the group with lembas, a highly nutritious waybread, and grey cloaks woven by Galadriel and her maidens. Lembas is a thin, cream-colored cake that can sustain a traveler for a full day of labor. The Company also receives silken, silver-grey ropes made of hithlain, a strong and light Elven material.

The Company boards three boats at the hythe where the Silverlode meets the Anduin. They see a great Swan-ship carrying Celeborn and Galadriel, who has a circlet of golden flowers in her hair. Galadriel sings a song of farewell in the ancient Quenya tongue as the boats drift into the main current. Celeborn warns the travelers about the stony moors of the River and the dangerous cataracts of Rauros further south. He advises them to avoid the Forest of Fangorn, a strange and legendary land on the borders of Rohan.

Galadriel presents individual gifts to the Company, including a gem-encrusted sheath and the green Elessar stone for Aragorn. Boromir, Merry, and Pippin receive belts of gold and silver, while Legolas is given a powerful bow of the Galadhrim. Sam receives a small box of earth from Galadriel’s orchard, and Gimli requests and receives three strands of the Lady’s golden hair. Frodo is given a crystal phial containing the light of Eärendil’s star to serve as a light in dark places. The Company departs down the Anduin, and Gimli weeps as the golden woods of Lothlórien vanish from sight behind them.
