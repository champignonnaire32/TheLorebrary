### 1: A Long-expected Party
Bilbo Baggins celebrates his eleventy-first birthday with a massive party in Hobbiton and adopts Frodo as his heir. The wizard Gandalf arrives with dwarves and a large cargo of fireworks to provide entertainment for the gathered hobbits. Bilbo delivers a speech to a gross of guests before putting on a magic ring and vanishing in a flash of light. He returns to Bag End, removes his party clothes, and prepares to leave the Shire forever with a small group of dwarves. Gandalf confronts Bilbo in his study and insists that he leave the magic ring behind for Frodo as promised.

Bilbo initially resists and refers to the ring as his precious, but he eventually yields and places the envelope on the mantelpiece. He departs Hobbiton on foot, singing as he travels into the night toward the mountains. Frodo finds Gandalf in the study and receives the ring along with Bilbo's inheritance and various legal documents. The next morning, Frodo distributes labeled parting gifts to numerous relatives and neighbors who crowd into Bag End. Otho and Lobelia Sackville-Baggins arrive to inspect the will and express their extreme displeasure at being passed over.

Lobelia accuses Frodo of being a Brandybuck and attempts to steal several small valuables inside her umbrella before being escorted out. Frodo evicts several young hobbits who are caught digging for rumored treasure in the cellars and pantries. Gandalf returns to Bag End and warns Frodo to keep the ring secret and safe while he investigates its nature. The wizard departs the Shire after cautioning Frodo that his unpopularity and rumors of a plot are spreading. Frodo remains alone at Bag End for many years while the wizard's visits become increasingly rare and then cease entirely.

### 2: The Shadow of the Past
Frodo lives alone at Bag End for seventeen years while rumors of strange events and a dark power in Mordor spread through Middle-earth. Gandalf reappears after a nine-year absence and tells Frodo the history of the Great Rings of Power and their corrupting influence. He reveals that the ring Bilbo found is the One Ring, created by the Dark Lord Sauron to rule all others. Gandalf tests the ring in Frodo's hearth fire, revealing glowing Elvish letters that identify it as the Master-ring. He explains that Sauron is seeking the ring and has learned through the torture of Gollum that it was found by a hobbit named Baggins.

The wizard recounts how Isildur cut the ring from Sauron's hand but lost it in the Great River Anduin where it lay for millennia. A creature named Deagol found the ring while fishing, but his friend Smeagol murdered him to claim the gold for his own birthday. Smeagol used the ring's invisibility to spy and steal until he was expelled and retreated into the dark tunnels under the Misty Mountains. The ring eventually abandoned Smeagol, who had become the creature Gollum, and was picked up by Bilbo Baggins in the dark. Gandalf warns that the Enemy has captured Gollum and now knows the name of the Shire and its location.

Frodo attempts to throw the ring into the fire but find he cannot bring himself to damage the object. Gandalf refuses to take the ring himself, fearing the power it would give him to become a new Dark Lord. Frodo decides he must leave the Shire to protect its inhabitants and decides to travel under the name Mr. Underhill. Gandalf discovers Sam Gamgee eavesdropping outside the window and hauls him into the room by his ear. As punishment for listening to their secrets, Gandalf decrees that Sam must accompany Frodo on his dangerous journey to see the Elves.

### 3: Three is Company
Frodo spends the summer preparing to leave Bag End and pretends to move to a new house in Buckland to avoid suspicion. He sells his home to the Sackville-Bagginses and plans to depart on his fiftieth birthday, which coincides with Bilbo's birthday. Gandalf leaves the Shire in June to seek news but fails to return by the appointed time in September. Frodo hosts a small farewell dinner for his friends Pippin, Merry, Sam, and Fatty Bolger before his official departure. Merry and Fatty drive a cart of furniture ahead to the new house at Crickhollow while Frodo and Pippin remain behind.

Lobelia and Lotho Sackville-Baggins arrive at midnight to take possession of the keys and inspect every item on their inventory. Frodo, Sam, and Pippin shoulder their heavy packs and walk through the fields at night to avoid the village roads. They cross the Water and enter the Green Hill Country, looking back at the distant lights of Hobbiton one last time. A tall black figure on a black horse appears on the road and sniffs the air as if searching for a scent. Frodo hides in the grass and feels a sudden, overwhelming urge to put on the ring as the rider approaches.

The rider departs, but later a second encounter occurs where a black shadow crawls toward the hobbits in the dark woods. A company of High Elves led by Gildor Inglorion arrives, singing a song to Elbereth, which causes the rider to flee. Gildor invites the hobbits to lodge with the Elves for the night in a hidden glade above Woodhall. They eat enchanted food and drink golden forest liquids while discussing the appearance of the Black Riders and Gandalf's delay. Gildor names Frodo an Elf-friend and warns him that he must flee the Shire immediately with his trusty companions.

### 4: A Short Cut to Mushrooms
The hobbits wake in the Elven bower to find the High Elves have already departed but left behind food and drink. Frodo decides to cut across the country toward Bucklebury Ferry to avoid the main road and its potential watchers. They struggle through thickets and cross the Stock-brook, where they spot a black rider standing on a ridge above them. The group halts under an elm tree for lunch and hears a long-drawn, piercing wail answered by another cry in the distance. They hurry across open grasslands and realize they have drifted too far south of their intended course for the ferry.

Pippin recognizes the land as Bamfurlong, the farm belonging to the formidable Farmer Maggot who once beat Frodo for stealing mushrooms. Three large dogs named Grip, Fang, and Wolf confront the hobbits before Maggot himself appears and recognizes Pippin. The farmer invites the travellers into his brick house for beer and news about a strange visitor on a black horse. Maggot describes how a hooded man offered him gold for information about Baggins but was chased away by the farm dogs. He warns Frodo that these black fellows are dangerous and likely seeking the gold Bilbo brought from foreign parts.

Mrs. Maggot serves a large dinner of mushrooms and bacon to the group and the farmer's large family. Farmer Maggot hitches his ponies to a wagon and drives the hobbits across the causeway toward the ferry in the growing fog. They hear hoofbeats approaching in the dark and Frodo hides under blankets in the back of the wagon. The rider turns out to be Merry Brandybuck, who has come out from the ferry landing to look for his overdue friends. Maggot gives Frodo a basket of mushrooms as a parting gift and returns home while the group prepares to cross.

### 5: A Conspiracy Unmasked
The hobbits board a large flat ferry-boat and Merry poles them across the dark Brandywine River toward the landing-stage. Frodo looks back and sees a dark, crouching figure searching the ground under the lamps on the western shore. They reach the Buckland side and hurry toward the new house at Crickhollow while the mist thickens around them. Fatty Bolger welcomes them into the warm, low-roofed house where he and Merry have arranged Frodo's furniture to resemble Bag End. The group takes hot baths in three large tubs and sings songs before sitting down to a late supper of mushrooms.

Frodo decides to reveal his plan to leave the Shire immediately, but Merry interrupts and explains that they already know his secrets. Merry reveals that he saw Bilbo use the ring years ago and has even glimpsed parts of Bilbo's private book. The friends admit they have formed a conspiracy to watch Frodo and have been tracking his preparations since the spring. Sam confesses that he acted as their chief investigator and reported Frodo's private conversations with Gandalf to the others. Merry and Pippin insist on accompanying Frodo into the wild, refusing to let him face the deadly peril alone.

The group decides to enter the Old Forest at dawn to bypass the watched gates and the main Brandywine Bridge. Fatty Bolger agrees to stay behind at Crickhollow to impersonate Frodo and tell Gandalf which path they have taken. They spend the night packing extra clothes and perishable food into six ponies kept in a nearby stable. Frodo falls into a troubled sleep and dreams of a tall white tower standing on a ridge above a dark sea. He hears the sound of thunder and sees a strange light in the sky before the morning light wakes him.
