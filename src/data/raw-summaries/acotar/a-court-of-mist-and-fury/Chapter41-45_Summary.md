# Chapter 41-45 Summaries

### Chapter 41
Rhys, Feyre, Cassian, and Azriel return to the Velaris town house to meet a pale Amren. Rhys leads the group into the courtyard garden to discuss the failed meeting with the human queens. The queens demand proof of the Inner Circle's good intentions before they will surrender their half of the Book of Breathings. Cassian suggests deposing the queens, but Rhys notes that the Book must be given freely for its magic to work. Rhys explains the history of Miryam, a half-Fae healer, and Drakon, a Fae prince who liberated human slaves from the Black Land.

The Black Land was a cruel desert kingdom where humans were enslaved by High Fae until Miryam and Drakon fled to find peace. Rhys refuses to involve the couple in the current conflict despite their powerful aerial army. He decides to show the human queens the city of Velaris as the necessary proof of his character. To ensure the queens believe the vision is real, Rhys plans to use the Veritas, a magical truth-telling orb. The Veritas is a guarded talisman currently held by Mor’s family in the Hewn City.

Feyre walks toward the Rainbow artist district but turns back out of fear before entering the quarter. Rhys waits for her in the foyer and expresses his reluctance to show her the "High Lord" mask he must wear at the Court of Nightmares. He reveals that Mor was once sold in marriage to Eris of the Autumn Court to serve as breeding stock. Mor slept with Cassian to ruin her value, leading her family to nail a note to her body and abandon her. Feyre takes Rhys’s hand and asks for instructions on the role she must play during the heist.

### Chapter 42
Rhys flies Feyre toward the Hewn City while Cassian and Azriel provide aerial flanking. During the flight, someone attacks the group with ash arrows designed to kill Fae. Rhys dives for the forest but finds no tracks because the enemy used magic to hide their presence. He orders Cassian to establish aerial patrols along the sea borders and commands Azriel to warn his spies. The group proceeds to the Hewn City, a massive metropolis carved directly into the dark stone of a mountain.

Feyre wears a black, glittering outfit and a diamond-flecked diadem to play the role of Rhys’s consort. They enter an ebony throne room supported by columns carved with scaled black beasts. Cassian and Azriel appear in scaled battle armor with seven magical Siphons apiece to manage their killing power. Rhys projects an aura of extreme malice as he takes the throne and pulls Feyre onto his lap. Feyre acts as a seductive distraction while Azriel vanishes into the shadows to locate the Veritas orb.

Keir, the Steward of the Hewn City and Mor’s father, approaches the dais to deliver reports. Rhys strokes Feyre’s skin and forces Keir to fetch her wine to demonstrate his absolute dominance. Azriel returns with a subtle nod to confirm he has successfully stolen the truth-telling orb. Keir hisses an insult at Feyre, prompting Rhys to use his power to shatter the Steward’s arm bones into fragments. Rhys forbids Keir from seeing a healer and orders the court to dump him in his room before departing.

**Sentence Count Check:** 15

### Chapter 43
Rhys winnows Feyre to a moonlit mountain lake and apologizes for the violence she witnessed at the court. He expresses a fear of his own protective instincts and worries about the "dark lord" reputation he carries. Feyre argues that he should stop hiding behind masks and accuses him of shutting out his friends. She points out that his fear of being seen as a monster might be what actually isolates him. Rhys flinches at her words and immediately winnows them back to the Velaris town house.

Rhys avoids the town house for two days to hunt the attackers who used ash arrows. Amren remains in her apartment decoding the Book of Breathings while using the Summer Court's blood ruby as a paperweight. She explains that the enemies can now track Rhys’s magic whenever he winnows or uses his power. Feyre learns that Starfall, a celebration of a yearly spirit migration, will occur the following night. Amren tells Feyre that Rhys is a dreamer who envisions a world of peace and freedom for all races.

Feyre writes three separate notes to Rhys using their magical communication paper. The first two notes remain in her hand, but the third note, which calls him a coward, finally vanishes. She prepares for the Starfall festivities while reflecting on the fact that Rhys was the person who truly freed her. Nuala and Cerridwen, the half-wraith twins who serve the house, arrive to help Feyre dress for the event. Feyre waits for a response to her letter, but the desk remains empty as sundown approaches.

**Sentence Count Check:** 15

### Chapter 44
Feyre wears a gown made of pale blue gems and diamond cuffs that makes her look like a fallen star. Cassian scoops her up and flies her to the House of Wind, where crowds have gathered on the balconies. Mor explains that she once chose to sleep with Cassian to exercise control over her own body before her family could sell her. Starfall begins as thousands of spirits, appearing as white and blue falling stars, migrate across the night sky. The citizens of Velaris cheer and dance to the sound of drums and harps as the spirits pass overhead.

Rhys leads Feyre to a private upper balcony to watch the migration away from the main crowd. He reveals that Amarantha forced him to service her every year during Starfall to ruin the holiday for him. Rhys explains that the "stars" are actually spirits on a yearly migration to an unknown destination. A stray spirit collides with Feyre's face, splattered her skin with glowing green stardust. Another spirit hits Rhys, leaving a streak of heavenly war paint across the left side of his face.

Feyre uses the glowing dust to paint a star on the back of Rhys’s hand. This act marks the first time she has felt the urge to create art since the events Under the Mountain. Rhys tells her she is exquisite and admits he was afraid she would reject him if he showed his true self. He confesses that he regrets the brutal way he kissed her Under the Mountain because he was jealous and angry. Feyre asks him to dance, and they join the celebration on the lower patio until the sun rises over Velaris.

**Sentence Count Check:** 15

### Chapter 45
The group winnows to an Illyrian war-camp called Wind-Haven, where the environment is characterized by mud and primitive tents. Lord Devlon, the camp leader, resists Rhys’s command to prioritize the training of female Illyrian children. Rhys informs the warriors that Feyre is "his" and threatens to grind their bones to dust if they touch her. He explains the Blood Rite, a brutal survival trial where Illyrian males must cross mountains without magic or supplies. Rhys, Cassian, and Azriel once won the Rite despite being dumped in separate locations to ensure their failure.

Rhys flies Feyre to a forested clearing to train her magic away from potential casualties at the camp. He reveals the history of his family’s death and the origins of his blood-feud with Tamlin. Tamlin’s father and brothers, including Tamlin himself, traveled to the Illyrian wilderness to ambush Rhys’s mother and sister. They slaughtered the females and sent their heads in boxes down a river to the nearest Illyrian camp. Rhys and his father retaliated by winnowing to the Spring Court manor to execute Tamlin’s entire family.

Rhys admits he personally killed Tamlin’s brothers by melting their brains while Tamlin killed Rhys’s father in a single blow. Feyre processes the revelation that Tamlin participated in the slaughter of Rhys’s mother and younger sister. She practices her magic, combining fire, ice, water, and darkness into a unified force that sweeps through the clearing. Feyre realizes that she now belongs only to herself and can forge her own future with her combined powers. She looks at Rhys in his fighting gear and tells him that she wants to paint his portrait.

**Sentence Count Check:** 15
