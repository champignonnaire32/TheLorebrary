# RED RISING: Chapter 5: Darrow: Voyager Cloak to Chapter 9: Darrow: Angelia

### Chapter 5: Darrow: Voyager Cloak
Darrow briefs his officers on Operation Voyager Cloak at a hidden construction site. He reveals that the Fear Knight, Atlas au Grimmus, was baited with a dataStack containing fake intelligence about a shield vulnerability. Darrow plans to lure Atalantia’s army into a killbox on the Plains of Caduceus to be destroyed by atomics. He introduces BlueReach Seven as part of Operation Tartarus, a secret contingency plan. Master Maker Glirastes has been utilized to modify seven ancient engines to handle massive data loads.

Imperator Harnassus confronts Darrow about using Gold technology and unearthing the dangerous ancient engines. Darrow admits the plan is a desperate gamble but insists it is the only way to define the terms of engagement. Harnassus argues that Mercury is lost because the Senate recalled half the fleet to Luna. Darrow forces Harnassus into submission by summoning ten Howlers in pulseArmor to surround him. Harnassus eventually delivers a reluctant salute and accepts Darrow’s command over the army.

Alexandar sends a distorted hologram message reporting that the Fear Knight did not attack the intended target at Eleusis. Instead, the enemy has moved toward the civilian city of Angelia in the mid-eastern Ladon. Darrow orders Orion to take her pilots to the Storm Gods, which are massive weather-altering engines. He gives Rhonna a master switch for the Storm Gods, hidden in his second thigh box, as a safety measure against Orion. Darrow sends a final message to Thraxa to reconnoiter Angelia but avoid direct engagement with the Fear Knight.

### Chapter 6: Lysander: Carnivores
Lysander meets Atalantia au Grimmus in her meditation garden aboard the battleship Annihilo. Atalantia wears a black vasta serpent around her neck and is surrounded by exotic carnivorous orchids. She has painted the faces of dead family members out of a massive mural, including Lysander’s own face. High-ranking Golds, including Scorpio au Votum and Asmodeus au Carthii, join via hologram to interrogate the returned heir. They accuse Lysander of being a Republic agent or the catamite of the traitor Cassius au Bellona.

Diomedes au Raa and Seraphina au Raa enter the chamber to propose an alliance between the Rim and the Core. Diomedes reveals that his father, Romulus, died walking Akari’s Path after admitting Darrow destroyed the Ganymede Dockyards. The Core Golds mock Romulus’s honorable suicide, prompting Lysander to defend the man’s character. Lysander reveals that the Minotaur, Apollonius au Valii-Rath, has seized the Venus dockyards, crippling Atalantia’s logistics. He uses this leverage to convince Atalantia that she requires the Rim’s fresh legions and resources to win.

Atalantia accepts the alliance but demands that the Rim prove their loyalty by falling in an upcoming Iron Rain. Diomedes refuses on diplomatic grounds, but Seraphina volunteers to fight alongside the Core. After the meeting, Ajax au Grimmus strikes Lysander for remaining with Cassius for ten years instead of returning home. Lysander kneels and asks Atalantia for permission to fall in the Rain to earn his Peerless Scar. Atalantia kisses Lysander and grants his request, ordering him to fall under the command of Ajax.

### Chapter 7: Darrow: The Calm
Darrow leads a group of twenty bodyguards into the desert on gravBikes to find his scouts. They locate Alexandar au Arcos hiding in an arroyo, looking physically haggard from weeks of tracking the enemy. Alexandar explains that he lost the Fear Knight’s trail after falling asleep at the controls of his bike. Darrow finds Thraxa au Telemanus on a ridge overlooking the civilian city of Angelia. She wears a chameleon wolfcloak and possesses a prosthetic arm made of unpolished asteroid metal.

Darrow uses optics to view a "forest" of two hundred impaled bodies erected by the Fear Knight outside the city. Thraxa admits they lost Atlas in the Buonides Range before he reached this position. Darrow contacts Orion, who has returned to BlueReach One to prepare the Storm Gods for activation. Orion explains that the weather-altering engines cannot be easily cooled once they reach primary horizon. Darrow realizes the Fear Knight is using the atrocities at Angelia as a distraction to buy time.

Darrow decides to move into the city despite the high risk of traps and Gorgon ambushes. He orders the Howlers to rally and prepare for an engagement with the Fear Knight’s elite units. Thraxa calls for medships to assist the impaled soldiers, but Darrow remains focused on the tactical situation. He reflects on the "Mind’s Eye" and the mental toll of the ongoing Solar War. Darrow gives the final command to initiate Operation Tartarus and activate the storm engines to mask his movements.

### Chapter 8: Lysander: The Machine
Lysander enters a military assembly line on the Annihilo to prepare for the imminent Iron Rain. He receives chemical injections, conditioning cocktails, and measurements for a suit of pulseArmor. Pytha, his pilot, warns him that he is walking into a death trap and calls him a fool. She reveals her past as a First Decurion in the Bellona fleet and her experience in previous Rains. Lysander argues that he must fight to prevent a three-hundred-year dark age of total chaos.

The Golds perform a Blood Benediction led by white-haired virgin girls carrying iron daggers. Lysander’s hand is sliced to symbolize the removal of cowardice before the descent. Ajax au Grimmus, wearing storm armor with thunderhead shoulders, delivers a speech to the Tenth Expeditionary Legion. He demands the heads of Darrow, Sevro, and other Republic leaders from his Iron Leopards. The legion of one hundred thousand soldiers from Earth roars in response to his call for blood.

Ajax reveals his secret plan to seize the city of Heliopolis instead of supporting the Votum attack on Tyche. He warns Lysander to stay close and listen for the "howl" that signifies Darrow’s presence on the field. Lysander observes Seraphina au Raa preparing her starShell, which is painted with the Grimmus death’s-head. He realizes Seraphina’s true motivation for joining the war is a deep-seated personal hunger for combat. Lysander enters his own starShell and prepares to launch into the atmosphere of Mercury.

### Chapter 9: Darrow: Angelia
Darrow inspects a "forest" of two hundred impaled soldiers outside the city of Angelia. Each body is branded with the Fear Knight’s mark, representing a child’s face ringed with serpents. Darrow notes that many of the soldiers are still alive but likely booby-trapped with explosives. He orders the Howlers to use thermals and sensors to navigate the city while avoiding the trapped promenade. Inside the city’s communications center, Darrow finds piles of mutilated civilians killed by a mottled pathogen.

Marbles, a Green slicer, jacks into the refinery computer and discovers "hardlines" connecting the mining cities. Darrow realizes Atlas used these analog lines to bypass electronic jamming and send commands. The Fear Knight has ordered the reactors in multiple cities to overload and destroy the planetary shield chain. Darrow orders an immediate evacuation as the Angelia reactor begins a thermal meltdown. The resulting explosion destroys the city and the impaled soldiers in a wave of steam and fire.

The northern shield chain fails as multiple reactors explode across the Waste of Ladon. Atalantia’s armada begins a heavy bombardment of the surface while friction trails signal the start of an Iron Rain. Darrow realizes his army is out of position and orders all units to execute Operation Tartarus. He sends Thraxa to Kydon to lead the tank legions while he prepares to meet Arcosian reinforcements. Darrow broadcasts a final rallying speech to the Free Legions as the horizon fills with atomic blasts.
