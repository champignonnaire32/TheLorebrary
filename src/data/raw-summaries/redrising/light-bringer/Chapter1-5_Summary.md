### 1: Darrow: Castaway
Darrow and his band of survivors are marooned on the trash moon Marcher-1632, hiding their crippled corvette Archimedes. They perform daily, monotonous repairs on the ship while waiting for Cassius to return with needed helium. Cut off from the Republic, Darrow struggles with guilt over his failed campaign on Mercury and his abandoned army. He finds a cryptic book left by Aurae, which he begins to read for guidance. After a shift, he looks out at the stars and visualizes home, briefly finding strength in the thought of his wife and son before the sun’s glare forces him into the base.

### 2: Darrow: The Book
Life in the old Sons of Ares base is a cycle of crushing routine, radiation-induced sickness, and constant worry for those left behind. Darrow avoids deep interaction with his soldiers, feeling the burden of their lost comrades. He discusses the suspicious nature of Aurae and the whereabouts of Cassius with Harnassus, the Orange engineer. Darrow visits Screwface, who is broken by his experiences and deeply bitter, refusing to engage in any hope for their return home. Darrow practices with practice blades in a gravity-shifted sparring chamber, listening to the voice of his wife to maintain focus.

### 3: Darrow: Revenants
The base’s proximity alarm alerts Darrow to an incoming ship, which turns out to be Colloway Char arriving in a stolen torchShip. Char brings two thousand survivors from Heliopolis, confirming that Virginia is governing from Agea, but reporting that the war is not going well. Darrow learns that the Rim forces, led by Helios and Dido, are successfully invading, and many of the Free Legions were decimated by Atlas. Cassius returns with the promised helium, bringing Aurae with him, though the men are skeptical of her intentions. Cassius reveals that Sevro is still alive and has been sold to Apollonius in a Syndicate auction.

### 4: Darrow: The Sordid Affair
Cassius shows Darrow a recording of the Syndicate auction and a message from Apollonius, who intends to hunt and kill Sevro for sport in ninety days. Darrow’s men urge him to take the now-repaired Archimedes and flee directly to Mars, fearing any detour. Despite their warnings, Darrow decides to intercept Apollonius at the Dockyards of Venus to rescue Sevro, believing it will also strike a blow to the Gold war industry. Aurae and Cassius agree to join the mission, with Aurae revealing she has her own reasons for helping. Darrow feigns a surrender to his men, then plans to sneak onto the ship with his allies.

### 5: Lysander: Games
Lysander is beloved by the people of Mercury, who celebrate his victory over the Rising with games in Heliopolis. He navigates complex political alliances with the Votum and Carthii houses, seeking to secure power while managing the influence of Atalantia and Apollonius. Lysander secretly supports the Minotaur’s activities to undermine Atalantia’s control over his stolen resources. During the chariot races, Lysander’s friend Cicero defies his promise and competes, publicly dedicating the race to Lysander. Lysander observes the political maneuvering of the crowd, carefully guarding his true intentions while building his public image as the Lightbringer.
