### Chapter 55: Flight

Eragon and Saphira depart from Ellesméra and fly south over the forest of Du Weldenvarden to rejoin the Varden. Oromis and Glaedr accompany them to the forest's edge before turning toward Gil'ead to assist the elves in battle. Saphira flies continuously for two days and nights, pausing only briefly for water and to allow Eragon to stretch. Eragon practices his swordsmanship with Brisingr during these short breaks to manage his growing anxiety about the upcoming siege. They cross the Hadarac Desert and enter the Empire, avoiding detection by the king's forces while heading for the city of Feinster.

Saphira suffers from extreme physical exhaustion as her flight muscles burn with unbearable pain during the third day of travel. Eragon offers to use magic to ease her suffering, but she refuses to ensure he retains his strength for the battle. They reach the Jiet River and spot a dull red glow on the horizon that indicates the location of Feinster. Eragon dons his armor and consumes a sip of faelnirv, an enchanted elven liqueur that restores his energy. He notices that the silver flask is already one-third empty and resolves to use the liquid more sparingly in the future.

The duo arrives at Feinster and observes a sea of Varden soldiers surging against the city's well-fortified walls and iron gates. Saphira announces their arrival with a massive roar and a blast of blue fire that illuminates the night sky. The Varden cheer at the sight of the dragon, while the city's defenders groan in despair. Trianna, a Varden sorceress, contacts Eragon mentally to report that Arya and another elf are trapped behind enemy lines. Saphira immediately dives toward the city to rescue the two elves before they are overwhelmed by imperial soldiers.



### Chapter 56: Brisingr!

Saphira dives into Feinster and lands on the parapet, knocking soldiers off the eighty-foot-high walls with her claws and tail. Eragon identifies Arya and Blödhgarm, an elf with fur and fangs, fighting off a large group of soldiers at the base of a tower. Saphira crushes several men upon landing and snaps the spine of another with her jaws to clear the area. Arya explains that the city gates are protected by magic and they were ambushed by spellcasters while trying to open them. Eragon drains the life force from dead soldiers to replenish Saphira's energy before they head toward the main gates.

Eragon draws his new sword, which shimmers with blue light, and decapitates a soldier as they race through the city streets. They encounter three robed spellcasters chanting in the ancient language near the iron-bound doors, but the magicians flee toward the keep. Fifty heavily armed soldiers block their path to the gates, and their captain curses Eragon for bringing war to their homes. Eragon attempts to persuade the men to surrender, but they refuse out of loyalty to Lady Lorana. While Eragon distracts the guard, Arya and Blödhgarm sneak into the towers to operate the gate mechanisms.

Eragon stabs the captain through his shield and chest, killing him instantly as the gears within the towers begin to turn. He cuts through the remaining soldiers with blinding speed while Saphira incinerates several others with her breath. The massive city gates swing open, allowing the Varden's battering ram and a column of warriors to stream into Feinster. Eragon greets Roran and Horst among the ranks and informs them that Brom, not Morzan, was his biological father. Jörmundur orders the group to fight toward the central keep while Saphira harries the imperial forces from the air.



### Chapter 57: Shadow of Doom

Eragon, Saphira, and Arya fly to the tallest tower of the keep to capture Lady Lorana and end the resistance. Saphira lands on the roof, and the two Riders drop onto a narrow stone ledge to enter a room filled with crossbows. They descend the stairs and find Lady Lorana, who explains that the King's spellcasters are attempting to create a Shade. Eragon experiences a mental vision of Glaedr and Oromis fighting Thorn and Murtagh in the sky above Gil'ead. He witnesses Oromis suffer a seizure and get struck down by Murtagh's sword, Zar'roc, while Galbatorix speaks through Murtagh's mouth.

Glaedr's physical body is killed in the vision, and his consciousness retreats into his Eldunarí, a golden gemlike heart of hearts. Eragon returns to reality in the Feinster tower as three magicians complete their ritual and summon spirits into a human vessel. The vessel transforms into a Shade named Varaug, who possesses crimson hair and maroon eyes. Varaug immediately attacks Eragon's mind with overwhelming force and lifts Arya into the air by her throat. Eragon struggles to isolate the Shade's disparate consciousness while Saphira remains immobilized by a similar mental assault.

Arya breaks the Shade's wrist and elbow to free herself, but the creature's bones instantly pop back into place. Eragon manages to distract the Shade's mind for a split second, allowing Arya to reach her fallen sword. She lunges forward and stabs Varaug through the heart, causing the spirits within him to tear his body apart in a burst of light. Eragon uses magic to heal Arya's internal injuries while Saphira keens in mourning for the loss of Oromis and Glaedr. They decide to inform Nasuada of the deaths and the existence of the Eldunarí once they reach safety.



### Chapter 58: Sunrise

Blödhgarm and the other elves rejoin Eragon and Arya in the keep courtyard after the Shade's destruction. Arya releases Eragon from his oath of secrecy so he can inform the others of Oromis and Glaedr's deaths. They take Lady Lorana into custody and hand her over to Jörmundur, who promises she will be treated with respect. Eragon and Saphira meet Nasuada in an abandoned house to report the events of the battle in private. He reveals the truth about his father, Brom, and shows them the Eldunarí he carried from Du Weldenvarden.

Nasuada is shocked to learn that Galbatorix's power comes from hundreds of enslaved dragon hearts of hearts. Arya explains that the elves would be enraged to know their ancestors' souls are being used as magical batteries by the king. Eragon demonstrates the dim glow within Glaedr's Eldunarí, noting that the dragon is currently lost in a state of deep grief. Nasuada resolves to find a way to separate Galbatorix from the Eldunarí to weaken him before the final confrontation. She declares that the Varden will next march north to capture Belatona and Dras-Leona on their way to the capital.

Eragon and Saphira leave the city to rest in the Varden's camp, accompanied by their eleven elven guards. They watch the sunrise from the city walls, observing the smoke rising from the burning hovels and the distant sea. Eragon realizes they are the last free dragon and Rider left in Alagaësia to oppose Galbatorix's reign. He hugs Glaedr's Eldunarí to his chest and feels a renewed sense of purpose despite the heavy loss they suffered. He presents the golden stone to the new day, smiling as he prepares for the final battles of the war.
