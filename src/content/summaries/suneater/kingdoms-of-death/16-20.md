### 16: Vultures
Hadrian awakens face-down in the mud of Padmurak’s waste-water tunnels following his fall from the bridge. Two scavengers named Carry and Looker discover him and debate whether he is alive or salvage. Hadrian identifies himself as a knight of the Sollan Empire and realizes he has lost Olorin’s highmatter sword (a blade of stabilized pentaquark baryons). He concludes that the Lothrian government orchestrated the bridge attack because the "rebels" moved with military precision. Looker prods Hadrian with a short silver object that Hadrian recognizes as his own missing weapon.

Hadrian seizes Looker’s wrist and activates the highmatter blade to force the scavengers to stand back. He introduces himself by name and learns the names of Carry, a tall gray-faced man, and Looker, a young zuk (a member of the Lothrian low-caste workers). Hadrian collapses from a concussion while attempting to explain that his companions are in danger. Carry and Looker decide to transport the injured "drowned man" to a doctor named Magda. They load Hadrian onto a boat-sledge (a hybrid boat and sledge vehicle) filled with scrap and navigate deeper into the concrete tunnels.

Carry uses a water valve to douse Hadrian and his suit in fresh water to remove sewage and oil. Hadrian drifts in and out of consciousness, experiencing disjointed memories of other possible lives. He observes that the tunnels are the fossilized remains of the original colony structures on Padmurak. Carry explains that there are no rebels and that they are simply scavengers living beneath the city domes. The scavengers provide Hadrian’s arm for support and guide him toward Magda’s hidden clinic.



### 17: The Adorator
Hadrian awakens in a small clinic hidden within the steam tunnels of Vedatharad. Magda, a matronly Lothrian doctor, treats Hadrian’s concussion with painkillers and tells him he must rest for several days. She reveals that she is a Museum Catholic, a practitioner of an ancient adorator faith protected by Imperial law. Magda explains that her mentor, Father Dias, was arrested by the Conclave for teaching zuks to speak Galstani. Hadrian observes Magda’s small greenhouse of genetically stunted fruit trees that provide her with fresh food.

While drifting in a drug-induced haze, Hadrian hears Magda singing an old English prayer for deliverance. He awakens screaming to find a deep, bleeding wound in his side that was not present when he arrived. Magda attempts to treat the injury, but Hadrian threatens her with his sword because he suspects she is harvesting his organs. He collapses again and experiences a state of white darkness where time and pain lose all meaning. Hadrian realizes that his consciousness is jumping between potential versions of himself across parallel bands of time.

He concludes that the Quiet’s influence allows him to trade his current state for a version of himself from a different probability. Hadrian awakens three days later to find that the wound in his side has vanished without leaving a scar. Carry informs him that he has been asleep for three days and provides him with a cup of fungal noodles. Hadrian dons his combat skin and armor, noting that the scavengers cleaned his equipment while he recovered. He demands that Carry take him to the surface so he can warn Valka and the Imperial consul of the Conclave’s betrayal.



### 18: Up Acheron
Hadrian finishes his meal and prepares to leave the clinic despite Magda’s warning that he is still unfit to travel. He thanks Magda for her help and she tells him that her God has a plan for his life. Carry and Looker transport Hadrian upstream in their boat-sledge toward the spillways of the First Dome. They pass several bloated corpses in the water, including a Conclave Guardsman wearing the red and white colors of the rebels. Carry explains that the Party often uses its own secret police to stage rebel attacks to maintain social control.

Hadrian learns that the Conclave Guard frequently conducts sweeps of the tunnels to take zuks to offworld work camps. He worries that the Lothrian government has already destroyed the Imperial embassy and killed his companions. Looker reveals that the scavengers know some Galstani and asks if Hadrian’s people will kill the Conclave. The boat enters a broad watercourse illuminated by orange wall sconces and covered in black slime. Carry points out a door at the base of a spillway that leads to the city streets above.

The scavengers dock the boat and Looker secures the rope to a rickety metal strand. Hadrian experiences a flashback to the death of his friend Cat in the sewers of Borosevo due to the similar smell of rot. Carry bids Hadrian farewell with the English phrase "Peace be with you," revealing his conversion to Magda's faith. Hadrian echoes the blessing and begins his ascent through the concrete stairwell toward the Great City. He emerges into the gray darkness of Vedatharad determined to reach the Sollan embassy before the Conclave acts.



### 19: The Turn of the Screw
Hadrian navigates the dark, nearly empty streets of Vedatharad while avoiding military police vans and cameras. Two Conclave Guards in matte black armor spot him and open fire with disruptors near a colonnade. Hadrian activates his shield and flees across a steel step bridge that spans a major highway. He uses his highmatter sword to sever the bridge’s supports, causing the structure to collapse and trap his pursuers. He reaches the Sollan embassy and identifies himself to the door guards before falling across the threshold in exhaustion.

Consul Damon Argyris greets Hadrian and reveals that Pallino, Crim, and the other survivors are already inside the building. Valka arrives in the lobby and embraces Hadrian, followed by Captain Otavia Corvo who has arrived from the Tamerlane. Hadrian informs the group that the Conclave Guard orchestrated the bridge attack and are currently hunting him. Argyris attempts to dismiss the accusation as "liberalist" propaganda, but Hadrian shouts him down and orders an immediate evacuation. Hadrian suspects the Lothrians want to kidnap him to study his biological and mental secrets in their science ministry.

The Imperial party gathers their equipment and prepares to board a convoy of vehicles provided by the Lothrians. As they reach the embassy carport, thirty Sollan legionnaires in embassy livery surround Hadrian’s group with stunners. Argyris admits his betrayal, stating that the Conclave threatened to burn the embassy if he did not surrender Hadrian. The consul claims that Hadrian is a traitor seeking to seize the Imperial throne and that his death will be blamed on terrorists. Hadrian draws Crim’s pistol and shoots Argyris in the face, killing the consul instantly before surrendering to the guards.



### 20: The Amazon
Embassy guards force Hadrian, Valka, and Corvo to their knees while a Lothrian Commissar (a high-ranking Party officer) arrives to take custody of them. The Commissar identifies Hadrian as an enemy of the People and orders his men to shackle the prisoners. Hadrian warns the legionnaires that the Conclave intends to kill them all and that he is their only way off the planet. Captain Otavia Corvo suddenly headbutts a guard and begins a violent assault on the surrounding Lothrian soldiers. Corvo displays unnatural strength by lifting a man into the air and ignoring direct hits from multiple stunner bolts.

Hadrian and Valka activate their shields as Corvo tackles the Commissar and beats him into a bloody pulp. One of the embassy guards decides to side with Hadrian and returns his highmatter sword to him. The Lothrian Conclave Guard opens fire on the Imperial soldiers, forcing the embassy legionnaires to unite with Hadrian’s Red Company. Hadrian kindles his blade and leads a charge toward the armored vans parked in the carport. Valka recovers her plasma repeater and kills several unshielded Lothrian peltasts (light infantry) who attempt to block their path.

The Imperial party reaches the front of the convoy and Corvo hijacks a blocky black police van after killing its driver. Valka takes the passenger seat to navigate toward the shuttle port using her perfect memory of the city's layout. Crim and Pallino load the remaining survivors into the rear compartment of the van as the carport becomes a battlefield. Corvo drives the vehicle through the exit ramp at high speed, swerving on its spherical wheels to enter the city streets. Smaller black vehicles and flying chariots (one-man aerial platforms) pursue the van and open fire with spotlights and weapons.
