### Chapter 11: The Grand Conclave
Hadrian Marlowe, Valka Onderra, and Tor Varro arrive at the palace ziggurat for an audience with the Grand Conclave. They enter a round amphitheater where thirty-five Chairs of the Commonwealth, gray-robed lords, sit behind an iron rail. Hadrian formally requests military ships and men to help the Sollan Empire fight the Cielcin invasion. He presents a crystal storage chit containing offers to end uranium and antimatter embargos in exchange for aid. The Ninth Chair, a young man with flinty eyes, questions the practice of equality within the Empire.

The Chairs utilize a procedure called "the Voice" to speak outside the rigid strictures of their legal codes. The Seventeenth Chair, a tall man in gray robes, demands settlement rights in the Upper Perseus Arm as a condition for assistance. Hadrian informs the Conclave that he must telegraph the Emperor before ceding such a strategic territory. The Ninth Chair asks why the Red Emperor sent a known warrior instead of a traditional diplomat to Padmurak. Hadrian responds by detailing his history of killing Cielcin princes and warns that the enemy will consume the Commonwealth next.

The First Chair concludes the audience and slams a black book called the Lothriad to signal the end of the session. This book is a physical volume embossed with a black star and represents the only permitted text in the nation. Hadrian observes that the Lothrians call slavery freedom while destroying the concept of individual personhood. The delegation is escorted back through the cyclopean concrete halls of the ziggurat to their penthouse. Hadrian reflects on the hypocrisy of a culture that glorifies the People while stripping every man of his name.





### Chapter 12: Commonwealth
Negotiations continue for weeks as data drips slowly across thousands of light-years via quantum telegraph. The Sollan Emperor refuses to cede the Upper Perseus because it would bring the Commonwealth too close to Jaddian allies. Hadrian offers to re-draw borders in the Rasan Belt, a neutral space between the two human powers. Valka stops attending the diplomatic sessions and remains in the palace apartments with the Imperial consulate staff. Chief Consul Damon Argyris hosts Hadrian in a private sauna and describes the decay of the Lothrian capital.

Argyris explains that Padmurak is a city of tunnels where foreigners are kept away from the general population. The consul points out that the "perfect order" of the Commonwealth hides crumbling roads and shuttered hives called vuli, blockhouses for the workers. Hadrian snaps at Argyris when the consul pinches the nipple of a golden-collared slave girl during their conversation. The consul claims that the Grand Conclave fears popular revolt and uses propaganda about "liberalist" revolutionaries to maintain control. He notes that while the city appears clean on the surface, it is actually suffering from a lack of breathable air.

The Eleventh Chair invites Hadrian and Valka to tour city farms and a polar ice harvesting facility. Argyris warns Hadrian that the ice mines are a ghastly business but will reveal the reality of Lothrian life. Hadrian learns that the original tunnels beneath the domes have been converted into failing infrastructure for water and ventilation. He reflects on the irony of enjoying the consul's luxury while the city outside the embassy tower falls into neglect. The consul promises to accompany Hadrian to a performance of the First Ballet before the polar expedition begins.





### Chapter 13: Still the Orchestra Plays
Hadrian and Valka attend the Lothrian First Ballet to watch a performance of Ademar Giallo’s Earth Afire. Fifty-two dancers in pale leotards take the stage to represent the fifty-two artificial intelligences of the Mericanii era. The ballet depicts the God Emperor rebuffing the advances of these machine "daughters" who once ruled Old Earth. The dancers move with mechanical precision across a floor of star-strewn glass while the Ninth and Seventeenth Chairs watch. Hadrian whispers to Valka that the performers are killed if they fail to maintain this level of excellence.

Valka suddenly experiences a mental flashback triggered by the MINOS virus and her machine eyes lose focus. She recovers quickly and prevents Hadrian from drawing attention to her distress by continuing their conversation in Galstani. The Seventeenth Chair asks Hadrian if the performance is to his liking while the Ninth Chair remains silent. Hadrian notes that the male dancers wear red leotards to signify the kings of men before their fall. The dancer playing the God Emperor wears white and stands over the female dancers like a conqueror in a harem.

The curtains fall to an awkward silence because the Lothrian audience is not permitted to clap or show emotion. The Ninth Chair stands and quotes the Lothriad regarding how individual skills must be turned to the service of the People. He claims that the Commonwealth is a nation without masters or gods where every action glorifies the collective. Hadrian bows and asserts that he also understands the nature of loyalty and service to a higher power. The group departs the theater to attend a dinner hosted by the Seventeenth Chair in his private apartments.





### Chapter 14: Ghost of the Machine
Dinner is served in a spartan marble room containing a large globe of Padmurak in magnetic suspension. The Seventeenth Chair, Lorth Talleg, removes his auto-translator and reveals he was educated at the Imperial athenaeum on Teukros. He admits that the Commonwealth’s goal is to replace "Old Bodies" with a new, sexless human nature to end disparity. Talleg explains that the Party attempted to introduce hermaphrodite homunculi to the population, but the zuk workers rejected them. Valka calls the Chairman a hypocrite because Party members keep their names while the workers are stripped of identity.

Talleg describes himself as a "handmaid" who will die building a paradise that dissolves the need for the Conclave. Hadrian argues that the Empire accepts human ugliness while the Commonwealth forces a toxic idealism onto its people. The Chairman quotes a scholiast aphorism about freedom being a sea that acts as an obstacle to virtue. Valka suddenly collapses as the worm set by the Extrasolarian magus Urbaine triggers a violent seizure in her mind. Her left pupil dilates independently and she begins to sweat while her fingers scratch at her own face.

Hadrian refuses Talleg's offer of physicians and carries Valka to an Imperial groundcar waiting outside the palace. Inside the car, Valka tells Hadrian that she sensed another neural lace nearby during the ballet performance. Hadrian realizes that the Commonwealth is an experiment in human lives rather than a functioning nation. He reflects that the Empire is like a river that accepts the limitations of nature and time. Valka recovers from the seizure as the car drives through the spiritless, monolithic streets of the Great City.





### Chapter 15: By Fire
Hadrian travels for two days by train to the south polar ice harvesting station known as Lahe Uenalochta. He observes a nameless zuk woman forced to stand naked on the ice as punishment for an unspecified crime. On the return trip, the motorcade is rerouted through Eighth Dome due to a reported traffic accident in a tunnel. The motorcars are trapped in heavy traffic on a high bridge overlooking the canals and towers of the lower district. Pallino notices military police and flares ahead, sensing that the detour was a planned trap.

Armed rebels wearing red and white armbands attack the motorcade using shotguns, pistols, and rockets. Hadrian recognizes the towers of Eighth Dome from a prophetic dream he experienced the night before the Conclave audience. A rocket strikes the groundcar and flips it, forcing Hadrian and his guards to spill onto the pavement. Hadrian kindles his highmatter sword, a blade of flowing mercury-like substance, and charges the rebels. He severs the arms of a shotgun-wielding rebel who attempts to thrust his weapon inside Hadrian's energy shield.

Pallino and the hoplites return fire with disruptors while the rebels take cover behind civilian vehicles. Hadrian suspects that the "liberalist" attackers are actually a staged "bogeyman" operation controlled by the Party. He kills a rebel with a highmatter blade and shouts "Earth and Empire" to rally his men. A third rocket strikes a nearby vehicle and the resulting explosion throws Hadrian over the bridge's rail. He falls into the dark air and loses consciousness as he plunges toward the watercourses a thousand feet below.
