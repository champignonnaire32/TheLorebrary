### 21: Heroes End
Otavia Corvo drives the getaway van through the city of First Dome on Padmurak. The van is pursued by armored vehicles and flying chariots, which are small repulsor-driven platforms for single riders. Pallino fires a plasma burner at a charioteer but finds the enemy protected by energy shields. A bullet enters the van and strikes a legionnaire named Baro in the throat. Hadrian attempts to use his connection to the Quiet to find a timeline where Baro survives, but he fails and the soldier dies.

Hadrian climbs onto the roof of the moving van to draw fire and protect the survivors. He leaps from the roof to intercept a charioteer and kills the pilot with his highmatter sword, a blade that uses a monomolecular field to cut through any substance. Hadrian takes control of the captured chariot and pursues the van toward the waterfront reservoir. He destroys several pursuing cruisers by firing the chariot's weapons at their tires. The van reaches the heavy steel gates leading out of the dome just as they are grinding shut.

Corvo successfully threads the van through a narrow gap in the closing gates, but Hadrian is too slow to follow on his chariot. Hadrian leaps from the chariot and slams into the sealed steel door while his vehicle falls into the water below. Trapped inside the dome, Hadrian dons his full armor and mirror-black mask to face the approaching Conclave Guard. He uses his highmatter sword to carve through several armored cars and kills multiple guardsmen in the ensuing melee. Eventually, a group of guards overwhelms him and strikes his helmet with heavy ceramic batons until he loses consciousness.





### 22: There in the Silence
Hadrian wakes up in a semi-conscious state while being dragged by Lothrian guardsmen. He experiences a vision of being led in chains through a sea of Cielcin, the pale-skinned alien enemies of humanity. In this vision, the Cielcin call him the "King of Rats" and lead him toward a massive black dome. He sees the "White Hand," a group of Cielcin generals including the winged Iedyr and a floating white eye called Hushansa. The vision ends when a guard strikes him, and Hadrian finds himself in a real prison cell on Padmurak.

Hadrian is stripped to his underlayment and chained by his wrists to a high point on the wall. He meets a cellmate, a withered old man with long white-streaked hair and violet eyes. The old man speaks with an Imperial accent and recites religious phrases about trusting in the Quiet. Hadrian realizes the man is a version of himself from a different point in time or a possible future. This older Hadrian has two missing fingers and warns his younger self that there is only one way through "the needle."

The older Hadrian tells him to "find us in you" before falling silent. A gaoler enters the cell and sets down a plastic dinner tray containing protein paste and mush. The gaoler uses a control wand to uncouple Hadrian's wrists from the wall chain so he can eat. When Hadrian looks back toward the corner of the cell, the older version of himself has vanished completely. Hadrian remains in the cell alone, left to contemplate the warning about his future path.





### 23: Who Holds the Strings
Hadrian spends thirty-four days in solitary confinement, marked only by the irregular delivery of foul-smelling protein bricks. The Lothrian guards utilize sleep deprivation and sensory isolation to break his mental stability. Finally, guards arrive and march him through a marble-tiled hall toward the center of the palace ziggurat. He is brought before the Grand Conclave, which consists of thirty-four high-ranking officials seated on a massive curved bench. The First Chair formally accuses Hadrian of multiple counts of murder and fomenting war against the Commonwealth.

The officials demand that Hadrian confess his guilt, using the forbidden word "you" to single him out. Hadrian refuses to confess and instead accuses Lorth Talleg and the other Chairs of orchestrating the embassy attack. Iovan of the Ninth Chair arrives and announces that the Sollan embassy has been liquidated and all its personnel are dead. Iovan further claims that the Tamerlane, Hadrian's ship, has been captured by Lothrian forces. The Ninth Chair reveals that he has arranged for Hadrian to be delivered as a gift to the Cielcin.

A ten-foot-tall Cielcin general named Vati Inamna enters the chamber accompanied by scahari warriors. Vati is an Iedyr, a cyborg warrior with opalescent armor and a segmented, six-fingered metal hand. The general strikes Hadrian across the face, mocking his connection to the god known as the Quiet. It is revealed that the Lothrian Commonwealth has been selling its own people to the Cielcin for meat for fifty years. Vati orders the Cielcin warriors to take Hadrian to a ship for transport to the black fortress of Dharan-Tun.





### 24: The Sorcerer
Cielcin warriors march Hadrian through the palace and gag him with a silken cord to stop his talking. He is loaded into an armored van and chained to an iron ring in the floor between two inhuman guards. Iovan of the Ninth Chair and the Thirteenth Chair join him in the van and reveal their true identities as MINOS sorcerers. They are the same thoughtform programs Hadrian encountered on the planet Arae, now inhabiting Lothrian bodies. The sorcerers possess gray, glowing mechanical eyes and speak in synchronized voices.

Iovan explains that they have spent three generations subverting the Lothrian Conclave to serve the Cielcin Prince. The sorcerers mock Hadrian, stating that the Chantry has kept humanity in a dark age for thousands of years. They claim the Cielcin gods, known as the Caihanarin or Watchers, are real and powerful beings. Iovan asserts that the Sollan Empire is doomed and that mankind will be "free" to evolve into machines like them. Hadrian retorts that the sorcerers are merely slaves to the Cielcin, which prompts a sharp rebuke from the Cielcin commander, Gorre.

The van arrives at a hangar containing a Cielcin shuttle with a striated, organic-looking fuselage. Hadrian is forced onto a lift platform that ascends into the stinking, dark interior of the alien craft. Iovan tells Hadrian that he will soon meet the Cielcin Prince on the world of Dharan-Tun. Before the lift rises, Hadrian headbutts Iovan, crushing the sorcerer's nose and causing him to stumble back. The shuttle's doors seal shut, leaving Hadrian trapped in the darkness as the vessel prepares for its journey through space.





### 25: Rebirth
Hadrian wakes up on a wet floor, coughing up amniotic fluid after being pulled from a fugue creche. A woman named Severine, a MINOS doctor wearing glossy coveralls, cradles his head and tells him to breathe. Severine informs Hadrian that his ship was destroyed and all his companions are dead. Hadrian realizes he is no longer on Padmurak but has been transported to the Cielcin fortress of Dharan-Tun. His hands are severely burned where his metal and ivory rings froze to his skin during the cryogenic process.

Prince Syriani Dorayaica, the leader of the Cielcin, enters the room wearing jeweled armor and black robes. The Prince is a tall, slender alien with white skin, egg-shaped eyes, and a crown of swept-back horns. Syriani mocks Hadrian for his failures and calls him "dunyasu," meaning accursed, and "attantar," meaning blessed. The Prince claims that the Quiet, Hadrian's god, will eventually betray and abandon him. Syriani reveals that his goal is to sweep away the Sollan Empire so the Cielcin might conquer the stars.

Hadrian stands up despite his weakness and challenges the Prince to kill him immediately. Syriani refuses and states that Hadrian must "die well" rather than simply being executed. The Prince orders Severine to fit Hadrian with a slave collar and prepare him for further travel. Syriani exits the chamber, leaving Hadrian alone with the witch who had survived their previous encounter on Arae. Severine gestures for Hadrian to follow her to the medical bay to treat his cryoburn injuries.
