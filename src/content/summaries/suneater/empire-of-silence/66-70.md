### Chapter 66: The Satrap and the Swordmaster
The Jaddian contingent arrives at Calagah, led by Satrap Kalima Aliarada Udiri di Sayyiph, a tall woman of Jaddian nobility, and her swordmaster, Olorin Milta, a renowned Maeskolos. Anaïs Mataro also accompanies them, reiterating her impending marriage to Hadrian, much to his discomfort. As they descend into the ruins, Hadrian notes the unusual, spindly appearance of the Jaddian mamluks, who he suspects are homunculi. He also notes Olorin's highmatter swords and shield-barrier. Inside the ruins, the Satrap finds similarities between the alien structure and Cielcin vessels.

Valka dismisses the comparison, citing the million-year age of the ruins compared to the Cielcin's shorter spacefaring history. The Satrap reveals Jadd captured a Cielcin outrider at Obatala years ago, and Hadrian pieces together that the Jaddians are en route to the front beyond Marinus. Anaïs expresses fear about the Cielcin war reaching Emesh. Hadrian is left alone in the sepulcher after the Jaddians depart, reflecting on Valka's contempt for him since his vision. Anaïs finds him, expressing her desire for their marriage and kissing him, which Hadrian recoils from. Valka then interrupts, coldly reminding them the Jaddians are waiting.



### Chapter 67: Lost Time
Hadrian reflects on the concept of time and destiny, observing Emesh's moons and meditating on recent events. He tries to draw Lady Kalima but is interrupted by Valka, who joins him by the sea. They discuss the tunnel incident, and Valka acknowledges the harsh words exchanged. Hadrian, still feeling the shame, attempts to change the subject to Anaïs, but Valka states she doesn't care for the social implications. She points out that Anaïs is "a good kid" and that Hadrian "could do a lot worse."

Hadrian reveals his true feelings, admitting he doesn't want to marry Anaïs or remain on Emesh, fearing Ligeia Vas's revenge. He confesses his past dream of becoming a pilot like Simeon the Red, traveling the stars and meeting xenobites. Valka shares her own past desire to be a pilot, revealing her father was a xenologist killed by the Inquisition on a dig. A flash of light in the sky interrupts their conversation, followed by an earth-shattering sound. Hadrian identifies it as a falling ship, realizing the Cielcin have come.



### Chapter 68: Help
As they huddle in the cliffs, shuttles from Springdeep arrive, sent by Archon Veisi. Centurion Vriell, a commander Hadrian knows, orders Elomas to return to Springdeep immediately. Hadrian deduces Knight-Tribune Raine Smythe has assumed emergency command of Emesh’s forces following the Cielcin attack. He then convinces Centurion Vriell to allow him to join the search for survivors from the crashed Cielcin ship, arguing that his ability to speak their language could prevent further bloodshed. He uses his palatine status and the threat of informing Anaïs Mataro of her stunning to sway the centurion.

Hadrian insists he can help negotiate a surrender, believing this moment defines his purpose. He reflects on having lost his signet ring, which he had thrown away in Borosevo, now realizing it is a symbol he no longer needs. Despite Vriell's protests about the danger and Elomas's reluctance, Hadrian demands to accompany the recovery team. He firmly states that his ability to communicate with the Cielcin is crucial, making it clear he will go even if it means Vriell must stun him.



### Chapter 69: Of Monsters
Hadrian accompanies the recovery team, led by Lieutenant Bassander Lin and Sir Olorin Milta, to the Cielcin crash site. The ship is a half-kilometer long, smoldering ruin. Hadrian offers to negotiate with any survivors, stating he speaks Cielcin, but Bassander dismisses the idea as madness. Olorin, intrigued by Hadrian's claim, allows him to stay, overruling Bassander's attempt to send Hadrian back. Hadrian gets into an altercation with a Jaddian mamluk, striking it after it attempts to restrain him, and asserts that wars are won with words, not just soldiers.

Lieutenant Jinan Azhar joins Olorin and Bassander, informing them that cutting teams found no Cielcin aboard the ship. Hadrian theorizes the Cielcin ejected in escape pods, which Bassander initially doubts but eventually accepts. They locate a cloaked Cielcin shuttle in a ventilation shaft leading into Calagah. The team descends into the ruins, where they are ambushed by four Cielcin. Olorin swiftly kills two Cielcin with his highmatter sword, while the others are stunned. One Cielcin is revealed to have a worm-like creature emerging from its neck wound.

Hadrian uses the Cielcin language to stop the fighting, demanding to know why they are there and how many others there are. He tortures one of the stunned Cielcin with a phase disruptor to extract information about the remaining eleven Cielcin. He experiences shame and regret over his actions, contrasting them with Valka's disapproval of violence. He ultimately lowers the weapon, unable to continue the interrogation, and instead asks for the location of the other Cielcin.



### Chapter 70: Demon-Tongued
Hadrian, Olorin, and Bassander Lin proceed into the tunnels, guided by the captured Cielcin’s vague information. Hadrian checks his shield’s charge, finding it at eighty-one percent. Olorin expresses discomfort with the narrow, limiting hallway, while Hadrian, acknowledging the presence of eleven Cielcin, leads them towards the sepulcher. Reaching the sepulcher, Hadrian attempts to communicate with the hidden Cielcin in their language, announcing they are surrounded and asking to talk. A high-pitched alien voice responds, confirming their presence and mocking the humans' small numbers.

Hadrian challenges their chances against his soldiers. A Cielcin leader, speaking with a deep voice, calls out that "The People do not surrender to animals." Hadrian emphasizes that he will not harm them if they surrender, pushing past his guards towards the darkness. A tall, thin, masked Cielcin emerges, questioning Hadrian's authority. Hadrian, refusing to give his name, identifies himself as someone fighting an inherited war and pleads for a peaceful resolution. The Cielcin commander, Itana Uvanari Ayatomn, agrees to speak with Hadrian, using a feminine construction in her speech, signifying a shift in power dynamics.

Hadrian and Bassander approach the altar where Captain Uvanari emerges, revealing her injured state. She introduces herself as the ichakta of the downed ship and acknowledges Hadrian's name. Hadrian asks for her surrender, and she questions the safety of her men. Hadrian admits he cannot guarantee their safety but promises to try. Uvanari, echoing the Cielcin sentiment "There is always a choice," states, "In the time of dying, we will die." Hadrian walks between Uvanari and her concealed people, pleading for their surrender to end the war.

Uvanari winces and collapses, revealing a broken rib. Hadrian calls for a med kit and attempts to help, but Uvanari tells him to leave her. She believes she will die there, accepting peace. She makes a gesture of fealty, crossing her fists, and announces, "We surrender, human."
