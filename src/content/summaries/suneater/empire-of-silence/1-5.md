### 1: Hadrian
Hadrian Marlowe is decanted from a vat in the presence of his parents, Alistair and Liliana, under the supervision of the High College. His father is the Archon of Meidua Prefecture, a powerful noble whose wealth comes from uranium mining on the planet Delos. Hadrian spends his childhood in the black stone palace of Devil’s Rest, raised primarily by servants and tutors rather than his distant parents. His education includes combat training with Sir Felix Martyn, decorum with Helene, and religious instruction from Abiatha. The scholar Tor Gibson serves as his primary tutor, teaching him history, biology, and the language of the alien Cielcin.

Hadrian trains in the fencing hall with his younger brother Crispin, who is physically larger and more favored by their father. They use training swords and energy shields that deflect high-velocity impacts but do not stop blades. Sir Felix supervises the session, criticizing Hadrian for his lack of commitment and Crispin for his sloppy form. During the sparring match, Hadrian uses his superior speed to outmaneuver Crispin’s aggressive but predictable attacks. He eventually defeats his brother by tripping him and landing a finishing blow to the side of his head.

Tor Gibson interrupts the training session to request a private word with Hadrian before the castellan arrives. The scholiast’s mind is trained to function as a living computer because the Chantry forbids the use of intelligent machines. Hadrian expresses a desire to become a scholiast himself to escape his father’s control and explore the galaxy. The chapter establishes the ongoing three-hundred-year war against the Cielcin, a nomadic alien race that destroys human colonies. Hadrian reflects on his future as the "Sun Eater," the man who will eventually destroy a star and billions of lives.



### 2: Like Distant Thunder
Hadrian visits Tor Gibson in his cluttered cloister cell, which is filled with books, microfilm, and crystal storage. Gibson uses the Lothrian language to avoid being understood by potential camera drones or hidden palace bugs. He reveals that a retinue from the Wong-Hopper Consortium is scheduled to arrive at the castle within a week. The news includes the total destruction of the planet Cai Shen by a massive Cielcin raid. This event makes House Marlowe the largest licensed supplier of uranium in the entire sector.

The news of the genocide on Cai Shen feels abstract to Hadrian, who has never left his homeworld of Delos. He correctly surmises that the Consortium is visiting to negotiate a new deal for uranium to replace their lost supply. Gibson admits that Lord Alistair ordered him to keep this information secret from Hadrian and the general public. Hadrian is outraged that his father is concealing vital political information from his intended heir. The scholiast notes that the Cielcin are becoming bolder, attacking systems far outside the frontier of the Veil.

Hadrian asks if the Cielcin might eventually reach Delos, but Gibson believes they are safe due to the planet's distance from the front. The conversation shifts back to the local political implications of the Consortium's visit and the uranium trade. Hadrian questions why his father refuses to involve him in the governance of the prefecture or the family business. He asks if his younger brother Crispin has been informed about the impending arrival of the Mandari guests. Gibson confirms Hadrian's fear by nodding, revealing that Crispin was told the news while Hadrian was kept in the dark.



### 3: Consortium
Hadrian is sent to meet with Lena Balem, a representative of the Mining Guild, while his father prepares for the Consortium's arrival. Balem reports that the uranium refinery at Redtine Point is failing and that worker mortality is rising due to ancient, broken equipment. She requests funds to replace radiation suits and drill rigs, but Hadrian explains he has no power to authorize such expenditures. During the meeting, a peltast guard threatens Balem for failing to address Hadrian with the proper noble titles. Hadrian leaves the meeting after offering hollow condolences for seventeen miners killed in a recent cave-in.

Lieutenant Kyra flies Hadrian back to the castle in a shuttle, passing over the city of Meidua and its Chantry dome. Upon landing, Hadrian discovers that the Wong-Hopper delegation has already arrived and is currently meeting with his father in the throne room. He bypasses the guards at the iron doors, including Sir Roban Milosh, and enters the hall without permission. The throne room is designed with architectural tricks to make the Archon appear larger and more imposing to visitors. Hadrian sees the Consortium retinue, led by Director Adaeze Feng, standing before his father’s ebony and wrought-iron throne.

Hadrian kneels before the guests and apologizes for his lateness, citing his meeting with the Mining Guild as the cause. Director Feng notes Hadrian's arrival while his father, Alistair, remains cold and silent in the shadows of the high seat. Crispin is already seated in a place of honor near their father, pointedly refusing to move when Hadrian approaches. Hadrian carries a small bloodwood chair onto the dais and sits beside his brother, ignoring the silent outrage from his father. The meeting concludes with the Consortium members shuffling on slippered feet while the Archon watches them with unfeeling violet eyes.



### 4: The Devil and the Lady
Lady Liliana visits Hadrian in his bedchamber to prepare him for the formal banquet welcoming Director Feng and her party. She wears a white silk gown with a gold eagle brooch representing House Kephalos, her mother's planetary house. Liliana criticizes Hadrian's long hair and informs him that he must lead his people rather than stand beside them. She warns Hadrian that his position as heir is not guaranteed and that Alistair may choose Crispin instead. Hadrian realizes his parents are essentially strangers to one another, living separate lives bound only by political necessity.

At the banquet, the guests discuss the Cielcin war and the involvement of the Prince of Jadd's grandson, the Darkmoon. Director Feng emphasizes that the numerous human and alien ships committed to the war effort will require massive amounts of uranium fuel. Crispin interrupts the meal to ask if the Cielcin truly eat people, prompting a tense moment among the diners. A dark-skinned Consortium executive named Terence confirms that the aliens use humans for food and take others as slaves. Hadrian contributes his knowledge of Cielcin biology and nomadic migratory clusters, known as scianda, to the conversation.

Hadrian explains that the Cielcin have a strategic advantage because they are not planetbound and can survive as long as one ship escapes. This statement causes both of his parents to stiffen, and his father accuses him of flirting with treason. Alistair demands that Hadrian apologize to Director Feng for his comments regarding human vulnerability and alien strength. The director waves off the offense and suggests that Hadrian might consider a career with the Chantry or the Consortium. Hadrian remains silent, feeling his father's wrath and his brother's mockery as the meal continues in awkward silence.



### 5: Tigers and Lambs
Hadrian arrives late to a council meeting held in the Rotunda beneath the Dome of Bright Carvings. The room is decorated with thirty-one funeral masks of his ancestors, all featuring the same aquiline features and violet eyes. Inside, Alistair discusses suppressing rebellions among asteroid belt workers and managing the uranium quotas for the vicereine. The Chantry prior, Eusebia, urges the Archon to arm the system with atomics to defend against the encroaching Cielcin threat. Alistair dismisses the request, noting that the system is already sufficiently armed and that the vicereine manages her own fleet.

The council members discuss the failing state of the planeted mines and the need to purchase new enrichment crawlers from the Consortium. Alistair refuses to authorize the purchase of nine replacement crawlers due to their high cost and the risk of Chantry interference. He explains that the Chantry monitors high technology to prevent the emergence of "daimons," the intelligent machines of the ancient Mericanii. After the meeting, Alistair orders Hadrian to stay behind, eventually isolating him with Crispin in the silent council chamber. The Archon reveals he knows Hadrian and Gibson have been speaking the forbidden Lothrian language in private.

Alistair threatens to retire Tor Gibson to a remote cloister, accusing the old scholiast of forgetting his place and meddling in politics. Hadrian defends his teacher, but his father remains unmoved and cold, staring at an oil painting of a sepulcher-walled island. When Hadrian asserts that he needs to be involved in governance to rule after his father, Alistair finally breaks his silence. He informs Hadrian that he has never officially named a successor and may never do so for many years. The Archon concludes the encounter by stating that if Hadrian continues his current behavior, he will never inherit the throne.
