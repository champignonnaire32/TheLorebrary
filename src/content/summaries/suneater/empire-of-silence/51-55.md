### 51: Familiar
Dorian asks Hadrian to clarify a Jaddian word while they sit in the starport overlooking Borosevo. Hadrian explains a draw-cut technique he used against a gladiator named Amarei of Mira, causing Dorian to admire his skill. He draws a portrait of Dorian as a myrmidon to show he has been practicing his drawing, which he claims is a vocation he learned from his scholiast. Anaïs eventually joins them, and they continue their conversation in Jaddian, though she is annoyed by the topic of the Colosso.

Hadrian feels uncomfortable when Anaïs suggests he could go back to the Colosso as a gladiator to be perfectly safe. He remains silent about his true feelings, though he privately worries about the consequences of the games as voiced by Valka. Anaïs speaks disparagingly of the Demarchy and expresses her dislike for Valka, who she calls a witch. Dorian disagrees, defending Valka’s appearance, which causes Hadrian to twitch in surprise as he hides his secret meetings with her.

Dorian asks for the charcoal portrait, and Hadrian reluctantly tears the page from his journal to give it to him. Dorian continues to ask about fighting in the Colosso, and Hadrian tells him his father allowed his own brother to fight. Anaïs suggests that Hadrian teach her and Dorian how to sword fight because their current teacher is inadequate. Hadrian eventually agrees to the instruction, knowing he has been outmaneuvered by the lordlings.





### 52: Little Talks
Hadrian instructs Dorian and Anaïs in sword fighting in the palace courtyard, consistently disarming Dorian. He uses the outdoor setting to get them used to harsh conditions, reflecting on his own training back home. The children show significant incompetence at fencing, making Hadrian wonder about the quality of their current teacher, Sir Preston Rau. Their session is interrupted by Gilliam Vas, who arrives to escort the lordlings to their father.

After the children leave, Gilliam seizes Hadrian by the biceps and aggressively questions him about his familiarity with the count’s children. He claims that other retainers find Hadrian's association with the palatine caste improper. Hadrian responds with sarcasm, pointing out that the count himself ordered him to instruct them. Gilliam remains angry and defensive, displaying his typical hostility toward Hadrian.

Gilliam accuses Hadrian of espionage and breaking into the gaol to see the creature known as Makisomn, calling it the sin of consortation. Hadrian denies that his intentions were harmful, claiming simple curiosity about the species that challenged mankind. Gilliam threatens him, warning him of the severe punishment for consortation if he does not stay away from the young lords. Hadrian remains defiant, refusing to be intimidated by the priest’s threats.





### 53: A Game of Snake and Mongoose
A formal state banquet is held at the court, featuring opulent food and many important figures of the Emesh System. Hadrian sits near a legion lieutenant named Bassander Lin, while Valka and her patron, Sir Elomas Redgrave, are nearby. The conversation eventually turns toward the Umandh natives, and Hadrian mentions his ability to speak the Cielcin tongue. This admission attracts the attention of Grand Prior Ligeia Vas, who sits near the head of the table.

Ligeia Vas confronts Hadrian, calling him a demon-tongued boy and questioning how he learned the language. She quotes a religious text to argue that the Cielcin must be wiped out, but Hadrian retorts that her source actually refers to witches. He tells a story about a Eudoran who pitted a mongoose against a snake, using it as an analogy for how men are sometimes snakes that rig fights. This cryptic insult to the priestess is well-received by the count, who laughs and praises Hadrian’s talent.

Ligeia Vas is displeased but maintains her composure, calling Hadrian a proper talent in a mocking tone. Hadrian dismisses her concern, maintaining his respectful but firm demeanor as the banquet continues. He feels a sense of accomplishment for standing his ground against the powerful priestess. Valka smiles at him, which Hadrian finds worth the risk of his confrontation with Ligeia.





### 54: Gaslight
Hadrian walks back to his room through the castle colonnades after an evening spent with Valka. He reflects on his increasing comfort in his prison and the growing bond between him and the Tavrosi doctor. He thinks about his plans with Pallino to buy a starship, noting that it will take several months before they can leave Emesh. He harbors a private, hopeful dream that Valka might choose to accompany him when he finally escapes.

Suddenly, Gilliam Vas accosts him, pinning him against a lamppost in the courtyard. Gilliam berates him for his baseborn antics and warns him against showing such familiarity toward the count’s children again. Hadrian refuses to show fear, provoking Gilliam further with his calm responses. The priest loses his temper and strikes Hadrian in the face with his ringed hand, drawing blood.

Hadrian remains resilient despite the physical assault, refusing to back down or show submission to the priest. Gilliam accuses him of plotting mischief with Valka and demands to know why he is really there. Hadrian denies his accusations and manages to avoid further strikes before Gilliam eventually leaves him alone. Hadrian is left with his injuries, contemplating the intense, inexplicable hatred Gilliam holds toward him.





### 55: The Quiet
Hadrian arrives at Valka’s room to meet her but is surprised when a naked man answers the door. Valka appears, seemingly having forgotten their appointment, and quickly sends her lover away. Hadrian feels a mix of jealousy and confusion as she invites him inside to share the bottle of wine he brought. Valka clears away her clutter, and they settle in to talk about her research.

Valka shares holographs of an ancient, mysterious site called Calagah located on Emesh. She explains that the ruins are composed of black, obsidian-like stone and are incredibly old, dating back perhaps a million years. Hadrian is stunned by this revelation, realizing that these structures could not have been built by the technologically primitive Umandh natives. This fact directly contradicts the Chantry’s teachings about the history of the galaxy.

Hadrian realizes that these ruins, along with other sites on various planets, are all part of one immense, ancient culture built by an unknown species she calls "The Quiet." He understands that this knowledge is heresy and that they would likely be killed if the Chantry found out. Valka remains unconcerned, revealing that she has already told Sir Elomas about her findings without consequence. The two share the wine, connected by this dangerous, world-shattering secret.
