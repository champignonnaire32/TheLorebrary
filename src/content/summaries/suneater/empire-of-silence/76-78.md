### 76: Deathbed Conversions
Hadrian enters the interrogation cell where Uvanari is held, acting as a translator for Inquisitor Agari. Agari demands the location of the Cielcin fleet while the cathars prepare instruments of torture. The cathars use a heated mace to spray molten lead onto Uvanari’s skin, causing him to scream in agony. Uvanari hints that his kind has encountered humans before, though Agari dismisses this as nonsense. Valka executes her plan to disable the power in the west wing, plunging the interrogation cell into darkness.

The electromagnetic clamps holding Uvanari fail during the blackout, allowing the Cielcin captain to break free. Uvanari immediately attacks and kills Cathar Rhom by tearing out his throat with his teeth. Hadrian draws his ceramic knife to defend himself and engages the injured Cielcin in a desperate struggle. During the fight, Uvanari retrieves the torture mace and uses its remaining heat to burn Hadrian’s shoulder. Hadrian manages to stab Uvanari multiple times in the abdomen, pinning him against the wall as he demands information.

Uvanari reveals the name of a world called Vorgossos where Cielcin have met Extrasolarian humans before. Hadrian delivers a final, fatal blow to Uvanari just as the power returns and Legion soldiers storm the room. The guards find Hadrian slumped on the floor next to the dead Cielcin captain and the mangled corpse of the cathar.



### 77: A Rare Thing
Hadrian stands before Count Balian, Lady Kalima, and Knight-Tribune Raine Smythe to explain the interrogation’s outcome. Inquisitor Agari argues for the immediate torture of the remaining Cielcin leader, Tanaran, to verify Hadrian's claims. Smythe opposes the Chantry’s methods, fearing that further deaths will ruin any chance of negotiation or intelligence gathering. Hadrian informs the council about Uvanari’s mention of Vorgossos and the link between Cielcin and Extrasolarians. Most officials dismiss Vorgossos as a pirate myth, but Hadrian insists it is the key to finding the alien fleet.

To avoid a forced marriage to the Count’s daughter, Hadrian proposes leading a covert mission to find Vorgossos. He suggests using a derelict ship and a crew of mercenaries to maintain plausible deniability for the Empire. Raine Smythe unexpectedly supports the plan and uses her authority to conscript Hadrian into the Imperial Legion. She officially requests the transfer of the Cielcin prisoners to her carrier, the ISV Obdurate. Smythe appoints Bassander Lin as the mission's formal captain, much to Hadrian’s personal disappointment.

After the meeting, Smythe reveals she knows Hadrian orchestrated the power cut but protects him for the mission's sake. Hadrian asks to include the Tavrosi xenologist Valka Onderra in his expeditionary group. He also requests the release and conscription of his fellow myrmidons from the Colosso.



### 78: Quality
Hadrian arrives at a private airfield on his final day on Emesh, wearing a heavy charcoal greatcoat. Valka Onderra meets him at the shuttle, confirming she will join the mission to study the Cielcin. Sir Olorin Milta, the Jaddian swordmaster, approaches Hadrian to offer a formal farewell before departure. Olorin presents Hadrian with a priceless Jaddian highmatter sword featuring a crystal blade and silver fittings. The swordmaster explains that the weapon is a recognition of Hadrian’s personal quality and character.

Hadrian activates the blade, which hums with power and feels weightless in his hand. Olorin promises that Hadrian’s reputation will precede him in the Principalities of Jadd. After Olorin departs, Hadrian boards the shuttle and finds his friends from the Colosso waiting for him. Ghen, Siran, Pallino, and Switch celebrate their freedom and their new roles as part of Hadrian's crew. Hadrian reunites with the myrmidons, insisting they call him by his nickname, Had, rather than his title.

Bassander Lin appears at the cockpit door, wearing his new captain’s uniform and maintaining a stiff, formal demeanor. The shuttle seals its hatch and lifts off, carrying the group toward the carrier Obdurate in orbit. Hadrian reflects on the friends he is leaving behind, including the dead and those he will never see again.
