### 46: The Doctor
Hadrian lives in the Mataro palace and tutors the noble children Anaïs and Dorian in Jaddian. He watches Umandh slaves, which are three-legged coralline-hided xenobites, perform manual labor under human overseers. Anaïs invites Hadrian to a harbor boat race and mentions the upcoming sacrifice of a Cielcin prisoner. While walking through the palace, Hadrian encounters Valka Onderra, a Tavrosi xenologist with golden eyes and a tattooed left arm. Valka is supervising the repair of light fixtures by Umandh workers using a handheld communication terminal.

Valka criticizes the human douleters for striking the xenobites with shock-sticks. Hadrian attempts to impress her by speaking in the Tavrosi languages of Nordei and Panthai. Valka explains that the Umandh function as a networked combine-organism that harmonizes through droning sounds. She mentions her primary work involves ancient non-human ruins at Calagah on the southern continent of Anshar. When she learns of Hadrian's past as a myrmidon in the Colosso, she mocks his history of killing for sport.

The doctor refuses to use noble honorifics when addressing Anaïs or Hadrian. She describes the Umandh droning as a method of networking rather than a traditional language. Hadrian identifies himself as an amateur enthusiast of archaeology by referencing the extinct Arch-Builders of Ozymandias. Valka offers to show him holographic images of the Calagah site at her quarters. She exits the colonnade after delivering a final verbal barb about his violent profession.

### 47: The Cage
Hadrian spends weeks confined to the Mataro castle under constant surveillance. He attends operas and accompanies the count's children to their daily lessons and business meetings. In his private room, he records sketches in his journal and reflects on his lack of freedom. He realizes the public believes he charmed his way into service, but he feels like a prisoner. He regrets the failed escape plan that alienated his friend Switch and left him without a starship.

Security forces monitor all holographic communication plates in his quarters. Hadrian is unable to contact his former companions Pallino, Elara, Ghen, or Siran. He recognizes that his comfort and fine meals have been traded for total loss of privacy. This forced isolation reminds him of his previous life on the planet Delos. He blames his own greed and stupidity for his current entrapment on the planet Emesh. 

The protagonist compares his situation to the myth of Daedalus trapped in the labyrinth. He acknowledges that any attempt to flee would be flagged by palace security immediately. The dream of buying a ship and leaving the Empire feels increasingly distant. He accepts that he is stuck in a "crystal cage" of his own making. Hadrian concludes that his quest for knowledge has come at a staggering personal cost.

### 48: Triumph
Hadrian attends the Ephebeia triumph for Dorian Mataro at the Borosevo coliseum. He watches the parade from the count's box while drinking Kandarene red wine. Dorian stands on a bier in gold and green armor holding a glowing highmatter sword. Chantry officials, including Grand Prior Ligeia Vas and Chanter Gilliam, lead the procession. The Borosevo Sphinxes and Imperial legionnaires in ivory armor march through the arena.

Valka joins Hadrian in the box and observes that the celebration is excessive for a birthday. Hadrian meets Valka’s sponsor, a knight named Sir Elomas Redgrave who funds the Calagah dig. Gilliam Vas interrupts their conversation to insult Hadrian for his lowborn status and past violence. The priest reveals that Hadrian previously broke into a prisoner's cell in the coliseum tunnels. Gilliam also mocks Valka by calling her a heretic whore before Sir Elomas intervenes.

The Cielcin prisoner Makisomn is brought into the arena wearing a white cleric's shift. Two cathar executioners force the drugged alien to its knees before the crowd. Grand Prior Ligeia delivers a religious invocation regarding the departure of Earth into the Dark. A cathar decapitates Makisomn with a five-foot ceramic blade called the White Sword. The executioner presents the severed head to Dorian, who displays it to the cheering audience.

### 49: Brothers in Arms
A messenger summons Hadrian to the palace barbican to meet an unexpected visitor. Pallino, the veteran myrmidon, waits in the tiled hall under the supervision of house guards. The older man mocks Hadrian's fine silk clothes but displays visible tension during the meeting. Pallino accuses Hadrian of abandoning his friends to live among the wealthy nobility. Hadrian explains that he is under constant guard and cannot leave the castle unescorted.

The two men move to the shadow of a red column to avoid being overheard by peltasts. They discuss the recent death of the gladiator Erdro during an antique weapons demonstration. Pallino reveals that Switch told the group about Hadrian’s plan to buy a starship. Hadrian confirms that his father owns land that could have funded the purchase of a vessel. He maintains his false identity as Hadrian Gibson while hinting at his true noble background.

Hadrian asks Pallino to tell Switch that he is sorry for the secrecy and the argument. Pallino explains that military service taught him to trust the men in his unit regardless of their origins. Hadrian proposes that he could still buy a ship and hire his friends as a crew. This plan would provide the gladiators with steady, safe work away from the killing pits. They part ways as Pallino returns to the coliseum to train new fodder recruits.

### 50: Without Pretense
Hadrian and Valka accompany the Mataro children to the Umandh alienage on the island of Ulakiel. The site is enclosed by a heated electrified fence that prevents the xenobites from swimming away. Vilicus Niles Engin, a man with ashen gray skin, greets the party on the beach. Valka expresses disgust at the poor living conditions in the Umandh shantytown. The group observes xenobites dragging fishing nets through the surf to feed the colony.

Valka shows Hadrian anaglyphs, which are woven hoops used by the Umandh for tactile record-keeping. Hadrian identifies the geometric patterns within the weaving, surprising the doctor with his intuition. Valka explains that her handheld terminal can spoof Umandh signals to make them obey human commands. She notes that the Umandh harmonize at different frequencies to communicate basic needs like hunger. Hadrian references the scholar Philemon of Neruda while discussing the link between language and spaceflight.

Inside a hovel made of trash, Valka reveals that Engin beats and chisels the Umandh. Hadrian admits his hatred for the Chantry's violence while they are away from surveillance cameras. Valka touches his arm and expresses a brief moment of shared understanding and sympathy. Anaïs interrupts the private conversation and displays jealousy toward the doctor's proximity to Hadrian. The vilicus then leads the group back to the beach to watch the xenobites perform a dance.
