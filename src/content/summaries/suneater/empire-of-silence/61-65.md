### Chapter 61: A Kind of Exile
Lord Balian Mataro confronts Hadrian in his sterile, modern office regarding the duel that resulted in Gilliam Vas's death. The Count reveals that the Chantry, led by Gilliam's mother Ligeia, now possesses significant leverage over his planetary rule. Mataro admits he cannot protect Hadrian from the Inquisition unless he becomes a permanent part of his house. He proposes a marriage between Hadrian and his daughter Anaïs to secure Hadrian’s superior gene complexes for his lineage. Hadrian learns he has been legally disavowed by his father and grandmother, rendering his signet ring and noble holdings worthless.

The Count confesses he contacted Hadrian’s father immediately upon Hadrian’s arrival to arrange this disinheritance and future marriage. Hadrian realizes he is being used as a genetic stud to elevate the Mataro family's standing to the peerage. He is forced to accept the arrangement because he has no other resources or protection from the Chantry. Mataro decides to send Hadrian away from the capital to Binah for three years until the wedding. Hadrian requests instead to accompany the xenologist Valka to the remote research site at Calagah.



### Chapter 62: The Gilded Cage
Hadrian drinks heavily in his apartments while reflecting on his transition from a noble to a genetic prisoner. Valka enters his room and finds him in a drunken state, initially mocking his self-pity before offering him water. She acknowledges the difficulty of taking a life, sensing Hadrian's genuine guilt over Gilliam's death. Hadrian explains the Count's plan for his marriage and his decision to leave the capital for Calagah. He admits that he no longer feels the Empire is a suitable place for him.

During the conversation, Hadrian removes his now-useless signet ring and throws it across the room in a fit of rage. Valka strikes him across the face to snap him out of his spiraling despair and blames him for his own predicament. She reminds him that his current suffering is the direct result of his own choices rather than external fate. Hadrian asks again to join her expedition to escape the immediate influence of House Mataro and the Chantry. Valka eventually softens her demeanor and agrees to take him with her to the alien ruins.



### Chapter 63: Calagah
The narrative shifts to the southern continent of Anshar where Hadrian has spent four months at the Calagah research site. He describes the landscape of black igneous rock and the cold, fierce winds that contrast with the heat of the capital. The research team includes Valka, Sir Elomas Redgrave, and the scholiast Tor Ada, who study the ancient alien structures. Hadrian observes the black-glass facade of the ruins, noting that the geometry is subtly non-parallel and diverges by precise fractions of a degree. Valka explains that the material of the ruins defies molecular analysis and cannot be sampled or scanned effectively.

Upon entering the main complex, Hadrian discovers a low, dim interior lit by phosphorescent tape and Tavrosi glowspheres. The walls are covered in circular anaglyphs and knot patterns that have remained unchanged for nearly a million years. While exploring, Hadrian touches a black stone step and experiences a sudden, agonizing sensation of supernatural cold. Valka touches the same spot but reports that the stone feels warm to her, dismissing his reaction. They reach a domed hub where the alien symbols humble Hadrian, making him feel insignificant in the vast cosmos.



### Chapter 64: The Larger World
The research team lives in a prefabricated hutment provided by Sir Elomas, which features luxury appointments like Tavrosi carpets and professional staff. Dinner conversations typically involve discussions about archaeology, linguistics, and Elomas’s past as a galactic duelist. During one meal, Elomas recounts his experiences in the Jaddian Principalities and his encounters with Mandari bioengineers. The group discusses the Chantry’s strict regulation of data concerning extinct alien races and the lack of artifacts at Quiet sites. Tor Ada suggests that the Chantry allows the expedition only because they believe it will ultimately prove fruitless.

The peaceful atmosphere is shattered when Elomas’s squire, Karthik, reports a news broadcast regarding a Cielcin attack. The broadcast claims a glorious victory for the Imperial Legions at the edge of the solar system, though the team remains skeptical. Hadrian recognizes the propaganda of the Ministry of Public Enlightenment and suspects the report is heavily sanitized. Despite the proximity of the war, the remote site at Calagah remains physically unscarred and quiet. The chapter ends with Elomas silencing the broadcast as the reality of the Cielcin threat looms over the team.



### Chapter 65: I Dare Not Meet in Dreams
Hadrian continues to assist with the excavation at Calagah, moving equipment and shadowing the senior researchers through the darkened halls. While exploring a tubular passage alone, he discovers a hidden crack in the wall that leads to a secret chamber. Inside the trapezoidal room, he finds a massive mural of a single circle broken by a ray, which he has never seen before. When he touches the carving, he experiences a violent vision involving green eyes and the destruction of worlds. He sees a great ship studded with statues and a silver-crowned Cielcin leader leading a host across the stars.

The vision concludes with the words "This must be," after which Hadrian finds himself back on the floor of the dry chamber. He rushes to find Valka and Elomas, only to discover that six hours have passed while he felt he was only gone for twenty minutes. Valka informs him that no such room exists in the tunnel and accuses him of lying to impress her. She dismisses his claims of visions as superstitious nonsense and calls him an ignorant savage. Despite her anger, Hadrian remains certain of what he saw and felt within the black stone of the ruin.
