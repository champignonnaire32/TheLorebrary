### 56: Meeting of the Minds
Valka returns from the Library stacks and tells Hadrian she found original journals from the Rubicon digs. Hadrian informs her that his old tutor Gibson is alive and currently residing in the Library. They travel down into the Archivists’ Grotto to find Gibson’s private apartment. The small chamber is filled with glowing algae and piles of books. Hadrian enters first and calls out to his former teacher to announce his presence.

Gibson appears with a cane and meets Valka for the first time. They sit on a worn couch while Valka helps Gibson prepare tea for the group. Gibson admits he believes Hadrian’s story about being killed and resurrected but does not understand the mechanism. They discuss the Quiet and why the entities might be using Gibson’s face in Hadrian’s visions. Valka mentions she witnessed Hadrian’s death on the battlefield during their time together.

Gibson reveals that Hadrian’s parents birthed a replacement sister named Sabine Doryssa Marlowe after he fled. Hadrian learns that his brother Crispin has children and their mother has moved to Haspida. Gibson notes that Sabine is expected to inherit their father’s seat on Delos. Hadrian laughs at the news of his replacement by a sibling he never knew existed. Valka stays behind for a moment to thank Gibson for raising Hadrian.





### 57: Gabriel’s Archive
A month-long delay occurs while the Library verifies Hadrian’s security codes with Forum. Primate Arrian warns the group that any misuse of Mericanii technology is a capital offense punishable by the Inquisition. The group descends a spiral staircase toward the lowest levels of the silo. Curator Imlarros explains that the archives are located below sea level beneath a massive reservoir. This reservoir is designed to flood the chambers or insulate them during an atomic detonation if the contents escape.

Imlarros, Gibson, and Varro use three iron keys and a series of levers to open the fifty-foot hangar doors. A foul, stagnant air rushes out as the mechanical gears turn for the first time in millennia. Inside, the group finds a vestibule containing the original United States Declaration of Independence preserved under glass. Yellow lights flicker on to reveal rows of filing cabinets and microfilm terminals. Hadrian realizes the archive is a repository for artifacts belonging to the machine-led Mericanii era.

The group views a hall of portraits depicting ancient leaders such as Washington and Felsenburgh. Gibson explains that Julian Felsenburgh was the last human lord who surrendered Earth’s governance to machines. Valka begins an intensive study of the archive and learns Classical English within a single week. Two years pass as Valka and Hadrian sift through the crumbling documents and microfilm. The soldiers of the Tamerlane are permitted to establish a shore-leave camp on the island of Thessa.





### 58: Island in Time
Gabriel's Archive functions as an uncurated museum containing records of the Foundation War. Technicians’ reports describe human bodies wired together in massive matrices inside machine pyramids. Valka discovers that the Mericanii established fifty-two offworld colonies led by AI entities called the "daughters of Columbia." Documentation details how House Windsor salvaged historical artifacts from Earth’s major cities like Rome and London. The records mention the Museum Catholic group moving their holy city brick by brick to the planet Caritas.

Three years pass while Valka methodically searches the archive for information on the Quiet. Hadrian spends his evenings walking with Gibson in the athenaeum’s gardens and courtyards. He expresses regret for banishing his friend Switch after a past betrayal. Gibson claims that Hadrian is more like his father Alistair than he realizes because they both fear their own nature. He argues that Valka's presence keeps Hadrian human and prevents him from becoming like his father.

Gibson warns Hadrian not to lose Valka or his own humanity during their quest. Hadrian asks about Gibson’s life before he joined the Order but receives a vague answer. The old scholiast refuses to join the group on their upcoming trip to the island of Thessa. He explains that his vows and age prevent him from leaving the Library’s walls. Hadrian accepts this decision and prepares to depart with his crew for their rotation of shore leave.





### 59: Island in the Sun
Hadrian and his officers arrive at the crescent-shaped island of Thessa for a year of vacation. The crew sets up prefabricated buildings on a stone shelf above a black sand beach. Prince Alexander complains about the lack of civilization while Pallino encourages him to interact with the locals. Hadrian publicly removes his tunic and his left-hand glove to signal his equality with his soldiers. A cheer goes up from the three hundred men and women gathered on the shore.

Crim engages in a bare-handed practice fight against the Irchtani warrior Udax. Hadrian intervenes to prevent blood from being spilled on the first day of their leave. He follows Valka up a cliffside path where she stands under a red Nipponese parasol. They embrace and discuss the mystery of the Quiet while watching the moons of Colchis. Valka admits she feels more like a witch than a scientist after their recent experiences.

Captain Otavia Corvo interrupts the couple to invite them to a feast on the beach. The group eats fresh trout and bass provided by local fishing charters. Siran spends her days at sea with the fishermen and the local eolderman named Lem. The soldiers spend their nights drinking, singing, and forming relationships with the islanders. Hadrian reflects on these peaceful moments years later while standing alone at a stone cairn he built on the cliff.





### 60: The Library Again
The group returns to the Library where Valka grows frustrated by the mundane nature of the archival records. She finds miles of shipping manifests and tax receipts instead of information regarding the Quiet. The records reveal that the AI Columbia ruled humanity for centuries after Julian Felsenburgh's death. Humans lived in virtual dreams while machines managed all production and reproduction through artificial tanks. Eventually, the machines turned and herded the population into white pyramids on Earth and its colonies.

Years pass as the majority of the Tamerlane's crew returns to fugue sleep. Gibson begins mentoring Prince Alexander in the Archivists’ Grotto near the black pool. He teaches the boy stoic self-regulation and the importance of being comfortable with one's own thoughts. Gibson suggests that the Emperor may have intended Alexander for the throne rather than his older brothers. He advises Hadrian to treat the boy as a future ruler and provide him with proper guidance.

Hadrian and Gibson discuss the nature of teaching and the wisdom found in silence. Alexander observes that no one is ever truly alone because they are always with their own mind. Hadrian admits that reflection is necessary because he and the prince are not inherently good men. Gibson reminds Hadrian that a teacher's goal is to make the world slightly wiser through their students. Valka suddenly bursts into the grotto to announce that she has finally understood a key piece of information.
