### Chapter 71: Whispers
Hadrian signals the troopers from the vacuum of the mountaintop on Annica without his helmet. Valka, Crim, and Pallino arrive on repulsor flight platforms to rescue him. After sealing his suit and collapsing, Hadrian wakes up forty days later in his quarters aboard the *Tamerlane*. His hands, which suffered burst capillaries in the vacuum, are nearly healed due to medical treatment. Valka informs him that while he was gone for forty days, his suit chronometer only recorded three days of elapsed time.

Hadrian describes his encounter with the Quiet and his visions of the future destruction of the sun. He explains that the anaglyphs in the ruins are not a language but components of a higher-dimensional machine. Valka reveals that Hadrian's suit feed went dead the moment he stepped onto the bridge on the mountain. On the bridge of the *Tamerlane*, Hadrian orders an immediate course for Berenike to stop the Cielcin prince Dorayaica. Captain Corvo and Bastien Durand question Hadrian’s claims and his sanity regarding the alien "gods."

To prove his transformation, Hadrian demands Bastien Durand’s railgun and holds it to his own chest. He forces Durand to pull the trigger, and the weapon fires a tungsten rod directly into Hadrian's sternum. Hadrian remains completely unharmed by shifting his consciousness into a parallel narrative where the bullet missed. The crew is shocked as the shattered projectile lies behind Hadrian while his chest remains unmarked. Hadrian commands the fleet to sail for Berenike to intercept the Legions before they fall into a Cielcin trap.



### Chapter 72: Between the Hammer and the Anvil
The *Tamerlane* arrives at Berenike, a mining planet characterized by the massive Storm Wall protecting Deira City. Hadrian observes the Imperial fleet performing maneuvers in the sky above the gray glass towers of the canyon. He travels to the Legion headquarters located in the barbican of the Storm Wall for a strategic briefing. First Strategos Titus Hauptmann presides over the meeting alongside other high-ranking officers and holographs. Hadrian warns the assembly that the Cielcin attack on Marinus was a feint designed to lure the Legions to Berenike.

Bassander Lin, showing signs of aging and fear, supports Hadrian’s assessment of Cielcin strategy. Lin has been altered by witnessing Hadrian’s "death" and no longer displays his former antagonism. Hadrian argues that the Cielcin prince Dorayaica has been studying Imperial logistics to plan a decisive ambush. Scholiasts and captains debate the typical duration of Cielcin invasions compared to the three-day sack of Marinus. Captain Corvo suggests a second Cielcin fleet is likely waiting beyond the Berenike heliopause to close a trap.

Hauptmann orders System Commandant Bancroft to provide reports on any recent ship disappearances in the system. Bancroft confirms that two ships vanished near each other within two months, suggesting an enemy presence in the Oort Cloud. After the meeting, Bassander Lin approaches Hadrian on a tram platform to discuss the upcoming battle. Lin reveals that Hauptmann plans to divide the fleet, leaving a token force in orbit while hiding the bulk out-system. Hadrian and Lin shake hands, acknowledging their shared duty as the Imperial fleet prepares for the Cielcin arrival.



### Chapter 73: Berenike
First Strategos Hauptmann orders the superdreadnought *Sieglinde* and the bulk of the fleet to hide 0.3 light-years out-system. He stays behind on Ondu Station in synchronous orbit above Deira City to coordinate the planetary defense. The Imperial Legions begin a massive evacuation of Deira City, moving millions of citizens into deep underground bunkers. Hadrian is appointed lieutenant to Legate Leonid Bartosz, commanding ground forces for the 437th Centaurine Legion. The city is emptied of all but utility workers and soldiers who set up barricades and armored groundcar patrols.

Hadrian inspects the defenses, which include Javelin-9 missile batteries and plasma howitzers along the inner walls. On the landing field outside the city, massive mobile artillery units called colossi wait in defensive positions. Hadrian holds a strategy session with Valka, Lorian, Durand, and the Irchtani warriors Udax and Kithuun-Barda. Lorian proposes the "close the lid" tactic, which involves drawing Cielcin troops into the empty city before attacking. The Irchtani are assigned to defend the anti-air artillery on the inner wall overlooking the city valley.

Hadrian decants all personnel from the *Tamerlane*'s cold sleep to maximize his available ground forces. Prince Alexander joins the defense, wearing a standard Legion uniform without his royal insignias. The Red Company cohorts are deployed to the industrial quarters to protect the approaches to the Storm Wall. Pallino suggests mining the lower gates of the city to prevent Cielcin forces from following a retreat into the bunkers. As the evacuation completes, the silence over Deira City deepens while the Imperial forces await the Cielcin warp signature.



### Chapter 74: Phylacteries
Hadrian stands atop the Storm Wall watching lightning strikes near distant nuclear power plants. Valka joins him, wearing a red leather jacket and a shirt featuring Tavrosi script. She expresses her deep frustration that her decades of research into the "Quiet" were based on the false assumption it was a language. Valka reveals her feelings of failure as a scientist after Hadrian discovered the true nature of the anaglyphs on Annica. Hadrian attempts to comfort her, emphasizing that they are both part of a much larger cosmic narrative.

Valka admits her fear of being overshadowed by Hadrian's growing status as a "Halfmortal" and a prophet. She presents Hadrian with a platinum phylactery, a convex metal disc containing her genetic material and a digital consciousness copy. This exchange is a Tavrosi tradition used when individuals have earned enough social credit to have a child. Hadrian accepts the gift, noting it is a symbolic compromise since legal marriage is impossible for a palatine knight. They embrace and kiss on the wall-walk while the winds of the approaching storm reach extreme speeds.

Hadrian reflects on the Quiet's message that he is the "shortest way" to a future trillions of years away. He realizes that every point in the infinite universe acts as its own center and every decision has consequences. Valka reaffirms her commitment to him, stating he is the only mystery she has left to solve. The storm intensifies as the thunder rolls across the Valles Merguli and rocketry fires in the upper atmosphere. The chapter concludes as they descend from the Storm Wall, marking the final time they will be together in peace.



### Chapter 75: The Noise of Thunder
Hadrian waits in a tunnel vestibule with Valka, Pallino, and the men of the First Cohort. Aristedes reports from the command center that a Cielcin fleet of twenty-two ships has emerged from warp. The Cielcin forces engage the Imperial fleet in high orbit, dropping massive Class-8 siege towers toward the planet. A young soldier named Renna kneels before Hadrian, asking if he is the "Chosen" or the "Son of Earth." Hadrian delivers a speech to his troops, disclaiming any desire for the throne while urging them to fight for the people.

The sky erupts with white light as the Cielcin siege towers enter the atmosphere and crash into Deira City. These dark towers deploy thousands of "nahute" snake-drones that weave into defensive screens to block Imperial missiles. The nahute drones begin hunting any living heat sources in the streets, using drill-bit mouths to kill animals and stragglers. Cielcin shock troops, the scahari, emerge from the towers wearing organic-looking black armor and horned masks. Hadrian observes the towers through Valka's enhanced vision as they deploy onto the terraces and bridges of the valley.

A Cielcin body wearing the "Pale Hand" symbol of Dorayaica falls onto a terrace near Hadrian’s position. Hadrian lures a squad of Cielcin into a tunnel ambush by pretending to retreat with his guards. When the Cielcin follow him into the archway, the hidden Red Company soldiers open fire with plasma and railguns. Hadrian engages the Cielcin commander, who is protected by a shield curtain, and cuts him in half with a highmatter sword. The engagement marks the formal beginning of the Battle for Berenike as the city begins to burn under the Cielcin assault.
