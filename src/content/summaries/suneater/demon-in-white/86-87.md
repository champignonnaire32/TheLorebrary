### 86: The Scourge of Earth
The death of the Cielcin general Bahudde fragments the enemy command, leading to infighting among the Pale vanguard. Pallino exploits this chaos to lead a wedge through the enemy ranks and set fire to their fleet of black ships. Hadrian orders the gates of the Storm Wall opened to retreat his wounded soldiers, including the unconscious Bassander Lin whose suit has hardened. Two Irchtani auxiliaries—winged, bird-like xenobites—carry the severely injured Valka into the safety of the fortress. Hadrian lingers at the gate to ensure his force is inside before the Cielcin fleet in orbit can begin a retaliatory bombardment.

Valka expresses deep regret for joining the mission and asks Hadrian to put her into cryonic freezing until she can return home for healing. She confesses her love for Hadrian before disappearing into the fortress under the support of the Irchtani. The chiliarch Kithuun-Barda lands beside Hadrian and reports that less than half of the thousand Irchtani who fought survived. Hadrian promises Barda that his tribe will be honored as full subjects of the throne for their sacrifice against the Pale. A massive beam of white light scours the field, prompting Hadrian to run for the closing bronze gates of the Storm Wall.

A miles-high holographic image of Syriani Dorayaica, the Prince of Princes and Prophet of the Cielcin, appears above the plains. Dorayaica demands the surrender of the city and addresses Hadrian in Galstani, calling him "kinsman" and the "Rat King" for his previous victories. Hadrian negotiates through an audio-only link, demanding safe conduct for the people of Berenike and the return of prisoners in exchange for his surrender. The Prophet refuses to return the prisoners, stating his soldiers must feed, but offers to withdraw from the system if Hadrian surrenders. Hadrian agrees to surrender the following morning at sunrise to stall for the arrival of the Imperial fleet, which is over twenty-four hours away.





### 87: No Sword Can Cut
Lorian Aristedes and Pallino argue with Hadrian in the command lift, insisting that the Imperial fleet might not arrive in time to save him. Hadrian refuses Pallino's request to accompany him to the surrender, ordering guards to physically restrain the myrmidon at the gate. He dons his greatcoat over black armor and exits the Storm Wall alone as the sun reaches its zenith. The holographic image of Syriani Dorayaica returns and mocks Hadrian's small stature and perceived weakness before the gathered human and Cielcin audiences. Dorayaica announces to the planet that he has broken the "Halfmortal" and intends to execute him publicly to prove he can be killed.

Instead of sending a shuttle for transport, the Cielcin worldship Dharan-Tun—a vessel larger than a moon—fires an orbital laser directly at Hadrian's position. Hadrian utilizes his connection to the Quiet to perceive infinite timelines and selects a narrative where the laser fails to strike him. He steps unscathed from the molten tarmac and white smoke, shouting his defiance at the shocked holographic specter above. The Prophet fires the orbital laser a second time, but Hadrian remains unburnt by focusing on the quantum probability of his survival. The Imperial fleet, led by Otavia Corvo on the Tamerlane—an Eriel-class battleship—suddenly arrives and begins a massive counter-attack.

The Tamerlane and other sector ships destroy a Cielcin vessel, causing Dorayaica's holographic image to flicker and vanish as his worldship retreats into warp. Hadrian walks back to the Storm Wall through the newly restored sunlight as the Cielcin fleet flees the system. Thousands of silent soldiers and Irchtani watch from the atrium as Hadrian enters, eventually erupting into cheers for the "Son of Earth." Pallino breaks free from his guards and confronts Hadrian with a mixture of rage and relief over the suicidal stunt. Alexander, the Imperial prince, watches the celebration with cold suspicion as the people begin to worship Hadrian as a living god.
