### 36: The First Steps
Hadrian joins Princess Selene and her ladies for a horse ride through the Royal Forest of Forum. He reflects on his inability to trust the horse and his preference for creatures of reason. The forest contains ancient trees from Earth and overlooks the flying city's infrastructure. Selene questions Hadrian about his experiences with the Cielcin and Irchtani aliens. Hadrian admits he once hoped for peace but discovered the Cielcin only recognize power.

Selene leads the group to a clearing containing the Arch of Titus, a triumphal arch brought from Earth. The ruin is still radioactive and is surrounded by protective pillars. Hadrian learns that new Emperors must walk through this arch alone during their coronation journey. He feels a sudden, dangerous impulse to touch the ancient stones. Selene explains that the location represents the "first steps" of an Imperial reign.

The group returns to the gatehouse where Hadrian's Red Company officer, Crim, arrives in a state of panic. Crim speaks to Hadrian in Jaddian to avoid being understood by the Martian Guards. He reveals that a murder occurred aboard the Tamerlane while Hadrian was away. Crim clarifies that Valka is alive but a member of the cleaning staff has been killed. Hadrian realizes an assassination attempt was made in his own quarters.



### 37: Blade Without Handle
Hadrian meets with Tor Varro, Otavia Corvo, Crim, and Lorian Aristedes to examine a broken Akateko knife-missile. The small device was found in Hadrian's apartments and used to attack Valka. A batman named Martin died after distracting the missile from its primary target. Valka survived by breaking the weapon against a bulkhead after it stabbed the servant. Hadrian orders the ship's security officers to investigate how the untraceable weapon was smuggled aboard.

The officers discuss the political implications of the Emperor's secret proposal to marry Hadrian to Princess Selene. Hadrian suspects the old noble houses like Hohenzollern, Mahidol, and Bourbon are responsible for the attack. He decides to suppress the official details of the incident to control the narrative. The crew is instructed to freeze nonessential witnesses and incinerate the batman’s body. Hadrian wants to leak false rumors to confuse his enemies.

Tor Varro suggests the attack was timed to occur while Hadrian was known to be off the ship. Hadrian begins to fear that Valka was the intended target rather than himself. He remembers insulting Prince Alexander and adds the prince to his list of potential suspects. Hadrian dismisses the meeting to visit Valka in the medical bay. Crim apologizes for the security breach and swears to find the culprit.



### 38: Valka Awakes
Hadrian enters the medical cell where Valka is recovering from a punctured lung. He watches the automated medical rigs repair her tissue while she remains under the influence of anesthetics. Hadrian experiences a vivid nightmare involving white fingers and his own severed head. Valka wakes and jokes about Hadrian usually being the one in the hospital bed. She explains that she fended off the knife-missile using one of Hadrian's folios as a shield.

Hadrian expresses his guilt for the attack, but Valka insists it is not his fault. She suggests that Princess Selene might have orchestrated the hit to remove a romantic rival. Hadrian remains skeptical of Selene's involvement despite the timing of their forest excursion. Valka reminds him of Udax, the Irchtani centurion who once attempted to assassinate him. They recall Udax's claim that a Chantry priest had hired him for the task.

The couple realizes the Holy Terran Chantry remains their most dangerous and likely enemy. Hadrian notes that his growing legend as the "Halfmortal" threatens the Chantry's religious authority. He perceives the Emperor’s marriage proposal as a way to co-opt his myth for the throne. Valka taps Hadrian's prosthetic hand to calm his growing rage as he vows vengeance. Hadrian promises to investigate his list of enemies from both ends to find the truth.



### 39: The Council of Ghosts
Ten days pass without further incident while Hadrian remains aboard the Tamerlane to watch Forum from orbit. A summons arrives from Prince Hector Avent requiring Hadrian to appear before the Imperial Council. Fearing another assassination attempt, Hadrian requests to attend the meeting via holograph. The Council consists of high-ranking ministers, including Synarch Vergilian of the Chantry and Augustin Bourbon of the War Ministry. Hadrian kneels on the projector plate to address the gathered ghosts of the Imperial government.

Hadrian lies to the Council, claiming the knife-missile was discovered in a supply crate before it could deploy. Synarch Vergilian demands that the weapon be handed over to the Inquisition for analysis. Hadrian informs the high priest that his soldiers have already incinerated the device to ensure safety. This news angers Vergilian, who cites the laws regarding the handling of suspicious artifacts. Hadrian suspects the Chantry would only use such a weapon if they intended to frame him.

The Council meeting turns into a debate about the potential for machine intelligence infestation on Hadrian's ship. Augustin Bourbon supports the Synarch's recommendation to impound the Tamerlane for a thorough search. Hadrian realizes the knife-missile may have been a distraction intended to justify an Inquisitorial raid. He fears the search will uncover Valka's illegal neural machine or his own prosthetic limb. Prince Hector orders the ship's communications and transport to be sealed immediately.



### 40: The Plan
The Inquisition initiates a total lockdown of the Tamerlane, jamming all outbound and inbound signals. Otavia Corvo reports the lockout to Hadrian, who explains that Bourbon and the Synarch are orchestrating a search. Hadrian fears the Inquisitors will plant evidence of forbidden technology if they do not find it. He instructs Valka to return to a fugue creche to hide her from the investigators. Valka initially resists but eventually agrees to be frozen for her own safety.

Hadrian and Valka share a final, intense kiss before she climbs into the cryogenic housing. Valka reminds him that finding how the knife-missile got aboard is their only hope. Hadrian meets with Crim and Lorian Aristedes in his secure private chambers to coordinate their defense. He hands Crim a metal box containing the only recording of his previous death. Hadrian orders Crim to hide the crystal ball where no one can find it.

Aristedes suggests that they look for the traitor among the security staff who could manipulate logs. He argues that someone must have edited the security footage to hide the missile's entry. Hadrian authorizes a team including Tor Varro to search the ship's changelogs for signs of meddling. He demands that they find proof of the conspiracy before the Inquisition docks. Hadrian insists the evidence must be smuggled off the ship at any cost.
