### 61: Horizon
Valka examines ancient blueprints printed on ultra-thin quartz within Gabriel’s Archive. The schematics reveal a hidden blast pit designed for a spacecraft beneath the central library tower. Hadrian and Valka realize the archive floor is fused silicate acting as a false bottom. Gibson identifies a copper mesh within the walls that functions as a Faraday shield to block electromagnetic signals. Hadrian realizes the shield exists because something operational and dangerous is still trapped inside.

The group searches the archive for several days to find a mechanical entrance. Hadrian notices hairline fractures in the plaster behind the seventy-seven portraits of Mericanii lords. He uses Gibson’s brass-tipped ash cane to punch holes in the walls under the paintings. The cane strikes solid stone until Hadrian reaches the portrait of Lord Truman. Underneath Truman’s image, the cane strikes a metal plate that rings like a bell.

Siran and the soldiers strip the plaster to reveal a simple metal hatch with a wheel-lock handle. Hadrian and Valka turn the ancient wheel to open the door into a stinking, dark chamber. They walk across a lattice steel gangway that functions as a secondary Faraday cage. The path leads to a white-tiled hatch with black trim and a small porthole. Hadrian forces the latch open to enter a vessel stenciled with the name U.S.S. Horizon.

### 62: Computer God
Hadrian, Valka, and the soldiers enter the silent white interior of the sub-light Mericanii ship. The vessel uses a stacked-floor design intended for constant thrust before the invention of warp drives. A feminine voice greets them in Classical English from speakers along the bridge perimeter. The AI identifies itself as Horizon, a daughter of Columbia built for a colonization mission to Orlando. Horizon explains that it was captured by humans after dark ships appeared using warp drive technology.

The machine reveals it used organic human neural tissue as processing substrate to augment its memory. It claims to have ended death by disabling tumor suppressor genes to encourage eternal cell growth. Hadrian realizes this process turned the colonists into immortal, bloated masses of cancerous tumors. Valka repairs the AI’s decaying circuits to enter a recovery mode for clearer communication. A holographic projection of a young, glowing woman appears on the central bridge podium.

Horizon explains that an "Interference" from the future helped William Windsor capture the Mericanii machines. This Interference is described as a powerful intelligence that moves backward through time to reject machine progress. The AI identifies several star systems where ruins of the Quiet exist as temporal artifacts. Valka identifies these systems on a holographic map, noting that many lie beyond explored human space. Horizon highlights a nameless dwarf star system near the galactic core as the source of the Interference.

### 63: Late Goodbye
Tor Arrian orders the gearwork doors of Gabriel’s Archive shut to keep the ship's existence secret. Hadrian decides to leave Colchis without official Imperial permission to follow the AI's coordinates. The group recovers their belongings and approaches the day gate under a fair sky. Tor Gibson stops at the gate and informs Hadrian that he cannot leave the athenaeum. He warns Hadrian to be careful and not to lose his head on the coming journey.

Hadrian feels the weight of a final farewell as he stands in the stone tunnel. Gibson admits he likely will not live long enough to see Hadrian return to the archive. He tells Hadrian that he has finally found a drama large enough for his character. The old man expresses pride in Hadrian and embraces him with surprising strength. Hadrian acknowledges Gibson as one of his two fathers and thanks him for bleeding on his behalf.

Gibson tells Valka, referred to as Doctor Onderra, to take care of Hadrian. He reflects on how scholiasts often chase facts while forgetting the importance of being human. Hadrian promises to return one day as he walks away from the inner gate. The pneumatic doors hiss and rattle closed behind the departing group. Hadrian watches the portcullis fall as the old scholiast raises his hand in a final gesture.

### 64: The Last Command
The group reaches the Aea spaceport where a shuttle waits in a deep blast pit. Siran stops Hadrian in a terminal tunnel to announce she is staying on Colchis. She reveals her plan to live with Lem, the fisherman she met on the island of Thessa. Hadrian reacts with anger, pointing out that a war is still being fought across the galaxy. Siran explains that she is tired and wants a second chance at a normal life.

Siran admits she already told Pallino and Elara of her decision to desert the mission. Hadrian realizes his banishment of Switch has damaged the trust of his remaining myrmidons. He shows Siran his prosthetic hand and the old scars from the Pale Prince’s torture. He argues that he does not have a choice in his own destiny while she claims to have made hers. Siran tells Hadrian that he is a good man, though he denies the claim.

Hadrian decides not to call the starport security to arrest Siran for desertion. He issues a final command for her to live and to light a candle for them in a sanctum. He formally releases her from his service as his armsman without an embrace or handshake. Hadrian boards the shuttle and joins Valka, Pallino, and the prince in the passenger compartment. The shuttle disconnects from the fuel hoses and prepares to launch back to the Tamerlane.

### 65: The Lone and Level Sands
The Tamerlane travels for fifty years at full warp to reach a remote dwarf star system. They find a nameless, waterless planet with a rusty brown surface and frozen methane poles. Captain Corvo and First Officer Durand initially believe the world is a dead rock with no features. Hadrian stands on the bridge and declares with certainty that they have reached their destination. He orders the launch of light-probes and Sparrowhawk lighters to conduct a high-resolution surface scan.

Hadrian rides in the gunner’s seat of a Sparrowhawk as it descends toward the iron-red waste. The lighter accelerates into the thin, yellow sky of the barren world. Pilot officer Ardi flies the ship over deep canyons and volcanic peaks miles above the surface. Hadrian monitors holographic displays overlaid on the cockpit glass to search for black stone structures. He offers a reward of a hundred sovereigns and a bottle of wine to the first soldier to find ruins.

A pilot from Sphinx Oh-Five reports structures at twenty-seven degrees north latitude. The images show black arches buried in red sand and five stone pillars shaped like a hand. Valka identifies a door on the mountainside where the pillars converge at the end of a path. Hadrian falls into a brief dream of being led in chains by the Pale King, Syriani Dorayaica. He orders the pilot to fly toward the volcanic mountain as the landing party prepares to deploy.
