### Chapter 26: The Oracle
Hadrian and Switch disembark from their ship onto the visitor's port of the Enigma of Hours on the sixth day. Hadrian wears bone-white Imperial combat armor and a heavy lacerna, though he remains unarmed per local law. The concourse is a massive, dark ring filled with diverse beings, including an android and a spider-bodied chimera. They pass various merchants, including a Jaddian selling artificial pets like a horn-crested owl. Hadrian attempts to follow an Irchtani xenobite, a bird-like creature with a hooked beak and green feathers, but loses Switch in the dense crowd.

While searching for his friend, Hadrian is approached by a Mandari nuncius named Marko who wears spectacles and gold teeth. Marko leads him through a service tunnel to meet an oracle named Jari who allegedly sees through time. Jari is a mangled Exalted—a cybernetically modified human—with a red cybernetic eye and limbs that move independently on the floor. The oracle has drunk from "the Deeps," which are pools of genetic-altering animalcules found on certain worlds. Jari speaks with the voices of people from Hadrian’s past, specifically mimicking the words of an old woman from Emesh and his friend Cat.

The oracle claims that Hadrian’s past is broken and that his ship’s history was intentionally wiped clean. Jari begins to shriek "Light!" repeatedly while his fragmented body thrashes against the dais. Marko intervenes, drawing scalpel blades from his fingernails and ordering Hadrian to flee the chamber immediately. Hadrian escapes the tunnel into the concourse while the oracle's cries of "Light!" echo behind him.





### Chapter 27: Valka
Hadrian spends the following day on the Mistral researching the Deeps through the Enigma's datasphere. He learns that the animalcules in the water are microorganisms that rewrite a host's DNA upon consumption. Valka visits his cabin and requests that he escort her onto the concourse for exploration. They observe a sleek Tavrosi ship called the Jayavarman while walking along the curved road of the port. Hadrian admits his discomfort with the heavily modified Exalted, though Valka notes that her own neural lace makes her technically inhuman.

Valka reveals that she served five years in the Orbital Guard of the Demarchy as a pilot. She confesses to a traumatic event involving Prachar terrorists, who are religious nationalists, that seized a Durantine trading cog near lunar orbit. Command ordered her to destroy the ship to prevent the terrorists from crashing it into the planet's orbital mirrors. Valka fired the shot, killing over two thousand innocent captives who were held in fugue states. She resigned the next day and left her home planet to escape the public's judgment.

Hadrian compares her burden to his own experiences, specifically the destruction of Emil Bordelon’s ship. They discuss the moral weight of violence and the necessity of good men performing terrible actions to prevent worse outcomes. Valka expresses deep concern for Hadrian’s future and his apparent commitment to a life of mercenary violence. They encounter a bookstore selling ancient printed volumes where they share a rare moment of quiet intimacy. The conversation concludes with Hadrian acknowledging that someone must prevent him from becoming a fool during their mission.





### Chapter 28: The Dark World
The Mistral reaches the dark world of Vorgossos, which orbits a failing brown dwarf star. An external power suddenly seizes control of the ship's systems and undocks it from the Enigma of Hours. The vessel is remotely piloted into a grim parade of other ships toward a massive space elevator. This "hightower" is a black spar of adamant that connects the frozen planet to a station in the stars. Hadrian and his primary team, including Valka and the legionnaires, board the desolate station platform.

They find the arrival hall empty of customs officers, merchants, or any other signs of human life. The station is decorated with distended human statues and dark metal scrollwork that appears ancient and worn. While the group explores the hall, the iris door back to their docking umbilical suddenly slams shut. Hadrian attempts to contact Captain Corvo on the Mistral but finds that all communication signals are blocked. They realize they are being observed by hidden cameras as they approach a battery of lift carriages.

The team activates their body shields and prepares for a potentially hostile descent to the planet's surface. Hadrian observes three faceless female statues made of jointed steel standing guard over the lift carriages. He compares their appearance to the Cielcin and the cybernetic Exalted he encountered previously. Despite the ominous atmosphere and the loss of contact with his ship, Hadrian draws Olorin's sword for readiness. He leads the group into a lift carriage, steeled by the words of a recurring prophecy.





### Chapter 29: The Profane City
The lift carriage descends for hours through the airless vacuum toward the surface of Vorgossos. Hadrian dozes and experiences a nightmare of being dragged into dark water by iron fingers. They eventually enter a massive concrete dome through a circular opening at its apex. Inside the dome lies a somber city of white stone and gray concrete inhabited by humans and homunculi. A river flows through the plaza, and the air is filled with the damp smell of moss and birds.

The group explores the marble plaza and discovers a city devoid of the holographs and advertisements common in other ports. The somber atmosphere reminds Hadrian of his childhood home in Meidua under his father's strict rule. They meet a beggar woman named Shara who recognizes Hadrian’s palatine status and asks for coins. She explains that the city's ruler, the Undying, is Kharn Sagara, a legendary figure from fifteen thousand years ago. Shara warns them that the Undying sees everything through the city's extensive surveillance network.

Thirty guards in dun uniforms, identified as SOMs with glowing skulls and dead eyes, surround the group. These "guardians" appear to be reanimated corpses with rubberized faces controlled by an external will. Hadrian identifies himself as an Imperial cousin on a diplomatic mission and demands to speak with Sagara. One of the SOMs orders the group to drop their weapons in a flat, mechanical voice. When Hadrian attempts to negotiate, the lead guardian strikes him with a stun baton.





### Chapter 30: The Suppliants
Hadrian regains consciousness on a velvet couch inside a luxurious chamber decorated with fine tapestries and paintings. He is greeted by Kim Hae Song, an elderly former Baron who wears a red toga and heavy rings. Song explains that they are in a waiting room for those seeking an audience with the Undying. Hadrian discovers he is surrounded by dozens of other elderly palatines who have been waiting for weeks or months. His companions have been taken to separate quarters, and his sword has been confiscated by the guards.

The room is serviced by silent homunculi with contrasting skin and hair colors who provide food and caffeinated drinks. Hadrian learns that the "Undying" is indeed an immortal who offers body and brain replacements to the wealthy. Kim Hae Song identifies the Undying's majordomo as Yume, an android daimon who manages the audience schedule. The other suppliants express frustration at the lack of order in how the "golem" selects people for meetings. Hadrian is shocked to find himself the youngest person in the room by a significant margin.

To pass the time, Hadrian begins playing Druaja, a complex game of labyrinth chess, against Baron Song. They discuss the limits of human memory and the biological necessity of brain replacement for extreme longevity. Hadrian realizes that while he is chronologically over a century old, he has spent most of that time in cryonic fugue. He remains cautious and does not reveal the true nature of his mission to the other lords. As days pass, Hadrian focuses on mastering the shifting board of the labyrinth game while awaiting his summons.
