### Chapter 61: Valka Again
Hadrian returns to the Mistral airlock after three days of failed political talks with the Cielcin. He reflects that the Cielcin are apex predators who do not understand the human concept of trade or reciprocity. Most of the ship's crew remains in icy fugue, leaving the corridors funereal and empty. Hadrian finds Valka Onderra sitting alone in the starboard gallery reading a book. They discuss the fundamental disconnect in communication between humans and the Cielcin.

Valka explains that their respective languages are rooted in brains shaped by different environments. Hadrian expresses fear regarding his prophetic visions of burning planets and his own death. He admits he wishes he and Valka were conducting the talks instead of soldiers and bureaucrats. Valka reveals she stayed with the expedition because the Cielcin possess knowledge about the Quiet. Hadrian worries that Captain Bassander Lin will kill him if the diplomatic mission fails.

Valka steps forward and kisses Hadrian, pressing him back against a couch. She tells Hadrian that she will not let him die.



### Chapter 62: The Limits of Reason
The human and Cielcin contingents meet for negotiations in a pavilion under a massive tree. Raine Smythe uses a projector to display a holographic map of the vast Sollan Empire. She informs Prince Aranata that the Cielcin cannot win a war against such a massive domain. Aranata is visibly shocked by the scale of human territory but refuses to become a slave. The Cielcin struggle with the concept of "mutually beneficial" relations, viewing all interactions through power dynamics.

Aranata strikes his slave girl translator after she uses a word with unintended sexual overtones. Hadrian rushes to help the wounded girl, who whispered "Kill me" to him in the tongues of men. The alien child Nobuta yanks the girl's chain, but Hadrian refuses to let go. Cielcin guards seize Hadrian and force him to his knees as Aranata demands an apology. Kharn Sagara intervenes, using his floating eye drones to threaten Aranata and force a ceasefire.

Hadrian refuses to apologize to the prince, choosing instead to assert his own dominance. Aranata respects this display of strength and returns to his seat to continue the discussion.



### Chapter 63: The Apostol
Lieutenant Greenlaw fetches Hadrian in the middle of the night for a meeting with Raine Smythe. They meet in Smythe’s office aboard the Schiavona, where she reveals she is nearly three hundred years old. Smythe proposes sending an "apostol," or permanent ambassador, to live among the Cielcin fleet. She asks Hadrian to take the position, believing his diplomatic skills make him the best candidate. Hadrian initially reacts with anger, accusing Smythe of treating him as an expendable slave gift.

He eventually considers the offer because it provides an opportunity to learn about the Quiet. Smythe promises Hadrian a retinue of guards, servants, and a ship for his mission. They agree that Tor Varro will accompany Hadrian, but Bassander Lin will be excluded from the staff. Hadrian asks for a list of the families of the soldiers he killed so he can make amends. Smythe informs Hadrian that he will likely receive an audience with the Emperor before departing.

Hadrian is awestruck by the prospect of meeting the sovereign of a dozen billion suns. He realizes the importance of the posting and agrees to the knight-tribune's proposal.



### Chapter 64: A Devil's Bargain
Negotiations resume with Smythe formally proposing the exchange of an emissary to Prince Aranata. The slave girl translates "emissary" as a gift, and Smythe specifies the term is for several years. Aranata mocks the humans for offering a gift with conditions, questioning if they are predators or prey. Hadrian intervenes to clarify the translation, but the slave girl translates "men" as "vermin." The child Nobuta becomes petulant and objects when Hadrian refers to the Cielcin as "people."

Hadrian realizes the Cielcin language is based on active and passive modes rather than simple gender. Aranata demands Hadrian specifically as the "gift," citing his status as a human lord. Hadrian attempts to negotiate for the return of Tanaran in exchange for his own presence. Aranata refuses to part with Tanaran, referring to the baetan as a prized possession. The herald Oalicomn objects to the presence of human soldiers within the Cielcin ship.

Nobuta appears ill or distressed, prompting Aranata to order the child and herald to leave. Smythe agrees to bring Valka to the next meeting after Hadrian suggests her xenological expertise is needed.



### Chapter 65: Of Gods and Engines
Hadrian wanders alone through the Garden on the Demiurge after a council session. He finds Kharn’s clone-children, Suzuha and Ren, throwing stones into a black pool. They discuss the "Brethren," ancient Mericanii computer gods kept chained by Kharn Sagara. Suzuha explains that the Mericanii once built "new men" to replace the original human race. This replacement led to a genocidal war that resulted in the sacrifice of Earth.

Suzuha claims the Mericanii built star-devouring weapons and engines that destroy matter. Hadrian informs the children that Vorgossos is doomed because the Empire has discovered its location. Suzuha is defiant, insisting that her father’s ancient war machines can defeat the legions. She quotes Kharn’s belief that machines could never understand love, duty, or the urge to create. The golem Yume arrives to escort the children back to Kharn’s palace for supper.

Suzuha mocks the Sollans one last time before being led away by the machine servant. Hadrian boasts that the Sollans defeated the daimons once and will do so again.
