### 16: THE TOMB
Hadrian Marlowe initiates the thawing process for Tanaran, a Cielcin xenobite stored in a large cryogenic crèche. Doctor Okoyo manages the technical sequence while Switch and Crim stand guard with phase disruptors set to stun. The protagonist explains that the Cielcin’s blood must be cycled back into its body using TX9 suspension fluid to prevent cryoburn. Hadrian reflects on his previous interactions with the alien species during the inquest at the Chantry bastille on Emesh. He ignores the fear of the human soldiers to personally monitor the xenobite’s awakening.

Tanaran is a tall, sinewy creature with white hair, snarling horns, and two beating hearts. The alien awakens with a wet gasp and recognizes Hadrian, immediately asking if they have found its master. Hadrian provides the creature with a black jumpsuit and dark robes to hide its inhuman appearance. He leads the group through the ship's black halls toward a lift, avoiding the winking camera blisters in the ceiling. A technician spots the group, prompting Crim to fire his stunner and forcing the team to accelerate their pace.

The team arrives at a lower floor where Crim stuns two bald-pated fugue techs in gray-striped uniforms. They move toward the hangar bay but are intercepted by Lieutenant Alessandro Hanas and six Jaddian aljanhi soldiers. Hanas demands Hadrian’s surrender, but the confrontation is interrupted by the surprise arrival of Pallino and Elara. Pallino knocks the shielded lieutenant unconscious with a physical blow while Valka appears to confirm she has disabled the security cameras. The combined group of mercenaries and the Cielcin proceed toward the hangar to make their escape.





### 17: THE BREAKING OF THE COMPANY
The group runs through the Balmung’s corridors as red alarm lights flash and security sirens drone throughout the ship. Valka approaches a security terminal and uses her technical skills to override the titanium bulkheads blocking the hangar bay. Hadrian stands in the center of the hall to draw fire, relying on his personal shield to deflect incoming stunner bolts. The team enters the hangar and finds thirty disorganized soldiers scattered among grounded shuttlecraft and storage crates. Deck Officer Brux uses the public address system to demand that Hadrian surrender and lay down his arms.

Hadrian climbs a service ladder to the catwalks and uses his highmatter sword to cut the power conduits of the attack lighters. He drops two heavy Sparrowhawk ships onto the hangar floor, crushing several soldiers and scattering the remaining legionnaires. Bassander Lin appears on the catwalk, blocking Hadrian’s path with his own iridescent highmatter blade drawn. The two men engage in a duel, clashing their vibrating exotic matter weapons while stunner fire arced below them. Hadrian successfully blocks a series of heavy slashes before Bassander strikes him in the face with a physical blow.

Bassander stands over the fallen Hadrian and demands his surrender, but Switch intervenes by shooting the captain in the face with a stunner. Hadrian recovers, seizes Bassander's sword, and flees toward the waiting shuttle with the rest of his companions. As the shuttle departs, Jinan appears in the hangar and fires several rifle shots that break against Hadrian's shield. She screams that she loved him as the hatch seals and the shuttle enters the vacuum of space. The group successfully reaches the Mistral and prepares for a warp jump away from the fleet.





### 18: THE OTHER EDGE
Hadrian stands on the bridge of the Mistral and watches the violet light of warp space as the ship travels. The vessel eventually reverts to normal space near a frigid blue supergiant star surrounded by a massive accretion disk. Otavia Corvo prepares a communication signal for March Station using the frequency retrieved from the Painted Man’s terminal. Hadrian observes multiple blue sparks in the disk below, which are fusion torches from mining ships trawling for heavy elements. The protagonist visits the ship's medica and find Pallino, Siran, and Switch recovering from the effects of the freeze.

He discovers that Valka has been awake for two weeks and has been teaching the Cielcin prisoner how to speak Galstani. Tanaran expresses its belief that humans are insane for traveling through space in a state of cryogenic fugue. The xenobite reveals its full name and titles, identifying itself as a Baetan of the Aeta Aranata. Otavia calls Hadrian back to the bridge to view a holograph of March Station, a massive Extrasolarian ring city. The station is roughly six hundred miles in diameter and orbits just above the plane of the planetary ecliptic.

The Mistral falls under the remote pilot control of the station's port authority for the final docking approach. Hadrian notes that the station has been under construction for centuries and remains hidden from the Imperial Chantry. He reflects on how the Empire is like a spiderweb that only covers a small portion of the galaxy's darkness. The ship prepares to dock, and Hadrian anticipates meeting the residents of this lawless Extrasolarian territory.





### 19: THE GATES OF BABYLON
The Mistral docks at March Station, and Hadrian leads his team through a fog-filled portal into the city. He discovers that the station features gray buildings rising through clouds and a ceiling that acts as the roof of the world. Oily rain falls constantly on the streets, which are filled with holograph advertisements in multiple languages including Jaddian and Nipponese. The crowd consists of people with visible machine implants, including hardware contacts, false porcelain limbs, and glowing aperture eyes. Hadrian observes a man with a massive steaming device replaced for an arm and girls with glass cables strung between them.

The protagonist recites a mental prayer to Holy Mother Earth for protection against the perceived perversion of the flesh. Crim leads the group through the ultramarine twilight, explaining that the Extrasolarians value freedom and unregulated markets. He notes that the Chantry is wrong about machine integration and that the residents use technology to achieve their desired forms. Hadrian worries that his status as an Imperial palatine is too obvious despite his simple black civilian clothing. The team considers posing as life-extension seekers to avoid drawing attention to their true mission.

They discuss the possibility of selling Tanaran to slavers, but Hadrian rejects the idea due to the complexity of the station. The group pauses in the shadow of a building to plan their search for a ship traveling to Vorgossos. Hadrian recalls the Painted Man's warning that the secret planet is only found by those its ruler wishes to see. They decide to begin their investigation by visiting local clinics and genetic bonecutters.





### 20: THE BONECUTTER
Hadrian enters a genetic clinic called Cento’s Biotechnic, which features neon signs and exposed industrial ductwork. The interior is polished white and plays soft music with the sound of falling water to disarm potential customers. A small, Lothrian doctor named Yevgeni Cento greets them while wearing a jeweler’s glass over a hollowed-out eye socket. Cento immediately identifies Hadrian as an Imperial palatine and mocks the idea that a lord would visit his grungy shop. Hadrian uses the pseudonym "Gibson" and claims he is seeking a cure for something the Empire cannot fix.

The doctor offers to grow new skin for Hadrian’s cryoburn scars for twenty thousand marks, but the protagonist refuses. Cento explains that while palatines have long lives, they eventually die from systemic cellular failure or terminal cancer. Hadrian clarifies that he is not seeking medical treatment but is looking for a way to "cheat death" as a mercenary. He mentions the name Vorgossos, causing the doctor to swear in his native tongue and become visibly agitated. Cento reveals that the planet is ruled by an "Undying" figure who shares the gift of immortality for a price.

The doctor explains that the Exalted are not an order but a group of captains who serve the ruler of Vorgossos. He claims that March Station contains traders and shipping companies who act as secret contacts for the hidden world. Hadrian brandishes a black universal Rothsbank card and demands a specific name for a referral. Cento looks at the card and prepares to provide information about the contacts who know the way to the dragons.
