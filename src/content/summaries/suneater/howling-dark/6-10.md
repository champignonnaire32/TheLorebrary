### 6: The Road to Vorgossos
Hadrian Marlowe awakens in a mobile office pod as the prisoner of The Painted Man, a blood-born homunculus. The creature possesses a silver terminal disc wired directly into its skull and brandishes Hadrian’s iridescent Jaddian sword. Hadrian remains paralyzed from a stunner blast while the Painted Man mocks him and demands a ransom from his mercenary company. The homunculus uses its skin-changing abilities to mimic the face and voice of Hadrian’s friend, Ghen, while claiming the soldier is dead. Hadrian notices the office is actually a private cable car traveling through the city of Arslan on the planet Rustam.

As his palatine nervous system recovers from the stunner, Hadrian engages the creature in conversation to buy time. He eventually seizes a heavy iridium meteor from a display table and hurls it into the Painted Man's face. The homunculus falls, becoming entangled in its own terminal cords and the loops of its stunner handle. Hadrian stomps on the creature's hand and smashes its head repeatedly with a heavy munitions crate until it is dead. He retrieves his iridescent sword, his shield-belt, and the silver terminal disc from the corpse.

Using the sword, Hadrian cuts a hole through the pod’s locked door to reveal the city streets thirty feet below. He leaps from the moving cable car and catches onto scaffolding surrounding a massive electronic billboard.



### 7: Things Unseen, Things Remembered
Hadrian wanders through the city of Arslan without a map or his personal terminal to contact his fleet. He discovers a rain barrel and washes away the blood of the Painted Man and his friend Ghen. The protagonist reflects on his childhood lessons with his teacher, Gibson, regarding the management of grief and emotion. He eventually locates a telecommunications booth and initiates a quantum telegraph link to his orbiting ships. Jinan answers the call and confirms that Switch, Ilex, and the other mercenaries survived the tea house battle.

She informs him that Ghen is dead and that the company was forced to leave his body behind on the planet. Hadrian reports that he killed the Painted Man and arranges a meeting point at a local cafe. He enters a small restaurant built beneath the landing pads of an old freighter and orders a bowl of fish noodles. Jinan arrives at the cafe wearing Hadrian’s coat, which Switch recovered from the site of the previous disaster. She explains that the Red Company was nearly destroyed by SOMs, which are human-based "Surrogate Operating Mediums" used as machines.

The two share a moment of physical affection and Hadrian leaves five kaspums on the table for the waitress. They walk arm-in-arm through the cold Arslan night to a waiting shuttle that returns them to the fleet.



### 8: The Council of Captains
Bassander Lin convenes a council meeting aboard the Pharaoh to discuss the loss of four men on Rustam. Valka identifies the attackers as Surrogate Operating Mediums, which are human bodies hollowed out and converted into combat machines. Bassander announces a general order to regroup with the Imperial fleet at Coritani for a massive war effort. He reveals that the Cielcin have completely destroyed the planet Tyras, resulting in the deaths of seven million people. Captain Otavia Corvo, a seven-foot-tall mercenary, refuses to commit her ship, the Mistral, to a pitched military battle.

Hadrian interrupts the bickering by slamming the Painted Man’s silver terminal onto the Mandari-printed marble table. He argues that the homunculus provided a concrete lead to a trading post called March Station located in the Veil. Ilex, the green-skinned dryad pilot, accepts the terminal to begin decrypting its internal ledgers and navigational coordinates. Jinan shocks Hadrian by supporting Bassander’s plan to hold position and wait for instructions from Knight-Tribune Raine Smythe. Bassander threatens to seize the Mistral and fire Otavia if she refuses to follow his commands as the company's commodore.

Following the meeting, Jinan finds Hadrian wandering the corridors and attempts to comfort him regarding his tactical disagreement. Hadrian describes how the Painted Man wore Ghen’s face during their final confrontation before he killed the creature. He rejects Jinan’s invitation to return to her quarters, choosing instead to remain alone in his room on the Pharaoh.



### 9: Absent Friends
Hadrian moves his possessions from the Pharaoh to the Balmung to avoid further interactions with Bassander Lin. He joins Jinan, Valka, Switch, Pallino, and Elara for a communal meal in the ship’s executive dining room. The group eats pasta prepared by Pallino while discussing Bassander’s habit of constantly monitoring security feeds. Pallino explains his heritage from the planet Trieste and his pride in serving the Emperor as a legionary. Valka notes that her own Tavrosi background is often misunderstood as being that of a sorcerer rather than a scientist.

Hadrian initiates a formal toast to Ghen, noting the empty chair left by the fallen myrmidon. He admits to the group that he feels responsible for Ghen’s death during the escape from the tea house. Switch consoles him by explaining that the Painted Man’s betrayal made the soldier’s death unavoidable for anyone present. Valka recounts a humorous story about Ghen wearing an ugly feathered hat sent to him by a Jaddian commander. Switch shares memories of Ghen sending him fake love notes during their time in the Borosevo coliseum.

Pallino describes a bar fight where Ghen threw several local men around to impress some girls. The friends spend an hour exchanging stories, transitioning from initial grief to shared laughter over Ghen’s eccentricities. Hadrian reflects on how fables and legends are often more powerful than the clinical truth of a man's death.



### 10: Jinan
Hadrian enters Jinan’s quarters on the Balmung, which she has decorated with a red silk curtain and a bronze incense holder. The two immediately engage in a heated argument regarding Jinan’s decision to support Bassander Lin during the council meeting. Hadrian expresses his fear that Bassander’s personal dislike for him is driving the commodore to abandon the mission. Jinan dismisses his concerns as dramatic and reminds him that they are soldiers who must follow established military orders. The protagonist recounts the torture he witnessed in the Borosevo bastille as his motivation for seeking an end to the war.

He argues that peace cannot be achieved through perpetual fighting and that the Cielcin prisoners are the key to a treaty. Hadrian proposes that they should desert the company and head for Vorgossos alone if Ilex discovers valid coordinates. Jinan points out that the loss of twelve planets since they left Emesh justifies Bassander’s desire to return to the front. She admits that a return to the Imperial fleet would likely mean the permanent end of their romantic relationship. Despite their tactical disagreements, Jinan reaffirms her love for Hadrian and they share a moment of physical intimacy.

Hadrian reflects on his own sense of unworthiness and the vow he has made to become a man deserving of her. He compares their shared time to a walled garden that is temporarily sheltered from the encroaching darkness of the war. The chapter concludes with Hadrian contemplating the heavy burdens he carries and the inevitable end of their paradise.
