# Chapter 21-25 Summaries


### 21: A Matter of Price
Hadrian, Crim, Switch, and Siran navigate the fishery district of March Station to reach Freight Lift 013. They enter a warehouse office and meet a homunculus receptionist named Eva. Crim introduces Hadrian as a palatine and mercenary captain seeking passage. Eva leads Hadrian and Switch to the office of Antonius Brevon, which features a massive alumglass window looking into the fishery. Brevon is an old man with dark glasses and a large raven named Hrothgar perched beside him.

Hadrian requests transport to the secret city of Vorgossos to track the Cielcin. Brevon reveals his artificial nature by shaking Hadrian's hand with a skeletal, metal limb. The merchant demands one hundred thousand marks for the coordinates and transport. When Hadrian admits he cannot pay, Brevon offers to buy the rights to Hadrian's palatine genome. Hadrian refuses to sell his own DNA to be used for the creation of new slaves or experiments.

Brevon removes his glasses to reveal blank metal curves instead of eyes. Hadrian proposes a different deal by offering a sample of upper-caste Cielcin blood. Brevon agrees to the trade, speculating that the genetic material could yield valuable pharmaceutical compounds. The merchant promises Hadrian a place on a ship leaving for Vorgossos within the month. Hadrian leaves the office, having committed to giving up Tanaran's blood to secure the trip.



### 22: Blood and Water
Hadrian returns to the Mistral and informs Valka and the Red Company officers of the deal. Valka laughs at the trade, while Captain Corvo approves of obtaining passage at no financial cost. Hadrian expresses his moral discomfort with selling genetic material to the Extrasolarian flesh peddlers. He orders the ship to remain under lockdown with no shore leave allowed during the two-week wait. Hadrian visits the ship's medica to collect an ampule and syringe from Doctor Okoyo.

Valka follows Hadrian to the brig and questions his sudden ethical concerns regarding slavery. They enter the dark cell where Tanaran is being held and explain the necessity of the trip to Vorgossos. Hadrian informs the Cielcin that he must provide a vial of its blood as payment for their transport. Valka attempts to translate the concept of genetics to the alien, describing it as the study of heredity. Tanaran initially refuses, fearing that the humans intend to use its blood to manufacture biological weapons.

Hadrian threatens to take the blood by force if the prisoner does not comply voluntarily. Tanaran surrenders and requests that Hadrian never tell the Cielcin leadership about this defilement. Hadrian draws a sample of black, ink-like blood from the vein in Tanaran's translucent neck. The injector seals the glass phylactery, and Hadrian secures the warm sample in his coat. Valka provides a kerchief to the wounded alien as Hadrian promises to return it to its people.



### 23: The Pilot
The Mistral unclamps from March Station and executes a departure vector into deep space. Captain Corvo directs the ship toward rendezvous coordinates located far below the ecliptic plane. First Officer Durand expresses suspicion that the meeting is a trap set by the bonecutter Brevon. Hadrian watches other vessels in the distance, including a rigger ship with blue-glass sails. Ilex explains that dryads like her were originally designed as biological machines for deep-space labor.

The Mistral reaches the rendezvous point three hundred thousand miles from the station but finds no ship. Sensor sweeps fail to detect any infrared signatures, reactor leakage, or gravimetric anomalies in the area. A sudden loud knocking sound reverberates through the hull near one of the lateral airlocks. Centurion Pallino reports the disturbance and readies a security team of twenty men in combat gear. Hadrian activates his personal shield and joins the security detail at the port airlock with his sword drawn.

The outer doors open to reveal a multi-armed mechanical creature pulling itself into the airlock. The entity stands on eight articulate steel ropes and possesses a bullet-shaped head with glowing red eyes. It projects a holographic image of a young brown-skinned boy to identify itself as Nazzareno. Nazzareno explains that he is the pilot sent by the Exalted to ferry them to their destination. The creature requests permission to come aboard the Mistral to begin the docking procedure with the Enigma of Hours.



### 24: The Enigma of Hours
Nazzareno leads the group to the bridge, moving with difficulty under the ship's centripetal gravity. The Exalted pilot demands total control of the Mistral’s navigation and its internal datasphere. Captain Corvo reluctantly grants access after Nazzareno confirms he has already bypassed their security. The pilot freezes the ship's instruments to protect the privacy of the Enigma of Hours. A massive shadow falls across the bridge as the Mistral enters the hangar of a gargantuan vessel.

Hadrian looks out the viewport to see orderly lines of lights forming streets and buildings within the ship. He realizes the Enigma of Hours is a city-sized Sojourner ship approximately one hundred miles long. Nazzareno recites a pre-recorded list of ship rules and warns that any violations will result in summary disembarkation. He defines summary disembarkation as being ejected from the hold without warning, regardless of fuel levels. Corvo prepares to put her crew into cryonic fugue, but Nazzareno informs her it will be unnecessary.

The pilot reveals that the journey to Vorgossos will only take three standard weeks. Valka explains that the Exalted ship is capable of traveling at several thousand times the speed of light. The Mistral undergoes a series of metallic booms as it is clamped into the Enigma’s central hold. Gravity shifts as the Sojourner enters warp, and the Mistral's weapons systems are remotely locked. Nazzareno welcomes the crew to the Enigma of Hours before departing the bridge.



### 25: Becalmed in Motion
The Mistral remains docked inside the Enigma of Hours as the ship travels at nine thousand times the speed of light. Hadrian overhears a private conversation between Captain Corvo and Switch on the stairs to the observatory. Switch expresses his distrust of the Cielcin and his belief that Hadrian is betraying the Empire by helping the alien. Corvo admits she shares his concerns but feels bound by her debt to Hadrian after the events with Emil Bordelon. Hadrian enters the observatory and finds the two drinking vodka while watching other ships through the alumglass.

Corvo points out several other vessels in the hold, including Durantine galleons and a whale-like Jaddian ship. She asks Hadrian about his future plans, but he admits he cannot see past the mission to Vorgossos. Hadrian reflects on his treasonous acts, including the assault on Bassander Lin and the deaths on the Balmung. Valka and Pallino arrive at the observatory with Tanaran, who has been released from the brig for a visit. The Cielcin is fascinated by the scale of the Enigma and compares it to the asteroid-cities of its own people.

Tanaran touches the window glass and leaves a filmy print of its six-fingered hand. It questions why so many different human tribes are gathered together in the merchant ship. Valka explains that the various groups are here to trade with the Exalted merchants. Tanaran identifies the concept of "merchants" with a Cielcin word for the most unclean, stationless outcasts. The group watches the violet ripple of spacetime through the open end of the cylinder as they travel toward the Cielcin home.
