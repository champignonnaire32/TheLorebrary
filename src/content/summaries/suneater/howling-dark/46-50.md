# Chapter 46: The Long Cold to Chapter 50: The Devil and the Honest Man - Summary


### [46]: [The Long Cold]
Hadrian recovers from his head injury while imprisoned in a concrete cell with Valka. He spends weeks examining a pure white shell fragment found in his pocket. The prisoners survive on ration bars that taste of false cinnamon and oily water. Hadrian uses a nail to scratch portraits of his family and friends into the walls. He admits to Valka that he still feels the loss of his sword, his house, and his military post.

Valka points out that Hadrian is a soldier, citing his own words to the children. Hadrian expresses doubt about his identity and mentions Jinan's offer to become a spice peddler. Valka mocks the idea of him being a merchant and calls him more than that. They debate the nature of freedom, with Hadrian using a raft analogy to argue for the necessity of constraints. Hadrian explains that he sacrificed his freedom when he chose to become a scholiast.

Valka notices that Hadrian has drawn everyone but her on the wall. Hadrian claims they aren't there to criticize his artwork like she would. She challenges him to overcome his fear of her criticism. Hadrian finally sets to work carving Valka's likeness into the stone.

### [47]: [One Villain and Another]
After months of neglect, the golem Yume arrives with six slack-faced undead SOM guards to open the cell. Hadrian and Valka are led through a dark, ruined city of plastic and concrete. They board an open tram car that rises along the massive cavern wall. During the ascent, Hadrian experiences a psychic connection with the beast Brethren. The inhuman mind projects a monolith into Hadrian's perception and speaks to him.

Brethren tells Hadrian to protect Sagara’s children because they are the future. The creature gives a cryptic warning that Lucifer and Prometheus are the same. It instructs Hadrian to seek hardship at the highest place at the bottom of the world. Valka snaps Hadrian out of his trance as the tram reaches the top. The group enters Kharn Sagara’s inverted pyramid and walks through a hall of dead art.

Sagara greets them from his life-sustaining throne with mocking courtesy. The heavy doors swing open to reveal Bassander Lin and a column of armored legionnaires. Bassander wears formal white plate and sports a mechanical replacement for his severed hand. He thanks Sagara for returning the fugitives on behalf of the Emperor and First Strategos Hauptmann.

### [48]: [A Red Reunion]
Hadrian stands speechless as Bassander Lin mocks his bedraggled appearance. He realizes the whole martial display is designed to highlight his total loss of status. Kharn Sagara accuses the Empire of being thugs who plunder and call it peace. Bassander reveals that an Imperial interceptor is already in orbit above Vorgossos. Valka asks how the fleet found them, noting they couldn't have followed from March Station.

Bassander mentions an accord between the Empire and Sagara regarding the Cielcin. He confirms that the Cielcin are currently on their way to the planet. Two legionnaires seize Hadrian and force him into electromagnetic manacles. Bassander ignores Hadrian's desperate questions about Captain Corvo and the Mistral's crew. Sagara states he will take his own ship to rendezvous with the Imperial flagship, the Obdurate.

Bassander refuses to pay Sagara until an introduction with the Pale is secured. Sagara warns Hadrian that his pet Brethren is never wrong. Hadrian stands straight despite his binders, feeling he is finally on the proper path. The legionnaires begin escorting the prisoners away for their transfer to the fleet.

### [49]: [Two Treasons]
Hadrian is moved to a cell aboard the ISV Schiavona, an Imperial interceptor shaped like a black and brass needle. He observes Kharn Sagara's massive black ship, the Demiurge, which resembles a gothic city of stone with many spires. The vessel is covered in a billion unique statues of men, gods, and angels. Hadrian's ruined clothes are laundered and returned to him by a sympathetic guard. He waits for orders from Knight-Tribune Raine Smythe while the ship enters a parking orbit.

Jinan enters the cell and projects a holograph from the Knight-Tribune. Smythe’s message confirms that Hadrian was acting under her emergency orders to find Vorgossos. Jinan reacts with fury, seizing Hadrian by the hair and slamming his head against the wall. She accuses him of murdering three men during his escape from the Balmung. Hadrian blocks her blows but refuses to strike back at the woman he once loved.

Jinan reveals that Switch was the person who telegraphed their location to the fleet. Switch apparently told the Empire that Hadrian had gone mad to save his own life. Hadrian is devastated by the betrayal of his oldest friend and blames Brethren’s interference. Jinan leaves the cell after announcing that Bassander Lin is coming to speak with him.

### [50]: [The Devil and the Honest Man]
Bassander Lin interrogates Hadrian at a glass table while the prisoner is chained. Hadrian warns the captain that Kharn Sagara possesses a dangerous Mericanii daimon. Bassander remains skeptical but admits that Switch likely used the daimon's help to send the signal. He reveals that the Schiavona has docked inside the landing bays of Sagara's ship. They are currently en route to meet the Obdurate and the Cielcin prince, Aranata Otiolo.

Bassander accuses Hadrian of being a traitor who murdered legionnaires and maimed his commanding officer. Hadrian defends his actions by citing Raine Smythe’s direct orders to pursue the Cielcin link. Bassander confirms that Switch betrayed the group to avoid execution by the Chantry for machine heresy. All of Hadrian's companions are currently being held under guard aboard the Mistral. Valka is to be released after the mission because her Tavrosi citizenship protects her from Imperial law.

Bassander announces that Hadrian will be confined to the Mistral until his fate is decided. He warns Hadrian that if Smythe is executed for this mission, he will kill Hadrian personally. Six guards arrive to escort the prisoner directly to his ship. Hadrian leaves the interrogation room with a split lip and a heart full of fury.
