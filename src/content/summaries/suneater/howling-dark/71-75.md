### 71: Hope Is a Cloud
Hadrian realizes the Cielcin are drilling into the *Schiavona* hull using nahute drones. He speaks with a medtech about Kharn's children, who are in a coma-like state filled with "praxis." Valka comforts a traumatized Hadrian, who is covered in Nobuta's blood. Hadrian meets Commander Sciarra on the bridge; the ship is under siege by hundreds of nahute. Valka suggests venting the warp drive's helium coolant to create a freezing fog screen.

This cloud will also displace oxygen, potentially asphyxiating the Cielcin. Hadrian dons spare legionnaire armor and delivers a rousing speech to two hundred soldiers. The ship's lights fail and emergency red lights slam on as the "quench" process begins. Hadrian activates his helmet, which uses entoptic projectors to display a clear view of the hold. The ramp opens, and Hadrian leads the charge into a world of blinding white light and fog.

He raises his highmatter sword in salute to Valka and Tor Varro before exiting. The suit's internal air supply whines to life to protect him from the subarctic chill.



### 72: The Pit
Hadrian leads the charge into the freezing helium fog, using infrared sensors to track Cielcin heat signatures. The nahute drones disengage from the ship to attack the soldiers, leading to a chaotic violet plasma skirmish. Hadrian loses his highmatter sword in the fog and is nearly strangled by a Cielcin warrior before being saved by a shot. He recovers his sword just as the fog clears, revealing that half of his two hundred men are dead. A nahute drone latches onto Hadrian's helmet and begins drilling into his skull, killing his entoptic displays.

Valka saves Hadrian by finding the radio "off switch" for the drones, causing them to fall limp. She explains that she and Tanaran studied the drone code while Hadrian was in fugue. The Red Company, led by Bassander Lin, Pallino, and Jinan, arrives to reinforce the *Schiavona* defenders. The Cielcin retreat toward the hold as the tide of the battle turns in favor of the human forces. Bassander orders the pursuit of Prince Aranata, who has fled toward the Garden.

Hadrian, Valka, and the Red Company veterans move forward to finish the battle. Pallino claps Hadrian on the arm, noting he looks like hell after the engagement.



### 73: Broken
The Red Company pursues the Cielcin into the burning Garden, where Kharn’s SOMs lie dormant. Bassander learns that Lieutenant Greenlaw is dead and realizes the hostage, Nobuta, is also dead. Cielcin scahari fighters ambush the group with grenades and nahute javelins, splitting the human forces. Hadrian saves Valka from three Cielcin attackers, killing one and allowing her to shoot another. Hadrian convinces Bassander that Prince Aranata is planning to destroy the Cielcin ship and everyone on it.

Hadrian, Valka, Pallino, and Crim break away from the main force to intercept the prince at the lakeside. Prince Aranata confronts Hadrian, wielding Raine Smythe's stolen blue highmatter sword. A duel ensues where Hadrian is severely outmatched by the prince's superior strength and speed. Aranata seizes Hadrian by the hair, slams him into a rock wall, and severs his right arm with a highmatter blade. The prince then decapitates Hadrian with his own sword, and Hadrian experiences the total darkness of death.

Valka and Bassander scream as they witness the execution from across the lakeside. Hadrian’s headless body topples like a tower as his vision darkens into nothingness.



### 74: Howling Dark
Hadrian's consciousness drifts in a "howling dark," feeling nameless and shapeless in a deep-water void. He hears his name thundering in the night and begins to regain memories of his life and his death. An image of his future self—adorned in black armor with a red and black cape—appears and points the way back. This future version of Hadrian wears a crest featuring a pitchfork and a pentacle within a laurel cartouche. Hadrian reaches for a star that turns into the piece of shell from his Vorgossos vision, opening a hole of light.

He wakes up in the shallow water of the lake, discovering that his head is reattached to his body. He realizes his left arm is missing, even though the prince had previously severed his right arm. A manifestation of the Quiet, appearing as Tor Gibson in green robes, stands on the water beside him. The Quiet informs Hadrian that he died but that they are "not done" with him yet. Hadrian is lifted to his feet by an invisible force as the spectral Gibson vanishes.

He struggles to sit up, feeling the intense pain of his mutilated arm in the cold stream. The Quiet’s final message "To exist" echoes through his mind as he stands alone in the Garden.



### 75: The Eleventh Hour
Valka, Pallino, and Crim defend themselves against Prince Aranata's remaining guard as Hadrian lies dead nearby. Aranata prepares to kill Valka with Smythe's sword, but Crim's throwing knife distracts the prince. Hadrian suddenly appears and decapitates Prince Aranata with his sword, ending the threat. Valka is shocked and weeping, having seen Hadrian's head previously crushed by the prince. Hadrian discovers he has his sword back, even though the prince was holding it seconds before.

The Demiurge begins to wake up as the SOMs activate and swarm the remaining Cielcin forces. Suzuha, now the new Kharn, appears in a golden robe and commands the SOMs to protect the humans. These machines tear the Cielcin limb from limb while leaving the Red Company soldiers untouched. Hadrian finds a severed right arm in the lake that matches his own armor, confirming a physical anomaly. He realizes that for a moment, two identical highmatter swords existed in the Garden.

He kicks the severed arm deeper into the lake to hide the evidence of the Quiet's intervention. Hadrian collapses into unconsciousness as Suzuha's forces eliminate the last of the Cielcin.
