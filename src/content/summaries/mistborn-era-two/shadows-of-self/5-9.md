### Chapter 5

Wayne awakes in Wax's mansion, notes the smell of morning bread, and steals a candelabra doily in exchange for a broken pocket watch. He enters Wax's study, where Wax is examining sketches of the crime scene to prove a Steelrunner's involvement. Wax deduces the killer bounced around the room to incite a firefight among the tense guests. Wayne refuses to visit the Terris Village to track the suspect, citing an obligatory monthly errand, and pockets a bottle of Wax's rum. In a different part of the city, Marasi buys a broadsheet highlighting Winsting's death and a possible cover-up by constables.

Meanwhile, Wax visits the Terris Village, traversing a primal forest within the city while wearing his guns and mistcoat. He speaks with his grandmother, Vwafendal, asking for a list of Steelrunners, and she names Idashwy. Wayne arrives at the Elendel University and bypasses two guards by paying gang leader Dims to create a distraction. He navigates past the female dormitory receptionist, Madam Penfor, by bribing her with an invitation to the governor’s party. He meets with Allriandre, hands her an envelope of money for killing her father, and is told to get out.

At the constabulary, Marasi observes Aradel organizing his men to manage a labor strike. Aradel assigns Marasi to observe the governor's upcoming speech and assess the crowd's reaction. He reveals that Marasi was hired as his assistant over Captain Reddi because of her promising application essays. Marasi then proceeds to her assignment, carrying copies of the broadsheets. Wax leaves the Village and flies toward the city, searching for his suspect.



### Chapter 6

Wax patrols the city from the air, dispersing a violent labor protest by landing amidst the picketers. He locates Wayne on a canal dock and asks about his errand. The two men then visit Idashwy's apartment in the Bournton District to question her. They kick open her door and find her body in the kitchen with a large hole in her chest. Wayne searches the apartment and discovers a cache of alcohol bottles in the bathroom.

At the Hub, Marasi secures a spot near the reporters to watch Governor Innate address the restless crowd. Innate defends his deceased brother Winsting and promises relief for flooded eastern regions. Marasi notices a guardsman acting erratically and screaming before pulling a revolver and leveling it at the governor. She creates an Allomantic speed bubble to trap the guardsman and grabs him by the legs. The assassin fires his weapon, but the bullet hits the perimeter of the speed bubble and deflects over the crowd.

In Idashwy's apartment, Wax shows Wayne a book written by the Lord Mistborn regarding Hemalurgy. Wax explains that Hemalurgy allows a killer to use a metal spike to steal another person's Feruchemical powers. Wayne searches the victim's pockets and finds a ledger sheet addressed to Wax. The paper bears the exact final words spoken by Bloody Tan, the mortician killer. Wax realizes the note is a message from the man who caused Lessie's death.



### Chapter 7

Wax and Wayne return to Ladrian Mansion, where Steris reminds Wax of their engagement to attend Lady ZoBell's party. Wax discovers the governor survived an assassination attempt and leaves the mansion with Wayne. Inside their carriage, Wax inserts a Hemalurgic earring and speaks directly with Harmony. Harmony reveals that the killer is a mad kandra named Bleeder, a Faceless Immortal who can mimic dead people using their bones. He explains that Bleeder removed one of her spikes to hide from his control and is using Hemalurgy to gain powers.

Wax and Wayne arrive at the constabulary and interrupt an interrogation session led by Captain Aradel and Captain Reddi. Wax observes the bound captive, Rian, and declares him a decoy used by Bleeder. He speaks with Rian, who claims a woman forced him to attack the governor. Wax uses a knife to slice open Rian's arm and extracts a coin hidden beneath the skin. Wax inspects the bloody coin and recognizes it as a message from Bleeder.

He abruptly leaves the interrogation room and asks Marasi if she has plans for the evening. Wax explains that the governor will attend Lady ZoBell's party despite the assassination attempt. He insists that they must also attend the party to protect the governor. Wax states that Bleeder intentionally left the decoy to mislead them. He guarantees that the kandra will strike at the party tonight.



### Chapter 8

Wax shows Steris the coin he pulled from the assassin and explains its history as a misprinted mint error. He recounts a memory of his Uncle Edwarn attempting to teach him about banking using the coin. In the memory, Waxillium gave the valuable coin to a poor laborer to save him from an exploitative loan. Wax notes that the laborer ultimately killed himself and his sons remained in debt to the bank. Steris and Wax ride a carriage toward ZoBell Tower but encounter a line of blocked traffic.

Wax unpacks a hooked steel spike and a rope sent to him by the gunsmith Ranette. He gathers Steris in his arms and fires his gun at the cobblestones to launch them into the air. Wax uses Allomancy and the tethered spike to swing between balconies on the exterior of the skyscraper. He ascends the outside of the building while carrying Steris through the swirling mists. They land securely on the top-floor balcony of the tower.

A small group of partygoers watches in surprise as the couple touches down. Wax winds up the rope and tucks it into his coat as the guards allow them to pass. Steris straightens her dress and smooths her hair before taking his arm. Wax uses an Allomantic shove to push open the balcony doors. They walk through the doors and enter the party together.



### Chapter 9

Wayne and Marasi stand in the long line outside ZoBell Tower and wait to enter the party. A bouncer denies them entry because the party is refusing guests not specifically named on their invitations. Wayne creates a speed bubble and reads the guest list to locate an absent attendee he can impersonate. He decides to pose as Professor Hanlanaze, a reclusive scientist from outside the city. Wayne instructs Marasi to use an academic textbook to formulate scientific jargon for his disguise.

Inside the party, Wax pours brown powder into his water glass to mimic brandy and turns on his steel bubble. Steris removes her metal bracelet and drops it into her handbag to avoid interfering with his Allomancy. Wax deactivates his steel bubble after accidentally knocking a metal nugget off a pedestal. Steris successfully intercepts a conversation with Lord Stenet by abruptly bringing up his infidelity. She guides Wax through the crowd to meet with Governor Innate and Drim, his chief bodyguard.

Wayne wanders the party in an academic disguise while Marasi poses as his assistant. A man named Rame Maldor approaches Wayne and attempts to discuss the Higgens effect with the supposed professor. Marasi covertly feeds Wayne scientific terms to scare off the rival scientist. Wax and Steris converse with the governor before a woman named Milan approaches Wax. Suddenly, Wax hears a crude voice in his head from Bleeder communicating through his Hemalurgic earring.
