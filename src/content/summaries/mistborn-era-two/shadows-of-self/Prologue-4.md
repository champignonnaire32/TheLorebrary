### Prologue

Waxillium Ladrian leaves his teenage companion Wayne with his horse and enters a saloon in the Roughs to hunt an outlaw named Granite Joe. Waxillium speaks to the tall barkeep, offering to pay for information about Joe's location. The barkeep takes the money and directs Waxillium to a small, dusty side room. Inside the room, Waxillium meets Lessie, a young woman with a squarish face who is also hunting Granite Joe for the bounty. The barkeep betrays them by alerting the saloon's patrons, prompting the armed thugs to surround the side room.

Gunfire erupts through the window, forcing Waxillium and Lessie to seek cover on the floor. Waxillium uses his Allomantic ability as a Coinshot to Push on metal, spoiling the aim of the thugs as he and Lessie flee up the stairs. Lessie finds an empty room on the top floor and considers escaping out the window. Crossbow-wielding assassins appear on the stairs, indicating they know Waxillium is an Allomancer. Waxillium grabs Lessie, draws upon his Feruchemical ability to suddenly increase his weight, and crashes through the wooden floors down into the basement.

In the basement, they land on a table directly in front of Granite Joe, a blocky-faced man with blue-grey skin indicating koloss heritage, and his two bodyguards. Granite Joe draws his weapon, prompting Waxillium to Push himself sideways and shoot one bodyguard while disarming the other. Lessie points a hand-crossbow at Waxillium before turning and shooting Granite Joe in the neck, killing him. The thugs rush down the stairs, but Waxillium strikes a pose over the bodies, causing the frightened men to retreat. Waxillium and Lessie leave the saloon, with Lessie taking Joe's pocketbook to claim the bounty.



### Chapter 1

Seventeen years later in Elendel, Lord Winsting Innate stands on a balcony and uses Allomancy to Soothe the emotions of his gathered guests. Winsting returns inside to host a corrupt auction where crime lords and nobles bid for his political favor in the Senate. Flog, Winsting's bodyguard, identifies a tall blonde woman accompanying the smuggler Dowser as a potential spy. Winsting orders Flog to assassinate the suspected spy after the event concludes. A sudden and massive gunfight breaks out among the attendees in the main room.

Flog grabs Winsting and drags him down a dim stairwell toward a secure bunker in the basement. They pass a dying bodyguard before reaching a heavy vault door at the bottom of the stairs. Flog locks Winsting inside the saferoom and returns upstairs to investigate the source of the shooting. Winsting waits alone in the bunker, worrying about the political fallout for his brother, the governor. Flog eventually returns, knocks on the door, and announces that everyone upstairs is dead.

Winsting opens the vault door to let Flog inside the saferoom. Winsting sits heavily in his chair and asks his bodyguard for advice on how to cover their tracks. In response, an unknown pair of hands grabs Winsting from behind by the hair. The attacker pulls Winsting's head back and efficiently slits his throat. Winsting bleeds to death in his chair inside the saferoom.



### Chapter 2

Waxillium sits in his study reading a small journal while his blonde-haired fiancée, Steris, plans their wedding. Steris details a list of Waxillium's enemies she intends to invite, anticipating a disruption during the ceremony. Wayne bursts into the room and announces that a criminal named the Marksman has robbed a bank. Waxillium arms himself and leaves the mansion with Wayne to chase the thief. They enter a motorcar, a gasoline-powered vehicle with rubber wheels, driven by Marasi, a young constable.

Marasi steers the motorcar toward the industrial district while Waxillium spots the Marksman leaping across rooftops. Waxillium exits the moving vehicle and uses his Coinshot abilities to follow the Marksman through the air. The Marksman throws coins to disrupt traffic, forcing Waxillium to temporarily land on a motorcar to prevent it from crashing into a canal. Waxillium catches up and crashes through a skyscraper window, firing a bullet through an interior wall to disarm the Marksman. The thief drops his submachine gun and flees down a hallway toward an open window.

Waxillium chases the Marksman out the window and follows him as he plummets toward the street. The Marksman cushions his fall and bounds away toward the Breakouts, a local slum where criminals find refuge. Waxillium prepares to shoot the Marksman in the leg to stop his escape. During the pursuit, Waxillium spots a familiar face in the crowd below and loses control of his Allomancy. Waxillium crashes into the street after recognizing Bloody Tan, the man who murdered Lessie.



### Chapter 3

Wayne, Waxillium, and Marasi enter the Breakouts in search of the injured Marksman. Wayne separates from the group and adopts a local accent to blend in with the slum residents. He shares whiskey with an old man and trades for a cotton cap to complete his disguise. Wayne sits with a group of men eating fruit and claims there is a large bounty on the Marksman. After the group disperses, Wayne trails a bald man through the alleys to a hidden building.

Wayne spies through the shutters and hears the bald man attempting to extort the Marksman for money. Wayne uses his bendalloy Allomancy to create a speed bubble, accelerating time for himself within a localized area. He crashes through the shutters and punches the Marksman in the jaw, knocking him unconscious before the others can react. Wayne tosses the unconscious Marksman out the window, exits the building, and carries the thief back to the main street. Waxillium and Marasi fail to extract information from the locals and reunite with Wayne.

As Waxillium prepares to transport the Marksman, an unseen sniper fires a crossbow bolt from a rooftop. The projectile strikes the Marksman in the head, killing him instantly in the middle of the road. Waxillium uses his Allomancy to fly up to the rooftops and pursue the sniper. A ragged man holding a crossbow emerges from an alleyway and grabs Marasi, pressing a glass dagger to her throat. Marasi draws a single-shot pistol from her handbag, places the barrel against the attacker's chin, and pulls the trigger.



### Chapter 4

Waxillium lands near Marasi and hands her a handkerchief to wipe the blood from her face. Wayne retrieves a crossbow bolt from the alleyway while other constables arrive to secure the area. Captain Reddi approaches Waxillium to report a political disaster involving the governor's brother. Waxillium leaves in a carriage with Reddi, prompting Marasi to follow them in her motorcar. They arrive at a fashionable mansion where two constables guard the front entrance.

Waxillium and Marasi enter the grand ballroom and find the floor littered with nearly thirty corpses. Constable-General Aradel informs them that the dead include numerous crime lords and high-ranking criminal figures. Waxillium examines the bodies and determines that the firefight was a chaotic melee rather than a coordinated external ambush. Aradel explains that Lord Winsting Innate hosted the gathering and is currently dead in the basement saferoom. Waxillium leads the group down a narrow stairwell into the basement to inspect Winsting's body.

Outside the saferoom, they find four dead guards who were shot in the head from behind. Waxillium deduces that the killer was a Steelrunner, a Feruchemist who stores physical speed, allowing them to execute the guards instantly. Inside the saferoom, they discover Winsting slumped in a chair with his throat slit and his tongue removed. Waxillium concludes that Winsting willingly let the killer into the saferoom, meaning the assassin was likely an associate. Waxillium warns Aradel that this massacre is a direct challenge from someone seeking to topple the city's power structure.
