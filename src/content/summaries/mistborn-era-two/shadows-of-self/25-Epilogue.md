### Chapter 25

Wax holds a gun on Bleeder, who reveals she was always Paalm the kandra and never the human Lessie. She explains that she used Tan's body to hide her identity and insists she genuinely wanted to be Lessie. Meanwhile, Marasi and Reddi lead a squad of constables into a misty alleyway to intercept a suspicious carriage. The constables clash violently with a group of Set thugs guarding the vehicle. Marasi crawls along the ground through the melee to reach the carriage undetected.

Marasi uses her Allomantic speed bubble to manipulate time and trick two guards into abandoning the vehicle. She enters the carriage and holds a pudgy Soother and a stylish Rioter at gunpoint. She threatens them with her pistol, forcing them to stop their manipulation of the crowd. High above on the bridge tower, Bleeder pushes Wax's gun away and shoots him in the leg. Wax falls from the tower and uses an Allomantic Push to slow his descent to the roadway below.

Bleeder lands on the empty bridge, illuminated by garish electric lights and a red glow from the city. Wax spots his gun, Vindication, resting near a gutter and leaps to retrieve it. Bleeder levels her own weapon at him but hesitates and refuses to fire. Wax aims Vindication and fires a special bullet forged from his Hemalurgic earring directly into Bleeder's forehead. The impact introduces a second spike into Bleeder's body, which allows Harmony to take control of her mind.



### Chapter 26

Marasi tows the captured Soother toward the rioting crowd and orders her to calm them. On the stage, MeLaan continues to impersonate Governor Innate while the crowd hurls objects at her. Constable-General Aradel steps up to the podium, points his pistol at the governor, and uses amplified voice cones to publicly arrest him. Aradel commands his constables to turn and face him, effectively siding with the citizens against the corrupt house lords. The crowd ceases its rioting and roars in agreement as Aradel hands MeLaan over to his corporals.

Marasi releases the Soother and instructs her to tell the Set to retreat. Meanwhile, Wax crosses the bridge, disarms Bleeder, and rolls her onto her back. Bleeder trembles violently and initiates her own death rather than submit to Harmony. Wax holds her bleeding body as she quotes an old joke, confirming she genuinely loved him during their time in the Roughs. After she dies, Wax screams into the night and waits on the empty bridge with her corpse.

TenSoon the kandra approaches on four legs in the form of a wolfhound and sits near Wax. He explains that Harmony sent Paalm to act as Wax's bodyguard because she was willing to break kandra prohibitions. TenSoon admits he was forbidden from telling Wax the truth about Bleeder's identity. He states that Harmony foresaw a disaster if Wax knew he was hunting the woman he loved. Wax closes his eyes and whispers that he has killed her again.



### Epilogue

Wax sits in silence at a victory party in Ladrian Mansion while Lord Harms and others celebrate the arrest of the corrupt house lords. Wayne approaches Wax on crutches and explains his plan to give the factory workers new pastimes before hobbling away. Wayne meets the daughter of Remmingtel Tarcsel, who eagerly discusses her plans to utilize electricity in the city. Marasi sits beside Wax and shows him a small, dark-red spotted spike recovered from Bleeder's body. Marasi places Wax's Hemalurgic earring on the table, but he refuses to look at it.

Marasi travels to the precinct offices and finds Aradel signing equipment-depreciation reports by candlelight. MeLaan arrives with a belt and asks for help tying a proper noose to fake the governor's suicide. Aradel agrees to cut her down in fifteen minutes and arranges to secretly cremate the bones she leaves behind. MeLaan tells Marasi that the strange spike is not made from any metal known to Harmony. She reveals that Harmony could not track Bleeder because the rogue kandra was being shielded by another god.

MeLaan slips away into the holding cells to perform her fake hanging. Marasi stands in the dark and remembers the name Trell, a god mentioned by Miles Hundredlives before his execution. She resolves to dig into the old records to uncover the true identity of Trell. Back at the mansion, Steris places a new log on the dying fire and sits on a footstool next to Wax. Wax turns to the side, rests his head on Steris's shoulder, and weeps.
