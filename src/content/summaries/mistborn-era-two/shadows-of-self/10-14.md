### Chapter 10

Wayne is approached by a young girl named Remmingtel Tarcsel who slaps him for supposedly stealing her father's incandescent light designs while in his disguise. The girl accuses Wayne, acting as the professor Hanlanaze, of causing her father's destitute death before storming away. Wax uses his Allomantic steelsight at the party to detect metal lines and is mentally contacted by the rogue kandra Bleeder. Bleeder taunts Wax about Harmony's control and reveals her plan to eventually kill the governor and Wax's father. Wax spots a server who he thinks is Bleeder and chases the man after he flees.

Wayne tackles the fleeing server while wearing a false beard, causing the governor's guards to pile on top of him. Wax spots a different man in a waiter's outfit moving toward the outer wall and takes aim with his gun. Bleeder speaks into Wax's mind again, taunting him about his dead lover Lessie. Wax fires his gun, but Bleeder dodges the bullet and shatters a window with a chair. The kandra jumps out of the shattered window, plummeting over twenty stories to the ground below.

Wax rushes to the broken window but stops Wayne from jumping out after the fleeing assassin. Believing the jump might be a distraction like the earlier attack, Wax orders Wayne to stay and guard the governor. Wax ignores Wayne's protests, shaking off the man's grip on his arm. Refusing to let his prey escape, Wax throws himself out the window into the misty night. He begins his pursuit of Bleeder through the city's darkened streets.



### Chapter 11

Wax falls through the mists and uses his Allomancy to safely land outside the building lobby. After questioning bystanders, he spots a fleeing motorcar and pursues it across the rooftops using his Steelpushes. Wax crushes the motorcar from above but discovers the driver is just a frightened cabbie who claims his passenger jumped out earlier. Wax learns the passenger exited at Tage Street and heads into a maze of industrial alleyways to search. While creeping through the mists, Wax is ambushed by a dozen heavily armed men who open fire on him.

A bullet grazes Wax's arm as he ducks behind a brick wall for cover. The attackers use aluminum bullets, making them immune to his Allomancy and proving Bleeder's deep financial backing. A tall woman from the party suddenly appears beside Wax, revealing herself as MeLaan, a kandra sent by Harmony to help. MeLaan pulls a handgun from a cavity in her transparent chest and prepares to assist Wax against the encroaching gunmen. The two allies split the targets, with Wax throwing a bullet casing to draw fire before attacking the men behind him.

Wax unleashes a massive Steelpush, sending thugs and weapons flying away in the mists. He drops his guns and uses a spiked rope to brutally disarm and incapacitate the remaining attackers in close-quarters combat. MeLaan kills the last standing thug by stabbing him in the back with a knife. Wax tells MeLaan to split up and instructs her to use the passphrase "all yellow pants" if they meet again. Fearing for Steris's father, Wax dives into the canal to evade snipers and swims to safety before heading to the Harms mansion.



### Chapter 12

Wayne questions the serving staff at ZoBell Tower and determines the fleeing server was not the kandra but a different type of mole. He punches a smug guard upon learning that Governor Innate and Drim have already been taken to a secure location without him. Wax races across the city using Allomancy and bursts into the Harms mansion to rescue Steris's father. He finds Lord Harms safe and hides him at the top of Feder Tower to keep him away from Bleeder. Wax tracks down Governor Innate's decoy carriage and forces his way inside to confront the politician.

Wax warns the governor about the kandra threat and gives him a gun for protection. He successfully convinces the skeptical Innate to let him review security protocols and establishes passphrases to verify their identities. A terrified servant interrupts their conversation to announce that Father Bin, a Survivorist priest, has been murdered. Marasi investigates the church crime scene and finds the priest nailed to a wall with spikes through his eyes. Conventicalists inform Marasi that the murder was committed by a man disguised as a Pathian priest who spouted blasphemy before revealing the corpse.

Marasi suggests to Captain Aradel that Bleeder is orchestrating the strikes and murders to break the city's government. She also confidentially informs Aradel that she has statistical proof of the governor's corruption. Wax arrives at the church, examines the spikes in the priest's eyes, and realizes they are not Hemalurgic. Wax deduces that Bleeder is attempting to incite a religious war between Survivorists and Pathians. Realizing they need more information about kandra motives, Wax invites Marasi to join him for an interview with MeLaan.



### Chapter 13

Wayne visits a Survivorist temple where he overhears working-class patrons angrily complaining about the city's economic inequality. He successfully averts a potential riot by taking over the altar and mixing unique drinks for the disgruntled worshippers. Wayne serves a blue-tinted drink to MeLaan, who playfully shifts her skin color to match the beverage. She correctly recites Wax's passphrase to Wayne, proving her identity as Harmony's promised kandra helper. Wax and Marasi arrive at the pub shortly after to find MeLaan and Wayne engaged in a belching contest.

MeLaan reveals her true crystal skull to the group and explains that she prefers maintaining a consistent body. She informs Wax that he must remove Bleeder's single remaining Hemalurgic spike to revert her into a primal mistwraith. Wax learns that Bleeder, also known as Paalm, was originally a highly trained assassin for the Lord Ruler. MeLaan provides Wax with two specialized syringes capable of temporarily turning a kandra's skin clear. She warns Wax that the serum in the syringes is lethal to humans and must only be used if he is certain of the target's identity.

Wax refuses to back down from his intent to kill Bleeder if necessary, angering the pacifistic MeLaan. Wayne playfully diffuses the tension by showing off his stacked coaster sculpture. Wax orders his team to split up and keep the governor under constant surveillance. Marasi departs to investigate a lead at the precinct while Wayne heads directly to the governor's mansion. Wax exchanges unique passphrases with MeLaan before setting off into the mists to track down Bleeder's escape carriage.



### Chapter 14

Wax traverses the city's rooftops and overhears a mob attacking a small Pathian sanctuary. He lands in front of the building with his gun drawn, instantly scattering the rioters into the mists. Wax advises the Terris missionary inside to flee to the Village for safety because of the recent Survivorist murder. He chases down one of the rioters and learns that two individuals disguised as Pathians incited the mob at a local pub. Wax realizes Bleeder likely has accomplices and continues his search for the silver-shielded carriage used in her escape.

He arrives at the Ashweather Carriage and Coach yard and flashes his badge to the proprietor, Cett. Wax demands records of all active drivers and discovers a single coach operating on an open-ended liberty fare. A young Allomancer employee bursts into the office, inadvertently revealing Cett's illegal use of Rioters to drum up business. Wax ignores the Allomantic infraction and demands the physical description of the missing carriage driver. Meanwhile, Marasi arrives at the constabulary precinct and is given the cold shoulder by the resentful records staff.

Marasi procures the case files regarding a recent dam failure from Constable Miklin despite his open disdain for her. She leads MeLaan to her desk outside Captain Aradel's office, refusing the kandra's offer to intimidate her bullies. Marasi explains to MeLaan that the precinct's lower ranks resent her because she skipped the traditional hierarchy due to her education. Examining the files, Marasi discovers that the executed saboteur responsible for the dam failure acted erratically and forgot his own children's names. MeLaan confirms that Bleeder likely impersonated the farmer, allowed herself to be executed, and then escaped the grave.
