### Chapter: Prologue

Sixteen-year-old Telsin and fifteen-year-old Waxillium (Asinthew) Ladrian sneak out of their Terris training hut in Elendel's Village. They meet with Kwashim, Idashwy, and Forch, the latter being a tall boy and a fellow Twinborn Coinshot. The group approaches the Synod's Lodge to listen in on Elder Vwafendal, Waxillium's grandmother. Telsin leads them through the Tin Gate, a stone archway etched with symbols, into the city, though Waxillium hesitates and turns back to the Lodge. Waxillium overhears a constable captain warning Vwafendal that Forch is a dangerous arsonist and leaves a bullet as a reminder.

Vwafendal discovers Waxillium spying and reprimands him, dismissing his evidence of Forch’s crimes as the acts of a misguided youth. Waxillium pockets the constable's bullet and returns to the dormitory, feeling isolated from the other Terris children. Later that night, Waxillium sees Forch carrying a sack through the rain and follows him to an abandoned three-story dormitory. Inside, Forch attacks Waxillium with a wooden post, then retreats into a room filled with candles and furniture. Waxillium discovers a five-year-old boy tied to a plank, surrounded by knives and trails of blood.

Forch Pushes nails at Waxillium using Allomancy, wounding his arm and knocking his metal flakes away. Forch dangles Waxillium out of a third-story window and drops him, but Waxillium Pushes on the bullet in his pocket to survive the fall. Waxillium returns to the room and Pushes the bullet toward Forch, who Pushes back, suspending the metal in midair. Waxillium identifies the bullet's three components and Pushes specifically on the primer, causing the casing to explode and the slug to kill Forch. Waxillium frees the child as the Terris elders arrive and realizes they will hate him for bringing violence into their sanctuary.



### Chapter 1

Twenty-eight years later, Waxillium and Wayne conduct a raid on a gang hideout in an old pub. Wayne infiltrates the gang disguised in a coachman’s hat while Waxillium acts as a decoy in his mistcoat, a garment with flared tassels. The lawmen subdue the criminals without fatalities, and Waxillium lets the constable captain take the prisoners into custody. Waxillium departs for his wedding, traveling through the Fourth Octant of Elendel via Steelpushing on streetlights and building frames. He reaches the Survivorist church, a building with a large glass and steel dome designed to view the mists.

Waxillium lands on a water tower across from the church to reflect on his grief over Lessie's death six months prior. He enters the church foyer and meets his fiancée, Steris Harms, who is wearing a fashionable white wedding dress. Steris reveals that she planned the morning raid to help manage Waxillium's public image as a ruffian from the Roughs. In a side chamber, Waxillium changes into a white wedding suit while his valet, Drewton, assists with the buttons and collar. Steris admits she made six sets of wedding pendants because she expects life to be unpredictable and full of disturbances.

Marasi Colms enters the room to announce that a man in a brown suit is demanding to see Waxillium. The visitor reveals himself as a kandra by making his skin translucent to show his bones. The kandra delivers the wedding pendants from Wayne and attempts to recruit Waxillium for a job on behalf of Harmony. Waxillium rejects the request and leads Steris to the altar, where the crowd waits amidst falling ash and rising smoke. As the ceremony begins, Waxillium notices the nearby water tower shifting and bending on its metal legs.



### Chapter 2

The water tower collapses, sending a wave of water through the church's glass dome. Waxillium Pushes off a button to launch himself and Steris away from the dais as the water crashes inside. After the flood, Waxillium assists the wet guests, including the constable-general, to their carriages. He returns to sit with Steris on the edge of the dais, where she is drenched and staring at the floor. Steris reveals she reserved a backup wedding date at a different church because she anticipated a disaster.

Marasi Colms investigates the church perimeter and notes that the bolts on the water tower were intentionally loosened. She finds Wayne in the kitchens across the street, disguised as a chef and scolding the staff. Wayne admits he hired "lads" to flood the church, though he originally intended only for a plumbing leak. Marasi confronts Wayne about the property damage, but he claims Waxillium will be secretly relieved the wedding was delayed. Wayne offers Marasi cooking sherry and then wine, encouraging her to move on from her unrequited feelings for Waxillium.

The kandra in the brown suit appears in the kitchen and identifies himself as VenDell. VenDell explains that his first choice for a mission rejected him, so he is now approaching Marasi. He asks Marasi what she knows about the nature of Investiture and Identity. Marasi feels her heart race as the ancient creature prepares to deliver a proposition. Wayne watches as the kandra turns his attention toward Marasi for a new endeavor.



### Chapter 3

Waxillium returns to Ladrian Mansion, a luxury estate he maintains with a minimal staff. He detects an intruder and Pushes on marble floor inlays to reach the second-story balcony with his gun, Vindication, drawn. He finds the kandra VenDell in his study alongside Wayne, MeLaan, and Marasi. VenDell uses an evanoscope, a device that projects large images with light, to show a chart of Feruchemical metals. The kandra explains that modern metallurgy has granted access to chromium, nicrosil, aluminum, and duralumin.

VenDell discusses "Identity," a Spiritual aspect that keys a Feruchemist’s Investiture specifically to their own soul. He proposes that storing Identity could create unkeyed metalminds usable by any individual. The kandra suggests it might be possible to create "The Bands of Mourning," artifacts that grant Allomancy and Feruchemy to anyone. Waxillium argues that creating such items would require a Full Feruchemist or a Mistborn, which are currently non-existent. VenDell reveals that a kandra named ReLuur was attacked in New Seran while searching for these legendary relics.

ReLuur is missing a spike and half his chest, causing him to lose his sanity and many of his memories. VenDell asks Marasi to travel to New Seran to recover the missing spike and any clues about the Bands. The kandra shows murals depicting a man holding a glowing spear and wearing the Bands of Mourning. Waxillium initially refuses to help, but VenDell shows a final image taken recently in New Seran. The picture reveals Waxillium's sister, Telsin, being held prisoner by two men in broad daylight.



### Chapter 4

Wayne searches Waxillium's mansion for valuables, questioning the logic behind the worth of gold and fish eggs. He trades a flower for a sock and checks his pockets for the silverware he took from the wedding breakfast. In the sitting room, Waxillium tells Steris that the man holding Telsin in the photo might be his uncle, Edwarn Ladrian. Waxillium decides to go to New Seran to find his uncle and free his sister under the cover of a political gathering. Steris insists on accompanying him to maintain social appearances and prevent their absence from looking suspicious.

Wayne enters the room and complains that VenDell, who has returned in a lighter tan suit, is being creepy. VenDell requests Wayne's skeleton for his collection, noting that Bloodmaker bones have distinctive joint oddities. The kandra provides a folder containing ReLuur's transcripts and a list of people involved with Waxillium's uncle. Waxillium asks VenDell to join the mission as an extra Metalborn, but the kandra refuses to go out into the field. VenDell explains that MeLaan will handle any necessary transformations, such as turning into a bunny.

VenDell notes that he honors the First Contract and finds the outside world too overwhelming. Wayne attempts to open the locked liquor cabinet but fails to find an offering for his personal rituals. Steris begins organizing her maids and valets for the journey, planning to meet at the train station in four hours. Waxillium agrees to the four-hour deadline and prepares to travel to New Seran on track seventeen. VenDell hands Waxillium a dull, bent earring from the old style as a final gift before departing.
