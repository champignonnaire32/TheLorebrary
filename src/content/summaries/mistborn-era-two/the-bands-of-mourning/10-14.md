### Chapter 10

Wax awakens on a cargo train arriving at the terraced, waterfall-filled city of New Seran. He grabs Marasi and uses his Steelpushing abilities to launch them from the moving train up to the highest city tier. While walking toward their hotel, Wax spots the red sigil of Ape Manton, a criminal from the Roughs, painted on a robbed mansion. Marasi suggests they investigate Manton, but Wax refuses and declares he is solely focused on finding his uncle, Mister Suit. The pair arrives at the Copper Gate hotel, checking into the penthouse suite and waiting for the rest of their crew.

Inside the suite, Wax forcefully breaks the latches on the painted-shut windows to create a viable escape route. Wayne attempts to barter his shoes for the elderly hotel owner's hat. Steris hands the bewildered hotel owner a detailed notebook outlining potential disaster scenarios, including explosions and shoot-outs. MeLaan, a shapeshifting kandra, interrupts the meeting multiple times to test out different bodies and bust sizes. After several attempts, MeLaan changes her skeleton entirely and assumes a muscular male body for the evening.

Marasi intercepts the hotel owner and orders her to acquire a list of all graveyard employees in the city. MeLaan declares she will infiltrate the party independently to provide backup for Wax. Wax assigns Wayne to accompany Marasi on her graveyard investigation to search for ReLuur's missing spike. Wax laments that he must attend the formal party while the others get to do the dirty work. The crew separates to sleep for a few hours before beginning their respective nighttime missions.



### Chapter 11

Wax dons a formal suit and rides in a carriage with Steris to a reception at Lady Kelesina Shores' mansion. Steris hands Wax a small notebook filled with tailored conversation starters and background information on the party guests. As they walk across the mansion grounds, a beggar with milky white eyes asks Wax for coins. Wax tosses the man a banknote, but the beggar hurls a strange coin back directly at Wax's head with perfect accuracy. Wax catches the coin and enters the mansion, where he is forced to surrender all his firearms to the doorkeeper.

Wayne and Marasi arrive at a local bank, where Wayne wears a top hat and loudly pretends to be a wealthy nobleman. Marasi displays her constable badge to a banker named Mr. Eriola and requests to see the deposit ledgers for local cemetery workers. The banker stalls until a clerk delivers a telegram confirming Marasi and Wayne's identities as legitimate constables. Marasi compares the ledgers against standard wages and discovers that every graveyard worker is depositing excess money. She concludes that all the cemetery workers are illegally grave robbing and selling valuables taken from the corpses.

Wayne distracts the banker by building a tower out of desk objects. He secretly steals a silver letter opener and a long rat's tail from the man's desk drawer. Marasi leaves a bottle of brandy on the desk and informs the banker she is not arresting anyone. She explains that she only needed the ledgers to identify which grave robber she should hire for her own investigation. Marasi and Wayne depart the bank together to seek out the corrupt cemetery workers.



### Chapter 12

Inside the ballroom, Wax orders drinks and accidentally pays the bartender with the coin the beggar threw at him. The bartender returns it, and Wax realizes the coin is stamped with the same symbols ReLuur found in the ancient temple. He shows the coin to Steris, noting it depicts a face with a spike driven through one eye. Following Steris's plan, Wax approaches Lord Gave Entrone to fish for information regarding the Bands of Mourning. Gave dodges Wax's questions and intentionally insults Wax's past relationship, prompting Wax to angrily leave the conversation.

Wax dances with Lady Demoux, but a dark-skinned Terris woman suddenly cuts in and takes over the dance. The woman asks Wax highly specific questions about how his weight-altering powers interact with his momentum while flying. She hands Wax a blank card with an Elendel address, asks him to experiment further, and leaves the dance floor. Wax tries to track the strange woman down, but the mansion's doorkeeper claims no one matching her description was admitted. Wax returns to Steris, who points out their next target: an informant named Devlin Airs.

Marasi and Wayne arrive at the Seran New District Cemetery, passing beneath a massive statue of the Survivor. Wayne alters his clothing and cuts up Marasi's dress to make them both look like unkempt vagrants. They approach Dechamp, the night gravekeeper, and Wayne uses a thick accent to propose a business transaction. Wayne offers Dechamp money to dig up the unmarked graves of victims from a recent explosion in the city. Dechamp accepts the cash, leads them into the mists to a fresh plot, and begins digging.



### Chapter 13

Wax approaches the informant Devlin Airs, who charges him a hundred notes to answer questions. Devlin reveals that a massive, secret building project involving Allomancers is occurring northeast of the city. The informant warns Wax to drop his investigation, claiming that the nobility is preparing to start a civil war. Wax tracks down the party's host, Lady Kelesina Shores, and intentionally shows her the mysterious spiked-eye coin. He plays dumb and loudly claims the coin is just a fake souvenir related to the secret northeast building project.

Visibly panicked by the coin, Kelesina abruptly excuses herself from the conversation and flees the ballroom. Wax tells Steris that Kelesina is likely going to contact Mister Suit, and they need a distraction so they can follow her. Steris immediately downs a vial of syrup of ipecac and vomits all over their table to create a scene. In the cemetery, Marasi wanders through the pauper section and finds an old, black marble statue of the Ascendant Warrior. She returns to the gravesite just as Dechamp finishes unearthing the burial plot.

Dechamp stops digging and pulls out a flask to take a drink of alcohol. Wayne pulls a sandwich from his pocket and takes a bite. Wayne suddenly freezes, grabs Marasi by the arm, and hurls her down into the open grave. He leaps into the shallow dirt hole immediately after her, knocking the wind out of her. A barrage of heavy gunfire erupts from the mists, raining bullets down upon their location.



### Chapter 14

Wayne creates a bendalloy speed bubble around himself and Marasi inside the grave, freezing the incoming bullets in midair. A speed bubble is an Allomantic ability that drastically accelerates time for anyone inside it relative to the outside world. Outside the bubble, Dechamp is struck by the mercenaries' gunfire and falls to the ground bleeding. Wayne drops the bubble momentarily, allowing Marasi to fire two shots from her pistol into the mists. Before the attackers can return heavy fire, Wayne creates a second speed bubble near the back of the grave.

A stick of lit dynamite floats into the speed bubble, forcing Wayne and Marasi to scramble out of the hole. Wayne throws his hat into a neighboring, deeper grave and dives in after it with Marasi. The dynamite explodes and showers them with dirt, but Wayne climbs out and neutralizes the gunmen with his dueling canes. At the mansion, Wax and Steris wait in a guest bedroom while the staff cleans up Steris's manufactured mess. Steris hikes up her dress and reveals she secretly taped a six-shot handgun to her thigh.

Wax peels the tape off her leg, retrieves the weapon, and leads her out into the empty hallway. They sneak through the mansion's central atrium, which features a diverted river running directly through an indoor garden. Wax spots a second-floor balcony and uses his Allomancy to jump up to it to scout ahead. Back at the cemetery, local constables arrest the surviving gunmen while Wayne loots Dechamp's hidden alcohol stash inside the gravekeeper's hut. Marasi finds the graveyard's master ledger and discovers a note instructing workers to report anyone investigating the kandra's plot directly to the manager.
