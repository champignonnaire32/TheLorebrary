### Chapter 30

Waxillium uses the power of the Bands of Mourning to pull the rogue Set airship down from the sky. Marasi sits on the temple steps and drinks choc, a sweet Malwish beverage that is far less bitter than Northern chocolate. Captain Jordis and her surviving crew emerge from the temple and demand the return of their ship and the legendary Bands. Steris interrupts the confrontation to propose a diplomatic solution involving trade agreements and technological exchange. The Malwish agree to relinquish the Bands to the kandra MeLaan for impartial safekeeping in exchange for their ship and new trading partners.

MeLaan makes the Bands of Mourning vanish into her shapeshifting flesh to ensure no government can use the weapon for conquest. Waxillium and Marasi search the snowy field for Telsin’s corpse but find only a pattern of frozen blood. Telsin utilized her Feruchemical healing to survive her gunshot wounds and stole the group’s skimmer to escape during the chaos. Waxillium realizes that his sister is still alive and active within the Set’s hierarchy. He decides that he must continue his pursuit of Telsin rather than mourning her supposed death.

The Southern people agree to transport the prisoners and Waxillium’s team back toward the city of Elendel. Waxillium says a final farewell to the statue of the Lord Ruler, who he now understands more clearly after holding his power. Marasi keeps a single spike recovered from the kandra ReLuur safely tucked in her pocket for the return journey. The group prepares to face the political fallout of giving away the most powerful artifact in history. Waxillium accepts that while the Bands are mostly drained, they remain a sublime and devastating threat to the world.



### Chapter 31

The Malwish airship flies smoothly toward Elendel as Wayne and Waxillium observe the city lights from a cabin window. Wayne explains that he shot Telsin because he was furious, though he intentionally left her metalminds so she could heal and escape. He claims that he stayed his hand from killing her as a final tribute to Waxillium's own merciful nature. Waxillium finds Steris sleeping in her bunk and thanks her for her invaluable assistance throughout the mission. Steris admits she felt like luggage for most of the trip, but Waxillium insists her presence and negotiations were essential.

The airship approaches Ahlstrom Tower, a massive skyscraper in central Elendel with a flat roof suitable for landing. Captain Jordis lands the vessel with precision, intending to take on supplies and depart for the South immediately. Allik removes his mask to say goodbye to Marasi and gifts her the broken wooden mask he had been wearing. He promises to return to the city in the future, provided that Marasi has more chocolate waiting for him. The five companions relinquish their borrowed Malwish medallions to Jordis as they debark onto the rooftop.

Governor Aradel meets the group on the tower roof and expresses shock at the size and technology of the foreign airship. Steris informs the governor that she has already secured initial trade treaties that should prevent immediate conflict with the South. Waxillium leads Steris into the building’s penthouse, where he has arranged for a Survivorist priest to be waiting. He proposes a real marriage to Steris, asking her to be his bride because he loves her rather than for a legal contract. Steris accepts his proposal with tears of joy, and the two are married immediately in a private ceremony.



### Epilogue

Marasi works late at the constabulary headquarters researching the Set, the god Trell, and the presence of red-eyed men. Edwarn Ladrian, also known as Suit, remains in a prison cell and refuses to reveal the Set's deepest secrets to his captors. A Faceless Immortal—an agent of Trell with glowing red eyes—enters Suit's cell to discuss the timeline of their plan. The Immortal explains that the Set is no longer necessary because Trell has decided to remove all life from the planet. The creature detonates a powerful explosive device that kills both Suit and itself, leaving no evidence behind.

Waxillium wakes up in the tower penthouse next to Steris and reflects on his deep affection for his new wife. He notices a small coin on his dressing table that had been given to him by a beggar in New Seran. Waxillium realizes the coin is actually a coppermind, a Feruchemical storage device for memories, made of nicrosil and copper. He taps the metalmind and is instantly pulled into a vision of a frozen, desolate wasteland. The memory shows a traveler with a spiked eye and scarred arms walking through a starving village of Southern people.

The traveler sees the people dying of cold because they have burned their homes and masks for warmth. He approaches the village elders and uses his powers to help the survivors in the wake of the world's restructuring. Waxillium sees the man's arm, which is covered in a dense network of horizontal scars. The traveler whispers the word Survive to the dying woman, revealing his identity as the Survivor, Kelsier. Waxillium drops the coin in shock as the memory ends and he realizes the Sovereign was not the Lord Ruler.
