### Chapter 5

Wax and Steris travel south on a train toward New Seran. Wax observes the high population density and electric lights in the outer cities of the Elendel Basin. A protest at a station against Elendel's economic oppression alerts Wax to political tensions. Steris explains how Elendel's hub system and tariffs unfairly tax the outer cities twice. Wax admits he has unknowingly voted for these tariffs while managing his house.

Steris attempts to research human reproduction using a textbook hidden in her luggage. Wax accidentally discovers the book, causing Steris extreme embarrassment over her lack of experience. They discuss the necessity of providing an heir to fulfill their marriage contract. Wax comforts Steris by emphasizing that their relationship is more than just a legal agreement. He demonstrates a new hook device, a handheld Allomantic tool that launches a tethered sphere with retractable spikes, sent by Ranette.

Wax uses the hook to explain his philosophy on how law provides boundaries for human freedom. Steris challenges Wax's hatred of politics by comparing it to the mystery of his hunts. She convinces him to help her audit the house ledgers to find a missing sum of money. They sit together and compare dates and payouts to identify accounting errors. Wax eventually finds a four-clip discrepancy caused by a miswritten digit from a dockmaster.



### Chapter 6

Marasi reviews documents from VenDell regarding the wounded kandra ReLuur. She studies a disturbing sketch of a red-faced creature with black voids for eyes and spikes on its head. ReLuur claims the beast fell from the sky and guarded the path to the Sovereign's bracers. Marasi consults a book by the Lord Mistborn about the dangers of Hemalurgic experimentation. She considers if the creature is a new creation of the Set or related to the god Trell.

Wayne joins Marasi in the dining car after mooching wine and accents from other passengers. Marasi expresses frustration about being in Wax's shadow and being hired only to keep an eye on him. Wayne tells a rambling and irrelevant story about a man named Chip to distract her. MeLaan appears in a fine lace gown, having sculpted her kandra body to fit the outfit perfectly. Marasi finds a Pathian earring and a note from Wax intended for her in the research folder.

Marasi enters Wax and Steris's room to find them still obsessively auditing ledgers. Wax proudly announces he has found the missing clips by identifying a late reimbursement request. Marasi hands over the kandra research and tells them to look at the monster drawings. She heads back to her room as the train begins to slow down unexpectedly. An armed man appears at the end of the corridor and fires a gun at Marasi.



### Chapter 7

Wax senses the train slowing and uses a steel Push to deflect a bullet fired at him. He launches into the hallway and kills the gunman with his pistol, Vindication. Wax discovers their private car has been uncoupled and is being chased by bandits on horseback. He realizes this is a full-blown armed robbery rather than a targeted assassination attempt. Wax increases his weight and Pushes against the tracks to propel the uncoupled car back to the train.

The private car reconnects with the train, and Wax prepares to sweep the cars for bandits. Steris provides Wax with backup vials of steel flakes suspended in cod-liver oil. Wax discovers the bandits are targeting a secret payroll shipment in a disguised courier car. He finds several dead guards and is nearly blinded by a gas or chemical released in the car. A small explosion occurs as the bandits blow open the courier car's safe.

Wax is suddenly drained of his Allomantic reserves by a mysterious metal cube, a small device engraved with unknown symbols that negates Allomancy. A massive bandit Coinshot attacks Wax, forcing him into a hand-to-hand fight he cannot win. Wax escapes to the roof, retrieves his gunbelt, and kicks the brute back into the car. Steris appears on the roof and fires Ranette's massive shotgun at the bandit. The recoil of the weapon launches Steris off the side of the moving train.



### Chapter 8

Wax dives off the train after Steris and swallows his last vial of steel flakes. He grabs Steris in mid-air and Pushes off the sinking shotgun to hover above the river. They plunge into the water to retrieve the heavy shotgun from the river bottom. Wax uses Ranette's hook devices to swing them back onto the bridge supports. Steris admits she feels safer near Wax during gunfights than anywhere else.

Wax launches them both in a massive Allomantic arc to intercept the train on a lower switchback. They land on the coal tender and find a bandit holding a gun to the engineer. Wax kills the bandit by Pushing expended shotgun shells through the back of the engine cab. The engineer reveals the bandits are installing a dynamite device to decouple more cars. Wax leaves Steris in the engine and prepares for a final sweep of the train.

Marasi soothes panicked passengers while searching for Steris in the lower-class cars. She encounters a slender man with a cane who asks if she is a Metalborn. The man drops a small metal cube that emits a strange signal before he flees. Wax disarms the dynamite and uses Ranette's hook cord to yank the massive bandit off the train. Marasi uses a cadmium speed bubble to freeze the remaining bandits on top of the train.



### Chapter 9

Doctors at Ironstand station treat the wounded while constables arrest the surviving bandits. Wax searches the train for Wayne and MeLaan, fearing they were killed or incapacitated. He finds the pair hiding in a luggage compartment where they had been distracted by a romantic encounter. Marasi is furious at Wayne's negligence, but Wax is relieved they are alive. MeLaan admits she hasn't heard of any kandra lore involving Allomancy-negating devices.

The group identifies the bandits as the Nightstreet Gang, a group of known criminals from the outer cities. Wax suspects the man with the cane was the true leader and was not part of the local gang. Marasi presents the metal cube she found, which bears intricate and unknown carvings. MeLaan causes a stir by casually mentioning she once ate a sixteen-year-old girl to copy her body. Wax decides they must continue to New Seran on a freight train rather than hunting the escaped bandits.

Marasi notices Steris is performing needlework, a sign that she is deeply upset by the night's events. Wax sits with Steris and shares his history of feeling out of place in Elendel's high society. He explains that his blunt persona is a sincere attempt to be the man he admires from the Roughs. Steris tells Wax that he only shows pure joy when he is flying through the air with Allomancy. She falls asleep on his shoulder as they wait for the cargo train to arrive and take them south.
