### Chapter 70

Steris Ladrian organizes the evacuation of the Elendel docks alongside Governor Varlance. She assigns specific tasks to train conductors, crane operators, and construction workers based on her contingency notebooks. Eight Allomancers in nondescript clothing arrive and offer their assistance to the governor. One man in the group displays a red tattoo of a stylized eye on his hand. He informs Steris that her sister, Marasi, sent them to help with the crisis.

Steris orders the Allomancers to sink the cargo ships currently waiting in the bay. She explains that scuttled ships on the seafloor will create drag to slow a potential tsunami. The governor authorizes the mass destruction of private property to protect the city. The Allomancers launch into the air using Steelpushes to reach the vessels. Steris monitors the execution of her plans from a workstation on the road above the bay.

Workers move cargo bins into positions to block streets and redirect floodwaters. Construction crews lead citizens inland toward designated reinforced buildings. Steris instructs people to congregate on middle floors away from glass windows. The governor praises Steris for her exhaustive and brilliant disaster management skills. Steris experiences a sense of pride as she waits for the outcome of the bomb threat.



### Chapter 71

Waxillium and Wayne fight their way to the bridge of the rocket-propelled ship. They find four officers dead on the floor from self-inflicted gunshots. The ship's controls are wired directly into the harmonium bomb and locked on course for Elendel. Harmony informs the men that the vessel will detonate upon impact in twenty minutes. Wayne suggests using a speed bubble to isolate the electrical detonation signal from the barrels.

Harmony reveals that a vial Waxillium carries contains lerasium, a legendary metal that turns a person into a Mistborn. Wayne drinks the vial, burns the lerasium, and gains the ability to use all Allomantic metals. He uses a Steelpush to shove Waxillium off the ship and into the ocean to save him. Wayne consumes multiple pouches of bendalloy and prepares to burn duralumin, a metal that enhances Allomancy. Harmony appears to Wayne as a transparent Terris man and offers guidance for the final task.

Wayne creates a specialized speed bubble that encompasses the three bomb barrels but excludes the wall-mounted trigger. He burns duralumin to compress time so severely that electrical signals and light appear to freeze. Wayne pours water from a canteen into each harmonium barrel to trigger smaller, localized explosions. He Pushes the activated barrels out of the speed bubble before they can signal the main detonation. The speed bubble shatters, and Wayne is consumed by a massive eruption of red fire and light.



### Chapter 72

Waxillium struggles to stay afloat in the dark waters of the bay. A blinding flash of light and a massive shockwave erupt from the ship's location. Waxillium sees a vision of a Terris man standing on the water and dulling the waves. Debris from the destroyed vessel rains down into the choppy sea around him. The light fades, leaving Waxillium alone in the darkness with a broken leg.

Waxillium burns steel and pewter to maintain his body heat and stay conscious. He chokes on his emotions while whispering a farewell to his friend Wayne. Fatigue and the weight of his mistcoat begin to pull him beneath the surface. He fights to keep his head above the waves despite his injuries and exhaustion. A tiny lantern light appears through the mists and drifts toward his position.

A small boat motors up to Waxillium's location in the open water. Hoid, dressed in a coachman’s outfit with white gloves, stands up on the deck. He reaches out a hand to pull the drowning Lawman from the bay. Hoid refers to the boat as a carriage for Waxillium's transport. Waxillium is rescued from the sea following the successful destruction of the bomb.



### Chapter 73

A massive shockwave from the explosion hits Steris and the governor in central Elendel. The sound of the detonation causes their ears to ring and windows to rattle. A brilliant light blazes on the horizon before slowly fading into the mists. Governor Varlance celebrates the early detonation and the preservation of the city. Steris watches the distant point of light and hopes for Waxillium's survival.

Steris warns the governor that a tsunami will strike the coast imminently. They observe the water pulling back rapidly from the docks in advance of the wave. Steris notes that the dockside buildings will require extensive rebuilding in the coming weeks. She expresses relief that her evacuation plans moved the citizens to safer inland locations. The governor straightens his clothes and acknowledges their successful contribution to the defense.

Steris explains that there was no need to remain at the docks once her orders were issued. She describes the governor as an agreeable man who has finally taken control of his office. The afterimage of the blast remains burned into her vision as she looks at the sea. She says a silent prayer while gripping the silver spear pendant at her neck. The chapter ends with Steris waiting for confirmation of her husband's safety.



### Chapter 74

Wayne's spirit floats high above the planet and observes the curvature of the world. Harmony, appearing as a vast figure in multi-colored robes, hovers beside him. Harmony explains that Wayne's Investiture allows him to persist briefly before joining the Beyond. A red haze representing the god Autonomy withdraws from the planet following the failure of her avatar. Wayne gloats about saving the world and claims the title of the greatest constable in history.

Harmony reports that Marasi collapsed the portal and restored his vision of the future. He warns that other cosmic forces are still aligning against the planet in the future. Wayne asks if his actions have earned him final forgiveness for his past crimes. Harmony replies that Wayne no longer needs forgiveness and has become a different man. Wayne feels a stretching sensation pulling him away from the physical realm.

Harmony offers to answer one final question before Wayne passes into the afterlife. Wayne asks if he created the largest non-divine explosion in the history of the world. Harmony confirms that the blast was the biggest man-made detonation ever recorded. Wayne requests that Harmony inform Steris of his accomplishment before he departs. Wayne shakes Harmony's hand and dissolves into the stars and the wind.
