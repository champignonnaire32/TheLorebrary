### Chapter 65

Wayne and Wax engage their doubles, Dumad and Getruda, in a ballroom at the top of the tower. Wayne tackles the Coinshot Dumad while Wax fights the Bloodmaker Getruda, who uses speed bubbles and pewter-enhanced strength. Dumad explains that they are copies of Wax and Wayne created to prove their worth to the god Trell through conflict. Wayne discovers Dumad uses a special super-metal called duralumin to fuel powerful Allomantic Pushes that rip up floor staples. Wax and Wayne briefly swap weapons, with Wax using a dueling cane against Getruda and Wayne firing Vindication at Dumad.

Getruda attempts to emulate Wax's supposed enjoyment of pain by laughing through injuries, though Wax recognizes her performance is forced. Wayne realizes Dumad must replenish his steel after every super-Push by drinking from an aluminum flask in his inner coat pocket. Dumad manages to snap aluminum handcuffs onto one of Wayne's wrists before Pushing them both through a skylight and into the air. While dangling above the city, Wayne spots Telsin Ladrian standing next to a rocket-shaped weapon on the construction-filled rooftop. Wax uses a metal doorstop as a bludgeon to shatter Getruda's limbs, preventing her from closing the distance effectively.

Wax identifies the location of Getruda's healing metalmind and uses a hazekiller round to blast her arm off, stopping her regeneration. He then delivers a fatal bullet to Getruda's head with a surgical Steelpush after she refuses to surrender. Wayne pickpockets Dumad's aluminum flask and tosses it away, leaving the Coinshot unable to Push as they plummet toward the roof. They crash back through the skylight, where Dumad's body is broken by the fall while Wayne survives using his stored health. Wax puts on a Pathian earring to communicate with the god Harmony, preparing for the final confrontation with his sister Telsin.



### Chapter 66

Wax demands that Harmony intervene directly if the god Autonomy chooses to fight him on the rooftop. Harmony explains that Autonomy has Invested Telsin primarily for communication and planning rather than direct combat. Wax and Wayne soar through the mists and land on the roof to confront Telsin, whose eyes glow red with divine power. Telsin mocks Harmony's perceived weakness and claims that Wax was created as a sword to act when the god cannot. Wax realizes that the rocket is a decoy because the Set has been unable to master advanced ballistics despite years of experimentation.

Wax Pushes against the rocket, causing the hollow construction to collapse and confirm it is not the actual bomb. He uses his enhanced senses within the mists to spot a Pewternaut-class warship departing the Bilming docks for Elendel. Telsin reveals that the bomb was moved to the ship as a backup plan because they knew Wax would eventually find the tunnel. She explains that the weapon is rigged to detonate if the ship is struck or stopped, ensuring Elendel's destruction. Wax calculates that he cannot reach the ship in time with a standard Steelpush before it enters the city's blast radius.

Telsin expresses her long-standing hatred for Wax and her desire to Ascend as a new god over the ruins of Elendel. Harmony intervenes by briefly stunning Telsin and severing her connection to Autonomy, causing her to fall unconscious. The god warns Wax that Telsin's body is being sustained only by divine power and will die if Autonomy withdraws. Wax looks through the broken skylight at Dumad's corpse while considering how to intercept the moving warship. He prepares to make an impossible leap toward the ocean to stop the bomb before it reaches the capital.



### Chapter 67

Steris Ladrian supervises the evacuation of Elendel's octants using cargo trains while news of the senators' flight spreads through the city. The kandra TenSoon informs Steris that the politicians stole the Bands of Mourning, which appear to have been drained of power. Governor Varlance assists Steris in the evacuation, lending his authority to her orders and helping keep the crowds organized. Intelligence reports arrive from Bilming stating that a single high-speed warship has been launched toward the capital. Steris correctly identifies the warship as the actual delivery mechanism for the bomb Wax was sent to find.

Constable Reddi questions if the ship can be shelled, but Steris explains that detonating the weapon early would still be catastrophic. Governor Varlance panics and attempts to board an evacuation train, fearing that the city is already lost. Steris prevents the governor from leaving, insisting that her husband will find a way to stop the vessel. She uses her notebook to consult disaster projections for offshore earthquakes to prepare for potential secondary effects. Steris orders the evacuation of the regions nearest the bay in anticipation of a possible tsunami caused by a massive explosion.

The governor expresses doubt that Wax can reach the ship, which is already far out at sea. Steris maintains her composure and tells Varlance that he has the opportunity to stay and be a hero. She acknowledges that while some things cannot be planned for, Wax's arrival at his destination is a certainty. Varlance chooses to remain with Steris as the last evacuation trains depart the central station. The city waits in silence as the threat of the Pewternaut warship draws closer to the harbor.



### Chapter 68

Marasi Colms opens a heavy metal hatch in the city of Wayfarer to investigate an observation chamber. She discovers that the "window" showing a destroyed world of falling ash is actually a sophisticated projection system. Armal and the other former captives of the Set realize they were locked in to prevent them from discovering the truth. Marasi finds a small door leading outside that reveals the city is actually intact and glowing with electric lights. The prisoners stare at the real city in shock, realizing that seven years of their lives were stolen by the Set's lies.

Armal asks Marasi if she has used her seven years of freedom to show her children a world of life. Marasi considers her time with Allik and her career achievements within the Elendel Constabulary. She observes that the Ghostbloods' secrecy allowed the Set to hide the truth from the captives for years. Marasi watches as the mists swirl around the distant Shaw, which is illuminated by powerful spotlights. An explosion occurs at the top of the tower, causing the lights to go out and signaling a major event.

Marasi determines that the truth must no longer be treated as a commodity by secretive organizations. She prepares to lead the survivors out of the underground facility and into the real world. The soldiers of Wayfarer continue to hold their positions, waiting for further orders after the capture of Lord Entrone. Marasi keeps her focus on the distant tower, knowing that Wax and Wayne are likely involved in the explosion. She begins the process of dismantling the Set's remaining influence over the terrified population of Wayfarer.



### Chapter 69

A sailor named Wellid stands watch on the deck of the Pewternaut A16 as it steams toward Elendel under cover of darkness. Gabria, a senior sailor, reprimands Wellid for opening his lantern and potentially revealing the ship's position to the enemy. Wellid believes the mission is a standard delivery of a weapon, while the other sailors appear more dedicated to Trell's cause. Wax Ladrian and Wayne climb the hull of the warship after making an Allomantic leap assisted by a Hemalurgic duralumin spike. Wax is pierced by a metal spike between his ribs to gain the necessary duralumin for the massive Steelpush to the ship.

Telsin awakens on the Bilming rooftop and realizes Autonomy is withdrawing her divine power due to her failure. She watches from the edge of the building as the god's influence leeches away, causing her skin to turn grey. On the ship, Wax and Wayne intercept Wellid, who reveals the bomb is located in the munitions hold followed by red lines. Wellid panics and jumps overboard into the ocean, leaving Wax and Wayne to infiltrate the lower decks alone. The two men fight their way through the metal stairwells, using Steelpushes to navigate the levels and neutralize armed sailors.

Wax and Wayne enter the munitions hold and find three large barrel-shaped explosive devices rigged with complex wiring. Harmony explains that the bomb utilizes the direct transformation of matter into energy by splitting harmonium with trellium. The god warns that the weapon features a dead man's switch that will detonate all three barrels if one is improperly disarmed. Harmony suspects the blast could release enough power to vaporize multiple cities or set the atmosphere on fire. Wax and Wayne realize they cannot safely disarm the device and must instead hijack the ship to move it away from civilization.
