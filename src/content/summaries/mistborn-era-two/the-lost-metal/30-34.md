### Chapter 30

Wayne, Wax, Marasi, and Kim arrive at a modern apartment building to investigate Tobal Copper’s disappearance. The group finds the building manager, who grants them access to Copper's twenty-second-floor residence. Wax identifies and disables three tripwire grenades and a pressure plate left as traps by the Set. Marasi observes that the bookshelf is filled with brand-new volumes meant as red herrings to replace Copper’s stolen research. Wayne discovers a bullet hole and blood residue, concluding that Copper was shot in the back while sitting at his desk.

Wayne puts on a discarded painter's cap to better imagine the victim’s personality and habits. He identifies a smudged ink imprint on an envelope in the desk drawer. The imprint contains the numbers seven, forty-two, and thirteen. Kim identifies these as potential locker numbers or coordinates for a large train station. Wayne argues that Copper would not have hidden his knowledge in a locker but would have sought to publish it.

The group realizes the numbers match the address of the Sentinel of Truth, a sensationalist local newspaper. This newspaper is known for syndicating the fictionalized adventures of Allomancer Jak. Wax recognizes that the Set likely missed this connection because they do not understand the impulse to share information freely. Kim looks up the specific office location on Seventh Street to confirm the match. The team departs the apartment to find the newspaper office before the Set can intervene.



### Chapter 31

Steris enters the Elendel Senate assembly hall to take her husband’s seat during a period of intense political chaos. Local broadsheets report on the deaths of Elendel constables in Bilming, framing the event as an act of war. Steris delivers official authorization forms to Vice Governor Adawathwyn to formalize her status as the acting senator for House Ladrian. She attempts to present a letter warning of a bomb threat, but the Senate leadership ignores her concerns. Adawathwyn calls the room to order and demands an explanation for Waxillium’s unauthorized activities in Bilming.

Steris defends Waxillium by stating his mission was fully documented and legal under the governor’s authority. She proposes the formation of a Governor’s Select Council to handle the emergency with Bilming in a private setting. The Governor’s Select Council is a small commission of senators and officials granted concentrated power for a limited duration. The Senate votes to approve the council, but the governor pointedly excludes Steris from the list of participants. Admiral Daal, the Malwish ambassador who wears a wooden mask with red lines, bribes his way onto the council by offering his personal airship.

Steris is left on the Senate floor, feeling humiliated by the governor’s public rejection of her expertise. Adawathwyn dismisses Steris’s warnings about a world-ending bomb as nonsense and house-specific paranoia. The governor tells Steris that the situation requires delicacy and tact rather than her blunt approach. Steris watches as the council members and the Malwish admiral retreat to the governor’s private chambers. Despite her embarrassment, she refuses to leave and begins formulating a new plan to force her way into the meeting.



### Chapter 32

Wax, Wayne, Marasi, and Kim find the offices of the Sentinel of Truth located in an old wooden fishing shack. The building is padlocked and appears dark, as the paper has only been publishing sporadically. Wax prepares to smash a window to enter, but Marasi stops him to insist on following proper legal procedure. Wayne leaves the group to buy chouta, which is a street food wrap consisting of savory meat and grease. Marasi and Kim wait on a nearby park bench while Wax stands guard by a tree.

Wax hears movement inside the attic and uses his steel Allomancy to detect blue metal lines indicating a gun. He Pushes on the nails in the roof to create a defensive steel bubble, which is a field that deflects metallic projectiles. Realizing the gun is trembling in the hands of a non-combatant, Wax identifies himself as a constable and calls for peace. A disheveled woman named Maraga Dulcet opens the shutters and lowers her rifle. Maraga identifies Wax as "Dawnshot" and mistakenly believes he is a close friend of the fictional Allomancer Jak.

Maraga explains that she has been in hiding because she fears the Set will kill her as they did Tobal. She unlocks numerous chains and padlocks to lead the group into the musty, book-filled interior of the shack. Wayne encourages Maraga’s belief in their friendship with Jak to gain her trust. Maraga leads the team past a printing press and down a set of hidden stairs into a basement. The basement walls are covered in sheets of tin that have been scratched with detailed diagrams and narrative notes.



### Chapter 33

Marasi explores the basement and finds that Maraga and Tobal have spent four years documenting the Set’s conspiracy on metal plates. The records describe the Set's research into rockets, which are self-propelled explosive shells designed for long-range bombardment. Maraga admits she initially thought Tobal was a crackpot but later realized his stolen evidence was authentic. The notes reveal that the Set is surveying underground caverns to use as bunkers and headquarters for an upcoming cataclysm. Wayne discovers a box of vulgar sketches while the rest of the team analyzes the technical data.

The group finds a list of prominent Elendel senators who are allegedly working as agents for the Set. Wax remains skeptical of the list because some of the named individuals are his trusted personal friends. Maraga explains that the Set intended to detonate their weapon two weeks ago but faced technical delays with the rocket fuel. Moonlight, an operative working with Marasi, takes rubbings of the metal plates to preserve the information. Maraga presents an evanotype photo, which is a colored photograph, showing Elendel buried under a thick layer of orange ash.

The photograph confirms that the Set intends to recreate the atmospheric conditions of the pre-Ascension era. Marasi realizes the stakes of the investigation involve the potential total destruction of the Basin's population. The basement atmosphere becomes somber as the team processes the concrete evidence of the impending attack. Suddenly, the locks on the floor above are bypassed, and a single set of footsteps crosses the room. Telsin Ladrian, the leader of the Set and Wax’s sister, descends the stairs and mocks the group for their slow progress.



### Chapter 34

Telsin reveals that she is a Hemalurgist, an individual who uses metal spikes to steal and use magical attributes. She explains that she can store her physical pain in a metalmind, which is a storage device for Feruchemical energy. Telsin claims the group has exactly two days to stop the Set's plan, though Wax believes this is a calculated lie. Wayne creates a speed bubble, which is a localized field where time moves faster for those inside it. Wax observes that Telsin’s eyes glow with a faint red light, indicating she is influenced by the god Autonomy.

Wax decides to split the team so he can interrogate Telsin directly while Marasi investigates the underground base. Marasi intends to use Kim’s knowledge of city archives to find a secret entrance to the Set's cavern system. Wayne expresses his reluctance to leave Marasi’s side after six years of working together as a team. He leaves a trade of a stapler for an old bowler hat before preparing to follow Wax and Telsin. Wax instructs Marasi to broadcast any findings to Steris and Captain Reddi via the city's radio network.

Marasi realizes that splitting up allows her to question Moonlight about the larger cosmic secrets she is hiding. Telsin leads Wax and Wayne out of the newspaper office to begin their psychological confrontation. Wayne warns Marasi that Kim might be hiding her own secrets despite her apparent cooperation. Marasi stays behind in the basement with Maraga, Kim, and Moonlight to plan their infiltration of the bunkers. The narrative shifts focus to the impending deadline and the escalating threat of a bomb made of harmonium, a rare god-metal.
