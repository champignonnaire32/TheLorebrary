### Chapter 50

Marasi kicks in a laboratory door and throws a grenade to trap guards in a slowness bubble. She engages in hand-to-hand combat with a guard, eventually slamming his face into a counter to incapacitate him. Moonlight uses a Soulstamp to create a temporary door, pulling a guard through it to subdue him. Marasi shoots and kills a scientist who attempts to burn notebooks and evidence in a trash can. The team rescues political prisoners, including Pielle Fromed and the editor of the Seasons newspaper, from nearby cells.

Moonlight uses a stamp to recover information from the charred remains of the burned notebooks. TwinSoul explains that Autonomy’s specialized troops might arrive through a perpendicularity, a portal between worlds. The editor of the Seasons provides Marasi with a notebook detailing the existence of an army barracks called the Community. The group attempts to usher the weakened captives toward an exit through the tunnels. Their escape is blocked by two dozen Set soldiers who arrive to reinforce the building.

The soldiers take up defensive positions within the tunnel, forcing Marasi's group to retreat toward the laboratory. TwinSoul prepares for a major confrontation while Marasi and Moonlight look for an alternate escape route. The situation escalates from a stealthy infiltration into an open battle between the Set and the intruders. Marasi realizes that Entrone has been using political opposition members for Hemalurgic experimentation. The group faces the immediate threat of being overwhelmed by the superior number of armed soldiers.



### Chapter 51

TwinSoul uses a jar of pure Investiture to grow a massive fortification of roseite crystal in the tunnel. Moonlight explains that the stone floor is too thick for her stamps to create a downward exit. TwinSoul encases himself in a transparent roseite boulder that expands into a twelve-foot-tall stone behemoth. The construct features articulated joints and massive arms, with TwinSoul sitting cross-legged at its heart. He identifies himself as a Grand Aetherbound and warns the soldiers to stand down or be destroyed.

TwinSoul forms a colossal roseite mace and charges the Set soldiers, shaking the ground with every step. Marasi and Moonlight use the distraction to retreat deeper into the tunnel complex toward the Community. Moonlight explains that TwinSoul is nearly invincible as long as his supply of pure Investiture remains. They find signs of expansion where the Set has blasted out new sections of the rock. Marasi notes that Gave Entrone appeared hesitant about the invasion but felt powerless to resist.

The two women navigate through mining-lit tunnels while covering their glowing jars of Investiture to avoid detection. They hear sounds of something following them through the darkness and increase their pace to maintain their lead. Marasi hopes that stopping the army here will complement Wax’s efforts to stop the bomb in Elendel. The geography of the tunnels changes as they enter the blasted-out sections leading to the Set's hidden barracks. They reach a point where the natural stone vanishes, replaced by jagged, artificial excavations.



### Chapter 52

Wax and Wayne traverse a storm drain tunnel that exits at the ocean docks of Bilming. Wayne stores health in his goldmetalminds, resulting in visible bags under his eyes and a sickly appearance. They observe six large metal warships in the harbor, which Wax considers insignificant compared to the threat of the bomb. Gunfire erupts from a warehouse window, prompting Wax to increase his weight and Push the bullets back. Wax uses a massive Steelpush to rip a section of the warehouse wall free, allowing Wayne to enter.

Three trucks flee the warehouse as a convoy, leading Wax to pursue them through the city streets. Wax uses streetlamps and buildings as anchors for Steelpushes to gain incredible height and speed. He identifies aluminum automatic rifles being fired from the rear of the third truck in the line. Wax realizes the trucks on the highway are decoys and spots a fourth truck moving through side streets. He maneuvers through the city, using balconies and railings as springboards to intercept the true target.

Wax fires a hazekiller round into the rear of the fourth truck, shredding the door and revealing boxes of documents. He drops into a storm drain and Pushes off metal grates to accelerate beneath the street. Wax emerges from a manhole and increases his weight hundreds of times to create a solid anchor. He Pushes the truck with such force that its front end crumples and the rear slams into the pavement. Wax confirms that while the bomb is not present, the truck contains vital ledgers and ledgers.



### Chapter 53

Marasi and Moonlight are stalked by a Hemalurgic monstrosity, a twisted creature with four legs and a human-like face. They don lab coats as disguises to move through the tunnels while Gave Entrone’s voice broadcasts through speakers. Entrone announces he is at the Community and orders all workers and scientists to stay in their quarters. Marasi reaches a cavern bisected by a wooden wall with a hidden, artificial town built on the other side. The tracking monster, a spikehead mutation, enters the cavern and hoots to call for reinforcements.

Moonlight creates a temporary door in the structure, allowing them to hide in a room with one-way glass windows. Marasi observes a town with neat rows of houses and painted flowers, lit by artificial floodlights from above. Moonlight explains that she can use an Essence Mark to transform into a different person with different skills. This transformation requires pure Investiture and will rewrite Moonlight's history and personality for as long as it is maintained. Marasi suggests a plan where she escapes into the Community while Moonlight distracts the approaching soldiers.

Moonlight prepares her stamps and requests specific coordinates to facilitate her eventual escape from the room. She dips a stamp into the jar of Investiture to create a long-lasting door for Marasi in the rear wall. Marasi takes a rucksack with a pistol and a jar of light but leaves her rifle behind. Moonlight warns that her new persona might not remember Marasi or the original mission details. As soldiers begin to break through the front door, Moonlight applies the stamp to her wrist to begin the transformation.



### Chapter 54

Moonlight absorbs a full jar of pure Investiture, causing her skin to glow with a brilliant white fire. Her physical appearance changes as her hair shrinks into a luminous bob and her personality shifts entirely. She identifies herself as Shay-I, a divine being, and treats Marasi as a mortal requesting a boon. Marasi explains the situation, and Shay-I draws glowing lines in the air to magically reinforce the breaking door. Shay-I uses her powers to create a perfect illusory duplicate of Marasi to act as a decoy.

Shay-I instructs Marasi to flee through the door while she prepares to deal with the soldiers. The new persona demands proper offerings and piety in exchange for the "blessing" of the decoy and protection. Marasi hands over the burned notebooks and leather folio of stamps to Shay-I for safekeeping. She abandons her lab coat and rifle to blend in with the common workers in the secret town. The duplicate Marasi begins moving around the room as a distraction for the tracking monsters and troops.

Marasi pushes through the door into the Community just as the front wall of the room begins to buckle. She glimpses Shay-I drawing complex patterns of light to repel the invaders before the door vanishes. Marasi finds herself in a peaceful neighborhood that contrasts sharply with the violence in the tunnels. She moves away from the one-way windows to avoid being spotted by anyone inside the Community's houses. The chapter ends with Marasi hoping the transformed Moonlight will successfully execute the distraction as planned.
