### Chapter 10

Marasi Colms returns to the Fourth Octant Constabulary headquarters and receives applause from her fellow officers for her recent field success. She meets with Constable-General Reddi and promises to address a humorous caricature Wayne drew of another officer. Reddi informs Marasi that two mysterious men in bowler hats are searching for Wayne regarding significant financial debts. Marasi presents a notebook recovered from the Cycle, the leader of the criminal group she confronted in the sewers. She highlights shipping manifests that detail how the group tested Elendel's customs inspections to smuggle mundane goods into the city.

The recovered data confirms that the criminal organization known as the Set is smuggling weapons and explosives out of Elendel to Bilming. Marasi proposes a sting operation where they will send the intended shipment to Bilming to capture higher-level Set operatives. She suggests using a kandra—a shapeshifting immortal—to impersonate the deceased Cycle leader for the mission. Reddi expresses discomfort with involving the Faceless Immortals but ultimately grants Marasi authorization and potential extra funding. He confirms that most constables-general have agreed to Marasi's proposed policing reforms for the city slums.

Marasi provides Reddi with three Hemalurgic spikes for scientific study but retains a fourth thinner spike for herself. Reddi identifies the metal as trellium, an offworld substance linked to the god Trell that was previously used in a prison explosion. He warns her of the metal's danger and agrees to let Lord Waxillium Ladrian study the sample. Marasi leaves the office feeling satisfied with her professional standing before grabbing a quick meal in the commissary. Wayne arrives in a cleaning lady's disguise to avoid his creditors and confirms he will attend a meeting with Waxillium at three o'clock.



### Chapter 11

Waxillium and Steris arrive at Ladrian Mansion, where they maintain separate rooms for their extensive projects, ledgers, and hobbies. Waxillium stops to acknowledge his old mistcoat—a garment made of thick ribbons used for hiding in the mists—and Lessie’s hat. Allik, a Malwish man serving as a junior goodwill ambassador who wears a bright red mask, greets them with fresh pastries. MeLaan arrives in the body of a large grey-and-white dog before shifting into a tall, androgynous human form to talk with the group. Marasi and Wayne enter the mansion to deliver the trellium spike Marasi recovered from the Set member in the sewers.

Waxillium inspects the thin, needle-like spike and notes its silvery appearance with distinct reddish patches. The group discusses how the Set has circumvented Harmony's ability to control individuals who possess multiple Hemalurgic spikes. Marasi reveals that the Set was packing weapons and field rations for a shipment to the Outer City of Bilming. Waxillium finds a note on his desk containing an iron earring and a message from Harmony instructing him to make a second one. Marasi requests MeLaan's assistance in using the Cycle's corpse to trick the Set during an upcoming sting operation.

MeLaan reveals she cannot assist because Harmony has ordered her to leave Scadrial to explore the broader cosmere and other planets. She explains that the cosmere—the surrounding universe—is becoming increasingly aware of their world's existence. Allik provides the group with cinnamon puffs and hot chocolate while Marasi presents the Cycle’s notebook containing annotated shipping records. The shipping logs detail specific crate sizes and inspection rates for goods being brought into Elendel. Wayne declines a drink and leaves for an appointment, while Waxillium prepares his goggles to begin metallurgical experiments on the trellium spike.



### Chapter 12

Wayne attempts to avoid his internal pain by pretending to be various heroic figures from old stories and legends. MeLaan leads him into a private sitting room within Ladrian Mansion to discuss their relationship and her upcoming departure. She informs Wayne that she is leaving Scadrial today and that their six-year romantic connection has officially ended. Wayne expresses his heartbreak and asks if their time together meant nothing to her during his struggle to process the news. MeLaan admits she asked for her new mission because she realized she was leading him on and wanted to avoid further pain.

The mission requires MeLaan to travel through Shadesmar, the inversion of the physical world, to explore other planets in the cosmere. She tells Wayne that she will be the first kandra to embark on a long-term exploration mission of this scale. Wayne remains silent and accepts the breakup, feeling that he is being punished for his perceived worthlessness. MeLaan kisses him one last time and compliments him by stating he was the best partner she has ever known. She then walks out of the room to finalize her preparations and gather her favorite True Bodies for the journey.

Wayne sits alone in the room without his hat, confronting the familiar emotional pain he has carried since his childhood. He reflects on the feeling of being worthless and the weight of knowing his true self without any disguises. The departure of the immortal agent of God leaves him feeling abandoned and deeply isolated in the quiet mansion. He acknowledges that his life has been defined by the pain of his past actions and his constant need to pretend. This loss reinforces his self-loathing as he prepares to face the rest of his day without MeLaan.



### Chapter 13

Waxillium, Steris, and Marasi descend into the mansion's basement, which Waxillium has converted into a sophisticated metallurgy lab. The facility contains metal samples, acids, spectrometers, and a forge for testing the physical and chemical properties of various substances. Waxillium and Steris put on protective goggles and aprons to begin testing the trellium spike recovered from the sewers. Waxillium determines that the metal has a hardness rating above nine and a melting point exceeding twenty-five hundred degrees. The metal remains solid even when subjected to an electric melter that heats it to a white-hot state.

Steris notes that God Metals like trellium and harmonium likely change states based on factors other than simple temperature. Marasi reports that the Set member she killed raved about men of gold and red and the return of global ashfalls. Waxillium explains that the current tectonic state of the world makes natural ashfalls impossible without external, ancient interference. He uses a spectroscope to burn shavings of the metal and discovers it emits a full spectrum of light with anomalous readings in the red. This result confirms the substance is a God Metal, similar to the properties previously observed in harmonium samples.

The group moves to a reinforced "safe box" to test how the trellium shavings react to various acids and bases. Marasi discovers Waxillium’s research wall and realizes he has been trying to split harmonium into its base components, atium and lerasium. Lerasium is the mythical metal that originally created Mistborn, and Waxillium believes the Set is attempting to replicate its power. Waxillium admits that he is a hobbyist and intends to eventually turn the spike over to the university's professional scientists. Steris interrupts the conversation to point out that the trellium spike is starting to react physically to the presence of harmonium.



### Chapter 14

Wayne hides in an alleyway to evade the two accountants in bowler hats who are pursuing him through the financial district. He attempts to process his grief over MeLaan's departure by treating the day as a personal funeral for his own happiness. Dressed in a fancy jacket and a flower lapel, he heads to the Drunken Spur, a Roughs-themed restaurant on Feder Way. He meets his friends Ranette and her partner Jaxy, who are already seated in the upscale but poorly decorated establishment. Wayne rings a service bell repeatedly and orders the worst vodka available to help numb his emotional state.

Ranette and Jaxy attempt to comfort Wayne, though Ranette maintains her characteristic dry and blunt tone during the conversation. Wayne explains that MeLaan dumped him and expresses his deep sense of loss for someone who understood his need to wear disguises. Jaxy questions Wayne about his monthly visits to Alliandre, the daughter of the man Wayne murdered twenty years ago. She suggests that Wayne’s insistence on seeing her in person is a form of selfish penance that causes the girl more pain. Wayne realizes that he may be ignoring Alliandre’s wishes just to satisfy his own need for punishment and suffering.

The realization ruins Wayne's appetite, and he leaves the restaurant after exchanging his service bell for his hat and a cufflink. Outside the restaurant, he is immediately confronted by the two accountants he had been trying to avoid all day. They inform Wayne that he has too much money and that his lack of investment diversification is a financial crime. They force him into their car to take him to their accounting firm to discuss his massive, unmanaged wealth. Wayne feels that his identity as he knows it is effectively dead as he is driven away by the bean counters.
