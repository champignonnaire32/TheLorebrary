### Chapter: Prologue

Wayne lives in a one-window home in Tinweight Settlement with his mother, who works in the nearby mines following the death of his father and uncle. While waiting for her to return from work, Wayne practices card tricks and pretends to be other people by trying on the laundry he washes. He fears the mine because it has already claimed his male relatives and seems to be physically draining his mother’s health and vitality. When his mother returns, she catches him with a deck of cards and a stolen pocketknife, gently correcting his behavior by explaining the difficulty of doing what is right. To comfort him, she tells the story of Allomancer Jak and his battle against the bandit Blatant Barm and his sidekicks.

In the story, Allomancer Jak uses his abilities to fly and shoot his enemies through their own weapon sights. He overcomes various obstacles, including the River Human and the Canyon of Death, which is filled with a million snakes. Wayne contributes gruesome details to the narrative, imagining exploding heads and melting bones as the villains are defeated. The story concludes with Jak being swallowed by Blatant Barm, who has transformed into a giant plateau, only for Jak to choke the monster from the inside. Wayne's mother suggests that everyone must occasionally walk into the beast's mouth, comparing the lawman’s courage to her own work in the mine.

Wayne finds comfort in his mother's stories and her belief that he can become whoever he wants to be. She recites a poem comparing him to the wind and stars, reinforcing his sense of security despite their poverty. They share a simple meal of oatmeal and beans before Wayne falls asleep under his blankets. This sense of peace is shattered the following day when another collapse occurs at the mine. Wayne's mother does not return home that night, leaving him alone. The chapter ends by jumping forward twenty-nine years to the start of Part One.



### Chapter 1

Marasi Colms and Wayne are investigating a series of disappearances by trekking through the sewers of Elendel. Marasi wears a constable's uniform with trousers and high boots to navigate the filth, while Wayne complains about the stench and the presence of spiders. They have been working together as official partners for six years following Waxillium Ladrian's retirement. Wayne reveals that he is upset because the kandra MeLaan has informed him that she intends to break up with him soon. Marasi offers him emotional support while they follow a map toward an unmapped cavern located near new train tunnel construction.

The pair discovers a hidden metal door in the sewer wall that shows signs of recent and frequent use. Marasi uses a knife to undo the bar holding the door shut from the other side, revealing a rough-hewn tunnel leading deeper underground. She suspects the gang they are hunting is funded by the Set, a shadowy organization she has been tracking for years. The Set is linked to the worship of a dark figure named Trell, whose machinations Marasi hopes to uncover. They dim their lantern to avoid detection and proceed into the ancient tunnels that predate the Catacendre.

Inside the tunnel, Marasi reflects on the bureaucratic failures that allowed this site to remain uninvestigated for so long. She carries a semiautomatic rifle and extra metals, prepared for a potential encounter with Metalborn enforcers. Wayne holds the lantern and his dueling canes, though he also carries a handgun despite his historical aversion to firearms. They move silently, hoping to find the entrance to the gang's secret lair without being spotted by lookouts. The chapter concludes as they descend further into the depths of the city's underbelly.



### Chapter 2

Waxillium Ladrian prepares for a session in the Senate with the help of his wife, Steris, and their son, Max. Steris provides him with lists, evidence, and unloaded pistols to maintain his intimidating "Lawman Senator" persona. Wax intends to argue against the Elendel Supremacy Bill, which would grant the capital's governor power over the mayors of the Outer Cities. Tensions are high due to aggressive posturing from the Malwish nation on the Southern Continent and growing civil unrest within the Basin. Wax fears that passing the bill will provoke a civil war, which he believes is the secret goal of the Set.

During the Senate proceedings, Wax uses a Malwish weight-affecting medallion to demonstrate his loyalty and the reality of external threats. He presents letters from Outer City politicians who are willing to cooperate but fear Elendel's imperialistic policies. To discredit his opposition, Wax reveals a letter proving that Senator Melstrom has been bribed by Houses Hasting and Erikell. While this causes a scandal, Governor Varlance insists on proceeding with the vote after temporarily removing the corrupt senators. Wax makes a final emotional plea for unity and representation, drawing on his reputation as Dawnshot.

Despite Wax's efforts and the evidence of corruption, the Elendel Supremacy Bill passes with a vote of 122 to 118. Wax feels a sense of dread, realizing that his political maneuver has failed to prevent the looming conflict. He sits next to his ally, Yancey Yaceczko, contemplating the influence of the Set and their mysterious deity, Trell. The chapter includes a broadsheet excerpt featuring a story about Nicki Savage and news of the bill's passage. This highlights the deepening divide between Elendel and the Outer Cities, particularly the interests in Bilming.



### Chapter 3

Marasi and Wayne track footprints through the dusty underground tunnels, noting that the gang likely has an easier secondary exit. They encounter a vertical shaft and use a climbing rope to descend further into the darkness. During the descent, Wayne attempts to tell jokes about the "laws of physics" and women to distract from the tension. Marasi corrects his outdated humor and notes that he is becoming more like her father, Lord Harms. They reach the bottom and continue toward a faint light visible in the distance.

They discover two guards playing cards and sneak past them into a side tunnel that offers a view of a large cavern. This cavern serves as a smuggling base filled with thirty-seven armed people boxing military supplies and explosives. Marasi identifies a man in an upscale outfit as a potential "Cycle," a mid-level officer in the Set. She plans to gather backup and coordination, but Wayne argues for immediate action to prevent the gang from vanishing. Their presence is suddenly discovered when they stumble upon a guard who had stepped away from the main group.

Wayne quickly knocks out the guard, but his shouts alert the entire cavern to their presence. Marasi realizes they cannot escape back up the rope with the gang pursuing them. She decides to follow Wayne's suggestion and engage the smugglers directly to capture the Cycle. Wayne draws his dueling canes and prepares to use his Allomantic speed bubbles to manage the combat. Marasi readies her semiautomatic rifle, preparing to provide cover fire from their elevated position.



### Chapter 4

Marasi and Wayne initiate an assault on the warehouse cavern to prevent the smugglers and their Set leader from escaping. Wayne uses a speed bubble to flank a guard while Marasi provides covering fire with her semiautomatic Bastion rifle. She utilizes Allomantic grenades, a Malwish technology that she charges with her cadmium abilities to create bubbles of slowed time. These grenades trap eighteen gangsters in stasis, significantly reducing the number of active combatants. Wayne moves through the chamber in a blur, using his own speed bubbles to engage the remaining enemies with dueling canes.

Wayne reveals that he has been practicing fine control over his speed bubbles, allowing him to overlap and extract individuals from Marasi's slowness zones. Marasi is impressed by this development, as managing the size and placement of bubbles is a highly difficult Allomantic feat. She provides sniper support from an overlook, picking off gangsters as Wayne navigates the chaotic battlefield. They coordinate their movements, with Wayne focusing on the groups of enemies while Marasi tracks the Cycle. The sounds of gunfire are deafening within the enclosed stone cavern, echoing like drumsticks on metal.

As the fight intensifies, the Cycle attempts to escape through a reinforced metal door at the rear of the cavern. Wayne takes cover behind a crate of explosives and uses a flash-bang to distract the remaining eleven gangsters. Marasi hands him her last flash-bang before dashing after the Set officer, picking the lock on the metal door. An explosion rocks the cavern behind her as she slips through the doorway to pursue her target. She enters a dark tunnel, determined to capture the Cycle and finally gain concrete answers about the Set and Trell.
