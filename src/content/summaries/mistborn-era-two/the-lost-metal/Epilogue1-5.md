### Epilogue 1: Marasi

Marasi Colms returns to Elendel by train ten hours after the explosion in Bilming. She coordinates with the local constabulary to ensure the testimonies of the rescued politicians are used against the corrupt lord mayor. Her Malwish partner, Allik, meets her at the platform and delivers the news of Wayne’s death. Marasi struggles to accept that her partner is gone while she embraces Allik in the crowd. She retreats to her flat to eat and change back into her uniform before receiving a mysterious invitation.

A messenger girl delivers an envelope containing a card with the three interlocking red diamonds of the Ghostbloods. Marasi travels to a grand estate in Elendel where she meets Kelsier, a man with a single iron spike driven through his right eye. TwinSoul, an aether-bound warrior who can manifest crystalline armor, offers her membership in the secret organization. Kelsier explains that Moonlight has been transformed into a different person by an Elantrian soulstamp and needs rescue. The Survivor informs Marasi that Harmony is becoming erratic and that Scadrial is being targeted by powerful cosmere forces.

Marasi asks Kelsier if the Ghostbloods have shared their knowledge about the threat of Trell with Harmony. She realizes that joining the organization would require her to live a life of secrets and lies. Marasi officially rejects the offer, stating that her primary duty is to the public and the law. She leaves the Ghostblood card on a table and returns to the streets of Elendel. As she walks through the city, she begins formulating a new plan for her future in the government.



### Epilogue 2: Steris

Two days after the bomb detonation, Steris Harms brings Waxillium Ladrian home from the hospital. Waxillium uses crutches to move as he mourns the loss of Wayne on their skyscraper balcony. Steris consoles him by emphasizing that Wayne made his own choice to sacrifice himself for the city. She consults her personal notes on trauma and psychology to provide the correct emotional support. Waxillium realizes that he must allow Wayne to be a hero and accepts that his friend is truly gone.

Waxillium drops a bullet casing and uses Steelpushing to propel both himself and Steris into the air. They ascend to their penthouse suite where their children, Max and Tindwyl, greet them with excitement. Steris reads a quote from Wayne’s notebook to honor his memory during their flight. Inside the home, Waxillium kneels to hug his son despite his medical orders to stay off his leg. Max tells his father that he saved the world but finds the idea of "getting the girl" to be stupid.

A nanny delivers a letter from the governor that is sealed with the official city crest. Steris opens the letter and discovers that the governor has offered her a position as the Disaster Preparations Officer. This role grants her a seat on the city council and a dedicated task force to prepare for future catastrophes. Waxillium encourages her to accept the appointment and suggests they make an effort to find happiness together. Steris agrees and immediately begins drafting a new list of plans to ensure their family’s stability.



### Epilogue 3: Allriandre

Allriandre returns to her small tenement flat five days after the disaster, exhausted from her job at the metal forges. She finds her daughter Ruri terrified because the landlady, Miss Coussaint, is shouting about a toothpaste mess on the walls. Allriandre calms the child and worries about the late rent payment usually provided by Wayne. She plays with Ruri by painting on the walls with toothpaste to turn the mistake into a game. The landlady demands payment for recent renovations before leaving the room in a huff.

Two men in suits named Mister Call and Mister Daring arrive at the door representing an accounting firm. They inform Allriandre that Wayne died during the detonation of the bomb in Bilming. Allriandre feels a mixture of relief and sorrow upon hearing that the man who killed her father is dead. The accountants reveal that Wayne has named Allriandre as the primary beneficiary of his massive estate. They explain that Wayne invested his wealth in aluminum, electricity, and industrial fabrication six years prior.

The accountants state that Allriandre is now worth approximately one hundred and twenty million boxings in liquid assets and holdings. This fortune makes her the fourth-richest person in Elendel and resolves all of her debts from a failed art studio. She receives a handwritten note from Wayne that contains only the word "Sorry" stained with an unknown substance. Allriandre realizes she can now move her family from the Roughs to the city to provide them with a better life. She holds her daughter close while contemplating the massive change in their social and financial status.



### Epilogue 4: Kelsier

Kelsier stands on the roof of a modern skyscraper three weeks after the events in Bilming. He uses a Hemalurgic spike in his eye to see the blue lines of Steelpushing and the polarities of the surrounding matter. Harmony, the dual-natured god of Scadrial, manifests beside him in his traditional Terris form. Sazed reveals that Marsh will survive because atium was recovered from the ruins of Waxillium’s laboratory. This atium was created as a byproduct of harmonium, the metal of Harmony, reacting with bavadinium, the metal of the god Autonomy.

Kelsier asks Sazed if the explosion also produced lerasium, the metal that grants Allomantic powers. Sazed confirms that no lerasium survived the blast and states it would not work on Kelsier’s current spiritual state anyway. Kelsier argues that Scadrial needs a new generation of full Allomancers to defend against interplanetary threats. He criticizes Sazed for allowing the Set to perform Hemalurgic experiments while the god remained passive. Kelsier insists on democratizing power by giving the people the means to defend themselves through technology and industry.

Sazed maintains that humanity must discover technology naturally to avoid subtle spiritual consequences. Kelsier counters that Autonomy is already sharing advanced industry with her followers on other worlds. He demands that Sazed lead a technological revolution to prepare Scadrial for the return of Autonomy’s armies. Kelsier warns that he will do whatever is necessary to protect the planet, even if it means opposing Harmony. Sazed responds that Kelsier's future actions will determine whether they remain allies or become enemies.



### Epilogue 5: Ranette

Ranette and her wife Jaxy return to Elendel six months after their honeymoon in the Malwish Consortium. Ranette grumbles about the lack of guns and engineering work during their time away on the southern continent. They arrive at Ranette’s workshop and find that the building has been extensively renovated and expanded. Jaxy explains that the project was a wedding gift funded by Wayne’s estate and managed by his accountants. The expansion includes a much larger plot of land and upgraded facilities for Ranette’s gunsmithing business.

Jaxy hands Ranette a note written in crayon that Wayne composed before his death in Bilming. The note explains that Wayne’s accountants pressured him into making estate decisions because his lifestyle was considered high-risk. Wayne expresses his hope that his death was Marasi’s fault so she would finally have a "hat" on her head. He thanks Ranette for their friendship and for not "throwing the Wayne out with the Wayne." The note concludes with a request for Ranette to use the new shop to build something truly awesome.

Ranette smiles and admits to Jaxy that she sincerely misses the deceased lawman. She reflects on how Wayne eventually learned to stop being a "miscreant" and became a genuine friend. Ranette expresses a desire to acquire some of the harmonium explosives that Wayne used to destroy the Set’s bomb. She envisions the technological possibilities of building devices with such a powerful explosive force. Ranette concludes that there was never anyone in her life who was more enjoyable to shoot than Wayne.
