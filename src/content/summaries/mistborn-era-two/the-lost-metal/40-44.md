### Chapter 40

Marasi meets a floating glowing sphere that projects a holographic representation of Kelsier’s head and spiked eye. Kelsier reports he is twelve hours away on an airship while moving toward Bilming to intervene. Moonlight informs the group that the Set has discovered the explosive relationship between harmonium and trellium metals. The Ghostbloods discuss the Set’s plan to destroy Elendel to prove their dominance to the goddess Autonomy. Kelsier authorizes his agents to use purified Dor, which is concentrated Investiture unkeyed from any Identity.

Moonlight opens an Identity-locked safe by speaking the command word "Respect" to the sentient lock mechanism. She retrieves three glowing jars of purified Dor to power their various extraordinary abilities during the mission. The agents explain that they have funded their operations for twenty years by producing aluminum through electrolysis. Marasi learns that the Ghostbloods prefer recruiting "discarded" and "ignored" individuals like her over high-profile figures like Wax. Moonlight packs the rucksack with explosives and guns while Marasi selects a rifle and aluminum ammunition.

TwinSoul joins the group wearing a suit with a yellow sash and a curved sword in an ornate sheath. He carries a large backpack filled with water to fuel his ability to create roseite crystal structures. Codenames stays behind to coordinate communications with other Ghostblood cells across the Elendel Basin. Marasi takes command of the small strike team consisting of herself, Moonlight, and the elderly TwinSoul. The group leaves the safehouse to find the nearest train station to reach the Set’s factory.



### Chapter 41

Wax arrives at Gave Entrone’s mansion, the Silver House, and uses his Allomancy to Push inside a second-story window. He observes that the room is messy and lived-in, featuring a bar with haphazardly capped spirits and disorganized bookshelves. Wax searches the desk and finds trade negotiation letters from various Elendel noblemen, including Senator Vennis Hasting. He discovers Gave’s desk calendar which lists numerous visits to a laboratory and mentions trajectory and distance tests. The calendar shows no appointments after the current day and contains a final cryptic note stating "They arrive."

Wax uses steel burning to identify metal lines within the room, including the hidden wires for electricity and light fixtures. He locates a safe hidden in the floor beneath a couch and moves the furniture to access it. The safe features an Allomantic lock that requires a specific sequence of Pushes and Pulls to activate the tumblers. Wax successfully Pushes the first two pins but the lock resets when he fails to provide a Pull on the third. He concludes that the safe is designed for a Mistborn or someone using Hemalurgy to mimic multiple powers.

Shouts and the smell of smoke signal that the building is on fire and the enemy has discovered his presence. Wax notices a specific pattern of nails in the floorboards that indicates the presence of a concealed trapdoor. He moves a rug to reveal the latch and opens the door to find a narrow shaft with a ladder. The ladder leads down between the walls toward the basement level of the mansion. Wax closes the trapdoor behind him and descends into the shaft to escape the encroaching fire and guards.



### Chapter 42

Marasi, Moonlight, and TwinSoul ride the high-speed elevated train through Bilming to reach the Set’s hidden tire factory. Moonlight explains that she possesses three soulstamps, including two universal stamps and one emergency Essence Mark for herself. One universal stamp creates a doorway in an object while the second repairs broken items by rewriting their history. TwinSoul describes his ability to manifest roseite crystal objects using water drawn from his own body as fuel. He reveals a transparent web of stone embedded in his palm that connects him to a primal aether named Silajana.

The group discusses their tactical approach to infiltrating the factory and finding the freight elevator to the underground caverns. Marasi notes that the city of Bilming features diverse populations, including masked Malwish tourists and koloss-blooded citizens in suits. They observe dark smoke rising from the direction of the mayor’s mansion and assume Wax is providing a distraction. The strike team leaves the train station and approaches an old brown-brick factory with a mottled sign out front. They see six trucks loading equipment and moving toward the government offices to reinforce the Set’s troops.

Moonlight uses a leather toolbelt containing three stone stamps to prepare for their entry into the back of the building. TwinSoul uses his aether powers to sense the layout of the rooms behind the boarded-up windows of the factory. Moonlight applies a stamp dampened with glowing red ink to the brick wall to create a temporary wooden door. The three agents slip inside a small storage room filled with washbasins, jugs of liquid, and a red light bulb. The magical doorway vanishes behind them, sealing the team inside the dark and quiet interior of the tire factory.



### Chapter 43

Steris reads a letter from Wax confirming the existence of a city-destroying bomb and a potential delivery mechanism. Wax warns that the Set might restart the Ashmounts and suggests closing all routes into Elendel immediately. TenSoon and Governor Aradel agree that the threat is urgent and begin discussing evacuation procedures for the city. Vice Governor Adawathwyn proposes using Coinshots to Push the incoming weapon away from the city’s high structures. Steris argues that the weapon will move too fast for Allomancers to intercept and focuses on her seven evacuation plans.

Adawathwyn suggests retrieving the Bands of Mourning to grant an Allomancer the supercharged powers required to stop the bomb. Ambassador Daal of the Malwish protests that using the relic would violate the treaty and constitute an act of aggression. TenSoon reveals that there are unknown interactions between powers that might allow the Bands to divert the weapon. The kandra agrees to fetch the Bands from the Faceless Immortals to aid in the city’s defense. In a secondary narrative, Nicki Savage and her companions pursue a woman named Vila across a misty chasm.

Vila falls into the mists after hitting Nicki with a magical compass, but Nicki is saved by a flying creature. The Haunted Man takes the Compass of Spirits and uses an aluminum key to activate its ethereal light. He enters a ghostly dimension and disappears, leaving Nicki behind on a cliffside outcropping. Nicki expresses fury at the betrayal while the Faceless Immortals KeSun and Tabaar revert to their human forms. Tabaar informs Nicki that there is another way to follow the Haunted Man into the ghostly realm.



### Chapter 44

Marasi leads her team through the cramped slums and narrow alleys located beneath the shadow of the high-speed railway tracks. She reflects on how urban progress can often stratify society and make life harder for those living in the shadows. The group reaches the tire factory and witnesses Lord Mayor Gave Entrone arriving in a bulbous black motorcar with bodyguards. Entrone shouts orders at his workers and mentions the name "Dawnshot" as he hurries into the bay doors. TwinSoul maintains his balance by leaning against walls as the team circles to the rear of the brick structure.

TwinSoul uses his aether connection to Silajana to sense that a small, uninhabited storage room lies behind the factory wall. Moonlight retrieves a stone stamp and applies glowing red ink to the bricks to initiate the structural transformation. The wall bricks grind and shift to create a temporary wooden door with peeling yellow paint for the team. Once inside, they find themselves in a dim room filled with washbasins and jugs of cleaning supplies. Moonlight’s magical door disappears, reintegrating the bricks and leaving the strike team trapped inside the enemy facility.

Marasi unzips her rifle from its case while TwinSoul draws his curved sword to prepare for a confrontation. They move into an adjacent pitch-black room that is filled with long rows of chairs arranged like a theater. Marasi locates a series of light switches on the wall and flips one to illuminate the dark chamber. Instead of standard lighting, a projector casts a brilliant moving image onto the opposite wall of the room. The projected film displays a crumbling city with thick ash falling from the sky to bury the ruins.
