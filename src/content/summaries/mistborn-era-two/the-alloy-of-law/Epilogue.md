### Epilogue

Marasi attends the execution of Miles Hundredlives, watching from an outer balcony above the firing platform. The constables bind Miles to a restraining pole and aim their rifles at him. When the firing squad shoots him, Miles's wounds repeatedly heal due to an unknown, hidden metalmind. Miles screams a warning about men of gold and red who will one day rule them. Finally, his stored healing runs out, the final volley of bullets kills him, and a constable shoots him three more times in the head to be certain.

Waxillium boards a train at Dryport and confronts his uncle, Lord Edwarn Ladrian, in a private compartment. Edwarn admits he orchestrated the recent robberies to commit massive insurance fraud against House Tekiel. Waxillium realizes the stolen goods are being returned to nullify claims after Edwarn's group purchased Tekiel's debts and seized control of the financial institution. Edwarn refuses to release the kidnapped women and reveals he has nearly twenty Allomancers guarding him on the train. When Waxillium threatens to kill him eventually, Edwarn's guards throw Waxillium off the moving train.

After leaving the execution, Marasi chases a mysterious figure in a black robe into an alleyway. The man reveals himself as the mythological figure Ironeyes, a towering man with railroad spikes driven through his eyes. Ironeyes uses emotional Allomancy to calm her, then hands her a small book to deliver to Waxillium. He claims Waxillium is doing his brother's work and then propels himself away over the buildings using Allomancy. Waxillium lands at the rail station, reunites with Wayne, and reveals he stole his uncle's pocket book during their confrontation.
