### Chapter 11

Miles stood on a balcony in his underground foundry hideout and smoked a cigar while talking to his lieutenants Tarson and Clamps. Clamps complained about leaving beggars alive at the old foundry, but Miles insisted that killing them would turn the underclass against his operation. Miles walked to a private office to speak with his backer, Mister Suit, a wealthy man dressed in black silk and a turquoise vest. Mister Suit criticized Miles for losing his temper and shooting Lord Peterus during their previous robbery. Suit provided Miles with blueprints for House Tekiel's new armored freight car and ordered him to execute a new heist.

Miles defended his Vanishers persona and stated his ultimate intention to cleanse Elendel of its corrupt elite. Mister Suit informed Miles that the kidnapped women would be handled separately and instructed him to deal with Waxillium. Meanwhile, on a moving train, Waxillium, Wayne, and Marasi discussed Miles Dagouter's abilities as a Twinborn Compounder of gold. Waxillium explained that Compounding allowed Miles to store health in a metalmind and burn it for near-infinite healing. Marasi refused Waxillium's suggestion to hide somewhere safe and insisted on remaining with them to help with the investigation.

Waxillium asked Marasi about her Allomancy, noting that she carried a pouch of metal shavings in her handbag. Marasi revealed that she was a Pulser, an Allomancer capable of slowing down time in a bubble around herself. She expressed shame about her power, explaining that her father considered it useless because it left her frozen while everyone else moved normally. Waxillium assured her that her ability could be useful and fingered his earring while thinking about his connection to Harmony. The train passed through the Outer Estates and finally pulled into their destination station.



### Chapter 12

Wayne traded his hat for a carriage driver's stiff felt cap after stepping out of their train car. Waxillium led Wayne and Marasi to a grassy hillside in the Outer Estates where the last railway robbery had occurred. Waxillium noted that the tracks curved extremely close to a deep canal meant for carrying barges into the city. He deduced that the Vanishers must have used heavy machinery positioned in the canal to quickly empty the locked train cars. Wayne wandered over to Marasi and asked her what would happen if they used their opposite time-altering powers simultaneously.

Marasi explained that their powers would cancel each other out, and Wayne suggested that her Pulser ability could still be used to skip forward in time. Waxillium found a perfectly rectangular indentation pressed into the dirt beside the canal. He concluded that the lack of footprints confirmed a machine had rested there to reach the tracks during the robbery. The group returned to the train and rode back toward the city in a first-class passenger car. Waxillium walked through several railcars to find an available washroom while Wayne and Marasi stayed in a compartment further down the train.

As Waxillium washed his hands, he deduced that Miles would execute one more quick robbery to acquire aluminum before going into hiding. He suddenly heard a faint scream and threw himself to the side just as a bullet shattered the railcar window. A masked figure stood in the next car holding a gun and looking through the broken glass. Waxillium recognized the figure's build and the way he held his weapon as matching his former ally. He realized that Miles had not gone into hiding, but had instead come directly to the train to hunt him.



### Chapter 13

Waxillium pushed on the doors with his Allomancy to block incoming fire and launched himself out of the railcar. He climbed onto the roof of the train and shot the masked attacker in the eye and forehead. The man ripped off his mask, revealing himself to be Miles, and immediately healed the fatal wounds using his gold metalminds. Miles leaped across the gap between the railcars and tackled Waxillium into the space between them. Miles grabbed Waxillium and punched him repeatedly in the stomach while his own body instantly healed from Waxillium's point-blank gunshots.

Waxillium increased his physical weight using his Feruchemy and shoved Miles backward onto the roof of the train. The two men stood on adjacent cars and shot each other simultaneously, with Waxillium blasting the gun out of Miles's hand. Miles yelled that the law was ineffective and admitted he was now working to destroy Elendel's corrupt elite. Miles drove a knife through his own arm in a display of painlessness and lunged forward to grab Waxillium. Waxillium increased his weight again and threw his shoulder into Miles, but Miles dropped to the roof and kicked Waxillium's legs out from under him.

Waxillium fell off the moving train, bounced off the ground by pushing against his dropped knife, and landed in a weedy field. He stood up, grabbed his dropped gun, and pushed off the metal train tracks to launch himself back onto the train. He threw an empty bullet casing at Miles and used his Allomancy to violently push it into the man's chest, launching Miles completely off the train. Waxillium rode to the next station where he bandaged his grazed side while engineers freed Wayne and Marasi from their locked car. Wayne urged them to flee immediately, warning that the immortal Miles would soon pursue them again.



### Chapter 14

Waxillium led Wayne and Marasi to a brick townhome in the city to seek help from a gruff female gunsmith named Ranette. Ranette initially aimed a shotgun at them until Waxillium bribed her with the aluminum revolver he had taken from the Vanishers. She unlocked her doors using her Lurcher abilities and led the group into a living room converted into a cluttered workshop. Ranette examined the aluminum weapon and identified its internal mechanics as the work of a missing gunsmith named Nouxil. Waxillium asked for a replacement weapon for the upcoming fight, but Ranette refused to give him another Sterrion revolver.

Ranette displayed boxes of custom-made Hazekiller rounds, which were specialized bullets designed to exploit the weaknesses of different types of Allomancers. Waxillium asked to borrow a silver, eight-chambered revolver named Vindication that Ranette kept hidden in the back of a cubby. Ranette explained that Vindication featured an internal safety mechanism that only a Coinshot or Lurcher could deactivate. She agreed to lend Waxillium the weapon on the condition that he provide a detailed field-test report after using it. Waxillium instructed Wayne to acquire shipping manifests from the railway offices and told Marasi to purchase strong fishing nets.

Marasi read a distress message scratched in High Imperial on a metal plate hidden inside the aluminum gun's grip. Waxillium confirmed to Ranette that Miles was the leader of the Vanishers and stated his intention to personally execute his former colleague. Ranette warned Waxillium that he would need a small army to defeat an immortal opponent like Miles. Waxillium assured her that he intended to bring an army and asked to borrow a room and some paper to sketch out his plan. Ranette begrudgingly allowed him to use her sitting room on the condition that he remain quiet.



### Chapter 15

Miles and Mister Suit walked through a dark tunnel connecting the dorms to the main forging room of the Vanishers' new underground lair. Mister Suit criticized Miles for revealing his identity to Waxillium and warned him that the Set's wealthy members considered him a liability. Suit ordered Miles to rob House Tekiel's train that night, surrender half the stolen aluminum, and go into hiding for six months. Suit departed on a lift and left two black-coated Allomancers behind to monitor Miles during the upcoming heist. Miles dropped twenty feet to the floor of the main chamber and instantly healed the fractures in his leg upon impact.

Miles ordered the two Set Allomancers to check his men for hidden metals while he evaluated his remaining thirty Vanishers. He briefly burned gold to see a phantom version of his former lawkeeper self, feeling a deep hatred between his past and present identities. Miles extinguished the gold and decided that he would continue his crusade to destroy Elendel's corrupt government regardless of his internal doubts. He retrieved his old white duster and tinted spectacles from a trunk and dressed in the attire of a Roughs lawman. Meanwhile, Waxillium hung a paper pad on the wall of Ranette's sitting room and used shipping manifests to track the Tekiel train route.

Waxillium deduced that Miles would attack the armored train near a canal at Carlo's Bend using a false train facade as a diversion. Marasi returned with the fishing nets and informed Waxillium that the constables had already discovered the Vanishers' old hideout. Waxillium told Marasi that she and Wayne would watch the upcoming battle from a hilltop while he faced Miles alone on the train. Marasi promised to help him execute the operation despite her legal training dictating that she involve the authorities. Waxillium praised Marasi's courage, finished writing his calculations on the paper pad, and inserted his metal earring into his ear.
