### Chapter 16

Wayne hobbles through a railway station disguised as an elderly woman named Abrigain. He approaches the Breaknaught, an armored train car built by House Tekiel to prevent robberies. A guard explains that the safe's door locks on a twelve-hour timer and cannot be opened by anyone. Wayne drops his disguise, fires a blank from his cane, and causes a panic in the station. He uses a speed bubble to quickly change into an aristocratic gentleman's outfit.

Wayne acts outraged at the guard captain and convinces him to fetch more men to chase the supposed Vanisher. When the captain leaves, Wayne changes his disguise again into a brown and yellow military coat. He meets up with Waxillium, who is wearing a matching uniform and a fake paunch. Gunshots erupt from a nearby rooftop, and Waxillium falls backward as fake blood sprays from his shoulder. Wayne drags Waxillium into the open door of the Breaknaught under the guise of pulling him to safety.

The rooftop gunfire continues, keeping the other guards pinned down behind cover. Wayne yells that the attackers are coming for the cargo and slams the heavy door of the Breaknaught shut. The door clicks into place, locking Waxillium securely inside the vault. The captain arrives and accepts Wayne's explanation that locking the vault stopped the robbery. The train departs on schedule, and Wayne slips away from the station undetected.



### Chapter 17

Waxillium removes his fake blood and oversized uniform, remaining inside the sealed cargo box with his weapons and dynamite. The train brakes heavily in the middle of the night along a misty section of the tracks. Wayne and Marasi watch from a nearby hill as a phantom railcar approaches the real train. A rotary gun fires from the phantom train, pinning down the House Tekiel guards. A large crane barge emerges from the nearby canal and extends its mechanical arm toward the tracks.

The crane lifts the entire vault car off the tracks and lowers it onto the floating barge. The Vanishers use the crane to replace the stolen vault car with a replica. The real train eventually departs, leaving the Vanishers to escape down the canal on their motorized barge. Miles Hundredlives rides on the front of the barge, aware that Waxillium is hiding inside the stolen car. The barge floats into the city and docks at the Ironspine Building's freight station.

The crane unloads the vault car, and the platform descends into a hollow underground foundry hall. Miles commands his men to secure the train car with chains and attach the door to an electric winch. He orders his men to arm themselves with aluminum weapons and prepare to fire when the door opens. The winch rips the heavy door off its mountings, dragging it across the floor. A hidden string attached to the door tightens, detonating Waxillium's dynamite inside the railcar.



### Chapter 18

The dynamite explosion blasts through the foundry room, killing at least four Vanishers. Waxillium leaps from the train car, shooting three more men in midair before taking cover behind fallen boxes. He exchanges fire with a black-suited Coinshot, but a Pewterarm named Tarson flanks him and slaps his gun away. Wayne crashes through the ceiling in a new duster and drops a speed bubble to shield them. Waxillium pushes his gun up through the ceiling hole and leaps after it into the misty night sky.

Waxillium dodges bullets from the Coinshot and a Lurcher as the three Allomancers battle up the side of the Ironspine Building. Waxillium uses a special bullet to knock the Coinshot away, but fails to penetrate the Lurcher's breastplate. Marasi shoots the Lurcher with an aluminum rifle round, sending him crashing to the ground. Waxillium grabs Marasi and carries her down through the hole into the underground foundry. Wayne battles a large group of Vanishers by rapidly dropping and erecting speed bubbles.

Waxillium tosses a stick of dynamite toward the Vanishers and shoots it in midair, causing a massive explosion. Miles steps from behind the broken train car, and Waxillium drops a heavy net over him. Miles detonates a stick of dynamite in his own hand to blow the net apart, surviving the blast due to his healing powers. The explosion throws Waxillium across the room, leaving him dazed, bleeding, and severely injured. Miles walks over, punches Waxillium in the stomach, and leaves him crippled on the floor.



### Chapter 19

Waxillium shoots Miles with two shotguns from his trunk, using the birdshot to push Miles into the wall. Wayne informs Waxillium that he was shot in the arm and has run out of bendalloy and healing reserves. Waxillium tosses a special ceramic-tipped bullet toward the Coinshot, who pushes it back and takes the ceramic tip directly in the eye. Waxillium launches himself into a wooden dormitory built over the train tunnel to search for the captive women. He fights his way through the building, blasting holes in the walls and floor while shooting the Vanishers.

Waxillium breaches a locked room to find Steris and a captive gunsmith named Nouxil. He flings Nouxil out a window into the canal, grabs Steris, and blasts a hole in the ceiling to launch them into the air. Drawing on hundreds of hours of stored weight, Waxillium pushes downward, crushing the entire wooden building into the train tunnel. He lands safely in the wreckage with Steris and eliminates the remaining Vanishers standing near the tunnel entrance. Waxillium hands Steris a shotgun and sends her to run to the nearest precinct station.

Waxillium walks down the dark tunnel and finds Tarson holding a gun to Marasi's head. Miles orders Tarson to shoot Marasi if Waxillium does not surrender by the count of three. Wayne drops a speed bubble around Waxillium, giving him time to fire a bullet wide of Tarson. As the bubble drops, Waxillium fires a second bullet and pushes it into the first, deflecting the original bullet into Tarson's head. Waxillium brawls with Miles in the mud, enduring a severe beating until Wayne arrives with over a hundred constables to arrest Miles.



### Chapter 20

Waxillium sits battered and bandaged in a precinct office while Marasi waits protectively beside him. Waxillium tells Marasi that a relationship between them is unviable due to his traumatic past. Constable-General Brettin approaches Waxillium and offers him a citywide deputized forbearance, granting him official powers of investigation and arrest. Waxillium notes that Brettin acts like a completely different person and accepts the offer to officially aid the constabulary. Wayne arrives with a new revolver for Waxillium, explaining that he traded a dead man's scarf for it.

Waxillium places the new gun in his holster alongside Vindication, which Marasi recovered from the Vanishers' hideout. Wayne tells Waxillium that the constables fished the kidnapped gunsmith out of the canal alive. Marasi confirms that she found no evidence regarding the location of the other kidnapped women. Waxillium feels a sense of failure, noting that Miles might be the only one who knows where the missing women were taken. Steris emerges from the captain's office and thanks Waxillium for rescuing her without compromising her purity.

Steris proposes they announce their engagement next week to capitalize on the dramatic rescue. Waxillium warns her that he intends to continue his dangerous constabulary work. Steris accepts his lifestyle, admitting that she came to the union with a measure of desperation after previous suitors rejected her. She drops her cold demeanor briefly to offer her sincere thanks for his actions. Waxillium agrees to move forward with the engagement, choosing Steris over Marasi.
