### Chapter 1

Waxillium Ladrian attends a party at Cett Mansion five months after the death of Lessie. He steps onto the balcony and observes Elendel, a sprawling metropolis lit by electric lamps. Lady Aving Cett interrupts him to suggest he meet potential wives to rescue his insolvent house. Wax climbs over the railing, falls three stories, and uses his Feruchemical ability to decrease his weight before landing in a coachmen’s yard. He retrieves his mistcoat, a shotgun, and his Sterrion revolvers from his carriage trunk.

Wax drops a bullet casing and Pushes against it using his Allomantic steel ability to launch himself into the air. He navigates by jumping between metal streetlamps until he reaches the unfinished Ironspine Building. Wax scales the skyscraper by throwing another spent casing, drinking a vial of whiskey mixed with steel shavings to replenish his metal reserves, and firing his shotgun to propel himself upward. From the top of the tower, Wax leaps into the mists and uses his shotgun and a Push to slow his descent to the ground. A sudden sound of gunfire draws his attention, and he sprints across nearby rooftops to investigate.

Wax finds a group of constables exchanging fire with criminals in an alleyway. He drops into the alley with his Sterrion drawn and targets a street thug aiming a rifle. Wax freezes when he hears a whimpering sound that reminds him of Lessie's death. The thug fires at Wax, and Wax's Allomantic steel bubble deflects the bullet away from his ribs. Wax flees the fight, returns to Ladrian Mansion, and gives his guns and mistcoat to his butler Tillaume to lock away permanently.



### Chapter 2

Six months later, Waxillium dresses in a formal suit and prepares for a meeting with Lord Harms and his daughter Steris. His butler Tillaume brings him tea, and his steward Miss Grimes informs him that another shipment of Ladrian steel has been stolen by a group called the Vanishers. Waxillium refuses to investigate the robbery and proceeds to the sitting room to wait for his guests. He finds his former law-keeping partner Wayne inside the sitting room playing with a clock. Wayne informs Waxillium that the Vanishers recently robbed a train car filled with food, and Waxillium tries to push Wayne out of the room before the guests arrive.

Lord Harms enters the room alongside Steris and her cousin Marasi. Wayne disguises his voice and introduces himself as Waxillium's Uncle Maksil. Steris hands Waxillium and her father copies of a twenty-page contract detailing the strict rules of their impending courtship and marriage. Wayne creates an Allomantic speed bubble of compressed time around himself and Waxillium while the guests sit frozen outside of its boundary. Inside the bubble, Wayne shows Waxillium a bullet he acquired from a recent Vanisher robbery at the Coolerim Playhouse.

Wayne collapses the speed bubble, and time resumes normally for the guests. The guests discuss the playhouse robbery and mention that the Vanishers kidnapped Steris's cousin Armal. Waxillium covertly tests the bullet in his hand and discovers it is made of Allomantically inert aluminum. Waxillium tells Wayne that the Vanishers are capturing hostages for an unknown purpose and using the stolen goods as a distraction. Wayne leaves the mansion after urging Waxillium to investigate the Vanishers.



### Chapter 3

Eight hours later, Waxillium sits at his desk in his study and reads a stack of broadsheets containing articles about the Vanishers. He discovers that the first robbery occurred seven months prior and targeted a shipment from House Tekiel. Tillaume enters the study and serves Waxillium a cup of strong, minty tea. Waxillium asks Tillaume to bring him a large pad of drawing paper. Tillaume returns with an artist's easel and paper, and Waxillium uses a pencil to draw a timeline of the eight robberies.

Waxillium checks House Ladrian's shipping ledgers and determines that House Tekiel was secretly smuggling aluminum on the first robbed train. He concludes aloud that the Vanishers stole the aluminum to manufacture the inert bullets they used in later robberies. Waxillium tells Tillaume that the four kidnapped women are likely secret Allomancers. He theorizes to Tillaume that the thieves are capturing Allomancers and stealing Allomantic metals to further a larger plan. Tillaume reminds Waxillium of his duties as a house lord and advises him to prioritize his family's business over the investigation.

Tillaume leaves the room, and Waxillium dims the electric lights to look out the window into the dark city. Waxillium inserts his Pathian earring and spends fifteen minutes meditating and praying to Harmony. He asks Harmony for guidance on his situation and receives an internal sense of encouragement in response. Waxillium touches the aluminum bullet and remembers the moment Lessie was murdered by a gunshot. He drops the bullet, removes his earring, and spends the rest of the night working on the house ledgers.



### Chapter 4

The next day, Waxillium and Steris attend a massive wedding dinner for House Yomen in a crowded ballroom. Steris explains that the hosts invited hundreds of guests specifically to insult the few people they deliberately excluded. The couple takes a seat at a table with Lord Harms and Marasi. A waiter approaches the table to pour wine and introduces the soup course with a Terris accent. Waxillium immediately recognizes the waiter as Wayne in a disguise consisting of a false nose and a wig.

Lord Harms mentions the Vanishers, and Marasi shares statistics arguing that the City is just as dangerous as the Roughs. As Waxillium drinks his wine, Wayne slips a wax-sealed bullet casing into Waxillium's mouth. Waxillium removes the casing from his mouth and extracts a small note written by Wayne. Steris becomes offended by Waxillium's stories of his time in the Roughs and walks away to visit the washroom. Lord Harms departs the table to visit the bar, leaving Waxillium and Marasi alone with Wayne.

Wayne drops his disguise by creating a speed bubble and changing back into his duster underneath the table. Marasi reveals that she studies criminal behavioristics at the university and recognizes Waxillium and Wayne from published reports. Wayne shows Waxillium a piece of paper containing the family lineages of the four women kidnapped by the Vanishers. Waxillium reads the paper and realizes that all the kidnapped women are descendants of the Lord Mistborn. He deduces that the Vanishers targeted the wedding reception to find their next hostage, right as the ballroom doors burst open.



### Chapter 5

Dozens of armed bandits enter the ballroom and fan out among the tables to demand jewelry and valuables from the guests. Waxillium prevents Wayne from drawing his dueling canes to avoid starting a firefight in the crowded room. Marasi takes out a notepad and begins writing down physical descriptions of the criminals. The masked leader of the bandits strikes Lord Peterus, a retired constable, and knocks him to the floor. Waxillium drops Marasi's notepad and Wayne's canes against the table leg and uses his Allomancy to pin them there out of sight.

A bandit with gray skin and koloss blood approaches the table and confiscates Marasi's pearl necklace. Waxillium acts terrified and throws his pocket watch and a pocketbook full of marked bills into the bandit's sack. The masked leader consults a piece of paper and scrutinizes the faces of the women in the room. The bandits capture Steris from the washroom and drag her into the main hall. The koloss-blooded bandit grabs Marasi, points a pistol at her head after another bandit finds her dropped notepad, and pulls her toward the exit.

Lord Peterus stands up and shouts at the bandits, offering himself as a hostage in place of the women. The masked bandit leader turns around and shoots Peterus dead. As the bandits head for the exit, Wayne creates a speed bubble around his and Waxillium's chairs. Waxillium swallows a pouch of steel shavings and draws two hidden pistols from beneath his clothing. Wayne readies his canes, and Waxillium prepares to open fire on the bandits as soon as the bubble drops.
