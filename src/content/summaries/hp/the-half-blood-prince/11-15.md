# Chapter 11-15 Summaries


### Chapter 11: Hermione's Helping Hand
Sixth-year students struggle with an increased workload and the requirement to perform nonverbal spells in most classes. Harry uses a brand-new copy of Advanced Potion-Making to disguise his annotated version from the Half-Blood Prince. Stan Shunpike is arrested under suspicion of Death Eater activity after gossiping about secret plans in a pub. Harry holds Quidditch tryouts and selects Katie Bell, Demelza Robins, and Ginny Weasley as his Chasers. The new Beaters are Jimmy Peakes and Ritchie Coote, while Ron wins the Keeper position over Cormac McLaggen.

Ron saves five penalties in a row during the trials, while McLaggen misses his fifth after moving the wrong way. Hermione later admits to Harry that she used a Confundus Charm on McLaggen to ensure Ron's success on the team. The trio visits Hagrid at his cabin and finds him distraught over the failing health of Aragog. Aragog is a gargantuan talking spider, or Acromantula, who leads a colony of predatory spiders in the Forbidden Forest. Hagrid reveals that the rest of the spider colony has become restive and dangerous as Aragog nears death.

Slughorn invites Harry and Hermione to a small supper party but pointedly ignores Ron during the encounter. Harry receives a detention from Snape for the same night, preventing him from attending Slughorn's gathering. Snape tasks Harry with sorting rotten flobberworms from good ones without the protection of gloves. Harry learns from the Daily Prophet that the Ministry searched the Malfoy residence but found no incriminating Dark objects. He remains convinced that Draco Malfoy has successfully smuggled a dangerous item into Hogwarts despite the new Secrecy Sensors.



### Chapter 12: Silver and Opals
Harry experiments with new spells from the Half-Blood Prince's book, including the Muffliato charm which creates a buzzing sound. He accidentally uses the nonverbal spell Levicorpus on Ron, which hoists him into the air by his ankle. Hermione expresses deep disapproval of Harry using unapproved, handwritten spells from a mysterious and potentially "dodgy" source. Dumbledore sends Harry a note scheduling their second private lesson for the following Monday evening. During a cold and sleety trip to Hogsmeade, Harry spots Mundungus Fletcher selling stolen items from Sirius Black's house.

Harry confronts Mundungus and pins him against a wall, but Mundungus Disapparates before he can be forced to return the loot. On the walk back to the castle, the group witnesses Katie Bell arguing with her friend Leanne over a package. The package tears open, and Katie is instantly hoisted into the air where she begins screaming in agony. Hagrid carries the cursed and thrashing Katie back to the school while Harry retrieves the fallen item. He uses his scarf to pick up an ornate opal necklace that was once displayed at Borgin and Burkes.

Professor McGonagall takes the necklace to Snape and questions the students about how Katie came into possession of the object. Leanne explains that Katie returned from the bathroom at the Three Broomsticks with the unmarked parcel and appeared to be Imperiused. Harry tells McGonagall that he suspects Draco Malfoy, but she reveals that Malfoy was serving detention with her at the time. The necklace was intended for someone inside the castle, but Katie accidentally touched it through a small hole in her glove. Harry suggests that Dumbledore, Slughorn, or himself might have been the intended targets of the botched assassination attempt.



### Chapter 13: The Secret Riddle
Katie Bell is moved to St. Mungo's Hospital while the school gossips about the mysterious curse and its intended victim. Harry attends his second lesson with Dumbledore and learns that Snape's knowledge of the Dark Arts saved Katie from immediate death. Dumbledore confirms that Mundungus Fletcher has gone into hiding after being caught stealing Black family heirlooms from Grimmauld Place. The lesson transitions to the Pensieve, where they view a memory from the co-founder of Borgin and Burkes, Caractacus Burke. Burke describes purchasing a priceless gold locket with Slytherin's mark from a pregnant and destitute Merope Gaunt for ten Galleons.

Dumbledore and Harry enter a memory belonging to a younger, auburn-haired Dumbledore visiting a Muggle orphanage in London. They meet Mrs. Cole, the matron, who describes the arrival of a dying Merope Riddle on New Year's Eve. Merope requested her son be named Tom for his father and Marvolo for her father before she succumbed to death. Mrs. Cole reveals that the young Tom Riddle is a "funny boy" who scares the other children and steals their belongings. She mentions an incident where two children were traumatized after entering a cave with Tom during a summer outing.

Dumbledore meets the eleven-year-old Tom Riddle, who is handsome, pale, and immediately suspicious of his visitor's eccentric velvet suit. Tom admits he can move objects without touching them, control animals, and make "bad things" happen to people who annoy him. Dumbledore proves he is a wizard by setting Tom's wardrobe on fire and then restoring it to its original state. He forces Tom to return a box of stolen trophies, including a silver thimble and a mouth organ, to his victims. Tom reveals he is a Parselmouth, meaning he can speak to snakes, which Dumbledore notes as a rare and ominous ability.



### Chapter 14: Felix Felicis
Harry tells Ron and Hermione about Riddle's childhood as they harvest pulsating green pods from gnarled Snargaluff stumps in Herbology. Hermione invites Ron to Slughorn's Christmas party as her guest, which temporarily eases the tension between the two friends. Harry appoints Dean Thomas to replace the hospitalized Katie Bell as a Chaser on the Gryffindor Quidditch team. Ron's performance in practice deteriorates significantly as he suffers from severe nerves and lashes out at his teammates. Harry discovers Dean and Ginny kissing in a deserted corridor, which triggers a surge of intense, scaly jealousy in his chest.

Ron and Ginny engage in a public shouting match where Ginny mocks Ron's lack of romantic experience and mentions Hermione's past with Viktor Krum. Harry struggles with his developing feelings for Ginny while trying to maintain his friendship with a now-aggressive and surly Ron. Before the match against Slytherin, Harry fakes adding the golden Felix Felicis potion to Ron's pumpkin juice in front of Hermione. Ron, believing he is under the influence of "liquid luck," plays a spectacular game and leads Gryffindor to a decisive victory. Harry catches the Snitch after tricking the Slytherin Seeker, Harper, into fumbling the ball during a high-speed chase.

After the game, Harry reveals to Ron that he never actually spiked the drink and that Ron's saves were his own. Hermione is furious at the perceived cheating and leaves the celebration in tears after Ron treats her with icy indifference. Harry finds Hermione in a classroom where she has conjured a circle of twittering yellow birds to keep her company. Ron enters with Lavender Brown and the two begin kissing passionately, leading a devastated Hermione to attack Ron with her birds. The flock of birds pecks and claws at Ron until Hermione flees the room in a fit of vindictive fury.



### Chapter 15: The Unbreakable Vow
Christmas approaches and Ron continues his public romance with Lavender Brown, while Hermione refuses to speak to him and remains in the library. Harry learns that Romilda Vane and other girls are plotting to use love potions from Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes to trap him. Hermione warns Harry that the potions are disguised as perfumes or cough potions to bypass Filch's security checks and owl searches. In a moment of spontaneity, Harry asks Luna Lovegood to attend Slughorn's Christmas party with him as a friend. Hermione reveals she is taking Cormac McLaggen to the party specifically to annoy Ron for his behavior with Lavender.

Slughorn's office is transformed into a vast tent with emerald hangings and real fairies fluttering in an ornate golden lamp. Harry meets Eldred Worple, an author who wants to write his biography, and his emaciated vampire companion named Sanguini. Hermione spends the evening hiding from McLaggen, who is aggressively trying to kiss her under the mistletoe throughout the party. Filch drags Draco Malfoy into the office after catching him lurking in a corridor without an invitation to the gathering. Slughorn allows Malfoy to stay, but Snape immediately takes the boy into a private classroom for a stern confrontation.

Harry follows Snape and Malfoy under his Invisibility Cloak and listens to their conversation through a keyhole in the corridor. Snape reveals that he made an Unbreakable Vow to Malfoy's mother, Narcissa, to protect Draco and assist his mission. Malfoy angrily rejects Snape's help, claiming he has his own secret plan and other "better" people assisting him. He mentions that he would have had Crabbe and Goyle as lookouts if Snape had not put them in detention. Malfoy accuses Snape of trying to steal his glory before storming out of the room and leaving Snape alone.
