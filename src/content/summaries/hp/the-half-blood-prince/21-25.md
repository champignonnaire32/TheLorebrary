### Chapter 21: The Unknowable Room
Harry searches his Potions book for a way to persuade Slughorn but finds only a new spell called Sectumsempra. Ron panics about the upcoming Apparition Test while Hermione successfully manages the magic twice. Kreacher and Dobby arrive in the Gryffindor common room to report that Draco Malfoy is visiting the seventh floor regularly. Harry realizes Malfoy is using the Room of Requirement and suspects he is using Polyjuice Potion to disguise Crabbe and Goyle as lookouts. Hermione warns Harry that he cannot enter the room without knowing its specific purpose for Malfoy at that moment.

Harry spends an hour unsuccessfully trying to force the Room of Requirement to open by walking past the wall with different requests. During Defense Against the Dark Arts, Snape docks points from Gryffindor and mocks Ron for failing to Apparate. In the boys' bathroom, Moaning Myrtle tells Harry and Ron about a sensitive boy who visits her to cry. Harry attempts to ambush Malfoy at the Room of Requirement by scaring Goyle, who is disguised as a small girl. He encounters Tonks in the corridor, who appears depressed and is looking for a missing Dumbledore.

The trio discusses Tonks's condition and the possibility that her Patronus changed because she was in love with Sirius Black. Harry continues his failed attempts to enter the Room of Requirement but finds the wall remains solid and unyielding. Ron manages to Apparate near Scrivenshaft’s during his extra practice session in Hogsmeade. Hermione remains concerned about Tonks leaving her guard post to visit the castle. Harry feels powerful knowing Malfoy is trapped inside the room but remains frustrated by his inability to witness the boy's actions.



### Chapter 22: After the Burial
Hagrid sends a tear-blotted note to Harry, Ron, and Hermione announcing that Aragog the giant spider has died. Ron and Hermione are taking their Apparition tests and refuse to risk detention for a spider that once tried to eat them. Hermione encourages Harry to use his Felix Felicis potion to obtain the missing memory from Slughorn. Harry witnesses the Montgomery sisters' distress after their five-year-old brother was killed by the werewolf Fenrir Greyback. The trio heads toward the entrance hall as the castle bells signal the start of the afternoon testing and lessons.

Slughorn hosts a small Potions class for Harry, Ernie, and Malfoy where he asks them to brew something amusing. Harry prepares an Elixir to Induce Euphoria using the Prince's notes, hoping to use it on Slughorn. Malfoy looks ill and distracted, leading Harry to believe his secret mission for Voldemort is failing. Slughorn avoids Harry after the lesson ends, fleeing the classroom before Harry can initiate a conversation. Hermione passes her Apparition test, but Ron fails after leaving half an eyebrow behind at the testing site.

Harry takes a gulp of Felix Felicis and immediately feels a sense of infinite opportunity and confidence. He decides to visit Hagrid for the burial instead of going directly to Slughorn’s office as originally planned. The potion leads him to Professor Slughorn, who is collecting valuable plants at twilight with Professor Sprout. Harry invites Slughorn to the burial, and the professor agrees after realizing he can harvest valuable Acromantula venom. They bury Aragog behind the pumpkin patch, and Slughorn leads them inside for a night of heavy drinking and singing.



### Chapter 23: Horcruxes
Harry returns to the castle as his luck wears off and meets Dumbledore in his office to deliver the memory. They enter the Pensieve and watch a young Tom Riddle ask Slughorn about the nature of Horcruxes. Slughorn explains that a Horcrux is an object used to hide a fragment of a soul created through murder. Riddle suggests that splitting the soul into seven pieces would make a wizard significantly stronger and more stable. Dumbledore explains that Voldemort likely created six Horcruxes to achieve a seven-part soul including the piece in his body.

Dumbledore identifies Riddle's diary and Marvolo Gaunt’s ring as two Horcruxes that have already been destroyed. He suspects Helga Hufflepuff’s cup and Salazar Slytherin’s locket are also Horcruxes due to Voldemort's obsession with founders' trophies. The headmaster believes the snake Nagini is another Horcrux created after the death of a Muggle man. He admits he has been traveling to locate a possible sixth Horcrux that may have belonged to Ravenclaw or Gryffindor. Harry learns that Voldemort does not feel the destruction of his Horcruxes because his soul is too detached.

The prophecy is explained as being significant only because Voldemort chose to act upon it and mark Harry as his equal. Dumbledore emphasizes that Harry's ability to love is the unique power that protects him from the lure of the Dark Arts. He reveals that Voldemort cannot possess Harry's whole soul without enduring mortal agony due to his own soul's mutilation. Harry realizes he would want to finish Voldemort even if the prophecy did not exist. Dumbledore promises to let Harry accompany him on the mission to find and destroy the next Horcrux.



### Chapter 24: Sectumsempra
Harry informs Ron and Hermione about the Horcruxes and his upcoming mission with Dumbledore during Charms class. Ron accidentally creates snow while distracted, leading to a public confrontation and breakup with Lavender Brown. Hermione informs Harry that Ginny and Dean have also ended their relationship after a series of silly arguments. Katie Bell returns from St. Mungo's but cannot remember who gave her the cursed necklace in the bathroom. Harry hides his growing attraction to Ginny while training the Gryffindor Quidditch team for the final match against Ravenclaw.

Harry finds Malfoy crying in a bathroom and hears him tell Moaning Myrtle that he cannot complete his mission. Malfoy attempts to use the Cruciatus Curse on Harry, who retaliates by shouting the unknown spell Sectumsempra. Blood spurts from Malfoy’s face and chest as if slashed by an invisible sword, and he collapses. Snape arrives and uses a song-like incantation to heal the deep wounds and stop the massive bleeding. Snape demands Harry's schoolbooks and uses Legilimency to search for the source of the Dark magic spell.

Harry hides the Half-Blood Prince's book in the Room of Requirement and presents Ron's Potions book to Snape instead. Snape discovers the name "Roonil Wazlib" inside the cover and sentences Harry to detentions every Saturday. Harry misses the final Quidditch match and spends his detention copying old records of student punishments in Snape's office. Gryffindor wins the match four hundred and fifty to one hundred and forty, securing the Quidditch Cup. During the victory party, Harry kisses Ginny in front of the entire common room and receives Ron's silent approval.



### Chapter 25: The Seer Overheard
Harry and Ginny begin dating while she studies for her O.W.L.s and Harry serves his weekly detentions with Snape. Hermione discovers a 1940s Daily Prophet photograph of a student named Eileen Prince who was a Gobstones captain. She suggests that Eileen Prince might be the Half-Blood Prince, but Harry insists the author's writing style is male. Professor Trelawney is thrown out of the Room of Requirement while trying to hide sherry bottles and hears a celebration. She tells Harry that she was first interviewed by Dumbledore at the Hog's Head when Snape was caught eavesdropping.

Harry realizes that Snape was the one who overheard the prophecy and reported it to Voldemort, causing his parents' deaths. He rages at Dumbledore for trusting the man who sent a murderer after the Potter family years ago. Dumbledore maintains his complete trust in Snape and reveals he has finally located a Horcrux in a seaside cave. He allows Harry to join him on the condition that he obeys every command without question or hesitation. Harry provides Ron and Hermione with the Marauder's Map and the remaining Felix Felicis to guard the school.

Dumbledore and Harry leave the castle under the Invisibility Cloak and walk through the deserted High Street of Hogsmeade. They pass Madam Rosmerta as she ejects a wizard from the Three Broomsticks and head toward the Hog's Head. Dumbledore instructs Harry to take his arm for side-along Apparition while they stand in a quiet side street. They vanish from Hogsmeade and reappear on a dark coastline filled with the smell of salt and cold air. Harry feels the crushing sensation of Apparition end as they arrive at the site of the hidden Horcrux.
