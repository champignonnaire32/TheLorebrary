### Chapter 26: The Cave
Harry and Dumbledore arrive at a towering cliff and swim through a narrow fissure to enter a large cave. Dumbledore identifies the presence of magic and locates a concealed entrance that requires a blood payment to open. He cuts his own arm to provide the blood, revealing an archway that leads to a vast underground lake. A small, ghostly green boat is summoned from the depths of the water using an invisible chain. They board the tiny vessel, and Harry notices many dead bodies floating just beneath the surface of the black water. They reach a small island in the center where a stone basin sits on a pedestal, filled with glowing green liquid. Dumbledore concludes that the potion must be drunk to reach the Horcrux hidden at the bottom of the basin. Harry is ordered to force-feed Dumbledore the remaining liquid even if he protests or suffers. Dumbledore drinks several goblets, falling into a state of extreme mental agony and begging for death. After the final drop is consumed, Dumbledore collapses, and Harry retrieves a golden locket from the empty basin. Thousands of Inferi, reanimated corpses with pale skin and sightless eyes, rise from the lake and attack Harry. Dumbledore regains consciousness and creates a massive ring of fire to repel the creatures and protect their retreat.


### Chapter 27: The Lightning-Struck Tower
Harry and Dumbledore Apparate back to Hogsmeade, where Dumbledore collapses in a state of extreme weakness. They see the Dark Mark, a glowing green skull with a serpent tongue, hovering over the Astronomy Tower. Madam Rosmerta provides brooms, and they fly to the tower, where Dumbledore undoes the school’s protective enchantments to allow their entry. Upon landing, Dumbledore hears someone coming and wordlessly immobilizes Harry under his Invisibility Cloak. Draco Malfoy bursts onto the ramparts and disarms Dumbledore, claiming he has let Death Eaters into the castle. Dumbledore calmly converses with Draco, revealing he knows about the cursed necklace and the poisoned mead. Draco explains he used a pair of Vanishing Cabinets to create a passage between Borgin and Burkes and the Room of Requirement. Four Death Eaters, including the werewolf Fenrir Greyback, join Draco on the tower and urge him to kill the headmaster. Dumbledore offers Draco protection and a chance to change sides, but the boy hesitates in his task. Snape arrives on the tower, pushes Draco aside, and looks at Dumbledore with revulsion. Dumbledore pleads with Snape, who responds by casting the Avada Kedavra killing curse. The jet of green light hits Dumbledore, blasting him over the battlements and out of sight.


### Chapter 28: Flight of the Prince
The Body-Bind Curse on Harry lifts as Dumbledore dies, and he chases the fleeing Death Eaters down the spiral staircase. Harry petrifies the last Death Eater on the tower and runs into a fierce battle in the corridor below. He sees Ginny dodging hexes from Amycus and Ron, Lupin, and McGonagall fighting other intruders. Snape and Draco Malfoy sprint toward the front doors, and Harry pursues them through the castle and out onto the grounds. He finds Hagrid attempting to stop the escapees near his cabin, but the Death Eaters set the house on fire. Harry attempts to use various curses against Snape, including Sectumsempra and the Cruciatus Curse, but Snape parries every attack. Snape reveals he is the Half-Blood Prince and mocks Harry for trying to use his own invented spells against him. A massive Death Eater tortures Harry with the Cruciatus Curse until Snape orders him to stop, citing Voldemort's command. Buckbeak the hippogriff attacks Snape, forcing him to flee beyond the school boundaries and Disapparate. Hagrid rescues his dog, Fang, from the burning cabin and helps Harry put out the flames with the Aguamenti charm. Harry tells the disbelieving Hagrid that Snape has murdered Dumbledore. They walk to the foot of the Astronomy Tower and find a crowd gathered around Dumbledore’s broken body. Harry finds the locket on the ground and discovers it is a fake containing a note from someone named R.A.B.


### Chapter 29: The Phoenix Lament
Ginny leads the dazed Harry from Dumbledore’s body back into the castle and toward the hospital wing. He learns that Bill Weasley was savaged by Fenrir Greyback and will be permanently scarred, though he was not bitten during a full moon. In the hospital wing, Harry finds Ron, Hermione, Luna, and Lupin gathered around Bill’s bed. Harry announces to the group that Dumbledore is dead and that Snape was the one who killed him. Professor McGonagall arrives and collapses in shock upon hearing of Snape’s treachery. Fawkes the phoenix sings a mournful lament that echoes across the grounds and comforts the mourners. Harry explains that the Death Eaters entered through the Vanishing Cabinet in the Room of Requirement. Ron and Ginny describe how Draco used Peruvian Instant Darkness Powder to bypass their guard. Hermione reveals that Snape stupefied Professor Flitwick before joining the battle on the side of the Death Eaters. Bill's parents and Fleur Delacour arrive, and Fleur fiercely declares her intention to marry Bill despite his injuries. Tonks publicly expresses her love for Lupin, who initially resists because he is a werewolf and "too dangerous." McGonagall takes Harry to the headmaster's office, where a new portrait of Dumbledore has appeared on the wall. The Heads of Houses meet and decide that Dumbledore will be buried at Hogwarts and the students will stay for the funeral.


### Chapter 30: The White Tomb
Hogwarts suspends all lessons and exams as wizards and witches arrive from across the country for Dumbledore's funeral. Harry, Ron, Hermione, and Ginny spend their remaining time together, visiting Bill in the hospital wing. Hermione identifies Snape's mother as Eileen Prince, a pure-blood witch who married a Muggle named Tobias Snape. Harry realizes Snape called himself the "Half-Blood Prince" to honor his mother's name and hide his Muggle ancestry. The funeral takes place by the lake on a beautiful summer day with hundreds of attendees, including Ministry officials and magical creatures. Centaurs watch from the shadows of the Forest, and merpeople sing a sorrowful song from within the water. Hagrid carries Dumbledore's body, wrapped in purple velvet, and places it upon a marble table. A small man in black robes gives a speech about Dumbledore’s nobility and intellectual contributions. White flames erupt around the body, and when they vanish, a white marble tomb stands in their place. Harry speaks to Ginny after the ceremony and breaks off their relationship to protect her from Voldemort. Ginny reveals she never truly gave up on him and accepts his decision with a sad understanding. Rufus Scrimgeour approaches Harry to ask for information about Dumbledore’s last mission, but Harry refuses to cooperate. Harry tells Ron and Hermione that he will not return to Hogwarts and intends to hunt the remaining Horcruxes. Ron and Hermione vow to accompany Harry on his journey after Bill and Fleur’s wedding.
