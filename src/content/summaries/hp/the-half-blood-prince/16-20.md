### 16: A Very Frosty Christmas
Harry and Ron spend their holiday at the Burrow peeling sprouts while discussing Snape's offer to help Malfoy. Ron explains that an Unbreakable Vow results in death if the magical contract is violated. Remus Lupin arrives and reveals he has been living as a spy among a colony of werewolves. He describes Fenrir Greyback, a savage werewolf who intentionally bites children to raise them as wizard-haters. Harry asks Lupin about the Half-Blood Prince, but Lupin denies knowing anyone by that title.

On Christmas Day, Harry receives a package of maggots from Kreacher and a "sweetheart" necklace for Ron from Lavender. Percy Weasley unexpectedly arrives at the Burrow accompanied by Rufus Scrimgeour, the Minister of Magic. Scrimgeour takes Harry for a walk in the garden to request that Harry act as a mascot for the Ministry. Harry refuses the offer and cites the Ministry's unjust imprisonment of Stan Shunpike as a primary reason. He shows Scrimgeour the scars on his hand from Umbridge’s blood quill to prove his distrust.

The Minister realizes Harry remains loyal to Dumbledore and leaves the property with Percy in a tense standoff. Harry returns to the house and confirms his allegiance to the headmaster despite the Ministry's pressure. He checks the publication date of the Half-Blood Prince’s book and finds it is fifty years old. This date proves that neither his father nor Sirius could be the original owner of the textbook. Harry falls asleep thinking about the connections between Snape, Malfoy, and the various threats facing the wizarding world.





### 17: A Sluggish Memory
Harry, Ron, and Ginny return to Hogwarts via the Floo Network under Ministry supervision. Hermione meets them at Gryffindor Tower and informs Harry that Dumbledore has scheduled their next private lesson. Lavender Brown immediately greets Ron with physical affection, causing Hermione to depart the room in frustration. Harry tells Hermione about the conversation he overheard between Snape and Malfoy regarding the Unbreakable Vow. Hermione remains skeptical and suggests Snape might be acting as a double agent to gather intelligence.

A notice appears in the common room announcing that twelve weeks of Apparition lessons will cost twelve Galleons. Students who will turn seventeen by August are eligible to participate in the training sessions. During a Charms lesson, Seamus Finnigan accidentally hits Professor Flitwick with a high-pressure jet of water. Harry attends his lesson with Dumbledore and reports that he openly declared himself "Dumbledore's man" to Scrimgeour. Dumbledore expresses his appreciation for Harry’s loyalty before beginning the night’s review of Tom Riddle's past.

The first memory shows a teenage Voldemort visiting the Gaunt hovel and meeting his uncle, Morfin. Voldemort learns of his father’s identity and steals Marvolo Gaunt’s gold ring before murdering the Riddle family. He frames Morfin for the crimes by modifying his memory and planting his own wand at the scene. The second memory shows Slughorn discussing Horcruxes with a group of students, but the memory has been crudely edited. Dumbledore assigns Harry the task of retrieving the genuine, unedited memory from Slughorn to understand Voldemort's secret.





### 18: Birthday Surprises
Harry attempts to research Horcruxes in the library but Hermione finds no detailed information in any available books. During a Potions lesson on Golpalott’s Third Law, Harry uses a bezoar from the store cupboard instead of brewing an antidote. Slughorn praises Harry’s "intuitive" grasp of the subject and awards Gryffindor ten points for his cleverness. Hermione is visibly angry that Harry bypassed the complex brewing process by following a note in the Prince's book. After class, Harry asks Slughorn about Horcruxes, causing the professor to become terrified and flee the room.

The sixth-year students gather in the Great Hall for their first Apparition lesson led by Wilkie Twycross. Harry positions himself behind Malfoy and overhears him arguing with Crabbe about the length of a secret task. Twycross instructs the class on the three D's: Destination, Determination, and Deliberation, using wooden hoops for targets. Susan Bones splinches herself during the practice, leaving her left leg behind as she moves five feet away. Harry uses the Marauder's Map to track Malfoy but finds that the Slytherin boy frequently vanishes from the castle.

On Ron’s seventeenth birthday, he accidentally consumes several Chocolate Cauldrons spiked with a powerful love potion intended for Harry. Harry takes the dazed and obsessed Ron to Slughorn's office to obtain an immediate antidote for the charm. After Ron is cured, Slughorn offers the boys a glass of oak-matured mead he intended to give to Dumbledore. Ron drinks the mead and immediately collapses, foaming at the mouth and turning blue from a lethal poison. Harry finds a bezoar in Slughorn’s potion kit and thrusts it down Ron’s throat to save his life.





### 19: Elf Tails
Ron recovers in the hospital wing while Harry explains the poisoning event to the Weasley family and Hagrid. Fred and George reveal they were in Hogsmeade considering a business purchase when they heard the news. Hermione points out that both the necklace and the poisoned mead failed to reach their intended target, Dumbledore. Hagrid accidentally reveals that he overheard Dumbledore and Snape arguing about a secret task Snape no longer wants to perform. Harry suspects that Snape is not investigating Malfoy thoroughly enough despite Dumbledore’s orders to do so.

Cormac McLaggen approaches Harry in the common room to claim the Keeper position for the upcoming Quidditch match. Harry spends the following days dodging Lavender Brown’s questions and McLaggen’s unsolicited strategic advice for the team. During the match against Hufflepuff, Luna Lovegood provides a wandering commentary that ignores the actual score and game play. McLaggen leaves his goalposts to argue with Harry and takes a Beater's bat to demonstrate a proper hit. He strikes a Bludger directly into Harry’s head, cracking his skull and knocking him unconscious.

Harry wakes up in the hospital wing to find that Gryffindor lost the match three hundred and twenty to sixty. He realizes he can use house-elves to follow Malfoy and calls Kreacher and Dobby to his bedside. The two elves begin a violent physical fight that Harry stops by using the Langlock hex on Peeves. Harry orders both elves to follow Draco Malfoy around the clock and report his every move and meeting. Kreacher is forced to obey despite his resentment, while Dobby enthusiastically accepts the mission to help Harry Potter.





### 20: Lord Voldemort's Request
Harry and Ron are discharged from the hospital wing on Monday morning and join Hermione for breakfast. Hermione informs Harry that Ginny and Dean had a serious argument because Dean laughed at Harry's Quidditch injury. Luna Lovegood delivers a new invitation for a lesson with Dumbledore and a Gurdyroot to ward off Gulping Plimpies. Harry arrives at the headmaster's office and encounters a disgruntled Professor Trelawney leaving a meeting. Dumbledore expresses sharp disappointment that Harry has failed to retrieve the true Horcrux memory from Slughorn.

Dumbledore explains that Tom Riddle worked at Borgin and Burkes after being denied a teaching position at Hogwarts. They enter the memory of Hokey the house-elf, who served an elderly and wealthy witch named Hepzibah Smith. Hepzibah shows a young Tom Riddle two of her greatest treasures: Helga Hufflepuff’s cup and Salazar Slytherin’s locket. Riddle’s eyes flash red as he examines the locket, which Hepzibah bought from Caractacus Burke after his mother sold it. Two days later, Hepzibah dies of poisoning, and Riddle vanishes along with the cup and the locket.

The final memory shows a distorted, waxy-faced Voldemort returning to Hogwarts years later to request the Defense Against the Dark Arts job. Dumbledore refuses the request and reveals he knows about the Death Eaters waiting for Voldemort at the Hog’s Head. Voldemort leaves the office in a silent rage after Dumbledore denies him the opportunity to influence the students. Dumbledore notes that no teacher has held the Defense Against the Dark Arts position for more than a year since that meeting. Harry leaves the office with the realization that he must prioritize obtaining the Slughorn memory to proceed.
