# HARRY POTTER: CHAPTER 21 - Hermione's Secret to CHAPTER 22 - Owl Post Again


### 21: Hermione's Secret
Harry and Hermione wake in the hospital wing to hear Snape and Fudge discussing Sirius Black’s capture and the impending Dementor’s Kiss. Madam Pomfrey feeds them chocolate while they fruitlessly attempt to explain that Sirius is innocent and Peter Pettigrew is the true traitor. Dumbledore enters the ward, requests to speak with Harry and Hermione alone, and reveals that he believes their story but lacks the legal power to overrule the Ministry. He instructs Hermione to use her Time-Turner, a small gold hourglass on a chain that allows travel into the past, to save more than one innocent life. Hermione loops the chain around Harry’s neck and turns the hourglass three times, transporting them three hours back in time to the entrance hall.

The pair hides in a broom closet and then sprints across the grounds to the Forbidden Forest to avoid being seen by their past selves. They witness the execution party of Fudge, Dumbledore, and Macnair approaching Hagrid’s cabin to kill Buckbeak. While the committee is inside the hut reading the official execution notice, Harry bows to the Hippogriff and unties its rope from the fence. He and Hermione lead the creature into the dense trees just seconds before the executioner emerges to find the pumpkin patch empty. Macnair swings his axe into the fence in a rage, which the past versions of the trio mistake for the sound of a successful beheading.

Harry and Hermione wait near the Whomping Willow and watch the events of the night unfold from their hidden vantage point. Harry observes his past self being attacked by the Dementors across the lake and realizes no one is coming to save them. He sprints to the bank and conjures a powerful silver stag Patronus, realizing that he was the "father" figure he saw earlier. After driving the Dementors away, they fly Buckbeak up to the seventh-floor window of Professor Flitwick’s office where Sirius is imprisoned. Hermione uses the Unlocking Charm to open the window, allowing Sirius to climb onto Buckbeak’s back and fly away to safety.





### 22: Owl Post Again
Harry and Hermione rush back to the hospital wing and arrive just as Dumbledore is locking the door to their ward. They slip inside their beds seconds before Madam Pomfrey returns and Snape bursts in, screaming that Harry helped Sirius escape. Dumbledore points out that the children have been locked in the room the entire time, making Snape’s accusations seem like a mental breakdown. Fudge removes the Dementors from Hogwarts after they attempted to perform the Kiss on innocent students, and Snape’s fury results in him losing his Order of Merlin. Ron wakes up confused in his hospital bed, and Harry tells him that Sirius has successfully escaped on the Hippogriff.

The next day, Hagrid reveals that Professor Lupin has resigned from his post because Snape "accidentally" leaked that he is a werewolf. Harry visits Lupin’s office and receives the Marauder’s Map and the Invisibility Cloak back from his departing teacher. Dumbledore arrives to see Lupin off and later consoles Harry about Pettigrew’s escape, explaining that saving a life creates a magical bond. He confirms that Harry’s Patronus took the form of a stag because that was his father’s Animagus form, known as Prongs. Hermione informs the boys on the Hogwarts Express that she has returned the Time-Turner to Professor McGonagall and dropped several classes to have a normal schedule.

During the train ride home, a tiny owl delivers a letter from Sirius Black addressed to Harry. Sirius explains that he sent the Firebolt as a birthday present and provides a written permission slip for Harry to visit Hogsmeade. He also gives the small owl to Ron as a replacement for Scabbers, and Ron names the bird Pigwidgeon after Crookshanks approves of it. Harry meets Uncle Vernon at King’s Cross station and gleefully mentions his "godfather," a convicted murderer who likes to keep in touch. Vernon is visible shaken by this news, and Harry leaves for the summer feeling more cheerful than ever before.
