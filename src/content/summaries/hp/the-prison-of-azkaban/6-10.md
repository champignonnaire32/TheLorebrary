### Chapter 6: Talons and Tea Leaves
Harry begins his third year with a full schedule and a new subject called Divination taught by Professor Trelawney. Trelawney is a thin woman with large glasses who wears a spangled shawl and many beads. During the first lesson, the students are instructed to read tea leaves at the bottom of their cups. Trelawney looks into Harry’s cup and screams when she sees a Grim, a large spectral dog that serves as an omen of death. Hermione expresses skepticism about the subject and argues with Trelawney before the class ends.

Later, the students attend their first Care of Magical Creatures class with Hagrid, who introduces them to a herd of Hippogriffs. A Hippogriff is a creature with the body of a horse and the head, wings, and front talons of a giant eagle. Hagrid explains that they are proud creatures and requires students to bow to them to earn their trust. Harry successfully bows to a Hippogriff named Buckbeak and is allowed to take a short flight on its back. The lesson turns into a disaster when Draco Malfoy insults Buckbeak and the creature slashes his arm with its talons.

Hagrid carries the bleeding Malfoy to the hospital wing while the rest of the class returns to the castle in shock. That evening, Harry, Ron, and Hermione visit Hagrid at his hut and find him drinking heavily from a large pewter tankard. Hagrid fears he will be fired because Malfoy’s father has complained to the school governors and the Ministry. After Hagrid gets drunk, he realizes Harry is out after dark and angrily escorts the trio back to the castle for their safety. He insists that he is not worth them breaking rules to visit him.





### Chapter 7: The Boggart in the Wardrobe
Draco Malfoy returns to class with his arm in a sling and uses his injury to force Ron and Harry to do his work in Potions. Professor Snape encourages this behavior and punishes Neville Longbottom when his Shrinking Solution turns orange instead of green. Hermione secretly helps Neville fix the potion, but Snape still takes points from Gryffindor for her assistance. During lunch, Seamus Finnigan shares a news report stating that Sirius Black has been sighted not far from Hogwarts. Malfoy taunts Harry by suggesting he should want revenge on Black, though Harry does not understand why.

Professor Lupin leads the class to the staffroom for a practical lesson involving a Boggart trapped inside a wardrobe. A Boggart is a shape-shifter that assumes the form of whatever the viewer fears most. Lupin explains that the charm to defeat it is "Riddikulus" and requires the wizard to imagine the fear in a humorous situation. Neville goes first, and the Boggart transforms into Professor Snape, whom Neville then dresses in his grandmother’s vulture-topped hat and green dress. Parvati transforms a mummy into a tangled mess, and Seamus turns a banshee into a voiceless entity.

Ron faces a giant spider and uses the charm to remove its legs, causing it to roll around helplessly. When it is Harry’s turn, Lupin suddenly jumps in front of him and the Boggart turns into a silvery-white orb. Lupin uses the charm to turn the orb into a cockroach, and Neville finishes the Boggart off. The class ends with Gryffindor earning points, but Harry is left feeling confused and insulted that Lupin did not let him face the creature. Hermione and Ron wonder what their own Boggarts would have been while Harry worries Lupin thinks he is weak.





### Chapter 8: Flight of the Fat Lady
Defense Against the Dark Arts becomes the favorite subject for most students as Lupin teaches them about Red Caps and Kappas. Red Caps are small goblin-like creatures that lurk in places where blood has been shed, while Kappas are scaly water-dwellers that strangle waders. Meanwhile, Quidditch practice intensifies as Oliver Wood desperately seeks to win the Cup in his final year at Hogwarts. Harry learns that the first Hogsmeade trip is scheduled for Halloween, but Professor McGonagall refuses to sign his permission form. Ron and Hermione head to the village while Harry stays behind and encounters Professor Lupin in his office.

Lupin introduces Harry to a Grindylow, a green water demon with long, brittle fingers that lives in a water tank. During their conversation, Lupin reveals he didn't let Harry face the Boggart because he feared it would turn into Lord Voldemort. Harry admits he actually thought of a Dementor, and Lupin praises him for fearing fear itself. Professor Snape enters the office and delivers a smoking, foul-smelling potion for Lupin to drink. Harry suspects the potion might be poison, but Lupin drinks it anyway and explains that Snape is helping him manage an illness.

The students return from Hogsmeade with bags of sweets from Honeydukes and stories about the Three Broomsticks. The Halloween feast is interrupted when the Gryffindors discover that the Fat Lady has vanished from her portrait. Her canvas has been slashed into pieces, and the portrait hole is blocked. Peeves the Poltergeist informs Dumbledore and the gathered students that the Fat Lady was attacked by Sirius Black. The Fat Lady is seen hiding in a map of Argyllshire on the second floor, screaming in terror.





### Chapter 9: Grim Defeat
Dumbledore orders all students to sleep in the Great Hall while the teachers search the castle for Sirius Black. Hundreds of purple sleeping bags are laid out on the floor, and the prefects stand guard at the doors. Harry overhears Snape expressing his suspicions to Dumbledore that Black had inside help to enter the castle. Snape specifically references a conversation they had before the term began regarding a certain appointment. Dumbledore dismisses the idea of internal treachery and leaves to inform the Dementors that the search is complete.

The Fat Lady’s portrait is replaced by Sir Cadogan, a short knight in armor who constantly changes the passwords. Security is tightened, and teachers now escort Harry through the corridors to ensure his safety. Professor McGonagall informs the Quidditch team that their match against Slytherin is cancelled because Malfoy’s arm is still supposedly injured. Gryffindor must play Hufflepuff instead, which features a new Seeker and Captain named Cedric Diggory. On the day of the match, a massive thunderstorm creates dangerous flying conditions with heavy rain and lightning.

During the game, Harry sees the silhouette of a large black dog in the stands and loses focus. At least one hundred Dementors enter the stadium, causing Harry to hear his mother’s dying screams before he passes out. He falls fifty feet from his broom, but Dumbledore uses magic to slow his descent and drive the Dementors away. Cedric Diggory catches the Snitch, resulting in Hufflepuff’s victory over Gryffindor. Harry wakes up in the hospital wing to find his Nimbus Two Thousand has been destroyed by the Whomping Willow.





### Chapter 10: The Marauder's Map
Harry remains in the hospital wing over the weekend while mourning the loss of his broomstick. He reflects on the fact that he hears his mother's last moments whenever the Dementors approach him. Professor Lupin returns to class and informs the students they do not have to do the werewolf essay Snape assigned. Lupin explains that Dementors affect Harry more than others because of the true horrors in his past. He agrees to teach Harry a defense against Dementors after the Christmas holidays are over.

Two weeks before the end of term, Fred and George Weasley pull Harry aside and give him the Marauder’s Map. The map is a magical piece of parchment that shows every person and secret passage within Hogwarts. It was created by four individuals known as Moony, Wormtail, Padfoot, and Prongs. Harry uses the map to find a secret passage that leads from a statue of a one-eyed witch to the cellar of Honeydukes. He sneaks into Hogsmeade and meets Ron and Hermione, who are shocked by his appearance.

The trio goes to the Three Broomsticks for butterbeer and hides when they see several teachers and the Minister of Magic enter. Harry listens from under a table as Cornelius Fudge reveals that Sirius Black was James Potter’s best friend. Fudge explains that Black was the Potters' Secret-Keeper and betrayed their location to Lord Voldemort. The group also discusses how Black murdered Peter Pettigrew and twelve Muggles with a single curse. Harry learns for the first time that Sirius Black is his godfather and was the one who betrayed his parents.
