### Chapter 11: The Firebolt
Harry returns from Hogsmeade and views an old photograph of Sirius Black at his parents' wedding. Ron and Hermione warn Harry against seeking revenge after hearing that Black betrayed the Potters to Voldemort. The trio visits Hagrid and finds him weeping over a letter from the Committee for the Disposal of Dangerous Creatures. The letter states that Buckbeak the Hippogriff, a large creature with an eagle head and horse body, must stand trial. Hagrid fears Lucius Malfoy’s influence will result in the execution of the animal.

On Christmas morning, Harry unwraps a mysterious package containing a Firebolt, an elite international-standard racing broom. Ron admires the broom's streamlined birch twigs and golden registration number while they speculate on the anonymous sender. Hermione enters the dormitory with her cat, Crookshanks, and expresses concern that the gift is a trap. During Christmas dinner, Professor Trelawney predicts disaster because thirteen people are seated at the table. Harry and Ron leave the meal first, which Trelawney claims marks them for an early death.

Professor McGonagall enters the Gryffindor common room accompanied by Hermione to inspect the new broomstick. Hermione admits she reported the Firebolt because she suspects Sirius Black sent it to harm Harry. McGonagall confiscates the broom to test it for lethal jinxes and hidden hexes. Ron shouts at Hermione for her interference, causing a rift between the friends. Harry stands silently as his prize is carried away for a weeks-long inspection process.



### Chapter 12: The Patronus
Oliver Wood discovers that Harry’s Firebolt was confiscated and promises to plead with Professor McGonagall for its return. Harry begins private lessons with Professor Lupin to learn how to repel Dementors. Lupin provides a Boggart, a shape-shifting entity that takes the form of a Dementor when it sees Harry. The professor explains that the Patronus Charm creates a silver guardian to shield the caster from the Dementors’ despair. Harry attempts to produce the charm while concentrating on his first memory of flying a broom.

The Boggart-Dementor emerges from a packing case and drains the warmth from the History of Magic classroom. Harry hears his mother’s dying screams and faints before he can complete the incantation. Lupin revives him with chocolate and encourages him to try a stronger happy memory. On his second attempt, Harry hears his father’s voice for the first time as James Potter confronts Voldemort. Harry fails again and collapses, but he insists on a third trial to ensure he can play Quidditch.

During the third attempt, a huge silver shadow bursts from Harry’s wand and halts the Boggart’s advance. Lupin forces the creature back into its box and rewards Harry with a large bar of Honeydukes chocolate. Harry asks if Lupin knew Sirius Black, and the professor admits they were friends at Hogwarts. Later, Professor McGonagall returns the Firebolt after Madam Hooch and Professor Flitwick find no jinxes on it. Ron discovers blood on his bedsheets and ginger cat hairs, leading him to believe Crookshanks ate Scabbers.



### Chapter 13: Gryffindor Versus Ravenclaw
Ron and Hermione stop speaking to each other following the disappearance of Scabbers the rat. Ron demands an apology for the cat's actions, while Hermione insists there is no proof of a kill. Harry practices with the Firebolt and finds that the broom responds to his thoughts with perfect precision. During the final practice, Wood reveals that Ravenclaw’s Seeker is Cho Chang, a skilled fourth-year flyer. Harry sees a pair of glowing eyes in the darkness near the pitch but realizes it is only Crookshanks.

The Quidditch match begins under clear skies as Harry searches for the Golden Snitch. Cho Chang tails Harry closely, using her Comet Two Sixty to block his movements and force swerves. Lee Jordan provides the commentary while frequently praising the technical specifications of Harry’s Firebolt. Harry spots the Snitch near the ground and dives, but a Bludger forces him to veer off course. Cho grins as the Snitch vanishes, and Wood bellows for Harry to stop being a gentleman.

Three Dementors appear on the pitch, and Harry immediately draws his wand to cast a Patronus. A massive silver-white shape erupts from his wand and drives the hooded figures away from the field. Harry closes his eyes and catches the Snitch, securing a victory for the Gryffindor team. The "Dementors" are revealed to be Malfoy, Crabbe, Goyle, and Marcus Flint wearing long black robes. Sirius Black breaks into the Gryffindor dormitory that night and slashes Ron’s bed curtains with a knife.



### Chapter 14: Snape's Grudge
The castle undergoes a security sweep after Sirius Black's intrusion into the Gryffindor dormitory. The Fat Lady returns to her post as the portrait hole guardian with a squad of security trolls for protection. Neville Longbottom receives a Howler from his grandmother that shrieks about the shame he brought to their family. Hagrid invites Harry and Ron to tea and urges them to reconcile with Hermione. He reveals that Hermione has been crying and working alone to prepare Buckbeak’s legal defense.

Harry sneaks into Hogsmeade using the Marauder’s Map and his Invisibility Cloak to meet Ron. They visit the Shrieking Shack, a boarded-up house rumored to be the most haunted building in Britain. Malfoy, Crabbe, and Goyle arrive and begin taunting Ron about his family's lack of wealth. Harry throws mud at the Slytherins while hidden, but his cloak slips and reveals his head to Malfoy. Harry sprints back to the castle through the secret tunnel to beat Malfoy to the school.

Professor Snape intercepts Harry near the one-eyed witch statue and demands that he empty his pockets. Snape discovers the Marauder’s Map and attempts to force the parchment to reveal its secrets. The map insults Snape's appearance and nose, prompting the professor to summon Lupin via the Floo Network. Lupin claims the map is a joke shop product and confiscates it to protect Harry from further trouble. Hermione arrives at the common room and announces that Buckbeak has been sentenced to death.



### Chapter 15: The Quidditch Final
Hagrid sends a letter explaining that the Committee for the Disposal of Dangerous Creatures has ordered Buckbeak’s execution. Ron and Hermione reconcile as Ron promises to help with the official appeal process. Hermione slaps Malfoy across the face after he mocks Hagrid’s tears in the castle entrance. She later misses her Charms class and falls asleep in the common room due to her heavy schedule. During Divination, Hermione insults Professor Trelawney’s crystal ball predictions and permanently quits the class.

The Gryffindor team prepares for the Quidditch final against Slytherin with intense daily practice sessions. Slytherin leads the tournament by two hundred points, meaning Harry must wait to catch the Snitch until his team is ahead. Tension rises as students from both houses engage in corridor scuffles that result in hospital wing visits. Harry keeps his Firebolt locked in his trunk to prevent Malfoy from sabotaging the expensive broomstick. On the night before the match, Harry sees Crookshanks prowling the grounds with a gigantic black dog.

The match begins with violent fouls as Slytherin players attempt to knock the Gryffindors off their brooms. Malfoy grabs the tail of Harry’s Firebolt to prevent him from reaching the Snitch first. Madam Hooch awards multiple penalties to Gryffindor, and Angelina Johnson scores to put the team sixty points ahead. Harry dives toward the grass and knocks Malfoy’s arm aside to seize the golden ball. Gryffindor wins the Quidditch Cup, and the team carries Harry to the stands to receive the trophy.
