### 16: Professor Trelawney's Prediction
Harry wins the Quidditch Cup and returns to a castle filled with exam preparation for June. Hermione maintains an impossible exam schedule while Hagrid prepares for Buckbeak's final appeal against the Committee for the Disposal of Dangerous Creatures. During the Defense Against the Dark Arts exam, Harry successfully navigates an obstacle course of dark creatures while Hermione flees from a Boggart. The trio encounters Minister Cornelius Fudge and an executioner carrying a sharp axe near the castle steps. Harry attends his Divination exam alone with Professor Trelawney after Ron fails his crystal ball test.

Harry stares into a crystal ball but sees only white fog before inventing a vision of a flying Hippogriff. As Harry prepares to leave, Trelawney enters a rigid trance and speaks in a harsh, unfamiliar voice. She predicts that the Dark Lord's servant will break free before midnight to rejoin his master. Trelawney immediately wakes with no memory of the event and dismisses Harry's questions about the prophecy. Harry returns to the common room to find a note from Hagrid stating that the appeal was lost.

Hermione retrieves the Invisibility Cloak from its hiding place in the secret passage under the one-eyed witch statue. The trio sneaks down to Hagrid's cabin under the cloak just as the sun begins to set. Hagrid accidentally breaks a milk jug while trembling and describes Dumbledore's failed attempts to overrule the execution order. Hermione discovers Ron's missing rat, Scabbers, hiding inside a milk jug in Hagrid's kitchen cupboard. Dumbledore, Fudge, and the executioner arrive at the cabin, forcing the students to flee out the back door.


### 17: Cat, Rat, and Dog
Harry, Ron, and Hermione stand hidden under the Invisibility Cloak and hear the sound of an axe striking. They believe Buckbeak has been executed and start heading back toward the castle in the deepening darkness. Scabbers bites Ron's finger and escapes his grasp, prompting Ron to throw off the cloak and chase him. Ron captures the rat near the Whomping Willow just as Crookshanks the cat arrives on the scene. A massive black dog suddenly lunges from the shadows and knocks Harry and Hermione to the ground.

The dog seizes Ron by the arm and begins dragging him toward a gap in the roots of the Whomping Willow. Harry and Hermione are struck by the tree's violent branches as they attempt to follow their friend. A loud crack signals that Ron's leg has been broken during the struggle before he disappears underground. Crookshanks presses a specific knot on the tree trunk to freeze the branches and allow Harry and Hermione passage. They slide down a tunnel that leads away from the school grounds and toward the village of Hogsmeade.

The tunnel terminates in a dusty, dilapidated room inside the Shrieking Shack where the furniture has been smashed. Harry and Hermione climb the stairs and find Ron clutching his broken leg on a bed in an upstairs room. Sirius Black steps out from behind the door and uses Ron's wand to disarm Harry and Hermione. Black admits to being an Animagus and claims that he has waited twelve years to commit a murder. Professor Lupin bursts into the room with his wand raised and uses a spell to disarm the students.


### 18: Moony, Wormtail, Padfoot, and Prongs
Lupin lowers his wand and helps Sirius Black to his feet, embracing him like a long-lost brother. Hermione screams that Lupin is a werewolf, a fact she discovered by tracking his monthly illnesses and Boggart forms. Lupin admits his lycanthropy and explains that Dumbledore provided him with the Wolfsbane Potion to remain safe during transformations. He reveals that the Whomping Willow and the Shrieking Shack were built specifically to house him during his school years. Lupin explains that his three best friends became illegal Animagi to keep him company while he was a wolf.

Harry learns that his father, James Potter, could transform into a stag nicknamed Prongs to roam the grounds. Sirius Black became a large dog named Padfoot, and Peter Pettigrew transformed into a rat named Wormtail. Lupin used the Marauder's Map in his office to track the trio's movements to Hagrid's cabin earlier that evening. He noticed a dot labeled Peter Pettigrew moving with Ron, which proved that the supposedly dead man was alive. Lupin demands to see Ron's rat and explains that Scabbers is actually Pettigrew in his animal form.

Sirius Black watches Scabbers with visible hunger while Lupin describes how James Potter once saved Severus Snape's life. Snape had attempted to follow Lupin into the Whomping Willow during a full moon as part of a dangerous prank. James pulled Snape back from the tunnel just before he reached the fully transformed werewolf in the shack. Dumbledore subsequently forbade Snape from revealing Lupin's secret to the rest of the school. As Lupin finishes his explanation, Severus Snape removes the Invisibility Cloak and points his wand at the group.


### 19: The Servant of Lord Voldemort
Snape binds Lupin with magical cords and threatens to hand both him and Black over to the Dementors. Harry, Ron, and Hermione all cast disarming spells simultaneously, knocking Snape unconscious against the wall of the shack. Black produces a newspaper clipping showing Scabbers on Ron's shoulder during their family trip to Egypt. He explains that Pettigrew faked his own death by cutting off a finger and blowing up a street full of Muggles. Black reveals that he persuaded the Potters to use Pettigrew as their Secret-Keeper instead of himself at the last moment.

Lupin and Black use a combined spell to force Scabbers back into his human form as a short, balding man. Peter Pettigrew grovels on the floor and confesses to passing information to Lord Voldemort for over a year. He admits he was a spy because he was afraid of the Dark Lord's power and weapons. Black and Lupin prepare to execute Pettigrew together as punishment for the betrayal of James and Lily Potter. Harry intervenes and stops the killing, insisting that his father would not have wanted his friends to become murderers.

Harry decides to take Pettigrew back to the castle to be handed over to the Dementors and Azkaban. Lupin uses a spell to create a splint and bandages for Ron's broken leg so he can walk. Snape is levitated in the air like a puppet to be moved back through the tunnel to the school. Black conjures manacles to chain Pettigrew's arms to Lupin and Ron to prevent him from transforming and escaping. The group leaves the Shrieking Shack with Crookshanks leading the way back toward the Whomping Willow.


### 20: The Dementor's Kiss
The group navigates the narrow tunnel back to the Whomping Willow with Snape's unconscious body floating behind them. Sirius Black tells Harry that he is his legal guardian and offers him a home away from the Dursleys. Harry accepts the offer immediately, and Black smiles for the first time since his escape from Azkaban. They emerge into the dark grounds and begin walking toward the distant lights of the castle. A cloud shifts to reveal the full moon, and Lupin begins a violent transformation because he missed his potion.

Black transforms back into a massive dog to wrestle with the werewolf and protect the students from attack. Pettigrew seizes Lupin's dropped wand and uses a spell to knock Ron and Crookshanks unconscious. He then transforms back into a rat and disappears into the grass before Harry can stop him. The werewolf flees into the Forbidden Forest while the injured Black crawls toward the edge of the lake. Harry and Hermione find Black surrounded by at least a hundred Dementors gliding across the water.

Harry attempts to cast the Patronus Charm but only manages to produce a thin wisp of silver mist. The Dementors force Harry to his knees and lower their hoods to prepare for the Dementor's Kiss. A blindingly bright silver light erupts from across the lake and drives the entire mass of Dementors away. Harry sees a glowing animal gallop back to the opposite shore where a familiar-looking figure welcomes it. Harry loses his remaining strength and faints on the grass next to his unconscious godfather and friend.
