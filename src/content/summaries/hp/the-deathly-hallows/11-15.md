### 11: The Bribe
Harry gives Kreacher the fake locket of Regulus Black as a gift. Kreacher is overcome with emotion and accepts the mission to find Mundungus Fletcher. Remus Lupin arrives at Grimmauld Place and reveals that the Ministry has fallen to the Death Eaters. He explains that Rufus Scrimgeour was killed after refusing to reveal Harry's location. Lupin offers to join the trio on their mission to protect them. Harry asks if Lupin intends to leave his pregnant wife behind to go with them. Lupin admits he regrets his marriage because he fears passing on his werewolf condition. Harry calls Lupin a coward for attempting to abandon his unborn child and family. Lupin strikes Harry with a spell and leaves the house in a rage. Harry reads a Rita Skeeter article suggesting Albus Dumbledore's sister was a Squib. Kreacher returns to the kitchen with a captured Mundungus Fletcher for questioning.


### 12: Magic is Might
Mundungus Fletcher reveals that Dolores Umbridge took the real locket from him as a bribe. Harry, Ron, and Hermione spend weeks spying on the Ministry of Magic to plan a break-in. They observe the daily routines of various Ministry employees to prepare for their infiltration. Harry experiences a vision of Voldemort interrogating a woman about a man named Gregorovitch. Voldemort murders the woman and her children after learning Gregorovitch has moved away. The trio uses Puking Pastilles to incapacitate three Ministry workers and take their places. Hermione transforms into Mafalda Hopkirk, while Ron becomes Reg Cattermole and Harry becomes Albert Runcorn. They enter the Ministry through a public toilet and find it heavily altered by Death Eaters. A new statue in the Atrium depicts wizards sitting on a throne of crushed Muggles. Corban Yaxley intercepts the trio and orders Ron to fix a magical rainstorm in his office. Ron is forced to separate from Harry and Hermione to handle the task. Harry and Hermione enter a lift and find themselves face-to-face with Dolores Umbridge.


### 13: The Muggle-Born Registration Commission
Dolores Umbridge takes Hermione to a courtroom to record proceedings for Muggle-born interrogations. Harry, still disguised as Runcorn, searches Umbridge's office for the locket Horcrux. He finds Mad-Eye Moody's magical eye embedded in the door and pockets it. Harry discovers a file on Arthur Weasley stating that the Ministry is monitoring his movements. He also finds a biography of Albus Dumbledore written by Rita Skeeter on a shelf. Harry leaves the office and reunites with a soaking wet Ron in a lift. They encounter Arthur Weasley, and Harry warns him that the Ministry is tracking him. Harry descends to the courtroom levels and encounters several Dementors guarding the hallway outside. He enters the courtroom where Umbridge is interrogating Mary Cattermole about her magical heritage. Umbridge wears the locket Horcrux and lies by claiming it is a family heirloom. Harry stuns Umbridge and Yaxley before summoning his Patronus to drive away the Dementors. Hermione duplicates the locket and gives the real one to Harry before freeing Mrs. Cattermole. They lead a group of Muggle-borns out of the Ministry and through the fireplaces.


### 14: The Thief
Yaxley discovers the infiltration and grabs onto Hermione as the trio attempts to Disapparate. Hermione brings the group to Grimmauld Place but Yaxley sees the door and enters the protection. She immediately Disapparates again to a forest, but Ron is severely splinched in the process. A large chunk of flesh is missing from Ron's arm, and he loses much blood. Hermione uses Essence of Dittany to stop the bleeding and seal the majority of the wound. She explains that they can never return to Grimmauld Place because Yaxley is now inside. The trio sets up a tent in the woods and Hermione casts numerous protective enchantments. Ron insists they do not speak Voldemort's name because he believes it is jinxed. Harry wears the locket and feels its cold metal heart beating against his chest. He experiences a vision of Voldemort interrogating Gregorovitch about a stolen item from years ago. In the vision, a young blond man steals something small from Gregorovitch's workshop and escapes. Gregorovitch tells Voldemort he does not know the identity of the thief before dying. Harry realizes he has seen the laughing face of the blond thief somewhere before.


### 15: The Goblin’s Revenge
Harry buries Mad-Eye Moody's eye in the woods and marks the spot with a cross. The trio moves to a new location but Harry is unable to produce a Patronus. Hermione realizes the Horcrux is affecting Harry's magic and they agree to take turns wearing it. Ron becomes increasingly irritable and complains about the lack of food and the mission's progress. They overhear a group of fugitives including Dean Thomas, Ted Tonks, and two goblins. The goblin Griphook reveals that the sword of Gryffindor in Gringotts is actually a fake. Griphook explains that the real sword is goblin-made and can only be used by its rightful owner. The trio learns that Ginny, Neville, and Luna were punished for trying to steal the sword. Hermione summons the portrait of Phineas Nigellus Black to question him about the sword's history. Phineas reveals that Albus Dumbledore used the real sword to destroy a ring before his death. Hermione realizes the sword is impregnated with basilisk venom and can destroy other Horcruxes. Ron accuses Harry of having no plan and expresses deep frustration with their living conditions. Harry tells Ron to leave, and Ron abandons the mission, Disapparating into the rainy night.
