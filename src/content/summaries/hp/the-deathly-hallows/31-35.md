### 31: The Battle of Hogwarts
Professor McGonagall organizes the evacuation of underage students while those of age remain to defend the castle. Voldemort’s voice echoes through the Great Hall, demanding that Harry Potter be delivered to him by midnight to spare the school. Pansy Parkinson attempts to point Harry out, but the other three houses stand to protect him before the Slytherins are escorted out. Harry realizes the final Horcrux is Rowena Ravenclaw’s lost diadem, a magical crown believed to bestow wisdom upon the wearer. He seeks out the Gray Lady, the ghost of Ravenclaw Tower, who reveals she is Helena Ravenclaw and stole the diadem from her mother.

Helena explains that the Bloody Baron, a ghost covered in chains as an act of penitence for his crimes, murdered her in an Albanian forest after she refused to return home. She admits she told Tom Riddle the diadem’s location, and Harry deduces that Riddle hidden it in the Room of Requirement. Hagrid and Grawp, an undersized giant, arrive at the castle as the midnight deadline passes and the Death Eaters begin their assault. Harry encounters Ron and Hermione, who have returned from the Chamber of Secrets with basilisk fangs used to destroy Hufflepuff’s cup. Ron suggests saving the house-elves, which prompts Hermione to kiss him before they all head to the Room of Hidden Things.

Inside the room, Harry is confronted by Draco Malfoy, Crabbe, and Goyle, who intend to capture him for Voldemort's reward. Crabbe loses control of Fiendfyre, a cursed fire that takes the form of sentient flaming beasts like chimaeras and dragons. The inferno consumes the room and its contents, including the diadem, which Harry manages to snatch from the air while flying a broomstick. Harry and his friends rescue Malfoy and Goyle, but Crabbe is killed by his own uncontrollable spell as the room is destroyed. Outside the room, the diadem breaks apart and leaks a tarry substance, signifying the destruction of another Horcrux as the battle intensifies.



### 32: The Elder Wand
Harry, Ron, and Hermione witness Percy Weasley reconcile with his family before a massive explosion rocks the corridor. Fred Weasley is killed in the blast, leaving his family devastated as the castle wall collapses around them. A monstrous spider, a descendant of the giant arachnid Aragog, attempts to enter the breach before Harry and Ron blast it backward. The trio hides Fred’s body in a niche and moves through the castle, which is now filled with dust and falling masonry. Hermione insists that they must prioritize killing Nagini, Voldemort's snake, to make him mortal once again.

Harry looks into Voldemort’s mind and discovers he is hiding in the Shrieking Shack, a derelict house near Hogsmeade. Voldemort has Nagini protected inside a starry, transparent sphere that acts as a magical cage and floating tank. The trio travels under the Invisibility Cloak, witnessed the chaos of the battle as giants and dementors join the fray. They reach the Whomping Willow, a violent tree that guards a secret tunnel leading to the shack, and use a levitated twig to freeze its branches. Crawling through the tunnel, Harry overhears Voldemort telling Severus Snape that the Elder Wand, the legendary unbeatable Deathstick, is not performing correctly for him.

Voldemort concludes that the wand cannot serve him because its true master is the wizard who killed its previous owner, Albus Dumbledore. He orders Nagini to kill Snape, believing that Snape's death will finally transfer the wand’s full allegiance to him. The snake's fangs pierce Snape's neck through the enchanted cage, and Voldemort leaves the shack with his protected serpent. Harry enters the room as Snape dies, collecting a silvery blue substance containing Snape's memories into a glass flask. Snape's final request is for Harry to look into his eyes, which go blank as he expires from his wounds.



### 33: The Prince’s Tale
Voldemort’s magically amplified voice offers the defenders of Hogwarts a one-hour ceasefire to collect their dead and treat the injured. He specifically commands Harry to meet him in the Forbidden Forest within the hour or face the total destruction of the school. Harry returns to the castle and finds the Great Hall filled with casualties, including the bodies of Remus Lupin and Nymphadora Tonks. Overwhelmed by grief and guilt, Harry retreats to the headmaster's office to view Snape’s memories in the stone Pensieve. The memories reveal that Snape loved Lily Evans from childhood and was devastated when he inadvertently caused her death.

Snape had spent the years since Lily's death acting as a double agent for Dumbledore to protect Harry for Lily's sake. Harry watches Dumbledore explain that he is dying from a curse on Marvolo Gaunt’s ring and that Snape must be the one to kill him. Dumbledore reveals that a fragment of Voldemort’s soul latched onto Harry on the night his parents died, making Harry an unintended Horcrux. This connection grants Harry the ability to speak Parseltongue, the language of snakes, and allows him to see into Voldemort's thoughts. The headmaster insists that Harry must die at Voldemort's hand for the soul fragment to be destroyed and Voldemort to become mortal.

The memories show Snape's Patronus is a silver doe, identical to Lily's, which he used to lead Harry to the sword of Gryffindor. Snape also planted the idea of using Polyjuice Potion and decoys to ensure Harry’s safety during his departure from Privet Drive. Harry sees that Snape’s apparent betrayal was a carefully orchestrated plan to maintain his position as Voldemort's trusted lieutenant. Dumbledore confirms that Harry must not know the truth until the final moment to ensure he has the strength to sacrifice himself. Harry emerges from the Pensieve with the realization that his ultimate purpose is to walk calmly into his own death.



### 34: The Forest Again
Harry understands that he must sacrifice his life to destroy the fragment of Voldemort's soul living within him. He leaves the headmaster's office and walks through the silent castle, avoiding his friends to prevent them from stopping his mission. Near the entrance hall, he encounters Neville Longbottom and instructs him to kill Nagini if Ron and Hermione are unable to do so. Harry spots Ginny comforting an injured student on the grounds but chooses not to speak to her as he continues toward the forest. He reaches the edge of the trees and feels the chill of the dementors, who guard the path to the Death Eater camp.

Harry retrieves the Golden Snitch and whispers that he is about to die, causing the metal shell to break open. Inside lies the Resurrection Stone, a jagged black gem that can bring back the shadows of the deceased. He turns the stone three times and is joined by the figures of James Potter, Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, and Lily Potter. These shades are less than flesh but more than ghosts, and they act as Patronuses to protect Harry from the dementors. Sirius assures Harry that dying is quicker than falling asleep, while Lily tells him how proud they are of his bravery.

Harry drops the stone at the edge of the clearing where the Death Eaters have gathered around a large fire. He reveals himself to Voldemort, who is surrounded by his silent followers and the caged Nagini. Hagrid is bound to a tree nearby and shouts in protest before he is silenced by the Death Eater Rowle. Voldemort considers Harry for a moment with a mirthless smile, noting that he is the Boy Who Lived. Harry does not raise his wand to defend himself as Voldemort performs the Killing Curse. A flash of green light fills the clearing, and Harry loses consciousness as the spell hits him.



### 35: King’s Cross
Harry wakes up in a bright, misty version of King's Cross station, appearing completely naked before clean robes materialize for him. He discovers a small, flayed-looking creature whimpering under a seat, which represents the mangled fragment of Voldemort's soul. Albus Dumbledore appears, looking healthy and whole, and explains that Harry is not truly dead because Voldemort used Harry's blood to rebuild his body. Lily's protective enchantment lives on in Voldemort's veins, tethering Harry to life as long as Voldemort exists. Dumbledore confirms that the Killing Curse destroyed the Horcrux inside Harry while leaving Harry's own soul intact and whole.

Dumbledore explains the history of the Deathly Hallows, revealing that he and Gellert Grindelwald once sought them to become masters of death. He admits that his own sister, Ariana, died during a three-way duel between himself, his brother Aberforth, and Grindelwald. Dumbledore confesses his lifelong guilt over her death and his realization that he was not fit to be trusted with great power. He identifies the Elder Wand, the Resurrection Stone, and the Invisibility Cloak as the three Hallows created by the Peverell brothers. Harry is the true master of death because he accepted his mortality instead of trying to run away from it.

The headmaster details how Harry’s wand recognized Voldemort in the graveyard and regurgitated the Dark Lord's own magic against him. He clarifies that Voldemort's ignorance of love and ancient magic led to his repeated failures to kill Harry. Dumbledore expresses his hope that Grindelwald felt remorse for his crimes during his long imprisonment in the fortress of Nurmengard. Harry asks if he must go back, and Dumbledore tells him that he has a choice to move on or return to the battle. Harry decides to go back to finish the fight, and the bright station fades as he prepares to return to his body.
