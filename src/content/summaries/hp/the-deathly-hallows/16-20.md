### 16: Godric’s Hollow
Harry and Hermione remain in the tent alone following Ron's departure after a heated argument. They spend several days in near silence while Harry monitors the Marauder's Map for any sign of Ron or Ginny. Hermione discovers a mysterious triangular symbol drawn in her copy of The Tales of Beedle the Bard. Harry recognizes the mark as the one worn by Xenophilius Lovegood at Bill and Fleur's wedding. He recalls Viktor Krum identifying the symbol as the mark of the Dark wizard Gellert Grindelwald.

Hermione eventually agrees with Harry's long-standing desire to visit Godric's Hollow to search for Gryffindor's sword. She reasons that Albus Dumbledore may have hidden the sword with Bathilda Bagshot, a celebrated magical historian living there. They spend a week preparing disguises using Polyjuice Potion and practicing Apparition under the Invisibility Cloak. On Christmas Eve, the pair Apparates to the village and finds a statue of James, Lily, and infant Harry. They walk to the local church graveyard where they find the graves of Kendra and Ariana Dumbledore.

Harry discovers an ancient headstone belonging to Ignotus Peverell which bears the same triangular symbol found in Hermione's book. They finally locate the white marble grave of James and Lily Potter which features a biblical quotation. Harry experiences a surge of grief and uses Hermione's wand to create a wreath of Christmas roses for the site. He realizes that Dumbledore's family is buried in the same graveyard but never shared this connection with him. Hermione takes Harry's hand as they prepare to leave the snowy graveyard and continue their search.



### 17: Bathilda’s Secret
Hermione notices a figure watching them from the bushes as they leave the graveyard in the dark. They pull the Invisibility Cloak over themselves and walk toward the edge of the village to find the Potter cottage. The ruins of the house remain visible as a monument, covered in ivy and snow with the top floor blasted open. Harry touches the rusted gate, causing a commemorative sign to rise from the ground covered in supportive magical graffiti. An old, stooped woman who Harry assumes is Bathilda Bagshot approaches them and beckons them to follow her.

The woman leads them into a dark, foul-smelling house filled with dust and stacks of old books. Harry notices several photographs are missing from frames in the sitting room but finds one of a young man he recognizes. He realizes the man in the photo is the thief who stole from the wandmaker Gregorovitch. Bathilda refuses to speak in front of Hermione and signals for Harry to follow her upstairs alone. Hermione stays behind to light a fire while Harry accompanies the silent woman to a lightless bedroom.

Upstairs, Harry's scar begins to burn and he experiences a brief mental connection to Voldemort's triumphant thoughts. Bathilda indicates that she has something for him hidden beneath a pile of dirty laundry on a dressing table. As Harry looks away, Bathilda’s body collapses and a giant snake, Nagini, emerges from her neck to attack him. The snake coils around Harry and pins him to the floor while he struggles to reach his wand. Hermione rushes upstairs and uses a Blasting Curse that allows them to jump out the window just as Voldemort arrives.



### 18: The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore
Harry wakes up in the tent after a night of vivid, shared memories with Voldemort regarding the night his parents died. He discovers that his holly and phoenix feather wand was snapped during the narrow escape from Godric’s Hollow. Hermione informs him that she used a Severing Charm to remove the Horcrux locket which had burned into his chest. She attempts to repair his wand with a Mending Charm, but the device remains broken and non-functional. Harry feels vulnerable and angry at Dumbledore for leaving them without adequate guidance or resources.

Hermione provides Harry with a copy of Rita Skeeter’s biography of Dumbledore which she took from Bathilda Bagshot’s house. They find a photograph of a teenage Albus Dumbledore standing arm-in-arm with the thief, who is identified as Gellert Grindelwald. The book reveals that the two boys became close friends in Godric’s Hollow following the death of Dumbledore’s mother. Rita Skeeter includes a letter from Dumbledore to Grindelwald discussing the idea of wizards ruling over Muggles for the "greater good." This correspondence suggests that Dumbledore once supported the very ideologies he later spent his life fighting against.

The biography details the death of Dumbledore’s sister, Ariana, and the subsequent physical fight between Albus and his brother Aberforth. Grindelwald fled Godric’s Hollow immediately after the girl’s death, and the two friends did not meet again for decades. Harry reacts with explosive rage at the revelation that Dumbledore was once a potential Dark wizard who plotted against Muggles. Hermione tries to argue that Dumbledore changed over his life, but Harry feels betrayed by the secrecy. He remains embittered by the fact that Dumbledore shared more with Grindelwald than he ever shared with his own student.



### 19: The Silver Doe
Harry and Hermione relocate their camp to the Forest of Dean where they spend several days in cold, snowy isolation. While Harry is on watch one night, a bright silver patronus in the shape of a doe appears among the trees. The silent creature leads Harry deep into the woods to a small, frozen pool containing a glinting object. Harry looks through the ice and sees the ruby-encrusted hilt of the sword of Gryffindor at the bottom. He strips off his clothes and dives into the freezing water to retrieve the weapon.

The Horcrux locket around Harry's neck suddenly tightens and begins to strangle him as he reaches for the sword. Harry loses consciousness under the water but is suddenly pulled out of the pool and onto the snowy bank. He discovers that Ron Weasley has returned and saved him from drowning while also retrieving the sword. Ron explains that he found them by using Dumbledore's Deluminator, which guided him with a ball of blue light. Harry insists that Ron must be the one to use the sword to destroy the Horcrux locket.

Harry speaks in Parseltongue to open the locket, revealing two living eyes and a voice that taunts Ron’s insecurities. Distorted, phantom versions of Harry and Hermione emerge from the locket and mock Ron for being "least loved." The Riddle-versions of Harry and Hermione embrace and kiss, causing Ron to cry out in mental anguish. Ron eventually overcomes the vision and stabs the locket with the sword, destroying the piece of Voldemort's soul. Hermione is initially furious with Ron upon his return to the tent and attacks him with her fists.



### 20: Xenophilius Lovegood
Hermione remains angry at Ron despite his role in destroying the locket and saving Harry’s life. Ron attempts to appease her by acting somber while privately celebrating their progress with Harry during chores. He informs Harry about the "Taboo" placed on Voldemort's name, which allows Snatchers to track anyone who speaks it. Ron explains that Snatchers are gangs of wizards hunting Muggle-borns and blood traitors for gold rewards. They discuss the possibility that the silver doe patronus was sent by a friend like Kingsley Shacklebolt or Dumbledore.

Harry struggles to use a new blackthorn wand that Ron captured from a gang of Snatchers during his absence. Hermione notices a triangular symbol in a copy of Dumbledore's letter to Grindelwald and insists they visit Xenophilius Lovegood. She believes the symbol is a hidden clue from Dumbledore that they must investigate to understand the mission. Ron supports the idea to gain Hermione's favor, and the trio eventually Apparates to the village of Ottery St. Catchpole. They find the Lovegood house, a black cylindrical tower that Ron compares to a chess rook.

Xenophilius Lovegood greets them at the door wearing a stained nightshirt and looking extremely unkempt and nervous. He leads them to a cluttered circular workroom filled with printing presses and stacks of The Quibbler. Hermione identifies a large horn on the wall as a dangerous Erumpent horn, though Xenophilius insists it belongs to a Snorkack. Xenophilius explains that Luna is down at the stream fishing for Plimpies and offers to go find her. He appears conflicted and frightened while serving the group Gurdyroot infusion in mismatched teacups. Harry waits in the room and notices a stone bust of a witch wearing a bizarre, handcrafted headdress.
