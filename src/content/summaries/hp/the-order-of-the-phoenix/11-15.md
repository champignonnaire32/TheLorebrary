### Chapter 11: The Sorting Hat’s New Song
Harry enters the Great Hall and notices the absence of Hagrid at the staff table. Professor Grubbly-Plank takes Hagrid's seat while the first-years enter for the Sorting ceremony. The Sorting Hat sings a new song that warns the school of external dangers and the need for internal unity. Albus Dumbledore introduces Dolores Umbridge as the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher. Umbridge interrupts Dumbledore’s speech to deliver a dull, Ministry-focused address about educational progress and tradition.

The Gryffindor students return to their common room where Harry realizes he does not know the new password. Neville Longbottom provides the password and shows Harry his new cactus, the Mimbulus mimbletonia. Inside the boys' dormitory, Seamus Finnigan reveals that his mother did not want him to return to Hogwarts. Seamus questions Harry about the night Cedric Diggory died and expresses disbelief in Harry's account. Harry shouts at Seamus and Ron Weasley intervenes as a prefect to stop the argument.

Neville defends Harry by stating that his grandmother believes Dumbledore’s claim about the return of Voldemort. Harry goes to sleep feeling isolated by the skepticism of his classmates and the Ministry’s influence. The next morning, Seamus continues to avoid Harry as they prepare for the first day of classes. Hermione identifies a sign in the common room advertising for student testers for Fred and George's joke products. She removes the sign and warns Ron that as prefects they must stop such unauthorized activities.



### Chapter 12: Professor Umbridge
Harry attends his first classes of the term, starting with a boring History of Magic lecture by Professor Binns. During a break, Harry encounters Cho Chang in the courtyard and attempts a brief, awkward conversation. Ron Weasley interrupts the meeting to question Cho about her support for the Tutshill Tornados Quidditch team. Hermione later scolds Ron for his lack of tact during Harry's interaction with Cho. The trio proceeds to the dungeons for a double Potions lesson with Professor Snape.

Snape instructs the class to brew the Draught of Peace and threatens to fail students who do not meet his standards. Harry forgets to add syrup of hellebore to his cauldron, causing his potion to emit grey steam. Snape vanishes the contents of Harry’s cauldron and awards him zero marks for the day. Harry leaves the dungeon in a temper and eventually heads to the North Tower for Divination. Professor Trelawney assigns a month-long dream diary and predicts Harry’s death once again.

The fifth-year students enter the Defense Against the Dark Arts classroom to find Professor Umbridge waiting. Umbridge instructs the class to put away their wands and read a theoretical textbook by Wilbert Slinkhard. Hermione challenges Umbridge’s course aims, noting the complete lack of practical spellwork in the curriculum. Harry shouts that the class needs to learn magic to defend against Lord Voldemort’s return. Umbridge gives Harry a week of detentions and sends him to Professor McGonagall with a note.



### Chapter 13: Detention with Dolores
Harry reports to Professor McGonagall’s office where she warns him to keep his temper under control. She offers him a biscuit and explains that Umbridge is reporting directly to the Ministry of Magic. Harry returns to the Gryffindor common room and observes Fred and George testing Fainting Fancies on first-year students. Hermione threatens to write to Mrs. Weasley to stop the twins from using younger students as test subjects. She also begins knitting woolly hats and hiding them in the common room to free house-elves.

The following evening, Harry reports to Umbridge’s office for his first detention at five o'clock. Umbridge provides a special thin, black quill and instructs Harry to write "I must not tell lies" repeatedly. Harry realizes the quill uses his own blood as ink and carves the words into the back of his hand. He refuses to show any sign of pain as the words repeatedly cut into his skin and then heal. The detention lasts past midnight, leaving Harry’s hand red and raw for the next day.

Harry skips breakfast to finish his homework and hides the injury on his hand from Ron and Hermione. He endures subsequent nights of the same punishment as the cut on his hand becomes permanently etched and inflamed. Angelina Johnson berates Harry for missing Quidditch tryouts due to his detentions with Umbridge. On Friday night, Harry watches the Gryffindor Quidditch trials from Umbridge’s window while continuing his lines. Harry returns to the common room to find that Ron has successfully become the new Gryffindor Keeper.



### Chapter 14: Percy and Padfoot
Harry wakes up early on Saturday and writes a cryptic letter to Sirius Black using the pseudonym Snuffles. He visits the Owlery where he encounters Cho Chang and helps her attach a parcel to an owl. Filch the caretaker interrupts them and accuses Harry of ordering Dungbombs based on a false tip-off. Harry returns to the castle and learns from the Daily Prophet that the Ministry believes Sirius is in London. Sturgis Podmore, a member of the Order of the Phoenix, is reported to have been sentenced to Azkaban.

The Gryffindor Quidditch team holds its first official practice session with Ron Weasley as the new Keeper. Ron performs poorly, dropping several Quaffles and missing goals while the Slytherin team jeers from the stands. Katie Bell suffers a severe nosebleed after being hit by a Quaffle and then accidentally swallowing a Blood Blisterpod. The practice is abandoned, and Ron returns to the common room feeling discouraged by his performance. Hermione helps Harry and Ron finish their Astronomy essays while they wait for the common room to empty.

Sirius Black’s head appears in the Gryffindor fireplace to communicate with the trio at midnight. Sirius explains that the Ministry is interfering at Hogwarts because Fudge fears Dumbledore is building a private army. He confirms that Hagrid has not yet returned from his mission and that the Order is uncertain of his status. Sirius warns the trio that his own presence in London has been reported to the Ministry by a reliable source. Harry and Hermione prevent Sirius from visiting Hogsmeade in his dog form, leading Sirius to depart the fire abruptly.



### Chapter 15: The Hogwarts High Inquisitor
The Daily Prophet announces that Dolores Umbridge has been appointed as the first Hogwarts High Inquisitor. This new role grants her the power to inspect other teachers and evaluate their performance in the classroom. Umbridge begins her inspections by observing Professor Trelawney in Divination and demanding she make a live prediction. Trelawney predicts grave danger for Umbridge, but the Inquisitor dismisses the prophecy and continues her notes. Harry receives a "D" grade on his moonstone essay from Snape, which represents a "Dreadful" mark.

Professor McGonagall conducts a Transfiguration lesson on vanishing mice while Umbridge observes from a corner. McGonagall repeatedly ignores Umbridge’s interruptions and eventually commands the Inquisitor to stop talking while she is lecturing. Umbridge later inspects Care of Magical Creatures and questions Professor Grubbly-Plank about Hagrid’s absence. Draco Malfoy takes the opportunity to tell Umbridge about his previous injury from a Hippogriff in Hagrid's class. Harry defends Hagrid’s teaching methods, resulting in another night of detention and a loss of house points.

Hermione suggests that Harry should teach the students Defense Against the Dark Arts himself since Umbridge refuses to do so. Harry initially rejects the idea, claiming his past successes against Voldemort were primarily due to luck and help. He has an angry outburst in the common room, describing the terror of facing death while Ron and Hermione watch in shock. Hermione insists that Harry’s experience is exactly why the other students need his instruction to survive. Harry eventually agrees to consider the proposal and goes to bed with his scar prickling in his sleep.
