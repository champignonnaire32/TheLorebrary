### 1: Dudley Demented
Harry Potter hides in a flowerbed at Privet Drive to listen to the Muggle news through an open window. He hears a loud crack like a gunshot and jumps up, but his head hits the window frame. Uncle Vernon reaches out and grabs Harry by the throat after the noise startles the Dursleys inside. Harry walks away and encounters his cousin Dudley and his gang returning from vandalizing a park. He taunts Dudley about his boxing and his night terrors as they walk into a dark alleyway.

The night suddenly becomes pitch black and freezing cold, causing the stars and streetlamps to vanish completely. Dudley panics and strikes Harry in the head, causing Harry to drop his wand in the total darkness. Harry crawls on the ground and finds his wand by casting a Lumos spell to ignite the tip. A Dementor, a tall hooded figure that sucks happiness and warmth from the air, glides toward Harry. Harry struggles to cast a Patronus Charm as the Dementor's slimy hands reach for his throat.

Harry thinks of his friends Ron and Hermione to produce a silver stag Patronus that drives the first Dementor away. He runs further down the alley and finds a second Dementor attempting to perform the Dementor's Kiss on Dudley. The silver stag charges the second creature and forces it to flee into the surrounding darkness. The stars and lights return to the alley as the cold atmosphere dissipates and the Dementors vanish. Mrs. Figg, Harry's neighbor, arrives panting and reveals she is a Squib who was assigned to watch him.



### 2: A Peck of Owls
Mrs. Figg reveals that a wizard named Mundungus Fletcher was supposed to be guarding Harry but left his post. She explains that Dumbledore ordered her to watch over Harry without his knowledge for several years. Harry hauls the semi-conscious and vomiting Dudley back toward Privet Drive while Mrs. Figg keeps watch. Mundungus Fletcher Apparates in front of them with a crack and is physically attacked by Mrs. Figg. She orders Mundungus to report the Dementor attack to Dumbledore immediately before he vanishes again.

Harry enters the Dursley house and is confronted by Vernon and Petunia about Dudley's traumatized state. Dudley points at Harry and accuses him of using magic to cause the terrifying experience in the alley. A screech owl arrives and delivers an official letter from the Ministry of Magic regarding Harry's expulsion. The letter states he performed a Patronus Charm in a Muggle area and Ministry representatives will destroy his wand. Harry prepares to flee the house with his wand until a second owl arrives with a message from Arthur Weasley.

Arthur's letter instructs Harry to stay at the house, keep his wand, and perform no further magic. Uncle Vernon demands an explanation of the events while Harry explains that Dementors are guards from the wizard prison. Aunt Petunia reveals she knows about Dementors and Azkaban because she overheard a conversation years ago. A third owl brings a letter stating the Ministry has suspended Harry's expulsion pending a formal hearing. A red Howler envelope arrives for Aunt Petunia and shouts the message "Remember my last, Petunia" before burning.



### 3: The Advance Guard
Harry spends several days locked in his room at Privet Drive after sending frantic letters to his friends. He receives no replies and is left to stew in silence while the Dursleys avoid him. The Dursleys eventually leave the house after being tricked by a fake award letter for a lawn competition. Harry hears a crash in the kitchen and finds a group of wizards standing in his hallway. Remus Lupin and Alastor Moody lead a guard of nine people who have come to rescue him.

Nymphadora Tonks, a Metamorphmagus, demonstrates her ability to change her hair color and helps Harry pack his trunk. The wizards use a Disillusionment Charm on Harry to make him look like a human chameleon for the flight. They mount broomsticks and fly in a strict formation across the night sky toward London. Harry experiences extreme cold during the long flight but enjoys the sensation of being back in the air. They land in a small, unkempt square and Moody uses a Put-Outer to extinguish the streetlamps.

Moody hands Harry a piece of parchment that reveals the secret location of the Order's headquarters. Harry reads that the headquarters are located at number twelve, Grimmauld Place in London. The wizards stand in the square and prepare to enter the hidden building that Muggles cannot see. They walk across the road as the group remains alert for any signs of being followed. Harry holds his Firebolt and follows the Advance Guard toward the invisible house between numbers eleven and thirteen.



### 4: Number Twelve, Grimmauld Place
A battered door and grimy walls materialize between numbers eleven and thirteen as Harry thinks of the secret address. The group enters a dark, damp hallway filled with the smell of decay and serpent-shaped decorations. Mrs. Weasley greets Harry with a hug and informs the group that a meeting is currently in progress. She leads Harry upstairs past a row of shrunken house-elf heads mounted on the wall like trophies. Harry is placed in a bedroom where Ron and Hermione are waiting to reunite with him.

Hermione hugs Harry and explains that Dumbledore forbade them from sending him any specific news during the summer. Harry shouts at his friends for four weeks of isolation while they were together at the secret headquarters. He lists his past accomplishments, including saving the Sorcerer's Stone and surviving the graveyard, to justify his anger. Ron and Hermione reveal that the Order of the Phoenix is a secret society founded to fight Voldemort. Fred and George Apparate into the room and demonstrate their Extendable Ears for eavesdropping on meetings.

The twins explain that Percy Weasley has broken ties with his family and is working for Minister Cornelius Fudge. Percy accused his father of having no ambition and chose to side with the Ministry against Dumbledore. Hermione explains that the Daily Prophet is running a smear campaign to portray Harry as a deluded liar. Mrs. Weasley interrupts the gathering to announce that dinner is ready and the meeting has concluded. Tonks trips over a troll-leg umbrella stand in the hall, causing a portrait of Sirius's mother to scream.



### 5: The Order of the Phoenix
Sirius Black and Remus Lupin struggle to close the curtains over the screaming portrait of Sirius's mother. Sirius explains that the house belonged to his parents and he has offered it as Order headquarters. The group moves into a basement kitchen where a large fire provides the primary source of light. Mr. Weasley and Bill are seen rolling up secret plans as the children enter the room. Mundungus Fletcher is present and attempts to apologize to Harry for leaving his guard post earlier.

Mrs. Weasley serves a dinner of stew and rhubarb crumble while the adults discuss recruitment and goblin relations. Sirius complains about being confined to the house because the Ministry is still hunting for him as a fugitive. After dinner, Sirius insists on answering Harry's questions about Voldemort's current activities and future plans. Mrs. Weasley objects and argues that Harry is too young to be involved in the Order's dangerous business. A heated argument breaks out between Sirius and Mrs. Weasley regarding Harry's welfare and his relationship to his father.

Lupin intervenes and decides that Harry should be given a general picture of the current situation. Sirius reveals that Voldemort is keeping a low profile to build his army in secret without alerting the Ministry. The Minister for Magic, Cornelius Fudge, refuses to believe Voldemort has returned and is discrediting Dumbledore. Sirius explains that the Order is trying to prevent Voldemort from obtaining a weapon he did not have before. Mrs. Weasley ends the conversation by ordering all the teenagers to go to bed immediately.
