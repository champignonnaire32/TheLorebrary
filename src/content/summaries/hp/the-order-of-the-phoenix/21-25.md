### 21: The Eye of the Snake
Hermione visits Hagrid through deep snow and learns he is planning a surprise lesson involving creatures he found in the Forbidden Forest. Harry, Ron, and Hermione later follow Hagrid into the woods where he presents half a cow carcass as bait for invisible predators. Only Harry, Neville, and a Slytherin student can see the creatures, which are revealed to be Thestrals, skeletal winged horses visible only to those who have witnessed death. Professor Umbridge arrives to inspect the class and mocks Hagrid's teaching style and parentage while taking critical notes on her clipboard. During a D.A. meeting, Harry celebrates the group's progress and eventually finds himself alone with Cho Chang in the Room of Requirement.

Cho cries while discussing Cedric Diggory's death and mentions how Harry's teaching helps her feel more capable. Harry and Cho kiss under a clump of mistletoe before Harry returns to the Gryffindor common room to recount the event to Ron and Hermione. That night, Harry falls into a deep sleep and experiences a vivid vision where he is a snake attacking a man in a dark corridor. He feels himself strike the man repeatedly and sees the victim collapse in a pool of blood as his own scar burns with intense pain. Harry wakes up screaming and tells Ron that Mr. Weasley has been seriously injured by a giant snake.

Professor McGonagall arrives at the dormitory and takes Harry and Ron to Dumbledore’s office after hearing Harry’s frantic explanation. Dumbledore uses silver instruments and portrait messengers to confirm that Arthur Weasley was attacked at the Ministry of Magic. The Headmaster ignores Harry’s direct gaze but organizes a Portkey to send Harry and the Weasley children to 12 Grimmauld Place. Upon arrival, Sirius Black greets them and explains that the Order of the Phoenix is handling the situation. A message from Mrs. Weasley eventually arrives via Fawkes the phoenix to confirm that Arthur is alive and currently being treated.



### 22: St. Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries
The Weasley children and Harry wait through a tense night at Grimmauld Place until Mrs. Weasley returns with news that Arthur will survive. Harry tells Sirius about his vision and confesses that he felt as though he were the snake during the attack. Sirius dismisses Harry's fears of possession and encourages him to get some sleep before they visit the hospital later that day. The group travels by the London Underground to a derelict department store called Purge and Dowse Ltd, which serves as a magical front. They step through a window display to enter the crowded reception area of St. Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries.

The hospital is filled with witches and wizards suffering from magical ailments like dragon pox and incorrectly applied charms. They find Arthur Weasley in the Dai Llewellyn Ward for Serious Bites, where his wounds continue to bleed due to snake venom. Arthur explains that he was on duty for the Order when a giant serpent ambushed him in a windowless corridor. Mrs. Weasley prevents the children from hearing more details about Arthur's specific mission or the weapon he was guarding. Mad-Eye Moody and Tonks arrive to visit Arthur and suggest that Voldemort might be using Harry to spy on the Order's activities.

Harry overhears Moody’s theory through Extendable Ears and becomes convinced that he is being possessed or used as a weapon. He retreats into a dark pantry and tells Sirius that he felt a sudden urge to attack Dumbledore during their meeting. Sirius insists that Harry is merely in shock and needs to stop blaming himself for witnessing a tragedy that saved a life. The group spends the rest of the day in a somber mood as they prepare to stay at Sirius's house for the holidays. Harry remains isolated and fearful of his own thoughts as he contemplates the connection between his mind and Voldemort's.



### 23: Christmas on the Closed Ward
Harry decides to run away from Grimmauld Place to protect the others but is stopped by a message from Dumbledore delivered by Phineas Nigellus. He spends several days in isolation, convinced that he is the weapon Voldemort is seeking to use against the Order. Hermione arrives for the holidays and joins Ron and Ginny in confronting Harry about his avoidant behavior. Ginny explains that her own experience with possession involved memory gaps, which Harry has not experienced during his recent visions. This realization relieves Harry's fear of immediate possession and he finally rejoins the group for the festive preparations.

On Christmas morning, the residents open gifts and discover that Percy Weasley has returned his hand-knitted jumper without a note. Hermione leaves a gift in Kreacher’s boiler-cupboard "den," where they find various Black family heirlooms and a photograph of Bellatrix Lestrange. The group travels to St. Mungo's in a borrowed car to visit Arthur, who has unsuccessfully tried to heal his wounds with Muggle stitches. While searching for tea, the teenagers encounter Gilderoy Lockhart, their former teacher, who is a permanent resident in the Janus Thickey Ward. Lockhart has lost his memory and spends his time signing autographs for fans who no longer write to him.

In the same ward, the group discovers Neville Longbottom visiting his parents, Frank and Alice, who were tortured into insanity by Death Eaters. Neville is visibly embarrassed as his grandmother, Augusta, berates him for not being proud of his parents' sacrifice for the wizarding community. Neville’s mother gives him an empty chewing gum wrapper, which Neville quietly pockets despite his grandmother’s instructions to throw it away. Harry reveals to his friends that Dumbledore told him the truth about the Longbottoms and Bellatrix Lestrange's role in their condition. The group leaves the hospital in a quiet and reflective mood after witnessing the permanent damage caused by the Cruciatus Curse.



### 24: Occlumency
The mood at Grimmauld Place darkens as the end of the holidays approaches and Sirius becomes increasingly sullen about Harry's departure. Snape arrives at the house to inform Harry that he must take private Occlumency lessons to protect his mind from Voldemort. Sirius and Snape engage in a heated argument that nearly results in a duel before the Weasley family interrupts them. Snape mocks Sirius's confinement and orders Harry to meet him in his office every Monday evening for the lessons. Harry receives a mysterious, wrapped package from Sirius with instructions to use it only if he needs help during his time at school.

The students return to Hogwarts on the Knight Bus and Harry eventually tells Ron and Hermione about his upcoming training with Snape. Harry asks Cho Chang to Hogsmeade for Valentine’s Day and she enthusiastically accepts his invitation. During the first Occlumency lesson, Snape explains that the Dark Lord is a master of Legilimency and can extract memories through eye contact. Snape repeatedly enters Harry's mind using the Legilimens spell, forcing Harry to relive painful memories of the Dursleys and Cedric's death. Harry struggles to clear his mind and accidentally sees a vision of the windowless corridor he has been dreaming about for months.

Harry realizes the corridor leads to the Department of Mysteries and suspects that Voldemort is seeking something hidden within that specific location. Snape becomes agitated when Harry mentions the Department of Mysteries and abruptly ends the lesson while demanding more practice. Back in the common room, Harry experiences a sudden burst of intense, maniacal happiness that he knows is coming from Voldemort. He collapses in pain as his scar burns, telling Ron that something very good has happened for the Dark Lord. Harry fears that his mind is becoming more vulnerable to Voldemort's emotions rather than more protected by the lessons.



### 25: The Beetle at Bay
The morning newspaper reveals a mass breakout from Azkaban involving ten high-security Death Eaters, including Bellatrix Lestrange and Antonin Dolohov. Cornelius Fudge publicly blames Sirius Black for organizing the escape despite Dumbledore's previous warnings about the Dementors' loyalty. Hermione receives a letter and arranges a secret meeting at the Three Broomsticks, though she refuses to disclose the details to Ron and Harry. Another article reports the death of Broderick Bode, who was strangled by a gift-wrapped Devil's Snare plant while recovering at St. Mungo's. Harry and his friends realize Bode was an Unspeakable and that his murder was likely intended to keep him from talking.

Umbridge issues Educational Decree Number Twenty-six, which prohibits teachers from giving students any information not related to their specific subjects. Hagrid is placed on probation after a series of inspections by Umbridge and becomes increasingly jumpy during his classes. Neville Longbottom shows remarkable improvement in D.A. meetings as he works relentlessly on defensive spells following the news of the breakout. On Valentine's Day, Harry meets Cho in Hogsmeade and they visit Madam Puddifoot's teashop, which is filled with kissing couples and decorative cherubs. The date turns disastrous when Cho brings up Cedric Diggory and becomes jealous of Harry's planned meeting with Hermione.

Cho leaves the teashop in tears after Harry refuses to discuss Cedric's death and laughs at her misunderstanding of his plans. Harry meets Hermione and Luna at the Three Broomsticks, where they are joined by a disgraced and disheveled Rita Skeeter. Hermione blackmails Rita into writing a true account of Harry's experience in the graveyard by threatening to report her as an unregistered Animagus. Rita is forced to agree to conduct an interview for The Quibbler, Luna's father's magazine, without receiving any payment for the work. Harry begins to tell the full story of Voldemort's return as Rita records his words with her Quick-Quotes Quill.
