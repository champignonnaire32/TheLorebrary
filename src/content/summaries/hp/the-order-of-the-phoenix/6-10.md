### 6: The Noble and Most Ancient House of Black
Harry and Ron wake up in the gloomy bedroom at Grimmauld Place and discuss the secret weapon mentioned by Sirius. Mrs. Weasley leads a group to de-Doxify the drawing room using Doxycide spray while the twins secretly harvest venom. Sirius shows Harry the Black family tapestry and explains how his mother blasted his name off it after he ran away. The family tree reveals Sirius is related to the Malfoys and the Lestranges, including the imprisoned Bellatrix. Kreacher the house-elf mutters insults while attempting to steal family heirlooms from the trash sacks.

The group spends several days cleaning the house, encountering a Boggart in a writing desk and a murderous ghoul in a toilet. Sirius tells Harry about his brother Regulus, who was murdered after trying to leave the Death Eaters. Harry learns that Dumbledore is the Secret Keeper for the Order of the Phoenix and that the house is unplottable. Mundungus Fletcher attempts to store stolen cauldrons in the house, triggering a screaming match with Mrs. Weasley. Sirius expresses his frustration at being trapped in the house and offers to escort Harry to his hearing in his dog form.

Mrs. Weasley irons Harry's best clothes and insists he wash his hair to make a good impression at the Ministry. Mr. Weasley informs Harry that he will be the one escorting him to the hearing the following morning. Harry worries about being expelled and having to return to the Dursleys permanently. He learns that Dumbledore visited the house during the night but did not ask to see him. The chapter ends with Harry feeling a crushing sense of dread about the upcoming disciplinary hearing.



### 7: The Ministry of Magic
Harry wakes up early and joins Mr. Weasley, Sirius, Lupin, and Tonks for a tense breakfast in the kitchen. Mr. Weasley wears Muggle clothing to travel to the Ministry in a non-magical fashion to avoid making a bad impression. They take the Underground into central London, where Mr. Weasley struggles to navigate the Muggle ticket machines and money. They reach a shabby street and enter a broken red telephone box that serves as the visitor's entrance. Mr. Weasley dials 62442, and the booth descends into the ground like an elevator to reach the Ministry.

They emerge into the grand Atrium, which features a golden fountain of magical brethren and many enchanted fireplaces. Harry goes through security where his wand is registered as eleven inches with a phoenix-feather core. They take a lift through various levels, including the Department of Magical Games and Sports and the Department of Magical Transportation. On Level Two, they visit the Auror Headquarters where Harry sees posters of Sirius and meets Kingsley Shacklebolt. Mr. Weasley and Harry briefly hide in Kingsley's cubicle to receive a message and a magazine for Sirius.

They reach Mr. Weasley's cramped office in the Misuse of Muggle Artifacts department where Perkins delivers urgent news. The hearing time has been moved up to eight o'clock and the location changed to Courtroom Ten. Harry and Mr. Weasley sprint through the Ministry and take the lift down to the Department of Mysteries. They run down several flights of stairs and through a stone corridor that resembles a dungeon. Mr. Weasley stops at the door of Courtroom Ten and tells Harry he must go in alone.



### 8: The Hearing
Harry enters Courtroom Ten and recognizes it as the place where the Lestranges were sentenced to Azkaban. He sits in a chair with chains on the arms while fifty members of the Wizengamot stare down at him. Cornelius Fudge leads the interrogation alongside Amelia Bones and the toad-like Dolores Umbridge. Fudge lists the charges regarding the illegal use of a Patronus Charm in a Muggle area. Albus Dumbledore suddenly arrives to act as a witness for the defense, much to Fudge's visible annoyance.

Dumbledore presents Arabella Figg as a witness who can testify to the presence of Dementors in Little Whinging. Mrs. Figg describes the cold sensation and the loss of happiness she felt during the Dementor attack. Fudge argues that it is impossible for Dementors to be in a Muggle suburb by coincidence. Dumbledore suggests that the Dementors were ordered there by someone within the Ministry of Magic. Umbridge interrupts in a girlish voice to mock the suggestion that the Ministry would attack a boy.

Fudge attempts to bring up Harry's past magical mishaps, but Dumbledore reminds him those are not relevant to the current charge. Dumbledore points out that the Ministry has been making many irregular changes to the law recently. Madam Bones calls for a vote, and more than half of the Wizengamot raise their hands to clear Harry. Fudge reluctantly declares Harry cleared of all charges in a voice distorted by rage. Dumbledore immediately leaves the courtroom without speaking to or looking at Harry once.



### 9: The Woes of Mrs. Weasley
Harry exits the courtroom and informs Mr. Weasley that he has been cleared of all charges. They encounter Lucius Malfoy talking to Cornelius Fudge on Level Nine, where Malfoy makes a snide comment about Harry's luck. Mr. Weasley warns Harry that Malfoy is likely giving gold to the Minister to influence Ministry policy. They return to Grimmauld Place where the Weasleys and Hermione celebrate the news with a war dance. Harry notices that Sirius seems moody and disappointed that Harry will not be staying with him permanently.

The Hogwarts booklists arrive, and Ron and Hermione discover they have both been appointed as Gryffindor prefects. Mrs. Weasley shrieks with joy and promises Ron a new broomstick as a reward for his achievement. Harry feels a pang of jealousy and resentment, wondering why Dumbledore did not choose him instead of Ron. He struggles with his feelings of arrogance but eventually manages to congratulate Ron sincerely. The twins mock Ron's new authority while Harry tries to process his own feelings of being overlooked.

During a celebratory dinner, Mad-Eye Moody shows Harry an old photograph of the original Order of the Phoenix. Harry sees his parents and the traitor Wormtail alongside many members who were eventually killed or driven mad. Later, Harry finds Mrs. Weasley in the drawing room sobbing over a Boggart that keeps changing into the corpses of her family. Lupin arrives to banish the Boggart and comforts her while she expresses her fear of losing everyone in the war. Harry's scar sears with pain again, making him feel much older and more burdened than before.



### 10: Luna Lovegood
The group wakes up early and rushes to King's Cross station under the protection of an Order guard. Sirius accompanies them in his animagus dog form despite Mrs. Weasley's protests that Dumbledore forbade it. They board the Hogwarts Express where Ron and Hermione must leave Harry to attend a prefects' meeting. Harry and Ginny find a compartment containing Neville Longbottom and a Ravenclaw girl named Luna Lovegood. Luna wears a necklace of Butterbeer corks and reads a magazine called The Quibbler upside-down.

Neville shows off a rare Mimbulus mimbletonia plant which accidentally sprays everyone in the compartment with stinking green Stinksap. Cho Chang stops by to say hello just as Harry is covered in the foul-smelling liquid, causing him great embarrassment. Ron and Hermione return and report that Draco Malfoy and Pansy Parkinson are the new Slytherin prefects. Harry reads an article in The Quibbler that claims Sirius Black is actually a singer named Stubby Boardman. Draco Malfoy appears at the compartment door to taunt Harry about not being a prefect.

The train arrives at Hogsmeade Station in the dark, and the students head toward the stagecoaches. Harry notices that the coaches are now pulled by skeletal, winged, horse-like creatures with dragonish heads. Ron and Hermione cannot see the creatures and express concern that Harry might be imagining things. Luna Lovegood tells Harry that she can see them too and that he is not going mad. The chapter ends as the group climbs into a carriage to head up to the castle.
