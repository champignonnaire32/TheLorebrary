### 16: The Goblet of Fire
The Great Hall hosts a feast where Dumbledore introduces the Goblet of Fire, a roughly hewn wooden cup filled with dancing blue-white flames. An Age Line is drawn around the artifact to prevent any student under seventeen from submitting their name. Fred and George Weasley attempt to bypass this barrier using an Aging Potion but are violently repelled and sprout long white beards. Harry, Ron, and Hermione visit Hagrid, who has slicked back his hair with grease and wears a hairy brown suit to impress Madame Maxime. The 24-hour entry period ends, and the students gather in the candlelit Hall for the official announcement of the champions.

Dumbledore extinguishes the candles and waits for the Goblet of Fire to eject the selected parchment slips. Viktor Krum is announced as the representative for Durmstrang and enters a side chamber for further instructions. Fleur Delacour, a student with silvery-blonde hair who is revealed to be part Veela (a race of beautiful, magical humanoids), is chosen for Beauxbatons. Cedric Diggory is named the official Hogwarts champion, prompting a massive celebration from the Hufflepuff table. The fire in the goblet suddenly turns red again and shoots out a fourth piece of charred parchment despite the three-school limit.

Dumbledore catches the paper and reads the name Harry Potter to the stunned and silent crowd. Harry sits frozen in his seat as he realizes he has been selected despite never entering the tournament. Hermione nudges him to get up, and he stumbles toward the staff table under the gaze of hundreds of people. He passes the teachers and enters the smaller room where the other three champions are already waiting. The Great Hall erupts into a loud buzzing of angry whispers and confusion as the door closes behind him.



### 17: The Four Champions
Harry enters the side chamber and finds Fleur, Krum, and Cedric standing by a roaring fireplace. Ludo Bagman enters and excitedly announces that Harry is the fourth Triwizard champion. Fleur Delacour dismisses the news as a joke, pointing out that Harry is too young to compete. The headmasters and teachers burst into the room to argue about the breach of the tournament rules. Madame Maxime and Professor Karkaroff accuse Dumbledore of cheating to give Hogwarts two chances to win the prize.

Snape blames Harry’s thirst for fame, but Dumbledore asks Harry directly if he submitted his name. Harry denies entering himself or asking an older student to bypass the Age Line on his behalf. Barty Crouch, the Head of the Department of International Magical Cooperation, rules that the Goblet's selection constitutes a binding magical contract. Mad-Eye Moody suggests that a powerful wizard used a Confundus Charm (a spell used to confuse objects or people) on the Goblet to trick it. Moody argues that Harry was entered under a fourth school to ensure his selection and potential death in the tasks.

The judges eventually accept the situation, and Crouch provides the champions with their first set of instructions. He explains that the first task will test their daring and will take place on November twenty-fourth. Harry leaves with Cedric, who clearly does not believe Harry’s claim that he did not enter. Upon returning to the Gryffindor common room, Harry is swarmed by celebrating students but finds Ron waiting in the dormitory. Ron reveals his deep jealousy and accuses Harry of lying about how he crossed the Age Line, ending their friendship.



### 18: The Weighing of the Wands
Hermione meets Harry on Sunday morning and brings him toast, confirming that she believes he did not enter the tournament. She explains that Ron is jealous of the attention Harry receives and feels shunted to the side. Harry writes a letter to Sirius Black to inform him of the situation as Sirius had previously requested. The school environment turns hostile as the Hufflepuffs treat Harry like a glory-stealer and the Slytherins wear magical badges. These "Support Cedric Diggory" badges can be tapped to display the glowing green message "Potter Stinks."

Draco Malfoy insults Hermione’s blood status, leading to a duel where Harry and Draco fire spells simultaneously. The spells ricochet, causing Goyle to grow boils and Hermione’s front teeth to grow past her chin. Snape refuses to help Hermione, claiming he sees no difference in her appearance, and she flees to the hospital wing in tears. Colin Creevey arrives to take Harry to the Wand Weighing ceremony, which is being held in a small classroom. Rita Skeeter, a reporter for the Daily Prophet, corners Harry in a broom cupboard and uses a Quick-Quotes Quill to write a sensationalized interview.

Dumbledore rescues Harry from the cupboard and introduces Mr. Ollivander, the wand-maker who will inspect the champions' tools. Ollivander tests Fleur’s wand, which contains a hair from a Veela, and Cedric’s wand, which holds a unicorn hair. He examines Krum’s wand, a Gregorovitch creation made of hornbeam and dragon heartstring, before checking Harry’s holly wand. The ceremony concludes with a long photoshoot where Skeeter repeatedly pushes Harry to the front of the group. Harry returns to his dormitory and receives a reply from Sirius asking for a face-to-face meeting in the common room fire.



### 19: The Hungarian Horntail
Rita Skeeter publishes a front-page article that portrays Harry as a tragic hero and invents quotes about his parents. The article also claims that Harry and Hermione are in a romantic relationship, leading to more teasing from the Slytherins. Harry continues to struggle with Summoning Charms in preparation for the unknown first task. During a Hogsmeade trip under his Invisibility Cloak, Harry sees Hagrid and Moody in the Three Broomsticks. Hagrid whispers for Harry to meet him at his cabin at midnight while wearing the cloak.

Harry sneaks out at midnight and follows Hagrid to the edge of the Forbidden Forest where the Beauxbatons carriage is parked. Hagrid leads Madame Maxime to an enclosure where four fully grown, fire-breathing dragons are being held by thirty wizards. Charlie Weasley explains that the champions must get past these nesting mothers to retrieve something. Harry identifies the four breeds: a Welsh Green, a Chinese Fireball, a Swedish Short-Snout, and a vicious Hungarian Horntail. On his way back to the castle, Harry bumps into a suspicious Professor Karkaroff, who is also scouting the task.

Harry rushes to the common room and finds Sirius’s head appearing in the glowing embers of the fireplace. Sirius warns Harry that Karkaroff was a Death Eater (a follower of Lord Voldemort) who betrayed others to gain freedom. He mentions that Bertha Jorkins, a Ministry witch, has gone missing in Albania near Voldemort’s last known location. Sirius begins to explain a simple spell to defeat a dragon, but the conversation is cut short by Ron’s arrival. Harry and Ron have a heated argument where Harry throws a "Potter Really Stinks" badge at Ron's head.



### 20: The First Task
Harry tells Cedric about the dragons because the other two champions already know through their headmasters. Mad-Eye Moody pulls Harry aside and advises him to "play to his strengths" to survive the challenge. Harry realizes he must use his flying skills and spends the night practicing the Summoning Charm with Hermione. After many hours of failure, he finally masters the spell "Accio" (used to bring objects to the caster) at two in the morning. On the day of the task, Ludo Bagman has the champions draw small dragon models from a silk bag to determine their order.

Harry draws the Hungarian Horntail and the number four, meaning he will go last in the enclosure. He listens to the crowd’s reactions as Cedric, Fleur, and Krum successfully retrieve their golden eggs. When his turn arrives, Harry enters the arena and uses his wand to summon his Firebolt broomstick from the castle. He flies with extreme speed and agility, using diversionary tactics to draw the Horntail away from its nest. The dragon eventually rears up and takes flight, allowing Harry to dive and snatch the golden egg from the ground.

Harry receives high scores from most judges, including a ten from Bagman, but Karkaroff gives him a biased four. Ron approaches Harry after the task and admits that someone is likely trying to kill Harry, leading to their reconciliation. Hermione hugs both of them and cries with relief that the two friends are speaking again. Bagman informs the champions that the golden eggs contain a clue for the second task scheduled for February twenty-fourth. Rita Skeeter attempts to get another interview, but Harry rudely brushes her off and heads back to the castle with Ron.
