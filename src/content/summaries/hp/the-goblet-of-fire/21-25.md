### 21: The House-Elf Liberation Front
Harry sends a letter to Sirius via Pigwidgeon to report his success in the first task. The Gryffindors hold a celebration party in the common room where Harry opens the golden egg, but it only emits a loud screeching noise. Hermione discovers the location of the Hogwarts kitchens after Fred and George describe a hidden door behind a painting of a fruit bowl. Fred and George reveal their latest invention, Canary Creams, which temporarily transform the eater into a giant canary. The trio visits the kitchens and finds Dobby, who is now a paid employee at Hogwarts wearing various mismatched clothes.

Dobby explains that he struggled to find work as a free elf until Dumbledore offered him a position with wages and time off. Winky is also in the kitchens but is devastated and drunk on butterbeer after being fired by Mr. Crouch. She refuses to accept her freedom and spends her time weeping while clutching her old master's secrets. Dobby reveals that Dumbledore pays him one Galleon a week, which is less than the original offer because Dobby prefers working to leisure. The other Hogwarts house-elves avoid Dobby and Winky, viewing their status as paid or freed workers as a source of deep shame.

Hagrid continues teaching Care of Magical Creatures using Blast-Ended Skrewts, which have now grown to six feet in length and exhibit dangerous fire-blasting behavior. Rita Skeeter arrives at Hagrid's cabin and interviews him about the creatures while ignoring his warnings about her presence on school grounds. Hermione expresses concern that Hagrid might have imported the skrewts illegally, which could jeopardize his job. Harry and Ron attend Divination where Professor Trelawney predicts Harry's death for the eighty-first time. The chapter ends with the trio discussing the mystery of the golden egg and Winky's strange comments about Ludo Bagman being a bad wizard.



### 22: The Unexpected Task
Professor McGonagall announces the Yule Ball, a traditional dance for fourth-year students and above held on Christmas Day. She informs Harry that as a champion, he is required to lead the opening dance with a partner. Harry experiences intense anxiety at the prospect of asking a girl to the dance, finding it more daunting than facing a dragon. The school becomes preoccupied with the event as students form groups and discuss their planned attire and dates. Ron suggests that Harry's status as a champion will make finding a partner easy, though Ron himself struggles with the task.

Harry attempts to ask Cho Chang to the ball, but he finds her constantly surrounded by a group of giggling friends. When he finally gets her alone, Cho informs him that she has already accepted an invitation from Cedric Diggory. Ron attempts to ask Fleur Delacour to the ball in a moment of panic but is ignored and flees the scene in embarrassment. Harry and Ron return to the common room ashen-faced and realize they are among the last students without dates. Neville Longbottom reveals that he asked Hermione to the ball, but she declined because she already has a partner.

Ron accuses Hermione of lying about having a date to avoid going with Neville, which leads to a heated argument. Ginny Weasley admits she is going to the ball with Neville because it was her only way to attend as a third-year. Harry finally secures a date by asking Parvati Patil, who agrees to go with him after a fit of giggles. Parvati also arranges for her sister, Padma, to go with Ron, though Padma is less than enthusiastic about the pairing. The chapter concludes with the boys feeling relieved to have found partners, even if they are not their first choices.



### 23: The Yule Ball
Christmas morning begins with Dobby visiting Harry's dormitory to gift him a pair of hand-knitted, mismatched socks. Ron gives Dobby a maroon sweater and violet socks, while Harry receives a penknife from Sirius and a book from Hermione. The students attend a grand Christmas lunch before preparing for the evening's festivities in the Great Hall. Ron is horrified by his moldy-looking, lace-trimmed dress robes and attempts to improve them using a Severing Charm. Harry meets Parvati Patil in the common room, and they head down to join the other champions for the procession.

The champions enter the Great Hall, which has been decorated with silver frost, mistletoe, and a grotto of living fairies. Harry is shocked to see Hermione arriving with Viktor Krum, looking unrecognizable with sleek hair and reduced front teeth. The champions lead the opening dance to a mournful tune played by the Weird Sisters, a famous wizarding band. During dinner, Percy Weasley represents Mr. Crouch, who is reportedly ill and remains at home. After the meal, the tables are cleared for more dancing, and Harry and Ron sit morosely as their partners abandon them.

Ron and Hermione engage in a loud argument over her choice to attend the ball with Krum, whom Ron labels "the enemy." Harry and Ron overhear a private conversation between Hagrid and Madame Maxime in the rose garden where Hagrid reveals he is a half-giant. Madame Maxime reacts with icy indignation, claiming she merely has "big bones" before storming away from the fountain. Snape and Karkaroff are also seen arguing, with Karkaroff showing Snape something on his arm that causes him great concern. Cedric Diggory catches Harry on the stairs and advises him to take a bath with the golden egg to solve the clue.



### 24: Rita Skeeter's Scoop
The day after the ball, Ron and Hermione continue their argument, with Hermione telling Ron he should have asked her first. Harry, Ron, and Hermione discuss Hagrid's half-giant status, and Hermione dismisses the hysteria as simple bigotry. Hagrid fails to appear for his Care of Magical Creatures class and is replaced by a temporary teacher named Professor Grubbly-Plank. Draco Malfoy provides Harry with a Daily Prophet article by Rita Skeeter that exposes Hagrid's parentage. The article identifies his mother as the giantess Fridwulfa and labels Hagrid as a dangerous and brutal individual.

The trio attempts to visit Hagrid at his cabin, but he refuses to answer the door or see anyone for several days. During a Hogsmeade visit, Harry spots Ludo Bagman talking secretly with a group of menacing goblins in the Three Broomsticks. Bagman pulls Harry aside to offer him help with the golden egg, but Harry declines the offer to avoid cheating. Rita Skeeter appears at the pub and attempts to interview Harry about Hagrid, but Hermione insults her and the trio leaves. Hermione leads the boys back to Hagrid's cabin, where they find Dumbledore attempting to convince Hagrid not to resign.

Dumbledore shares stories of his own family's scandals to comfort Hagrid and refuses to accept his resignation from the school. Hagrid eventually emerges from his cabin and shows the trio a picture of his late father, a short wizard who was proud of his son. He thanks the students for their loyalty and encourages Harry to win the tournament to prove that blood status does not matter. Harry feels a pang of guilt because he has been lying to Hagrid about his progress with the golden egg. He finally decides to follow Cedric's advice and visit the prefects' bathroom to solve the mystery.



### 25: The Egg and the Eye
Harry sneaks out of the Gryffindor common room at night using his Invisibility Cloak and the Marauder's Map. He enters the luxurious prefects' bathroom on the fifth floor after providing the password "pine fresh" to the statue. Moaning Myrtle appears while Harry is in the bath and suggests he open the golden egg underwater. Harry submerges himself and the egg, hearing a song from merpeople about something they have taken from him. The song warns that he has only one hour to recover what he will "sorely miss" from the bottom of the lake.

Myrtle points out the mermaid in the painting on the wall, confirming that the second task involves the merpeople. As Harry leaves the bathroom, he notices a dot labeled "Bartemius Crouch" moving inside Snape's office on the Marauder's Map. Harry's leg gets caught in a trick step on a staircase while he is still under the Invisibility Cloak. The golden egg falls and bursts open, emitting a loud wail that attracts Filch and then Snape. Snape reveals that someone has broken into his office and stolen potion ingredients from his private stores.

Mad-Eye Moody arrives and uses his magical eye to see Harry trapped in the stairs, though he does not reveal him to Snape. Moody summons the Marauder's Map before Snape can grab it, claiming it is his own property that he dropped earlier. Snape is convinced Harry is present but is forced to leave after Moody threatens to tell Dumbledore about his suspicions. Moody frees Harry's leg and borrows the Marauder's Map, stating it might be exactly what he has been looking for. He suggests that Harry consider a career as an Auror and sends him back to bed before the second task.
