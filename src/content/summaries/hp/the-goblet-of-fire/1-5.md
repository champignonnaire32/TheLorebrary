### CHAPTER 1 - The Riddle House
Frank Bryce, an elderly gardener with a stiff leg, investigates a light in the abandoned Riddle House. Inside, he overhears Lord Voldemort, a Dark wizard in a weak state, and Peter Pettigrew, a small balding man nicknamed Wormtail. Voldemort mentions his plan to use Harry Potter and reveals he murdered Bertha Jorkins, a Ministry witch with a scatterbrained reputation. Nagini, a twelve-foot-long giant snake with a diamond-patterned tail, slithers past Frank into the room. The snake informs Voldemort of Frank's presence outside the door in the hallway.

Wormtail invites Frank into the room on Voldemort's cold, high-pitched command. Frank stands defiantly before the armchair but cannot see the speaker hidden inside it. Voldemort identifies Frank as a Muggle, which is a term for a person lacking magical abilities. Frank threatens to report the talk of murder to the police once he leaves the house. Voldemort tells Frank that nobody knows he is there and orders Wormtail to turn the chair.

Wormtail whimpers but eventually turns the heavy armchair to face the gardener. Frank sees the horrific occupant of the chair and lets out a loud scream. Voldemort raises a wooden wand and speaks a lethal incantation. A flash of green light fills the room and Frank Bryce falls dead instantly. Two hundred miles away, Harry Potter wakes up with a burning pain in his forehead.



### CHAPTER 2 - The Scar
Harry Potter wakes in his bedroom at Privet Drive with his lightning-bolt scar stinging intensely. He recalls a dream involving Voldemort, Wormtail, and an old man being killed in a dark room. Harry realizes the scar only hurts when Voldemort is near or feeling particularly powerful emotions. He examines his room, which contains his owl Hedwig, his broomstick, and various magical schoolbooks. Harry considers writing to Professor Dumbledore but decides the letter would sound too foolish.

He imagines the reactions of his best friends, Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley. Harry fears Mrs. Weasley will fuss over him or that Fred and George will think he is losing his nerve. He decides he needs advice from an adult wizard who understands Dark Magic and cares for him. Harry remembers his godfather, Sirius Black, who is currently an escaped fugitive from the wizard prison Azkaban. Sirius is a wizard who was wrongly convicted of murders committed by Wormtail and is now in hiding.

Harry sits at his desk and writes a letter to Sirius explaining the pain in his scar. He mentions Dudley's forced diet and how the Dursleys are terrified of Sirius's reputation as a murderer. He omits the specific details of the dream to avoid appearing overly worried. Harry sets the finished letter aside to wait for Hedwig to return from hunting. He gets dressed for breakfast while the morning sun begins to light his bedroom.



### CHAPTER 3 - The Invitation
Harry joins the Dursleys for a breakfast consisting of a small quarter of unsweetened grapefruit. The entire family is on a diet because the school nurse reported that Dudley is the size of a killer whale. Harry secretly supplements his meals with cakes and pies sent by his friends via owl post. Uncle Vernon receives a letter from Molly Weasley that is covered in dozens of postage stamps. The letter invites Harry to attend the Quidditch World Cup final with the Weasley family.

Uncle Vernon is livid because the postman rang the bell to comment on the unusual envelope. Harry explains that Quidditch is a wizarding sport played on flying broomsticks. Vernon struggles between his desire to keep Harry unhappy and his wish to get him out of the house. Harry mentions that he is writing to his godfather, Sirius Black, to check in. Terrified of the supposed mass-murderer, Vernon immediately grants Harry permission to go.

Harry rushes upstairs and finds a tiny, hyperactive owl zooming around his room like a firework. The owl, later named Pigwidgeon, carries a note from Ron confirming they are coming to get him. Ron states they will arrive at five o'clock on Sunday whether the Dursleys agree or not. Harry ties a reply to the small owl's leg and sends it back to the Burrow. He then attaches his letter about the scar to Hedwig and sends her to find Sirius.



### CHAPTER 4 - Back to the Burrow
Harry packs his trunk with his Invisibility Cloak, his Firebolt broomstick, and the Marauder's Map. Uncle Vernon puts on his best suit to look intimidating for the arrival of the wizards. Dudley sits in an armchair with his hands over his backside to protect himself from magic. Five o'clock passes without a car appearing, causing Uncle Vernon to pace the room in a rage. Loud banging and shouting suddenly erupt from behind the Dursleys' boarded-up electric fireplace.

Arthur Weasley, Fred, George, and Ron are trapped inside the chimney because the fireplace is blocked. Mr. Weasley uses his wand to blast the electric fire and the boards across the room. The Weasleys emerge in a cloud of dust and rubble, wearing long wizarding robes. Mr. Weasley explains he connected the house to the Floo Network, a system for traveling through fireplaces via green flames. Fred and George go upstairs to fetch Harry's trunk while Mr. Weasley tries to chat with the terrified Muggles.

Mr. Weasley starts a magical fire in the hearth and uses emerald-green Floo powder to send the boys home. Fred "accidently" drops a bag of Ton-Tongue Toffees, which are sweets that cause physical transformations. Dudley eats one of the toffees and his tongue begins to swell into a long, slimy purple python. Uncle Vernon starts throwing china ornaments at Mr. Weasley while Aunt Petunia screams and pulls on Dudley's tongue. Harry steps into the green flames and shouts "The Burrow" to escape the chaotic living room.



### CHAPTER 5 - Weasley's Wizard Wheezes
Harry arrives in the kitchen of the Burrow, a rickety magical house that is several stories high. He meets Bill and Charlie Weasley, the eldest brothers who work at Gringotts Bank and with dragons. Mr. Weasley arrives home furious because Fred and George's prank on Dudley undermined Muggle relations. The twins reveal they gave Dudley the toffee because he is a bully who was tormenting Harry. Mrs. Weasley enters and discovers the twins have been creating "Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes," which are magical joke products.

Ron, Hermione, and Ginny take Harry upstairs to avoid the shouting match in the kitchen. They explain that Mrs. Weasley burned the twins' order forms for their dangerous joke inventions. Percy Weasley pokes his head out of his room to complain about the noise while he writes a report. He is working for the Department of International Magical Cooperation on standardizing cauldron thicknesses. In Ron's room, the tiny owl Pigwidgeon zooms around his cage while Ron complains about Percy's boss, Barty Crouch.

The family eats a large dinner outside in the garden under a clear blue sky. Bill and Charlie use magic to make two tables fly and smash into each other for fun. Charlie discusses the Quidditch World Cup and mentions that England lost to Transylvania three hundred and ninety to ten. Percy mentions that a Ministry official named Bertha Jorkins has been missing in Albania for over a month. Mrs. Weasley orders everyone to bed early so they can wake up at dawn for the journey to the match.
