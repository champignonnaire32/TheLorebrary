### 26: The Second Task
Harry, Ron, and Hermione discuss the mystery of Snape and Moody while practicing Banishing Charms in class. Harry spends weeks in the library searching for a way to breathe underwater to retrieve his stolen possession from the lake. On the morning of the task, the house-elf Dobby wakes Harry and provides him with a ball of slimy gillyweed. Gillyweed is a magical plant that causes the consumer to sprout gills and webbed hands and feet for approximately one hour. Harry swallows the plant and dives into the lake, where he discovers he can move with incredible speed and see clearly through the murk.

As he swims deeper, Harry encounters a group of grindylows, which are small horned water demons, and repels them using a jet of hot water. Moaning Myrtle, a ghost inhabiting the school pipes, appears and directs Harry toward the merpeople village in the center of the lake. Harry finds Ron, Hermione, Cho Chang, and a small girl tied to a stone statue in the Merchieftainess's village square. Cedric Diggory arrives using a Bubble-Head Charm to rescue Cho, and a partially transfigured Viktor Krum rescues Hermione. Harry waits for Fleur Delacour to arrive, but he eventually realizes she has been incapacitated and decides to save the remaining hostage himself.

Harry threatens the surrounding merpeople with his wand to allow him to cut Fleur's younger sister free from her bindings. He struggles to swim back to the surface while dragging both the unconscious Ron and the small girl through the water. The gillyweed wears off just as Harry breaks the surface, and he is pulled onto the bank by the judges and medical staff. Fleur is hysterical with relief and kisses Harry and Ron for saving Gabrielle, her eight-year-old sister. Despite finishing last, the judges award Harry forty-five points for moral fiber, tying him for first place in the tournament overall.

### 27: Padfoot Returns
Ron enjoys his newfound fame by telling increasingly exaggerated stories of his struggle against the merpeople in the lake. Hermione becomes the target of a malicious Witch Weekly article by Rita Skeeter that labels her a "scarlet woman" for her alleged love triangle. In Potions class, Snape reads the embarrassing article aloud to the students and threatens Harry with the use of Veritaserum, a powerful truth potion. Igor Karkaroff, the head of Durmstrang, enters the classroom to show Snape a mark on his arm that is growing darker and clearer. Harry, Ron, and Hermione travel to a rocky cave outside Hogsmeade to meet Sirius Black, who is living as a shaggy black dog.

Sirius is accompanied by Buckbeak the hippogriff, a creature with a horse's body and an eagle's head, and survives by eating rats. He explains the history of Barty Crouch, a high-ranking Ministry official who was once tipped to be the next Minister of Magic. During the height of Voldemort's power, Crouch authorized Aurors to use Unforgivable Curses and sent suspects to Azkaban without trials. Crouch's reputation was ruined when his own son was caught with a group of Death Eaters and sentenced to Azkaban by his father. Sirius recalls seeing the boy die in prison a year later and witnessing the dementors bury him outside the fortress walls.

Sirius reveals that Crouch's wife also died shortly after her son, leaving the once-powerful wizard alone and disgraced. He expresses deep suspicion regarding Crouch's recent absence from the tournament and his supposed illness. The group discusses the disappearance of Bertha Jorkins, a Ministry witch whom Sirius remembers as having an excellent memory for gossip. Sirius warns Harry to stay close to Ron and Hermione and to avoid wandering around the castle grounds alone at night. The students return to the castle while Sirius, in his Animagus dog form, accompanies them to the edge of the village.

### 28: The Madness of Mr. Crouch
Harry and his friends visit the Hogwarts kitchens to give Dobby a collection of colorful socks as a thank-you gift. They find Winky, the former Crouch house-elf, in a state of extreme depression and intoxication from drinking bottles of butterbeer. Winky claims she still knows her master's most important secrets but refuses to divulge any information despite Hermione's urging. Hermione receives a deluge of hate mail from Witch Weekly readers, including an envelope that sprays her hands with painful bubotuber pus. Hagrid leads a lesson on nifflers, which are fluffy black creatures with flat paws used for finding buried treasure and shiny objects.

Ron wins a large slab of chocolate when his niffler digs up the most leprechaun gold from a patch of earth Hagrid prepared. After the lesson, Ron realizes the gold he gave Harry at the Quidditch World Cup was leprechaun gold and had vanished hours later. The champions are summoned to the Quidditch field to see the third task, which is a massive maze filled with magical obstacles. Ludo Bagman explains that the Triwizard Cup will be placed at the center, and the first person to touch it will win. While walking back, Viktor Krum pulls Harry aside to ask if he has a romantic relationship with Hermione, which Harry denies.

Barty Crouch suddenly staggers out of the forest, looking ragged and delirious while muttering to a tree as if it were his assistant. Crouch sporadically regains his senses and frantically begs Harry to bring Dumbledore so he can warn him about a terrible mistake. Harry leaves Krum to guard the incoherent man and sprints to the castle to find the Headmaster. After a brief delay caused by Snape, Harry returns with Dumbledore only to find Krum unconscious and Mr. Crouch missing. Krum claims Crouch attacked him from behind, and Dumbledore sends Hagrid and Moody to search the grounds for the vanished official.

### 29: The Dream
Harry, Ron, and Hermione meet in the Owlery at dawn to discuss the mysterious events surrounding Mr. Crouch's disappearance. They overhear Fred and George Weasley arguing about a blackmail plot involving a high-ranking individual to fund their future joke shop. Hermione suspects that Fred and George are trying to get money because their father cannot afford to help them start a business. During History of Magic, the trio is too exhausted to pay attention as they speculate on how Crouch could have vanished from the grounds. Harry visits Professor Moody, who admits he used the Marauder's Map to search for Crouch but found no trace of him.

Moody advises Harry to focus on training for the final task and warns the group to maintain constant vigilance. Sirius sends a stern letter demanding that Harry promise never to wander out-of-bounds again following the attack in the forest. For the next several days, the trio spends their free time in empty classrooms practicing defensive spells like the Stunning Spell. Harry struggles to master the Impediment Curse, a hex that slows down attackers, while Ron and Hermione act as practice targets. On a sweltering afternoon in Divination, Harry falls into a deep sleep while Professor Trelawney lectures on the movements of Mars.

Harry experiences a vivid vision of Voldemort sitting in a dark room and torturing Wormtail with the Cruciatus Curse. In the dream, Voldemort learns that a "blunder" has been repaired and that he plans to feed Harry to his giant snake, Nagini. Harry wakes up screaming and clutching his lightning-bolt scar, which is searing with intense physical pain. He ignores Trelawney's excitement over his "premonition" and leaves the classroom immediately to find Dumbledore. After guessing the password "Cockroach Cluster," Harry gains entry to the Headmaster's office and overhears an argument between Dumbledore, Fudge, and Moody.

### 30: The Pensieve
Harry waits alone in Dumbledore's office while the Headmaster and Ministry officials inspect the grounds where Mr. Crouch was last seen. He discovers a glowing stone basin called a Pensieve, which is a magical object used to store and review excess thoughts. Upon touching the silvery substance with his nose, Harry is pulled into the memories of Albus Dumbledore. He finds himself in a dungeon-like courtroom witnessing the trial of Igor Karkaroff, who is attempting to trade names for his freedom. Karkaroff names Severus Snape as a servant of Voldemort, but Dumbledore testifies that Snape became a spy for the side of light.

The memory shifts to the trial of Ludo Bagman, who is accused of passing information to a Death Eater named Augustus Rookwood. Bagman is cleared of all charges after the jury reveals their admiration for his legendary skills as a Quidditch player for England. A third and more somber memory shows Barty Crouch presiding over the trial of four people, including his own teenaged son. The group is accused of using the Cruciatus Curse to torture the Aurors Frank and Alice Longbottom into permanent insanity. Crouch disowns his screaming son and sentences all four defendants to life imprisonment in Azkaban despite his wife's sobbing protests.

Dumbledore pulls Harry out of the Pensieve and explains how the basin allows him to spot patterns and links in his own memories. Harry describes his recent vision of Voldemort, and Dumbledore theorizes that Harry's scar hurts when the Dark Lord is nearby or feeling murderous. Dumbledore reveals that Neville Longbottom's parents are still alive but are patients in St. Mungo's Hospital because they no longer recognize anyone. He asks Harry not to share the secret of Neville's parents, as it is Neville's choice when to tell his friends. The Headmaster expresses concern over the linked disappearances of Bertha Jorkins, Mr. Crouch, and a Muggle named Frank Bryce before wishing Harry luck.
