### 16: Through the Trapdoor
Harry, Ron, and Hermione finish their final exams while Harry’s scar continues to cause him stabbing pains. Harry realizes Hagrid unwittingly told a disguised stranger how to calm Fluffy, the three-headed dog guarding the trapdoor. The trio attempts to warn Dumbledore but discovers he has been summoned to London by an urgent Ministry owl. Professor McGonagall dismisses their warnings about the Sorcerer's Stone, insisting it is perfectly safe. Snape threatens Harry with expulsion after catching the students loitering near the forbidden third-floor corridor.

The trio decides to enter the trapdoor themselves that night to reach the Stone before Snape does. Neville Longbottom attempts to block their exit from the Gryffindor common room to prevent further point loss. Hermione uses the Full Body-Bind Curse, a spell that freezes the victim's limbs and jaw, to immobilize Neville so they can leave. They navigate the castle under the Invisibility Cloak and trick Peeves by pretending Harry is the Bloody Baron. Upon reaching the third floor, they find the door already ajar and Fluffy asleep next to a harp.

Harry plays a flute to keep the dog asleep while the group drops through the trapdoor onto a mass of Devil’s Snare. Hermione uses a jet of bluebell flames to repel the light-sensitive plant and free the boys from its constricting vines. They proceed to a chamber filled with winged keys and use broomsticks to capture a specific silver key with a damaged wing. In the next room, Ron directs the trio as living pieces in a violent game of wizard chess against giant stone statues. Ron sacrifices his position and is knocked unconscious by the white queen so Harry can checkmate the king.



### 17: The Man With Two Faces
Harry enters the final chamber and discovers Professor Quirrell standing before the Mirror of Erised instead of Snape. Quirrell reveals that he attempted to kill Harry during the Quidditch match while Snape was actually muttering a counter-curse. He admits to letting the troll into the school on Halloween as a distraction to reach the Stone. The Mirror of Erised, a magical glass that shows the viewer's deepest desire, acts as the final protection for the Stone. Quirrell unwraps his turban to reveal the face of Voldemort, a chalk-white visage with red eyes, living on the back of his head.

Voldemort commands Quirrell to use Harry to find the Stone, and Harry sees his reflection drop the object into his real pocket. Harry lies about seeing himself winning the House Cup, but Voldemort detects the deception and orders Quirrell to seize him. When Quirrell touches Harry’s skin, his hands and face blister and burn with unbearable agony. Harry clings to Quirrell’s arm to keep him in pain, eventually passing out as the struggle reaches its peak. He wakes up three days later in the hospital wing to find Dumbledore waiting with a pile of sweets.

Dumbledore explains that the Stone was destroyed and that Harry’s skin is protected by his mother’s ancient sacrifice of love. During the end-of-year feast, Dumbledore awards last-minute points to Ron, Hermione, Harry, and Neville for their bravery and logic. These points allow Gryffindor to surpass Slytherin and win the House Cup for the first time in years. The students receive their exam results, learn they have all passed, and board the Hogwarts Express to return home. Harry departs King's Cross with the Dursleys, secretly intending to use the threat of magic to keep Dudley in check all summer.
