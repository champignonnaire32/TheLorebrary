### 11: Quidditch
The cold November weather arrives at Hogwarts as Harry prepares for his first Quidditch match against Slytherin. Harry discovers Professor Snape limping with a mangled, bloody leg after catching him in the staffroom with Filch. He overhears Snape mentioning a three-headed dog and realizes Snape was bitten while trying to bypass the creature on Halloween. Harry concludes that Snape let the mountain troll into the school as a diversion to reach the guarded object. The Gryffindor team enters the field for the match while Ron and Hermione watch from the stands with Hagrid.

During the game, Harry's Nimbus Two Thousand broomstick begins to buck and lurch violently out of his control. Hermione observes Snape in the stands staring at Harry and muttering a continuous jinx under his breath. She navigates the stands and sets fire to the hem of Snape's robes with her wand to break his concentration. The distraction allows Harry to regain control of his broom as he dives toward the ground. He accidentally catches the Golden Snitch in his mouth and falls onto the field on all fours to reveal the win.

Hagrid takes the trio back to his hut where he accidentally reveals that the three-headed dog is named Fluffy. He explains that he lent the dog to Dumbledore to guard a top-secret object. Harry and his friends mention their suspicions about Snape, but Hagrid insists the teacher would never steal from the school. Hagrid further slips up by mentioning that the secret involves a man named Nicolas Flamel. The chapter ends with the trio realizing they have a new name to research in the library.

### 12: The Mirror of Erised
Christmas arrives at Hogwarts with heavy snow and Harry decides to stay at the castle instead of returning to the Dursleys. Harry, Ron, and Hermione spend their time searching the library for information on Nicolas Flamel without success. On Christmas morning, Harry discovers a small pile of presents at the foot of his bed including a hand-knitted green sweater from Mrs. Weasley. The final package contains a silvery, fluid Invisibility Cloak that once belonged to Harry's father. An anonymous note accompanying the cloak instructs Harry to use it well while returning the heirloom to him.

Harry uses the cloak at night to enter the library's Restricted Section but flees after a book screams when he opens it. He hides in an unused classroom to avoid Filch and Snape and discovers a large gold-framed mirror inscribed with "Erised stra ehru oyt ube cafru oyt on wohsi." Upon looking into the glass, Harry sees his deceased parents and dozens of other relatives smiling and waving at him. He returns the following night with Ron, but Ron only sees himself as the Head Boy and Quidditch Captain. Harry becomes obsessed with the mirror and returns for a third night to sit before his family's reflections.

Professor Dumbledore is waiting in the room on the third night and explains that the Mirror of Erised shows the heart's deepest desire. He warns Harry that many have wasted away or gone mad while staring at the impossible visions in the glass. Dumbledore informs Harry that the mirror will be moved to a new location the next day and asks him not to look for it again. Harry asks Dumbledore what he sees in the mirror and the headmaster claims to see himself holding a pair of thick woolen socks. Harry returns to his bed wondering if the headmaster was telling the truth about his own reflection.

### 13: Nicholas Flamel
Harry suffers from nightmares about his parents' death as the new school term begins and Quidditch practice intensifies. Wood informs the team that Snape will be refereeing the next match against Hufflepuff to ensure Gryffindor does not overtake Slytherin. Neville arrives in the common room with his legs stuck together by Malfoy's Leg-Locker Curse. Harry gives Neville a Chocolate Frog to cheer him up and discovers a Famous Wizard card featuring Albus Dumbledore. The card mentions Dumbledore's work on alchemy with his partner Nicolas Flamel.

Hermione retrieves a massive book from her dormitory and identifies Flamel as the only known maker of the Sorcerer's Stone. The text explains that the Stone turns metal into gold and produces the Elixir of Life which grants immortality. The trio realizes that the three-headed dog Fluffy is guarding Flamel's Stone to prevent it from being stolen by Snape. Despite his nerves about Snape refereeing, Harry decides to play in the match to show he is not intimidated. Dumbledore attends the game, which Harry interprets as a sign of safety since Snape would not attack him in front of the headmaster.

The match ends in record time when Harry catches the Snitch after only five minutes of play. Following the victory, Harry follows a hooded Snape into the Forbidden Forest and witnesses a secret meeting with Professor Quirrell. Snape threatens Quirrell and asks if he has found a way to get past Hagrid's beast yet. Harry reports the encounter to Ron and Hermione, concluding that the Stone is protected by multiple enchantments. They realize the Stone's safety depends on Quirrell's ability to resist Snape's intimidation and refusal to help him.

### 14: Norbert the Norwegian Ridgeback
The trio monitors Professor Quirrell's health while studying for their upcoming final exams ten weeks away. They find Hagrid in the library researching dragons and visit his hut to find a large black egg heating in the fire. Hagrid reveals he won the Norwegian Ridgeback egg from a stranger during a card game at the local pub. Despite the illegality of dragon breeding, Hagrid keeps the egg until it hatches into a spiny, black creature he names Norbert. Draco Malfoy spies through the window and sees the baby dragon, creating a significant threat to Hagrid's position.

Norbert grows rapidly and bites Ron's hand, causing it to swell and turn a poisonous shade of green. Harry suggests sending the dragon to Ron's brother Charlie who works with dragons in Romania. Charlie agrees to have his friends pick up Norbert from the tallest tower at midnight on Saturday. Malfoy discovers the plan after stealing a book from Ron that contained Charlie's response letter. On the night of the transport, Harry and Hermione use the Invisibility Cloak to carry Norbert's crate up to the astronomy tower. They witness Professor McGonagall catching Malfoy in the corridors and giving him detention for being out of bed.

The pair reaches the top of the tower and hands the crate over to Charlie's friends who arrive on broomsticks. Norbert is harnessed between the brooms and flown away toward Romania while the students watch from the roof. Harry and Hermione descend the stairs with a sense of relief and triumph over Malfoy's detention. Their celebration is cut short when Filch intercepts them at the foot of the spiral staircase. They realize they left the Invisibility Cloak on top of the tower and have no excuse for being out of bed. The chapter ends with the students caught and facing severe consequences for their late-night excursion.

**



### 15: The Forbidden Forest
Filch escorts Harry and Hermione to Professor McGonagall's study where they find Neville also in trouble. Neville explains he left bed to warn them about Malfoy, but McGonagall believes they lured Malfoy out as a prank. The professor deducts fifty points each from Harry, Hermione, and Neville, totaling a loss of one hundred and fifty points for Gryffindor. This massive penalty drops Gryffindor from first to last place in the house championship. The rest of the school treats Harry with hostility and he becomes the most hated student at Hogwarts.

Harry overhears Quirrell sobbing in a classroom and assumes Snape has finally broken his resistance regarding the Sorcerer's Stone. A week before exams, the four students receive their detention instructions to go into the Forbidden Forest with Hagrid. They discover that something has been killing unicorns and drinking their silver blood to keep a dying person alive. Hagrid splits the group into two parties to search for the wounded unicorn deep within the dark woods. Harry and Neville go with Hagrid while Hermione and Malfoy follow Fang the boarhound.

Hagrid encounters centaurs named Ronan and Bane who speak in riddles about Mars being bright tonight. After a false alarm caused by Neville, Hagrid switches the groups so Harry goes with Malfoy and Fang. Harry finds the dead unicorn in a clearing and sees a hooded figure crawling across the ground to drink its blood. The figure lunges at Harry but a centaur named Firenze rescues him and carries him away on his back. Firenze explains that Voldemort is the one drinking unicorn blood to sustain himself until he can steal the Sorcerer's Stone.
