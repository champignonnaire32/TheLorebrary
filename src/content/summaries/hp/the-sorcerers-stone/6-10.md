### 6: The Journey From Platform Nine and Three-Quarters
Harry spends his last month at Privet Drive reading his textbooks and caring for his new owl, Hedwig. Uncle Vernon drives Harry to King's Cross station but abandons him when he cannot find platform nine and three-quarters. Harry observes a family with flaming red hair and follows them toward the barrier between platforms nine and ten. He meets Ron Weasley, a tall, thin boy with a long nose and freckles, who is also starting his first year. Harry runs at the solid barrier and passes through a wrought-iron archway to reach the Hogwarts Express, a scarlet steam engine.

On the train, Fred and George Weasley help Harry lift his trunk and discover his lightning-shaped scar. Ron joins Harry in a compartment and explains his anxiety about living up to his five older brothers. Harry buys a large assortment of magical sweets from a cart, including Chocolate Frogs which contain collectible cards of famous wizards. He reads an Albus Dumbledore card and watches the headmaster's image vanish and reappears within the frame. Hermione Granger, a girl with bushy brown hair and large front teeth, enters the compartment looking for a lost toad named Trevor.

Draco Malfoy, a pale boy Harry met in Diagon Alley, arrives with his large bodyguards Crabbe and Goyle to offer an alliance. Harry refuses Malfoy's hand, leading to a confrontation where Ron's rat, Scabbers, bites Goyle's knuckle. The train arrives at Hogsmeade station, where Hagrid gathers the first-year students for a boat trip across a black lake. Harry sees Hogwarts for the first time, a vast castle with many turrets and towers perched on a high mountain. The students enter the castle and are met by Professor McGonagall, a stern witch wearing emerald-green robes.



### 7: The Sorting Hat
Professor McGonagall explains the house system and leads the first years into a small chamber to wait. About twenty translucent, pearly-white ghosts stream through the walls and converse with the nervous students. The students enter the Great Hall, which features thousands of floating candles and a ceiling bewitched to look like the night sky. McGonagall places a four-legged stool and a patched, frayed wizard's hat in front of the students. The Sorting Hat opens a rip near its brim like a mouth and sings a song about the four Hogwarts houses.

Each student puts on the hat, which shouts out their assigned house to the entire hall. Hermione Granger and Neville Longbottom are both sorted into Gryffindor, while Draco Malfoy is immediately placed in Slytherin. When Harry's name is called, the hat suggests he could be great in Slytherin but Harry silently begs not to be put there. The hat grants his request and shouts "GRYFFINDOR," prompting the Weasley twins to cheer that they "got Potter." Ron is also sorted into Gryffindor, and the students sit down to a massive banquet that appears magically on golden plates.

Harry speaks with Sir Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington, the Gryffindor ghost who is nearly headless due to an incomplete execution. During the meal, Harry looks at the High Table and feels a sharp, hot pain in his scar when Professor Snape glares at him. Albus Dumbledore warns the students that the Forbidden Forest and the third-floor corridor are out of bounds. Percy the Prefect leads the Gryffindors to their common room, which is hidden behind a portrait of a Fat Lady. Harry falls asleep in a four-poster bed with red velvet curtains after dreaming about Quirrell's turban and Snape's laugh.



### 8: The Potions Master
Harry struggles to navigate the one hundred and forty-two staircases and trick doors of Hogwarts during his first week. He meets Argus Filch, the school's wheezing caretaker who patrols with a scrawny, lamp-eyed cat named Mrs. Norris. In Charms, the tiny Professor Flitwick squeaks and falls off his stack of books when he reads Harry's name on the roll. Professor McGonagall demonstrates Transfiguration by turning her desk into a pig, but the students only practice turning matches into needles. Defense Against the Dark Arts is taught by Professor Quirrell, whose classroom smells strongly of garlic to ward off vampires.

On Friday, Harry receives an invitation for tea from Hagrid and attends his first Potions lesson in a cold dungeon. Professor Snape, a teacher with greasy black hair and a hooked nose, publicly humiliates Harry with difficult questions. Snape reveals that asphodel and wormwood create the Draught of Living Death, a powerful sleeping potion. During the practical session, Neville melts a cauldron and is covered in a potion that causes angry red boils to erupt on his skin. Snape blames Harry for Neville's mistake and takes a point away from Gryffindor, increasing Harry's dislike of the teacher.

Harry and Ron visit Hagrid's wooden hut on the edge of the Forbidden Forest and meet his large boarhound, Fang. Hagrid serves rock cakes, which are hard, raisin-filled lumps that are difficult to chew. Harry notices a newspaper clipping on Hagrid's table about a break-in at Gringotts Bank on July 31st. He realizes the break-in occurred on his birthday and involved the same vault Hagrid had emptied earlier that day. Hagrid avoids Harry's eyes when questioned about the vault, leaving Harry suspicious of the secret package's contents.



### 9: The Midnight Duel
Gryffindor students learn they will have flying lessons with the Slytherins, much to Harry's frustration. Neville receives a Remembrall, a glass ball filled with white smoke that turns red if the owner has forgotten something. Draco Malfoy snatches the Remembrall from Neville during breakfast but is forced to return it by Professor McGonagall. During the flying lesson, Neville loses control of his broom and falls, breaking his wrist. Madam Hooch, a teacher with yellow hawk-like eyes, takes Neville to the hospital wing and forbids anyone from flying.

Malfoy takes Neville's Remembrall into the air, and Harry flies after him despite the direct orders to stay grounded. Harry executes a fifty-foot dive to catch the Remembrall just before it hits the ground, proving his natural talent for flight. Professor McGonagall catches Harry and introduces him to Oliver Wood, the burly captain of the Gryffindor Quidditch team. Instead of being expelled, Harry is appointed as the new Seeker for the house team, becoming the youngest player in a century. Malfoy challenges Harry to a wizard's duel at midnight in the trophy room, and Ron volunteers to be Harry's second.

Harry, Ron, Hermione, and Neville sneak out at night and find themselves pursued through the corridors by Argus Filch. They realize Malfoy tricked them by tipping off Filch and they flee into the forbidden third-floor corridor. Hermione uses the Alohomora spell to unlock a door, leading the group into a room occupied by a monstrous three-headed dog. The giant creature, which fills the space from floor to ceiling, growls at them until they escape back to their dormitory. Hermione points out that the dog was standing on a trapdoor, and Harry concludes it is guarding Hagrid's mysterious package.



### 10: Halloween
Professor McGonagall sends Harry a Nimbus Two Thousand, a sleek broomstick with a mahogany handle and gold lettering. Harry meets Oliver Wood at the Quidditch field to learn the rules involving the Quaffle, Bludgers, and the Golden Snitch. Wood explains that the Seeker's job is to catch the tiny, winged Snitch to earn one hundred and fifty points. In Charms class, Ron insults Hermione's bossy behavior, causing her to spend the rest of the day crying in the girls' bathroom. During the Halloween feast, Professor Quirrell runs into the Great Hall and announces that a troll is loose in the dungeons.

Dumbledore orders the students to their dormitories, but Harry and Ron sneak away to warn Hermione about the troll. They accidentally lock the twelve-foot-tall mountain troll inside the girls' bathroom where Hermione is hiding. The troll, which has granite-gray skin and a lumpy body like a boulder, begins smashing the sinks to reach her. Harry jumps onto the troll's neck and jams his wand up its nostril while Ron uses the Levitation Charm on the creature's club. The heavy wooden club falls onto the troll's head, knocking it unconscious and saving Hermione from injury.

Professors McGonagall, Snape, and Quirrell arrive and find the trio standing over the defeated mountain troll. Hermione lies to the teachers, claiming she went to find the troll herself and that Harry and Ron saved her life. McGonagall takes five points from Hermione but awards five points each to Harry and Ron for their bravery. Harry notices that Snape has a bloody leg wound, suggesting he was bitten by the three-headed dog during the distraction. The three students return to the Gryffindor common room and become firm friends following their shared victory over the troll.
