### Chapter 1: The Worst Birthday
Harry Potter argues with Uncle Vernon over breakfast at Privet Drive regarding his owl Hedwig. The Dursleys ignore Harry's twelfth birthday while preparing for a dinner party with Mr. and Mrs. Mason. Uncle Vernon locks Harry's wizarding school supplies in a cupboard under the stairs. Harry retreats to the garden and observes two large green eyes staring at him from a hedge. Dudley mocks Harry's lack of birthday cards until Harry scares him with fake magic words.

Aunt Petunia punishes Harry by assigning him a long list of outdoor chores in the hot sun. Harry cleans windows, washes the car, mows the lawn, and repaints a garden bench. He eats a small meal of bread and cheese alone in the kitchen before the guests arrive. Uncle Vernon orders Harry to stay in his room and pretend he does not exist. Harry enters his bedroom to find a small creature sitting on his bed.

The creature is a house-elf named Dobby who has large bat-like ears and tennis-ball-sized green eyes. Dobby wears a tattered pillowcase and has been watching Harry from the garden hedge. Harry realizes this entity is responsible for the eyes he saw earlier in the day. The elf warns Harry that he must not return to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Harry refuses to comply with Dobby's demand despite the elf's warnings of a dangerous plot.





### Chapter 2: Dobby's Warning
Dobby explains that house-elves are bound to serve one wizarding family and must punish themselves for disobedience. The elf bangs his head against the window and wall whenever he speaks ill of his masters. Dobby reveals he intercepted Harry's mail from Ron Weasley, Hermione Granger, and Hagrid to make Harry feel isolated. Harry attempts to seize the letters, but Dobby sprints downstairs into the Dursleys' kitchen. The elf uses a Hover Charm to suspend Aunt Petunia's violet pudding in midair.

Dobby drops the pudding onto the floor when Harry refuses to promise he will stay away from school. The elf vanishes with a loud crack, leaving Harry covered in cream and shattered glass. Uncle Vernon attempts to explain the mess to the Masons until a Ministry owl arrives with a letter. The letter warns Harry that he has performed a Hover Charm and faces expulsion for future underage magic. Mr. Mason flees the house after the owl drops the parchment on his wife's head.

Uncle Vernon fits iron bars to Harry's window and installs a cat-flap in the bedroom door. He declares that Harry will never return to Hogwarts and locks him inside the room around the clock. Harry receives small amounts of cold soup through the cat-flap three times a day. He spends three days in isolation, sharing his meager food with his owl Hedwig. Harry wakes during the night to see Ron Weasley peering through the bars from a flying car.





### Chapter 3: The Burrow
Ron, Fred, and George Weasley arrive in an enchanted turquoise Ford Anglia to rescue Harry. They attach a rope to the window bars and pull them out using the car's engine power. Fred and George use a hairpin to pick the lock on Harry's bedroom door and retrieve his trunk. Uncle Vernon wakes and grabs Harry's ankle as he tries to climb into the vehicle. The Weasleys pull Harry into the car, and Fred accelerates away from Privet Drive into the sky.

Harry explains Dobby's warning and the intercepted letters to the Weasley brothers as they fly toward Devon. The twins suspect Draco Malfoy sent the house-elf to prevent Harry from returning to Hogwarts. They arrive at the Burrow, a multi-story house that appears to be held together by magical means. Mrs. Weasley meets them in the yard and shouts at her sons for taking the illegal car. She eventually invites Harry inside for a large breakfast of sausages and fried eggs.

Mrs. Weasley orders her sons to de-gnome the garden as punishment for their night-time excursion. Harry assists Ron in catching the leathery, potato-headed creatures and swinging them in circles to make them dizzy. They throw the gnomes over the garden hedge into a nearby field. Mr. Weasley returns home from the Ministry of Magic after a night of raiding shops for illegal Muggle artifacts. Ron takes Harry to his orange-themed bedroom at the top of the house to see his Chudley Cannons posters.





### Chapter 4: At Flourish and Blotts
Harry receives his Hogwarts booklist which includes numerous titles by the famous wizard Gilderoy Lockhart. The Weasleys and Harry travel to Diagon Alley using Floo powder, a glittering substance thrown into fireplaces. Harry misspells his destination as "Dia-gon Alley" and ends up in Borgin and Burkes in Knockturn Alley. He hides inside a black cabinet and watches Lucius Malfoy sell illegal items to the shopkeeper. Draco Malfoy complains to his father about Harry Potter and Hermione Granger's academic success.

Hagrid finds Harry wandering in Knockturn Alley and escorts him safely back to Diagon Alley. Hermione and the Weasleys reunite with Harry in front of Gringotts Bank to withdraw money for school supplies. Mr. Weasley meets Hermione's Muggle parents and expresses intense interest in their currency and lifestyle. The group visits various shops, including a joke shop and a secondhand robe store, before entering Flourish and Blotts. A large crowd gathers inside the bookstore to attend a signing event for Gilderoy Lockhart's autobiography.

Lockhart spots Harry in the crowd and pulls him to the front for a Daily Prophet photograph. He announces that he will be the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher at Hogwarts this year. Mr. Malfoy and Mr. Weasley engage in a physical brawl after Malfoy insults the Weasleys' financial status. Hagrid separates the two men, and Malfoy thrusts a battered Transfiguration textbook back into Ginny Weasley's cauldron. The Weasleys and Harry use Floo powder at the Leaky Cauldron to return to the Burrow.





### Chapter 5: The Whomping Willow
The Weasleys and Harry prepare for their departure to Hogwarts on the morning of September first. Mr. Weasley drives the Ford Anglia, which has been magically expanded to fit eight people and their luggage. They arrive at King's Cross station with only minutes to spare before the Hogwarts Express departs. Percy, Fred, George, and Ginny pass through the magical barrier at platform nine and three-quarters without issue. Harry and Ron attempt to follow but crash their trolleys into a solid brick wall.

The barrier remains sealed, preventing the two boys from reaching the train before it leaves the station. Ron suggests using the flying car to reach Hogwarts, and they take off into the sky. They follow the train tracks northward while remaining hidden behind the car's Invisibility Booster. The booster fails mid-flight, forcing Harry and Ron to fly the visible car through the clouds. They reach the Hogwarts grounds at night but lose control of the vehicle as the engine stalls.

The Ford Anglia crashes into a violent tree known as the Whomping Willow on the school grounds. The tree strikes the car with its heavy branches, smashing the windshield and denting the roof. Ron's wand snaps in half during the impact, and the car eventually ejects the boys and their luggage. The battered vehicle drives itself into the Forbidden Forest, leaving Harry and Ron behind. Professor Snape discovers the boys and takes them to his office to face disciplinary action for their public flight.
