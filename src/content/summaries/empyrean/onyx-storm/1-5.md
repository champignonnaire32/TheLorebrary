### Chapter 1
Violet Sorrengail flies a dangerous winter patrol on Tairn while Andarna remains grounded for her safety. Tairn and Andarna argue over her lack of flight practice while the squad searches for dark wielders near Basgiath. During the flight, Aotrom nearly collides with Tairn, leading to a aggressive display of dominance from the larger dragon. Violet lands to find the riders and dragons deeply divided between Navarrian and Aretian loyalties. Tairn warns Violet that Xaden’s soul is no longer his own due to his turn as a venin.

Imogen informs Violet that Garrick and Bodhi are concerned about her sleeping arrangements with Xaden. The squad visits Sawyer in the infirmary, where he is struggling with the loss of his lower leg. Colonel Chandlyr, commander of the retired riders, visits Sawyer to discuss his future. Violet learns that Mira is working on a plan to help the fliers that involves potential treason. Tairn continues to express his lack of faith in Xaden’s redemption despite Violet’s protests.

A sudden, shrill scream echoes through the infirmary, signaling an immediate and terrifying threat. A cadet in infantry blue stumbles in with a gray venin handprint marked on his neck. He announces that everyone outside the infirmary has been killed by the infiltrating enemy. Violet immediately unsheathes her daggers and prepares to face the dark wielders within the walls. The squad realizes the venin are not just patrolling the borders but have successfully breached Basgiath.



### Chapter 2
Violet, Rhiannon, and Ridoc discover desiccated bodies in the hallway, confirming the venin have been feeding on cadets. They encounter a venin disguised in scribe robes and Violet kills him with a thrown alloy-hilted dagger. More venin are found dressed as scribes, allowing them to move through the school and feed without being noticed. The group heads down to the brig, suspecting the venin are attempting to rescue the imprisoned Jack Barlowe. They find two dark wielders attempting to open Jack's cell while another kills a guard.

A female venin with a silver braid and a forehead tattoo disappears and reappears unnaturally fast during the skirmish. Violet kills one venin with a dagger to the throat, but the others move with superhuman speed. Xaden arrives and uses his shadows to kill the remaining dark wielders in the interrogation chamber. During the subsequent debrief, Colonel Panchek reveals that General Melgren is already aware of the infiltration. Xaden admits to Violet that he can now sense the venin because he is one of them.

Garrick and Bodhi continue to hover over Xaden, distrusting his ability to control his new dark urges. Xaden confesses that he feels the source of power pulsing beneath his feet and hears the venin calling to him. He expresses fear that he is no longer whole and that his connection to Sgaeyl is strained. Violet insists she will find a cure for his condition and refuses to leave his side. Xaden warns her that his love for her will persist even if he becomes a Maven.



### Chapter 3
Violet meets with the Senarium, where she is officially assigned to a task force to find the seventh dragon breed. General Melgren informs her that Captain Grady, her RSC professor, will lead the mission instead of a student. The council ignores Violet's demands on behalf of Andarna, treating the mission as a non-negotiable military operation. Violet learns that Xaden's involvement in the task force is at Grady's sole discretion. A missive from Tecarus arrives, giving Violet only three days to fulfill their previous bargain.

Violet wakes up after a full night's sleep to find Xaden resting beside her in their shared bed. They share an intimate moment where Xaden expresses his deep love and possessiveness over Violet. The tension between them escalates as they engage in physical closeness, testing Xaden's fragile self-control. Violet shares a memory of their past intimacy through their mental bond, intensifying the emotional and physical heat. Xaden suddenly pulls away in pain, his face twisted in a look of unbearable torture.

Xaden reveals that he accidentally channeled power from the earth during their moment of high emotion. Two faint marks of discoloration appear on the wooden headboard where his thumbs were resting during the channel. He realizes he took the power without thinking or making a conscious choice to do so. Xaden expresses horror at the thought that he might have accidentally drained Violet instead of the wood. He vows to maintain strict control and avoid any further physical intimacy that might trigger his hunger.



### Chapter 4
Xaden explains that he felt Violet's power rise and immediately sensed his own loss of control. He insists on keeping his distance to ensure her safety, fearing his hunger for power is becoming instinctual. Violet discovers that Xaden's eyes show no trace of red, indicating the channeling was minimal and caught early. They discuss the political situation in Tyrrendor and the presence of high-ranking Houses at Basgiath. Brennan interrupts them, demanding Xaden's presence at a meeting regarding the ongoing treaty negotiations.

Mira returns to Basgiath early, bringing a new conduit for Violet and a breakthrough in runework. She presents a runed practice disk that has been altered to nullify specific runes without destroying the others. This discovery allows for the possibility of providing fliers with protection within the wards without full integration. Mira calculates that the wards at Aretia will collapse entirely within six months if a solution is not found. Brennan admits he is willing to risk Violet on the mission because the alliance needs the seventh breed.

Mira accuses Brennan of acting like their mother by sacrificing family members for the perceived greater good. She throws the runed disk at him, demanding he show it to the Senarium as a better alternative. The siblings argue over the morality of the mission and the true cost of the war against the venin. Tairn warns Violet that a hidden den of dragons will likely not welcome their intrusion or help. Violet realizes she must use Mira's discovery to save the negotiations and her deal with Tecarus.



### Chapter 5
Violet, Rhiannon, and Ridoc wait in the gathering hall for news while the first-years tour with the fliers. Rumors spread through the Navarrian riders, causing them to suddenly vacate the hall and head for the courtyard. Baylor and Sloane arrive, reporting that First Wing has seized a flier and is forcing a illegal challenge. Aura Beinhaven holds a dagger to the throat of a first-year flier in front of a divided crowd. Dain Aetos attempts to de-escalate the situation by citing the Codex, but Aura rejects his authority.

Aura accuses the Aretian riders of being cowards and deserters who allowed the wards to fall. The Navarrian riders cheer as Aura demands a champion to fight her in exchange for the flier's life. Dain disarms Aura and pushes the flier to safety, but she challenges him to a hand-to-hand fight. Ridoc and Aaric prepare for a riot as Navarrian and Aretian riders draw their weapons against each other. Aura mocks Dain's birthright and his status as a rebel, further inciting the aggressive crowd.

Andarna points out to Violet that Dain is not the strongest rider present in the courtyard. Aura prepares to use her fire-wielding signet against Dain, who remains unarmed to avoid further violence. Violet throws a dagger that pins Aura's glove to a wooden support, preventing her from igniting her flame. Violet steps forward to accept the challenge, declaring herself a revolutionary rather than a rebel. She opens the door to Tairn’s power, readying herself to fight Aura and prevent a total massacre.
