### Chapter 36
Xaden and Aaric provide medical aid to Garrick after he collapses from a poisoned slice of cake at the triumvirate's dinner. Violet identifies the poison's scent and confronts the triumvirate, claiming she poisoned their drinks earlier in the evening with illegal arinmint tea. She threatens Faris with a slow death unless he provides an antidote, though she is actually bluffing to gain leverage. While Mira and the others secure the house, Violet and Ridoc search the kitchen and find the cook hiding in a pantry. Violet realizes the poison is from fermented zakia berries after noticing blue stains on the cook's fingernails and smelling overripe fruit. The cook attacks them with a knife, and though Violet pins him to a doorframe with a dagger, Ridoc is nearly stabbed in the side.


### Chapter 37
Ridoc reveals the cook's knife only sliced his flight jacket, leaving him unharmed, and Violet confirms she knows the antidote for the zakia berry poison. Violet uses her father's field guide to identify that fig or lime slurry will neutralize the poison, and Dain retrieves figs from their room. Garrick regains consciousness after swallowing the fig mixture, and Xaden reacts with rage, nearly killing Faris before Violet pulls him back. Violet forces Faris to drink a "slurry" she claims is the antidote to the arinmint tea, though it is merely a non-lethal mixture used to coerce him. She reveals that Andarna is currently stationed at Faris's parents' house as an irid to ensure the squad's safe departure from the island. The group flies northwest toward Zehyllna, and they eventually land at a massive amphitheater near the capital city of Xortrys.


### Chapter 38
The people of Zehyllna welcome the squad with cheers rather than weapons, and Violet notices the presence of ambient magic that allows her to communicate with Tairn and Andarna. An emissary named Calixta explains that the squad must play a game of luck dedicated to the god Zihnal to earn an alliance and entry to the city. Each member draws a card representing a gift that must be accepted without complaint, ranging from wine to physical violence. Dain receives a slap to the face, while others receive items like a broken mirror, a rusted bucket, and a broken compass. Violet accepts the broken compass, and Ridoc is kissed on the cheeks by a local man, while Cat receives a ruby necklace. The game turns deadly when Trager draws "The Arrow" card, and an archer immediately shoots him through the heart. Trager dies instantly in front of the squad, and his dragon Silaraine collapses and dies moments later from the broken bond.


### Chapter 39
Xaden forces Violet to maintain her composure and prevent a violent retaliation so Trager’s death is not in vain. Violet suppresses her grief and thanks Calixta for the "gift" of the arrow to satisfy the requirements of the Zihnal trial. The squad negotiates favorable terms for an alliance with Zehyllna, securing a promise of forty thousand troops for the upcoming war. After the negotiations, the group gathers their dead and decides to fly north to a minor uninhabited island to perform funeral rites. Cat insists on burning Silaraine and Trager together on an island where their remains will not be disturbed by locals. Violet struggles with feelings of inadequacy as a leader, but Xaden insists she was chosen by the dragons for a reason. They sleep in a circle formed by the dragons and prepare to search the remaining minor islands for the irids at dawn.


### Chapter 40
The squad flies to a volcanic island with black sand where they build a funeral pyre to burn the bodies of Trager and Silaraine. Violet divides the group into five search teams to scour the surrounding unmapped islands for any sign of the irid dragons. Xaden admits to Violet that he can feel the temptation of the ambient magic beneath his feet but promises not to channel from the earth. While waiting for the funeral fire to burn out, Violet stays behind on the beach with Ridoc and a sleeping Andarna. She hears the sound of heavy footsteps and sees talons flexing in the sand, though no dragons are visible. Six massive dragons, including one with sky-blue scales, reveal themselves by dropping their camouflage and surrounding Violet and Andarna. One of the dragons speaks directly into Violet's mind, confirming they have finally located the elusive irids.
