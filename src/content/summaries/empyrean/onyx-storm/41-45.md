### Chapter 41
Violet and Ridoc encounter six iridescent dragons who communicate telepathically with them. These dragons, known as irids, possess the unique ability to shift their scale colors to perfectly mirror the sky or jungle. Andarna reveals her true nature as an irid to her bonded humans and the other dragons. Tairn and Aotrom arrive to defend the humans, but the irids state they only wish to speak with Andarna. The irids, all of whom appear as feathertails—the golden-scaled juvenile form of dragons—express shock that Andarna chose her own scorpiontail.

Andarna explains that she transitioned from juvenile to adolescent and selected her tail by right. The irids listen in silence as Andarna recounts her time in Navarre and her bonding with Violet. She describes her choice to present herself to a human and participate in the harvest of riders. Tairn growls at the irids when they question Andarna's decisions, but she scolds him for interfering. Andarna details how she used her juvenile gift to slow time and protect Violet from attacks.

The irids learn about the rebellion, the war games, and the battle at Resson where Liam died. Violet notices that the irids seem increasingly judgmental as Andarna speaks of using magic in war. Andarna asks if the irids know how the venin were defeated or if a cure for them exists. The irids request privacy and walk into the ocean, changing their scales to match the water. Three dragons launch into the sky while the remaining three return to deliver their verdict.



### Chapter 42
The remaining irids declare that Violet has failed their test because she used Andarna as a weapon. They claim their breed is dedicated to peace and that humans have not evolved beyond violence. The tallest irid refuses to help fire the Aretian wardstone, stating human problems are not their concern. They suggest that the corrupted dark wielders should devour the land until they either die or change. Xaden and Garrick arrive on the beach just as the irids announce there is no cure for venin.

The female irid explains that stealing magic from the earth kills the soul one piece at a time. She identifies Xaden as an abomination because the rims of his irises shine bright red. The irids revoke Andarna's right to enter their isle or learn their ways due to her corruption by humans. All three irids vanish into the sky, leaving the humans and dragons in a state of total failure. Andarna retreats into the jungle in shame, blocking her bond with Violet as she shifts back to gold.

Ridoc notices Xaden's red eyes and realizes the commander has become a dark wielder. He demands that Violet provide a full explanation of the situation and the secrets she is keeping. Tairn follows Andarna into the vegetation to provide comfort while the others remain on the shore. Xaden slams his mental shields down to block Violet out as he processes the irids' insults. Violet stands in silence as guilt settles on her shoulders and her last hope for a cure disappears.



### Chapter 43
Violet explains Xaden's condition to Ridoc while they are isolated in the jungle under an agate sound-shield rune. Ridoc expresses anger over the secrets and compares Xaden to the dangerous dark wielder Barlowe. Violet defends Xaden, stating he only channeled magic from the earth to save others. Xaden overhears their conversation and reveals himself, maintaining a cold and distant demeanor. Ridoc agrees to keep the secret on the condition that Rhiannon, Sawyer, and Jesinia are informed.

The group begins a ten-day flight back to Basgiath, stopping in Deverelli to repair Andarna's harness. Xaden maintains his distance from Violet throughout the journey, feeling guilty about the mission's failure. They cross into Poromiel and fly over drained lands but avoid any direct encounters with venin. Upon returning to Basgiath, the group is met by Rhiannon and Sawyer on the flight field. The reunion is somber as Violet informs her friends that Trager was killed during the journey.

General Aetos interrupts them and mentions that King Tauri has forbidden any punishment for Aaric. He hands Violet a letter from Theophanie that confirms she was watching them beyond the wards. The letter taunts Violet, stating that the enemy has the one thing she seeks above all else. Aetos reveals that Suniva has fallen to dark wielders and Queen Maraya is dead. The news of the queen's death and the fall of the city leaves the riders in a state of shock.



### Chapter 44
Leadership interrogates the returned riders for twelve hours to gather details about their unauthorized journey. Scribes record the testimony as officers look for inconsistencies or evidence of forbidden knowledge. Battle Brief reveals that half of Krovla has been lost to the enemy's rapid and coordinated advance. Professor Devera explains that Suniva was destroyed by twelve venin and twelve wyvern in a single night. The attackers used a thunderstorm to mask their approach and bypass the city's fifty-foot walls.

A lightning-wielding Maven—a magic user of exceptional skill and power—likely Theophanie, incinerated the textile district. Twenty-five thousand people died in the fire and subsequent assault on the barracks and armory. The venin stole six crates of alloy-hilted daggers that had just been delivered to the city. Devera notes that the precision of the attack suggests there are traitors within the Navarrian ranks. Rhiannon identifies Violet as the only asset capable of countering such a powerful Maven in battle.

After the briefing, Ridoc insists that the squad meet in a private room within the Archives. Sawyer discusses his struggle with his prosthesis and his fear of mounting his dragon, Sliseag. Violet encourages Sawyer to find his own way to fly, much like she had to do with her saddle. Jesinia provides the squad with privacy and mentions she intercepted Violet's mail from General Aetos. Violet finally confesses to Rhiannon and Sawyer that Xaden is turning into a venin.



### Chapter 45
Violet wakes from a visceral nightmare in which a Sage forces her to watch the destruction of Draithus. She finds a silk nightgown and a note from Xaden, providing a moment of comfort in her room. During flight maneuvers, Professor Kaori introduces a new "battle-mount" tactic for quick combat transitions. Bodhi demonstrates a perfect run-on landing and remount onto his dragon's claw while in motion. Violet and Tairn practice a dangerous variation where Tairn drops her from a vertical dive.

Tairn catches Violet by the shoulders just before she hits the ground, allowing her to strike the target. Violet channels lightning to incinerate the projection before Tairn scoops her up and tosses her back to her seat. After the successful exercise, Xaden admits he loves Violet despite his ongoing internal struggle. Violet presents Xaden with an onyx wrist cuff made from a stone from the ruins of Riorson House. She explains that the gift is meant to remind him of their future together in Tyrrendor.

Xaden reveals he has gone forty-seven days without channeling from the earth since the event in Deverelli. He mentions a specific number of days he needs to reach to feel a sense of stability. Felix, a silver-bearded instructor from Aretia, arrives at Basgiath with a bundle of missives for Xaden. He reports that two wyvern recently breached the Aretia wards before crashing into a mountain. Felix warns that the wards are failing and insists on resuming Violet's training immediately.
