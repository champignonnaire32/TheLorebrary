### 16: Chapter 16
Violet receives medical treatment for her sprained ankle and a sword wound after bonding two dragons. Professor Kaori splints her leg while Jack Barlowe records his bond with an Orange Scorpiontail named Baide. Violet learns that Tynan died during the Threshing and Trina fell from her dragon’s back. General Melgren’s massive dragon Codagh arrives to lead a meeting of the Empyrean, the dragon leadership council. The council must decide the legality of Violet's unprecedented bond with both Tairn and Andarna.

Dain Aetos urges Violet to choose the smaller golden dragon for her own safety and career stability. Xaden Riorson interrupts to reveal that his dragon Sgaeyl is the mate of Violet’s dragon Tairn. Mated dragons cannot be separated, meaning Violet and Xaden’s lives are now physically and magically interdependent. The Empyrean rules that the double bond is legal because no dragon law explicitly forbids it. Violet officially becomes the first rider in history to bond two dragons simultaneously.

A permanent magical relic appearing as two dragons appears on Violet’s back to signify her new status. Xaden warns Violet that forty-one unbonded cadets will now hunt her to steal Tairn’s power. Violet discovers she can communicate mentally with Tairn and Andarna through their new connection. Dain kisses Violet in the shadows, but she realizes she no longer feels any romantic attraction to him. The chapter concludes with Violet recognizing she is now permanently tethered to her mother's enemy, Xaden Riorson.

### 17: Chapter 17
Violet moves into a private room in the first-year dormitory and begins a routine of self-imposed isolation. The unbonded cadets are assigned manual labor tasks previously handled by the first-years to maximize flight training time. Oren Seifert displays open hostility toward Violet in the dining hall, signaling the beginning of the unbonded’s hunt. Social dynamics in the quadrant shift as other cadets begin to fear Violet’s connection to the powerful Tairn. Xaden orders Imogen, a second-year with a rebellion relic, to oversee Violet’s physical conditioning.

Violet receives her flight leathers, which are reinforced uniforms designed without identifying patches for use behind enemy lines. She begins flight lessons on Tairn’s back but repeatedly falls off during high-speed combat maneuvers. Tairn initially uses magical energy bands to hold Violet in place before allowing her to test her own strength. The dragon explains that he will not use parlor tricks and intends to train Violet for actual warfare. Violet realizes she must strengthen her leg muscles significantly to survive the upcoming aerial challenges.

Dain tells Violet that their kiss was a mistake because he prioritizes his military rank and the Codex rules. Violet finds his obsession with political advancement disappointing and loses respect for her childhood friend. Imogen takes Violet to a specialized training room filled with wooden resistance machinery and pulleys. Violet begins a grueling leg-press routine to build the inner thigh strength required to grip a dragon’s back. She suspects that Imogen’s sudden helpfulness is a direct order from Xaden to ensure his own survival.

**



### 18: Chapter 18
Violet performs her assigned chores in the Archives and reunites with her scribe friend Jesinia Neilwart. She searches for "The Fables of the Barren," a book containing forbidden folklore about wyvern and venin. Wyvern are described as two-legged dragons created by venin, dark magic users who drain power from the earth. Jesinia reports that no records of the book or the legendary creatures exist in the official catalog. Professor Markham reminds Violet that the Archives use a fire mitigation system that limits oxygen to protect the books.

Sawyer manifests the signet of metallurgy, allowing him to magically bend and manipulate metal objects like forks and swords. Imogen warns the first-years that failure to manifest a signet within six months results in magic consuming the rider. The dragon relic serves as a conduit for magic that must be released through a unique signet power. Violet continues to struggle with flight lessons as Tairn performs steep dives and spirals that eject her from her seat. Tairn growls a warning to Dain, threatening to burn the ground if the squad leader harms Violet.

Violet and Dain have a public argument regarding his lack of faith in her survival and abilities. She rejects his constant pessimism and decides to distance herself from their lifelong friendship. Jeremiah, a first-year cadet, manifests as an inntinnsic, a rare and illegal rider capable of reading minds. Professor Carr, the quadrant's power specialist, executes Jeremiah by snapping his neck in the courtyard to protect military secrets. Violet watches the execution with horror, realizing that manifesting the wrong power is a guaranteed death sentence.

**



### 19: Chapter 19
Violet wakes from a dream of her father to find six unbonded cadets attacking her in her bedroom. The attackers include Oren Seifert and a female rider who used a stolen key to unlock the door. Violet uses her daggers to wound several assailants while Tairn bellows warnings in her mind. Oren pins Violet against the wall and begins to strangle her while holding a knife to her throat. Just before Oren can strike, Andarna uses her feathertail gift to stop the flow of time completely.

Violet moves through the frozen room while her attackers remain suspended in mid-action. Xaden Riorson enters the room and uses his shadow-wielding abilities to restrain the remaining intruders. He executes the six attackers, including Oren, by using shadows to slit their throats or crushing their windpipes. Xaden discovers Violet wears hidden dragon-scale armor and checks her bruised ribs for serious injuries. He leads her through a secret stone tunnel built into the mountain that leads directly to the flight field.

The dragons reveal that Andarna is a two-year-old feathertail, a golden hatchling with temporary magical gifts. Andarna’s gift allows Violet to pause time in small increments, a power that disappears as the dragon matures. Xaden warns Violet that the ability to stop time must remain a secret to prevent her execution by leadership. The dragons explain that feathertails are guarded in the Vale because their gifts are highly unpredictable and dangerous. Violet reveals to Xaden that Wingleader Amber Mavis was the one who unlocked her door for the assassins.

**



### 20: Chapter 20
Captain Fitzgibbons reads the morning death roll, which includes the names of the six cadets Xaden executed. Xaden reassigns Liam Mairi, a powerful first-year with a rebellion relic, to serve as Violet’s bodyguard. Liam is described as the strongest first-year in the quadrant and is bonded to a Red Daggertail. Xaden publicly accuses Wingleader Amber Mavis of orchestrating the illegal midnight assassination attempt on Violet. The accusation causes a massive uproar in the courtyard as Amber denies any involvement in the breach of the Codex.

Dain Aetos refuses to believe Amber is guilty and demands that Violet share her memories with him. Violet refuses to let Dain touch her, knowing he would discover her secret time-stopping ability. The wingleaders form a quorum, a legal council of four leaders, to judge the validity of Xaden’s claim. Tairn intercedes by mentally sharing Violet’s memories with the other dragons perched on the courtyard walls. The dragons confirm Amber’s guilt, forcing her Orange Daggertail dragon Claidh to submit to their collective judgement.

The wingleaders find Amber Mavis guilty of violating the Dragon Rider’s Codex and sentence her to death. Amber pleads for her life and tries to command Claidh to defend her, but the dragon remains paralyzed. Tairn lean forward from the wall and incinerates Amber with a single blast of high-temperature fire. The entire quadrant watches in silence as Amber is reduced to ash for her crimes against a fellow rider. Violet realizes that the justice of the Riders Quadrant is absolute and offers no mercy to those who break the law.
