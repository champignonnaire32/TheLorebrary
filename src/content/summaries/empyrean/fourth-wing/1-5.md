### Chapter 1: Chapter 1
Violet Sorrengail prepares for Conscription Day at Basgiath War College despite her lifelong training to become a scribe. Her mother, General Sorrengail, forces her into the Riders Quadrant against the protests of Violet's older sister, Mira. Mira provides Violet with specialized rider boots and a custom corset reinforced with dragon scales from her own dragon, Teine. Violet discards most of her sentimental books to lighten her rucksack for the treacherous parapet crossing. She learns that Xaden Riorson, the son of the rebellion leader who killed her brother Brennan, is a wingleader in the quadrant.

At the base of the turret, Violet meets Rhiannon Matthias and Dylan, two fellow candidates eager for the rider life. She notices candidates with swirling rebellion relics, marks given to children of traitors by General Melgren's dragon during their parents' executions. Violet trades one of her high-grip boots to Rhiannon after realizing Rhiannon's standard-issue soles are dangerously smooth for the rain. They climb two hundred and fifty stone steps to reach the opening of the parapet. Violet observes Xaden Riorson for the first time, noting his immense height, onyx eyes, and the jagged scar on his face.

The weather turns into a violent rainstorm as candidates begin the crossing over the two-hundred-foot drop. Dylan loses his footing on the slick stone and falls to his death in the river-bottomed valley below. Xaden recognizes Violet as the General's daughter and expresses his overt hatred for her family. He mocks her fragility and refuses to kill her himself, claiming the parapet will finish the job. Violet steps onto the narrow stone bridge as the rain intensifies, focused on surviving her first day.

### Chapter 2: Chapter 2
Violet begins her crossing of the narrow stone parapet while reciting historical facts about Navarre and Poromiel to maintain her focus. She crawls low to the stone when a powerful gust of wind threatens to blow her into the ravine. Behind her, a candidate named Jack Barlowe throws another scrawny candidate off the bridge to eliminate competition. Jack threatens to kill Violet next and begins pursuing her across the slick bridge with terrifying speed. Violet slips and nearly falls, but she manages to regain her footing and sprint for the citadel walls.

As she reaches the courtyard of the Riders Quadrant, Violet jumps from the parapet and draws a dagger. She holds the blade against Jack Barlowe's groin as he skids to a halt behind her. Two senior riders intervene, citing the Codex rule that prohibits cadets from harming one another in formation. Jack is forced to back down, but he scents Violet like a predator and promises to kill her later. Violet officially signs the roll as a cadet of the Riders Quadrant, narrowly surviving the crossing.

The senior riders inform Jack that he will face execution if he kills Violet outside of the sparring ring or authorized times. Violet realizes that while the Codex offers some protection, she is now surrounded by people who want her dead. She sheathes her weapon and prepares to face the next stage of her training. Rhiannon greets her in the courtyard, relieved that they both made it across safely. The chapter ends with Violet standing among the survivors, realizing her life has permanently changed.

### Chapter 3: Chapter 3
Violet experiences a physical collapse from adrenaline and a knee injury after her confrontation with Jack Barlowe. Her childhood friend Dain Aetos, now a second-year squad leader, finds her and hides her in a private alcove. Dain expresses shock and anger at Violet's presence in the quadrant, believing she will not survive the training. He reveals his signet power is the ability to read a person's recent memories through physical touch. Violet refuses his offer to sneak her into the Scribe Quadrant, fearing her mother's retribution if she deserts.

Dain guides Violet to his room to wrap her injured knee and provides her with a brief moment of safety. He explains that his dragon, Cath, is a Red Swordtail who has begun channeling magic to him. Violet returns to the courtyard and joins the formation for the official induction ceremony led by Commandant Panchek. The Commandant announces that three hundred and one candidates survived the parapet while sixty-seven died. Violet learns that she has been assigned to Second Squad, Flame Section, Second Wing, under Dain's command.

Xaden Riorson uses his authority as wingleader to move Violet’s entire squad into Fourth Wing, placing her directly under his control. Eight dragons land on the citadel's outer wall, serving as a terrifying reminder of the power the cadets hope to bond. A red dragon incinerates a fleeing cadet, bringing the total death count for the day to seventy. Xaden addresses the remaining cadets, warning them that they are prey rather than elite until they earn a dragon. Violet stands her ground as the navy-blue dragon, Sgaeyl, watches her with golden, judging eyes.

### Chapter 4: Chapter 4
The following morning begins with a somber reading of the death roll, commending the fallen souls to Malek, the god of death. Violet stands in her new Fourth Wing uniform, wearing a single silver star to denote her first-year status. Dain warns his squad that they must be prepared for the sparring ring, where riders are truly made or broken. Violet worries about her physical limitations and the "Gauntlet" obstacle course she must eventually face. She heads toward her first academic classes, trying to commit the names of her squadmates to memory.

Violet meets with Dain privately behind a marble pillar in the rotunda to discuss her safety. Dain confirms that Xaden Riorson moved her squad specifically to have a direct line of command for potential revenge. He describes Xaden's dragon, Sgaeyl, as a vicious Blue Daggertail that even other dragons avoid. Dain also reveals that he wears a compass patch to hide his classified memory-reading signet from the general population. He urges Violet to cut her hair to avoid giving opponents an advantage in sparring, but she refuses.

Xaden Riorson appears at the top of the rotunda steps and observes the private interaction between Violet and Dain. He mocks their childhood connection and implies that their relationship is an obvious liability. Dain grows defensive and warns Violet that Xaden will likely attempt to kill her within the rules of the Codex. As Xaden descends the stairs toward them, the atmosphere becomes lethally tense. Dain orders Violet to run to her next class to avoid a confrontation, and she bolts from the room.

### Chapter 5: Chapter 5

Violet notes the strategic layout of the tiered room and the presence of various wing patches among the students. She tries to focus on the military intelligence being shared despite her exhaustion and the looming threat of Xaden.

Violet realizes that the Riders Quadrant is essentially a meat grinder designed to produce the most effective weapons for the war. She observes the different dragon breeds represented on the patches and uniforms of her fellow cadets. The atmosphere is thick with a mixture of academic rigor and the constant threat of violence. Violet understands that her knowledge of history and geography might be her only advantage in this environment.

She remains alert to her surroundings, knowing that any moment could lead to a test of her survival. The specific details of the rebellion and her family's role in it continue to cast a shadow over her interactions. She prepares herself for the sparring sessions that will follow the academic briefs.
