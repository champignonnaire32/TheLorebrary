### Chapter 36
Xaden and the riders arrive at Resson to find venin, which are magic-corrupted humans with red eyes and distended veins, attacking the trading post. The group discovers the existence of wyvern, two-legged winged monsters created by venin to compete with dragons. Xaden offers the riders a chance to flee to their official headquarters, but everyone chooses to stay and defend the civilians. Tairn leads the dragons into the city while Violet uses her lightning to provide cover for the evacuation. Liam is thrown from his dragon, Deigh, after a massive gray wyvern eviscerates the red dragon in midair.

Violet catches the falling Liam and secures him to Tairn’s saddle while Deigh crashes into the hillside below. Tairn confirms that Deigh has died, which triggers a fatal physical and mental collapse in his rider. Xaden creates a wall of shadows to hold back a horde of dozens of incoming wyvern while Violet lands to reach her friend. Liam extracts a promise from Violet to protect his sister, Sloane, before he dies in her lap. Xaden releases a soul-rending scream of grief for his foster brother before preparing for the final stand against the monsters.

Violet mounts Tairn with a focus on revenge and prepares to wield lightning against the massive swarm of wyvern. Xaden maintains the shadow wall despite approaching burnout to give Violet time to target the enemy riders. Tairn identifies the lead venin as the primary threat among the dozens of riderless and rider-led monsters. The riders realize they are trapped between the burning trading post and the overwhelming force of the wyvern horde. Violet lets her power loose to strike a wyvern, but she is blindsided when Tairn roars in sudden agony.



### Chapter 37
A female venin lands on Tairn’s back and stabs him with a sword behind his wings. Violet unbuckles her straps and climbs onto the dragon's back to engage the enemy in hand-to-hand combat. The venin stabs Violet in the side with a poisoned dagger before Violet manages to plunge a runed blade into the attacker's ribs. The death of the venin causes three riderless wyvern to immediately fall from the sky and die. Violet realizes that killing the venin creators is the only way to destroy the entire horde of wyvern.

Xaden drops his shadow wall to allow Violet to target the remaining venin riders in the approaching swarm. Andarna, the golden dragon, uses her innate magic to stop time so Violet can aim her lightning strikes precisely. Violet draws massive amounts of power from Tairn and Andarna to strike the lead venin rider with a silver-blue bolt. The strike kills the leader and triggers the immediate death of more than half of the attacking wyvern. Xaden uses a rope of shadows to pull the final venin from his mount and executes him with a dagger.

The remaining wyvern fall from the sky as their creators are eliminated, ending the battle at Resson. Violet loses consciousness from the venin poison and falls from Tairn’s back as her strength fails. Xaden dives and catches her midair before she can hit the ground near the ruined trading post. The poison burns through Violet’s veins, turning her blood black and cutting off her mental connection to her dragons. Tairn and Sgaeyl remain close as Xaden frantically tries to find a way to save Violet from the magical toxin.



### Chapter 38
Violet drifts in and out of consciousness while Xaden holds her and assesses the severity of the poisoned wound. The riders observe that Violet's blood has turned black, confirming the magical nature of the venin toxin. Garrick and Imogen argue about whether to return to Basgiath or seek help at a closer, secret location. Xaden decides to fly to a hidden fortress despite warnings that doing so will expose their greatest secrets. The group begins a grueling twelve-hour flight across the Poromish border to find a mender, a rider with the ability to restore living tissue, who can treat the poison.

The flight takes the group over the Cliffs of Dralor, a massive mountain range that serves as a natural barrier. Violet feels the poison paralyzing her physical body and severing her ability to access her lightning or dragons. Xaden whispers confessions of love and pleas for her to stay alive while she struggles to breathe. He promises to tell her every secret if she survives the night and fights the darkening of her blood. Tairn roars in agitation as he follows Sgaeyl toward the hidden destination while the riders remain on high alert.

The group lands at a hidden fortress where Xaden carries Violet through the halls in a state of desperation. He ignores the protests of other residents and orders a healer to save her life at any cost. Violet experiences hallucinations of the rebellion leaders and the faces of those who have died in the battle. The pain of the magical fire cauterizing her veins eventually causes her to fade back into a deep blackness. Xaden stays by her side, refusing to leave even as the other riders prepare for the fallout of their choices.



### Chapter 39
Violet wakes up three days later in a bedroom located in the secret, rebuilt city of Aretia, a fortress with identical green roofs and stone arches. Xaden explains that he has not left her bedside and admits that his foster brother, Liam, is dead. He reveals that Aretia was never fully destroyed and has been serving as the headquarters for the rebellion. The city is protected by the rebellion relics, which prevent General Melgren from seeing any outcome where the marked riders assemble. Violet discovers that her poisoned wound has been mended, leaving only a thin silver scar on her hip.

Xaden confesses his long-standing feelings for Violet and explains why he kept the existence of Aretia a secret. Violet identifies the Temple of Amari from drawings and realizes the scale of the deception maintained by her mother and Navarran leadership. She informs Xaden that while she will join the revolution, she can no longer trust him with her heart. Xaden gives Violet a small wood carving of Andarna that Liam was working on before he died. He vows to spend every day earning back her trust despite her refusal to reconcile.

Violet notices that Andarna has grown significantly larger and has lost her golden time-stopping ability during her transition to adolescence. Xaden introduces Violet to the council and explains that the iron box retrieved from Resson contains a new weapon or technology. The siblings and riders discuss the upcoming war against the venin and the need to recruit more riders to the cause. A figure appears in the doorway, and Violet is shocked to see her brother, Brennan, standing there alive. Brennan, who is now a mender for the revolution, welcomes Violet and reveals he saved her life using his ability to restore life to the dying.
