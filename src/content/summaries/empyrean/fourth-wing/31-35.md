### 31: Chapter Thirty-One
Violet finds a hidden note from her late father inside a book of fables, warning her that history can be erased and to make the right choice when the time comes. Xaden reveals he was at the Athebyne outpost during his secret mission, and the two argue about their escalating emotional and sexual connection. Violet refuses to continue their physical relationship unless Xaden acknowledges his feelings, leading to a period of tense avoidance. During Battle Brief, Professor Devera discusses the weakening wards and the need for reinforcements while Violet and Xaden exchange mental arguments. The chapter ends with Violet and Liam attending a Reunification Day party where King Tauri and General Melgren are present. Violet eventually leaves the party to find Xaden, who is mourning his father alone on the parapet.

### 32: Chapter Thirty-Two
Violet risks her life by walking the parapet in the dark to reach Xaden, ultimately confessing that she is in love with him. Xaden admits he has wanted her since they first met and reveals his own jealousy regarding her friendship with Dain. They reconcile and return to Xaden's room, where they engage in a night of intense physical intimacy that causes Violet’s lightning power to accidentally shatter his window. Between rounds of sex, Xaden tells Violet that he views his rebellion relic as a gift rather than a curse. He promises to keep her and admits he doesn't deserve her, though he stops short of saying he loves her back. Their night is interrupted by Garrick pounding on the door with news of an urgent mandatory formation. Garrick reports that the quadrant is under attack, forcing them to dress and arm themselves immediately.

### 33: Chapter Thirty-Three
The entire Riders Quadrant gathers in the courtyard for an urgent formation at four in the morning to begin the final War Games exercise. Colonel Aetos reads a scenario involving falling wards and a massive gryphon rider attack, assigning Fourth Wing to the southeast outposts. Xaden is ordered to establish his headquarters at Athebyne, an outpost located beyond the protection of the magical wards. To Violet's shock, Xaden selects her, Liam, and Imogen for his headquarters squad, separating them from Dain and their usual sections. Dain protests the decision, but Xaden insists that the bond between Sgaeyl and Tairn makes the separation of the riders impossible. The chapter concludes with the riders mounting their dragons and departing Basgiath for the long flight to the border outposts. Violet notes that she has chosen to follow Xaden, effectively changing the course of her life forever.

### 34: Chapter Thirty-Four
The riders fly for seven hours, crossing beyond the magical wards where Violet feels a distinct snap and a change in the atmosphere. They land at a lake near the Athebyne outpost to rest and hydrate before the final leg of their journey. Xaden and Violet share a private moment behind some boulders, where he massages her sore legs and they discuss their relationship. Their intimacy is interrupted when a pair of gryphon riders, known as fliers, arrives at the lake to meet with Xaden. The fliers warn Xaden that a village was recently destroyed by venin, creatures Violet previously believed were only found in children's fables. Xaden reveals he has been working with the fliers, which causes Violet to realize he has been aiding the enemy. The fliers depart after warning that a horde of venin is moving toward the border trading post.

### 35: Chapter Thirty-Five
Violet realizes that everyone in Xaden's chosen squad carries a rebellion relic and that she is the only rider who was not in on the secret. She feels betrayed by Xaden and her own dragons, Tairn and Andarna, who admit they knew about Xaden's activities because of the bond between mates. Xaden explains that he has been supplying weapons to the fliers to help them fight the venin, which he insists are a real and growing threat. He reveals that the Navarrian leadership knows about the venin but chooses to do nothing to protect those outside the wards. Xaden gives Violet a special dagger made of an alloy that can kill venin, explaining that the material comes from his mother's stolen supply. Violet struggles to reconcile her love for Xaden with the knowledge of his treason against their kingdom.
