### Chapter 31
Xaden visits Violet’s room to help her clean the furniture they destroyed during their night together. Violet discovers a hidden letter from her father inside an old book of fables warning her that history can be erased. She confronts Xaden about her feelings and refuses to continue their physical relationship unless he admits he is falling for her. Xaden admits he previously flew to Athebyne, a border outpost, with Bodhi but remains evasive about the details of his mission. They agree to stop their sexual encounters for now to avoid further complicating their emotional bond.

At lunch, Sawyer informs the group that King Tauri will be visiting Basgiath to celebrate Reunification Day, the anniversary of the Battle of Aretia. During Battle Brief, Professor Devera discusses the weakening wards and the diminishing number of riders in the quadrant. Violet questions Xaden mentally about his relationship with Imogen while Professor Devera lectures the class. Xaden counters her questions by suggesting she pay attention to the tactical lecture instead of her insecurities. The school prepares for the mandatory celebration where students must wear their formal black dress uniforms.

The Reunification Day party transforms the courtyard into a ballroom filled with officers and cadets from all quadrants. Violet sees her mother, General Sorrengail, holding court alongside King Tauri and the unnerving General Melgren. Liam faces public scrutiny for his rebellion relic, the swirled mark on his skin, while escorting Violet through the crowd. King Tauri and Melgren question Liam about his loyalty and inquire about Xaden’s current whereabouts. Violet leaves the festivities with Liam to find Xaden and the other marked ones who are absent.

### Chapter 32
Violet walks the dangerous parapet, the narrow stone bridge between the walls, barefoot and in a dress at night. She confesses her love for Xaden despite his previous warnings to stay emotionally distant from him. Xaden admits his own jealousy regarding Dain and confirms he has wanted Violet since their first meeting. They leave the cold bridge together and return to the safety of the dormitory walls. Xaden leads her to his large third-year bedroom which is located at the end of the Fourth Wing hallway.

Violet takes off Xaden’s boots and flight leathers while he watches her with intense interest. They engage in a passionate encounter where Violet realizes she is fully committed to their relationship. During their intimacy, Violet’s uncontrolled lightning power surges and shatters the glass windows of the dormitory room. Xaden uses his body to shield Violet from the flying shards as the storm rages outside. He assures her that he has a shield up and tells her not to worry about the broken glass.

Their night is interrupted by Garrick pounding on the door with urgent news of an attack. Garrick informs them that the entire Riders Quadrant must immediately report to the courtyard for formation. Violet and Xaden scramble to find their clothes and weapons in the dark room. Xaden tells Violet that the wards might be falling and they need to be ready for battle. They join the sea of riders running through the hallways to reach the mandatory muster.

### Chapter 33
Violet dresses quickly and wears Xaden’s wingleader flight jacket to hide her formal attire during the emergency muster. Commandant Panchek and Colonel Aetos reveal the alert is the final exercise of the annual War Games. The scenario involves a massive gryphon rider invasion occurring while the magical wards of Navarre are failing. Each wing is assigned a region of the border to reinforce during the five-day simulated war. Xaden is ordered to establish a headquarters at the Athebyne outpost located beyond the kingdom’s wards.

Xaden handpicks a specialized squad including Violet, Liam, and Imogen to accompany him on the mission. Dain Aetos protests the decision, claiming Xaden is putting Violet in danger to get revenge on her mother. Xaden argues that Violet’s lightning signet is a necessary asset for the defense of the outpost. Sgaeyl and Tairn demonstrate their bond’s necessity by intimidating Dain into accepting the arrangement. Violet chooses to follow Xaden, leaving a disappointed and hurt Dain behind on the flight field.

The riders gather their gear and prepare to depart Basgiath on their dragons for a long flight. Andarna appears with a special triangular harness designed by Xaden to hook onto Tairn’s chest. Tairn and Andarna coordinate their movements to ensure the smaller dragon can survive the journey. Violet watches as the different wings disperse in various directions to their assigned outposts. The squad takes flight into the early morning air, heading southeast toward the Cliffs of Dralor.

### Chapter 34
The squad flies for seven hours and crosses the border of the wards where magic feels wilder. Violet experiences a snapping sensation in her chest as the dragon-protected barrier is left behind. They skirt the Cliffs of Dralor, the massive stone heights of Tyrrendor, and navigate the jagged mountain peaks. The group stops at a secluded lake near Athebyne to hydrate and allow the dragons to rest. Andarna detaches from Tairn’s harness and lands on the shoreline to shake out her wings.

Xaden takes Violet behind a cluster of boulders to help her recover from the long flight. He massages her sore leg muscles and they share a moment of private intimacy. Xaden admits he is greedy for Violet and kisses her with a slow, luxurious intensity. Their quiet time is interrupted by the arrival of two gryphon fliers, riders from the neighboring kingdom of Poromiel. The gryphons are third the size of dragons but possess sharp beaks and lethal claws.

Tairn lands near the shoreline to defend Violet as she prepares to wield lightning against the fliers. The gryphon riders recognize Violet as the daughter of the famous General Sorrengail. Xaden intervenes and prevents Violet from attacking the perceived Navarrian enemies. He reveals a pre-existing relationship with the fliers and speaks to them about a missing shipment. Violet watches in shock as Xaden treats the enemy riders with familiarity instead of hostility.

### Chapter 35
Xaden discusses a village destroyed by venin with the fliers, confirming the mythical monsters are real. The fliers warn that a horde of dark wielders is heading north toward the trading post near Athebyne. They describe the venin as creatures who tap into magic from the ground without a dragon or gryphon. The male flier insults Violet and suggests ransoming her to the Navarrian king for weapons. Xaden uses his shadows to threaten the fliers and protect Violet from any immediate physical harm.

The gryphon riders depart on their mounts to return to their own people in Poromiel. Violet identifies every member of her current squad except herself as a carrier of the rebellion relic. She accuses Liam of silence while he watched her fall in love with Xaden. Tairn explains that dragons are bound by the sacred bond between mates, connecting him to Sgaeyl. Violet stands alone while the squad members gathered by the lake watch her with expectation.

Xaden explains that he is smuggling weapons made from ward-powering alloy to the fliers to fight the venin. He reveals that Navarrian leadership knows about the venin attacks but refuses to intervene. The kingdom allows civilians to be slaughtered outside the borders to maintain the magical wards’ integrity. Xaden hands Violet a rune-marked dagger and attempts to justify his treasonous actions against the crown. Violet learns that her entire world is built on lies and deception regarding the safety of the realm.
