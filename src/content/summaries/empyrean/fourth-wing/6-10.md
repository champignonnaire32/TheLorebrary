### Chapter 6: Chapter 6
Dain carries an injured Violet through a stone bridge into the Healer Quadrant. Winifred the healer receives them and summons Nolon, a rare rider mender with the power to restore things to their original state. Dain argues against mending Violet so she can use the injury as an excuse to transfer to the Scribe Quadrant. Violet refuses the transfer and bites down on a leather strap while Nolon twists her shoulder back into place with magic. The process causes Violet white-hot agony before she loses consciousness from the intensity of the mending.

Violet returns to the barracks wearing a light-blue sling to immobilize her arm and shoulder. Rhiannon greets her and promises to help her prepare for upcoming hand-to-hand challenges on the mat. Violet discovers a journal under her pillow with a note from her sister Mira. The book belonged to their late brother Brennan and contains maps and survival strategies for the Riders Quadrant. Brennan’s writing reveals that instructors decide challenge matches a week in advance to weed out the weakest cadets.

Violet realizes she can use this information to prepare for her opponents before ever stepping on the mat. She hides the journal from her mother, General Sorrengail, who previously burned all of Brennan's belongings. The text in the journal provides a map showing where instructors meet and how to access secret areas. Violet forms a plan to gather resources during the two weeks before challenges officially begin. She smiles because she has finally identified a concrete way to survive the upcoming assessments.





### Chapter 7: Chapter 7
Violet sneaks out after curfew to the Iakobos River to gather lavender fonilee berries. These berries function as a potent tool for poisoning when they are picked prematurely and dried. While climbing an oak tree, Violet spots Imogen and Xaden Riorson approaching the riverbank below. Nearly two dozen riders with rebellion relics, which are swirling marks on the skin of children of rebels, gather under the tree. This meeting is a capital offense because marked ones are legally forbidden from assembling in groups larger than three.

Xaden leads the group and offers training to the first-years who are struggling with hand-to-hand combat. He refuses to help a panicked cadet who wants to go home, stating that the cadet will not survive. Violet overhears the group discussing her mother's role in the execution of their parents. Xaden claims the right to handle Violet himself and forbids the others from killing her. The group disperses in threes to avoid detection, leaving Xaden alone at the base of the tree.

Xaden uses his signet ability as a shadow wielder to pull Violet out of the tree with dark, finger-like shadows. Violet defends herself by throwing daggers at the tree trunk surrounding Xaden’s head. Xaden retrieves her blades with his shadows and returns them to her, mocking her defensive stance. Violet promises not to reveal the secret meeting because the cadets were only seeking survival advice. Xaden lets her go but warns that he will handle her when she least expects it.





### Chapter 8: Chapter 8
Violet works breakfast duty to secretly spike her opponents' food with various poisons and herbs. She uses powdered fonilee berries to induce illness in Oren Seifert during his morning meal. In Professor Kaori’s class, Violet studies illusions of various dragons, including the aggressive Red Scorpiontail. Kaori reveals that fewer dragons are choosing to bond this year, threatening the integrity of Navarre's magical wards. The class views an image of Sgaeyl, the rare Blue Daggertail bonded to Xaden Riorson.

Violet learns that Naolin, a previous rider, died while attempting to use his siphoning signet to resurrect her brother Brennan. Jack Barlowe expresses his desire to bond a powerful black dragon, which are the smartest and rarest of the breeds. Kaori privately tells Violet she is smarter and more compassionate than her siblings were at her age. Violet taunts Jack by throwing two daggers that land inches from his head and groin. She steps onto the mat to face Oren Seifert, who is already vomiting from the poison she administered.

Violet wins the match when Oren yields while retching, allowing her to claim his heavy dagger as a trophy. Throughout August, she continues to use mushrooms, roots, and leaves to incapacitate her weekly challengers. She gains five new daggers and maintains her position in the quadrant despite her physical frailty. Her strategy fails when her intended opponent, Rayma Corrie, is sent to the healers before their scheduled match. Xaden Riorson steps onto the mat to fill the vacancy and challenge Violet directly.





### Chapter 9: Chapter 9
Xaden faces Violet on the mat and removes all of his weapons, handing them to Imogen. Violet throws a dagger at his chest, but he catches the blade with his bare hand. Xaden disarms her repeatedly, pinning her to the mat and kicking her weapons away to show her vulnerability. He whispers that he knows about her poisoning strategy and warns that she cannot poison every enemy in battle. Xaden instructs her on where to strike larger opponents, such as the kidneys or gaps in armor.

Violet realizes she is attracted to Xaden as he holds her pinned between his thighs on the mat. He slides a ruby-hilted dagger back into her sheath before ending the training session. Xaden tells Dain that Violet needs more instruction and less overprotective sheltering. Later that night, Violet sneaks into Dain’s room for a massage to soothe her bruised muscles. Dain reveals he bonded a dragon named Cath and explains that dragons can sense the thoughts of their riders.

Dain informs Violet that he asked Colonel Markham to allow her a late transfer into the Scribe Quadrant. Violet becomes angry that Dain went behind her back to arrange her exit from the riders. Dain argues that other cadets will hunt her during Threshing, an event where dragons choose their riders. He describes how first-years use the chaos of the event to eliminate those they perceive as liabilities. Violet eventually promises to think about the transfer but remains committed to staying for now.





### Chapter 10: Chapter 10
The squad begins practicing the Gauntlet, a vertical obstacle course featuring five switchbacks and various moving traps. Professor Emetterio explains that the course mimics the balance and strength required to ride a dragon in combat. Sawyer, a repeat cadet, demonstrates the course by successfully navigating spinning logs and iron pillars. The obstacles include a rotating wheel, swinging metal rods, and a final vertical ramp. Sawyer hauls himself over the top edge of the ramp to complete the flawless run.

Tynan freezes in terror while clinging to a swinging buoy ball over a thirty-foot drop. Rhiannon uses a safety rope to help Tynan climb down, but his cowardice draws mockery from Ridoc. Violet begins her run and recites dragon facts to stay calm while navigating the spinning log and pillars. She manages the swinging metal rods but slams her face into the mountain when her hand slips. Violet uses a rope to regain her footing and completes the twisting staircase of rotating timbers.

Aurelie follows behind her but loses her footing on the spinning posts and falls to her death. Violet watches in shock as Aurelie tumbles down the cliffside before she can reach for a rope. During the morning roll call, Violet listens as Captain Fitzgibbons reads the names of those killed on the course. Violet carries Aurelie’s heavy pack to the roof of the academic tower to incinerate it in the burn pit. She stands on the turret edge and watches the shadows of dragons flying back toward the Vale.
