### Chapter 26
Jack Barlowe returns to the quadrant and smiles at Violet. Tairn identifies that Baide hid Jack's survival from the other dragons in the Vale. Rhiannon, Ridoc, and Sawyer discuss the necessity of killing Jack again. Professor Devera announces an unprecedented attack on Samara outpost by three drifts of gryphons. Violet searches for her bond with Xaden but cannot feel him from such a distance.

Devera reports that the outpost was defended successfully with no rider fatalities. She signals Xaden's injury by scratching her neck where his relic is located. Violet immediately leaves the briefing without permission despite Professor Markham's shouts. Tairn commands Violet to get to the courtyard for an immediate flight. Bodhi intercepts Violet to give her his flight jacket so she will not freeze.

Violet boards Tairn and flies toward Samara during an eight-hour journey. She struggles to separate her own anxiety from Tairn's primal desperation. Tairn's need to see Sgaeyl unharmed floods Violet's senses and overrides her logic. Violet fears Xaden is severely wounded based on the signals from the briefing. The flight concludes with Violet counting every second until they reach the outpost gates.



### Chapter 27
Violet lands at Samara and rushes inside the outpost before the portcullis fully opens. She observes masonry damage from the gryphon attack but finds Mira unharmed. Mira informs Violet that Xaden is currently in the sparring gym. Violet finds Xaden sparring aggressively with Garrick and realizes he is not incapacitated. Relief and rage consume Violet as she realizes she flew eight hours for a non-emergency.

Xaden notices Violet's lack of emotional control and carries her to a bathing chamber. He uses a cold water spray to shock her into raising her mental shields. Once Violet is stable, Xaden notices she is wearing Bodhi's third-year flight jacket. He expresses territorial jealousy before Violet explains her reckless departure from Basgiath. Xaden reveals a deep, puckered scar on his arm where he was nearly severed.

Xaden admits he would be equally reckless if he heard Violet was in danger. They argue about their relationship before reconciling and having sex on a stone bench. Violet's power manifests as lightning during her climax, which Xaden's shadows quickly smother. Xaden tells Violet he needs her more than she can possibly understand. They clean up and prepare to fly to a secret rendezvous with gryphon riders.



### Chapter 28
Violet and Xaden fly beyond the Navarrian wards at midnight to meet a Poromish drift. They land by a dark lake where they meet Syrena and seven gryphons. Gryphons are magical creatures that are half-eagle and half-lion in appearance. Xaden provides two bags containing twenty-three alloy daggers to the fliers. Syrena introduces Catriona, a beautiful flier who looks at Xaden with unmistakable longing.

Catriona criticizes Xaden for failing to acquire the luminary from Viscount Tecarus. Xaden threatens to stop all weapon shipments if the fliers continue attacking outposts. He explains that the outposts power the wards that protect the dragon hatching grounds. Violet realizes the fliers know Xaden personally and have a history with him. Xaden admits he would rather let fliers die than sacrifice Navarrian security.

Syrena warns that venin will reach the border within a single year. Xaden keeps one dagger for himself and specifies the next drop location near Athebyne. Violet settles back into the saddle as they launch into the night sky. She remembers Bodhi's previous comments and realizes Catriona is Xaden's former lover. A hollow feeling of jealousy and insecurity settles in Violet's stomach during the flight.



### Chapter 29
Tairn lectures Violet on her jealousy regarding Catriona during the flight back to Samara. Xaden confirms Catriona is his ex-girlfriend but insists the relationship ended before he met Violet. He explains that Viscount Tecarus wants to see Violet wield lightning in exchange for the luminary. Xaden refuses the deal because Tecarus is known for collecting and imprisoning precious things. He gives Violet a hidden alloy dagger to protect herself against the venin.

Violet informs Xaden about Jack Barlowe's survival and the signet-blocking elixir used at Basgiath. She returns to the college and finds Sloane Matthias practicing at the Gauntlet. Professor Emetterio advises Violet to blame her absence on Tairn's uncontrollable mating bond. Varrish and his henchmen intercept Violet in the hallway to search her for contraband. Professor Kaori intervenes and announces that Panchek has cleared Violet of all charges.

Varrish confiscates Xaden's flight jacket but finds nothing hidden in the pockets. Rhiannon uses her new signet ability to pull the alloy dagger through a wall. Violet realizes Rhiannon's power allows her to move objects through solid matter. She invites her squadmates into her room and decides to reveal the truth. Violet tells Rhiannon, Ridoc, and Sawyer everything about the venin and the secret war.



### Chapter 30
Violet explains the venin threat and the Navarrian cover-up to her shocked squadmates. Rhiannon immediately resolves to help Violet research ward-weaving in the Archives. Ridoc mentions a ledger in General Sorrengail's office that lists journals by the First Six. The First Six were the original riders who established the wards and the college. The group plans to find the journals of Lyra and Warrick in a sublevel vault.

Research continues for weeks as the group avoids the strangely reformed Jack Barlowe. Andarna remains in a deep sleep that Tairn admits is longer than expected. During Threshing, Aaric bonds a Blue Clubtail and Sloane bonds a Red Daggertail. Jesinia sends a coded message that she has found the location of the vault. Violet and Ridoc head to the academic tower for watch duty while the others celebrate.

Four infantry assassins ambush Violet on the turret to deliver a lethal message. The assassins kill Eya before pushing Violet toward the edge of the tower. Violet loses her daggers and clings to the stone lip of the turret. The tallest assassin prepares to pry her fingers loose and drop her to her death. An unknown savior stabs the assassin through the spine from behind as Violet dangles.
