### Chapter 21
Violet starts her Thursday with a sprained shoulder in a sling after a rough rolling dismount from Tairn. Professor Devera fills in for history class because Professor Levini is called away to an attack on the Eastern Wing. Devera announces that recent graduate Masen Sanborn died in the attack, which Violet suspects is an assassination to cover up the events at Resson. The class discusses the historical costs of Navarrian unification, specifically the loss of original languages and folklore from provinces like Tyrrendor. Violet argues with a Third Wing cadet about the limited access and possible translation errors within the Archives' historical texts.

During combat practice, Wingleader Dain Aetos invokes his right to challenge Violet to a match despite her injury. Bodhi and Imogen watch as Violet enters the mat and successfully defends herself using a dagger and techniques taught by Xaden. Dain pins Violet and attempts to speak with her privately about the secrets surrounding Athebyne and Xaden's scars. Violet threatens to kill him if he touches her to steal memories, and she explicitly blames him for the deaths of Liam and Soleil. Dain reveals that General Sorrengail was the one who personally carved the scars into Xaden’s back before he taps out.

Rhiannon confronts Violet about the tension with Dain and correctly guesses that he has the signet ability to read memories. Violet confirms the secret but begs Rhiannon to stop asking questions to avoid putting her life in danger. Aaric, a first-year whose real name is Cam, breaks his wrist in a match and walks with Violet to the infirmary. He reveals that he is actually one of King Tauri's sons who went into hiding to join the riders. Aaric admits he knows the truth about the revolution and the daggers because his father’s guards carry weapons with similar alloy discs.



### Chapter 22
Violet experiences a nightmare where a venin, a dark wielder who draws power from the earth, captures her in a desiccated field. The venin is a gaunt figure with red-rimmed eyes and distended veins who calls himself her Sage. He mocks Violet for her failed tactics and claims he has waited centuries for someone with her specific power. The venin offers her the choice to join him or die before draining her energy and causing her to wake in a panic. Violet realizes the dream felt unusually vivid and maintains her grip on her black-hilted dagger for comfort.

On Saturday, Xaden leads Violet to the Basgiath forge located in the tunnels beneath the quadrant. Violet shares her research about the First Six carving the first wardstone and suggests a similar stone exists in Aretia. Their progress is interrupted in the stairwell by Major Varrish and Professor Grady, who are accompanied by captured riders. Varrish identifies that Xaden has warded Violet's door to prevent her from being taken for mandatory interrogation training. Rhiannon, Ridoc, and Sawyer appear with their hands bound, signaling that their entire squad has been selected for the exercise.

Xaden attempts to protect Violet by citing her shoulder injury and the fact that he is not in Varrish's chain of command. Varrish threatens to cancel Violet’s leave and separates her from Xaden by ordering her into the training facility. Violet communicates with Xaden's dragon, Sgaeyl, to convince Xaden to stand down and avoid revealing the contraband weapons he is carrying. Professor Grady binds Violet’s hands together in front of her body to accommodate her sling before leading her away. Xaden tells Violet that she is unbreakable as she is taken to the secret interrogation caves under the cliffs.



### Chapter 23
The squad is taken to a windowless cave facility where they are stripped of their weapons and placed in a classroom setting. Professor Grady explains that the interrogation exercise is an assessment designed to teach survival and identify individual psychological weaknesses. The cadets are given secret phrases and instructed to share a group secret that they must protect during the upcoming scenario. Violet reveals to her squadmates that she is in love with Xaden, but they reject it for being common knowledge. She provides a new secret instead, stating that Navarrian infantry is killing Poromish civilians at the border.

Violet identifies that the apple juice provided to the squad contains a serum designed to disconnect riders from their dragons and signets. The squad dumps the liquid into a toilet to fake the disconnect while maintaining their magical abilities in secret. Professor Grady returns to begin the graded scenario and shackles the squad members to the stone walls and a central chair. Two unmemorable interrogators enter and begin using physical violence to extract the secret phrases and the shared squad secret. The female interrogator repeatedly strikes Violet’s face, causing her eye to swell shut and her mouth to bleed.

The interrogators transition to striking the other squad members to force Violet into talking, but the group remains silent. Major Varrish enters the chamber and expresses dissatisfaction with the slow progress of the standard interrogation protocol. He mocks the squad's "Iron Squad" patch and claims he has a specialized method to break them quickly. Varrish summons Wingleader Dain Aetos into the room to assist with the interrogation despite the cadets' injuries. Rhiannon reacts with visible fear as the squad realizes their wingleader is being brought in to use his signet against them.



### Chapter 24
Dain enters the interrogation chamber and expresses shock at the severity of Violet's injuries. Xaden communicates with Violet through their mental bond and offers to stage a rescue, but she orders him to stay away. Varrish pressures Dain to use his memory-reading signet to find the truth about the events at Athebyne. He suggests that Violet might be a spy and taunts Dain with the possibility of seeing why she chose Xaden over him. Dain ultimately refuses to participate in the interrogation of an injured cadet and walks out of the chamber.

Varrish becomes enraged by Dain's refusal and punches Violet’s dislocated shoulder, causing her to lose consciousness from the pain. Violet wakes up mended in a bunk with Colonel Nolon, who reveals he was ordered to heal her so Varrish can break her again. Nolon leaves the door unlocked and the window hatch open while tricking Varrish into leaving the antechamber for another errand. The squad uses this opportunity to escape their cell by having Sawyer melt the hinges of the door with his signet. Violet uses a specialized dagger given to her by Xaden to magically unlock the final exit door.

The squad retrieves their weapons from the desk in the antechamber and prepares to fight their way out of the cave. Violet realizes the dagger she used to open the lock is made of a unique alloy that reacts to her touch. They emerge into the morning sun and encounter Professor Grady, who is waiting for them outside the cave entrance. Rhiannon draws her sword and demands the silver key patch, which is awarded to squads that successfully escape the interrogation assessment. The squad earns the patch, but Violet remains shaken by the knowledge that Varrish was willing to kill her.



### Chapter 25
Violet avoids Dain in the following days and learns that another second-year rider named Sorrel died during her interrogation assessment. The squad receives a circular silver patch with a black key to signify their successful escape from the caves. During Battle Brief, cadets find illegal leaflets reporting that the Poromish city of Zolya was destroyed by Navarrian dragon fire. The leaflet claims ten thousand lives were lost, including General Fenella, the commander of the Braevick gryphon fleet. Violet correctly identifies that the leaflets were printed locally within Basgiath to spread unapproved information.

Scribe Master Markham dismisses the leaflets as propaganda and mocks the cadets for believing that a riot of only blue dragons would be used. He collects all the parchment to prevent further discussion and transitions the briefing to a major announcement. Markham reveals that Colonel Nolon has successfully mended a rider who was previously thought to be dead. He introduces Jack Barlowe, the rider Violet thought she killed by collapsing a mountain on him during War Games. The briefing room erupts in cheers as Jack returns to his squad, appearing humble and reformed to the public.

Violet makes eye contact with Jack and realizes his glacial blue eyes still harbor the same homicidal intent. Tairn confirms the situation is dangerous, and Xaden’s dragon, Sgaeyl, is reported to be in a state of extreme anger. Violet’s squadmates realize that Jack will likely attempt to kill her again as soon as he has the opportunity. The chapter ends with a note from Major Frederick’s guide clarifying that menders are not bound by the same non-harm codes as healers. This revelation emphasizes that Nolon’s work on Jack may have created a dangerous weapon rather than a saved life.
