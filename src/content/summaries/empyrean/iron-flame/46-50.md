### Chapter 46
Violet practices rune carving in Xaden’s room while he prepares for a weapons run to the Stonewater River. Xaden explains that he placed a specialized unlocking rune on Violet’s daggers that responds to her physical need to open doors. He shows Violet a protection rune stone crafted by Colonel Mairi that was designed to counter dragonfire during executions. This rune stone is revealed to be the source of the rebellion relics that appeared on the children of the executed officers. Violet chooses not to ask Xaden about the deal he made with her mother despite her curiosity.

The next week, Professor Devera and flier professors announce that rider and flier cadets will no longer be separated for classes. The leadership implements a challenge system based on the Dragon Rider’s Codex to allow cadets to settle grievances through sparring. Fliers and riders are informed that each rider squad will absorb a drift of fliers to ensure unit cohesion for the war. Tensions remain high as the fliers continue to insult Violet regarding the death of Luella during the flight from Cordyn. Rhiannon and Trager engage in the first challenge match, which Rhiannon wins quickly.

Bragen, a flier leader, knocks Quinn unconscious during a match using a fast punch combination. Imogen and Neve spar to a draw after both sustain bleeding injuries from precise blows. Devera calls Violet and Catriona to the mat for the final challenge of the day. Violet hands her thirteen weapons to Rhiannon and prepares to fight Catriona without signets or blades. Catriona takes a fighting stance similar to Xaden’s and targets Violet’s defensive weaknesses. The match begins with Catriona making a verbal attack regarding her past relationship with Xaden.



### Chapter 47
Catriona uses her gift as a flier to amplify Violet’s jealousy and rage during their hand-to-hand match. She strikes Violet with a left hook that causes extreme pain to Violet’s cheekbone but does not break the skin. Violet counters with a blow to Catriona’s face that draws blood and marks her cheek. Catriona sweeps Violet’s legs out from under her and pins her to the mat with a forearm against her throat. She whispers that Xaden was once engaged to her for a crown and claims he is incapable of love.

Violet breaks the hold by hitting Catriona with her head and an elbow to the stomach. She pins Catriona and begins to choke her, fueled by a vortex of amplified anger and power. Xaden arrives and speaks to Violet, helping her regain enough control to release her grip on Catriona’s throat. Catriona refuses to tap out and continues to shout insults at Violet’s family while wheezing for air. Violet’s lack of emotional control causes a massive lightning strike that sets the wooden bleachers on fire. Xaden uses his shadows to extinguish the flames and carries Violet away from the gym.

Xaden takes Violet to the Assembly chamber and orders Brennan and the other leaders to leave the room. He examines Violet’s injuries and confirms he heard every word Catriona said during the fight. Violet admits she felt completely unhinged and was prepared to kill Catriona for the mental intrusion. Xaden explains that Catriona was using mindwork to manipulate Violet’s emotions throughout the challenge. He places Violet in a large chair on the dais to discuss the events in private. The chapter ends as Xaden prepares to address Violet's emotional state and the accusations made by Catriona.



### Chapter 48
Xaden forces Violet to sit in a massive blackened wood throne inside the Assembly chamber. He removes her boots and socks before engaging in a sexual encounter to prove his devotion. Violet experiences intense pleasure as Xaden uses his mouth and fingers while she holds her power-filled conduit. Xaden connects to Violet’s mind during the act, sharing his own desire and love through their bond. Violet reaches a peak of ecstasy that feels like shattering into glittering shards of light and power. Xaden refrains from seeking his own release to demonstrate that Violet is not a "convenient placeholder."

Xaden explains that he was never in love with Catriona despite their previous betrothal. He clarifies that the betrothal was a political arrangement for an alliance that failed when the luminary was withheld. Xaden informs Violet that he ended the betrothal because they were incompatible and he was already in love with her. He reveals that the dagger he gave Violet is runed to protect her from Catriona’s emotional manipulation. Violet learns that Catriona can only amplify existing emotions rather than planting new ones. Xaden urges Violet to find her center and regain control over her power and emotions.

Violet asks if Xaden is the king of Tyrrendor, and he explains he is the Duke of Aretia by birth. He admits that he would have told her about the betrothal if she had asked him directly. Xaden promises that Catriona will never sit in the throne or wear a Tyrrish crown. He tells Violet that nothing she could ever do would be unforgivable to him. They walk out of the hall together as Violet prepares for her history class. Xaden asks Violet to come to bed early that night before they part ways in the busy hallway.



### Chapter 49
The next morning, Rhiannon distributes green Iron Squad patches to the six fliers joining their unit. Violet approaches Catriona and offers her the patch while explaining the significance of rider ranks and markings. Catriona reluctantly takes the patch but vows to make Violet’s life miserable and claims the crown is still hers. Violet retorts that the crowns of Tyrrendor were melted down six hundred years ago for the unification. She makes a suggestive comment to Catriona about the sexual techniques Xaden learned from her. Imogen laughs as the riders walk away, leaving a stunned Catriona behind.

Violet goes to the library with Rhiannon, Ridoc, and Sawyer to study and find her center. Sawyer greets Jesinia using sign language while Violet unpacks books sent to her by King Tecarus. Violet asks Dain Aetos for help translating Warrick’s journal because of his expertise in languages. Dain agrees to help and they spend time in the cold wardstone chamber working on the text. They discuss their past friendship and the way the Riders Quadrant has hardened them both. Dain apologizes for his role in the deaths of Liam and Soleil at Resson.

Dain begins imbuing the Aretia wardstone with power, a process he estimates will take weeks or a month. Violet sets a boundary that they will not discuss the tragedy of Resson again for a long time. She informs Dain that she is truly in love with Xaden and that her feelings will never change. Dain accepts this and promises to trust Xaden as much as Violet does. They agree to meet daily for thirty minutes to continue the translation and imbuing work. The chapter concludes with Violet heading back to her other responsibilities after the session with Dain.



### Chapter 50
Professor Felix instructs Violet on how to precisely control her lightning strikes during a snowy practice session. He directs her to strike a conduit orb held inches from her fingers rather than a distant target. Violet successfully produces delicate filaments of blue energy that flow into the conduit without causing destruction. Felix explains that she must learn to use her power like a dagger instead of a battle-ax. He challenges her to maintain the energy flow from a distance of fifteen feet by their next lesson. Tairn expresses pride in Violet's developing control despite her initial fears of her own power.

Violet returns to Riorson House and finds Garrick, Bodhi, and Heaton on the second-floor landing. Heaton is severely injured with a shattered arm and a massive contusion on their face. They report that the town of Pavis has been overrun by seven venin and a hoard of wyvern. Senior wingleader Nyra Voldaren and her dragon Malla were killed and drained of their life force during the battle. The venin skipped over hundreds of miles of territory to target Pavis as a strategic staging point. All first and second lieutenants are recalled to Aretia to regroup for the worsening conflict.

Leadership plans to restructure the wings because the front lines of the war have become fluid. Mira Sorrengail is reported to be safe but is currently assisting the healers with other wounded riders. Xaden is home early and is in a state of exhaustion and grim frustration following the mission. Felix requests that both Xaden and Violet meet him in the Assembly chamber at five o’clock. Violet realizes that the venin are now actively targeting their locations and closing in on Draithus. The chapter ends with the realization that the tipping point of the war has arrived sooner than expected.
