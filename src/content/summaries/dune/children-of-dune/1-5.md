### 1: covering an iron core of simpler, more deterministic awareness. And that
Stilgar stands in Sietch Tabr and observes that the Fremen have become careless with water discipline as Arrakis turns green. He anticipates the arrival of the Lady Jessica, the mother of Paul Muad’Dib, who is returning from the planet Caladan today. Stilgar worries that the Bene Gesserit, an order of women with advanced mental training, will sense his doubts about the current regime. He contemplates the royal twins Leto II and Ghanima, who were born with the full memories of their ancestors due to spice saturation in the womb. The Naib fears these children are Abominations, a term used by the Bene Gesserit for those possessed by the egos of their predecessors.

He fingers his crysknife, a blade made from the tooth of a Shai-Hulud sandworm, while debating whether to kill the children for the safety of the tribe. Stilgar reflects on how the religion of Muad’Dib has turned the government into a bureaucratic monster where law is treated as sin. He realizes that the twins are not merely children but ancient beings in young flesh who sit on the Royal Council. The Lady Jessica’s return is rumored to be a reconciliation with the Sisterhood, who want the twins’ genetic material. Stilgar considers how easy it would be to end the Atreides line with a single stroke of his blade.

He decides that the twins are a lens revealing new shapes in the universe rather than the cause of chaos. The Naib recalls the old Fremen religion and the importance of not hastening God’s command. He feels the tingling in his fingers as he resists the urge to draw his weapon against the royal heirs. Stilgar ultimately chooses loyalty over the complexity of a future he cannot understand. He leaves the twins’ bedchambers and prepares to meet the day as an attendant to the Atreides family.





### 2: quickly. ‘Will she believe we fear her?’ Leto asked.
Leto II and Ghanima prepare for the arrival of their grandmother by donning stillsuits, specialized survival gear that recycles body moisture. Harah, one of Stilgar’s wives, warns the twins not to fear the Lady Jessica despite her status as a Reverend Mother. The twins discuss how their Aunt Alia, the Regent of the Empire, fears that Jessica will recognize her as an Abomination. They utilize their genetic memories to speak in ancient languages, hiding their adult-level intellect from the sietch attendants. Leto quotes the Bene Gesserit Azhar Book, which describes the pre-born as being vulnerable to possession by damned personas.

He suggests that he and his sister remain safe from possession because their parents’ personas stand guard within their minds. The children mention a heretical figure known as the Preacher who wanders the desert and causes Alia significant distress. Alia arrives wearing golden water rings and warns the twins not to provoke Jessica with their advanced behavior. Leto observes subtle physical markers on Alia’s face that reveal the genetic influence of the villainous Baron Vladimir Harkonnen. Alia insists that the twins stay at Sietch Tabr while she travels to the spaceport to receive her mother.

Ghanima sheds a tear for Alia, recognizing that her aunt has already succumbed to the internal assault of her ancestors. The twins decide to avoid the spice trance, a state of deep vision, to prevent their own personalities from being overwhelmed. They acknowledge that Alia’s addiction to melange has made her a victim of the very memories she carries. Leto and Ghanima realize they must eventually discuss the threat of Abomination with their grandmother. The children return to the sietch crowd, determined to maintain their facade while monitoring the changes to their planet.





### 3: the chord of living flesh. Gurney Halleck would not make that separation
Alia travels by ornithopter, a bird-like flying craft, toward the Arrakeen Basin to meet the landing transport. She considers how to use Gurney Halleck, a loyal Atreides warrior and musician, to manipulate the royal twins. The Regent plans to lure Leto into a spice trance so he can use prescience to secure her failing power. She reflects on the Bene Gesserit’s attempt to breed a Kwisatz Haderach, a male with total awareness, and how they now fear her. On the planet Salusa Secundus, a Levenbrech aide to a Bashar prepares a pair of Laza tigers for a hunt.

These tigers are genetically modified predators with servo-stimulators implanted in their brains to allow remote control. The man uses a transmitter to release the tigers’ restraints as two children dressed in Atreides white bourkas appear. The children are red-haired and wear the hawk crest of House Atreides, serving as stand-ins for Leto and Ghanima. A hidden transeye records the event, transmitting the visual data to Prince Farad’n, the grandson of the late Emperor Shaddam IV. The Laza tigers lope toward the children, who stop and stare at the predators instead of fleeing.

The predators kill the children instantly by breaking their necks before they begin to feed on the corpses. This exercise confirms that the tigers’ conditioning is sufficient for an assassination attempt on the real Atreides heirs. The Levenbrech expects a promotion to the rank of Bator for his successful management of the predatory cats. He anticipates the restoration of House Corrino to the throne once the Atreides twins are removed. Transport arrives to collect the soldier while the tigers finish their meal in the barren landscape.

The Lady Jessica looks out from her transport at a crowd of half a million pilgrims and Fremen gathered on the Arrakeen landing plain. She observes the massive plasteel fortress known as the Keep, which serves as the seat of Alia’s religious government. As she scans the delegation, Jessica immediately identifies Alia as an Abomination who has fallen into the forbidden way of ancestral possession. Jessica steps onto the ramp alone and raises her arms in a religious benediction to keep the massive crowd subservient. Gurney Halleck and his men dash into the throng to seize or kill potential rebels who were identified by their delay in kneeling.

The crowd believes the Reverend Mother has returned to weed out slackers and blesses her presence. Jessica meets Stilgar at the foot of the ramp and orders him to close the port so the captives can be interrogated. Alia confronts her mother with anger, demanding to know why she planned the military action without consultation. The Lady Jessica openly acknowledges her daughter’s status as an enemy, and Alia feels a terrible delight that their conflict is public. Princess Irulan, the widow of Paul Muad’Dib, stands with the Atreides family but Jessica suspects she has returned to the Sisterhood.

Two priests named Javid and Zebataleph approach to lead Jessica to a Lustration ceremony at the Keep. Jessica finds Zebataleph to be a ruthless but simple man, while Javid possesses a surly and dangerous demeanor. She notices that Javid has a strange accent, suggesting he originates from an isolated pocket of the Fremen people. The Lady Jessica uses the Voice, a vocal technique that compels obedience, to dismiss the priests’ requests as “priestly nonsense.” She ultimately agrees to attend the ceremony to gain political leverage and keep Javid close for further observation.

Gurney Halleck uses hand signals to tell Jessica that many captured rebels are actually Alia’s own agents. He reports that several prisoners died from conditioned heart-stoppers, devices that kill the host upon speaking forbidden words, after mentioning Jacurutu. Jessica orders Gurney to use his connections with spice smugglers to verify if the legendary sietch of Jacurutu truly exists. At Sietch Tabr, Leto II plays a baliset and reflects on his inherited memories of the planet Caladan. He tells Ghanima that sandtrout were introduced to Arrakis to encyst water, creating the desert needed for sandworms to thrive.

The boy warns that the current ecological transformation will destroy the sandtrout and lead to the total extinction of the sandworms. Leto reveals he is having prescient dreams of himself in armor and of the forbidden location known as Jacurutu. He identifies the need to find the Preacher, a blind heretic who may be his father, Paul Atreides. Ghanima is disturbed by Leto’s visions, as prescience is associated with the downfall of their family and the threat of Abomination. In the city of Arrakeen, a blind man known as the Preacher arrives accompanied by a young guide named Assan Tariq.

The Preacher wears an old-style stillsuit with a black vine braid nose tube, marking him as a figure from Dune’s past. He visits the steps of Alia’s Temple, where pilgrims from the Hajj gather to seek religious salvation. The blind man listens to mummers performing a disputation about the nature of sentience and the universe. He observes the decadence of the city-dwelling Fremen and the commercialization of his son’s religion through the sale of relics. Sand Dancers at the foot of the temple steps collapse in ecstasy while shouting prophecies of the city’s eventual destruction.
