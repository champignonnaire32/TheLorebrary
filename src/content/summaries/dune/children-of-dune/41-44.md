### 41: ‘Who are you?’ Halleck insisted.
Gurney Halleck confronts the Preacher and Leto in a sietch passage and wonders if he is being tested by Jessica. The Preacher reveals he is Paul Atreides and explains that he has renounced his status as a moral symbol. Gurney realizes the blind man's identity when he hears the familiar voice and sees Paul's aged face. Leto uses superhuman strength to shove a mob of Fremen aside and crumbles a stone wall with his bare hands. He warns Gurney that Farad'n Corrino will soon arrive on Arrakis for a final test before departing the sietch.

Buer Agarves, a military advisor with a round face and agitated eyebrows, meets with Stilgar and Irulan in an abandoned djedida. He offers a full pardon from Alia, but Rajia, a darkly ascetic young woman with a distrustful expression, questions the motive. Agarves admits his own disgust with Alia's influence and claims that her presence fouls everything she touches. As Stilgar begins to consider the offer, a group of kidnappers sneaks into the meeting hall and seizes Ghanima. They press a drug-soaked rag over her face and carry her unconscious body out through a hidden door.

Leto stands in the desert and observes the sandtrout membrane, a living suit of skin-like organisms that has penetrated his internal organs. He feels the living suit adjusting his internal functions and recognizes that he is no longer completely human. He meets Paul outside a palmyrie and dismisses the child guide to speak with his father privately. Leto informs Paul that they must return to Arrakeen together to meet Farad'n and Ghanima. Paul agrees to accompany his son as a guide, knowing they are approaching a shattering historical crisis.





### 42: – Leto Atreides II
Alia watches pilgrim women dancing in the Temple plaza while she waits to greet Farad'n and supervise his meeting with Ghanima. She believes her plan to have Ghanima kill Farad'n is proceeding correctly and that Stilgar has already killed Buer Agarves. The Preacher arrives at the plaza with a massive mob following him, causing Alia to feel a sense of fear. Zia, an amazon aide, informs Alia that Farad'n and the Sardaukar officer Tyekanik have arrived at the aerie. Alia orders Ghanima to be dressed as a Fremen bride and equipped with a ceremonial crysknife for the encounter.

Farad'n and Jessica enter Alia's chambers and move to the window to watch the Preacher in the plaza below. The Preacher delivers a sermon condemning the people for forsaking their desert ways and shattering the qanats. He claims that the waters spread upon the desert have become blood and warns of the last wind of Kralizec. The crowd reacts with angry mutterings as the Preacher points a quivering finger at the Temple steps. Five Temple Priests in yellow robes descend into the mob to seize the blind man as Alia watches in fascination.

The Preacher condemns Alia by name as a blasphemy, causing a shocked silence to grip the entire plaza. The Temple Priests storm down the steps and one of them stabs the Preacher in the chest with a crysknife. Paul Atreides falls dead on the steps, and the Temple doors are slammed shut to keep the mob out. Alia screams that the Priests have killed Muad'Dib as Jessica realizes her son is dead. Jessica watches the murder from the window and sees her son's body reduced to a heap of bloody rags.





### 43: ‘Well enough,’ Leto said.
Leto and Ghanima break into Alia’s chambers after Leto shatters a reinforced plasteel door, a thick construction meant to withstand energy attacks, with his bare hands. Leto uses his immense strength to skid the half-ton door across the room, pinning two guards against the wall. He informs Farad'n that Alia intended for Ghanima to assassinate him during their first meeting. Alia orders her remaining guards to seize the children, but the men refuse to move out of fear. Leto advances on Alia, who crouches in a corner and draws her jeweled knife to defend herself.

He leaps toward the ceiling and strikes Alia's head with a kick that sends her sprawling across the stone floor. Leto grabs Alia by one foot and swings her body around his head at high speed before dropping her gently to the ground. Alia begins to rave in multiple jumbled voices as her internal ancestors take control of her consciousness. The voice of the Old Baron Harkonnen, a sensual and autocratic ancestor, erupts from her mouth to curse the children. Leto uses three swift blows to shatter the unbreakable crystal-reinforced glass, a supposedly indestructible material, from the window frame.

Alia briefly regains her own voice and pleads with Jessica to help her stop the internal voices. Jessica refuses to intervene, forcing Alia to face the trial of possession alone without the Sisterhood's assistance. Leto offers Alia the choice between a formal trial or jumping from the open window to her death. Alia struggles against the Baron’s commands but eventually drags herself over the windowsill in a jerking motion. She falls to her death on the Temple steps below, striking them with a sodden thump as the crowd shouts.





### 44: ‘It amused you and flattered your real talent, which is that of historian.
Leto sits on the Lion Throne to receive homage from the Fremen tribes who have witnessed his superhuman invulnerability. He has already demonstrated his power by walking through fire and toppling a Guild frigate with his bare hands. Ghanima informs Farad'n that Leto's sandtrout skin is a permanent part of his body that will last four millennia. Leto explains that he will now manage the Bene Gesserit breeding program and mold human evolution himself. Stilgar approaches the throne and presents the headband Ghanima wore during her time in the desert.

Leto returns a strip of Ghanima's robe to Stilgar as a symbolic gift and commands him to keep it as a reminder of human error. He orders Stilgar to read the inscription on a jar containing the water of Muad'Dib, which is stored in a gold vessel. Leto announces that the sandworms will be extinct within two hundred years as the desert environment is destroyed. He dismisses Stilgar and sends Gurney Halleck to Sietch Tabr to act as a military advisor for the new regime. The Fremen Naibs depart in a state of submissive terror after witnessing Leto's monarchic authority.

Leto informs Farad'n that he will marry Ghanima himself to maintain the royal line despite his lack of human organs. Farad'n must serve as the secret father of the Atreides dynasty to ensure the bloodline continues through the generations. Leto identifies himself as a community dominated by Harum, an ancient ruler who once led a three-thousand-year dynasty. Farad'n receives the name Harq al-Ada, meaning "Breaking of the Habit," and agrees to surrender his Sardaukar legions. Leto and Ghanima stand back-to-back to signify their intent to watch in opposite directions while guarding their shared bloodline.
