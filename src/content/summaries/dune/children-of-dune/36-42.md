### 36-37: restore her memory. Time for that later … if they survived this terrible
Alia sits on her bed reciting the Litany Against Fear while the Old Baron's voice mocks her internally. Reports indicate that rebels are attacking qanats and that Leto survived a leap from Shuloch, which Alia struggles to believe. Farad’n sends a contingent of his household guard to Arrakeen to assist Alia and prepare for their official betrothal. Alia uses these new troops to close down the Shuloch basin and arrest dissidents among the Fremen Naibs. The Old Baron pressures Alia to invite Buer Agarves, a young Temple guard aide whose water-fatness reflects a new breed of Fremen, to her chambers.

Leto rides a worm to a rendezvous point in the Tanzerouft desert where he encounters The Preacher and his young guide, Assan Tariq. The worm stops at Leto's command, revealing his developing control over the environment and his physical transformation. Leto identifies himself as Leto Atreides and speaks in Chakobsa to confirm his identity to his father, Paul Atreides. Paul admits he is no longer the father Leto knew but is instead a relic known as The Preacher. Assan Tariq, a youth of the Cast Out who serves as a guide for the blind, attempts to use a pseudo-shield technology that maddens sandworms to kill both Atreides men.

Leto uses his amplified physical speed to intercept and kill Assan Tariq by snapping the youth's neck. He retrieves the hidden pseudo-shield and hurls it far into the desert to divert the approaching worm. Leto informs Paul that he has accepted the Golden Path and the physical metamorphosis into a human-sandworm hybrid. Paul expresses despair at the inhuman consequences of Leto's choice but agrees to be guided by his son. The two men prepare to travel together to meet Gurney Halleck as Leto begins creating a new legend for Arrakis.

### 38: Fremen were the first humans to develop a conscious/unconscious
Stilgar and Duncan Idaho spend an entire night discussing the political instability of the Regency within a rock-walled room at Sietch Tabr. Idaho presents evidence that Alia is possessed and has been collaborating with the Jacurutu to export worms off-planet. Stilgar maintains his neutrality and refuses to allow Idaho to send any messages to the Lady Jessica or the Corrino court. He insists that Sietch Tabr must remain a sanctuary for Ghanima and Princess Irulan despite the evidence of Alia's corruption. Idaho realizes that he must force Stilgar into a decisive action to save Ghanima from the Regency's influence.

Javid enters the room to deliver messages from Alia, and Idaho immediately draws his knife and stabs the unsuspecting aide to death. Idaho then directs three lethal insults at Stilgar, calling him a servant who wears a collar and a thief of Fremen water. He further attacks Stilgar's lineage by claiming that none of the Naib's descendants carry his actual blood. Stilgar reacts with uncontrollable rage at these violations of his hospitality and honor. He draws his own crysknife and pursues Idaho into the outer passage of the sietch.

Stilgar kills Duncan Idaho by driving his knife through the man's diaphragm and into his heart. Idaho dies with a smile, having successfully provoked Stilgar into a criminal act that forces him to become an outlaw. Stilgar realizes that Alia will be compelled to seek revenge for the death of her consort despite Idaho's provocations. He immediately orders his household to pack their belongings and summons worms for a mass flight into the open desert. Harah joins Stilgar as he takes Ghanima into custody to protect her from the reach of the Regency.

### 39-40: After the Fremen, all Planetologists see life as expressions of energy
Gurney Halleck waits at the entrance of Tuek's Sietch while the smugglers decide whether to grant him sanctuary. He observes thousands of sandtrout, which are small primitive forms of the giant sandworms, being harvested for their water after a qanat breach. Repair teams work to fix the shattered stone walls of the qanat while the sietch's water supplies dwindle. Halleck realizes that the smugglers have become a defensive and colonial population under the pressure of Alia's regime. He is eventually granted entry by a sietch attendant named Melides, though he secretly plans to steal an ornithopter and escape.

In Arrakeen, Alia receives news from Buer Agarves that Duncan Idaho and Javid are both dead at Sietch Tabr. Agarves reports that Stilgar claimed responsibility for the killings and has fled into the desert with his followers. Alia realizes that Idaho deliberately provoked Stilgar to force him into rebellion and to protect Ghanima. She appoints Agarves as the new Naib of the Tabr people on the condition that he brings her Stilgar's head. Alia hides her lack of grief from her attendants and retreats to her private chambers to vent her rage.

Alia finds an old silver-and-platinum belt buckle belonging to Duncan Idaho on the floor of her bedroom and clutches it convulsively. The internal struggle for control of her body intensifies as the persona of the Old Baron mocks her emotional reaction. She experiences a physical breakdown, collapsing onto her bed while her internal voices demand to know who is crying. The Regency's stability is further threatened as Stilgar, Ghanima, and Irulan are now confirmed rebels in the open desert. Alia resolves to use Farad'n's household guards to hunt down the fugitives and consolidate her power over the Fremen tribes.

### 41-42: creator. He refused to reenact the evolutionary sequence, saying, ‘That,
Ghanima and Stilgar's band of sixty fugitives take shelter in an abandoned djedida, a small desert town, in the Tanzerouft region. The town is falling into ruin because its qanat has been repeatedly destroyed by a mysterious entity known as the Desert Demon. Ghanima observes that the ecological transformation of Arrakis is reversing as Fremen abandon new settlements and return to their ancient cave-warrens. Stilgar refuses to discuss the global crisis and instead focuses on his new role as a carefree outlaw with a high price on his head. Harah informs Ghanima that Stilgar has arranged a secret parley with Buer Agarves at the djedida.

Leto brings Gurney Halleck and The Preacher to the basin of Shuloch using an ornithopter he stole from the smugglers. He uses his superhuman strength to destroy the Shuloch qanat with his bare hands, demonstrating his near-invulnerability to the Cast Out. The Cast Out, who are Fremen outcasts and spice-smugglers, prostrate themselves before Leto, believing him to be the physical incarnation of Shai-Hulud. Leto commands them to rebuild the qanat, plant an oasis garden, and stockpile all the spice they can collect. He informs Gurney that he is now the head of House Atreides and that his transformation is irreversible.

Gurney Halleck confronts Leto and The Preacher, attempting to determine if the boy has been possessed by an internal persona. The Preacher, who is Paul Atreides, explains that Leto has achieved an inner cooperation between his ancestral lives that prevents Abomination. He describes Leto as "The Healer" who has survived a lethal environment to lead humanity onto the Golden Path. Leto explains that he has accepted a thousands-year-long life as a hybrid creature to ensure the survival of the human species. Gurney finally accepts Leto's authority and agrees to assist him in creating the new legend that will reshape the universe.
