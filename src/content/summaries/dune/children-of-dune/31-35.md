### 31: – The Dune Catastrophe
Ghanima stands on the roof garden of the Keep at sunset, worrying about her susceptibility to inner possession. She recalls her grandmother Jessica’s warning about Abomination, a term for the pre-born whose inner lives split into benign and malignant psyches. Jessica admits she fled in terror of Alia’s possession but now regrets abandoning her daughter too soon. Ghanima explores her inner memories and encounters an apparition of her mother, Chani, who warns her to bar the inner door. Desperate for order, Ghanima recites the Sisterhood’s Credo to stabilize her mind and suppress the inner clamor.

Irulan approaches Ghanima on the parapet and scolds her for being out in the open without a stillsuit. She delivers a message that Farad’n accepts the betrothal but wishes to delay the ceremony. Irulan also mentions that Duncan Idaho is being sent home while Jessica chooses to remain on Salusa Secundus. Ghanima dismisses Irulan’s attempts to dissuade her from the marriage and expresses her intent to kill Farad’n. She reminds Irulan that the Atreides family has a bloody history and that she possesses the memories of her ancestors.

Ghanima reveals that Alia is sending Irulan south to a new town to prevent her from interfering with the murder. Irulan pleads with Ghanima to reconsider, expressing her love for Paul and her care for his daughter. Ghanima maintains a cold Fremen perspective, asserting that she is distinct from Irulan due to her heritage. She mentions her water debt to Irulan but insists that Farad’n’s life is forfeit. The conversation ends with Ghanima reflecting on her status as a pre-born and her constant struggle against Abomination.

Ghanima tells Irulan that she contains the memories and frailties of every Bene Gesserit in the breeding program. Irulan embraces the child as night falls over the desert of Arrakis. Leto II wakes up in a cell at Jacurutu, a legendary sietch once thought to be destroyed by the tribes. He senses Sabiha, a young woman who is feeding him food laced with spice essence to keep him in a trance. He realizes Sabiha is a tool used by his captors to force him into a specific marriage alliance.

Sabiha feeds Leto more spice essence, which keeps him caught between the real world and visionary dimensions. Leto observes the tribal tattoos on Sabiha’s temples and recognizes that the outcasts of Jacurutu still survive. He has a vivid vision of a future with Sabiha involving physical intimacy and domestic life in the desert. After Sabiha leaves, Leto uses the last of his strength to hypnotize her and induce a deep sleep. He seals his stillsuit and escapes the cell, moving toward a side passage to find an exit.

Gurney Halleck and Namri discover that Leto has escaped and interrogate a confused Sabiha. Namri reveals that he disabled the heel pumps on Leto’s stillsuit, meaning the boy will die of dehydration. A massive sandstorm is approaching Jacurutu, making a search party impossible and cutting off all communications. The storm is expected to be so severe that it will sliver bones to fragments if it catches Leto. Halleck realizes that bats will not fly in the storm to deliver messages, leaving Leto alone in the wasteland.





### 33: ‘Not if he took to Fremkit, and hid in the sand,’ Sabiha said.
Alia greets Duncan Idaho at the Keep after he arrives unannounced from Salusa Secundus. Duncan reports that Jessica is training Farad’n in Bene Gesserit ways, which infuriates Alia. He observes Alia with his mentat eyes and concludes that she is fully possessed by a demon psyche. Alia informs Duncan that she has sent Ghanima to Sietch Tabr under Stilgar's protection for safety. She complains that raiders have breached the qanats at Kagga Basin, wasting precious water into the desert.

Alia orders Duncan to go to Sietch Tabr to watch over Ghanima and Irulan during the priesthood negotiations. She secretly plans to have Duncan eliminated in a fatal accident during his journey. Duncan senses the deception and realizes that Alia’s pet amazon, Zia, is meant to facilitate his death. He steals an ornithopter and flies toward the Shield Wall, leaving the Keep’s guards behind. Alone in the craft, he weeps for the loss of his wife, acknowledging that the real Alia is dead.

Leto II crouches at the edge of Jacurutu and prepares to travel south into the Land of Terror. He spots a massive storm rising from the Great Flat and realizes he is directly in its path. Despite the danger, he decides to use the storm to cut the threads of his predictable futures. He plants a thumper to call a worm and successfully mounts the creature as it emerges from the sand. Leto drives the worm directly into the heart of the approaching storm to vanish from his enemies.

Leto II enters a dormancy trance inside a stilltent, a collapsible shelter that preserves moisture, to survive the storm. He awakens in the stale air, uses a compaction tool to tunnel out, and emerges into the moonlight. He finds his stillsuit’s heel pumps have been sabotaged and performs a field repair to stop the water loss. After summoning a new worm, he travels southward toward a butte known as Shuloch. He encounters a spice-scout and a harvester, marking the presence of renegade Fremen in the deep desert.

A man named Muriz, the judge of a spice-hunting band, lands an ornithopter to confront Leto. Leto identifies himself as the child of Paul Muad’Dib and correctly names the secret location of Shuloch. Muriz draws a maula pistol, but Leto uses a feint to disarm him and threatens him with a crysknife. Leto reveals he knows the renegades are the "Cast Out" who escaped the original destruction of Jacurutu. To secure a truce, Leto forces Muriz to bite his thumb and drink his blood, establishing a water bond.

Jessica continues her training of Farad’n on Salusa Secundus, teaching him the Bene Gesserit view of molecular memory. She recites a graduation prayer that effectively converts Farad’n from a Corrino into a member of the Sisterhood. Tyekanik enters the room but realizes he is too late to stop the psychological transformation. On Arrakis, Leto reaches Shuloch and discovers a qanat filled with predator fish and open water. He meets Sabiha again, who has been sent to Shuloch as punishment for letting him escape earlier.





### 35: ‘The spice will only give you visions,’ Sabiha said. The long silences
Sabiha feeds Leto a spice-rich gruel in a crude hut at Shuloch while Muriz watches for signs of a vision. Leto realizes Sabiha and her people are trapped by their reliance on absolutes and their fear of the unknown. He leaves the hut and walks to the qanat, where he encounters a swarm of migrating sandtrout. Leto places the sandtrout, diamond-shaped creatures that encapsulate water, on his skin to form a symbiotic membrane. This membrane acts as a self-repairing stillsuit and grants him superhuman strength and speed.

Leto tests his new abilities by running and leaping across the desert, covering fifteen kilometers in a short time. He confronts a giant sandworm in the canyon, which refuses to attack him because it perceives him as a sandtrout. He returns to the hut and shows Sabiha that he can control the worm through his new transformation. Muriz and his men attempt to capture Leto, but he escapes by jumping off a two-hundred-meter cliff. He bounces off narrow ledges using his amplified muscles and vanishes into the moon-shadowed dunes.

Namri informs Gurney Halleck that Leto is in a safe place, but Halleck grows suspicious of the Fremen’s motives. Halleck provokes Namri into a fight and kills him with a concealed knife after seeing armor beneath Namri's robe. He learns that Jessica is a prisoner on Salusa Secundus and that Alia is plotting her own schemes. Leto burrows into the sand to hide from pursuit and plans to raid qanats to disrupt the ecological transformation. He commits himself to the Golden Path, a long-term strategy for human survival, knowing he is no longer fully human.
