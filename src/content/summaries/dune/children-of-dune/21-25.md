### 21: Jessica rocked forward and arose with that fluid grace which never quite
Lady Jessica and Duncan Idaho escape from Sietch Tabr in an ornithopter to avoid Alia’s tightening control. Jessica realizes that Idaho is no longer under the Regent's command but is following the mysterious orders of the Preacher. Idaho uses mechanical restraints to trap Jessica and announces they are flying to Salusa Secundus to meet Prince Farad’n Corrino. On the planet Salusa Secundus, Wensicia Corrino reveals to her son Farad’n that she has orchestrated a plot to restore House Corrino. The Prince resents the lack of consultation regarding the plan but eventually acknowledges his commitment to the imperial goal.

Leto II and Ghanima are ambushed by two genetically conditioned Laza tigers in the rocks near Sietch Tabr. These tigers are massive predators controlled by a remote transmitter used by a hidden assassin from House Corrino. Ghanima is clawed in the leg and arm but manages to kill one cat with her poisoned crysknife. Leto kills the second tiger by luring it into a narrow cleft where he can reach its paw with his blade. The twins realize they must separate to fulfill their plan for the Golden Path and protect their futures.

Leto gives his Fremkit to Ghanima and prepares to ride a sandworm to the forbidden sietch of Jacurutu. Ghanima uses a hypnotic process to convince herself that Leto was killed by the tigers to fool imperial Truthsayers. She watches a worm consume the tiger carcasses and believes her brother has been lost to the desert. Leto mounts the sandworm and disappears into the night as the twins’ shared childhood officially ends. Ghanima begins her solitary journey back to Sietch Tabr to report the tragedy to Stilgar and Alia.





### 22: a human with a transmitter. She worked her way up the rocks and across to
Ghanima moves through the moonshadows of the dunes to avoid detection by the tiger-handler who remains at large. She observes a search party from Sietch Tabr but maintains a broken walking rhythm to avoid attracting worms. The princess reaches the water qanat and prepares to cross into the sietch through a covert and hidden passage. On Salusa Secundus, Prince Farad’n and Bashar Tyekanik discuss the unconfirmed reports of the tiger attack on Arrakis. They speculate on the motives of Duncan Idaho and the potential value of the Lady Jessica as a captive.

Tyekanik warns that the Bene Gesserit may have planted a psychological trigger within Idaho’s regrown ghola mind. Farad’n argues that understanding the Atreides requires a deep analysis of Arrakeen culture and the stillsuit’s specific function. The Prince believes that environmental stress is the primary tool needed to defeat the current Atreides regime. He recognizes that the marriage of Atreides and Fremen created a unique power that House Corrino must eventually match. Farad’n decides to accept Idaho’s offer to house Jessica despite the inherent danger of her presence.

Ghanima observes the covert exit of the sietch and finds the seals have been left carelessly open. She sees a fan of light broadcasting into the night, signaling a major breach of sietch security protocols. A man and a woman stand in the passage, holding hands and gesturing furtively within the bright glow. Ghanima screens out the sounds of the night to focus on the suspicious behavior of the pair. She realizes these watchers are waiting to see if any victims escaped the genetically conditioned tiger attack.





### 23: Who was the woman, then? No matter. She was someone being used by
Ghanima identifies the man in the passage as Palimbasha, a schoolteacher and the grandson of a Fremen Naib. She notices a Tleilaxu transmitter attached to his belt, confirming he released the Laza tigers into the desert. The princess recognizes the woman as Muriz, a weak follower who is being used as a tool by the conspirator. Palimbasha frequently presses a key on the device, expressing anger at the lack of a return signal. Ghanima realizes that every sietch entrance likely has similar watchers waiting for her and her brother.

She feels a surge of rage at the man who she believes killed Leto during the ambush. Ghanima recalls a memory of an ancient priestess of Jowf to determine the best method for her attack. She prepares a poisoned needle using spice-fiber and a tube from her specialized sand-snorkel air device. The princess moves slowly through the alfalfa field to get within range of the broadcasting light. She observes piume flies swarming around the glowglobes to ensure her poisoned dart goes unnoticed by the pair.

Ghanima expels her breath and sends the poisoned needle into Palimbasha’s cheek with a single surge. The man swats at his face, believing he was bitten by an insect, before his legs collapse beneath him. Muriz attempts to support his dead weight as Ghanima emerges from the shadows with her blade drawn. The princess presses a crysknife to the woman’s waist and demands total and immediate obedience. She informs Muriz that Palimbasha is dead and that she is now a prisoner of the Atreides.





### 24: formal investiture. That binds him to me now.’
Prince Farad’n receives the Lady Jessica and Duncan Idaho in the old State Audience Chamber of House Corrino. The captives are bound in suspensor chairs with shigawire that will cut them at the slightest physical movement. An armed mute stands guard with orders to kill the prisoners if Farad’n is influenced by Bene Gesserit wiles. Farad’n reveals that Leto II is dead and that Ghanima is being held as a hostage by Alia. Reports from Arrakis indicate an abortive civil war has broken out between desert tribes and military Fremen.

Wensicia enters the chamber and provides a message cube containing a formal protest from the Landsraad Council. The Bene Gesserit Council on Wallach IX accuses House Corrino of assassinating the Atreides heir on Arrakis. Jessica reads the message and realizes the Sisterhood is playing a complex game with the Corrino heir. Farad’n becomes enraged at his mother for making deadly decisions in his name without his prior consent. He threatens to renounce his position as heir unless he is granted absolute and final decision-making power.

Wensicia yields to her son’s demand and agrees to his formal investiture as head of House Corrino. Jessica observes the internal break in the royal family and sees an opportunity to manipulate the young Prince. The Reverend Mother proposes that she become Farad’n’s teacher to prove her choice to stay was free. Farad’n recognizes that his mother orchestrated the tiger attack and banishes her from the Corrino court. The Prince agrees to Jessica’s terms, seeking to gain the same mental training that once produced Muad’Dib.

Duncan Idaho uses the shigawire bindings to deliberately sever an artery in his wrist during the royal audience. He claims he is tired of the game and wishes to remove himself from the Atreides' service. Farad’n provides medical aid and realizes that Idaho’s loyalty has been overexploited by the Regent Alia. Jessica realizes the Sisterhood intended to control both Houses by uniting Farad’n Corrino and Ghanima Atreides. Farad’n accepts Jessica as his teacher and prepares to adopt the Atreides battle language for his own use.

Leto II rides a sandworm across the desert toward the forbidden and tabu sietch of Jacurutu. He reflects on Stilgar’s lessons about the water-hunting Iduali tribe who were once wiped out for their crimes. The boy hides in the sand during the day, using a stilltent and sand-snorkel to survive the heat. Leto acknowledges that he must follow the Golden Path to save humanity from his father’s limited vision. He understands that his survival depends on hiding among the smugglers and the dead of another age.

The Preacher appears on the steps of Alia’s Temple in Arrakeen during the Holy Day of Ayil. He defies the Priesthood by preaching that Muad’Dib’s religion has become a moral stable for the weak. Alia watches the blind man from the crowd, wondering if he is truly her brother Paul Atreides. The Preacher challenges the faithful to lift their heads and reclaim their humanity from the entrenched bureaucracy. He bellows that Muad’Dib is dead and questions if men will commit moral suicide in his wake.
