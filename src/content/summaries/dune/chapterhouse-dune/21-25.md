### Chapter 21
Duncan Idaho sits at his console in the no-ship and encounters a stored entry that triggers a vivid vision of the past. He sees an elderly couple standing in a lush garden behind a shimmering, jeweled net of green, blue, gold, and silver lines. The couple appears ordinary yet possesses a godlike stability that makes Idaho feel exposed under their intense gaze. This vision has moved beyond the Great Hold and now appears to him even when he is alone at his console. Idaho notes that the woman wears a white apron with garden tools and the man holds a small metal ball that emits a piercing whistle.

Idaho recognizes a strange familiarity in the couple's faces and suspects they might be Face Dancers due to their pug noses and upcurved smiles. The vision vanishes and is replaced by Murbella, who finds Idaho sweating and distressed from the experience. He explains his suspicion that the Tleilaxu planted something in his original cells that is now manifesting as these visions. Murbella expresses concern over these wild talents, fearing the Bene Gesserit's reaction if they discover his instability. Idaho realizes his awareness is different from any previous ghola life and that his current consciousness is the one making choices.

Murbella's expression becomes veiled and remote as she begins to adopt the persona of a Reverend Mother. Idaho asks her what will happen if the Bene Gesserit fail, but she leaves without providing a direct answer. He speaks aloud to the ship's recording devices, addressing Odrade and claiming he has begun to understand the Honored Matres through Murbella. He throws down a gauntlet by stating he is now looking for ways to overcome the Bene Gesserit themselves. Idaho concludes that the couple in his vision are independent entities who belong to no one, not even the Tleilaxu Masters.



### Chapter 22
Darwi Odrade experiences the painful morning restoration following her first dose of melange, which she views as a necessary but grueling addiction. She watches the morning rain from her window and notes the flinty aftersmell, which signals an inescapable and resentful change in the planet's climate. Her assistant Streggi enters to update the desert map, which shows the drylands widening significantly toward Sheeana's station. Odrade lectures Streggi on the dangers of energy greed in governments and the trap of becoming uncaring pushers of narcotics. She explains that while the spice extends life, it remains a deadly addiction that requires constant, painful management.

Odrade informs Streggi that they will be traveling south to visit Sheeana and instructs her to find Tamalane to make arrangements. She observes that Sheeana and Idaho have been using a secret Atreides hand-language to communicate, which Bellonda wants to investigate. In her workroom, Odrade finds a massive stack of archival records left by Bellonda and becomes enraged at the lack of prioritization. She summons Bellonda and berates her for acting like a bureaucrat and failing to delegate or keynote the significant information. Odrade orders Bellonda to sit in the Mother Superior's chair and handle the daily deluge of data while she is away.

Before leaving, Odrade wanders the corridors to inspect the performance of the acolytes and the cleanliness of the kitchens. She encounters an acolyte reading Tolstoy and provides a specific quote about being a net rather than a river. In the kitchen, she engages in a ritualistic "dance" with the senior chef, Placido Salat, to order a special oyster stew. Tamalane arrives to announce that they are ready to depart for Eldio via the transport tubes, though Odrade prefers surface travel. As they board, Odrade reflects on the high risks of off-planet communication and the continued silence from the Rabbi and Dortujla.



### Chapter 23
The Rabbi wails in despair as he sits cross-legged on the cold floor of a cramped no-chamber. Rebecca stands before him and chides his lack of hope, reminding him that they are hiding from the "unspeakable." The no-chamber is a smuggled, barrel-shaped device only twelve and a half meters long, hidden beneath an ash pit and a chimney. It smells of burned things and the stench of the small recycling system that serves as their toilet. The Rabbi mourns that they cannot even leave the door open for the Stranger during Passover in such a hole.

Joshua, the builder of the chamber, monitors his instruments and informs the group that the searchers are looting the nearby farm and leaving. He is a slender man with a triangular face who looks too young to possess such technical knowledge of no-technology. The Rabbi insists that the searchers will return and that their current safety is merely a temporary reprieve. Rebecca realizes that her Bene Gesserit awareness gives her a unique perspective on the Jews' susceptibility to intellectualism and myth. She decides her primary role is to keep the small group from falling into the destructive trap of boredom.

The Rabbi begins to organize food rations while others sleep on cots in the darkened end of the narrow chamber. Rebecca contemplates the dangers of prescience and the "sweetness of the new" that is lost when the future is known. She reflects on Muad'Dib's travail and how an oracle's future becomes identical to a boring, unchangeable past. Joshua warns that an approaching storm will likely turn the ash pit into a cement-like seal over their entrance. Rebecca remains undeterred, stating they have tools or their own hands to dig themselves out when the time comes.



### Chapter 24
Duncan Idaho views his life in the no-ship as a complex game and realizes that the young Teg's entry is a deceptive move. He practices evading the ship's camouflaged spy devices with the help of Sheeana, who is polishing her Honored Matre techniques. The no-ship is a massive, energy-intensive laboratory where the Sisterhood seeks to nullify the ship's ability to hide from human senses. Idaho suspects the Bene Gesserit are banking on people, food, and ancient loyalties to maintain their dwindling empire. He uses his console to hide personal data and poignant memories in plain sight through encrypted second messages.

Idaho feels the weight of his serial ghola lives and the debt he owes the Atreides family, which spans multiple lifetimes. He reflects on his intense sexual attraction to Murbella and the energy it takes to suppress the magnetism pulling them together. During an intimate moment, Murbella analyzes their relationship and describes it as a game where one of the pieces cannot be moved. She tells Idaho that the Sisters want their children and their willing participation in the Bene Gesserit dream. Idaho recalls Odrade showing him a holorecord of Van Gogh's "Cottages at Cordeville" to explain her hold on humanity.

Murbella expresses fear about her upcoming Spice Agony and the fact that she is already "in the chute" toward becoming a Reverend Mother. She admits that she loves Idaho but knows the Sisterhood forbids such attachments as a flaw. Idaho realizes that Murbella is changing into a remote and calculating "witch" as her Bene Gesserit training takes hold. He contemplates the "net" of his visions and concludes that he is a network linked outside of Time. Terrified that the Sisters will label him a Kwisatz Haderach and kill him, he resolves to work for his own survival.



### Chapter 25
Odrade travels south toward the desert in a ground vehicle and observes that the countryside has become disturbingly drier in just three months. She has received news that the Honored Matres have obliterated four more planets, leaving the Bene Gesserit with only twelve remaining worlds. The party passes through hillsides of cork oaks where truffles are failing to grow due to the lack of rain. Streggi sits beside Odrade in the lead car, while Tamalane follows in a thirty-meter bus filled with an unnecessarily large escort. Odrade notes that the orchards near the community of Pondrille are leafing too early because of the expanding desert's heat.

At Pondrille, Odrade is met by the leader Tsimpay and the Orchard Mistress Fali, who reports a serious complaint from the gardeners. The gardeners are bitter about the lack of rain and the impending loss of their fruit crops to the encroaching desert. Odrade explains that the planet casts the final vote and that weather can no longer be adjusted to save every plantation. She tells Fali to inform the gardeners they can either grumble or elect to go into the Scattering to escape the Honored Matres. The group continues south toward the sea at Eldio, noting the windmills that provide the community with local power.

The sea has receded into a "dreadfully condensed remnant" of its former self, with fishing villages now stranded far from the shoreline. Odrade decides to swim nude in the warm waters of a cove, accompanied by Streggi and two other acolytes. As she swims, she decides that the sea must be eliminated quickly through a dehydration scheme to conserve energy and manage the planet's total weather. She instructs Tamalane to have Weather plot a swift plan to finish the sea's removal, prioritizing survival and procreation over nostalgia. Odrade grows calm as she leaves the water, accepting that no joy is permanent and that the species' survival is the ultimate goal.
