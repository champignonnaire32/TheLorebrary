### Chapter 26
Dama oversees the new Honored Matre control center on Junction and observes the botanical gardens. She receives a report from Logno indicating that the Reverend Mother on Buzzell was refused sanctuary by her superiors. Dama orders Logno to bring the Buzzell witch and her attendants to Junction for interrogation. She also commands that several Futars be starved to ensure they are hungry for an unspecified purpose. Dama reflects on her recent execution of two Ixian scientists who demanded more information about a secret weapon. She views the religion of Guldur as a useful tool for managing the masses through a tyranny of the minority.

Logno reports that the orders regarding the Buzzell witch and the starving Futars have been issued. Dama warns Logno about security failures after three Futars escaped their cages and wandered into the garden. Logno retreats when she notices the orange blaze of anger in Dama's eyes during their confrontation. Dama inquires about a woman named Rebecca from Gammu who has temporarily eluded capture. She considers Rebecca to be bait because a Bene Gesserit witch was found among her people. Dama concludes her morning by mentally listing the captive planets currently under Honored Matre control.

The Honored Matres continue their efforts to find and destroy the central Bene Gesserit school on Chapterhouse. Dama believes that managing reality through terror is necessary to prevent the chaos of uncontrolled cultures. She suspects that the witch Lucilla possessed dangerous knowledge of how to manipulate the masses. Logno's subservient tone provides Dama with a sense of enjoyment and temporary security in her leadership. The Honored Matres intend to hold their newly acquired empire with more caution than their previous one. Dama remains alert to the furtive sounds of her aides moving behind her in the assembly room.





### Chapter 27
Bellonda travels to the no-ship to confront Duncan Idaho while Mother Superior Odrade is away from Central. She reviews Idaho’s archival records and notes his history of impulsiveness and his past service as a Mentat and Truthsayer. Bellonda finds Idaho at his console and is surprised when he reveals he expected her arrival. Young Teg enters the room and stands beside Idaho as a silent protector during the meeting. Idaho accuses Bellonda of wanting him dead and claims that Sheeana uses him as a Missionaria tool. Bellonda enters a Mentat state but finds it difficult to maintain focus under Idaho’s mocking gaze.

Idaho challenges Bellonda’s motives and suggests that the Bene Gesserit are seeding the universe with enemies. He points out that no Scattered Reverend Mother has ever returned or sent a message back to Chapterhouse. Idaho claims the Sisterhood needs his inventiveness and imagination to survive the Honored Matre threat. He mentions his past life on Wallach IX where he suppressed a Mentat school at the Tyrant's command. Bellonda feels a mental intrusion from her ancestor Anteac as Idaho speaks about the history of the Mentats. Idaho suggests that the Honored Matres might be a small group using the Old Empire as a final retreat.

Bellonda stands to leave after Idaho questions why Sheeana is immune to sexual bonding while other adepts are not. Idaho stops her to ask why Futars are reported to hunt Honored Matres when Murbella knows nothing of it. After Bellonda departs, Teg asks if she really intended to kill Idaho, and Idaho confirms the danger. Murbella returns from a practice session and reacts with rage when she hears about Bellonda’s visit. Idaho helps Murbella undress in the shower and they engage in a sexual encounter that is monitored by the watchdogs. Bellonda returns to Archives and expresses jealousy toward Sheeana while reflecting on the magnetic power of sexual ecstasy.





### Chapter 28
Tamalane informs Odrade that drifting sand has made the glazeway road impassable in six places due to large dunes. Odrade orders transport to provide ornithopters for her party, but they are delayed until noon by community relocations. The group travels south toward the desert fringe and observes abandoned foundations of uprooted communities. Odrade climbs a sandhill near an observation terminal to view the encroaching desert of her own creation. A communications acolyte delivers a weather report requesting more ground equipment to phase out the sea. Odrade approves the request and reflects on the persistence of Atreides family traditions on Caladan.

Bellonda sends an urgent message reporting that strangers took all Reverend Mothers away from the Buzzell punishment station. The messenger, a First-Stage acolyte named Fintil, describes the leader as a woman with Honored Matre features. Odrade orders that Fintil be held for interrogation and potential spice agony to test her resourcefulness. Tamalane warns Odrade not to place Sheeana on the Council without discovering what she and Idaho are hiding. Odrade arrives at the Desert Watch Center and meets Sheeana in her spartan tenth-floor quarters. She examines a black-on-white drawing of a sandworm that Sheeana created using Other Memory.

Sheeana reports that her search teams found a small spice mass thirty kilometers northeast of the watch center. Odrade tells Sheeana that she has been approved to succeed Tamalane on the Bene Gesserit Council. Sheeana is shocked by the appointment and admits she has been polishing a young man named Trebo for Duncan. Odrade observes Trebo sunning himself on a neighboring roof and feels a sense of amusement at the distraction. After Odrade leaves, Sheeana reflects on her success in deflecting questions about her secret activities. She has discovered a way to reactivate the no-ship prison and defuse the mines placed by Bellonda.





### Chapter 29
Murbella experiences a vivid nightmare involving predatory words and linguistic heritage after a session with Bellonda. She awakens in the night and laughs uncontrollably at the absurdity of the phrase "sexual collision." Duncan Idaho awakens and explains that the Sisters use Zensunni techniques to remove trauma connections to words. Murbella expresses fear because Honored Matre teachers warned against falling into Zensunni hands. She reflects on her probation status and the fact that her teachers discuss her in private. Duncan tells her that the Sisterhood uses mind and body to reinforce each other through rigorous discipline.

Duncan discusses the military genius of Miles Teg and explains why the Sisterhood values his tactical inventions. He compares a woman plowing a field to basic reality and claims the Bene Gesserit never miss such truths. Murbella mentions rumors from Gammu about Teg moving faster than the eye can see during his escape. Duncan dismisses the stories as acolyte gossip intended to make people cautious of the Bashar. Murbella reveals that Odrade wants her to "work on" the Tleilaxu prisoner Scytale to gather information. She expresses revulsion at the idea and reaffirms her exclusive love for Duncan.

Duncan reminds Murbella that she is part of a plan to restore Teg’s memories using imprinting techniques. Murbella realizes that Sheeana has been trained as an imprinter to avoid the physical pain of memory restoration. She recounts her own history of being kidnapped by Honored Matres during a planetary sweep as a child. Murbella describes how her parents disappeared and her home was destroyed by explosions during the raid. She experiences a moment of freedom from her past and recognizes that the Sisters are always listening. Duncan caresses her back and reflects on his own agony of loving her too much.





### Chapter 30
Odrade spends two months securing approval for Sheeana to succeed Tamalane on the Bene Gesserit Council. Bellonda reports that Murbella is eight days away from giving birth to her fourth child. Odrade walks with the Tleilaxu Master Scytale through the vineyards south of Central to discuss a bargain. Scytale complains about his confinement and whines about the destruction of his people by the Honored Matres. Odrade uses the secret Tleilaxu language, Islamiyat, to claim she shares his Great Belief. They enter Odrade's workroom where Tamalane waits in the shadows to observe the proceedings.

Odrade questions Scytale about his flight from Junction and his attempt to secure a herd of Futars. Scytale admits that the Tleilaxu were betrayed by returnees from the Scattering who provided bait Futars. Bellonda’s projection appears above the table to announce that a spice blow has occurred on Chapterhouse. Sheeana enters the room towing a transparent pod containing a miniature live sandworm. Scytale gasps in awe as Sheeana lifts the tiny creature and cradles it in her arms. Odrade identifies the worm as a revenant of the Prophet and claims Chapterhouse is becoming another Dune.

Scytale views the presence of the worm as a sign that his bargaining power has dissolved. Odrade warns him that the Old Empire's Great Convention is ended and that hatred is building for a Jihad. She offers Scytale a partnership based on shared necessity rather than shared belief. Scytale asks for his own Face Dancer attendants and his own axlotl tanks to preserve Tleilaxu secrets. Odrade promises that the Council will consider his requests and orders the guards to return him to the no-ship. Bellonda enters the room and suggests killing Scytale, but Odrade orders her to review the meeting holo.
