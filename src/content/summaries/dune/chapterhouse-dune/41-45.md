### Chapter 41: Chapter 41
Odrade and her party arrive at Junction and wait in a small lobby while being watched by Ixians and Honored Matres. Odrade amuses herself by interfering with the lobby robos, which are functional Ixian servos that jitter and hum when blocked. She ignores the syrupy-voiced autoreceptionist, a black plaz tube, and eventually demands to be sent to her quarters. Their luggage is inspected and brought to rooms that are luxurious but repellent, featuring yellow floors and black plaz beds. Odrade accepts the discomfort of a cushionless bed to unsettle her watchers before being summoned to meet the Great Honored Matre.

Odrade meets her escort, a green-robed Honored Matre with a face like a stone wall, who leads her to an action center filled with military consoles. She finally encounters the Spider Queen, Dama, a small woman with red leotards and orange-flecked eyes, accompanied by her aide Logno. Dama wears an intense floral perfume meant to act as an identity marker for her native flamebush. The two women walk into a garden where Odrade reveals her knowledge that the Honored Matres were driven back to the Old Empire by a mysterious enemy. Dama is visibly scowled and disturbed by this revelation but leads Odrade into her private workroom to continue negotiations.

In the workroom, Dama receives word that Odrade’s pilot destroyed his ship to prevent inspection, which Odrade claims was a deliberate suicide to protect their coordinates. Dama shows Odrade the "Ballet Master," a dancing figure made of feathery planes suspended by shigawire, which she took from a destroyed planet. Logno brings wine spiked with a substance intended to nullify Odrade's shere protection against probes, but Odrade's metabolism renders it harmless. Infuriated by the failure, Dama hurls her glass to the floor and stares at her mechanical figure while Logno watches her intently. Logno suddenly poisons Dama, who collapses in a heap, and declares herself the new Great Honored Matre who will use the Bene Gesserit as a weapon.





### Chapter 42: Chapter 42
Murbella watches the battle for Junction from a no-ship’s command center as Teg directs an assault on the Citadel. The millions of lives from Lampadas are being Shared and preserved within the ship by Rebecca and other Sisters using Extremis Progressiva. Teg leads his troops through the botanical gardens surrounding the Citadel, avoiding inviting avenues in favor of harder paths. He sits astride the shoulders of his aide, Streggi, after his Command Pod is destroyed by a laser hit during the engagement. Haker, his personal aide, reports that the last pockets of defenders are being mopped up but warns Teg to stay out of danger.

Teg orders his reserve pod to be brought in, but learns it has also been destroyed, forcing him to rely on primitive comlinks. He instructs his team to broadcast images of destroyed Honored Matre ships to all bands to educate the enemy on their losses. Odrade is taken to the Citadel's tower by Logno, who uses a countersignal shield to silence Odrade’s vital-sign transmissions. Logno concentrates on a small operations console and a T-probe hood while Honored Matre soldiers enter the room in shock over their defeat. Logno abruptly informs Odrade that the three companions who accompanied her are dead and that the Honored Matres are now prisoners.

Teg arrives in the tower and finds Odrade confronting the captive Honored Matres, who glower at the sight of a male commander. He notices that the number of casualties in the botanical gardens is suspiciously small and that the defenders surrendered with unexplained abruptness. As Suks move among the bodies, Teg realizes that the fallen figures show almost no blood and that there are no wounded survivors. He experiences a deep sense of dread and orders his aides to gather urgent casualty figures and reports on captured weapons. Teg tells Odrade that the Honored Matres have used a secret weapon that the Sisterhood has not yet discovered.





### Chapter 43: Chapter 43
Sheeana stands at Desert Watch on Chapterhouse, sensing the presence of tiny sandworms in the sand waves through their scent of cinnamon and brimstone. She has spent the day dancing for the worms to prove she can still call them, though they remain small due to the planet's dampness. Walli, the senior acolyte in charge of the station, brings Sheeana a sandwich while they discuss the Missionaria Protectiva’s plan to use Sheeana as a religious weapon. Sheeana fears that a religion built around her could become an uncontrollable flame but considers it a potential path for escape. She and Duncan Idaho have secretly planned to flee Chapterhouse because they view it as a death trap for the Sisterhood.

Sheeana studies a topomap with green threads and flags, preferring the physical tactile sensation over holographic projections. She asks Walli to help her gather a few worms to bring back to the no-ship’s hold at the Landing Flat for study. Walli notes that the air is becoming drier as the desert expands, a necessary change for the sandworms to grow into giants. Sheeana believes that the sandworms may carry the "endless dream" of the Tyrant, Leto II, and wonders if she should follow his path of metamorphosis. She recalls her childhood on Dune where her family was killed by a giant worm that spared her and led her to the Bene Gesserit.

As the sun sets, Sheeana walks into the desert using the random "sandwalk" stride of her Fremen ancestors to avoid attracting worms. She sees arcs of fire in the northern sky, signaling the return of the ships from the battle at Junction. Sheeana senses the desert changing and hears the slithering of kangaroo mice and insects that have been introduced to support the new ecosystem. She reflects on Odrade’s childhood words about Sheeana being something new lifting over the horizon that the Sisterhood had long awaited. Sheeana decides that she must lead her own migration and find a new planet with moons where her worms can thrive.





### Chapter 44: Chapter 44
Murbella rushes to Junction in a one-man lighter after seeing images of Teg and Odrade sprawled on the floor of the Citadel tower. She confronts a squad of armed Honored Matres led by an old woman named Elpek and claims she is a Hormu adept who escaped a no-ship. Murbella uses a Hormu-style kick to kill an ambitious challenger in the squad to demonstrate her combined Bene Gesserit and Honored Matre combat skills. Elpek is impressed by the display of lethality and agrees to escort Murbella to meet the Great Honored Matre. Murbella identifies herself as the personal student of the Mother Superior to gain entry into the inner circle of the powerful Senior Dames.

Inside the tower, Murbella finds Odrade standing unfettered while Teg lies swathed in shigawire in a corner next to dead comeye specialists. Logno, the new Great Honored Matre, stands over the bloodless bodies produced by a secret weapon that has now been exhausted. Murbella insults Logno’s leadership and style, calling her a consummate liar and a bag of insolence in front of her Council. Logno attacks with a blindingly fast kick, but Murbella catches her foot and levers her into the floor, pulping her head. In two heartbeats, Murbella kills two more aides, ending the short reign of Logno and leaving the survivors immobilized by fear.

Murbella discovers that Elpek has killed Odrade, who apparently tried to interfere during the combat in the tower. Murbella kills Elpek with a kick to the larynx before kneeling to Share with Odrade’s corpse and absorb her memories. She orders the release of Teg and demands a red robe, formally assuming the dual titles of Great Honored Matre and Mother Superior. Murbella promotes a cruel-eyed woman named Angelika to her Council after dumping her hard to prevent an assassination attempt. Teg, rubbing his arms where the shigawire bit deep, agrees to return Odrade’s body to a cemetery orchard on Chapterhouse.





### Chapter 45: Chapter 45
Murbella returns to Chapterhouse with an entourage of Honored Matres and orders the Rabbi and his party to remain hidden in the no-ship. She brings Futars and Handlers from the Scattering to investigate the biological weapons that Honored Matres fear. Duncan Idaho refuses to leave the no-ship, claiming he must assemble a mental mosaic and test the limits of his new stability. Murbella assumes Mother Superior’s chair and summons Bellonda, who assesses the debt Odrade has imposed on the Sisterhood. Bellonda compares the current crisis to the advent of the Tyrant and agrees to usher in the new Honored Matre High Council.

Murbella addresses the Honored Matre Council, which includes ambitious women like Angelika who are eager for rewards and power. She explains that the Honored Matres must learn from the Bene Gesserit witches to avoid extinction by the mysterious ones of many faces. When Council members babble in protest, Murbella shouts them down and threatens to bloody the floor with their bodies. She declares herself both Great Honored Matre and Mother Superior, forcing a qualified acceptance of the union from the surviving leaders. Murbella views this hybridization as a way to grow stronger, even as she feels trapped by the inevitable progression of her new roles.

Murbella discusses the state of the no-ship with Bellonda, who reports that Scytale is being kept under wraps as a bargaining chip. Sheeana remains at the ship studying the control of worms in the Great Hold, while the Rabbi’s party grows worried about the Honored Matre presence. Murbella instructs Bellonda to treat the Honored Matres with a pretense of love and admiration to overcome their bureaucratic origins and greed. She feels the heavy burden of the Atreides payment for survival and struggles with her love for Duncan Idaho, which she fears will interfere with her duties. Finally, Murbella realizes that Duncan has made a decision to flee the planet in the no-ship, a mosaic piece that cannot be moved.
