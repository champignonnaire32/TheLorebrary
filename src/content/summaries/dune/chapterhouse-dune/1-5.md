### 1: Chapter 1
Mother Superior Darwi Odrade celebrates the birth of a baby ghola in her private dining room at Central. The child is grown from fingernail scrapings of the deceased Bashar Miles Teg using the first functional Bene Gesserit axlotl tank. Council members Tamalane and Bellonda attend the breakfast summons but show impatience due to the ongoing threat from Honored Matres. Odrade notes that the hunters have already exterminated sixteen Bene Gesserit planets as they seek to destroy the Sisterhood. She hopes to restore Teg's original memories to gain a military genius capable of moving faster than the human eye.

The Mother Superior reflects on the fragility of the Old Empire's factions following the Scattering and the Divided God's death. Bellonda expresses deep fear and resentment, focusing on the efficiency of the Archives under her own guidance. Odrade views the orchards and pastures of Chapterhouse from her window while contemplating her own strengths as a Reverend Mother. She considers using Other Memory to find solutions but warns herself against the danger of losing her identity to past lives. The Bene Gesserit view humans as life designed by evolution to create order against the disorder of their hunters.

Odrade officially writes off the Bene Gesserit Keep on Palma by marking an affirmative "Yes" on Bellonda's morning report. This decision condemns eleven hundred Reverend Mothers and countless acolytes to death because evacuation is impossible before the imminent attack. Tamalane remains largely silent, offering only sparse commands to buy no-ships or brief the young witch Sheeana. Odrade recognizes that the Honored Matres are hysterical xenophobes who sexually enslave men to maintain their power. She vows not to wait in defensive enclaves and prepares to remove the "weeds" threatening her Sisterhood's survival.





### 2: Chapter 2
The Great Honored Matre sits on a high dais within a massive audience room on the planet Junction. She watches a captured Reverend Mother named Sabanda who lies bound in restrictive shigawire on the yellow-tile floor. Shigawire is a high-tensile metallic cord capable of slicing through flesh if the captive attempts to struggle. The Great Honored Matre appears as a scrawny, withered woman with orange flecks in her eyes that signal her anger. She mocks the prisoner and mentions that Odrade has nicknamed her the Spider Queen.

Sabanda remains calm and refuses to be intimidated despite the threat of torture or mental probing. The prisoner is infused with shere, a drug that causes a body to deteriorate quickly to prevent posthumous interrogation. Great Honored Matre asks if the Sisterhood teaches the youth to worship the young witch Sheeana. Sabanda replies that Sheeana would not want worship and asserts that one can know the dead as well as the living. The leader of the Honored Matres is frustrated by the fearlessness and self-control displayed by the primitive Bene Gesserit.

She orders an aide to administer a new drug injection to the prisoner to see if it will break her silence. Sabanda dies within seconds of the injection, choosing death over the possibility of betrayal or enslavement. Servants remove the corpse to feed it to captive Futars kept by the Honored Matres. Futars are humanoid-animal hybrids from the Scattering that refuse to breed or obey commands in captivity. The Great Honored Matre vows to find the witches' hiding place and root out the persistent cult of Sheeana.





### 3: Chapter 3
Young Miles Teg plays in the orchards around Central under the guidance of Mother Superior Odrade. He is aware of his status as a ghola but struggles to understand the full meaning of his previous life. In his seventh year, Teg begins to experience disturbing flashes of memories that he cannot fully recall. He studies the history of the original Bashar Miles Teg and the military brilliance the man once possessed. Odrade explains that Teg's skin cells were used to recreate him after his death in the destruction of Rakis.

The boy notices that the desert is expanding southward across Chapterhouse as part of a terraforming plan. This environmental change is triggered by sandtrout, the precursors to sandworms that produce the vital spice melange. Teg walks through a dell of aromatic herbs and correctly identifies rosemary and sage without prior instruction. Odrade is pleased by this sign and suggests that he is accessing original memories from his previous incarnation. She teaches him about symbiosis by explaining how fungi and roots depend on each other for survival.

The Mother Superior warns that most of the orchards will eventually die to make room for the new desert. Teg realizes that the Sisterhood needs the sandworms to maintain their existence and control over the spice. He boasts that he will be Odrade's Bashar and protect the Bene Gesserit from the Honored Matre threat. Odrade reminds him that the original Teg was famous for winning victories without engaging in physical battle. She instructs his Proctors to shift his training toward the subtle uses of power held within the mind.





### 4: Chapter 4
Logno enters the pitch-black sleeping chamber of the Great Honored Matre, who is also known as Dama. The darkness serves to unsettle visitors and protect the leader from potential assassination attempts in her bed. Logno hears animalistic grunts and moans coming from the shadows where Dama may be bedding a Futar. The Great Honored Matre has salvaged fragments of "The Weapon" from the disaster occurring in the Scattering. "The Weapon" is a small black tube that projects a beam of bloodless death across a limited range.

This technology is currently incomplete because it requires a "Charge" held by the Council to be functional. Logno reports that Ixian technicians do not understand how the device works and cannot yet duplicate it. Dama expresses deep hatred for the Ones of Many Faces who caused the destruction of her former empire. These enemies utilized Futars and awesome powers to confiscate the majority of the Honored Matres' advanced weaponry. The Great Honored Matre orders a test of the defenses on the planet Buzzell, which is rich in soostones.

Soostones are valuable gems harvested from the waters of Buzzell and are highly prized throughout the universe. Smugglers report that the planet is undefended, but Dama suspects the Bene Gesserit have set a trap. She orders Logno to bond more smugglers to verify the intelligence before launching a full-scale assault. The Honored Matres believe that the witches are weak but potentially wily enough to deceive them. Dama threatens the Ixians with her displeasure if they fail to replicate the destructive power of "The Weapon."





### 5: Chapter 5
Darwi Odrade sits in her workroom and experiences recurring "water images" from her childhood on the planet Gammu. She envisions herself as Sea Child, a persona that represents her ability to maintain balance on strange seas. This internal strength was instilled by her foster mother, Sibia, who taught her to love herself before the Sisterhood intervened. Odrade possesses a minor prescient talent that warns her of immediate perils threatening the Bene Gesserit. She dreams of walking a tightrope over a chasm while an axe-wielder approaches from behind to cut the rope.

The Mother Superior worries about the security of the no-ship hidden at the Chapterhouse spacefield. A no-ship is a massive vessel capable of remaining hidden from prescient searchers and Guild Navigators. Inside the ship are the Tleilaxu Master Scytale and the sexually bonded pair Duncan Idaho and Murbella. Bellonda enters the workroom with ridulian crystal records and reports the total destruction of the planet Lampadas. Lampadas was the site of the Sisterhood's premier school and the home of ten billion people.

The report confirms that the Bashar Alef Burzmali and all defense forces have been killed by Honored Matres. Odrade fears for the Reverend Mother Lucilla, who was supposed to escape with the school's collective Other Memory. The news of the massacre arrives during a cold snap as Weather Control begins plunging Chapterhouse into winter. Odrade suggests that the Sisterhood may need to employ viciousness of their own to blunt the enemy's assault. She decides that the young witch Sheeana is the best candidate to eventually replace the withdrawing Tamalane on the Council.
