### 1: Chapter 1
Three rebels, including Siona Atreides, run through the Forbidden Forest on Arrakis while pursued by D-wolves, which are giant grey predators bred for their keen eyesight. A runner named Ulot, described as short and round, falls behind and is killed by the wolves. The remaining runners, Kwuteg and Siona, wear black running suits of tightly knitted cotton to help them navigate the forest. Kwuteg, an agile man with long black hair, realizes he cannot outrun the pack due to a knee injury sustained at the God Emperor's Citadel fortress. He sacrifices himself by bit-attacking the wolves, allowing Siona to reach the Idaho River.

Siona carries a stolen kit containing Citadel plans and two cryptic volumes printed on ridulian crystal paper, a material only one molecule thick. She plunges into the river, knowing the D-wolves are territorially conditioned to stop at the water's edge. One wolf watches her from the bank but eventually lopes back into the forest with the pack. Siona emerges on a narrow beach, checks that her stolen packet is dry, and swears to destroy the tyrant Leto II. The text transitions to the journals of Leto II, where he describes his three-thousand-year reign as God Emperor.

Leto II explains his transformation into a "pre-worm" hybrid, seven metres long and two metres wide, with a ribbed body and human face. He possesses a massive hoard of spice melange, the geriatric substance required for interstellar travel and Bene Gesserit powers. He uses an Ixian "dictatel" device to record his thoughts directly onto crystal sheets via mental command. Leto reveals he watched Siona's escape through Ixian surveillance technology and allowed her to survive as a test of her potential. A fragment of dialogue between Siona and her father Moneo, Leto's chief aide, reveals Moneo's belief that Leto and the "worm of God" are separate entities.





### 2: Chapter 2
Leto II waits in a massive circular hub beneath his Citadel, resting on a Royal Cart manufactured by the Ixians. He anticipates the arrival of Duncan Idaho, a ghola created by the Tleilaxu using original cells from the ancient swordmaster. Leto performs Bene Gesserit hand exercises, noting that his fingers remain human under a grey sandtrout skin membrane. He reflects on his "Golden Path," a vision for human survival that requires his enforcement of a multi-galactic peace. Moneo signals that Idaho is approaching the crypt, and the Duncan arrives wearing an old Atreides uniform with a golden hawk insignia.

Idaho carries a briefcase containing a concealed Ixian lasgun, intended to assassinate the God Emperor for his perceived lack of morality. Leto reveals that Siona stole plans for the Citadel and copies of his secret journals during her recent raid. Idaho reports on a cult of Alia on Giedi Prime, which Leto correctly identifies as a Bene Gesserit cover for finding a hidden spice hoard. Leto uses his "Voice," a powerful vocal conditioning, to shout at Idaho and assert control over the conversation. He explains that radicals and rebels are necessary to master chaos, though he often co-opts or kills them.

Idaho draws the lasgun and aims for Leto's face, the only part of his body still covered in human skin. Leto hurtles off his Royal Cart with surprising speed, using his heavy worm body to strike Idaho. The lasgun fires and burns away part of one of Leto's atrophied leg-flippers before Idaho is crushed. Leto hears Idaho's bones crack and watches the life leave the ghola after Idaho gasps Siona's name. The God Emperor climbs back onto his cart, noting that his brain is actually distributed in nodes throughout his five-ton body.





### 3: Chapter 3
In a windowless room beneath the city of Onn, a muscular woman named Nayla writes a message to Leto II. Nayla uses a wall-mounted desk and keyboard to report that Siona intends to give stolen journals to the Bene Gesserit, Guild, and Ixians. She transmits the message through an Ixian-made device implanted in her skull by the God Emperor himself. Nayla prays to Leto and then conceals her workstation behind a rectangular grey metal wall panel. She leaves the room and joins Siona and a man named Topri in a larger chamber containing stolen Citadel artifacts.

Topri is a fat, nervous man with bulging green eyes who serves as an assistant to Siona’s rebel group. Siona reveals she has discovered a strand of Ghanima’s hair and a starflower blossom pressed between the pages of the stolen books. She reads aloud a poem Leto wrote about the death of his sister Ghanima, which he identifies as the "dark wet edge" of a tideflow. Siona concludes that Leto is a sentimentalist, a trait she views as a weakness to be exploited. Nayla observes Siona’s leadership and reflects on her own secret vow of obedience to both Siona and the God Emperor.

Siona questions Nayla about why she remains loyal to the rebellion despite the obvious dangers. Nayla answers that she has sworn to God to obey Siona, an answer that Siona interprets as a commitment to their "higher power." Siona asserts that their rebel group is the force that will eventually settle with the "tyrant Worm." Topri watches the exchange with suspicion, while Nayla realizes her double-loyalty serves Leto's hidden purposes. The rebels continue analyzing the ridulian crystal volumes in hopes of finding a cipher that will lead them to Leto's spice hoard.





### 4: Chapter 4
Moneo descends in an Ixian lift to the Citadel crypt to manage the aftermath of Duncan Idaho's death. He observes Idaho's corpse on the floor and the disabled Ixian lasgun nearby. Leto informs Moneo that the injury to his flipper is inconsequential and will heal within his sandtrout skin. Moneo offers to dispose of the body personally, acknowledging that only nineteen Duncans have ever died of natural causes in Leto's service. Leto orders Moneo to present the disabled lasgun to the Ixian ambassador and reward a Guildsman with ten grammes of spice.

Leto discusses Siona's potential, noting that she sometimes fades from his prescient view, a unique and valuable phenomenon. Moneo expresses concern for his daughter, but Leto reminds him that Siona is a product of a controlled breeding programme. Leto rails against the "pharaonic disease" of government, calling himself the last of a line of feudal chieftains and emperors. He describes his inward travels through ancestral memories, viewing uncounted civilizations as a series of repetitive posturings. Moneo disables the lasgun by breaking a connection and crushing a small capsule within the weapon's mechanism.

Leto explains that technology like the lasgun breeds anarchy by putting savage power into the hands of individuals. He claims that government is a shared myth and that he and Moneo are "myth-killers" working to break the cycle of human stagnation. Moneo mentions talk of a new jihad against thinking machines on planets like Parella and Dan. Leto dismisses the idea, noting that jihads only create new armies and distribute technology more widely. The God Emperor decides to retreat to his tower to mourn, while Moneo informs him that a new Duncan ghola is already arriving.





### 5: Chapter 5
A new Duncan Idaho ghola arrives on Arrakis via a Guild shuttle and is taken to a city called Onn. He finds himself in a cube-shaped room with yellow light, wearing coarse brown clothing and sandals provided by the Tleilaxu. The Tleilaxu Face Dancers, who can shift their features into plastic flows of flesh, leave him alone to wait. Idaho recalls his original death defending Paul Atreides and realizes his memories were restored through a violent psychological trigger. He struggles to believe the Tleilaxu claim that over thirty-five hundred years have passed since his first life.

Two women from the Royal Guard, Luli and a masked figure called Friend, enter the room to collect him. Friend wears a cibus hood of light-drinking black, an Ixian device that hides her features from all sensors. Luli wears a blue uniform with the Atreides hawk and informs Idaho that he is to command the Royal Guard. Friend explains that she must determine if the "dirty Tleilaxu" have tampered with Idaho's psyche. Idaho learns that Leto II has prohibited the training of Mentats, the human computers he once knew and worked with.

Luli delivers a message from Leto, stating that he still loves Idaho with the traditional loyalty of House Atreides. Idaho observes the fanatic obedience of the two women and notes their competence with needle knives. Friend decides that Idaho is safe to be brought to the Citadel since the Tleilaxu presence is "not a good place." Idaho requests a warm bath to remove the "stink" of his Tleilaxu creators before meeting the God Emperor. As they depart, Idaho wonders what happened to the previous version of himself and if Leto will provide the answer.
