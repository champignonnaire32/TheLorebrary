### Chapter 31
Face Dancers perform a dance in Onn depicting the spread of Muad’Dib’s religion throughout the Empire. Hwi Noree sits beside Leto in the Royal Cart wearing a silver dress and jade necklace. Leto announces his betrothal to Hwi, causing shock and anger throughout the Festival City. Moneo returns from the Citadel to find the crowds disturbed by the news of the wedding. Moneo enters Leto’s reception room and expresses his fury over the political implications of the union.

Leto defends his decision by stating he has rediscovered the sweetness of human emotions. He explains that denying emotion leads to losing touch with the internal universe. Moneo warns that the Empire will laugh and the Tleilaxu lies will be believed. Leto claims that Hwi serves as the antithesis of mechanical and non-human attitudes. Duncan Idaho arrives at the portal, shouting for an audience with the Emperor.

Leto instructs Moneo to arrange a wedding ceremony at Tabur Village in the Sareer. Moneo is told he will stand beside Hwi and give her in marriage using Fremen ritual. The ceremony will require water rings, specifically those belonging to Ghanima. Leto insists that Siona must be included in the wedding party if she survives her test. Moneo records the instructions on an Ixian memocorder while Duncan continues to shout outside.



### Chapter 32
Duncan Idaho enters Leto’s chamber, exhibiting the "Since Syndrome" of suspicion toward the God Emperor. Idaho observes the room’s gold and purple cushions and the museum-piece Fremen rugs. He questions his own reality and memories of dying and being awakened in axlotl tanks. Leto attempts to calm him, insisting that he needs the ghola’s wits and skills. Idaho accuses Leto of committing a crime by resurrecting gholas without their consent.

Idaho asks for confirmation regarding Leto's upcoming marriage to the Ixian Ambassador Hwi Noree. Leto describes his love for Hwi as deeper than any man has ever felt. Idaho expresses revulsion and mentions Nayla, the large Fish Speaker who guards Siona. Leto reveals that Nayla is the same person Idaho previously knew as "Friend." Idaho challenges Leto on the nature of trust and loyalty within their relationship.

Leto defines the aristocrat's duty as teaching through example, even if that example is horrible. Idaho asks if there is a frontier where he can escape Leto's total control of the Empire. Leto claims he wants frontiers and surprises but argues that humankind is currently a single-celled creature. Leto orders Idaho to avoid Hwi Noree and never see her again. Idaho compares Leto’s grandmother Jessica to a witch before striding out of the chamber.



### Chapter 33
Leto and Moneo stand in a golden stone room atop the Citadel’s south tower. Leto gazes out at the Sareer and observes the mobility of the distant dunes. He informs Moneo that Duncan Idaho has disobeyed the order to avoid Hwi Noree. Moneo defends Hwi, stating she only has sympathy for the rootless ghola. Leto counters that Idaho is courting Hwi and is more dangerous than previous gholas.

Moneo reports that the Tleilaxu do not have a replacement Idaho ready for another year. Leto expresses a painful conflict, stating he cannot hurt Hwi but must stop the courting. Moneo notices Leto’s hands vibrating and his eyes glazing, signaling the Worm’s approach. Moneo appeals to the human side of Leto by mentioning Ghanima and Harq al-Ada. He asks if Leto is physically capable of mating with Hwi Noree.

Leto’s voice becomes hideously distant as the Worm's consciousness begins to dominate him. Moneo backs toward the door, terrified of being crushed by the God Emperor’s massive body. A heavy thumping sound erupts from within the aerie after Moneo exits the room. Moneo encounters an auburn-haired Fish Speaker guard who questions the disturbance. Moneo tells the guard that Leto is merely impatient for his marriage before hurrying away.



### Chapter 34
Leto uses a secret tunnel lined with fused silica to reach the Little Citadel tower. He leaves his cart and crawls through the Sareer sand to restore his worm-self. The tower stands three thousand meters tall and is constructed of plasteel and superlight alloys. Moneo brings Siona to the aerie by ornithopter just as night falls. Siona arrives wearing a white robe over a black uniform and approaches Leto’s bulk.

Leto takes Siona to a balcony to discuss the nature of his rule and his transformation. He explains that his stomach only accepts dry substances and occasional melange. Siona asks about the passage of time, which Leto says will eventually freeze like a pearl in ice. Leto reveals that Harq al-Ada is the true ancestor of the modern Atreides line. He describes his loneliness and the gulf created by his non-human shape.

Siona questions Leto’s right to govern and accuses him of being caught by an Ixian trap. Leto claims he rules by the right of loneliness and serves all of humanity. He tells Siona that he fears her ignorant violence more than any other threat. Leto informs her that she has been brought here to be tested for her future role. Moneo is summoned to return Siona to the Citadel while Leto prepares for the final trial.



### Chapter 35
Duncan Idaho strides through the Citadel halls, causing guards to snap to attention. He recalls eating grain-and-pseudomeat fare before Moneo interrupted his meal. Moneo delivers a message that Idaho must stop being enamored of Hwi Noree. Idaho shouts that the God Emperor cannot marry Hwi and demands an audience. Moneo warns that the Worm can dominate Leto at any moment and refuses further argument.

Idaho returns to his quarters and finds Hwi Noree waiting on his sling-divan. Hwi reveals that she used Ixian-made keys to open his palm-locked door. She admits she was bred and trained by Ixians specifically to woo an Atreides. Idaho and Hwi embrace and move to the bedroom to consummate their attraction. Hwi explains that she chose to give joy despite her impending marriage to Leto.

After their encounter, Hwi states she will tell the God Emperor about their actions. Idaho fears that Leto will harm Hwi, but she insists he will not destroy her. She claims Leto needs her more than Idaho does for his larger plans. Hwi describes Leto as having honesty and goodness despite his monstrous appearance. Idaho expresses fear that he and Hwi are both hostages to the Emperor's needs.
