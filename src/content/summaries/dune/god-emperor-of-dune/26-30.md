### 26: Chapter 26
Duncan Idaho confronts Leto about his visit to the Citadel and his conversation with Siona. Leto questions Duncan about his desire to see Moneo and his reaction to the Siaynoq ritual. Duncan expresses confusion over the government and the behavior of the Fish Speakers, Leto's all-female army. Leto explains that he is binding the Fish Speakers to Duncan as their commander. Duncan objects to being used as a "stud" for the Fish Speakers who want his children.

Leto defends the Fish Speakers' loyalty and explains the difference between a police force and his army. Duncan accuses Leto of tempting him with power as a test. Leto argues that prisons are illusions and that he deals with sins rather than crimes. He defines the primary sins as corruption or attempted corruption of his government. Leto asserts his godhead and tells Duncan he does not know his secret.

Leto speaks to Duncan using the voice of Muad'Dib, his father, to emphasize their friendship. He warns Duncan that the Ixians are building a machine to conceal their machinations. Duncan mentions meeting Hwi Noree, the Ixian Ambassador, as she left Leto's chamber. Hwi told Duncan she serves the God Emperor now, but Duncan does not believe her. Leto orders Duncan to stay away from Hwi because he has special plans for her.





### 27: Chapter 27
Moneo enters Leto’s chamber to deliver news about the Ixians and the Tleilaxu. He informs Leto that the Reverend Mother Anteac is likely a mentat, a human trained for advanced logic and data processing. Leto reveals he has granted amnesty to several Face Dancers, Tleilaxu shape-shifters, for use during the Festival. Moneo explains that the Tleilaxu helped the Ixians create a "cellular restructuring" inside a shielded chamber. This experiment resulted in an infant brought out by Malky, the former Ixian Ambassador.

Leto correctly identifies that this infant was Hwi Noree and was created twenty-six years ago. He notes that Hwi is the exact opposite of Malky in gender and temperament. Leto orders Moneo to bring Hwi to him immediately to reassure her of his intentions. Moneo expresses fear of the spice melange, which he avoids to prevent its psychological effects. Leto explains that the spice is necessary for the Guild Navigators and the Bene Gesserit Sisterhood.

Leto mentions that Siona will receive a package of translations from a Guild courier today. He instructs Moneo not to interfere with the delivery of the package. Leto discusses the various names for melange in the Galach language and their specific meanings. He offers Moneo advice on handling Hwi Noree by referencing the story of Eve and the apple. Moneo leaves to summon Hwi as Leto prepares for their meeting.





### 28: Chapter 28
Hwi Noree visits Leto and remarks on Moneo's agitation during their trip. Leto warns Hwi about a failed Ixian project to create hunter-seekers, which are self-propelled death weapons with machine minds. He explains that machine-makers risk becoming machines themselves and causing ultimate sterility. Leto reveals that the Ixians are concealing their current activities from his prescient vision. He asks Hwi to tell the Reverend Mother Anteac everything she remembers about her birthplace.

Leto describes his Golden Path as a continuum that prevents the end of the human stream. He tells Hwi he will die four deaths: of the flesh, soul, myth, and reason. Leto explains that his tyranny fills people with a longing for travel and freedom. He predicts future famines and violence that will follow his reign as a necessary lesson for humanity. Hwi realizes the Fish Speakers teach about survival through the reflection of birth and death.

Leto identifies his role as the ultimate predator and a cohesive force for the species. He describes the qualities of a good administrator as making immediate choices without hiding errors. Leto asks Hwi to marry him, and she accepts his proposal immediately. He instructs her to go to Anteac and provide the details of her "genesis." Leto concludes that while anything can fail, brave friends help the process.





### 29: Chapter 29
Reverend Mother Anteac prepares a report for the Bene Gesserit Chapter House before her departure for Ix. She records her message on a black square that will be impressed on an acolyte's nerves. Hwi Noree visits Anteac's spartan quarters accompanied by a large escort of armed Fish Speakers. The Fish Speakers carry lasguns, weapons that fire high-energy light beams, along with grenades and knives. Hwi informs Anteac that she intends to marry the God Emperor and orders her to keep it secret.

Hwi explains that the Ixians chose her as Ambassador to beguile Leto, though she claims loyalty to truth first. She dismisses Tleilaxu rumors about Leto's habits and defends her choice to wed him. Anteac suspects Leto is changing the design of his religion through this marriage. Hwi recounts every detail of her upbringing to Anteac as Leto commanded. Anteac uses her mentat abilities to absorb the data about Hwi’s birthplace.

Anteac considers the possibility of a machine that can shield its contents from Leto's prescience. She believes Leto chose her for the Ixian mission because he does not trust his Fish Speakers with this knowledge. Anteac decides to omit the news of the impending marriage from her immediate report to the Chapter House. She reflects on the "wheels within wheels" of Leto’s complex plans. The Reverend Mother prepares to leave Arrakis to investigate the Ixian concealment site.





### 30: Chapter 30
Three explosions rock the City of Onn during the fifth night of the Festival. Leto orders his Fish Speakers to kill all Face Dancers before racing to the surface in his cart. He finds the plaza in chaos and charges his cart into the attackers outside the Ixian Embassy. Leto is thrown from his cart and rolls through the battle, his worm body lashing out at the rebels. The Fish Speakers wipe out the attackers, who are identified as a Tleilaxu-contact rebel element.

Leto returns to his quarters and uses superheated dry air to cleanse the water and blood from his skin. He promotes a Guard captain named Kieuemo, a heavyset woman with rounded features, for ensuring Hwi Noree’s safety during the attack. Leto orders Anteac to locate Malky on Ix and bring him back or execute him. He contacts Nayla, a secret Fish Speaker loyalist, and instructs her to reveal her identity to Siona. Leto tells Nayla to create opportunities for Siona and Duncan Idaho to be alone together.

Leto meets with Bashar Nyshae, a tall and bone-thin commander with aquiline features, and learns that the rebels stole lasguns from the official stores. He announces his betrothal to Hwi Noree and plans a Face Dancer performance for the next day. Leto explains to Hwi that his journals will eventually reveal his true nature to future generations. He calls himself "Worm and wife" to Hwi and emphasizes the importance of the Golden Path. Hwi accepts her role despite the pain and hatred that will follow Leto’s eventual death.
