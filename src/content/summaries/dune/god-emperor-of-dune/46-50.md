### 46: Chapter 46
Idaho rests in a room in Tuono while Siona reads stolen Ixian translations of Leto's journals. An aide named Teishar, who has a wrinkled face and hair like rotting grass, enters and announces that the God Emperor will come to Tuono for his wedding. Siona reads a passage from the journals to Idaho to distract him from his jealousy over Hwi. Idaho criticizes Siona's leadership for losing nine companions to the D-wolves, which are large predatory beasts, during the raid on the Citadel. Siona reveals that she used poison to eventually kill the wolves after the initial failure.

Siona explains that Leto showed her the Golden Path, a vision of human survival that she finds insufficient to justify his current form. Idaho and Siona discuss how Leto will arrive at the village via the Royal Road. They decide to use Nayla's lasgun, a continuous-wave laser projector, to attack Leto's cart as he descends from the perimeter wall. Siona expresses her lack of trust in Nayla despite the woman's apparent obedience. Idaho mentions that he saw rope in Garun's storage rooms to aid their plan.

Idaho tells Siona that true Fremen would never keep company with someone they would not want to die with. Garun, the leader of the Museum Fremen, admits that Idaho is the only such person he has ever met. Siona agrees that the Worm must be destroyed to free humanity. They begin preparing for Leto's arrival by scouting the village and the heights. Idaho focuses on the physical preparations needed to intercept the Imperial procession.





### 47: Chapter 47
Moneo prepares the Imperial procession for the three-day peregrination, a ritual journey on foot, from the Citadel to Tuono. Leto travels in his Royal Cart with Hwi Noree seated beside his face. The Fish Speakers and courtiers follow behind the cart in a large baggage caravan on suspensor sleds. Leto explains to Hwi that Moneo is trapped by his inability to live in the present. Hwi tells Leto that she senses the supernatural and believes he is a mystic who understands love.

Leto experiences a rare moment of emotional vulnerability when Hwi touches his cheek to brush away a tear. The sandtrout skin on his face reacts to the moisture with wisps of blue smoke. Leto admits that Hwi is the first person to touch him since his transformation began. They discuss Malky and Leto's fear of Ixian inventions that could lead to catastrophe. Leto promises to share his soul with Hwi as the procession climbs the perimeter wall.

The group moves through the dusty air toward Ringwall West and the Sareer. Leto observes Moneo's physical movements and senses the majordomo's growing detachment. Hwi continues to probe Leto's internal state with gentle questions about his identity. Leto focuses on the road ahead while maintaining the constant trot required for the journey. The procession leaves the first incline and begins the long trek toward the desert boundary.





### 48: Chapter 48
Idaho begins a dangerous free climb up the nine-hundred-meter barrier wall enclosing the Sareer. He carries a light rope and nine makeshift pitons to establish a path for the others. Nayla, Siona, and Garun watch from the sand at the base of the wall. Idaho uses his fingers to find cracks and ledges shaped by three thousand years of erosion. He reaches a narrow ledge twenty meters up and rests before continuing the ascent.

Nayla experiences a physical reaction as she watches Idaho's small figure defy gravity on the rock face. She believes that Idaho's success is governed by the will of God and feels a growing affinity for him. Siona waits on a lower ledge, ready to attempt the climb herself if Idaho falls. Idaho reaches the top of the wall and discovers a wide grey roadway marked by man-height rock pillars. He observes the Royal Road curving toward a faery bridge that spans the river gorge.

Idaho identifies the bridge as a potential trap and scouts the surrounding terrain for tactical advantages. He notices that the river flows through a man-made chasm far below the bridge's plasteel trusses. The roadway is five hundred meters wide and provides a clear view of Leto's expected approach route. Idaho signals his success to the group below by waving from the edge of the wall. He drops the light rope down to enable Siona and the others to join him on the heights.





### 49: Chapter 49
Leto wakes at dawn in the three-sided courtyard of a guest house during a stop in the peregrination. Moneo expresses concern about the lack of ornithopters to guard the sky during the descent to Tuono. Leto insists on using the traditional Fremen method of ropes and suspensors for the final stage. Hwi appears in a new golden gown decorated with silver and jade by the Fish Speakers. She jokes about being a sacrifice while Leto warns that decoration often precedes a ritual killing.

Leto tells Hwi that their fates are joined and she should not fear the future. Moneo returns to report that the procession is ready to resume the journey. Leto unfolds Hwi's seat into the cart and prepares to set out for the final destination. The Fish Speakers and courtiers assemble for the last leg of the trip toward the village. Leto reflects on the small space of humanity Hwi has provided him between the superhuman and the inhuman.

The procession moves out of the guest house area and onto the final stretch of the Royal Road. Moneo leads the group at an undeviating stride toward the bridge. Leto keeps the cart's cover opaque to hide himself and Hwi from the observers. The sound of the cart's wheels on the sandy roadbed echoes through the canyon. They approach the narrow bridge that will take them across the gorge to Tuono.





### 50: Chapter 50
Nayla waits by a rock pillar near the bridge and watches the reflection of Leto's cart approach. Siona and Idaho stand nearby, having already positioned four Museum Fremen to block the road. Nayla sights her lasgun on the bridge's support cables as the Royal Cart reaches the midpoint. She fires the weapon, causing the bridge to lurch and the roadbed to tip steeply toward the abyss. Moneo, Hwi, and the courtiers fall from the shattered structure into the boiling rapids of the river below.

Leto falls into the water and experiences the agony of the sandtrout skin reacting to the moisture. The sandtrout, which are small primitive organisms that formed his living skin, desert his body to begin new colony lives. Leto crawls onto a sandbar and drags his blue-dyed, writhing form into a shallow cave. Siona, Idaho, and Nayla find him, and Idaho kills Nayla in a rage for the death of Hwi. Leto explains that he has given humanity the Golden Path and made Siona's descendants invisible to oracles.

Leto dies as his body disintegrates into a puddle of blue spice essence and melting pink flesh. Siona and Idaho stand hand in hand as they realize the magnitude of the change. They climb back up the wall to the waiting Museum Fremen and the Fish Speakers. Siona notes that she is the new Atreides and will lead the remaining forces. The story concludes with a historical report discussing the discovery of Leto's journals thousands of years later.
