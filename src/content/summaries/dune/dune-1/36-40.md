### Chapter 36: Stilgar repeated the words, speaking slowly and solemnly.
Paul and Stilgar perform a ritual blade dedication ceremony to solidify their alliance. The Fremen crowd recognizes Paul as the Lisan al-Gaib and Jessica as the Reverend Mother who shows the way. Paul instructs Stilgar to summon a Council Gathering and prepare his lieutenants for an upcoming battle. Jessica waits in the inner chamber and contemplates the necessity of Paul marrying a princess for political power. She reflects on her grandson's resemblance to the late Duke Leto while practicing Bene Gesserit breathing exercises.

Gurney Halleck enters the room and immediately holds a knife to Jessica’s throat. Gurney reveals he believes Jessica was the traitor who betrayed Duke Leto to the Harkonnens. Paul enters the chamber and commands Gurney to stop before he kills Jessica. Gurney explains he spent years seeking vengeance for the Duke who rescued him from slave pits. Paul informs Gurney that Dr. Yueh was the actual traitor and that the evidence is conclusive.

Paul describes how his father knew the Harkonnens would try to make him suspect the woman he loved. Gurney realizes his error as Paul explains that Atreides loyalty is built on love rather than hate. Paul tells Gurney he still loves him despite the mistake and orders him to put away his weapon. Jessica forgives Gurney and asks him to play his baliset, a stringed musical instrument, to ease the tension. Gurney plays a song about old fears and scars while Paul leaves to meet with the arriving Fremen leaders.





### Chapter 37: time by the poison that gives life. Thus was the prophecy made true
Chani travels from the south to the Cave of Birds after receiving an urgent summons from Jessica. Otheym leads Chani through secret passages to a hidden alcove where Jessica waits alone. Jessica informs Chani that Paul has been unconscious in a deep coma for three weeks. She explains that no known poison has been detected in Paul’s blood despite exhaustive Bene Gesserit testing. Chani realizes that Paul has attempted to transform the Water of Life, the poisonous blue fluid of the maker.

Jessica admits she failed to revive Paul and felt an instinctual need to summon Chani for help. Chani views Paul’s waxen and rigid body in a storeroom converted into a medical cell. She learns that the Fedaykin believe Paul is in a sacred trance to gather holy powers. Chani requests raw Water of Life, the liquid produced by drowning a small sandworm. She applies a drop of the poison to Paul’s lip to trigger his metabolic response.

Paul awakens and reveals he successfully converted a single drop of the deadly fluid within his body. He explains that he has explored places in his mind where Reverend Mothers fear to look. Paul drinks the rest of the Water of Life from the ewer to achieve total prescient awareness. He forces Jessica into a mental rapport, dragging her awareness into a terrifying dark region of the psyche. Jessica collapses onto a cushion, exhausted by the power of Paul’s newly awakened Kwisatz Haderach abilities.





### Chapter 38: The strong young arms felt so good to Jessica. She clung to Chani.
Paul explains that he is the fulcrum between the ancient forces of giving and taking within humanity. He reveals that the Guild fleet and the Padishah Emperor Shaddam IV are currently orbiting Arrakis. Paul describes the arrival of Baron Harkonnen and the massed ships of the Great Houses. Paul orders the collection of a large quantity of the Water of Life to use as a weapon. Paul explains that placing the Water of Life over a pre-spice mass will trigger a lethal chain reaction.

This "Water of Death" would kill the sandworms and permanently end all spice production on the planet. Paul asserts that the power to destroy a thing gives him absolute control over the entire universe. The Guild navigators are unable to see the future because Paul’s presence creates a wall of darkness. Paul moves to a high rock on the Shield Wall to observe the Emperor's massive metal hutment. Gurney Halleck counts nine levels on the Imperial ship and estimates the strength of the Sardaukar legions.

Paul watches the Harkonnen frigates and the CHOAM Company banner waving on the landing field. Stilgar warns that a massive "great-great-grandmother" of a sandstorm is approaching the basin. Paul orders his troops to deploy and prepares the rocket launchers for the final assault. He refuses to use atomics against humans but plans to blast a hole in the Shield Wall. The Fremen gunners set their sights while Paul waits for the storm to disable the enemy shields.





### Chapter 39: ‘My son is dead,’ Paul said, and knew as he spoke that it was true. ‘My
Gurney Halleck depresses the blast trigger to detonate atomic weapons against the Shield Wall. The explosion creates a massive breach, allowing the storm and Fremen warriors to flood the basin. Paul receives a garbled message reporting that Sietch Tabr was raided by the Emperor’s forces. He learns that his infant son Leto II has been killed and his sister Alia is a captive. Paul experiences a cold clarity as he realizes his personal grief is part of the unavoidable future.

In the Imperial audience chamber, Emperor Shaddam IV confronts the Baron over the Fremen resistance. Sardaukar guards bring in Alia, who appears as a fearless four-year-old child in Fremen robes. Alia mocks the Baron for his weakness and identifies herself as the sister of Muad’Dib. Reverend Mother Mohiam labels Alia an "Abomination" because she possesses the memories of her ancestors. Alia informs the Emperor that Paul is currently attacking the palace with an army of sandworms.

The storm’s static charge short-circuits the palace shields, and the building rocks from a direct hit. Alia stabs Baron Harkonnen with a poisoned needle called a gom jabbar, killing him instantly. Massive sandworms with gaping mouths and crystal teeth breach the palace walls and deposit Fremen warriors. The Sardaukar legions are overwhelmed by the Fremen onslaught and retreat toward the Emperor's ship. Paul’s forces secure the plain of Arrakeen as the Emperor prepares for a final stand.





### Chapter 40: wave of the hand, saying merely: ‘I am the Kwisatz Haderach. That
Paul occupies the Arrakeen governor's mansion to symbolize his total victory over the Harkonnens. Gurney Halleck reports that the storm has caused extensive damage to the spice storage yards. Paul orders the capture of a Sardaukar officer to carry a message of surrender to the Emperor. He presents his ducal signet ring as proof of his identity and promises to guard the Emperor's life. Stilgar informs Paul that Rabban is dead and that Alia is busy killing wounded enemy soldiers.

Jessica enters the Great Hall and observes the profound changes in her son's ruthless demeanor. Paul declares himself the Kwisatz Haderach and explains his burden of living billions of lives. Chani arrives at the Residency to mourn the death of their son, Leto II, with Paul. The Emperor and his entourage enter the hall under a Fremen lance guard for the final negotiations. Thufir Hawat appears among the captives, revealing he was forced to serve the Harkonnens while poisoned.

Paul offers Hawat his life, but the old man dies in his arms after refusing to betray the Atreides. Paul identifies Count Fenring, a genetic-eunuch and killer with a weaselish face who is invisible to his prescience. He confronts the Guild agents and threatens to destroy the spice if they do not obey him. Paul commands the Guild to send the orbital fleet away from Arrakis immediately. He identifies Princess Irulan as his future bride and the key to his claim on the Imperial throne.
