### 16: She searching within herself for the coupling of fact and fact and fact –
Jessica uses Bene Gesserit data assessment to realize the magnitude of their loss. Paul delivers a message from Duke Leto stating he always trusted and loved Jessica. Jessica weeps for Leto while Paul feels a disturbing inability to feel grief. Paul's mind enters a state of prescient awareness, viewing time as a spectrum of probabilities. He identifies the spice as an addictive poison that prevents them from ever leaving Arrakis.

Paul accuses Jessica of breeding him to be the Kwisatz Haderach without consulting the Duke. He reveals that Jessica is pregnant with a daughter who will be born in the desert. Paul identifies their lineage as Harkonnen, revealing Jessica is the Baron's secret daughter. Jessica recognizes the truth in her body's signals and her ancestral connections. Paul explains that his new sight shows him available paths through the terrain of time.

He sees the Missionaria Protectiva has prepared a "bolt hole" for them among the Fremen. Jessica activates a glowtab, a small portable light, and sees the look of awareness on Paul's face. Paul describes the future as a windblown gauze kerchief with shifting and impermanent surfaces. He experiences the heat and cold of uncounted probabilities within a single heartbeat. The chapter ends with Paul asserting their Harkonnen identity while Jessica recoils in shock.





### 17: ‘You’re the Baron’s own daughter,’ he said, and watched the way she
Paul explains that the Bene Gesserit ordered a sister to seduce the Baron for his genes. Jessica realizes Paul is the Kwisatz Haderach, but he claims to be something unexpected. Paul sees two main branchings in the future, one involving the Baron and one involving Jihad. He describes a warrior religion spreading across the universe under the Atreides banner. The vision includes a shrine of his father's skull and legions of spice-drunk fanatics.

Paul identifies the Jihad as a biological necessity for the human race to renew its inheritance. He realizes he cannot choose the path of evil but fears the violence of the religious war. Jessica asks if the Fremen will provide sanctuary to them in the deep desert. Paul confirms they will and predicts they will call him Muad'Dib, the desert mouse. He explains that Muad'Dib means "The One Who Points the Way" in the Fremen tongue.

Paul feels the "terrible purpose" filling him as he realizes he is a seed in fertile ground. He looks at his mother and sees her inbred patrician lines through his new awareness. Paul decides that he can finally mourn his father now that a path is clear. Tears course down his cheeks as he accepts the burden of his future role. The chapter concludes with Paul mourning Leto while preparing for the coming transformation.





### 18: ‘Now Harkonnen shall kill Harkonnen,’ Paul whispered.
Paul and Jessica wake up inside a stilltent that has been buried by a sandstorm. Paul monitors a proximity detector while Jessica recalls a dream of Leto's name in the sand. Jessica remembers being a child and her unknown Bene Gesserit mother leaving her. Paul asserts that they must attack the Harkonnens by targeting the spice production. He explains that "desert power" and the Fremen are the keys to their survival.

Paul uses a static compaction tool, a device that realigns sand grains, to create a hole. They dig out of the buried tent and emerge into the starlit Arrakeen night. In the distance, they witness jetflares and purple lasgun beams cutting through the desert. Paul identifies the orange glares as an aerial battle between Harkonnen 'thopters and their prey. He compares the Harkonnen search pattern to someone stamping out a nest of insects.

They see the dust-reddened first moon of Arrakis lifting above the horizon. Paul decides to head south toward the rocks to evade the patrolling ornithopters. Jessica observes the pillars of fire and realizes the Harkonnens are killing everything that moves. They shoulder their packs and prepare to trek across the open sand to the escarpment. The chapter ends as Paul hears the hiss of gliding aircraft approaching their position.





### 19: I’ve always prided myself on seeing things the way they truly are,’ Thufir Hawat said.
Thufir Hawat hides under a rock overhang with twenty wounded survivors of his force. A Fremen scout meets Hawat and reports that many Harkonnen patrols are searching the area. Hawat reflects on the massive scale of the invasion, which involved over two thousand ships. He realizes the Baron spent fifty years of spice income to fund the total destruction of the Atreides. Hawat blames the Lady Jessica for the betrayal and vows to strangle her personally.

The Fremen informs Hawat that Gurney Halleck is safe with a group of smugglers. Hawat learns that the Fremen have captured three Sardaukar and one of their big artillery guns. The Fremen discusses a "water decision" for the wounded, implying they must be sacrificed for the tribe. Hawat agrees to a "bond of water" to join his men with the Fremen tribe. Fremen warriors take Hawat's dead soldier to "accept his water" for the tribe's use.

A Fremen uses a small bat as a messenger to coordinate an ambush on a troop carrier. Hawat watches a captured ornithopter suicide-dive into a large troop carrier containing three hundred men. The Fremen considers the sacrifice of the pilot a reasonable exchange for the enemy's losses. Sardaukar troops in Harkonnen uniforms descend from the cliffs using suspensors to attack Hawat's group. Hawat is knocked unconscious by a stunner projectile, a non-lethal weapon, after a knife kills his companion.





### 20: decision of prophecy, perhaps the choice of one word over another,
Duncan Idaho and the planetologist Kynes find Paul and Jessica in the desert. Idaho lures Harkonnen pursuers into a trap by placing a shield at full force. A lasgun beam hits the shield, triggering a subatomic fusion explosion that destroys the attackers. They retreat to an Imperial Ecological Testing Station, a secret laboratory hidden in the rocks. Paul observes cages of brown-winged bats and instruments used for planetary transformation.

Paul offers Kynes his total loyalty in exchange for help in claiming the throne. He proposes using the Fremen to prove the Emperor's Sardaukar are fighting in Harkonnen uniforms. Kynes is moved by Paul's sincerity and accepts him as his Duke and superior. Harkonnen troops discover the station and attack, forcing Idaho to defend the passage. Idaho is killed by multiple wounds while holding off the swarm of enemy soldiers.

Paul, Jessica, and Kynes escape through a secret tunnel hidden behind filing cabinets. Kynes separates from them to lead the pursuers away while providing a 'thopter for their escape. Paul and Jessica find a hidden ornithopter and fly it toward a massive Coriolis storm. Pursuing craft fire projectiles at them, but Paul banks into the storm wall to escape. The chapter ends as they are engulfed by the wind and sand of the lethal storm.
