### Chapter 6: full.’ She glanced down at her dress ‘Why, you know, my Lady, I don’t even
Lady Jessica meets the Fremen housekeeper Shadout Mapes in the Great Hall of the Arrakeen palace. Mapes tests Jessica's knowledge of the Missionaria Protectiva legends by presenting a crysknife, a sacred blade made from a sandworm's tooth. Jessica identifies the knife and explains that the term "Maker" refers to the great sandworm of Arrakis. Mapes warns that the crysknife must be kept near human flesh or it will disintegrate within a week. Jessica draws a drop of blood from Mapes' breast to "blood" the blade before sheathing it in her own bodice.

Jessica orders the head of the bull that killed the Old Duke to be hung in the dining hall. Dr. Yueh stands by a window watching the local people look at the palace's twenty date palms with envy. Yueh explains that each palm tree requires forty liters of water daily, equivalent to the needs of five men. Jessica observes Paul sleeping and notices how his features combine her own lines with Duke Leto's coloring. Yueh feels intense guilt for his secret betrayal of the Atreides but believes he must kill the Baron Harkonnen.

Jessica discusses the lack of water on Arrakis and her suspicion that something is plugging the underground wells. Yueh tells Jessica he had no children with his wife Wanna, wondering if the Bene Gesserit had a reason for it. Jessica expresses her fear of the Harkonnen trap and her belief that Thufir Hawat's methods of death and deceit are necessary. Yueh hides his true intentions as Jessica explores the south wing to assign quarters. Jessica leaves the room feeling that Yueh is holding something back to protect her feelings.



### Chapter 7: there some Bene Gesserit reason? Was she, perhaps, instructed to serve a
Dr. Yueh contemplates whether his wife Wanna was instructed by the Bene Gesserit to serve a purpose he cannot grasp. Jessica notices the dark sky of Arrakis and complains that every interaction on the planet involves the scarcity of water. Paul fakes sleep to avoid his mother and Dr. Yueh so he can explore the palace freely. A hunter-seeker, a tiny lethal assassination device that targets motion, enters Paul's room from a hidden compartment in the headboard. Paul remains in near catatonic immobility to avoid detection by the seeker's distorted visual field.

The hunter-seeker is a ravening sliver of metal designed to burrow into flesh and chew through nerve channels. Shadout Mapes opens the door to tell Paul his father has sent for him, drawing the seeker's attention. Paul snatches the humming seeker out of the air and smashes its nose eye against the metal doorplate to disable it. Mapes realizes Paul saved her life and warns him that there is a traitor in the Atreides midst. Paul tells Mapes to have Thufir Hawat's men seal the house and find the seeker's operator.

Paul buckles his shield belt and runs toward the south wing to find his mother. Jessica discovers an oval door at the end of the south wing and opens the palm lock with her training. Mapes informs Jessica that men with the ducal signet are waiting to escort Paul to the Duke. Jessica finds an airlock door propped open with a stick carrying Hawat's personal mark. Paul runs toward the south wing while Mapes prepares to carry out his instructions to find the assassin.



### Chapter 8: to do his bidding. The house would be swarming with Hawat’s men in a
Paul runs through the palace halls toward the south wing while Thufir Hawat’s men begin a systematic search for the traitor. Lady Jessica enters a wet-planet conservatory filled with exotic plants like mimosa, flowering quince, and roses. A fountain in the center of the room wastes water conspicuously by pouring it into a fluted metal bowl. Jessica finds a note from Margot Lady Fenring, the wife of the former Imperial proxy, hidden among the leaves. The note contains coded Bene Gesserit dots warning that Paul’s bedroom is loaded with death traps.

The message also states that the Duke faces defection from a trusted companion and that the Harkonnens plan to give Jessica to a minion. Paul bursts into the conservatory and short-circuits the smashed hunter-seeker by immersing it in the fountain's water. Hawat's men report that they caught a Harkonnen agent in a cellar cairn with the seeker's remote console. The agent died during the struggle, but he appeared to be an Arrakeen native hidden in the cellar for a month. Jessica and Paul observe light signals being sent between the Shield Wall cliffs and the plain.

Duke Leto stands on a landing control tower watching the Shield Wall and the lights of Arrakeen. Leto feels rage over the attempt on Paul's life and loneliness regarding the ritualistic formality of the Imperial transition. Gurney Halleck arrives at the landing field with the final wave of Atreides troops from the Guild Heighliner. Leto orders Gurney to give Hawat three hundred men for security work and to persuade spice workers to stay. Leto heads to a staff conference while reflecting on the need to look confident for his men.



### Chapter 9: boy who had just escaped a close brush with death. They said: ‘O
Paul reads an inscription at the Arrakeen landing field pleading for those leaving the planet to remember the residents in their prayers. Duke Leto holds a staff conference in a room furnished with a long table, three-legged chairs, and a map projector. Thufir Hawat attempts to resign due to his failure to secure the house, but Leto refuses to accept it. Hawat reports that the Fremen have sent gifts, including stillsuits and maps of Harkonnen strongpoints, to show their potential as allies. The staff discusses "Liet," a figure the Fremen seem to follow who may be a leader or a deity.

Leto plans to offer the spice smugglers a ducal tithe in exchange for legalizing their operations and bypassing Harkonnen graft. Hawat estimates the Harkonnens extracted ten billion Solaris from Arrakis every three hundred and thirty days. The inventory shows that most of the spice-harvesting equipment left behind by the Harkonnens is broken or ready to fail. Hawat projects a tri-D image of a harvester factory, a buglike machine one hundred and twenty meters long on wide tracks. Paul asks if sandworms are large enough to swallow the entire factory, and Hawat confirms that deep-desert worms can do so.

Duncan Idaho reports that shields attract sandworms and drive them into a killing frenzy, explaining why the Fremen avoid them. Leto orders the confiscation of property from any Harkonnen-aligned entrepreneurs found in the city to fund the Atreides treasury. Idaho introduces Stilgar, a Fremen sietch leader who warns that anyone who has seen a crysknife cannot leave Arrakis. Stilgar spits on the conference table as a sign of respect, which Idaho explains is a gift of precious body moisture. Idaho accepts a dual allegiance to Stilgar and the Duke, becoming a bridge between the Atreides and the Fremen.



### Chapter 10: here. There are some things about the command posts I want to discuss with
Duke Leto instructs Thufir Hawat to plan a diversionary raid on Giedi Prime using Duncan Idaho’s men and Fremen volunteers. Hawat reveals an intercepted Harkonnen message suggesting that a "beloved hand" will eventually strike the Duke. Leto is horrified by the implication that Lady Jessica is the traitor and defends her sixteen-year record of loyalty. Hawat argues that Jessica might be a Harkonnen plant or an orphan seeking revenge for unknown Atreides actions. The Duke orders constant surveillance on Jessica and suggests alerting Dr. Yueh to watch Paul.

Leto walks through the palace at night and finds Paul sleeping on the conference table under a guard's robe. From the balcony, Leto watches the sunrise and observes dew gatherers collecting water from the morning flower fields. Leto tells Paul the truth about the traitor message and explains that he must pretend to distrust Jessica to smoke out the real spy. Paul realizes his father is morally tired and despondent about the "melancholy degeneration" of the Great Houses. Leto admits he should have married Jessica but kept her as a concubine to maintain political alliances.

The Duke explains that the pervasive spice in Arrakeen food and air provides a natural immunity to many common poisons. Leto describes his transition from sea and air power on Caladan to the need for "desert power" on Arrakis. Paul sees his father's fatalism and feels an empty sensation in his chest as they discuss the possibility of becoming a guerrilla House. Leto orders Paul to capitalize on the "Mahdi" and "Lisan al-Gaib" legends if the Atreides are forced into hiding. Princess Irulan notes in her histories that her father, the Emperor, secretly wished Duke Leto had been his own son.
