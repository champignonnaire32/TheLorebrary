### Chapter 21: She saw the negative shake of his head in the light from the panel. ‘Not
Paul Atreides pilots the ornithopter into a massive sandstorm to escape Harkonnen pursuit. He uses his Mentat abilities to find a vortex that lifts the craft above the elemental fury. Baron Vladimir Harkonnen receives a report from Captain Nefud that Paul and Jessica are dead. The Baron orders the Imperial Planetologist Kynes to be killed in a way that looks like an accident. He also decides to keep the captured Mentat Thufir Hawat alive to serve him through deception and poison.

The Baron summons his nephew Rabban and grants him control over Arrakis once again. He instructs Rabban to squeeze the planet for every cent of profit to repay the costs of the invasion. Rabban learns that Fremen have been defeating Sardaukar troops in the deep desert. The Baron dismisses the Fremen as rabble and focuses on consolidating political power. He plans for his younger nephew Feyd-Rautha to eventually take the throne as a savior figure.

Paul and Jessica land their damaged ornithopter on a sand dune near a rock outcropping. A giant worm, which is larger than a Guild spaceship, devours the aircraft shortly after they flee. They trek through the rocky terrain using stillsuits to conserve their body's moisture. Paul experiences a prescient vision that aligns with the desert landscape he sees before him. He quotes the Orange Catholic Bible while preparing for the next stage of their survival journey.



### Chapter 22: She recognized that his question was directed at her pregnancy, said:
Jessica admits to Paul that her unborn daughter is still healthy despite the physical strain of their flight. Paul senses the presence of melange spice in the air as dawn approaches the desert. He searches the escape pack and finds a thumper, which is a mechanical stake that generates rhythmic vibrations to lure worms. They decide to move toward a windward cliff face to find a crevasse for daytime shelter. Paul guides his mother through the rocks as the second moon fades from the sky.

A sudden sandslide buries Jessica deep within a narrow fissure during their descent. Paul uses his Bene Gesserit training to remain calm and recall the exact movements of the slide. He locates Jessica’s arm beneath the sand and pulls her into a safe rock gut. Paul realizes their essential survival pack is buried under tons of loose sand. He uses a paracompass power pack and spice essence to create a chemical foam that stabilizes the sand.

Jessica digs through the foam-reinforced shaft and retrieves the pack just before the barrier collapses. They set up a rib-domed stilltent in a shaded crevasse to avoid the intense morning heat. Paul observes the distant desert floor through binoculars and identifies scrawny saguaro plants. He senses that they have found a place where Fremen likely hide from their enemies. Jessica insists that Paul continue his prana-bindu physical training despite their exhaustion.



### Chapter 23: we paid was the price men have always paid for achieving a paradise
Gurney Halleck meets with the smuggler leader Staban Tuek in a cavernous office on Arrakis. Staban confirms that his father Esmar Tuek was killed by either Harkonnens or a traitor. Gurney learns that Duke Leto is dead and that Paul is presumed lost in the storm. The smuggler offers Gurney and his seventy-four surviving men sanctuary in exchange for their service. Gurney vows to stay on Arrakis to seek revenge against Beast Rabban for his family's deaths.

Tuek warns Gurney that the Fremen live a hunted life and are currently facing a pogrom. The smuggler emphasizes that his organization must maintain a circumspect relationship with the Spacing Guild. Gurney accepts the offer and pledges his sword to Staban Tuek's cause. He believes that the Lady Jessica is the traitor responsible for the fall of House Atreides. Staban provides the Atreides soldiers with quarters and medical attention within the rock-carved base.

A lieutenant brings Gurney his nine-string baliset, which is a guitar-like instrument, to fulfill a dying soldier's request. Gurney walks to the litter of a man named Mattai to provide a final comfort. He sings a song titled "My Woman" as the soldier passes away from his wounds. The Atreides survivors maintain their discipline and care for their own even in defeat. Gurney prepares to use his military skills to help the smugglers while waiting for his vengeance.



### Chapter 24: to spy on the proceedings. All of us spied on my father as a matter of
Paul and Jessica wait inside their stilltent for the desert heat to dissipate. Jessica views a salt pan through her binoculars and realizes that water once flowed across the land. They begin their trek across four kilometers of open sand as the first moon rises. Paul teaches Jessica to walk without rhythm to avoid attracting the attention of a worm. They hear the thumper he planted earlier drumming against the rocks behind them.

A worm eventually detects them and begins a hissing approach across the moonlit dunes. They reach a patch of drum sand, a geological formation that amplifies sound, forcing them to run. The giant creature chases them toward the rock escarpment with a massive burrow-mound of sand. They scramble into a narrow crack in the rocks just as the worm's mouth reaches them. The creature's mouth is eighty meters wide and filled with thousands of crystal teeth.

Another thumper sounds in the distance, drawing the worm away from their hiding place. Paul realizes that Fremen must be nearby because they summoned the creature to a new location. They follow a line of wind-scratched poles that mark a path up the cliff face. They discover a hidden basin filled with bushes, cacti, and small desert mice. A group of Fremen confronts them in the darkness and demands their water.



### Chapter 25: Whose mirage fluttered like a host.
Liet-Kynes wanders blindly through the desert after being abandoned by Harkonnen soldiers without a stillsuit. He suffers from severe delirium and hears the voice of his dead father lecturing him. The planetologist realizes he is standing on a forming pre-spice pocket and smells the rank gases. He hallucinates a detailed plan for transforming Arrakis into a lush grassland with open water. Kynes recognizes that his own ecological theories are being repeated back to him during his final moments.

Carrion-eating hawks circle overhead and wait for Kynes to succumb to heat and dehydration. The dying man thinks about how the Harkonnens underestimated the resilience of the Fremen people. He recalls his instructions to the sietch villages to find and protect Paul Atreides. Kynes feels the heat of the sun burning the remaining moisture from his exposed skin. He tries to shout at the birds but lacks the physical strength to make a sound.

The ground beneath Kynes begins to rumble as the carbon dioxide bubble reaches critical pressure. He realizes that a pre-spice blow, which is a massive underground gas explosion, is about to occur. The hawks fly away in terror as the enormous explosion of gas and dust erupts. Kynes is lifted by the swelling bubble and then dragged down into the cool darkness. He dies as the desert reclaims his body and his moisture in a violent burst.
