### Chapter 11
Paul Atreides enters a massive spice trance and witnesses a vision of a moon falling from the sky. He interprets this event as a symbol of his civilization's collapse and the inevitable end of the Jihad. Paul walks onto his balcony in the Keep to overlook the city of Arrakeen, which is built on the site of his former battles. The ghola Hayt, a Tleilaxu-created biological duplicate of Duncan Idaho with steely, pitted metal eyes, approaches the Emperor. Paul asks Hayt to analyze his vision using both Zensunni philosophy and Mentat logic.

Hayt criticizes Paul for using prescience as a crutch and warns him that he is avoiding the present moment. The ghola suggests that by focusing on a specific future, Paul might be giving that future its actual substance. Paul expresses his hatred for the imperial city and the rigid patterns of government he has inadvertently created. He describes the falling moon vision to Hayt, noting the terrifying sound of a star being quenched in the sea. Hayt remains impassive, his expressionless metal eyes reflecting nothing of the internal grief Paul suspects he feels.

Paul reflects on the death of a young woman found in the dunes, a Fremen semuta addict, as part of a hateful pattern. He realizes that in his vision of the future, Chani is dead and returned to the water and sand of Arrakis. Three pilgrims in grimy yellow robes enter the temple plaza below, one of them limping with a dragged foot. Paul accepts that his own body is merely a brief stir in the waters of time before submission to eternity. The Emperor concludes that he must follow the path of his vision despite the personal cost and Chani’s eventual absence.



### Chapter 12
Reverend Mother Gaius Helen Mohiam is escorted through the massive halls of the Arrakeen Keep by a deaf-mute Fremen guard. She observes the immense engineering of the citadel, which features Ixian pneumatic transport orifices and vaulted passages eighty meters high. Paul Atreides receives her in the Grand Reception Hall while seated on a throne carved from a single Hagar emerald. The Emperor is flanked by his sister Alia, his advisor Stilgar, and the ghola Hayt. Paul dismisses diplomatic talk and offers Mohiam a bargain to save Chani's life from the conspiracy.

He offers his genetic seed for the Bene Gesserit breeding program if they ensure Chani survives her pregnancy. Paul specifies that Princess Irulan will be artificially inseminated but her children will never sit on the Imperial throne. Mohiam is horrified by the proposal, as artificial insemination violates the ancient prohibitions of the Butlerian Jihad against machine-like breeding. The Emperor reveals the secret that Chani is already pregnant with his child, a fact known to him for two days. Mohiam realizes that accepting the offer would save the Atreides genes but would never grant the Sisterhood a path to the throne.

The ghola Hayt smiles during the negotiation, which deeply disturbs the Reverend Mother and draws Alia's attention. Alia and Mohiam share a brief moment of telepathic communion regarding the potential dangers of Tleilaxu interference in the gene line. Mohiam considers the possibility of a cross between Paul and his sister Alia to preserve the desired genetic characteristics. The Reverend Mother requests permission to send a message to the Bene Gesserit Council on the planet Wallach to discuss the terms. Paul grants the request but warns her that he will not wait indefinitely while the Council debates his proposal.



### Chapter 13
Scytale, a Tleilaxu Face Dancer capable of mimicking any human form, visits the Guild Navigator Edric in his spice-gas tank. Edric floats in orange melange gas within a transparent container, wearing a dark leotard and moving with the swimming motions of a newt. Scytale warns that Paul Atreides is successfully dividing the conspirators by making a separate deal with the Bene Gesserit. The Face Dancer instructs Edric to prod the ghola Hayt into action by questioning him about his original life as Duncan Idaho. Edric expresses fear of Alia's powers, though Scytale reminds him that his Navigator presence should hide them from oracles.

Scytale describes the Jihad as a contagious mental epidemic that has taken root in human chaos and cannot be stopped by orders. The Tleilaxu agent asserts that the Jihad is using Muad’Dib rather than the Emperor controlling the movement himself. Scytale notes that the Imperial government lacks a permanent civil service and instead relies on individual men holding personal power. Edric argues that they can absorb the divided factions once the Emperor is removed, but Scytale warns the system has two heads. The Face Dancer identifies Alia as the second head of the Imperium and predicts that she will eventually marry and produce heirs.

Scytale mocks Edric’s limited intelligence and warns him that he is an infant compared to the prescient powers of the Atreides. He reminds the Navigator that the Tleilaxu once created their own Kwisatz Haderach, a being who exists within the spectacle of Time. Scytale insists that they must move faster against the Emperor's concubine Chani to secure their victory. The Face Dancer warns that if the conspiracy fails to act, they will feel a metaphorical thunderbolt of destruction. Edric remains agitated in his tank, sensing the growing instability of the plot as the future becomes a muddy stream of prophecies.



### Chapter 14
Paul Atreides finishes a practice session with the ghola Hayt using a crysknife and a short sword. Chani enters the room in a state of violent rage, her hand resting on the knife at her hip. She reveals that someone has been surreptitiously feeding her contraceptives, which will cause dangerous complications during the birth of her child. Chani declares that her life is burning faster because of the pregnancy and that she must consume massive amounts of spice to survive. Paul prevents Chani from killing the poisoner, Irulan, because he knows the Princess's actions inadvertently prolonged Chani's life.

The Emperor feels a deep sense of grief, knowing that for Chani, the moment of birth will also be the moment of her death. Paul questions Hayt about whether the ghola is the tool of his ultimate undoing as a ruler. Hayt answers that if the present moment is changed, the future is changed, but he warns Paul that he will die of money and power. Chani asks the ghola if it is possible for a biological duplicate to be restored to his original human memories. Hayt salutes his forehead and explains that his flesh is reborn from a genetic imprint rather than his original birth.

Paul probes the ghola's mind, suggesting that there is a plastic memory within the cells that remembers being Duncan Idaho. The ghola becomes visibly disturbed by the questioning, clenching his fists as his metallic eyes center on the Emperor. Chani witnesses a brief moment where the ghola's intense vitality makes him appear as the true Duncan Idaho she once knew. Paul admits to Chani that he is both angry and sorrowful about the path his visions have forced him to take. Chani informs Paul that the fetus is developing at an unnaturally rapid pace, requiring her to eat three or four times her normal amount.



### Chapter 15
A young woman claiming to be Lichna, the daughter of the Fedaykin commando Otheym, gains an audience with Paul Atreides. Security Officer Bannerjee, a solid man with blue-blue eyes and dark skin, searches the messenger before bringing her to Paul's private office. Paul stands by a desk containing a suspensor-driven Ixian orrery, a mechanical model that tracks the moons of Arrakis in a Worm Trine alignment. The Emperor uses his Bene Gesserit voice training to identify the messenger as a Tleilaxu Face Dancer despite her perfect physical mimicry of Lichna. Scytale, disguised as the girl, uses a Fedaykin code phrase to indicate that he bears bad tidings regarding a conspiracy.

The Face Dancer delivers a message stating that Otheym has discovered a plot against the Emperor and wishes to meet in person. Scytale insists that Paul must bring Chani to the meeting to act as a Sayyadina and witness the Water matter according to tribal rules. Paul refuses to bring Chani, citing her failing health and the complications of her pregnancy caused by the secret poison. The Emperor offers to bring Harah, Stilgar’s wife, to serve as a witness in Chani's stead, which visibly frustrates the Face Dancer. Scytale pleads for asylum, claiming his father Otheym commanded him not to return because the plotters would kill him.

Paul grants the request for asylum but places the messenger under special guard by Bannerjee’s security attendants. Bannerjee identifies a guide who can lead the Emperor to Otheym’s hidden location in the desert. The Emperor decides to go to the meeting alone, ignoring Bannerjee’s warnings that the journey is too dangerous. Paul realizes that he is following a path where any deviation would result in a future of even greater violence. Scytale remains in the Lichna shape, hoping the ghola weapon will eventually lead the Atreides to accept a Tleilaxu alternative.
