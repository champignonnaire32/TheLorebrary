### 11: The Reverend Mother Taraza,
Duncan Idaho wakes in the Gammu Keep on his fifteenth birthday and questions Patrin about his status as a ghola, a resurrected biological duplicate. He bypasses security using a secret route to reach a Forbidden Window, an unauthorized opening that allows outside scents into the Keep. While smelling the forest, Duncan experiences flashes of genetic memory and identifies trees not listed in the library records. Back in his room, he recalls an image of a woman with amber hair and sweetly rounded features appearing on his wall. He remembers the death of guards and intruders who were loaded with shere, a drug that protects cells from the mind-raiding Ixian Probe.

Lucilla and Schwangyu meet in a study to assess Duncan's progress and discuss his defiance of the Keep's rules. Schwangyu admits she failed to anticipate Lucilla exposing the flaws in Duncan's training to make him more independent. Lucilla reveals she is bonding Duncan to her using the techniques the Sisterhood uses for its own acolytes. Schwangyu expresses fear that Duncan might be a kwisatz haderach, a male with prescient powers the Sisterhood avoids creating. Lucilla asserts that Duncan is primitive genetic stock and not bred for the purpose of becoming a kwisatz haderach.

The Bene Gesserit suspect the Tleilaxu modified Duncan's nerves and muscles during his nine months in their care. Schwangyu demands to know if Taraza's plans will produce another Tyrant or a second Paul Atreides. Lucilla informs Schwangyu that she has already begun increasing Duncan's intake of melange to accelerate his development. Schwangyu reacts with anger, pounding her desk and accusing the others of inviting the destruction of the Sisterhood. The chapter ends with Duncan remaining isolated in his room while his teachers dispute his future and genetic potential.

### 12: Bene Gesserit Analysis Archives Code: BTXX441WOR
On Rakis, the four-year-old Sheeana watches from a rooftop as manacled prisoners are transported toward the desert for execution. She commands the guards to bring the prisoners back, using the phrase "You are servants unto Shaitan." High Priest Tuek and Councillor Stiros arrive to confront Sheeana after she declares the dirty prisoners are her people. Sheeana stamps her foot and argues that the prisoners should not be fed to Shaitan, defying the priests' religious labels. Mother Commander Tamalane reviews reports of this event and notes that fear of the priests is increasing on Rakis.

The Bene Gesserit spread rumors that Sheeana is the long-awaited child come to chastize the corrupt priesthood. Stiros and six other priests interrupt Sheeana's lunch to protest her interference with their sacrifices to Shai-Hulud. Sheeana insists that the Divided God is Shaitan and tells Stiros that even street children know this truth. Stiros secretly meets with Tuek in blue chairdogs to propose new tests for Sheeana's power over the worms. Tuek refuses to order Sheeana into the desert but allows Stiros to suggest the trip to her.

Sheeana demands to be taken to the site of her destroyed village and uses a thumper, a device that summons worms, to call a monster. She stands three meters from the worm's open mouth and demands to know why it destroyed her home. After receiving no verbal answer, Sheeana waves the worm away and it obediently retreats beneath the sand. The priests begin building observation towers in the desert to monitor Sheeana's frequent, silent conversations with the monsters. Tamalane reports to Chapter House that the populace has begun praying to Sheeana instead of the traditional worm gods.

### 13: Bene Gesserit response to an Ixian
Miles Teg travels from Gammu to a Guild Transport ship marked with the Ixian symbol to meet Mother Superior Taraza. He enters a white-walled room that smells of shere and finds Taraza appearing uncharacteristically fatigued. Taraza gives Teg a secret hand signal confirming they are being watched and orders him to awaken the ghola. Teg enters Mentat mode, a state of hyper-logical computation, to analyze the presence of wealth from humans returning from the Scattering. He pulls aside a curtain to confront a tall man in military clothing who is armed with a concealed weapon.

Teg informs the intruder that two Bene Gesserit no-ship monitors, vessels invisible to prescience, have the Guild Transport targeted for immediate destruction. The intruder, identified as a servant of the Honoured Matres, attempts to argue but subsides when Teg activates a timed nerve signal. Taraza and Teg lead an entourage including Darwi Odrade and four acolytes through a hatch guarded by surprised Ixians. Once they reach their lighter, Odrade informs Teg that she countermanded his orders to destroy the Guild ship. Taraza reveals that the Sisterhood allowed themselves to be captured to lure the intruders into revealing their interest in the ghola.

She explains that the Guild and Ixians were caught taking sides and will now face business consequences for their treachery. Taraza tells Teg that he is a product of the Tyrant's conditioning and praises his ability to think like his enemies. Teg observes Odrade and notices she has an oval face and striking eyes that remind him of Lucilla. The lighter plunges toward Gammu while the Ixian-branded Guild ship remains in orbit as commanded by Teg's forces. Taraza concludes the conversation by noting that the Sisterhood aims to settle the most passionate situations off the battlefield.

### 14: Bene Gesserit Council notes;
Darwi Odrade sits in the cramped lighter and reflects on the inviolability of Standard Years and religious traditions. She considers her past training as a breeding mistress and the emotional bonding she felt during her first mate matching. Odrade suppresses her feelings of love for Teg, recognizing it as a dependency that the Sisterhood's conditioning is designed to avoid. Taraza lectures Teg on the concept of dependency infrastructure, citing melange and hydraulic despotism as tools of central control. She defines a "key log" as the single element that can release a jam, identifying the Tyrant Leto II as one such force.

The lighter buffets through Gammu's atmosphere, forcing the passengers to rely on their restraining harnesses for stability. Taraza asserts that religions can be created psychologically to establish powerful dependencies among a population. She describes the Honoured Matres, a group from the Scattering, as dangerous for combining sexual ecstasy with worship to enslave followers. Teg enters Mentat mode and notes that the Honoured Matres' strategy is an addict’s dead-end street that could destroy humankind. Taraza warns that the Bene Gesserit are setting powerful elements adrift in the human current that may cause significant damage.

She orders Teg and Odrade to go aside once they land so Odrade can prepare him using the Atreides Manifesto, a provocative document authored by Odrade. Teg studies Odrade’s face and again notes the remarkable physical likeness she shares with the Imprinter Lucilla. Odrade realizes that Taraza is using the conversation to test her own reactions and loyalty to the Bashar. The lighter levels out after a period of severe buffeting as it approaches the Keep's private landing field. Taraza emphasizes that worshippers and the worshipped are united by faith, a bond that can be manipulated or divided.

### 15: Analysis of the Tyrant, The Taraza File: BG Archives
Miles Teg steps onto the charred landing field at Gammu and perceives the planet with a new, dislodged Mentat perspective. He notes that the local population carries the physical traits of the Harkonnens and Atreides, such as long noses. Lucilla meets the party at the decontamination chamber and uses hand signals to confirm the ghola is safe. Schwangyu arrives via a lift tube and displays visible anger by glaring at Teg for bringing Taraza to the Keep. Taraza ignores Schwangyu’s protests and takes Lucilla to the courtyard to discuss the ghola’s intense training.

Teg leads Odrade to a secure service room where he confirms that no spies can observe their private conversation. Odrade reveals she possesses the complete set of female Other Memories, the internalized genetic records of her ancestors. She explains that the Sisterhood gathered all of Teg’s children into their own breeding lines to control his genotype. Odrade admits she authored the Atreides Manifesto, a provocative document distributed by the Tleilaxu, under Taraza’s explicit orders. Teg asserts that Reverend Mothers have removed themselves from humanity to become something closer to the long-dead Tyrant.

He compares Guild Navigators, who physically mutate in melange tanks, to the mental divergence of the Sisterhood. Odrade argues that she still feels human but acknowledges the Sisterhood's ability to simulate and discard love instantly. She recalls a painting by Vincent Van Gogh titled "Cottages at Cordeville" that allows her to feel a link to ancient humanity. Taraza and Lucilla return from their meeting smelling of garlic after stopping for a brief meal in the Keep. Odrade turns away to wipe tears from her eyes, surprising Teg with this display of genuine emotion.
