### 31: Guide Announcement: 
Odrade, Waff, and Sheeana ride a worm to the ruins of the ancient wall surrounding the Sareer. The worm stops at a specific section of the barrier, and Sheeana commands the group to dismount. Odrade observes the heat and smell of melange emanating from the creature's mouth before it departs back into the desert. They discover a fissure in the rock wall that leads into a hidden underground complex. Waff accidentally triggers a sand slide that spills the entire party down into a deep, circular chamber.

Odrade identifies the location as Sietch Tabr, the ancestral home of Stilgar and Paul Muad'Dib. She orders Sheeana to keep Waff restrained at the entrance while she explores the upper levels of the sietch alone. Ascending a rock stairway, she finds rows of desert-mummified bodies stripped of clothing and showing ritualistic stab wounds. The walls of the higher passages bear scars from lasgun fire and melted rock, indicating past violence. Odrade eventually reaches the great gathering chamber where the Tyrant once concealed a legendary spice hoard.

Odrade discovers the word "Arafel" burned into the dust of the floor and follows a hidden arrow to a secret room. She triggers a mechanism that reveals a massive, undetected chamber filled with tons of ancient melange. On the walls, she finds a message from the God Emperor addressed to a future Reverend Mother. The text accuses the Sisterhood of failing to build the Golden Path and warns of their impending extinction. Odrade realizes the Tyrant's design is still active and that the Sisterhood must ally with him to survive.



### 32: Songs of Gurney Halleck, Museum 
Miles Teg, Duncan Idaho, and Lucilla emerge from the no-globe into a freezing, snow-covered forest glade. Teg carries a modern lasgun while Duncan is armed with an antique Harkonnen field model. The group waits for their contact, Burzmali, but Teg notes that the unexpected snow could betray their tracks. Lucilla expresses deep mistrust of Burzmali and questions the safety of the escape plan. Duncan remains alert, comparing the cold environment to a "Harkonnen night" where subjects were hunted for sport.

The silence is shattered by mechanical rumblings and the hissing of burners as a battle erupts around their position. Teg identifies the landing of multiple no-ships in the valley below and realizes they are surrounded by Face Dancers. Recognizing that they have been discovered, Teg orders Lucilla to take Duncan and flee up the hillside. He uses the modern lasgun on maximum discharge to sweep the slope, creating a wall of fire that incinerates the advancing attackers. The Tleilaxu forces stop their return fire, hoping to capture the fugitives alive rather than killing them.

Teg shifts his position across the rocks, using intermittent bursts to trick the attackers into believing his weapon is nearly empty. He retreats to a defensive pocket in the crags and waits for the Face Dancers to draw within close range. As the Tleilaxu Masters bark commands from the shadows, Teg opens fire with his second antique weapon to inflict massive casualties. He successfully draws the enemy's attention away from Duncan and Lucilla's escape route through the upper ridges. As he attempts to join his companions, a sudden stunner blast strikes Teg in the chest and knocks him unconscious.



### 33: Missionaria Protectiva, 
Odrade observes Sheeana training in a courtyard on Rakis while she prepares to confront the Tleilaxu Master, Waff. She notes that the Sisterhood's embassy in Keen is the largest permitted by the priesthood. Waff is held in a penthouse sanctuary flanked by two guardian sisters who are prepared to use violence if necessary. Odrade reflects on the fact that High Priest Tuek has been replaced by a Face Dancer. She realizes that the councillors supporting the modernization of the city are actually working for the Tleilaxu.

Odrade initiates a psychological confrontation with Waff, using Missionaria Protectiva techniques to exploit his religious vulnerabilities. She reveals her knowledge of the Tleilaxu's "Great Belief" and their placement of Face Dancers on Ix and among the Fish Speakers. Waff reacts with hysteria, accusing the Sisterhood of giving his people no room to negotiate. Odrade dismisses her guards to speak with Waff in the ancient language of the Islamiyat. This shift in tongue alters Waff's personality and makes him more receptive to her proposals.

Odrade proposes a formal alliance between the Bene Gesserit and the Bene Tleilax to combat the Honoured Matres from the Scattering. She offers the Tleilaxu access to the Sisterhood's breeding records and breeding mothers in exchange for an unrestricted spice supply. Waff accepts the terms, believing that the Tleilaxu will ultimately rule the alliance through their control of the Idaho ghola. Odrade reveals that she is a true Atreides descendant and the author of the Atreides Manifesto. She commits to spreading the Tleilaxu faith across the universe using Sheeana and the ghola.



### 34: Songs of the Scattering
Burzmali establishes a command post inside the hollow core of a giant pilingitam tree on a ridge overlooking the no-globe. He uses the tree's biological signature to mask his team from electronic life-scanners while they monitor the battle. Lucilla and Duncan arrive at the swale, and Burzmali's troops quickly usher them into a tunnel leading to the tree's interior. A group of decoys continues the flight to distract the Face Dancer pursuers from the real fugitives. Duncan identifies himself to Lucilla using the Atreides silent battle language as they are loaded into an armored groundcar.

Burzmali detonates explosives to destroy the hollow tree and launches the armored car across the forest wreckage. The vehicle rockets through an abandoned aqueduct tunnel before being programmed to explode as a final diversion. Duncan and Lucilla are led into a secret underground chamber covered in light-green algae that shields them from tracers. They meet Sirafa, a woman with a seamed face who wears a black robe embroidered with gold dragons. Sirafa explains that she is part of a group from the Scattering trained specifically to kill Honoured Matres.

Sirafa orders Lucilla to disguise herself as a "playfem" adept from the Order of Hormu to infiltrate the city of Ysai. Lucilla is given a dragon robe taken from a dead Honoured Matre postulant and a pouch of currency. Burzmali will pose as her customer, a field worker named Skar, while Duncan is disguised separately as a Tleilaxu Master. Lucilla demonstrates her mastery of prana-bindu sexual techniques to prove she can maintain the cover story. The chapter ends with Lucilla realizing that she must sexually "entertain" Burzmali to establish their cover before they depart.



### 35: Leto II, the Voice of Dar-es-Balat
Mother Superior Taraza reviews the reports from Rakis in her quarters at Chapter House Planet. She reflects on the security of the Sisterhood's base, which has been established for fourteen hundred years. Taraza observes the sprawling establishment through her window, noting the ring of orchards and the pain-fenced residences. She considers the statuette of Chenoeh, a sister who died in the spice agony after knowing the God Emperor. Her councillors remain divided over Odrade's independent actions and the proposed alliance with the Tleilaxu.

Reverend Mother Bellonda argues vehemently for Odrade to be executed as a traitor to the Sisterhood. Bellonda points to Odrade's Atreides and Corrino ancestry as evidence of an inherent and dangerous hunger for power. She warns that the Tleilaxu are immoral monsters who create abominations and cannot be trusted. Taraza counters that Odrade is a product of their own breeding program and has been conditioned to survive the harshest environments. The debate highlights the Sisterhood's total dependence on melange, for which they have found no substitute.

Taraza suspects that the Idaho ghola project may have been a trap set in motion by the Tyrant millennia ago. She acknowledges that the Scattering was the God Emperor's plan for human survival and that his "Golden Path" continues to dictate their fate. Despite Bellonda's protests, Taraza realizes that Odrade's discovery at Sietch Tabr has irrevocably changed their strategic options. She believes that the Tleilaxu's continued production of the Idaho ghola follows a secret order from the Tyrant. The chapter concludes with Taraza recognizing that Odrade has created the necessary conditions for the Sisterhood's survival.
