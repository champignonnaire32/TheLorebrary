### Chapter 6

Vin wakes up in a private, comfortable room at Clubs's shop, which serves as the crew's new headquarters. She observes the shop's front, noting that Clubs is a master carpenter and a "Smoker" Misting who can hide Allomancy. Dockson informs her that Kelsier has planned a meeting for the afternoon once Breeze and Ham arrive. Vin explores the building, noticing its unusual cleanliness and the respectful treatment she receives from the skaa staff. She practices staying observant, keeping track of everyone's locations within the shop throughout the morning.

Kelsier leads a planning session with the core crew members to outline the steps for the rebellion. The group identifies several major obstacles, including the twenty-thousand-man Luthadel Garrison and the power of the Steel Ministry. Kelsier proposes a strategy to incite a house war among the high nobility to create chaos in the city. He explains that they will gather an army of ten thousand skaa to be hidden in the northern Arguois caverns. The plan involves a feint attack on the Pits of Hathsin to draw the Garrison away from Luthadel.

To arm the army, Kelsier reveals he has replaced a nobleman named Lord Renoux with an impostor to act as their front. This front will allow them to purchase weapons and supplies legally while providing an "in" with Luthadel high society. Kelsier assigns Vin the task of infiltrating the nobility by playing the role of Renoux's niece and heir. The meeting concludes with the arrival of Kelsier's brother, Marsh, who expresses deep disapproval of Kelsier's involvement in the rebellion. Marsh and Kelsier agree to speak privately while the rest of the crew departs for the evening.



### Chapter 7

Marsh confronts Kelsier about his motives, accusing him of using the rebellion for personal gain and revenge. Kelsier defends his actions, claiming he truly cares about the cause and needs Marsh's help to infiltrate the Steel Ministry. Marsh, a Seeker who can sense Allomancy, eventually agrees to listen to Kelsier's plan despite his skepticism. After Marsh leaves, Kelsier catches Vin eavesdropping at the door and tells her it is time to begin her training. They leave the shop and head out into the foggy night streets of Luthadel.

Kelsier teaches Vin that the mists are a source of power and protection for Mistborn, rather than something to be feared. He explains that Mistborn are rare and often lead double lives as both aristocrats and clandestine Allomancers. Vin begins her formal training by "burning" pewter, which grants her enhanced physical strength, speed, and durability. She then burns tin, which sharpens her senses, allowing her to see through the mists and hear distant sounds. Kelsier emphasizes the importance of using pure metals and warns her about the dangers of flaring metals for too long.

The lesson continues with an introduction to iron and steel, the metals used for Pulling and Pushing other metals. Kelsier demonstrates how to use a coin as an anchor to launch oneself into the air through Steelpushing. He explains the physical laws of Allomancy, noting that an Allomancer's weight determines whether they or the object will move. To test her, Kelsier has Vin Steelpush off a metal ingot to jump to the top of the city wall. After a terrifying climb and a controlled descent, Vin learns the basics of Allomantic flight and the necessity of carrying minimal metal.



### Chapter 8

Vin and Kelsier trek through the barren, ash-covered landscape outside the Luthadel city walls. They encounter a mistwraith, a scavenger creature with translucent skin that incorporates the bones and muscles of corpses it finds. Kelsier explains that the creature is relatively harmless but serves as a disturbing reminder of the world's dangers. They eventually reach the imperial highroad, where they meet a tall Terrisman steward named Sazed. Sazed has brought a carriage to transport them to the nearby city of Fellise.

During the carriage ride, Kelsier and Vin discuss his strained relationship with Marsh and his past in the Pits of Hathsin. Kelsier gives Vin a bag of three thousand boxings, the money stolen from Camon, and offers her the choice to leave. Vin chooses to stay, admitting she wants to see how the plan unfolds rather than because she trusts him. They arrive at Manor Renoux, a lavish estate that serves as the base for their noble front. Vin is overwhelmed by the extreme cleanliness and opulence of the manor compared to the slums.

Kelsier introduces Vin to the impostor Lord Renoux, who is actually a kandra wearing the nobleman's skin. Sazed stays with Vin while Kelsier and Renoux discuss the details of her role in the infiltration. They decide Vin will play the part of a rural cousin rather than Renoux's direct heir to avoid excessive scrutiny. Sazed, a Keeper who preserves forbidden religions, offers to be Vin's tutor in the ways of the nobility. Vin realizes that Kelsier is a truly good man, a concept she has never encountered before in her life.



### Chapter 9

Several months pass as Vin continues her rigorous training in both Allomancy and noble etiquette. She practices Steelpushing and Ironpulling through the streets of Fellise, gaining a sense of freedom and mastery over the night. During a training session with Kelsier, she uses a coin pouch as a decoy to trick him and practices high-speed maneuvers. Kelsier praises her rapid progress but warns her to avoid direct physical matches with opponents who weigh more than she does. Vin realizes she must rely on stealth and precision rather than brute force to succeed against other Mistborn.

Back at the manor, Cosahn cuts Vin's hair into a more refined style suitable for a young lady. Sazed uses this time to teach Vin about Trelagism, one of the hundreds of forbidden religions he has memorized. He explains that Keepers store these beliefs in "metalminds" to preserve them for a future when the Final Empire might fall. Vin learns the backstory of her alias, Lady Valette Renoux, and practices the social protocols she will need at court. Kelsier informs her that she will attend her first ball, hosted by Lord Venture, at the end of the week.

Kelsier holds a progress meeting with the crew to assess the status of the rebellion's various components. They have recruited nearly two thousand men, but this is far short of the ten-thousand-man goal for the army. Marsh reports that infiltrating the Steel Ministry is proving difficult due to their tight internal security. Vin suggests using a corrupt obligator from one of Camon's old jobs to help Marsh get closer to the Ministry. The meeting ends on a grim note as Ham's guards report that the Ministry has discovered and raided Camon's old lair.



### Chapter 10

Vin travels through the Luthadel slums, known as "The Cracks," to meet Breeze at a hidden soup kitchen. She observes the crushing poverty and the constant presence of the Garrison guards who harass the skaa population. Breeze explains that emotional Allomancy, like Soothing and Rioting, is an extension of the natural human art of manipulation. He teaches Vin that a successful Soother must read a person's existing emotions before attempting to nudge them. Vin practices using her copper to hide her own Allomancy from other Seekers while watching Breeze work.

Kelsier arrives at the kitchen to give a speech to a group of potential skaa recruits for the rebellion. He speaks plainly about the tragedies of skaa life and the indifference of the Lord Ruler. Breeze and his team of Soothers subtly manipulate the crowd's emotions to increase their curiosity and passion for Kelsier's words. Kelsier reveals the scars on his arms as proof that he survived the Pits of Hathsin, styling himself as "The Survivor." Many workers are moved by the speech and agree to join the cause, despite the inherent risks.

After the meeting, Kelsier stands on the roof of Clubs's shop, looking toward the Lord Ruler's palace, Kredik Shaw. Sazed joins him and discusses the history of the Jaists, a religious sect that refused to acknowledge the Lord Ruler's divinity. Kelsier reflects on the lack of faith among the skaa and his desire to give them something to believe in again. He mentions his plan to personally visit the Lord Ruler and his hope for the "Eleventh Metal" to provide a weakness. The chapter ends as Ham brings news that the Steel Ministry has slaughtered everyone left at Camon's old headquarters.
