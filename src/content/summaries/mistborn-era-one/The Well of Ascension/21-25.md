### Chapter 21

Allrianne Cett arrives at the palace atrium and surprises Breeze with an affectionate greeting. Elend and Ham witness the encounter while Breeze attempts to manage the young woman’s unexpected presence. Allrianne is the daughter of Lord Ashweather Cett, the ruler currently besieging Luthadel from the West. She explains that she fled her father’s war camp to seek asylum and find Breeze. She provides minor intelligence about her father's supply lines from the Haverfrex cannery.

Elend grants Allrianne temporary asylum and orders servants to prepare her a room in the palace. Breeze remains deeply uncomfortable as the crew jokes about his relationship with the much younger woman. Dockson suggests using Allrianne as a hostage or a bargaining chip to negotiate with her father. Elend decides to keep her in the city to distract Cett and buy more time for Luthadel. Vin observes the interaction from the doorway, skeptical of Allrianne’s sudden appearance and behavior.

The crew discusses the political implications of keeping the daughter of an enemy king. Vin mentions that Allrianne was already demanding specific luxuries like hot baths and favorite foods. Breeze defends her wit while the others mock the incongruity of the situation. Elend takes responsibility for the decision and prepares to face the consequences of harboring a runaway noblewoman. The meeting ends as the crew prepares for the evening dinner with their new guest. Elend hopes this move will create a useful wrinkle in the ongoing siege.



### Chapter 22

Vin follows Ham through the mists as he inspects the palace guard posts and patrols. She uses OreSeur to startle Ham, forcing him to flare his pewter and prove he is not the kandra impostor. After confirming Ham's identity, Vin admits she has run out of atium, leaving her vulnerable to other Mistborn. Ham explains that defeating an atium-user requires either overwhelming force or catching them in an unavoidable trap. Vin leaves Ham and retreats to the city wall with OreSeur to continue her patrol.

Vin questions OreSeur about the nature of kandra and whether they can possess Allomantic powers. OreSeur initially refuses to answer but eventually admits that sensing Allomancy proves a person is not a kandra. Vin confronts the kandra about his role in Kelsier’s death and his refusal to warn the crew. OreSeur explains the binding nature of the Contract and his lack of personal investment in his masters. Vin realizes her own history of abuse as a thieving crew member mirrors the mistreatment of kandra.

The conversation shifts as OreSeur explains the origin of the Contract as a survival mechanism against Mistborn hunters. Vin offers to free OreSeur from his Contract, but he notes that kandra law would simply bind him to another master. They discuss the difficulty of kandra existence and the lack of trust between humans and their kind. Vin detects a faint Allomantic pulsing in the distance that matches the rhythm mentioned in the Hero’s logbook. She spots a tall figure in robes approaching the keep through the thick night mists. Vin discovers the traveler is Sazed, who has returned to Luthadel with urgent and disconcerting news.



### Chapter 23

Sazed informs Elend and the crew that King Jastes Lekal is approaching with an army of twenty thousand koloss. He explains that the koloss have become volatile and have been attacking villages and garrisons since the Lord Ruler’s death. The crew realizes that this third army represents a catastrophic threat to the city and the other besieging forces. Sazed also reports that the mists have begun appearing during the day in the Outer Dominances. He provides witness accounts of the mists killing individuals by curling around them and inducing seizures.

The group discusses the impossibility of the mist being lethal, as Allomancers and thieves have used it for centuries. Sazed insists his research is sound and notes that the incidents of mist-death are moving inward toward Luthadel. Allrianne interrupts the meeting in the kitchen, claiming she heard voices despite her room being far away. Vin notices that Allrianne’s hair is intentionally mussed to hide the fact that she was eavesdropping. After the meeting, Sazed retires to his room where he finds Tindwyl waiting for him. They argue about Sazed’s decision to return to the Central Dominance instead of teaching in the South.

Tindwyl accuses Sazed of inventing the mist threat because he is afraid of becoming irrelevant after the Collapse. Sazed defends his intuition, reminding her that his previous disobedience led to the downfall of the Final Empire. Tindwyl acknowledges the progress she has made in training Elend to act like a true king. She expresses frustration that Sazed remains a rebel despite his status as a wise Keeper of Terris. Sazed remains committed to his investigation of the mists, fearing for the safety of the entire land. He believes the death of the Lord Ruler has unleashed something unanticipated and dangerous.



### Chapter 24

Vin sits atop Keep Venture and reads a page from the Hero’s logbook while burning tin. She hears a rhythmic thumping in the distance that matches the descriptions of the pulses mentioned in the text. OreSeur waits beside her, and Vin wonders if she is the only one who can hear the beat. Zane arrives on the roof, having expected to find Vin at Keep Hasting where they last fought. He questions Vin about her childhood desire for power and why she remains subservient to Elend. Vin argues that she chooses to protect Elend and that he does not give her orders.

Zane claims that both he and Vin are merely toys for their respective masters, unable to break free alone. He points out that Vin holds back her instincts to kill the enemy kings because of Elend’s influence. Vin considers going north to investigate the source of the thumping but decides to stay and protect the city. Zane uses his Allomancy to Pull a coin into his hand, demonstrating his skill and confidence. He tells Vin that Mistborn often lose sight of the value of things, seeing everything as a tool. Zane flips the coin into the night and departs, leaving Vin alone with her thoughts and OreSeur.

Vin feels a sense of loss as Zane leaves, realizing she misses talking to another Mistborn who understands her. She reflects on the brutal logic of assassinating Straff and Cett to end the siege immediately. Reen’s voice in her head suggests this path, but she chooses to trust in Elend’s diplomatic methods. She continues her vigil over the keep, burning bronze to watch for the mist spirit. The distant thumping continues to haunt her, drawing her attention toward the mysterious events in the north. Vin remains torn between her nature as a killer and the role Elend wants her to play.



### Chapter 25

Tindwyl wakes Vin early in the morning and insists they go shopping for a suitable dress for the parlay. Vin reluctantly joins Tindwyl, Allrianne, and Spook on a trip to a dressmaker in the city. During the ride, Allrianne asks Vin about her background and is shocked to learn she is skaa. At the shop, Vin puts on a blue gown and finds herself slipping back into the grace of her Valette persona. Tindwyl encourages Vin to embrace her complexity and helps her choose a more practical style for a queen.

A crowd of skaa gathers outside the shop to catch a glimpse of the "Lady Heir" who killed the Lord Ruler. Vin steps outside and addresses the people, promising them that Elend will protect Luthadel from the armies. She realizes she is giving them false hope but feels compelled to calm their terror with her Soothing. The crowd disperses after Vin defends Elend’s character against claims that he will surrender the city. Suddenly, warning drums sound from the city walls as one of the armies begins a maneuvers. Vin rushes back into the shop to change out of her dress and join the defense.

Elend arrives at the wall to find his soldiers in chaos and unable to mount an effective defense. Straff Venture’s army charges the gates, supported by Allomancers who shoot coins and hooks at the defenders. Thugs use a battering ram to smash into the gate while Elend watches helplessly from above. Vin arrives on the wall and prepares to attack, but the enemy army suddenly retreats back to their camp. General Clubs explains that the skirmish was a test to feel out the city’s preparations and tactics. Elend realizes that his father has demonstrated that Luthadel can be taken at any time.
