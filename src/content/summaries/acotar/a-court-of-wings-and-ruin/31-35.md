### Chapter 31
Feyre and Nesta flee from the King of Hybern's Ravens into the pitch-black basement of the library. The Ravens reveal that the youngest human queen was transformed into a withered crone by the Cauldron as punishment for Nesta’s theft. Feyre collapses bookshelves to block the Ravens' path and protect Nesta's escape route while her own magic remains drained. In the darkness, Feyre encounters a sentient, ancient entity that demands "company" in exchange for assistance. She agrees to the bargain, resulting in a permanent black iron tattoo appearing on her left forearm as a mark of the debt.

The entity manifests as a terrifying presence that causes the Ravens to scream in absolute terror before slaughtering them. Feyre keeps her eyes shut as requested while the creature snaps bones and splatters blood throughout the pit. Cassian arrives and leads the sisters away just as Rhysand lands with enough force to make the mountain shudder. Rhysand creates a veil of darkness to hide the remains of the Ravens from Feyre’s sight before finishing them off. The group launches skyward as the creature begins screaming again in the silence of the library’s depths.

Back at the House of Wind, Cassian provides brandy to the sisters while they recover from the traumatic encounter. Rhysand returns with blood-coated hands and confirms that the Ravens are dead after he extracted information from their minds. He explains that the King of Hybern used a powerful, one-time spell to cleave the city's wards and infiltrate the library. Nesta reveals that the queens want her dead because she robbed the Cauldron of something vital during her transformation. The chapter ends with the realization that Elain is a seer who correctly predicted the arrival of the twin Ravens.





### Chapter 32
The realization that Elain is a seer settles over the group as they gather at the town house in Velaris. Mor confirms Elain’s new status while Lucien watches his mate with a whirring mechanical eye. Elain describes a vision of a sixth human queen who was not present at the initial meeting with the Night Court. This queen, named Vassa, was sold by the other queens to a sorcerer-lord who keeps her under a curse. Elain explains that Vassa is a firebird by day and a human woman only at night.

The sorcerer-lord keeps Vassa and other women with white feathers captive at a hidden lake deep within the continent. Rhysand identifies Scythia as the realm of this sixth queen and notes its reputation for trade and military strength. Cassian argues that finding Vassa would take too much time and they must focus on the upcoming High Lords' meeting. Mor counters that Vassa could be a vital ally in convincing human forces to turn against Hybern’s army. Lucien interrupts the debate by volunteering to go find the sixth queen alone to prove his worth.

Lucien explains that his mechanical eye can see through the spells and glamours that likely hide the cursed queen's location. Rhysand agrees to winnow Lucien as close to the continent as possible to begin his dangerous solo mission. Cassian offers to provide Lucien with Illyrian steel weapons for his protection during the journey through human territory. Nesta watches the scene with concern for how Lucien’s potential death might affect Elain’s fragile mental state. The group prepares for the departure while Rhysand washes the blood of the Ravens from his hands in a quiet moment with Feyre.





### Chapter 33
Feyre assists Rhysand in washing the blood from his hands while he expresses deep guilt over failing to protect the priestesses. He reveals that the library no longer feels like a sanctuary to him after the King of Hybern’s successful infiltration. Feyre comforts him by insisting the blame lies solely with Hybern and that they must move forward with their plans. Rhysand regains his composure and jokes about giving Feyre a salary raise for her counsel and "vital services." He then orders a city-wide curfew in Velaris as Amren prepares to hunt for any remaining spies.

The next morning, the group gathers in the foyer to see Lucien off on his mission to the continent. Lucien wears Illyrian leathers and carries a heavy pack filled with weapons, including a bow and several daggers. He shares a brief, silent moment with Rhysand that signals a tentative truce between the former enemies. Elain stands at the top of the stairs, watching Lucien with a pale and expressionless face as he prepares to leave. Lucien bows to her without speaking, hiding the longing and sadness in his eyes before turning away.

Rhysand winnows Lucien to the edge of the human continent and returns to Velaris within a few minutes. Elain retreats to her room in silence, leaving the others to continue their preparations for the war. Nesta continues her mental training under Amren’s guidance, focusing on building invisible walls to protect her mind from intrusion. Amren reports that no other assassins were found in the city after her night of hunting through the streets. The chapter concludes with the sudden arrival of Amren, who brings dire news that Hybern has launched a full-scale attack.





### Chapter 34
Amren announces that the Summer Court is under siege and Hybern’s forces are currently attacking the city of Adriata. Azriel blames himself for the intelligence failure as he and Cassian immediately prepare for battle at the town house. Rhysand winnows Cassian to the Illyrian war-camps to mobilize the legions for an immediate counter-strike against the invaders. Azriel vanishes to scout the situation in the Summer Court while Rhysand summons extra blades for the upcoming conflict. Mor and Feyre arm themselves with Seraphim and Illyrian steel, preparing to join the fray as soon as the order arrives.

Nesta watches the frantic preparations from the staircase and asks if Feyre and Mor are truly ready to fight. Mor explains that she is using Seraphim blades, which are light, curved weapons gifted to her by Prince Drakon. The Night Court expects no other allies to arrive in time to save Tarquin’s outnumbered forces from the siege. Amren stays behind to guard Velaris and the Archeron sisters while the rest of the Inner Circle heads into battle. Rhysand sends a mental signal through the bond, indicating that the Illyrian legions have arrived and the battle has begun.

Mor winnows Feyre to a hilltop overlooking the bay of Adriata, where they witness the chaotic destruction of the city. The water in the harbor is stained red with blood, and thick black smoke rises from burning ships. Tarquin’s fleet is severely outnumbered by the gray-sailed ships of the Hybern armada that hem in the bay. Mor admits that the sights and sounds of war are as terrible as she remembered from five hundred years ago. They prepare to winnow into the palace to assist the High Fae who are currently barricading themselves in the upper levels.





### Chapter 35
Feyre and Mor winnow directly into the middle levels of Tarquin’s palace, which are filled with the sounds of heavy thudding. They realize the High Fae are barricading themselves in the residential quarters, leaving the lesser faeries trapped below without defense. Feyre expresses disgust at this abandonment and follows Mor down the stairs to intercept the approaching Hybern soldiers. Mor warns Feyre to stay close and not to linger over the wounded as they press forward into the palace. She explains that they must move precisely through the halls to assess threats before engaging the enemy forces.

Mor draws her Seraphim blade, which glows with a silver light, while Feyre readies her dark Illyrian sword. They encounter fifty Hybern soldiers who have already reached the lower levels and are beginning their climb toward the residents. Mor takes the lead, moving with the lethal grace of a warrior who fought in the Great War centuries ago. Feyre struggles with a dry mouth and a racing heart but maintains her grip on her weapon as they advance. The palace's once-beautiful seashell walls are now the backdrop for a desperate, bloody struggle for survival.

The Illyrian warriors swoop down from the sky in determined lines, clashing with Hybern’s fleet in the harbor below. Rhysand’s power appears somewhat diminished, possibly due to the strain of winnowing the entire army or the use of faebane. Mor and Feyre reach the three levels down where the primary Hybern breach is occurring and prepare to strike. Feyre commits to the necessity of killing the invaders after Mor offers to let her provide magical shields instead. They dive into the melee, determined to stop the soldiers from reaching the vulnerable civilians trapped in the upper palace.
