### Chapter 26
Eris, the red-haired heir of the Autumn Court, joins the meeting in the Hewn City to offer a formal alliance. Rhysand reveals that Eris will help secure Keir’s cooperation in the upcoming war against Hybern. Keir demands unrestricted access to the city of Velaris for his people in exchange for his army's service. Rhysand agrees to the bargain with limited conditions, causing Mor to weep at the perceived betrayal of her home. Feyre demands the Ouroboros mirror, and Keir grants her ownership if she can survive looking into it.

Keir warns that every person who has gazed into the mirror has either gone mad or been broken beyond repair. Eris reveals he knows about Feyre’s stolen powers but has kept the secret from his father, Beron. The Autumn heir admits he saved Lucien from execution and sent word to Tamlin to protect the border. Eris claims he ended his betrothal to Mor to give her freedom after she slept with another male. Azriel threatens Eris with his blade when the High Lord’s son continues to taunt Mor about her past.

The price for Eris's silence and alliance is the Inner Circle's support for his bid to seize the Autumn throne. Eris hints that there are hidden forces behind his past actions that Mor has never considered. Rhysand admits to Feyre through their bond that he anticipated Keir’s demands and took precautions with the city governors. The meeting concludes with Eris promising to ensure Beron attends the upcoming gathering of High Lords. The group leaves the Hewn City feeling fractured by the heavy political costs of their new alliances.



### Chapter 27
The group returns to Velaris where Mor confronts Rhysand about allowing Keir’s people into the city. Rhysand explains he met with the governors of the Palaces to ensure Keir’s court is never sheltered or entertained. Mor remains devastated, comparing the bargain to working with Amarantha to ensure the Inner Circle's survival. Rhysand admits he would have served Amarantha again if it meant keeping his family and city safe from Hybern. Amren stops the group from splintering and reveals the secret of her own escape from the Prison.

She explains that she bound her original "burning grace" into a Fae body to become mortal enough to walk out. Amren describes herself as a former messenger and assassin for a wrathful god in a young world. To free the Bone Carver, the group must bind him into a Fae body, a process that requires the Ouroboros. Elain emerges from her room in a trance, claiming she can hear someone who is "changed" but not dead. She provides a cryptic prophecy involving a box of black stone and a feather of fire melting snow.

Azriel winnows away immediately after hearing Elain’s words about "young hands withering with age." Nesta admits to Feyre that her training with Amren in the Hewn City's treasure room was a failure. Feyre senses a lingering heaviness and requests the healer Madja to examine Elain the following morning. Lucien offers to help Elain and suggests a healer check her for both internal and external magical damage. Rhysand and Feyre discuss their shared guilt over the political bargains and the looming threat of the Ouroboros.



### Chapter 28
Feyre attempts a flying lesson at the House of Wind but crashes into a red rock wall during her landing. Cassian supervises her physical training and notes that exercise helps him focus during the stress of war preparations. Azriel instructs Feyre on wind currents but remains emotionally withdrawn following his recent confrontation with Mor and Eris. Mor receives responses from the High Lords of Day, Dawn, and Winter, who agree to meet in a neutral location. Tarquin of the Summer Court and the High Lords of Spring and Autumn have not yet replied to the summons.

Madja, an ancient High Fae healer with wrinkled dark skin and fine hair, arrives to examine Elain. The healer uses cooling magic to scan Elain’s body but finds no physical ailments despite her thinness and lethargy. Madja reveals she cannot enter Elain’s mind because it is Cauldron-Made and protected by unknown forces. The healer suggests that Lucien should attempt to use his mating bond to sense Elain's internal state. Nesta reacts with hostility, demanding another healer and forbidding Lucien from attempting any mental contact with Elain.

Feyre forces Nesta to be quiet and insists that Lucien try the suggested method to help their sister. Mor explains to Feyre that she understands why Rhysand made the bargain with Keir to protect Feyre’s secrets. She admits that her father likely knew Velaris was the only price she would find intolerable for his help. Mor decides she will not let her father’s victory impact her joy or her relationships with the Inner Circle. Lucien prepares to sit with Elain to test the strength and sensory capabilities of their mating bond.



### Chapter 29
Lucien and Elain sit in silence by the fireplace while Feyre and Mor observe from the window. Nesta and Amren work at the dining table on mental exercises to find and repair holes in Nesta’s mind. The training is interrupted when Lucien accidentally tugs on the mating bond, which Elain describes as a thread tied to a rib. Elain enters another trance and warns that twin ravens, one white and one black, are coming for them. Rhysand and Cassian return from meeting with Keir’s Darkbringer army commanders to prepare for the upcoming war.

Feyre spends two days researching the Ouroboros mirror and its history of driving philosophers and High Lords insane. She learns that the Weaver in the Wood once used the mirror to hunt beautiful young maidens for her own vanity. During a flying lesson, Feyre crashes into multiple pine trees and injures her wings and palms. Azriel tends to her wounds and shares the story of Nephelle, a Seraphim with malformed wings who saved Miryam. The Seraphim are a race of feathered, winged warriors whose society allows women to lead and fight.

Nephelle’s small wings allowed her to navigate a narrow rock labyrinth on the seafloor that larger warriors could not enter. Azriel explains the "Nephelle Philosophy," which teaches that a person's greatest weakness can become their biggest strength. Feyre returns to the town house and receives a warm bath and a restorative massage from Rhysand. Rhysand uses oils scented with rosemary to knead Feyre's sore muscles and the sensitive nerves of her wings. The couple shares an intimate night together before Rhysand urges Feyre to sleep and recover for the next day.



### Chapter 30
Feyre wakes up too sore to train and asks Cassian to fly Nesta to the House of Wind library. Azriel brings a tin of healing salve for Feyre's back and then accompanies Elain into the garden. Elain warns Cassian that his bones will be broken again by the same enemy who snapped his wings. Nesta and Feyre descend into the deepest levels of the library to search for information on the Cauldron. The library is a shadowed place filled with stacks of books and managed by a silent order of priestesses.

Feyre admits she is still learning to read and reveals that Rhysand has been teaching her in secret. Nesta confesses that she does not know why certain information about the wall "clicks" for her automatically. The sisters feel a sudden tremor in the world as the faelights in the library begin to extinguish. Feyre realizes the wards have been breached and attempts to signal Rhysand through their mental bond without success. Two Hybern commanders, known as the Ravens, emerge from the darkness wearing gray jackets with bone-white embroidery.

One Raven has dark hair and the other has white hair, and both possess cold, dead eyes. The Ravens use a spray of blue faebane dust to strip Feyre of her magic and physical strength. They reveal they used the King of Hybern’s power to get into the minds of the library priestesses. The white-haired Raven explains they have come to reclaim the power that Nesta stole from the Cauldron. Feyre grips her Illyrian knife and prepares to defend her sister against the king’s elite far-flying talons.
