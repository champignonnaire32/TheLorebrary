### Chapter 41
The Inner Circle prepares for a summit of High Lords held at the Dawn Court to discuss the war with Hybern. Amren and Nesta remain behind initially to continue researching the Book of Breathings to find a way to repair the wall. Rhysand takes Feyre to a warded treasure chamber above the library in the House of Wind to select a crown. Feyre chooses a silver and diamond crown featuring stars and crescent moons to wear with her Starfall gown. Rhysand, Mor, Cassian, and Azriel gather in the foyer of the townhouse to winnow to the meeting location.

Nesta interrupts their departure wearing a dark blue gown and announces her intention to join the meeting as an emissary. She states that she does not want to be a coward and wishes to help the humans living beneath the wall. Rhysand formally welcomes her to the court and the group begins gambling on how quickly a fight will break out. Azriel winnows first to scout the location for potential traps or ambushes before the others follow. Mor prepares to transport Cassian and Nesta while Rhysand prepares to winnow Feyre.

Cassian approaches Nesta and the two share a moment of intense eye contact and unspoken tension regarding his survival. He interlaced his fingers with hers and orders Mor to transport them to the Dawn Court. Feyre observes the interaction before Rhysand winnows her into the clouds of the new territory. They arrive on a sunstone veranda where a Peregryn attendant in gold-and-ruby livery greets them. The palace is constructed from opalescent golden stone and features numerous bridges and towers spiraling into the sky.



### Chapter 42
The group ascends a spiral staircase along the face of a tower to reach the meeting chamber. Feyre notices Nesta's expression of awe as she views the castle in the clouds and the countryside below. Rhysand communicates with Feyre through their mental bond, joking about the beauty of Thesan’s palace compared to their own. They pass open-air chambers filled with silk pillows and fountains as they climb toward the summit. Azriel informs the group that the High Lords of Day, Winter, and Dawn have already arrived.

Rhysand, Feyre, and their companions enter an open-air chamber featuring a circular reflection pool filled with water lilies and fish. Thesan, the High Lord of the Dawn Court, greets them alongside his captain and lover who leads a legion of avian Fae called Peregryns. Kallias, the High Lord of Winter, and Helion, the High Lord of Day, are also present in the chamber. Mor and Viviane, the wife of Kallias, share an emotional reunion involving crying and laughter. Viviane thanks Feyre for returning her mate to her after the events Under the Mountain.

Helion, the Spell-Cleaver, questions Rhysand about whether Tamlin knows Feyre is now a High Lady and his mate. Feyre introduces Nesta as her sister and the emissary to the human lands to the assembled lords. Nesta reveals to the group that she was Made by the Cauldron after being forced into it by Hybern. Feyre坐 down with Nesta and accuses Tamlin and the High Priestess Ianthe of selling out Prythian. The group waits for the final High Lords to arrive while Mor and Viviane discuss the mating bond.



### Chapter 43
Tarquin arrives with Varian and Cresseida, ignoring Rhysand and Feyre due to the recent theft in the Summer Court. Varian glowers at Cassian while Tarquin apologizes for their tardiness caused by the recent Hybern attack. Beron, the High Lord of Autumn, enters the room with his wife and several sons, including Eris. The Autumn Court delegates display open hostility toward the Night Court members and the Summer Court representatives. Thesan officially opens the meeting by asking Rhysand to explain the urgency of the gathering.

Before Rhysand can begin his explanation, Tamlin winnows directly into the chamber without an entourage or invitation. He appears in his usual green tunic and smiles at the group with a look of pure hatred directed at Feyre. Thesan orders his attendants to bring a chair for the unexpected arrival of the High Lord of Spring. Tamlin stares at Feyre's new crown and the mating tattoo on her right hand while the room falls silent. He takes a seat between Helion’s entourage and one of Beron's sons.

Tamlin congratulates Feyre and Rhysand with a flat, sharp tone that reveals his underlying rage. Rhysand refuses to discuss sensitive war plans in front of an individual he now considers an enemy. Helion laughs at the exchange while Tamlin insults the nature of Rhysand and Feyre's relationship. The tension in the room escalates as the High Lords react to Tamlin's presence and his accusations. Feyre remains silent while Rhysand maintains a cold, bored expression despite the emotional turmoil flowing through their bond.



### Chapter 44
Tamlin accuses Rhysand of stealing his bride and claims he only bartered with Hybern to get her back. He tells the gathered lords that Feyre destroyed his territory for a petty grudge or on Rhysand's orders. Tamlin makes a graphic comment regarding Feyre’s sexual intimacy with Rhysand to humiliate her in front of the others. Azriel threatens Tamlin for the insult to his High Lady while Feyre struggles to maintain her composure. Tamlin further claims that Feyre and Rhysand are ambitious tyrants seeking to rule all of Prythian.

Rhysand mocks Tamlin's performance and Kallias questions the High Lord of Spring about his true motivations for attending. The conversation shifts to the murder of two dozen children in the Winter Court during Amarantha's reign. Kallias accuses Rhysand of standing by while the children were butchered to punish a rebellion. Rhysand reveals that he was confined to Amarantha's bedroom and tried to stop the killings through mental manipulation. He swears on Feyre’s life that he was not involved in the massacre of the Winter Court younglings.

Tamlin offers the group charts of Hybern’s armies and faebane caches to prove his loyalty to Prythian. He warns the other lords that Rhysand might be a Hybern agent and points out that Velaris was barely damaged. Varian counters by stating he was the one who warned the Night Court about the attack on Adriata. Tamlin continues to insult Feyre, calling her "spoiled goods" and questioning her choices. Rhysand eventually uses his power to magically silence Tamlin, preventing him from speaking further.



### Chapter 45
Rhysand tells the High Lords that he could easily control their minds if he were truly allied with Hybern. Tarquin announces that he is rescinding the debt of the blood rubies because the Night Court helped Adriata without asking for anything. Rhysand restores Tamlin's voice and declares that he believes Tamlin will fight for Prythian despite their personal history. Eris insults Mor by calling her a "slut," prompting Azriel to immediately tackle him and shatter his shield. Azriel brutally chokes Eris while Rhysand allows the attack to continue for several moments.

Feyre intervenes by placing her hand on Azriel's shield and asking him to come sit beside her. She warns Eris that she will not stop Azriel if he insults Mor again during the meeting. Thesan introduces a High Fae alchemist named Nuan who has developed an antidote for faebane. Nuan explains that she used samples provided by Lucien to create a powder that grants immunity to the substance. Rhysand and Eris both agree to take the antidote, though Beron forbids the rest of his family from doing so.

Tamlin reveals that the villages Feyre intended to rebuild in the Spring Court have been burned to cinders by Hybern. He claims that Feyre's actions left his people defenseless and that he is now rebuilding his forces in the East. Beron refuses to house refugees from the Spring Court and insults the humans living below the wall. He continues to mock Rhysand for his sexual history with Amarantha while the other lords listen in disgust. Feyre loses her restraint and blasts Beron with a lance of white-hot flame.
