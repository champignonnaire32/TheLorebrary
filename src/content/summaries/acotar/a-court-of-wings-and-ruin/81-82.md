### Chapter 81
Rhysand stands at the kitchen window in Velaris listening to the laughter of Mor, Azriel, and Feyre. He watches the garden blooms that Elain Archeron recently tended during her recovery. Cassian and Azriel enter the kitchen carrying glasses of ancient liquor from Rhysand's private collection. Azriel uses a blue-tinged breeze to float a heavy crystal tumbler to Rhysand's hand. The High Lord drinks the burning liquor while standing in the kitchen doorway.

Rhysand notes that his brothers have opened the most expensive bottles in the house. Feyre’s laughter echoes from the sitting room, signaling her recovery from the war. Azriel quietly observes that their survival and the peace in the city are finally real. Rhysand tells his brothers he hopes not to face another war for five hundred years. The three Illyrian warriors clink their crystal glasses together in a silent toast.

Mor shouts for the males to bring a large spread of food and extra bread to the sitting room. Rhysand feels a surge of joy through the mating bond he shares with Feyre. He slings his arms around the shoulders of Cassian and Azriel to lead them out of the kitchen. Rhysand recalls the vision of a peaceful future Feyre shared with him through their mental link. The group gathers in the sitting room to celebrate their victory and the safety of the Night Court.



### Chapter 82
Feyre finds Rhysand sitting alone on the roof tiles of their home under a starry sky. She sits in his lap wearing a sheer red nightgown gifted by grateful shopkeepers in the city. Rhysand squeezes her knee and jokes about the Court of Dreams spending all his gold. They discuss Amren, who survived the battle but has been transformed into a High Fae female. Rhysand confirms that Amren will remain their Second despite the loss of her ancient powers.

The High Lord confesses that he heard Feyre’s voice while he was dead in the aftermath of the battle. Feyre demands that Rhysand never lie to her again regarding his personal safety or potential sacrifices. They formalize a new bargain to ensure they both enter the afterlife at the same moment. A tattoo consisting of whorls and swirls appears on Feyre’s left arm, integrating with her existing black band. Rhysand reveals a matching tattoo on his own left arm that extends from his wrist to his elbow.

Rhysand informs Feyre that Bryaxis, a sentient shadow-creature, has escaped the library and vanished from the city. Rhysand lifts Feyre into the air and flies her over the lights and riverfront cafes of Velaris. Feyre summons her own wings and practices maintaining a steady flight rhythm in the warm wind. They observe piles of rubble and ruined buildings that citizens have decorated with candles. The pair sails over the Rainbow district together to begin their long-term recovery of the Night Court.
