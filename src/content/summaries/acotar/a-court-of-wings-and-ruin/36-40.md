### Chapter 36
Feyre and Mor engage in brutal close-quarters combat against Hybern soldiers inside the Summer Court palace. Feyre uses her fire and water magic to execute several High Fae soldiers who are terrorizing servants. Mor beheads multiple enemies while Feyre drowns a soldier by forcing water into his lungs. They discover a dormitory where Hybern soldiers are torturing faeries and choose to kill them slowly or leave them for the survivors to finish. Below the palace, they find Tarquin’s silver-and-blue armored soldiers flagging against a larger Hybern force.

Feyre blankets the corridor in magical darkness to disorient the enemy while she and Mor winnow through the ranks. They systematically slaughter the blinded Hybern soldiers and then clear the remaining levels of the palace. Emerging into Adriata's streets, they continue the purge as the Illyrian lines and Tarquin’s armada push the Hybern fleet back. Feyre experiences a state of cold, unyielding vision and physical exhaustion as she kills repeatedly with blades and magic. During a brief pause, she reaches through the mental mating bond to find Rhysand’s mind shielded behind impenetrable black adamant walls.

A crack in the shields allows Feyre to enter Rhysand’s perspective as he fights aboard a Hybern warship. Rhysand faces a magical damper that makes his power feel oily and difficult to wield against the enemy fleet. He lands on the flagship deck and kills six soldiers instantly before confronting a new threat. The King of Hybern, an ancient High Fae male wearing slate gray and bone-colored clothing, steps from belowdecks. Feyre remains trapped in Rhysand’s mind, forced to watch as the King smiles at her mate.



### Chapter 37
The King of Hybern confronts Rhysand on the deck of the warship without drawing a weapon or shedding blood. Rhysand attempts to use his magic to find a way to take the King alive for interrogation by Azriel. The King taunts Rhysand about his past under Amarantha and his relationship with Feyre. Rhysand realizes the King has placed a spell on the ship to stifle the High Lord's powers. The armada continues to push Hybern's forces out of the harbor while the two leaders trade verbal barbs.

The King mentions Feyre’s actions against his niece and nephew, Dagdan and Brannagh, revealing his rage. He claims Tamlin has plans for revenge against Rhysand and Feyre for the destruction of the Spring Court. The King threatens to kill Rhysand and take Feyre for himself or give her to Tamlin. Rhysand rallies his magic, attempting to strike despite the oily damper affecting his strength. The King explains his motivation as a desire to reclaim wealth and resources for the Loyalists who feel stifled by human-allied faeries.

Rhysand hurls a javelin of power at the King, but the projectile passes through him harmlessly. The King reveals he is actually an illusion or shade and was never physically present at the battle. He warns that this attack is merely a taste of what he plans to unleash on Prythian. As the King's image vanishes, the magical suppression on the High Lord's power immediately dissipates. Rhysand retaliates by vaporizing the remaining Hybern soldiers and their ship into red mist and splinters.



### Chapter 38
Rhysand ejects Feyre from his mind as he unleashes his full power against the Hybern soldiers. Feyre returns to her own body in the streets of Adriata and immediately vomits from the shock and violence. Mor leads Feyre into an alley while a massive storm of darkness and wind drives the Hybern fleet away. Feyre explains to Mor that the King of Hybern was an illusion and had not actually been on the ship. They winnow back to the palace, which has been converted into a frantic field hospital for wounded soldiers.

Feyre observes the red-stained stream in the palace hall and the arrival of blood-drenched Illyrian and Summer Court warriors. She encounters Varian, the Prince of Adriata, who is covered in blood and tending to a dying soldier. Varian directs Feyre and Mor to the oak dining room where Tarquin is coordinating the city's defense. Tarquin stands over maps in gore-flecked armor, looking grim and cold compared to his previous kindness. He accuses Feyre of coming to finish the job after her previous deception and the fall of the Spring Court.

Rhysand joins the meeting and reveals to Tarquin that Feyre is his mate and the High Lady of the Night Court. Tarquin blames them for opening the door to Hybern and orders the Night Court forces to leave his territory. Rhysand warns Tarquin that this attack is only a fraction of the King's true plans. Feyre offers their ongoing assistance, but Tarquin rejects her and warns Rhysand to control his mate. Rhysand defends Feyre's status before kissing her temple and winnowing them away from the Summer Court.



### Chapter 39
The Illyrian legions establish a camp in the hills above Adriata to treat soldiers with shredded wings and severe wounds. Azriel organizes information from his scouts while Cassian uses his Siphons to perform emergency battlefield patching on the injured. Feyre works alongside the healers, fetching water and helping restrain thrashing soldiers during surgery. Rhysand maintains the appearance of a confident High Lord while walking through the camp to oversee the recovery efforts. Feyre eventually collapses from exhaustion and wakes up in a tent three hours later with Rhysand watching over her.

Rhysand reviews casualty lists and explains that Cassian will visit the families of the fallen soldiers. He expresses his fear that the King targeted the library and Adriata specifically to toy with their history and emotions. Feyre insists they need allies and urges Rhysand to move the meeting of the High Lords to three days away. She proposes that they stop wearing masks and reveal the truth about Velaris and their true characters to the other lords. Rhysand agrees to show the Court of Dreams to their potential allies but decides to keep Feyre’s stolen powers secret.

They discuss the disappearance of Miryam, Drakon, and their aerial legion, noting that Azriel has found no trace of them. Rhysand admits he should have established communication protocols with the people of Cretea centuries ago. Feyre discovers Rhysand has a severe bruise on his ribs from a spear tipped with faebane. They share an intimate moment in the tent to find solace amidst the surrounding death and war. The group returns to Velaris the next morning, where Nesta and Amren are waiting to hear the outcome of the battle.



### Chapter 40
Feyre and Rhysand visit the Prison to speak with the Bone Carver, who still appears as a young boy. Feyre asks the Carver to choose an object other than the Ouroboros mirror as payment for his aid. The Carver refuses, insisting on the mirror and taunting Rhysand about his bloodied appearance. Rhysand tosses a chicken bone from lunch to the Carver as they leave the lightless cell. As they walk back through the Prison, Feyre shares a mental image of the boy with Rhysand.

Back at the town house, they find Elain in the kitchen learning how to bake bread with Nuala and Cerridwen. Elain appears to be slowly recovering, showing a slight sparkle in her eyes and an interest in learning new skills. Feyre and Rhysand retreat to their room to bathe and eat while Elain continues her baking. Rhysand surprises Feyre with plates of food and they spend the evening together in their bedroom. The next morning, Nesta waits at the breakfast table, silently watching for Cassian's return from the war-camp.

Amren continues to study the Book of Breathings, searching for the specific spell Nesta must wield to repair the wall. Nesta asks Feyre why she went into battle without hesitation, and Feyre explains it was because people needed help. Feyre observes the ruby necklace Varian gave to Amren hidden beneath the ancient female's gray blouse. The atmosphere in the house remains tense as they prepare for the imminent meeting with the other High Lords. Feyre leaves the town house to winnow to the House of Wind for a flying lesson with Azriel.
