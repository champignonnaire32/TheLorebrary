### 26: Virginia: Labyrinth
Sovereign Virginia au Augustus watches the Nucleus superheat as particle beams compromise its thermal wall. The technician Oro fails to reach the escape lift and dies when his skin sloughs off in the extreme heat. Holiday and the Lionguard force Virginia into a lift that eventually stalls due to debris in the shaft. The group activates gravBoots and abandons the lift to ascend into the Bastion's corridors. They find themselves cut off by Votum and Rath forces pouring into the station levels.

Virginia leads twenty-eight Lionguards through a series of running firefights in the darkened hallways of Phobos. The Votum legionnaires and Apollonius’s Martian veterans deploy particle shields and traps to box the Sovereign in. A Lion named Arminius is killed by an unknown weapon that leaves only a leg behind. Virginia uses her Sovereign implant to manipulate doors and maintain a tactical advantage while retreating toward shaft D. She realizes Apollonius is attempting to capture her alive for Lysander au Lune’s planned triumph.

The Lionguards set a trap for Apollonius using nanowire and a shieldDome in the tank garage hangar. Apollonius charges into the hangar and is snared in an electrified net while Virginia stabs him in the knee with her razor. Holiday attempts to execute the Minotaur with a misericord, but the weapon fails to deploy its tranquilizer or drill. Apollonius uses his horns to impale Holiday in the stomach before firing his boots to disrupt the dome. A grenade rolls under the shield and explodes, allowing the dazed Minotaur to rise as the Sovereign calls for a retreat.



### 27: Virginia: A Good Death
Virginia and eighteen surviving Lionguards flee from Apollonius through a lateral tram tunnel toward the brig. The Sovereign decides to recruit high-security prisoners to serve as a distraction and reinforcement against the closing enemy. She bypasses Cicero au Votum by hiding in an alcove until the Gold and his legionnaires move past the brig gate. Inside the cells, she finds Valdir the Unshorn, a disgraced Obsidian commander who was once loyal to Darrow. Virginia challenges Valdir’s pride by telling him that Darrow is alive and returning to Mars.

Valdir agrees to help the Sovereign in exchange for freedom for his brothers or a good death in battle. Virginia provides the sixty-three Obsidian traitors with ghostCloaks, optics, and razors retrieved from the armory. Valdir leads his "ghouls" into the darkness to employ psychological warfare tactics learned from Darrow and Sevro. They target the enemy’s psyche by killing scouts loudly and playing death chants before striking from the shadows. The Obsidians infiltrate the command chain of the Votum and Rath forces to trigger a mass panic.

The chaos allows Virginia’s group to reach the training room where the enemy had previously gathered. They find a tower of blood and armored corpses, though only ten of Valdir's braves survive the counter-slaughter. Valdir confirms that the Minotaur was wounded but killed ten Obsidians before retreating to regroup his forces. The group reaches the sanitation sector and enters a waste tube that leads toward the fertilizer plants near the Hollows. Valdir shoves Virginia down the tube as the sound of approaching enemy radios grows louder in the distance.



### 28: Lysander: War Engine
Lysander au Lune takes cover behind a tank as Republic snipers fire down from the bulwark protecting Sector Eight. He coordinates with Horatia, who reports heavy casualties but progress in the breach toward Sector Two. Ajax au Lune leads a column of starShells and Gold knights to successfully clear the remaining Republic defenders from the bulwark. Rhone and the Praetorians prepare to push the house legions forward to maintain the momentum of the invasion. Lysander believes that Apollonius is successfully driving toward the Hollows to collapse the Republic's defense sectors.

Diomedes au Raa participates in the attack by flooding a reactor to disable power to a Bastion before moving to Sector Six. The Rim knight operates independently, skipping his phalanx across the moon's surface to cause chaos behind the enemy lines. Lysander praises Diomedes’s professionalism and trusts the knight to hold his position without constant support. A suicide bomber disguised as a medicus attempts to kill Ajax, but the Praetorian Kyber identifies the threat and shoots the man. Lysander enters his command tank to review a feed showing the Pandemonia escaping toward Sector Three.

Rhone informs Lysander that Victra au Julii has punched through the orbital blockade and dropped twenty thousand Pegasus Legion troops. Cicero au Votum has abandoned his post at Bastion One to pursue Virginia into the latrine lines against orders. Lysander warns Cicero and Apollonius that they are flying into a trap set by Victra’s fresh reinforcements. Ajax volunteers to lead fifty Gold knights on a high-speed mission to intercept and kill Victra before she can ruin the invasion. Lysander grants Ajax permission to go, hoping his friend can secure a final victory on the edge of glory.



### 29: Virginia: Pity Them
Virginia and her survivors emerge from the waste collector covered in filth and begin running through the hydroponic farms. They are rescued by a massive counterattack from the Hollows consisting of armored Reds, Greens in mechs, and Harnassus in a towering machine. The Sovereign realizes that the Votum and Rath pursuers abandoned their hunt because of an external threat. She leads the weary Lionguards and Valdir's Obsidians toward the safety of the Republic’s main defensive lines. The Obsidians clean themselves in the farm's water and joke about the sweetness of fruit after enduring the waste tubes.

Valdir explains that the braves will mourn their dead brothers later but currently choose to smile for their "good deaths." Virginia feels comforted by the Obsidian’s perspective as they exit the tangerine groves and meet Screwface. Screwface informs the Sovereign that the Nucleus's failure to arrive at the command center had caused widespread alarm. He reveals that Victra au Julii dropped the Pegasus Legion behind the Society lines to stop their momentum. Victra personally relayed a message to Virginia, telling the Sovereign not to worry but to pity her enemies.

The Republic fleet remains in a desperate struggle, with Niobe and Char fighting a losing battle against the Society ships. Screwface takes Virginia toward the fallback command center while the survivors of the Lionguard are treated by medici. Virginia grants Valdir and the remaining Obsidians permission to join the Howler Den to fight under their standard. The fresh Lionguard troops salute the ragged survivors as they pass through the Hollows. Virginia touches her chest in respect for the courage of her guards and the successful management of the retreat.



### 30: Lysander: Edge of Glory
Lysander arrives in Sector Six to find Diomedes eating an apple and his Dustwalkers lounging in the broken cityscape. The Rim knight notes that while they have taken territory, they may struggle to hold it against the Republic's remaining forces. Diomedes asks about the status of Victra au Julii, and Lysander assures him that Ajax is dealing with the threat. Rhone calls Lysander into the command tank, stating that there is a report from Ajax that must be heard in person. Diomedes accompanies Lysander into the tank to witness the unfolding news of the battle.

Rhone explains that Cicero's vanguard was trapped by Victra's forces in a theater of industrial plants and smelters. Ajax had attempted to strike Victra by coming down a hauler shaft, but the enemy scouts anticipated his arrival. Victra and another knight, along with a group of Obsidians, were waiting for the Society party as they exited the shaft. The Society knights were outnumbered three to one and were systematically whittled down by the Republic defenders. Lysander watches grainy drone footage showing the final moments of the skirmish amidst the Drachenjäger assembly area.

The footage depicts Victra and Thraxa au Telemanus killing Ajax in a dispassionate, animalistic struggle. Victra decapitates Ajax, stands on his corpse, and roars in a silent display of triumph. Diomedes identifies Thraxa's "Windsweeper" combat style and realizes that not all Obsidians defected to Volsung Fá. Diomedes announces he will pull his phalanx back to prepare for a slow grind instead of a rapid conquest. Lysander realizes the invasion has stalled and his ambitious plan for a quick victory has been shattered by the loss of his friend.
