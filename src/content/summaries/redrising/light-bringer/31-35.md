### 31: Virginia: Détente
The battle for the interior of Phobos continues as a brutal war of attrition in the tunnels. Virginia observes the mounting casualties while her fleet remains unable to challenge the enemy away from the moon's surface. Holiday Skuggi remains by Virginia's side despite her crushed ribs and hospital confinement. Virginia visits the overflowing medical wing of Bastion Four to comfort the wounded soldiers. She listens to the dying whisper the names of the Reaper and the Republic before returning to her command center.

The Hollows command center is fueled by coffee and protein injections as the enemy creeps forward meter by meter. Virginia feels the pressure of the losses and the desperation of the holding action against Lysander's forces. Niobe wakes Virginia from a short sleep to respond to the latest assault on their front lines. A sudden commotion breaks out in the assembly area where troops have gathered to shout and cheer. Niobe leads Virginia through the mob to investigate the source of the unexpected celebration.

Victra au Julii strides into the assembly area looking feral and bloodshot after being lost behind enemy lines. Thraxa au Telemanus and the fighters of the Pegasus Legions march in directly behind her. Victra announces that she has brought tidings and gifts from Sector One for the Sovereign. She thrusts forward Cicero au Votum on a leash to present him as a prisoner of war. Victra then holds up the rotted head of Ajax au Grimmus to confirm his death in combat.





### 32: Virginia: Parley
Virginia walks across the metal wasteland of Phobos to a secret meeting with Lysander au Lune. They meet under the shadow of a crashed warship while wearing magnetic welder boots as stipulated by the parley terms. Lysander appears taller than Virginia remembers and wears patched pulseArmor from weeks of corridor fighting. The two leaders perform the formal rites of parley by touching palms and exchanging an analog audio cord. They confirm their identities and lineages before beginning the negotiation under the vacuum of space.

Lysander reveals that he has Darrow's original razor and the warship Morning Star in his possession. He claims to have broken Darrow's arm and put a razor through his chest during their previous encounter. Virginia points to the statue of Darrow and describes the collection of enemy figureheads held in its hands. Lysander states that he has seventy thousand Republic troops encircled in Sector Three and plans to kill them by week's end. Virginia counters by mentioning that she has deployed the veteran Rat Legion to the moon's tunnels.

Lysander offers safe passage for all Republic legions to the planet Mars if Virginia surrenders Phobos. Virginia agrees to the deal on the condition that she receives two Oracles in exchange for Kavax au Telemanus. Lysander accepts and requests the return of Ajax's head as part of the honorable bargain. Virginia returns to her commanders and presents the deal to a room filled with initial outrage and rage. Kavax is returned to the Republic in a wheelchair, and Victra agrees to give up Ajax's head.





### 33: Lysander: Master of the Spoils
Lysander hosts a battle honors ceremony on Phobos as the last Republic ships depart the moon. He receives his scorched and bloodied white cape back from a heavily burned Red Helldiver named Orlow. Lysander pins a golden phalera featuring a burning torch on the man's uniform and promises to make him whole. He continues the ceremony by kissing his Gray commanders and awarding medals to the remaining Helldivers. Pytha receives the Civic Crown, a decoration made of oak leaves, for turning the Lightbringer into a hospital ship.

Lysander addresses several dozen new Gold men and women, calling them the New Shepherds of the Society. He gives them custom razors with pearl crescents and cuts their cheeks opposite their Peerless Scars. Rhone attaches Lysander's blood-stained cape to his armor as the general faces his cheering troops. A hologram shows the destruction of Darrow's statue at the north pole as allied ships fire upon it. The figureheads inside the statue's hands scatter into space while the Republic watches their idol fall during their retreat.

Julia au Bellona drinks wine with Lysander in Victra's gardens during a victory party hosted by Apollonius. Lysander tells Julia that he gave Apollonius the dockyards so that he could eventually give her the planet Mars. He proposes launching a full Iron Rain on Mars within three days to shock the worlds before Atalantia intervenes. Dido au Raa agrees to stay and fight if Atalantia arrives, ensuring the Rim fleet protects Lysander's flanks. During the party, Lysander is poisoned by the Lament and collapses in the garden while experiencing intense physical agony.





### 34: Virginia: Remember Earth
Virginia waits in her stateroom on the Dejah Thoris for a tightbeam transmission from the Archimedes. She looks at a menu from a Phobos eatery and notices a dessert named after Ragnar Volarus. Darrow's face appears on the viewscreen, and they perform a prearranged security test to confirm their identities. Virginia informs Darrow that their son Pax is alive and that Kavax has been returned to them. Darrow reports that he is with Cassius and Sevro and is currently several days out from Mars.

Virginia tells Darrow that Phobos has been surrendered to Lysander and the evacuation is complete. Darrow suggests sallying out with the fleet to bring him in, but Virginia refuses the request. She explains that half the fleet is destroyed and the Republic has lost its primary repair yards. Virginia states that Mars needs Darrow to remain outside the Gold siege line to find a path to victory. She orders him not to return to the planet yet because the enemy is waiting to exterminate them all.

Darrow admits he should have listened to Virginia before leaving Luna for the Mercury campaign. Virginia informs him that Quicksilver has fled to a hidden location in the Belt with a private fleet. She explains that Darrow must find the billionaire and convince him to rejoin the war effort. Darrow accepts the orders and asks Virginia to tell Pax that he is proud of him. The transmission cuts out before Darrow can finish his message, leaving him alone in the dark room.





### 35: Darrow: Winds of Duty
Virginia's hologram shows the last known location of Quicksilver's flagship near an unremarkable asteroid numbered 12193. She explains that Quicksilver has spent ten years siphoning helium and mining rare metals to build a secret military force. The asteroid serves as a factory and laboratory for research that could shift the balance of the war. Five Republic ships previously sent to the station have disappeared or lost contact with Mars. Darrow, Sevro, and Cassius must use the cloaked Archimedes to sneak through a diversionary assault at Ceres.

Darrow's mission is to reconnoiter the asteroid and persuade Quicksilver to bring his hoarded might back to Mars. Sevro reacts with anger and bitterness when he learns he cannot return home to see Victra and his baby. He insults Virginia regarding her clone brother before leaving the lounge in a state of visible distress. Virginia offers Cassius a battlefield promotion to Morning Knight of the Republic, which he formally accepts. She also warns Darrow about a girl named Lyria of Lagalos and the threat of Volsung Fá.

The Archimedes signal is cut, and Darrow is left with his new orders as the ship turns toward the Belt. He leaves a plate of ham for Sevro outside the escape pod hatch as a peace offering. Darrow joins Aurae in the galley where she plays a lyre and sings of the dreams of slaves. Cassius and Darrow drink a toast to the Republic and their Sovereign before retreating to their bunks. Darrow looks at the phrase "lux ex tenebris" on his ceiling and feels a renewed sense of purpose.
