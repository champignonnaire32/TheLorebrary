### 21: Petard
The Society armada circles Mars, testing the Republic's orbital battle stations (OBCs) with skirmishes while Virginia monitors the enemy's movements from Phobos. Lysander’s ship, the Lightbringer, appears to stall to recover escape pods from a damaged destroyer, which Virginia correctly suspects is bait to lure her fleet out of position. Virginia notices a massive velocity object inbound and orders evasive action, but it is too late for Victra’s fleet at the north pole. The enemy uses restored, massive railguns to fire corvette-sized durosteel slugs—made from the scrap of the White Fleet—at six times the speed of a warship. The barrage devastates Victra's Task Force Spear, though she survives and begins rallying remaining ships amidst the debris. Virginia realizes this is a decapitation mission targeting her best ships and orders Niobe and Char to reinforce the pole.

Virginia prepares Phobos for a direct assault as Lysander’s fleet aligns with the moon’s orbit. Oro, the Blue commander, prepares the moon’s giant anti-ship guns while Virginia readies the legions for a ground defense. Kavax remains at Virginia's side, providing a calm presence even as the Republic's intelligence failures become apparent. Virginia anticipates that Lysander is using stolen Republic technology to facilitate his attack on the moon. The Nucleus staff works to recover from the shock of the railgun attack and prepares for the incoming fleet. Virginia resolves to draw enemy blood after the Republic's initial heavy losses.

The chapter ends with the enemy armada angling toward the north pole to engage Victra in a "knife fight." Virginia understands that Phobos is now vulnerable without fleet coverage but trusts in its internal defenses. She dons her own command gear, readying for the personal defense of the moon. Kavax observes the Lightbringer’s approach, noting that Lysander is no longer an amateur. The tension peaks as the countdown to alignment with Lysander's fleet reaches its final minutes. Virginia rolls out her shoulders, bracing for the inevitable collision.





### 22: Iron, Death, Gold
Lysander enters the Lightbringer's main hangar with his elite Praetorian Guard and his drop century, including Ajax and Rhone. The hangar is filled with forty-three thousand Praetorians and two house legions raised on Mercury, all preparing for an imminent launch against Phobos. Ajax mocks Lysander's nerves, noting that the young Lune is risking his entire reputation and lineage on this single gamble. Lysander maintains a front of quiet professionalism despite his internal dread and the high stakes of the operation. He confirms that the "Twins"—the massive railguns—were his strategic contribution to the campaign. Ajax admits he is only there to ensure Lysander doesn't die, signaling a personal rather than political loyalty.

Rhone micromanages the preparations, shouting orders at the Grays and ensuring every piece of equipment is combat-ready. Lysander approaches his Red Helldiver corps, composed of Martian Gammas and Votum miners, who are tasked with operating the clawDrills. He performs a calculated political gesture by wrapping his white cape around the shoulders of the Red headTalk, promising a better Society for their loyalty. The hangar is also filled with young Gold "berserkers," many of whom are new men eager to earn names through sacrifice. Lysander views these Golds as expendable assets and prepares to spend them in the coming breach. The notice lights flash yellow, signaling the transition to the spitTubes for the drop.

Ajax shares a rare moment of vulnerability with Lysander, revealing he is fighting because Atalantia's murder of Lysander's parents is an unforgivable betrayal of family. He bumps foreheads with Lysander, calling him "little brother" and promising to see him in hell. Lysander loads into his spitTube headfirst and experiences a moment of paralyzing terror and nausea in the dark chamber. He finds old vomit stains from Darrow's veterans in the tube, which oddly consoles him in his fear. He struggles to access the Mind’s Eye but ultimately hardens his resolve as the final ten-minute countdown begins. He repeats a mantra to himself: "I am Iron. I am Death. I am Gold."





### 23: Grim Glory
Lysander’s destroyers charge Phobos, acting as a screen for the Lightbringer as they enter the range of the moon’s particle beams and railguns. Virginia watches as the first two destroyers are obliterated, their hulls peeling away to reveal twisted skeletons under the Republic's concentrated fire. She realizes Lysander is using the empty, remotely piloted destroyers as battering rams to collapse Phobos’s shields through pure kinetic impact. Despite the Republic's heavy fire, the gamble works, and the destroyers reach the halfway point to the moon. Virginia orders the guns to refocus on the destroyers to knock them off their collision courses. Five damaged destroyers remain on a trajectory to hit the moon with gigatons of kinetic energy.

The first destroyer impacts the main shield, causing the Nucleus to tremble and the shield to turn crimson. Virginia orders the shield to be lowered just before the final impacts to prevent a general system overload that would leave the moon defenseless for a day. One destroyer slams directly into the Hive, shearing off the tops of a hundred spires and creating a two-kilometer wide crater. The Lightbringer moves in to hover just one kilometer above the city, and a close-range slugging match begins between the ship and the moon. Virginia orders the shields back up in Sector One, even though the radiation from the melted inhibitors will kill the engineering team led by Centurion Legard. Lysander’s forces begin dropping hundreds of clawDrills to bypass the Bastions and penetrate the moon's interior.

Virginia tasks Legate Dunlo and Red Legion I with stopping the drills before they reach the core reactors. Kavax prepares to lead a Drachenjäger charge on the surface to wipe out the enemy at the breach points. Virginia gives Kavax her pack of jellybeans as they say a professional farewell, acknowledging they may never see each other again. Sophocles the fox howls in anxiety as Kavax leaves the Nucleus to join the battle. The Republic's air forces are ordered to ignore enemy fighters and focus entirely on destroying the incoming clawDrills. Virginia steels herself to manage the complex defense, feeling a part of her heart leave with Kavax.





### 24: Drop Shock
Lysander launches from the spitTube and experiences the sensory overload of "drop shock" as he falls between the Lightbringer and the surface of Phobos. He fights the instinct to alter course, knowing that any deviation could cause a lethal chain reaction among the thousands of descending men. Rail slugs and missiles fill the sky, but Lysander focuses solely on his target: a specific clawDrill beacon. He witnesses the chaotic destruction in isolated frames, including a pinwheeling destroyer crushing a city block and the disintegration of a ice monument. His theater of war shrinks as he nears the surface, eventually spotting his drill on an apartment complex mesa. He lands and finds himself staring at a Green woman through her window as his second wave descends.

The heat generated by the clawDrill's descent causes the Green woman and her apartment to incinerate as Lysander watches with a detached, "insectile" perspective. Ajax snaps him out of his trance, and Rhone reports that Republic ripWings destroyed half of the initial clawDrill wave. Lysander observes the drills disappearing into the moon's crust, creating geysers of debris as level after level depressurizes. He sees the Republic's main shield reactivate, cutting his vanguard off from orbital support. From his vantage point, he watches a dozen Drachenjägers emerge from Bastion One to sweep down the pyramid toward his position. He realizes that if the Republic mechs take the landfall, his forces will be trapped and eliminated.

Ajax and Rhone urge Lysander to move toward their objective inside the moon rather than lingering on the surface. Lysander listens to his centurions coordinating the defense against the approaching Drachenjägers. He reflects on the strange beauty of the hellish landscape and the nature of human warfare compared to the "peace" he hopes to build. He briefly looks up at the Lightbringer, hoping for reinforcements to follow through the breaches. Despite the overwhelming odds and the reactivation of the enemy shield, Lysander hardens his heart. He dives into the mouth of the breach, following the demonic light of the clawDrill into the depths of Phobos.





### 25: Virginia: War Prism
Virginia manages the defense of Phobos through her command crown, treating the battle as a complex game of resource accounting. The clawDrills successfully bypass the Bastions, forcing Virginia to commit almost all of Bastion One’s reserves to stop the internal penetration. She watches as Red Legion I makes contact with the enemy thirty-six levels down, destroying one drill but facing elite Praetorian reinforcements. The combat descends into bitter melee chaos, with the Republic's superior numbers struggling against the heavy armor of the Praetorians. Virginia triages the engagements, sacrificing units to contain the enemy's progress toward the moon's reactors. She notes that more than half of the drills have been destroyed, but a few key vanguard units are still advancing rapidly.

An enemy unit of 150 men, led by Flavinius and possibly Ajax or Lysander, breaks through the perimeter and heads for the reactor. Virginia orders Virgilus to lead a thousand Lionguards to intercept them, but Nakamura refuses to leave Virginia's side in the Nucleus. Victra and Char send fragmented reports through the jamming, indicating that the fleet battle is turning as Diomedes moves toward Phobos. Virginia realizes she must let the fleet fight its own battle and focus on the moon, even as her situation deteriorates. She sends her remaining Lionguard reserves to reinforce the shield generator, leaving herself with no further backups. The Republic's defense begins to fail as the shield generator is lost and the main shield over the sector goes down permanently.

Votum and Rath ships launch a second wave of invasion, overwhelming the Republic's exhausted defenders. Virginia realizes Sector One is lost and orders a fighting withdrawal to Sectors Two and Eight to prevent a total rout. Kavax’s Drachenjäger charge on the surface is stalled and then crushed by the arrival of Apollonius’s reinforcements. Virginia prepares to evacuate the Nucleus, which serves as an escape pod, but stays as long as possible to manage the retreat of tens of thousands. Nakamura forces her to initiate the drop sequence as clawDrills begin chewing through the Nucleus's outer armor. A final communication comes through, but instead of Kavax, it is Apollonius, who has captured a Republic Drachenjäger. He taunts Virginia, claiming to have broken her champion and announcing that he is coming for her.
