### Chapter 76: Darrow: By the Laws of the Ice
Darrow chases Volsung Fá across the island of Hades until the warlord reaches a headland with no escape. Sevro, Cassius, Diomedes, Volga, and Lyria arrive as a massive audience of Obsidians and Ascomanni gather on the cliffs. Fá’s armor is tattered and he bleeds from numerous wounds while his mechanical voice box fails. Darrow demands a public confession of Fá's true identity and his ties to the Society. Fá admits he is Vagnar Hefga, a former slaveknight of House Grimmus and a member of Atlas au Raa’s Cohors Nihil.

The warlord reveals that Atlas raised him among the Ascomanni to unite them as a weapon against the Republic. Fá claims Atlas knows all his enemies' secrets and will ultimately prevail even if Fá dies. After delivering his confession, Fá unfastens his armor and kneels in submission to his master, Atlas. Darrow refuses to kill Fá himself and instead calls Volga forward to handle the execution. He declares that the laws of the ice dictate blood should judge blood since Fá is a traitor to his family.

Volga dons a heatseeker gauntlet, a heavy metal weapon with sharp mechanical fingers, and approaches her biological father. Fá attempts to justify his actions by claiming he wanted Volga to understand and love him. Volga ignores his plea and plunges the gauntlet into his chest to rip out his heart. The Ascomanni flee into the darkness as the Obsidians watch the death of their king in silence. Sevro announces a transition period where the jarls must vote for a new monarch within twenty-four hours.

### Chapter 77: Darrow: Old Stoneside
Darrow and Cassius visit Lorn au Arcos's abandoned estate, Harmonia, on a wandering island in the Discordia Sea. They walk through the derelict castle where Darrow reflects on his history with his former mentor. Darrow finds Lorn's bedroom and uses a razor to shave the beard he grew during his time as a hermit. Cassius also shaves while they descend to the training grotto where the Willow Way was perfected. Darrow kneels to offer a silent apology for the death of Alexandar, Lorn’s grandson and Darrow’s student.

Cassius consoles Darrow by stating that students are a reflection of their teachers, just as Darrow reflects Lorn. Lyria arrives in a shuttle and performs a dramatic landing to impress Cassius before joining them. Volga follows and confronts Darrow about his decision to let her kill Fá in front of the Volk. She accuses him of manipulating her into a position where the braves want to crown her as their queen. Darrow explains that he wants her to be a bridge for the Obsidians to escape their cycle of violence.

He offers the Volk a path back to the Republic and an opportunity to defend Mars alongside his forces. Volga expresses fear that the Republic will eventually discard the Obsidians or that the Rim will demand their heads. Darrow suggests she meet with Diomedes and Athena to negotiate the terms of their survival and restitution. Lyria reports that the Volk have finished their vote and are waiting for Volga to return. Volga asks Darrow not to attend her coronation so she can establish her own authority as a leader.

### Chapter 78: Darrow: The Monster in the Storm
Sevro reports via a fuzzy coms link that Volga has been elected as the new Queen of the Volk. During her coronation, Volga destroyed Fá’s throne and cut off her own valor tail to show her commitment to change. The jarls and braves followed her example by discarding their gold and shaving their heads in a display of unity. Volga declared that she is going home to fight for Mars and invited her people to join her. Darrow, Cassius, and Diomedes watch the storm bands of Jupiter from the cockpit of the Archimedes.

The group is currently traveling toward Io to contact Ionian resistance fighters hidden in subterranean bunkers. Darrow carries the severed head of Volsung Fá in a box as a trophy and potential piece of leverage. Cassius shuts down the ship’s engines and kills the power when he detects a massive sensor ghost. They observe a scout torchShip hiding its burn within Jupiter's magnetosphere to avoid detection by Ionian defenses. A large Core strike force of warships passes directly beneath the Archimedes, heading toward the moon of Io.

The group identifies the flagship leading the fleet as Darrow's former vessel, the Morning Star. The ship has been reclaimed by the Society and repainted with black Lune crescents and golden pyramids. Diomedes recognizes the tactical move as an attempt to catch the Ascomanni and the Rim survivors by surprise. Darrow realizes that Atlas’s plan has shifted and the Core is now moving to seize the Garter. The proximity of the massive warships forces the Archimedes to remain silent and adrift to avoid being spotted.

### Chapter 79: Lysander: Teeth of Civilization
Lysander launches a massive Iron Rain onto the moon of Io to liberate the city of Plutus. His fleet, led by the Lightbringer, annihilates the Ascomanni warships orbiting the moon in a lopsided space battle. The New Shepherds, Lysander's volunteer force, and the House Lune legions descend toward the surface to slaughter the remaining garrison. Lysander and Cicero lead the vanguard, piercing the atmospheric pulseDome and engaging the Ascomanni in the Arbor of Akari. The Core forces utilize superior technology and professional discipline to overwhelm the disorganized tribes of the Allfather.

In the city, Lysander and Cicero clear a firing control chamber and begin saving lowColor citizens from cages. They find thousands of captives being loaded into cosmosHaulers for transport to the Ascomanni's deep space asteroid homes. Cicero sustains a spear wound to his thigh but refuses medical attention until the civilian triage is underway. Lysander views the rescue operation as a crucial part of his propaganda to win over the Rim’s population. He oversees the descent of hearthcraft, support ships filled with medical supplies and food, to the spaceport.

Lysander meets with his Praetorians and learns that several high-value prisoners have been recovered from the Arbor. Gaia au Raa, the grandmother of Diomedes, has survived her captivity but appears physically broken and aged. She and other Moon Lords demand weapons and immediately execute the surviving Ascomanni prisoners in the courtyard. Gaia thanks Lysander for his intervention, and the Moon Lords swear their indebtedness to him for saving Io. Lysander lies to Gaia about Diomedes's fate, claiming he did not see him during the battle on the Dustmaker.

### Chapter 80: Darrow: Stirring Stuff
The Archimedes receives a barrage of propaganda broadcasts from the Lightbringer detailing Lysander’s victory on Io. The footage shows Lysander rescuing civilians, liberating Gaia au Raa, and being embraced by the people he saved. Darrow reacts with fury at the effectiveness of the propaganda and the way Lysander has positioned himself as a hero. Lysander issues a public call for the children of Akari to join him in a joint force to attack Europa. He vows to bring Volsung Fá to justice, unaware that Darrow has already executed the Obsidian king.

Darrow believes Lysander is working for Atlas as the "Good Tyrant" intended to pacify the Rim through deceptive kindness. Cassius and Diomedes argue that Lysander’s presence might actually be a positive development if it stops the slaughter. Darrow counters that the Volk and the Daughters of Athena are still in danger because the Core ships are faster. He realizes that Atlas has planned for the Core fleet to arrive and finish off any surviving resistance. Diomedes points out that the Volk and the Daughters must unite their fleets to have any chance of survival.

Darrow and Diomedes decide to wait before making their next move to see if Atlas’s plan has any gaps. Diomedes suggests they travel to the surface of Io to find Gaia and reveal the truth about Lysander. They believe that showing the Raa the reality behind Lysander’s staged heroics is their best chance for leverage. Cassius accelerates the Archimedes toward Io, aiming to land while the Core forces are still busy with the occupation. Darrow asks what Lysander fears losing most, and Cassius responds that it is his reputation.
