### Chapter 51: Darrow: Midnight Lands
Darrow and his crew arrive at Io to find the moon devastated by Volsung Fá's forces. They wait in Jupiter's ring system for three days until the Volk navy departs for Callisto. Darrow observes that Demeter’s Garter, the moon's vital agricultural belt, is occupied by Ascomanni warships and haulers. To avoid detection, Cassius lands the Archimedes—a stealth ship—in a volcanic range southeast of Sungrave. Darrow and Sevro disembark in Godkiller armor, which features stealth, regular, and high-power modes.

The duo travels across the sulfurous plains of Io toward the city of Sungrave during a period of total eclipse called nivalnight. They pass the ruined city of Darkfall, where the atmospheric paradomes have been shattered, leaving the population exposed to Jupiter's radiation. Darrow notices that the temperature drops significantly during nivalnight, causing sulfur to frost over the landscape. Sevro remains uncharacteristically quiet during the journey, still processing the death of his infant son, Ulysses. High-altitude Ascomanni patrols circle above, but Darrow and Sevro use their armor's "reptile" stealth mode to remain undetected.

Darrow reflects on the strategic brilliance of Fá, who seized Io to control the food supply of the entire Rim. He compares Fá to a darker version of himself, noting the efficiency of the warlord’s brutal tactics. The travelers reach the Waste of Karrack, where they witness colorful auroras caused by particles flowing off Jupiter. Darrow worries that the resistance forces led by Athena may have already been wiped out by the Obsidian invasion. Despite his fears, he remains committed to reaching Sungrave to light Aurae’s emergency signal, known as an omega torch.



### Chapter 52: Darrow: Sungrave
Darrow and Sevro observe the remains of Sungrave from a ridgeline, noting a graveyard of destroyed war machines outside the city. Cassius and Aurae arrive shortly after on a gravBike, bringing the prisoner Diomedes au Raa. Diomedes is a high-ranking Gold of the Rim and a Peerless Scarred who was captured on the Archimedes. Cassius insists on bringing Diomedes so the man can witness the destruction of his home and possibly offer his parole. Aurae, a former Pink slave of the Raa family, warns Darrow that Diomedes remains extremely dangerous despite his current lack of armor.

The group enters Sungrave through a forty-meter-wide breach in the mountain fortress that was once deemed impregnable. They navigate through corridors filled with the frozen corpses of the city’s defenders, including Grays, Greens, and common citizens. Upon reaching the Spine, a massive central stairway, they discover the decapitated head of Abraxes, the Raa family's ancient sigil-beast. Darrow explains that the Obsidians likely ate the dragon's body as part of a ritual to absorb its power. The air inside the city is breathable but cold, smelling strongly of decomposition as the pulseFields remain partially functional.

Darrow divides the party, sending Sevro, Cassius, and Aurae to the market district to locate the omega torch. He stays behind with Diomedes, choosing to remove the man's helmet and bindings after receiving his word of parole. Diomedes is visibly devastated by the sight of his ruined home, showing none of his usual stoicism. Darrow hopes that treating the Raa heir with honor will lead to a future alliance against Volsung Fá. He leads Diomedes toward the Raa family compound to search for any surviving relatives among the ghosts of the city.



### Chapter 53: Darrow: Eyes of Stone
Darrow allows Diomedes to search the defiled Raa family compound alone as a test of the man's honor. Inside the ancestral sanctum, Diomedes finds the headless bodies of his young brothers, Marius and Paleron. Darrow waits in the grotto, collecting tissue samples from dead Peerless knights to analyze the Ascomanni poisons used during the siege. Meanwhile, Sevro and Aurae reach a hidden long-range communications station concealed within a stone perfumery. Aurae uses a needle-trigger to activate the omega torch and sends a message to the resistance leader, Athena.

Darrow and Diomedes engage in a tense conversation about the nature of war and the identity of their true enemy. Darrow argues that the goal of conflict should be peace rather than mutual destruction. He presents his theory that Volsung Fá is being guided by an unseen hand, citing the warlord's suspicious logistical precision. Diomedes counters by accusing Darrow of seeking to manipulate the Rim into providing ships and soldiers for Mars. The discussion is interrupted when Athena's station confirms receipt of Aurae’s message and instructs the group to stand by.

Cassius reports via comms that a group of Volk led by Jarl Skarde is capturing Pink and Violet children near a subterranean garage. Skarde is a former centurion of Darrow's who now serves Fá’s Obsidian Alltribe. Cassius refuses Darrow’s order to stay hidden, choosing to intervene and rescue the children from being taken as slaves. Sevro declines to assist Cassius, arguing that protecting the contact with Athena is more important than saving "Moonie" children. Darrow orders Lyria to fly the Archimedes to Sungrave for fire support before racing off to aid Cassius.



### Chapter 54: Darrow: Pella! Pella! Pella!
Darrow sprints through the tunnels of Sungrave and takes flight to reach Cassius's position outside the city gates. Cassius initiates a "pella maneuver," an airborne combat tactic where he ricochets between surfaces to kill enemies with his razor. The Obsidians recognize the move and form defensive wedges, a counter-tactic Darrow himself taught them years ago. Cassius is struck by a heavy hammer and grounded, facing six armored braves who prepare to execute him. Darrow arrives from the sky, killing three airborne Obsidians with his razor and pulseFist before they can react.

Darrow and Cassius fight side-by-side against the remaining Volk warriors, utilizing the superior speed and shields of their Godkiller armor. Darrow kills several more braves by using a falling body as cover and shifting between vertical levels of the battlefield. During the skirmish, Darrow's armor takes a significant hit from a rail slug, leaving a large dent in his chest plate. Two surviving Obsidians retreat toward their transport ship, and Cassius leaps into the hold just as the vessel takes off. Darrow lands on the exterior hull and hacks off the ship's communications array with his razor, Bad Lass.

The transport ship nosedives and crash-lands in a volcanic range after Darrow is bucked off the hull during the ascent. Darrow retrieves his blade from the wreckage just as a concussed Cassius emerges, having killed the crew inside. They discover that the Pink and Violet children in the hold survived the crash and are huddled in cages. The children express relief at their rescue, and Darrow feels a renewed sense of respect for Cassius’s selfless bravery. Their moment of victory is cut short as four Republic assault dropships arrive on the horizon to investigate the crash.



### Chapter 55: Darrow: Demigod
Four dropships carrying Obsidian aerial cavalry arrive and jam the Archimedes' long-range communications. Darrow realizes the reinforcements are led by Jarl Skarde, a greedy former officer of his who now scavenges for Fá. Rather than fleeing, Darrow decides to stand his ground and spears the heads of fallen Obsidians onto his razor to provoke Skarde. Skarde lands and demands the "Golds" surrender their high-tech armor for his personal collection. Darrow removes his helmet, exposing his face to the toxic air to reveal his true identity as the Reaper.

The Obsidians are paralyzed by the sight of Darrow, whom they believed was dead on the sands of Mercury. Skarde’s son, Sigurd, recognizes Darrow's blade and the platinum "rat" and "white fist" campaign medals on the warriors' armor. Darrow calls the braves by name and accuses them of breaking their oaths to the Republic and Ragnar Volarus. He declares ashvar—a formal Obsidian blood feud—against Volsung Fá for the murder of Sefi the Quiet. Skarde refuses to fight Darrow but acknowledges that Fá will stop at nothing to eliminate him.

Darrow demonstrates his lethality by killing three braves who attempt to attack him despite Sigurd’s calls for a truce. He tells Skarde that he has come to claim the right of the Valkyrie Spires to challenge Fá directly. The tension peaks as Skarde tries to justify his desertion by claiming the Rim is Darrow's enemy as well. Suddenly, a sniper attack from an unknown third party strikes the group, hitting Sigurd and killing a nearby brave. Cassius tackles Darrow to the ground as a chaotic firefight erupts on the volcanic plain.
