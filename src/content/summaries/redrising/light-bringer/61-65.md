### Chapter 61: Darrow: The Three Masters
Darrow and Diomedes are imprisoned in a stone cell where they find a bruised and shackled Cassius who was captured during a failed rescue mission on the Archimedes. Diomedes confesses that an imposter mimicking Helios’s physical appearance used the Cestus, a naval tactical device, to fire upon the Rim fleet. Darrow and Diomedes deduce that Atlas au Raa is the true Allfather of the Obsidians and that his creature, Volsung Fá, is leading the destruction of the Rim. They realize Atlas’s plan is to let the Rim burn so Lysander au Lune can arrive as a "snow-white savior" to restore order. Diomedes reveals that Lysander was likely the unarmed Gold who dragged him to a lifepod on the Dustmaker’s bridge before being pulled out. Cassius finds redemption in the news that Lysander mourned him, while Darrow fruitlessly shouts for Athena to release them so they can stop Fá.





### Chapter 62: Darrow: The Tyrants’ Debris
Athena and Aurae escort the prisoners to a war room where they view the Obsidian armada approaching the moon of Europa. Sevro appears wearing the Twilight Helm, a piece of high-tech camouflage gear that renders him invisible, and gives a speech invoking the legacy of Ares. He challenges the Daughters and Darrow to stop waiting for a savior and instead act as the light that breaks the current darkness. Darrow proposes a plan to kill Volsung Fá in an ashvar, an Obsidian duel of honor, to break the momentum of the invading army. Athena commutes the sentences of the prisoners and returns her black spear, Pyrphoros, to Darrow as a reminder of his oath to face judgment later. Diomedes accepts a new oath to champion the abolition of the Gold hierarchy in exchange for the Daughters opening the sealifts to save the people of the Rim.





### Chapter 63: Lyria: Mashed Taters
Lyria steals a Daughter uniform and gun polish to sneak past guards and gain entry into the command center where Darrow is planning the defense of Ilium. She finds Darrow eating and demands to be included in the mission, reminding him that he destroyed her home in the mines. Lyria proposes using Sigurd to infiltrate the Obsidian forces so she can reach Volga and convince her to turn against Volsung Fá. She argues that her small stature as a Red allows her to move unnoticed and that her personal connection to Volga is a unique strategic asset. Darrow is initially dismissive of her request, but Sevro supports the idea as a way to potentially gain evidence against Atlas. Sevro labels Lyria "Truffle Pig" and agrees to the plan despite the high risk of her being tortured or killed if caught.





### Chapter 64: Lysander: The Noble Lie
Lysander arrives at his expeditionary fleet near Valetudo in a battered shuttle and assumes his role as a survivor of the Kalyke massacre. He observes the Lightbringer, his flagship, which has been partially repaired and now displays the Lune crescent inside a gold pyramid on its hull. Lysander meets with his thirty-three thousand Praetorians and Reformer allies, using wounds inflicted by Atlas to bolster his false narrative of surviving the Obsidian attack. He manipulates the ranks into a "hunt" for Volsung Fá by appealing to their pride and their desire to defend civilization against chaos. Lysander orates to the entire fleet, claiming the enemy has razed Callisto with atomics and seized the Garter, a vital agricultural ring around Io. Later, in his private stateroom, Lysander lies to his close friend Pytha about the events at Kalyke to preserve her trust and his own image.





### Chapter 65: Lyria: Into the Maelstrom
Lyria travels through the Heraklion sealift where thousands of refugees are being funneled into the undercity to escape the surface bombardment. Cassius and Sigurd accompany her past suspicious Gray guards to a station overlooking the sea where a shuttle waits to take them to the Obsidian rendezvous. Sigurd reveals that Volsung Fá recently executed Krypteia assassins by skinning them and leashing them to his throne. As they walk through the crowded station, a giant warrior known as a Harbinger suddenly appears from a ghostly camouflage guise among the refugees. The Harbinger, a naked eight-foot-tall warrior in a shark-head helmet, begins slaughtering civilians with two massive blades. Cassius draws his razor and activates his pulse shield while Sigurd explains that the Harbingers must have bypassed sonar by swimming through the ocean.
