### Chapter 16: Lysander: The Two Hundred
Lysander attends a summit of the Two Hundred at the refurbished Colosseum on Earth. Atalantia strips House Carthii of their Venus dockyards by claiming them for the state and dispatching ten legions to secure them. Diomedes au Raa delivers a report on the heavy casualties sustained by the Rim forces during the recent campaigns. Dido au Raa replaces her son at the rostrum to accuse the Core Golds of exploiting Rim sacrifices while delaying the promised assault on Mars. Lysander observes the tension and identifies Atlas au Raa sitting above the assembly as a silent threat to any dissenters.

Atalantia dismisses Dido’s complaints and maintains that the reclamation of Luna must precede the invasion of Mars. She announces that Lysander will lead the vanguard of the Luna Rain in six months to restore House Lune to power. Diomedes begins a walkout of the Rim delegation in protest of the Core's continued prioritization of their own capitals. Lysander notices that many fence-sitting house heads are cowed by Atalantia’s presence and the lack of opposition from the Reformer bloc. Ajax au Grimmus watches from the wings, visibly relieved that Lysander has not yet challenged the Dictator’s plan.

Lysander experiences a moment of nausea thinking about his mentor Glirastes, who is currently being held in a dog kennel. He realizes that staying seated will lead to the permanent dissolution of the alliance between the Core and the Rim. Horatia au Votum gives Lysander a prearranged signal of three blinks to indicate that her preparations are complete. Ajax reaches out a hand to stop Lysander, but Lysander stands to interrupt the proceedings. He ignores the risk to his own safety to prevent the Rim delegation from leaving the Colosseum.


### Chapter 17: Lysander: Mars Must Fall
Lysander stops the Rim delegation's exit by shouting a challenge and invoking his right of interjection as a descendant of a Conqueror. Julia au Bellona validates the interjection after Horatia's Reformer bloc provides the necessary thirty supporting votes. Lysander delivers a speech criticizing the Gold caste for its internal greed and for becoming wolves rather than shepherds. He warns the assembly that failing to unite will lead to an inevitable civil war between the Core and the Rim. He nominates Apollonius au Rath to lead an immediate campaign against Mars instead of following Atalantia's plan for Luna.

Horatia reveals that her brother Cicero has brokered a ceasefire on Venus between House Rath and House Carthii. A holocube recording shows Apollonius and Valeria au Carthii confirming the end of their blood feud and the restoration of the dockyards. Valeria explains that her father Asmodeus is dead and that House Carthii now supports the unified push for Mars. Apollonius pledges his legions to the cause and calls for the destruction of Darrow and the Republic. The majority of the Two Hundred, including the Rim delegation and Julia au Bellona, signal their support for the Mars resolution.

Atalantia accepts the defeat of her political strategy but announces she will remain on Luna with her own fleet. She mocks Lysander's supposed honor and warns him that he will sacrifice many more friends in the future. Ajax breaks his allegiance to Atalantia by joining the assembly in the horns gesture of support for the Mars campaign. Atalantia orders the execution of Glirastes as she exits the Colosseum, taunting Lysander with the leather made from his skin. Lysander and Ajax watch as Atlas au Raa flies away from the top of the Colosseum on skipBoots.


### Chapter 18: Virginia: The Returned
Virginia sprints to the executive wing of the Citadel of Agea to meet four survivors who have just arrived from Venus. She finds Harnassus, Screwface, Thraxa au Telemanus, and Colloway Char emaciated and battle-worn in the executive lounge. Screwface breaks down in tears during Virginia's embrace, revealing the psychological toll of his three-year infiltration of the Ash Legions. Victra au Julii enters the room and confirms to Virginia that both Darrow and Sevro are still alive. Virginia learns that they were responsible for the recent tactical maneuvers observed over the planet Venus.

The group reviews sensor data showing a massive enemy fleet amassing at Eros, a Mars-crosser asteroid. Colloway identifies the largest vessel in the enemy fleet as the Morning Star, Darrow's former flagship. Virginia realizes that Lysander has officially arrived at the war front and has likely taken command of the vessel. Victra smiles at the prospect of killing Lysander and agrees to stay and defend Mars instead of launching a rescue. Virginia orders the immediate recall of all Republic ships within a week's sail to prepare for the invasion.

Harnassus and the other survivors volunteer for active duty despite their physical exhaustion and recent trauma. Virginia acknowledges that the enemy has successfully blinded the Republic's telescope and sensor networks through electronic warfare. She worries about the effectiveness of the Rim's nimble ships against her own defensive drones and scouts. Victra and Virginia decide to prioritize the defense of their home planet over searching for their husbands. The survivors snap to attention, providing Virginia with a much-needed boost in morale and military leadership.


### Chapter 19: Virginia: Rising Dirge
Virginia participates in a symbolic ceremony at the Lykos mines where Darrow’s mother, Deanna, addresses a massive crowd. Deanna recounts the names of her family members who were hanged at the Lykos gallows by the Society. The crowd repeats the names Lorna, Dale, Eo, and Darrow as a collective act of remembrance and defiance. Deanna declares that the people of Mars are no longer dreamers or mothers but are now "swords" and "wrath." Fifty Reds from Lambda use power hammers to smash the gallows to pieces in front of the assembly.

Deanna wipes the Gold sigils of Virginia and Victra with red Martian mud to symbolize their unity with the Red cause. Thousands of citizens perform the Fading Dirge by pounding their chests in a rhythmic heartbeat to mourn their dead. Virginia and Victra ascend to the surface to find two dreadnaughts and thirty thousand legionnaires of the Pegasus Legion. Thraxa au Telemanus leads the legion while wearing a white wolfpelt to signal her readiness for the coming battle. Victra delivers a speech to the troops, urging them to act as wolves and make the enemy remember the Free Legions.

Victra reflects on the murder of her infant son Ulysses and the sale of her husband Sevro into slavery. She tells Virginia that her grief has transformed into a pure and unstoppable wrath against the approaching Society fleet. Virginia expresses her fear that she and Darrow will not be fighting side by side during this final stand. Victra cups Virginia's face in a rare gesture of sisterly affection before flying up to her flagship, the Pandemonia. Virginia boards her own flagship, the Dejah Thoris, and prepares for the enemy to pierce the orbital defense shell.


### Chapter 20: Virginia: Nucleus
Virginia arrives at the Nucleus, the military nerve center of Phobos, as the enemy armada approaches Mars. She wears a black battle crown that links her directly to the moon’s orbital defense systems and sensor feeds. The Republic’s fleet is divided into three task forces led by Victra, Niobe au Telemanus, and Colloway Char. Phobos orbits Mars every eight hours and serves as the primary anchor for the two-layered orbital battle-station complex. Virginia observes the enemy's formation, which consists of six battlegroups led by Helios, Dido, Diomedes, Julia, Apollonius, and Cicero.

The Morning Star, now renamed the Lightbringer, trails behind the enemy battlegroups as a massive but seemingly under-equipped flagship. Blue technicians report that the Lightbringer has low reactor output and fewer than a third of its surface guns. Virginia suspects the ship is being used by Lysander as a hollow symbol to lure her fleet out of position. Kavax au Telemanus notes that Nine unpainted destroyers surrounding the Lightbringer appear to be new vessels from the Venus dockyards. The enemy armada unexpectedly reduces its velocity, puzzling Virginia and her tactical officers about their intended approach vector.

Virginia worries about potential internal sabotage despite Holiday’s assurances that all vital installations have been swept and guarded. She observes that the enemy has sacrificed their speed advantage to spend more time within the range of the OBC guns. The Republic forces maintain a defensive posture to maximize the effectiveness of their static gun batteries and minefields. Virginia feels a sense of dread about playing defense while the enemy dictates the timing of the engagement. She remains on the command deck of the Nucleus, waiting for the first shots of the battle to be fired.
