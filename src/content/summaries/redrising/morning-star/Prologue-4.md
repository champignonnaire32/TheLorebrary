### Prologue
Darrow rises in a ship away from the garden of his betrayal. The Golden man killed by Darrow lies dead on the cold metal deck. Adrius, known as the Jackal, puts an eyeless mask and muzzle on Darrow's face. Adrius tells Darrow he is alone and that this is how the story ends. Roque sits in the corner weeping for his own losses rather than for Darrow.

Darrow reflects on the shackled Colors, the caste-based classes of society, across the worlds ruled by Gold. He thinks of Sevro, Ragnar, and Dancer as sources of remaining hope. The ship rumbles as it departs while the Telemanuses, a loyal and physically massive Golden family, attempt a failed rescue from orbit. Darrow identifies himself as the Reaper, a title signifying his role as a revolutionary leader. The Jackal's words are quiet and distant as he completes Darrow's immobilization.

Autumn wind whips Darrow's hair before the mask shuts out his sight completely. He refuses to let his silence be the end of his narrative. Rage burns within the hollow space the Jackal has carved in his soul. Darrow remains paralyzed but mentally defiant against his captor's assertions. The chapter concludes with his internal declaration that this is not how it ends.



### Chapter 1: Only the Dark
Darrow is imprisoned in a ball in a pitch-black stone cell. He is naked with his hands cuffed behind his back. Yellows, the medical and scientific Color of society, have threaded tubes into his gut for nutrients. Time passes without measure as Darrow tracks his breath and heartbeat to remain sane. He experiences hallucinations and voices mocking his failures and his love for Mustang.

Darrow recites ancient literature in various languages to maintain his mental faculties. He slams his head against the stone to stop the voices of self-doubt. The Jackal previously offered him a comfortable life if he would say "I am broken." This bargain required Darrow to sacrifice his family to the prisoners of the fortress. Darrow's family is held in the bowels of the Attica fortress, the Jackal's primary stronghold on Mars.

He believes the Vale, the Red afterlife, is a lie told to comfort the starving. Darrow attempts to commit suicide by repeatedly smashing his skull against the stone wall. He screams at the darkness as he tries to deliver a final killing blow. A sudden earthquake-like rumbling interrupts his attempt to end his life. A blazing sword of light slashes down through the cracking darkness of his cell.



### Chapter 2: Prisoner L17L6363
The ceiling of the cell opens and the floor rises to reveal Darrow on a table. Darrow discovers his cell was a hollow space inside a meter-wide marble table. The Jackal has been eating dinner directly above Darrow for nine months. Cassius au Bellona, now the Morning Knight, stands among the guests in white and gold armor. Aja au Grimmus, the Sovereign's Fury, is present to take Darrow for dissection.

Antonia au Severus and several Boneriders, the Jackal's elite killers, watch the spectacle. Darrow's body is skeletal, pallid, and matted with filth and scars. Cassius drapes his white cloak over Darrow's shivering, naked body. The Jackal identifies Darrow as "Homo flammeus," a new classification for his carved race. Lilath, a bald Bonerider, slurps hummingbird eggs while mocking the death of Lorn au Arcos.

The Jackal reveals that Mickey the Carver is believed to be alive in Tinos. Aja informs the Jackal that she is taking her Thirteenth Legion back to Luna. The Jackal demonstrates his control by showing a Pink servant, a Color raised for pleasure, who has no tongue. He forces the Pink, named Calliope, to hold a gun to his head to prove her fear. The chapter ends with the Jackal's declaration that fear and extermination are the only methods.



### Chapter 3: Snakebite
A Gray soldier named Danto shaves Darrow's head with an electric razor. Twelve lurchers, specialized urban combat Grays, drag Darrow through the halls using an electric collar. They pin Darrow in a corner and blast him with a high-pressure power hose. Darrow is dressed in a black prisoner's jumpsuit and restrained in a metal chair. The group includes three Obsidian men, the massive warrior Color of the hierarchy.

A Yellow doctor examines Darrow and notes his abnormally strong circulatory system. Danto stomps on Darrow's foot, splitting his toenail to inflict casual pain. Holiday ti Nakamura and her brother Trigg, disguised as Grays, suddenly open fire. They execute the centurion and the other dragoons with silenced slug shooters. Trigg shoots Danto between the eyes and secures the processing room.

Holiday identifies themselves as members of the Sons of Ares, a revolutionary group, sent to rescue Darrow. Trigg returns Darrow's razor, a shape-shifting weapon capable of becoming a whip or a blade. A holographic message from Sevro confirms the rescue and reveals Darrow's family is safe. Holiday injects "snakebite," a potent and painful stimulant, directly into Darrow's heart. The drug causes Darrow's body to explode with kinetic energy, enabling him to move.



### Chapter 4: Cell 2187
Holiday and Trigg escort a hooded Darrow through the prison toward a gravLift, a vertical transportation platform. Vixus, a Gold who participated in the Triumph massacre, enters the elevator. The Grays claim Darrow is an Obsidian prisoner being traded for a package. Darrow recognizes Vixus's voice and realizes the Gold will eventually identify him. Darrow breaks his shackles, draws his razor, and stabs Vixus in the shoulder.

Trigg forces Vixus to drop his weapon by jamming a slug shooter into his mouth. Vixus reveals that Victra au Julii is alive in cell 2187 on level 23. Darrow insists on rescuing Victra despite Holiday's warnings about the evacuation window. They reach the cell where Victra is being tortured with constant light and noise. Victra is emaciated and paralyzed from the gunshots she took during the betrayal.

She attacks Darrow in a feral state until Trigg suppresses her with tranquilizers. Trigg carries the unconscious Victra while Holiday prepares for the final escape. Darrow returns to the cell to confront Vixus, who begs for his life. Darrow slashes Vixus's throat to send a message to the Jackal. Alarm sirens begin to wail throughout the citadel as the group departs.
