### Chapter 5: Plan C
Darrow, Victra, and the siblings are trapped in an elevator as the Jackal's men override the controls. Holiday and Trigg realize they are being redirected to a floor where guards and Obsidians, engineered giants, await. Trigg initiates Plan C by contacting a secure frequency and requesting secondary extraction from the Sons of Ares. Holiday prepares a spherical EMP device, a highly illegal technology that generates a massive electromagnetic pulse to disable electronics. The siblings equip themselves with antique gunpowder weapons that will remain functional after the pulse disables modern pulse weaponry.

When the elevator doors open, Holiday detonates the EMP, disabling the lights and all modern pulse guns in the vicinity. The siblings step out and systematically slaughter the defenseless Grays and Obsidians using their archaic firearms at point-blank range. Holiday uses an antique grenade to blow a hole in the ceiling, raining plaster and debris onto the enemy squads. Darrow carries the unconscious Victra through the carnage as the surviving Grays flee from the expert marksmen. The group navigates through darkened marble halls past the Jackal's collection of historical artifacts and stolen treasures.

They reach a service door and emerge onto a metal walkway on the side of the mountain fortress. A paved bridge connects the mountain to a circular concrete landing platform that hangs over a thousand-meter drop. Holiday deploys green smoke canisters to signal their extraction point to the approaching rebel rescuers. Trigg plants six proximity mines along the bridge to slow down the pursuing forces of the Jackal. Darrow huddles with the wounded Victra in the snow while the wind howls around the exposed landing pad.



### Chapter 6: Victims
A defensive shield activates over the city, preventing any friendly ships from entering the airspace to rescue the group. The Jackal's Grays find backup weapons and pin Trigg down on the bridge with heavy, sustained gunfire. Holiday is shot in the ribs and must manually inject herself with a stimshot because her armor's systems are disabled. Aja au Grimmus, a deadly Olympic Knight, emerges from the fortress and pursues Trigg across the bridge. Aja catches Trigg and impales him through the torso with her razor, hoisting his twitching body into the air.

Holiday tries to cover her brother with her rifle, but her weapon runs out of ammunition. Aja taunts Darrow and then tosses Trigg's body off the bridge to fall hundreds of meters onto a granite ledge. Cassius joins Aja on the bridge and calls for Darrow to surrender so he can be brought to justice. Darrow drags the unconscious Holiday and Victra to the far edge of the landing pad as enemy reinforcements approach. Cassius tells Darrow he cannot escape because the sky is blocked and no ships can reach the platform.

Darrow refuses to surrender and reminds Cassius of the family members the Golds have murdered in the past. A groaning sound rises from the valley floor as a clawDrill, a massive mining machine with a titanic metal hand, gnaws through the rock. Darrow tells Cassius to expect him before pushing off the edge of the landing platform into the void. He flings himself backward into the open air, dragging Victra and Holiday with him into the thousand-meter drop. The three fall toward the dark city below as they descend toward the mining machine's newly carved tunnel.



### Chapter 7: Bumblebees
The three fall toward a massive clawDrill that has erupted through the streets of the snow-covered city. The mining machine creates a titanic hole in the ground and then sinks back into the earth to reveal a tunnel. Darrow loses his grip on Victra as the ground rushes up to meet them at terminal velocity during the fall. Dozens of armored figures in gravBoots, specialized boots that allow flight and hovering, scream up from the tunnel. Sevro, wearing a spiked helmet and a black wolfcloak, catches Victra seconds before she impacts a skyscraper.

Ragnar Volarus, a massive Obsidian in white armor, catches Darrow and Holiday in mid-air and secures them. The Howlers, a squad of elite rebels wearing wolfcloaks, provide cover fire with shoulder-mounted miniguns as they ascend. Ragnar secures Darrow behind his neck and dives into the darkness of the clawDrill's tunnel to escape. The group flees into the pitch-black depths while being pursued by enemy pulsefire from a RipWing starfighter. Darrow's shoulder is burned by a near-miss bolt of superheated energy as Ragnar weaves through the rocky passages.

A pulsefire bolt destroys the gravBoots of the rebel flying ahead of Darrow, causing the man to crash into the ceiling. Darrow sees red light throbbing through his eyelids as he regains consciousness on a rebel transport ship. He observes surgeons using glowing saws to cut through the melted armor of dying men to free them. A young rebel boy lying next to Darrow mouths the Reaper's name before passing away from his injuries. Darrow succumbs to his wounds and exhaustion as the ship maneuvers deeper into the belly of the planet.



### Chapter 8: Home
Darrow experiences a dream of walking through a Vale-like landscape while hearing his Uncle Narol's voice in the distance. He wakes up in a medical bed in Tinos, the hidden city of the Sons of Ares built inside the rock. Narol sits by the bedside, appearing grizzled and sunburned with a slingBlade sewn onto his black military jacket. Darrow's mother is also present, having survived her stroke and the Jackal's potential threats during his imprisonment. She explains that Darrow has been unconscious for two days following his rescue from the city of Attica.

Narol explains that he led the drillteam that punched into the city to retrieve Darrow, Victra, and Holiday. Dancer arrives and informs Darrow that Harmony was the one who betrayed him to the Jackal. Harmony believed the rebellion was compromising too much and revealed Fitchner's identity as the leader of Ares. Dancer reveals that the Golds staged a fake execution of a Darrow look-alike nine months ago to demoralize the public. The rebels had believed Darrow was dead until Sevro's insistence and Theodora's intelligence network led to the rescue mission.

Darrow meets his brother Kieran and Eo's sister Dio, who are now married and living in the rebel city. He holds their infant daughter, noting how the child is unaware of the scars and war surrounding their family. Kieran describes the improved living conditions in Tinos compared to the mines, including the luxury of water showers. Narol and Kieran express their hope that Darrow's return means he has a plan to save the failing rebellion. Darrow lies to his brother, claiming he has a plan, while acknowledging the darkness of their current situation.



### Chapter 9: The City of Ares
Sevro and Ragnar arrive at Darrow's family dormitory to take him to the hangar for a briefing. Sevro reveals that he has had Darrow's original red eyes surgically implanted into his own head by Mickey. Ragnar, who has had his teeth replaced and learned to read, pushes Darrow's wheelchair through the stone corridors. The group enters a massive hangar carved into the rock by laser drills to house the rebel fleet. Darrow sees several ripWings, fast and agile rebel starfighters, being serviced by teams of Reds and Oranges.

Darrow reunites with Howlers Pebble and Clown, who are wounded but in high spirits after the rescue. They pass a transport ship where the floor is being hosed down to remove the blood of fallen soldiers. Darrow discovers that Tinos is built into an inverted forest of stalactites hanging over a massive underground cave. Below the stalactites, a refugee camp holds nearly half a million people from mines the Jackal has purged. Sevro explains that the Jackal is using the Liquidation Protocol to pump poisonous gas into suspected rebel mines.

The refugee camp suffers from disease, dwindling rations, and violent internal riots between different Red clans. Sevro mentions that Ragnar is extremely popular among the refugees for his role in stopping violent riots. Darrow observes the makeshift symbols of different clans and the overall misery of the sprawling subterranean city. He realizes the rebellion is struggling with crime, murders, and a critical lack of medical supplies. Sevro finishes the tour by revealing that Cassius is the one who killed his father, Fitchner au Barca.
