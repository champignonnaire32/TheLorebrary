# Chapter 65-Epilogue Summary


### Chapter 65: The Vale
In the weeks after the bombing of Luna, Mustang becomes the Sovereign and many Gold ships defect to her side. The Ash Lord retreats to Mercury with his remaining loyal forces as the fleet fractures. Mustang disbands the Senate and dissolves the Board of Quality Control to dismantle the Color Hierarchy. Darrow recovers from his injuries and has his severed hand reattached by the carvers Mickey and Virany. Sefi departs for Mars to begin settling her Obsidian people on warmer lands set aside for them.

Darrow says farewell to Cassius, who decides to leave Luna rather than join the new government. Cassius takes Lysander au Lune, the young grandson of the former Sovereign, with him into exile. Sevro believes leaving Lysander alive is a mistake, but Darrow chooses to let him go to prove the world has changed. Cassius returns the ring belonging to Julian au Bellona to Darrow as a final gesture of brotherhood. Darrow and Mustang travel to Earth to visit a rain forest on the Pacific Rim.

Mustang reveals that she has been testing Darrow's ability to build a world rather than just destroy one. A ship lands on the beach carrying Kavax, Dancer, Victra, Sevro, and Darrow's mother. Darrow’s mother walks onto the sand and carries a laughing child with golden hair, Pax, in her arms. Mustang introduces the child to Darrow, revealing that he is their biological son. The boy is named Pax, and Darrow finally meets him for the first time on the shores of Earth.





### Epilogue
Pax was born nine months after the Lion’s Rain while Darrow was imprisoned by the Jackal. Mustang kept her pregnancy and the child's existence a secret to protect him from their many enemies. The child was raised in the asteroid belt under the protection of Kavax’s wife during the war. Darrow’s mother also knew about the boy but agreed to keep the secret so Darrow would remain focused on the revolution. Mustang wanted to ensure Darrow had become a man who could be a father before introducing him to his son.

Darrow reflects on the fact that he is no longer alone and has friends to help carry Eo’s dream. He acknowledges that the war is not fully over and that the Ash Lord still poses a threat from Mercury. The sacrifices made to take Luna remain a heavy burden on the new world they are building. Darrow realizes he can now be both a sword for his people and a father to his child. He credits Eo and Fitchner for believing in his potential to be a builder of a new society.

Darrow feels the presence of his fallen friends and family within his heart as he looks at Pax. He plans to tell his son about the rage of Ares and the strength of the Obsidian warrior Ragnar. He will teach Pax about the honor of Cassius and the loyalty shown by Victra and Sevro. The stories will include the dream of Eo, the Red girl whose sacrifice started the entire uprising. Darrow embraces his new role as a leader who will create a world fit for future generations.
