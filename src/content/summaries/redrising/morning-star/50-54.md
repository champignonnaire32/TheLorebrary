### 50: Thunder and Lightning
Darrow manages the aftermath of the naval battle, having seized a quarter of the Sword Armada and destroyed over half. He sends Victra and Thraxa in corvettes to recapture Kavax from Antonia’s forces. Darrow conducts a holo-conference with Romulus and lies about finding nuclear weapons on the Colossus to secure the Moon Lords' independence. Romulus demands that the Rising fleet leave the boundaries of the Jovian moons within three days. The fleet performs repairs and searches debris for survivors before departing toward the asteroid belt.

Darrow and Mustang secretly move Roque’s body to a metal torpedo-casket for a sun burial. Cassius attends the ceremony in magnetic manacles and kisses Roque’s forehead before Sevro slams the lid shut. Darrow presses a button to launch the casket into the sun as his friends recite the phrase "Per aspera, ad astra." Later that night, Darrow enters the Imperator’s stateroom, which still contains Roque’s personal books and trophies. He searches the ship’s computer and finds a menu of recently played videos from their time at the Institute.

Darrow summons Cassius to the lounge, where the prisoner arrives wearing forty-kilogram magnetic boots. The two men drink Lagavulin scotch, a gift from Lorn au Arcos, and discuss their fathers. They watch hologram recordings of their younger selves and fallen friends like Quinn and Lea at the Institute. Cassius notes that he, Darrow, and Roque were the ones who truly killed Roque through their ongoing war. Darrow hands Cassius a holocube to watch in his cell before the prisoner is escorted away by the Sons of Ares.





### 51: Pandora
Sevro wakes Darrow to report that Victra has engaged Antonia’s forces within the asteroid belt. Darrow, Mustang, and the Howlers travel on a Telemanus torchShip, a lean and fast vessel, to reach the coordinates. Victra sends a message confirming she has captured Antonia and freed Kavax from her custody. Upon arrival, Darrow finds Victra commanding fifty black ships, which are fast, nimble vessels belonging to House Julii. Victra stands on the deck of her new flagship, the Pandora, wearing a black uniform with a spear-pierced sun emblem.

Kavax greets Mustang with a fox named Sophocles on his chest despite having a cast on his arm. Victra explains that she secured the fleet because Antonia’s paid allies surrendered once they believed Darrow’s main fleet was present. She has already executed three disloyal Praetors and imprisoned the others in the brig. Sevro questions Victra’s commitment to the Rising now that she has regained her family's property and empire. Victra responds that she still lacks her revenge because Antonia is still alive.

Darrow enters the brig and finds Antonia sitting in a cell with bruised cheeks and black-nailed hands. He discovers Thistle, a former Howler and "Bronzie," in the adjacent cell with artificially lightened hair and eyes. Darrow demands information on the Jackal’s troop positions, supply routes, and the location of nuclear weapons. He issues an ultimatum stating that only the prisoner who provides the most information will be allowed to live. Thistle attempts to apologize for her betrayal, but Darrow reminds her that she also betrayed Sevro and the other Howlers.





### 52: Teeth
Darrow and his lieutenants watch the brig monitors while the temperature in the cells is raised to forty degrees Celsius. Thistle weeps and expresses her intent to tell Darrow everything she knows about the Jackal’s plans. Antonia manipulates Thistle by claiming that Darrow will execute her once she is no longer useful for information. She suggests that Thistle needs to withhold facts to barter for her life incrementally. As Thistle approaches the bars to speak to the camera, Antonia grabs her hair and jerks her head against the metal.

Antonia kills Thistle by repeatedly smashing the top of the Howler’s skull against the cell bars. Victra breaks into the cell and beats Antonia into unconsciousness, shattering her nose and orbital bone. Sevro tackles Victra and restrains her on the floor to prevent her from killing her sister. Darrow feels Thistle’s pulse and confirms she is dead before Mustang calls for a medical team. Victra leaves the brig in tears, pulling one of Antonia’s broken teeth from her own bloodied knuckles.

The Sons of Ares search Thistle’s suite and find a cured wolf fur hidden under a false cabinet bottom. Sevro and the original Howlers gather to mourn their former friend and reflect on their time at the Institute. Victra tells Sevro that she is "poison" and ends their romantic relationship despite his confession of love. Sevro expresses his fear that the Rising is outnumbered and that the Jackal has a secret weapon prepared. Mustang tells Darrow that they must change the paradigm to survive the coming conflict with the Core.





### 53: Silence
The fleet remains in the asteroid belt while ship doctors use forceps to repair Antonia’s crushed nasal cavity. Antonia cooperates during interrogation and reveals that the Jackal is gathering a fleet around Phobos. She claims there is a mass exodus of patricians from Mars toward refugee centers on Luna and Venus. Darrow attempts to access Quicksilver’s digital dataDrop but finds that the Society is jamming all long-range frequencies. Holiday joins Darrow on the bridge of the Pandora and shares whiskey from a tin coffee mug.

Holiday discusses her brother Trigg and his fiancé Ephraim, a pretty-boy specialist in high-value property recovery from Luna. A single broadcast from Mars breaks through the interference and displays a recording of an interrogation room. The Jackal enters the frame holding a pistol and stands behind Darrow’s Uncle Narol, who is shackled to a chair. Narol attempts to speak to Darrow before the Jackal pulls the trigger and shoots him in the back of the head. Darrow realizes the recording is three weeks old and orders the bridge of every ship to be sealed.

The video of the execution leaks to the fleet, triggering immediate riots and violence between different Colors. Reds on the ship Persephone kill three Golds, while Mustang’s lieutenants kill two Obsidians who attempted a takeover. Mustang reports that she is trapped on the Morning Star while trying to evacuate seven Gold logistical support personnel. She informs Darrow that a mob has stormed the prison and intends to execute Cassius and other highColor prisoners. Darrow learns that Sefi is leading the insurrection and orders the Pandora to travel at maximum thrust to the main fleet.





### 54: The Goblin and the Gold
Darrow lands his shuttle on the Morning Star and meets the Telemanus family, who have arrived armed for battle. Kavax ignores Darrow’s orders and insists on rescuing Mustang from the "savages" who have seized control. Darrow convinces Kavax to wait while he attempts to negotiate with the mob to prevent a total fleet collapse. Inside the hangar, twenty-five thousand people watch as Sefi hangs seven Golds from a twenty-meter-high walkway. The victims are suspended by rubber cable ligatures and have had their scalps and tongues removed.

Sefi drags a beaten Cassius to the edge of the walkway and prepares to carve out his tongue with a knife. Sevro interrupts the execution by slamming down on the bridge wearing his spiked Ares helmet of war. He claims Cassius is his personal property because the Bellona killed his father, Fitchner. Sevro forces Cassius to publicly admit to the murder before pushing him off the railing with a cable around his neck. The crowd roars as Cassius gags and kicks while suspended above the hangar floor.

Sevro announces to the mob that he is also a murderer and asks what should be done to him. He wraps a cable around his own neck and backflips off the railing to hang alongside Cassius. Sefi uses her axe to cut both cables, allowing the crowd to catch the two men before they suffocate. Sevro stands and gives a speech about hope, forgiveness, and the shared dream of a free world. He publicly forgives Cassius and declares that he is no longer afraid, effectively ending the riot.
