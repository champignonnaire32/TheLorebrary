### Chapter 30: The Quiet
Ragnar lies dying in the snow with his stomach cut open by Aja. Darrow attempts to stop the bleeding, but a medical resGun is ineffective against the severe wounds. Mustang watches the hillside as Aja flees from an approaching threat. A griffin, a giant genetically engineered predator with a lion's body and an eagle's head, lands near the group. Sefi the Quiet, Ragnar’s sister and leader of the Valkyrie, dismounts with her warriors to find her dying brother.

Ragnar recognizes Sefi and speaks to her of their childhood before his strength fails. Sefi identifies Darrow by his slingBlade and realizes he is the leader Ragnar served. Ragnar tells Darrow in the Gold language that he must kill Alia Snowsparrow, his own mother. Sefi offers Ragnar an axe so he can die as a warrior, but he rejects it. Darrow promises to free the Obsidian people and build a house for Ragnar in the Vale.

Darrow uses his razor to pierce Ragnar’s heart and end his suffering. Ragnar dies holding Sefi’s hand and a handful of snow. The Valkyrie rappels into the crevasse to search for Aja but finds no sign of her. Sefi remains silent as she mourns over her brother's corpse. The group prepares to move as enemy cannibals begin to gather in the distance.

**



### Chapter 31: The Pale Queen
The Valkyrie transport Darrow, Mustang, and Holiday to the Valkyrie Spires on the backs of griffins. The Spires are a series of artificial mountain peaks created through tectonic manipulation by Gold engineers. Cassius is taken away by Obsidian guards to be treated for his neck wound. Darrow and his companions are shackled in iron manacles and thrown into a stone cell. Mustang picks the locks on their chains using a shard of bone.

The prisoners are brought before Alia Snowsparrow, the Queen of the Valkyrie, who sits with Ragnar’s body. Alia speaks HighLingo, the sacred language of the Golds, revealing her status as a favored servant. She describes the rebellion as a heresy and labels Ragnar’s return as a betrayal of the gods. Darrow introduces himself as a brother to Ragnar and offers a diplomatic alliance. Alia rejects the offer, calling Darrow a "little elf" and a maker of chains.

Mustang challenges Alia by stating that the Golds are not gods but flesh and blood. Alia reveals she already knows the Golds are mortal but chooses to maintain the lie to protect her people. She views the Golds as mortals with the power of gods who must be obeyed to ensure survival. Alia orders Sefi to take the prisoners to Asgard in chains to be judged by the Golds. Darrow realizes Alia is a tyrant who will never willingly join the Rising.

**



### Chapter 32: No Man’s Land
The Valkyrie fly the prisoners toward Asgard, a black mountain fortress suspended in the air. Asgard serves as the seat of the Aesir, the Gold rulers who oversee the Obsidian tribes. Below the fortress lies the Valley of the Fallen, filled with the frozen corpses of those who failed the trials. Sefi lands the griffins at the Shadowmouth Temple, the entrance to the Way of Stains. Darrow asks to speak with Sefi alone in a private courtyard within the temple.

Darrow tells Sefi that all Obsidians who leave the ice become slaves to the Golds. He explains that Ragnar was treated like a prized dog and forced to fight in pits for entertainment. Darrow accuses Alia of selling her own family members into bondage for centuries. He asks Sefi to honor her brother’s sacrifice by helping him expose the false gods. Sefi considers the information and chooses to unlock Darrow’s chains with an iron key.

The group disguises themselves as Valkyrie using bone masks and riding cloaks provided by Sefi's loyalists. They climb the long staircase of the Way of Stains to reach the Gold complex. At the top, they encounter a gnarled black tree with white blossoms that burn with cold fire. A Violet priest emerges from a temple shaped like a screaming face to greet the "Valkyrie." Darrow lures the priest closer by claiming a ship has fallen from the sky.

**



### Chapter 33: Gods and Men
The Violet priest leads the group into a black stone antechamber where they wait for the gods. Two Golds, Freya and Loki, deactivate their ghostCloaks—technology that renders the wearer nearly invisible—to address them. Loki wears a metallic wolf visage, while Freya appears in the likeness of a goddess. Darrow maintains his disguise and describes the crash of a ship with "soft-faced" men. The Golds move closer, their pulseShields creating visible ripples in the air as they investigate the news.

Freya reaches for Darrow’s razor, which is wrapped in seal skin to hide its true form. Mustang removes her mask and identifies herself as a member of House Augustus to distract them. Darrow seizes his razor and decapitates Freya before she can activate her pulseFist. He hurls the blade at Loki, and Mustang kicks the hilt to drive it through the Gold’s armor. Loki is revealed to be Proctor Mercury, a former instructor from the Institute.

The Valkyrie watch in shock as the blood of the "gods" pools on the floor. Darrow forces Mercury to remove his helmet, proving he is a mortal man with physical injuries. Sefi touches the blood and realizes it is not divine ichor but human fluid. Darrow gives Sefi a razor and teaches her how to toggle its forms between a whip and a blade. The group prepares to hunt the remaining five Golds hiding within the Asgard complex.

**



### Chapter 34: Godkillers
Darrow and his team systematically capture the remaining Golds on Asgard using stolen pulseArmor and weaponry. They secure a shuttle and use Gold access codes to bypass the fortress's remaining security systems. Victra contacts Darrow to report that the Sons of Ares have seized a third of the Martian fleet. The group returns to the Valkyrie Spires with seven Golds, two dead and five in chains. Darrow melts the iron doors of the throne room with a pulse cannon to announce his return.

Sefi drags the Gold captives before Alia Snowsparrow and the assembled warchiefs of the Spires. Darrow publicly declares that the gods are false and displays the corpses of the fallen Peerless Scarred. Alia labels the display as blasphemy and orders her warriors to kill the intruders. Sefi steps forward and decapitates her mother with a razor before the warchiefs can act. Sefi speaks her first words in twenty-five years, confirming that Alia knew the truth.

Sefi crowns herself Queen of the Valkyrie and opens her mother’s fabled warchest. She retrieves Throgmir, a massive double-headed axe made of duroSteel, and executes the remaining Gold captives. One Gold woman recites a poem by Aeschylus as a battle cry before she is slain. Sefi removes the tongues of the dead Golds and calls the tribes to war against the false gods. The Obsidian warchiefs chant in unison as they prepare to join the Rising.
