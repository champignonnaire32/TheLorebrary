### 35: Part III: Glory - Chapter 35: The Light
Darrow travels with Sefi to the Obsidian tribes to announce the fall of Asgard. Sefi and twenty Valkyrie begin training with gravBoots and pulse weapons under Darrow and Holiday’s instruction. Obsidian leaders visit the conquered mountain to view the preserved corpses of the Golds they once worshipped as gods. Most clans accept the truth and join Darrow’s cause, while some warchiefs commit suicide in shame. Darrow earns the title Morning Star as his legend spreads among the traditionally warring Obsidian tribes.

Mustang leads counterintelligence efforts to hide the Obsidian uprising from the Board of Quality Control in Agea. Quicksilver provides hackers to mask the movement of hundreds of thousands of people across the Antarctic. Mustang conceives a plan to relocate the entire pole population to subterranean tunnels in twelve hours. Thousands of merchant shuttles and Sons of Ares ships are utilized for the massive troop and civilian movement. Darrow, Sefi, Mustang, Holiday, and Cassius depart on the eighth day to oversee the final migration preparations.

The group arrives at Tinos where the broken body of Ragnar is carried down the ship's ramp. Sevro greets the Valkyrie in Nagal, the guttural Obsidian tongue, and offers Ragnar’s bear cloak to the fallen warrior. Sefi allows the Howlers to take stewardship of her brother’s body for a burial in his adopted home. Dancer expresses concern that the influx of Obsidians into the crowded Red tunnels will cause a violent eruption. Darrow stays with Sevro in the laboratory to mourn as Mickey prepares to embalm Ragnar’s massive frame.





### 36: Chapter 36: Swill
Darrow encounters Victra in the hallway and learns she has taken command of the newly acquired rebel navy. Victra informs Darrow that Dancer is currently venting his frustrations about Gold arrogance in the warroom. Darrow finds Mustang drinking in the commissary with Uncle Narol, Kavax, Daxo, and his own family. Kavax and Daxo Telemanus are massive Golds with tattoos of angels who have bonded with Darrow's mother and brother. Mustang tells stories of the Institute to the gathered crowd, charming the lowColors with her easy nature and humor.

Darrow’s mother recounts a story about a young Darrow trading his father’s watch for medicine to save Kieran. Mustang pulls Darrow aside and shares her frustration regarding Dancer’s refusal to share intelligence with her. Daxo and Kavax warn Darrow that they do not trust the "barbarian" Obsidians and fear their impact on Martian cities. Mustang provides Darrow with the communication frequency for Orion, who is currently causing chaos in the asteroid belt. The group discusses the Jackal’s intellect and his history of self-punishment when failing to solve complex puzzles.

Mustang argues that the Jackal will easily predict any attempt to use clawDrills for a city-wide invasion. She emphasizes that her brother is obsessed with Darrow and will prioritize his destruction over all other goals. Daxo demands assurances that Darrow is not planning to establish a dictatorship or a full democracy upon victory. Kavax insists that family is the only thing that matters and officially welcomes Darrow into their circle. Holiday interrupts the gathering to inform Darrow that Cassius has urgent information and is requesting an audience.





### 37: Chapter 37: The Last Eagle
Darrow enters the Tinos infirmary where Cassius is manacled to a medical gurney among hundreds of wounded rebels. Cassius receives no painkillers because the patients voted to give the limited morphon supplies to burn victims and amputees. The guards move the gurney to a locked pharmacy area to allow Darrow and Cassius to speak in private. Cassius asks Darrow if it was him or Mustang who prevented the Obsidians from blinding and mutilating him. Darrow admits that he respects Cassius’s honor despite the many betrayals and violent conflicts that have defined their history.

Cassius explains that the Sovereign originally sent him to negotiate because she is terrified of the Jackal’s recent actions. Intelligence agents discovered that the Jackal has been falsifying reports of Sons of Ares attacks to hide helium theft. The Jackal has successfully stockpiled enough helium to rival the Imperial Reserves held on Luna and Venus combined. Cassius reveals that the Jackal has also bribed forty Senators to support his eventual claim for the title of Sovereign. Darrow learns that asteroid S-1988, a barren silicate rock, was recently robbed of five hundred nuclear warheads.

These nuclear weapons are thirty-megaton "world killers," massive atomics capable of glassing entire planetary surfaces. Although the theft was blamed on Ares, the Sovereign traced the hijacker’s ship to a company formerly owned by the Julii. Cassius warns that the Jackal intends to detonate these weapons on Mars if his control over the planet is threatened. The Bellona family has been on Mars as long as the Reds, and Cassius fears for his mother’s safety there. Darrow realizes that his plan to invade the cities will provoke the Jackal into murdering the entire Martian population.





### 38: Chapter 38: The Bill
Darrow informs the war council of the five hundred nuclear warheads the Jackal has hidden in his arsenal. Quicksilver dismisses the claim as a ploy by Cassius, but Victra confirms the existence of Society nuclear "goody bags." Sevro offers to torture Cassius with a screwdriver to verify the information, but Dancer and Darrow refuse the request. Victra reveals the acid scars on her back given to her by Antonia as evidence that the Golds are capable of any atrocity. The council decides to trust Cassius’s information without resorting to torture, recognizing the genuine threat to the planet.

Cassius proposes a holoconference with the Sovereign to discuss an alliance against the Jackal's unstable nuclear threat. Sevro mocks the idea of an alliance with the woman who enslaved their race and murdered their families. Mustang warns that the Jackal will expect a direct personal attack and will have prepared lethal traps for the Howlers. Sefi demands that the Valkyrie be allowed to participate in any mission targeting the Jackal to avenge Ragnar's death. Darrow presents a new strategy that involves abandoning Mars to prevent the Jackal from using the planet as a hostage.

The plan requires the Rising to rove and remain mobile rather than becoming trapped in a defensive war for Martian cities. Dancer protests the abandonment of Mars, arguing that the rebellion was started for the lowColors living in the tunnels. Sevro silences the bickering council members by stabbing his razor into the table and demanding they listen to Darrow. Darrow sets his sights on Ilium, the collective name for the four largest Jovian moons, and the Sword Armada. He vows to destroy the undefeated fleet in forty days to rip the heart out of the Society's military power.





### 39: Chapter 39: The Heart
Tinos becomes a frantic hub of activity as hundreds of shuttles prepare for a massive population migration. The Obsidian warriors prepare to join the fleet in orbit while the civilians are moved to safer subterranean depths. Darrow watches the engine flares of the departing ships and reflects on the legacy of Fitchner au Barca. Dancer expresses his regret that Fitchner did not live to see Sevro wearing his father’s wolf-crested helm. The Red Armada prepares to burn hard for Jupiter once the evacuation of the Antarctic population is complete.

Darrow explains that the Jovian campaign relies on tricking the Moon Lords into believing the Rising is helping them. He tells Dancer that he is the heart of the rebellion and thanks him for his years of mentorship and love. Dancer reveals that he was always a good man, despite Darrow’s self-perception as a cold-blooded killer. Darrow encounters his family and Mustang as they prepare for their separate roles in the coming conflict. Uncle Narol and the Pitvipers prepare for a sabotage mission against the Jackal’s deep-space communication relays.

Darrow’s mother presents him with a plastic box containing dirt from their home in the Lykos mine. She tells Darrow and Mustang that they both deserve to be happy and urges them to return home alive. Hundreds of Sons of Ares, Obsidians, and Grays gather in the hangar to salute Darrow with raised fists. Darrow returns the salute, feeling the weight of the people who chose him to lead their revolution. He boards the shuttle with Sevro, Mustang, and Victra to begin the dangerous voyage to the Jovian moons.
