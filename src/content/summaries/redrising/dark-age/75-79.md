### Chapter 75: Ephraim: Grarnir

Ephraim ti Horn infiltrates Griffinhold using spider gloves and a thermal suit to reach Ozgard’s tower. He wakes the drunken shaman and plays a datadrop revealing Xenophon's treachery. Ozgard informs Ephraim that Sefi is isolated and influenced by Xenophon, who has arranged a peace summit with a Republic ambassador. Ephraim recruits eighty skuggi spirit warriors to assist in a rescue mission. They use a jamField and tactical distractions to overwhelm Valkyrie guards at the prison bridge to liberate Valdir.

Ephraim breaks into Xenophon’s spartan room and discovers the White watching a hologram from Atlas au Raa. The message instructs Xenophon to incite the Obsidian warbands to Mars once they are united. Ephraim confronts Xenophon, who reveals himself as a Fear Knight Gorgon named Pavor Nocturnus. Xenophon explains that Volsung Fá is also a Gorgon asset intended to manipulate the Obsidian trajectory. Five Ascomanni warriors with metal-fused skulls and mutated features suddenly ambush Ephraim from the windows.

Xenophon uses a heartspike controller to incapacitate Ephraim while the Ascomanni knock him unconscious. Ephraim wakes up bound in Sefi’s private chamber alongside a captured Ozgard. Sefi accuses Ephraim of attempted coup and orders him and Ozgard to be skinned and hung. They are dragged into the Hall of Eagles where six hundred warjarls are assembled before the Ice Throne. Sefi displays the skinned bodies of the skuggi and prepares to receive the Republic diplomatic shuttle.



### Chapter 76: Ephraim: He Who Walks the Void

A Republic shuttle lands in the Hall of Eagles, but instead of diplomats, Volsung Fá emerges. He wears a massive triple-horned beast skull helmet fused with asteroid metal and precious gems. Fá drags a heavy metal chain hung with the skulls of Ascomanni kings and carries a spear-saw. Xenophon proclaims Fá to be Vagnar Hefga, the long-lost father of Ragnar and Sefi. Fá removes his helmet to reveal a scarred face, a metal throat, and a metal left arm.

Sefi orders her Valkyrie to attack, but Fá kills three guards with his mechanical spear-saw in seconds. Several male warjarls form a protective circle around Fá, revealing they have already defected to his side. Fá mocks Sefi’s leadership and her adoption of Republic culture while boasting of his conquests in the Kuiper Belt. He challenges Sefi to single combat to prove who is worthy of leading the Obsidian Volk. Sefi draws her war axe and Aja’s razor to accept the challenge in the center of the hall.

The two duel, with Fá’s superior strength and reach eventually overwhelming Sefi’s agility. Fá systematically punctures Sefi’s ankles, knees, and rotator cuffs before performing a "blood eagle" on her while she is still alive. He carves her ribs open, tosses salt on the wounds, and eventually tears out her lungs and heart. Fá eats Sefi's heart in front of the assembled jarls and takes his seat on the Griffin Throne. Ozgard goes insane with grief and blinds himself with Fá’s knife as the Ascomanni begin sacking the city.



### Chapter 77: Ephraim: Worthy

Ephraim watches from the plaza as two thousand Ascomanni feast while the city of Olympia is systematically sacked. Obsidian braves join the slaughter, burning the city with phosphorus bombs and enslaving the civilian population. Xenophon approaches Ephraim and explains that the city was a necessary sacrifice to transform the Obsidians back into their primal forms. The White attempts to recruit Ephraim into Atlas au Raa's service, claiming Sefi was doomed by her own guilt. Ephraim insults Xenophon and demands a final audience with Volsung Fá on his throne.

Xenophon leads Ephraim to Fá, who has been selecting women for a harem while watching his men load slaves onto ships. Fá orders Ephraim’s cuffs removed and states his intention to mold Volga into his own image as an heir. Ephraim claims Volga died at sea, then provokes Fá by calling him a coward for not killing him personally. Fá orders Xenophon to kill Ephraim with the heartspike controller so it looks like a natural heart failure. Ephraim reveals he has planted a bomb in Xenophon’s pocket by palming a modified heartspike earlier.

The explosive detonates, killing Xenophon instantly and throwing Ephraim across the stone floor with a broken spine and missing arm. Ephraim experiences sensory loss and hallucinations of his dead husband, Trigg, as he waits for death in the sunlight. Volsung Fá survives the blast with half his face melted and his iron crown fused to his skull. The beast laughs at the pain and approaches the dying Ephraim to mock his final effort. Fá reaches into Ephraim's chest, tears out his heart, and eats it while calling him "worthy."



### Chapter 78: Lysander: A Visitor

Lysander au Lune waits at Glirastes's estate for the signal to begin the Gold loyalist uprising in Heliopolis. Glirastes informs him that Darrow’s departure is imminent and that Atalantia’s bombers are preparing for an attack. The sculptor visits Lysander in the garden one last time before heading to the spaceport to finalize their plans. Lysander reflects on the necessity of his path and the debt he owes to the future of his Color. He receives a gift from Glirastes: the Horn of Helios, a relic used to start races in the Hippodrome.

Lysander prepares for combat by opening a crate containing gravBoots, a razor, and military hardware provided by his loyalists. He cuts the security tracking spike out of his shoulder while a fake security feed hides his actions. A servant interrupts his preparations to announce the arrival of Alexandar au Arcos and Rhonna. Alexandar enters the library and greets Lysander with a hug, unaware of the young man's true identity or plans. Rhonna accompanies him, displaying her mechanical arm bolts that allow her to interface with military vehicles.

Alexandar brings a bottle of Venusian brandy as a farewell gift before the Free Legions depart the city. Lysander feels a mix of irritation and danger as his timetable for the uprising is severely threatened. He must maintain his cover as Cato au Vitruvius while his allies are already in motion at the spaceport. The presence of Rhonna, whom Lysander views with Color-based disdain, adds to the tension of the encounter. Lysander realizes he has no way to warn his Green hackers to adjust the falsified security data.



### Chapter 79: Darrow: Bad Blood

Darrow watches Atlas au Raa through a glass cell wall while Screwface reports the prisoner has remained silent. Atlas recites a Latin poem by Catullus that promises violent retribution for those who steal from him. Screwface and Darrow discuss the urgency of the evacuation and the threat of Atalantia’s looming attack. Darrow returns to the Mound to oversee final preparations for the army's surprise departure from Heliopolis tomorrow. He remains uneasy and reviews surveillance footage of Glirastes’s ward, Cato au Vitruvius, searching for hidden threats.

Thraxa visits Darrow with fish pies and they discuss the emotional toll of the war and their missing families. Darrow fixates on a specific recording of Cato muttering in his sleep during the surveillance gaps. He hears the phrase "Truth over all" and realizes it is the motto of House Lune. Darrow runs to his science team and demands a DNA check on the bloody robes Sevro took from the desert. The test confirms that Cato is actually Lysander au Lune, the grandson of the late Sovereign.

Darrow immediately orders a full-scale military alert and commands the destruction of Glirastes's EMP machine. He realizes the sculptor has betrayed the Republic and that an internal coup is coordinated with Atalantia’s assault. Screwface is ordered to mobilize his Howlers for a strike while Harnassus prepares the Morning Star for combat. Darrow patches into the communications of Alexandar and Rhonna to warn them of the danger. He tells them their companion is Lysander au Lune and orders them to wait for reinforcements before engaging.
