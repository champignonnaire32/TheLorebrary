### Chapter 75: Ephraim: Grarnir
Ephraim ti Horn infiltrates Griffinhold using specialized gear and creates a narrow opening in a pulseShield to enter Ozgard's room. He douses the drunken shaman with wine to wake him and presents a datadrop proving that a parasite is manipulating the Queen's counsel. Ozgard identifies the traitor as Xenophon and explains that the Queen has become increasingly paranoid and unreachable. Ephraim proposes they use the skuggi spirit warriors to liberate Valdir, the only ally capable of swaying the Queen. They gather eighty skuggi and infiltrate the prison, which is guarded by Valkyrie warriors.

Ephraim encounters the guards, neutralizes them with gravity mines, and skillfully disarms the sophisticated traps surrounding Xenophon's room. He discovers Xenophon watching a holographic message from Atlas au Raa, which details a plot to incite war while the Obsidian forces are on Mars. Xenophon reveals himself as a member of the Pavor Nocturnus, a covert intelligence unit known as the Gorgons. He explains that their master intends to conquer the Obsidian tribes from within and manipulate the Raa and the Republic simultaneously. Ephraim manages to record this confession while Xenophon attempts to recruit him into Zero Legion.

The discussion is interrupted by the arrival of five Ascomanni, mutated warriors from the Kuiper Belt who serve the Gorgons. Ephraim is unable to win the fight and plays dead after being subjected to a heartspike attack, only to be dragged before Sefi. She accuses him of plotting a coup with Valdir, and Ephraim is forced to witness the assembly of the jarls. The Bellona Doors open to reveal the arrival of the Fear Knight's emissaries. Sefi attempts to maintain control, but the situation rapidly deteriorates as the gathered warjarls confront their own internal divisions.


### Chapter 76: Ephraim: He Who Walks the Void
The Obsidian honor guards salute as a massive figure wearing a skull-based helmet, Volsung Fá, enters the Hall of Eagles. He commands the room, claiming to be the father of Ragnar Volarus and the true ruler of the Obsidian people. Volsung demonstrates his overwhelming strength by brutally slaughtering three Valkyrie bodyguards with a specialized spear-saw. He asserts that Sefi is a liar who has betrayed her people and challenges her right to lead based on her gender and reliance on others. The male warjarls are swayed by his rhetoric and begin to chant for a fight between Sefi and the challenger.

Sefi accepts the challenge, stripping down for combat against the man she believes is her father's successor. Volsung proves superior in skill and savagery, quickly disabling Sefi by crippling her ankles, knees, and shoulders before beating her into submission. He then performs a ritualistic execution by carving open her back, hacking her ribs, and removing her lungs and heart in front of the horrified assembly. Fá eats her heart, asserting his dominance as the new leader of the Obsidian people. Ozgard, driven to madness by the sight, kills himself while Fá takes the throne and orders the sacking of the city.

The city of Olympia is subjected to systematic brutality as Obsidian forces turn on the citizens they once lived alongside. Ephraim, trapped and disgusted, witnesses the massacre and realizes the inevitability of the carnage now unleashed. Xenophon attempts to execute Ephraim with the heartspike, but Ephraim reveals he had previously sabotaged the device with explosives provided by Pax. A massive blast ensues, killing Xenophon, but Ephraim is mortally wounded and left immobile. Volsung Fá, surviving the explosion with severe burns, finishes Ephraim by tearing out and eating his heart.


### Chapter 77: Ephraim: Worthy
Ephraim, near death in the aftermath of the explosion, reflects on his life and choices while watching the destruction of Olympia. He experiences a sense of peace, realizing that his efforts to save Volga and stop the Gorgons have reached their conclusion. His final moments are interrupted when Volsung Fá, his face hideously scarred and melted, approaches him. Fá confirms his own survival, mocks Ephraim's suffering, and finishes him off. The monster consumes Ephraim's heart, reinforcing his claim to power and his belief in his own worthiness.


### Chapter 78: Lysander: A Visitor
Lysander au Lune prepares for his own strike, working through his plan while maintaining his facade as the libertine Cato au Vitruvius. He removes the security spike from his shoulder, replacing it with a falsified transmission loop to hide his true location. Glirastes departs, leaving Lysander with the legendary Horn of Helios and military gear needed for the impending conflict. Lysander is interrupted by an unexpected visit from Alexandar au Arcos and Rhonna, who arrive to bid him farewell. He is forced to entertain them while concealing his equipment, all while knowing that his plan is reaching a critical stage.


### Chapter 79: Darrow: Bad Blood
Darrow continues to interrogate the captive Atlas au Raa, who remains silent but appears to be psychically monitoring the surroundings. Darrow is haunted by the Fear Knight's cryptic poetry and grows increasingly suspicious of the visitor, Cato au Vitruvius. Upon reviewing surveillance footage and noticing a slip in Cato's behavior, Darrow realizes that Cato is actually Lysander. Darrow alerts his forces and dispatches a team to intercept Lysander while attempting to warn Alexandar and Rhonna of the true identity of their host. He realizes too late that the enemy is already inside the city, and the situation is rapidly spiraling toward a major conflict.
