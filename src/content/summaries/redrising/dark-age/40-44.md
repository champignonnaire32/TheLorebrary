### Chapter 40: Ephraim: Kjrdakan
Ephraim submits a tactical brief detailing how his skuggi can neutralize Quicksilver’s mine security on Mars. Sefi’s warlord Valdir prefers a frontal assault despite the high civilian casualties such a strategy would ensure. Ephraim discusses the political fallout of an Obsidian occupation with Pax, who has grown harder since his mother’s death. Pax suggests that the Republic is dying and that an alliance with Obsidian is necessary against Atalantia. Ozgard arrives to lead Ephraim to a traditional war ritual called the Kjrdakan.

Sefi initiates the ceremony by splitting the skull of an albino aurochs with a massive axe. She and Valdir consume the beast’s raw tongue to seal their pact before the war leaders. Ozgard butterflies the animal’s torso and lights a fire within its belly to read the future. During the Lord Wager, several commanders offer meager insults to Ephraim’s plan by betting single torcs. Insulted by their derision, Ephraim cuts his own palm and lets his blood drip into the rib cage.

Sefi matches Ephraim’s high-stakes blood wager by slicing her own forearm with a dagger. She declares that victory will follow her new way while defeat will cost her head. Ephraim realizes he is now personally responsible for the success of the infiltration and the lives of the skuggi. Sefi acknowledges Ephraim’s fear and pride, noting that they both spit in the face of terror. She promises him treasure and honor at the Hunt of the Last Light if they prevail.





### Chapter 41: Ephraim: Obsidian Rising
Ephraim waits soberly on a Julii ore hauler as the Obsidians launch their massive heist on the Martian helium mines. He worries that the skuggi are unprepared for the coordinated infiltration required to disable the automated defense grids. Ozgard dismisses Ephraim’s anxiety and claims that Sefi’s fate is protected by the breath of the Allmother. During a tense moment, Ephraim accidentally eats hallucinogenic spirit berries from Ozgard’s bag, thinking they are walnuts. The drug takes immediate effect, causing the metal walls to pulse and reality to distort into a fourth-dimensional plane.

As the heist begins, Ephraim hallucinates that the hangar is the mouth of a beast filled with vibrating dark teeth. He sees Sefi as a luminous goddess in sky-blue armor surrounded by a crackling cloud of spiritual lightning. The Obsidians perform a death chant that Ephraim feels as a physical vibration deep within his bones. Ozgard strips naked and bellows a creed as the hangar doors open to the freezing wind of Mars. Ephraim is swept up in the madness and jumps from the transport ramp without wearing any protective armor.

Hallucinating that he is a spirit warrior, Ephraim falls toward the Cimmerian landscape through a maelstrom of railgun rounds. He lands inside an open mine shaft and prepares to face a pack of hunterkiller robots with his weapon. The "terrible rifle" in his hand turns out to be a simple cleaning mop left behind by a worker. Sefi and Valdir land seconds later to initiate a violent slaughter that leaves thirty-five thousand Obsidians dead. The skuggi successfully lower the defense grids, allowing the Alltribe to seize control of the valuable helium infrastructure.





### Chapter 42: Lysander: A Chorus Upon the Pale
Lysander wanders the Mercurian desert for days while suffering from severe dehydration and a partially blinded right eye. He survives by eating cacti and raw snakes while cursing the culture that allowed Ajax to grow so wicked. After discovering an abandoned farmhouse, he uses solar power to cook food and distills aloe into a healing paste. He eventually reaches the Valley of Erebos only to find the city has been drowned by a broken dam. At the city’s entrance, he encounters the horrific sight of four hundred Martians impaled on metal poles.

The impaled victims are naked and displayed as a warning to those who follow the Slave King Darrow. Lysander realizes that his grandmother’s former hostage, Atlas au Raa, has become a monster known as the Fear Knight. He attempts to provide a merciful death to a dying Red man by piercing his heart with a razor. The surrounding victims laugh as Lysander inadvertently trips a pressure-sensitive mine trap hidden beneath the lavender. A tacNet entangles him, and he becomes immobilized in the sun as buzzards begin to circle his bleeding body.

Gorgon soldiers on gravbikes arrive at the scene and mistake the unscarred Lysander for a low-ranking Pixie or Rising supporter. They mock his condition and seize his Sciantus-made razor, which they recognize as a high-quality weapon once owned by a Peerless. One soldier identifies the flower petals on the hilt, noting the blade’s exceptional detailing and value. The Gorgons decide to take Lysander prisoner rather than leaving him to be eaten by the scavengers. They search him for trackers before knocking him unconscious with a heavy desert boot to the head.





### Chapter 43: Lysander: The Enemy
The Gorgons transport Lysander to a high-desert mining town before moving him to a cold cave in the mountains. He wakes up chained to a stone wall alongside ten other Gold prisoners who have been brutally interrogated. Lysander listens to the captives discuss a mysterious figure who uses drugs to make time slow down during torture. He identifies the prisoner leader as Alexandar au Arcos, the grandson of Lorn and nephew of Kalindora. To protect himself, Lysander adopts the false identity of Cato au Vitruvius, a fictional Mercurian civil engineer.

Alexandar and the other prisoners, including Drusilla and Ignacius, test Lysander’s story with specific questions about Erebos. Lysander uses his childhood memories of the library city to pass their tests and maintain his cover identity. He learns that the prisoners are Howlers and Arcosian Knights who were captured during the ongoing Mercurian campaign. Ignacius remains skeptical of Lysander, calling him a duty-dodging Pixie and a slaver who cannot be trusted. Alexandar reveals that Darrow led the Republic army across the Ladon desert under the cover of a massive storm.

This desperate maneuver allowed Darrow to strike Ajax au Grimmus while the Society was busy besieging the city of Heliopolis. Lysander realizes that Darrow has either regained control of the war or lost it completely to the madness of the desert. The prisoners hope for a rescue, though Ignacius believes the Senate Vox has already betrayed the Sovereign. Lysander watches the Gorgon guards in the tunnel while contemplating his proximity to his estranged cousin and his enemies. He remains silent about his true heritage as the Heir of Silenius while the Arcosian Knights discuss their fate.





### Chapter 44: Ephraim: Hunt of the Last Light
Sefi rewards Ephraim with a sleek sixty-meter jade ship named Snowball for his legendary performance during the mine heist. He travels to the South Pole to join the Hunt of the Last Light, a traditional Obsidian sundeath ritual. The Valkyrie spend six days stalking a massive black ice drake, a lab-engineered predator with nine lateral tusks. Pax joins Sefi on her griffin Godeater, riding in the archer’s saddle to participate in the sacred event. Ephraim watches from a mountain ledge as the chasers flush the leviathan from its alpine cavern into the valley.

During the final engagement, Sefi appears physically unable to draw her massive skybow to deliver the killing blow. The drake breaks free of its tethers and nearly kills Pax and Sefi after they are trapped against a cliff. Ephraim ignores the Obsidian creed against firearms and uses a high-powered sniper rifle to intervene in the hunt. He shoots the drake through its eye, saving Sefi and the young warrior Freihild from certain death. Valdir acts as a spotter for Ephraim, signaling his approval of the illegal shot to protect his Queen.

The tribes celebrate the kill with a massive feast where they harvest the dragon’s meat, scales, and medicinal organs. Pax claims the bow failed because he forgot to use a heat sheath, but Ephraim suspects Sefi is hiding a serious illness. Valdir’s obvious lust for the younger Freihild creates a palpable tension that threatens the stability of Sefi’s inner circle. Ephraim confronts Valdir about his indiscretion, warning him that the Queen will not tolerate his wandering eyes. The chapter concludes with the Obsidians feasting by the fire while the sun sets on their reclaimed frozen homeland.
