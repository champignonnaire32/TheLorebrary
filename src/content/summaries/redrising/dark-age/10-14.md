### Chapter 10: Lysander: The Ash Rain
Atalantia gives the command for the Ash Legion to drop from the Annihilo. Lysander is loaded into a spitTube, a honeycomb launch mechanism for starShells. He uses the Mind’s Eye technique to suppress his terror during the high-velocity ingestion. The starShell is a four-meter-tall mechanized suit designed for atmospheric entry and combat. Lysander enters the mesosphere and observes the scale of the Society’s massive invasion fleet.

The target is the Ladon equatorial desert, protected by the Republic’s shifting tropospheric shields. Society orbital artillery uses atomics to blast a thousand-kilometer-wide breach in the shield dome. Lysander joins the flight flow of the Grimmus, Falthe, Carthii, and Votum houses. He flies through a hellmouth, a corridor of protective fire maintained by orbital ships. His suit undergoes extreme friction, shedding thermal soak tiles as it enters the blue sky.

Decade One, led by Ajax au Grimmus, fractures into smaller units once through the breach. The group encounters railgun fire from a Republican mountain installation in the Hesperides range. Seraphina au Raa, a Rim pilot, identifies the electrical spike of the charging guns. An orbital strike from the Annihilo destroys the mountain peak and the gun battery. Ajax welcomes Lysander to the war as they prepare to engage aerial infantry.



### Chapter 11: Darrow: Red Reach
Darrow retreats across the Ladon desert in the Necromancer shuttle while under orbital bombardment. Colloway, the Blue pilot, outmaneuvers fifty squadrons of enemy ripWing fighters. The shuttle reaches Red Reach base, where the Second Army is sheltered under a shield dome. Darrow docks at Skyhook Eleven, a floating supply platform, to prepare for a counterattack. He dons a starShell and prepares to lead the Fifteenth Legion Helldivers.

Drachenjägers are forty-meter-tall six-armed war machines equipped with ion cleavers and cannons. A Society omega-atomic falls toward the Red Reach base as the skyhook’s blast doors close. Darrow watches a Martian war room vaporize on his viewscreen just before the bomb impacts. The blast incinerates a Red pitman who is trapped outside Darrow’s starShell canopy. A million Republic soldiers and their equipment are instantly reduced to radioactive ash.

Darrow survives the thermal wave and shockwave behind the mountain’s granite cliffs. He orders the Necromancer to Kydon to tell Thraxa au Augustus to retreat toward Tyche. The remaining five thousand Drachenjägers power up their engines within the mountain garage. Darrow intends to intercept the Society forces at the coastal highlands to protect Tyche. He lifts his slingBlade and orders the Helldiver Legion to ride into the coming storm.



### Chapter 12: Lysander: White Golems
Lysander moves north with the XX Fulminata and the Iron Leopard legions. He clears Republic bunkers in the mountains and records several kills during the advance. Ajax uses Lysander's targeting data to destroy a mining town with an orbital strike. The strike kills two snipers along with hundreds of Red and Brown civilians. Lysander feels disgust at the casual slaughter but remains silent among his peers.

The legions land in a playa to recharge their starShell energy cores. Kalindora au Grimmus, the Love Knight, critiques Lysander's flying but acknowledges his combat success. SubLegate Rhone ti Flavinius arrives with one thousand Praetorian guards formerly loyal to Octavia. The Praetorians swear fealty to Lysander, recognizing him as the true heir of House Lune. Ajax reacts with silent wrath at the political power Lysander has suddenly gained.

A message from Fury Command warns of five hypercanes forming over the Sycorax Sea. Lysander identifies these as man-made storms generated by ancient terraforming engines called Storm Gods. A massive sandstorm hits the landing zone, destroying sixty Society transports and many soldiers. Lysander comforts Ajax during a panic attack while they shelter behind a heavy tank. Lysander, Kalindora, and Seraphina lead the Praetorians west to destroy the Storm God.



### Chapter 13: Darrow: Plains of Caduceus
Orion uses the Storm Gods to generate hypercanes that disrupt the Society's air superiority. Darrow leads his columns of Drachenjägers across the golden sunblossom fields of Mercury. They ambush four Votum legions that are attempting to land between Tyche and the plains. The Republic forces detonate four small nukes to vaporize the enemy's heavy titan armor. Darrow’s army charges through the confusion, using particle cannons and railguns at point-blank range.

The Drachenjägers use blue-white ion swords to butcher the disordered Votum infantry and mechs. A sixty-meter-tall Votum titan attempts to rally the enemy forces with a gravity gun. Darrow and Alexandar au Arcos scale the massive machine to reach its cockpit. They defeat the Gold defenders and extract the pilot from the titan’s upper hull. Darrow identifies the pilot as Scorpio au Votum and sentences him to death for war crimes.

Darrow cuts Scorpio in half with his slingBlade and hurls the body into the mud. Alexandar captures the Votum legion standard and mounts it on Rhonna’s Drachenjäger. The Republic force captures fourteen legion standards as they shatter the enemy division. They salvage power cores and batteries from the dead to sustain their starShells. Darrow orders his army to push toward the coastal highlands to confront Atalantia au Grimmus.



### Chapter 14: Lysander: Into the Storm
Lysander, Kalindora, and Seraphina lead the Praetorians through eight hundred kilometer-per-hour winds. They use a Rim technique of connecting starShells with tow wires to avoid separation. The group encounters Cicero au Votum and the remains of his Scorpion Legion in the sand. Cicero is unaware that Darrow has already executed his father, Scorpio, in the plains. Lysander promises to support Votum’s claim to Heliopolis to secure Cicero's military cooperation.

The combined force reaches the eye of the storm, a calm zone fifty kilometers wide. A Storm God, a massive gray metal monolith, floats kilometers above the desert surface. Gravity engines on the monolith create a shadow that traps various desert animals in the air. Lysander observes the marble cloak of clouds and lightning generated by the terraforming engine. He identifies the engine's potential to kill millions through tidal waves and orders the attack.

Kalindora organizes the Praetorians and Scorpions into six columns for an assault on the monolith. She prepares three wedges for a flank charge against the expected Storm God garrison. Lysander checks his ammunition and prepares to engage the enemy forces. Seraphina speaks to Lysander just as a massive rail slug strikes her starShell. The projectile decapitates the mech and kills Seraphina instantly, spilling her remains into the desert.
