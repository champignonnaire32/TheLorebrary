### 85: Lysander: Lune Invictus
Lysander leads a brutal charge against the Red soldiers in Heliopolis, viewing the slaughter with systematic detachment. He surveys the battlefield as his Praetorians and Kalindora crush the enemy resistance. Despite the intense fighting, Lysander remains focused on finding Darrow, the Reaper of Mars. Darrow appears, cutting through Lysander’s forces with an impossibly aggressive display of skill. The Red forces execute a desperate "Red Rain" tactic, dropping from rooftops to sow chaos among the Golds.

An enormous Obsidian, Thraxa Telemanus, lands on Lysander, throwing him from his horse and into an apartment. A violent, close-quarters duel ensues between Lysander and Thraxa in the building before he manages to escape back to the street. Thraxa is severely wounded by Rhone and Lysander, but she is saved by two Reds. Darrow breaks through the lines once more, his army beginning to collapse into a full-scale rout as the infantry breaks.

Lysander pursues the retreating Darrow and his small group, eventually catching up to them on a quiet boulevard. He challenges Darrow to a duel, and they charge at each other on horseback. Lysander throws a concealed razor into Darrow’s chest, causing Darrow to fall from his mount. Darrow manages to escape into an alley, leaving behind the shattered hilt of his iconic slingBlade as a trophy.


### 86: Darrow: Legion’s End
Darrow flees through the dark, labyrinthine streets of Heliopolis, suffering from a grievous chest wound after being struck by Lysander’s hidden blade. He reflects on the loss of his slingBlade, which he sees as the end of an era. The city has been effectively captured by Atalantia’s forces, who are slaughtering the remaining resistance. Darrow arrives at the Mound of Votum, the last refuge, and learns that his commanders and forces are shattered.

Survivors gather at the Mound, including a badly wounded Thraxa and a traumatized, partially scalped Screwface. Darrow remains in a daze, barely able to move, as medics struggle to treat his chest wound. Despite the grim outlook, Darrow refuses to surrender and tries to organize a final, hopeless defense for the incoming enemy forces. He climbs to the tower of the Mound, watching the light of incoming starShells and accepting the inevitability of his defeat.

Harnassus, his veteran commander, joins him in the tower and finally breaks down under the weight of their failures. Darrow realizes that he lost faith in the Republic’s ideals and pursued his own path, leading to this disastrous end. As they prepare to be captured or killed by the approaching Atalantia, they are interrupted by the arrival of a ship. Darrow is stunned to find his old friend Cassius, believed dead, alive and ready to assist him.


### 87: Lysander: Ghost
Lysander oversees the final stages of the battle, watching the slaughter of Darrow’s remaining forces in Heliopolis. He reflects on his hollow victory, feeling a strange sense of loss as the legends of his youth are killed or defeated. Ajax au Grimmus arrives with his Ash Guard, harboring intense jealousy and hatred toward Lysander for his survival and success. Lysander provides Ajax with Darrow’s broken weapon, securing the begrudging respect of the gathered Peerless Scarred knights.

A celebration rages through the city as the populace turns on the invaders and revels in their own liberation from the siege. Lysander sits with his remaining Praetorians, reflecting on the irony of his position and the fleeting nature of glory. The celebratory mood is shattered when the *Archimedes* suddenly appears, using an advanced cloak and engine technology to rescue Darrow from the Mound. Lysander recognizes the ship and realizes that Cassius is not only alive but has helped Darrow escape.

The revelation that Cassius is alive and working against the Core leaves Lysander with mixed emotions of exultation and confusion. He understands that the war is far from over, despite the tactical victory in Heliopolis. The enemy has slipped through his fingers, and his focus must now shift to the broader struggle.


### 88: Lyria: Mercury Has Fallen
Lyria and the others attend the funeral for Ulysses, Victra’s young son, in a rose garden on Mars. The small, somber group reflects on the devastating loss as Victra struggles with her grief. Victra eventually leaves the group to swim out into the sea, a traditional Julii mourning ritual. Later, she returns and swears a life oath to protect the survivors and to seek vengeance for her family’s destruction.

The survivors learn the tragic news that the free legions on Mercury have been slaughtered and the Reaper is dead. This news shatters the morale of everyone present, including Lyria. ArchGovernor Kieran O’Lykos explains that the Obsidian warlord Volsung Fá is the father of Ragnar Volarus and is now demanding Volga as his heir to leave the Republic alone. Volga chooses to sacrifice herself and go to Fá to save others, despite Lyria’s desperate pleas.

Pax, having learned about Lyria’s internal parasite through hacked medical reports, offers her a chance to become someone who can truly make a difference. He reveals that his mother, the Sovereign, is alive and coming to Mars. Pax provides Lyria with a map to a mysterious city called Oculus, suggesting it holds the key to finding her lost brother, Liam.


### 89: Lysander: Triumph of the Long Night
Lysander observes the aftermath of the battle from the *Lady Beatrice*, struggling to come to terms with the reality that Cassius is alive and fighting for the Rising. He prepares for his Triumph in Heliopolis, acknowledging the complicated political landscape he must navigate with Atalantia. Despite his success, he feels a sense of isolation and mistrust as he prepares to be honored. He appoints Rhone to the high position of Dux, cementing his hold on the loyalist forces.

The Triumphal parade through the city is a massive, complex affair, symbolizing the unity Lysander is trying to project despite the internal fractures of the Society. Lysander meets Atalantia at the palace steps for the formal proceedings, where she mocks him while acknowledging his new status. He kneels before her, feigning submissiveness to secure their alliance and solidify his position within the Core.

The Triumph ends, and Atalantia takes Lysander to her ship for a private celebration. She reveals her plan for him to lead the restoration of the ruined city of Tyche, aiming to use him as a symbol of her benevolent rule. The chapter concludes with a tense, intimate encounter between them, highlighting the complex, predatory nature of their newly formed alliance.
