### 50: Lyria: Parasite
Lyria and Volga escape the Pandora through a maintenance corridor guided by Fig. Fig uses a retinal forger to access Julii’s personal escape craft, which is hidden behind a maintenance closet. Sevro au Barca appears as a hologram to provide a sarcastic farewell before the craft launches. Lyria is thrown into the craft’s lounge and encounters a heavily armed Victra au Barca and her Sol Guards. Victra punches Lyria in the face just before the ship is attacked and begins to spiral toward the surface of Mars.

The escape ship crashes into a Martian fjord, causing the hull to shred and several passengers to die. Lyria wakes up trapped in a protective black gel cocoon while dangling over a vertical drop. She manages to free herself from the crash harness and helps Volga escape her own melting crashpod. The two women climb out of the wreckage just before the ship slips off the cliff and sinks into the water. They find a second section of the ship nearby and begin searching the interior for survivors and supplies.

Lyria finds Fig trapped in a crashpod and discovers she is mortally wounded by a metal shard. Fig babbles about an heir and a "parasite" before a mechanical, squid-like device bursts from her nostril. The device, which Fig calls a "Figment," crawls over Lyria’s eyes and forcefully enters her nasal passage. Lyria experiences excruciating pain as the parasite integrates with her nervous system and begins speaking in her mind. Fig dies after the transfer is complete, and the parasite informs Lyria that she is now the Figment.



### 51: Lyria: Jade Witch
Volga discovers Lyria standing over Fig’s body and begins harvesting Fig’s eyeballs for a bounty contract from Quicksilver. Lyria hides the fact that a mechanical parasite has entered her brain and watches as Volga loots a black orb from Fig’s bag. A high-pitched humming sound leads Lyria into the forest, where she finds Victra au Barca trapped under a fallen tree. Lyria threatens Victra with a scavenged pulseFist but is stopped from firing by Volga. Victra reveals that the "dungeons" Lyria and Volga were kept in were actually her daughter Electra's playrooms.

Victra offers the women money to lift the tree off her legs, but Lyria refuses and prepares to leave. Volga decides to stay and help Victra, citing a debt for returning her child to her safely. Lyria gathers survival supplies and starts walking south alone across the icy Cimmerian Highlands. She spots incoming shuttles and uses oculars to identify them as ships belonging to the Red Hand. Lyria realizes the Red Hand is approaching the crash site and runs back to warn Volga and Victra.

The three women flee into the forest as Harmony and the Red Hand soldiers begin searching the wreckage. Lyria experiences heightened senses and pixelated vision as the parasite inside her activates in response to Harmony’s presence. Victra explains that Harmony is the leader of the Red Hand and has a blood feud with her family. Despite Volga’s offer to snipe Harmony from a distance, Lyria decides to spare her to avoid bringing the shuttles down on them. The group resolves to head south across the sound to find a communication array or a boat.



### 52: Ephraim: Pale Rain
Ephraim walks through the streets of Olympia and observes the progress Sefi has made in restoring the city's infrastructure. He notices that the local Reds have gained a sense of purpose and employment under Obsidian rule. A pickpocketing attempt by a Red boy reminds Ephraim of his own reputation and the growing unrest among the Reaper's supporters. The peaceful atmosphere is shattered when news broadcasts show the Pandora under attack by ships bearing Alltribe colors. Thousands of naked bodies are dropped from the sky over the city of Agea in a "pale rain" of corpses.

Ephraim realizes that Volsung Fá is likely behind the attack to frame Sefi and trigger a war with the Republic. He decides to extract Pax and Electra immediately before Sefi can move them to a secure bunker. Ephraim paralyzes several skuggi guards with designer rounds and sets explosives on the southern landing pad as a diversion. He programs his ship, the Snowball, to fly a specific path and deploy a catch-hook for the children. Ephraim uses a massive neodymium magnet to incapacitate a unit of Valkyrie guards by disrupting their metal armor and gravBoots.

Pax and Electra use specialized harnesses to snag the Snowball's catch-hook and fly away from Eagle Rest. Ephraim is injured during the extraction and discovers that Sefi had implanted a tracker and a heartspike in his body. Electra performs emergency surgery to remove the devices while Pax pilots the ship toward the northern hemisphere. Ephraim recovers from the surgery and learns that the children are determined to find their families. They decide to fly dark toward the crash site of the escape pods to search for Victra and Volga.



### 53: Virginia: Pandemonium
Virginia is imprisoned in the Pandemonium Chair and subjected to a linear assault on her memories by the Vox. Lilath and her Greens attempt to extract secret codes, base locations, and the identities of active Howlers. Virginia uses mental conditioning to populate her memories with false totems to mislead the psychotechs. She experiences extreme physical pain from psychoSpikes and remains in a state of sensory deprivation between sessions. Virginia reflects on the deaths of Daxo and Dancer while floating in a void of isolation and despair.

Virginia eventually realizes that the psychoSpike has been used to make her a quadriplegic while keeping her conscious. The psychotechs also hijack her brain's language center to prevent her from speaking to her captors. Lilath uses chemicals to flood Virginia’s brain with dopamine, forcing her to feel joy despite the horror of her situation. Virginia is forced to watch a hologram of a public funeral procession and execution ceremony for her loyalists. Theodora and Servilla au Arcos are among those brought before the Obelisk of Ares to face the People’s Tribunal.

Publius reads the charges of treason and conspiracy against the prisoners before they are executed. Theodora is crushed to death by the massive stone monument as the crowd cheers and dips banners in her blood. Publius visits Virginia in the courtroom and delivers a long monologue about the necessity of terror in a revolution. He explains his plan to remake the world into a classless society where all serve the common good. Publius informs Virginia that she has been found guilty and will be executed the following day.



### 54: Virginia: Justice of the Meek
Virginia sits before the People's Tribunal as Publius reads a list of high crimes, including the assassination of Dancer. A sudden hack disables the robotic cameras, and a noise of violence erupts from outside the courtroom. Three transparent figures drop from the ceiling and decapitate the Wardens guarding Publius. The attackers deactivate their ghostCloaks to reveal they are Boneriders from House Pluto who were thought to be dead. Lilath, the Queen of the Syndicate, enters the room carrying a severed head and wearing a black iron crown.

Lilath reveals that she was disguised as a Red during the Forum and has been manipulating Publius as a puppet. She kills a Blue senator with a hatchet and forces Publius to crawl on his knees and wear a dog collar. The remaining members of the tribunal are similarly collared and enslaved by the Boneriders. Lilath announces the arrival of her Emperor, and a ten-year-old boy with flaxen hair enters the court. The boy is the Abomination, a clone of Virginia's twin brother, Adrius au Augustus.

The Abomination delivers a speech about the insignificance of the senators while feeding them pieces of the dead Blue senator's brain. He reveals the Sword of Silenius and commands the Vox to eat as a sign of their submission. The Boneriders cheer "Hic sunt leones" as the natural order of the Jackal’s rule is restored. Publius is the first to comply with the order to eat human remains while crying in humiliation. The Abomination turns to Virginia and greets her as his sister to conclude the meeting.
