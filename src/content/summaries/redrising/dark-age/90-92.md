### 90: Lysander: The Love Knight
Lysander visits a dying Kalindora at her seaside villa. Kalindora reveals that Atalantia and she were responsible for the death of Lysander's parents, orchestrated by Octavia. She explains that Octavia used a machine called the chair to erase Lysander's memories of his mother. Kalindora urges Lysander to assume his role as Sovereign and stop Atalantia from taking the throne. Lysander leaves, feeling hollow, and attends Kalindora's sunburial later that night.

Atalantia, feigning grief, later seduces Lysander in her meditation chamber. Lysander reflects on his lost childhood and the treachery of those closest to him. He feels a sense of dark realization about the true nature of his grandmother and Atalantia. He resolves to do his duty to prevent the worlds from burning under Atalantia's rule. The weight of these revelations leaves him feeling emotionally dead.

He continues to play his part as Atalantia's lover while secretly planning his next moves. He maintains his composure during the public spectacle of the sunburial. He finds himself caught in a web of political intrigue that he must navigate. His resolve hardens as he considers the fate of the worlds. He prepares to secure his future despite the manipulations surrounding him.





### 91: Virginia: Salvation or Vengeance
Virginia arrives on Mars with her fleet to find a planet rallying to her cause. She feels the weight of her failures, including the loss of Darrow and the capture of Sevro. Cassius, having escaped his Rim imprisonment, has pledged his service to the Republic. Virginia faces the grim reality of the war and the loss of her closest allies. She prepares to reclaim her position and fight for the future of the Republic.

She meets with Kavax, who is mourning his son Daxo and fearing for his remaining family. Despite his despair, Kavax pledges his strength to Virginia for the coming battle. Virginia performs the Iron Circle, a traditional display of dominance over a planet. She is greeted by a crowd of millions, their fists dipped in red, showing their support for her. The sight of this unity brings a moment of hope to the devastated Sovereign.

Virginia reunites with her son, Pax, finding him changed by the hardships he has endured. She witnesses the mobilization of House Julii and House Barca for total war. She reconciles with Victra, who is driven by grief and a desire for vengeance against their enemies. They share a moment of solidarity, united by their shared losses and determination. The news that Earth has fallen to the combined forces of the Rim and the Society shatters their brief peace.





### 92: Lysander: Graveyard of Tyrants
Lysander sets up a feast in the desert of the Ladon to meet with Apollonius au Valii-Rath. He dismisses his guard, Rhone, and prepares for the arrival of the dangerous Minotaur. He reflects on the corrupt nature of the previous Sovereigns and the concept of honor. He concludes that fairness has no place in the pursuit of power. He waits, ready to manipulate the situation to his advantage.

Apollonius arrives, appearing as a formidable warrior, and they dine together at the feast. Lysander reveals his plan to raise the statue of Silenius au Lune to symbolize his new order. Apollonius is impressed by the theatrical display and the prospect of fighting Darrow. Lysander offers Apollonius the freedom to act without the constraints of rule if he becomes his ally. Apollonius accepts the alliance, eager for battle and the chance to defeat his enemies.

Lysander successfully secures the Minotaur's blade and cunning for his cause. He envisions a future where he stands as the architect of a new empire, free from the failures of his predecessors. He reflects on the graveyard of tyrants and the burden of his newfound responsibility. The statues of the past Sovereigns now point toward Mars, signaling his next objective. He prepares to lead the worlds into a new era of conflict and conquest.
