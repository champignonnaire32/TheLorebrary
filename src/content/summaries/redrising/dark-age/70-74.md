### Chapter 70: Lyria: Thunder Bottle
Lyria is tied to a mess hall table in the Gray encampment while Harmony interrogates her about Duncan's death. Harmony threatens to maim her, eventually using a hammer to break her fingers when Lyria reveals no useful information. A Red girl named Vanna, previously captured by the Red Hand, enters the mess hall armed with a pistol. Vanna shoots and kills Picker, Harmony's subordinate, before cutting Lyria free with a knife. Lyria retrieves the pistol from Vanna and they manage to escape into the township.

Lyria and Vanna scale the township's levels, taking advantage of the chaos caused by the acid attack that incapacitates many Red Hand guards. They reach the "Can," a fortified barracks building, by climbing cable ladders and hiding in the shadows of the structure. Lyria discovers the parasite in her head is not completely dormant, providing her with a heightened sense of movement and awareness. Inside the Can, they find the former Mine Magistrate's quarters, which now serve as Harmony's base.

After searching the room, they find a map and a communications console. Lyria, unable to operate the complex equipment, allows Vanna to attempt a broadcast. Lyria decides to use the signal to call for help, despite the risk of drawing enemies to their location. She leans into the microphone and begins her broadcast as the chapter concludes.




### Chapter 71: Ephraim: From the Static
Ephraim, Electra, and Pax have been searching for survivors for two weeks aboard their ship. They monitor passive sensors for signs of life while managing the psychological toll of their failed mission. Suddenly, a weak, broken signal interrupts the static, providing coordinates and a plea for aid. Ephraim recognizes the voice as Lyria's, despite the common belief that she was lost.

The trio works together to reconstruct the fragmented message, confirming it is indeed Lyria asking for help. They realize she is at the Red Hand headquarters, which was the same site where Volga was likely held. Although Pax wants to head there immediately, Ephraim hesitates, noting they lack the manpower to assault such a heavily fortified position.

They discuss potential allies, ruling out the Republic and Alltribe, who are on the brink of war and would likely view their signal as a trap. With few options available, Pax suggests that Mars itself might be their only hope if they can boost the signal. They decide that amplifying Lyria's cry for help is the only way to gain the resources needed to intervene.




### Chapter 72: Lyria: One Last Tooth
Lyria and Freckles navigate the metal halls of the Can, eventually running into Red Hand guards near the communications room. They are quickly overpowered, and Lyria is thrown into a jail cell. She finds Victra au Julii imprisoned inside, beaten and barely conscious. Harmony arrives with her men, demanding to know where the acid is hidden, but Lyria manages to trick her.

Lyria uses a hidden molar containing acid to free Victra from her manacles and open the cell door. Victra, despite her severe injuries, launches a brutal attack on the guards, killing them in a display of lethal efficiency. Harmony flees the scene, but Victra, though wounded, pursues her, ultimately defeating her in a confrontation. Harmony is thrown into a pit of vipers, suffering a horrific death.

Victra, Lyria, and Freckles then descend into the township to confront the remaining Red Hand forces. Victra engages the Red Hand guards, systematically destroying them while Lyria and Freckles provide support. They eventually reach the main tunnel where Volga and her fellow slaves are fighting the Red Hand. The arrival of Victra and the grenades provided by Lyria help turn the tide, causing the Red Hand to disintegrate as the slaves push upward.




### Chapter 73: Lyria: At Last, She Screams
Victra and Lyria continue their pursuit of Harmony through the tunnels. Victra eventually corners Harmony in a chamber, leading to a tense standoff where Harmony attempts to lure Victra into a trap. Lyria intervenes, revealing a hidden chasm filled with pitvipers by tossing a flare into the dark space. Harmony is thrown into the pit, where she is immobilized and brutally killed by the vipers.

After Harmony's death, the Red Hand's organization collapses, and the slaves successfully liberate the encampment. Lyria finds her girls, including Lion and Freckles, safe in the aftermath of the battle. Volga reunites with Lyria, but Victra warns them that their problems are far from over. They discover that a massive Red Hand torchShip is rising from the sea, posing a new and deadly threat to their freedom.

Despite their victory in the tunnels, the threat of the torchShip looms over them, seemingly insurmountable. As they monitor the situation, they spot dots appearing on their scanners, signaling reinforcements. Lyria whispers that help has arrived from Mars, shifting the balance of power once more.




### Chapter 74: Ephraim: Son of the Rising
Ephraim, Pax, and Electra lead a ragtag fleet composed of various ships to assault the Red Hand torchShip. Their efforts are supported by the arrival of hundreds of ships from across Mars, answering the signal broadcast by Lyria. The combined fleet launches a coordinated attack on the torchShip, which is forced to defend itself without advanced AI or Blue support. Ephraim takes command of the fleet, utilizing his experience and Pax's piloting skills to execute a daring strategy.

The Snowball, flown by Pax, plays a critical role in the assault, diving at the torchShip and delivering a decisive blow to its underbelly. The combined attack causes the torchShip to break in two and plummet into the sea, effectively destroying the threat. The civilian ships land near the mine to rescue the survivors, including Volga, Victra, and Lyria. The victors celebrate their success, marking a pivotal turning point in the conflict.

Following the battle, Ephraim reunites with Volga, and they share an emotional moment. Pax and Electra are reunited with Victra, who shows unexpected signs of compassion. Ephraim eventually decides that he must return to help Sefi deal with the traitor Xenophon. Before leaving, he shares a final, heartfelt moment with Volga, promising to return to her, and sets off on his mission to secure their future.
