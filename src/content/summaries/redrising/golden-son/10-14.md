### 10: Broken
Darrow watches a video of Eo’s execution and hears her reveal her pregnancy to Dio on the scaffold. He retreats into isolation at the Jackal’s private skyscraper to grieve the loss of his unborn child and wife. Harmony shares the story of her own children dying from radiation to convince Darrow to embrace savagery. The Jackal undergoes medical treatment for his burned arm while planning to purchase Darrow’s contract at the Summit’s end. Darrow returns to the Citadel and spends three days training in the gymnasium while avoiding his friends.

Theodora accidentally spills wine on Darrow’s white uniform and reacts with visible terror. Darrow decides to wear a black uniform instead and visits Roque’s room before the gala begins. Roque reveals that he plans to bid on Darrow during the auction to keep him in House Mars. Darrow asks Roque if he ever feels lost, prompting Roque to share a story about two carrier pigeons. Roque promises to protect Darrow and affirms their friendship regardless of what transpires at the gala.

Darrow produces an automatic syringe and injects a sedative into Roque’s neck. Roque falls onto his bed as the drug takes effect and his eyes drift closed. Darrow realizes that saving Roque’s life will expose his knowledge of the coming bombing. He leaves his sleeping friend behind to join the ArchGovernor’s entourage for the evening’s festivities. The act of betrayal causes Darrow significant emotional distress as he prepares for the mission.



### 11: Red
Darrow joins Victra and Tactus to walk toward the Citadel Gardens for the closing gala. The Jackal arrives in a separate car and joins the Family Augustus to present a united front. The group walks through a groomed garden filled with rose trees and genetically engineered animals like monkeycats and tigerlynx. They board a marble lift with gravthrusters alongside the rival Falthe family to ascend the Sovereign’s tower. Darrow wears his razor coiled around his forearm as a concealed weapon among the other lancers.

The gala takes place in a simulated winter fairyland at the top of a two-kilometer-high tower. Snow falls from invisible clouds and settles on the trees and the guests’ formal attire. Darrow attaches a pegasus pendant containing a bomb to the underside of the ArchGovernor’s table. He encounters Antonia au Severus, who mocks him and warns that the Bellona family is coming for him. Darrow wanders the party and observes the Sovereign, Octavia au Lune, standing with her three Furies.

Karnus au Bellona shares a drink with Darrow and insults his dead brother Julian. Mustang enters the gala wearing a black funeral gown and escorted by Cassius au Bellona. Cassius is revealed as the new Morning Knight, one of the Sovereign's twelve Olympic Knights. Darrow watches them from a distance and feels a deep sense of failure and jealousy. He initially prepares to activate his bomb but stops himself to seek a different path for the rebellion.



### 12: The Willow
Darrow retrieves the bomb from under the table and walks toward the Bellona family seating area. He leaps thirty feet in the low gravity and lands directly on top of the Bellona table. Darrow shouts for attention and publicly accuses the Bellona family of being dishonorable cowards and liars. He demands a duel to the death to satisfy his stained honor after the events at the Academy. Darrow tips a wine decanter onto Cassius’s lap to force a direct confrontation.

The Sovereign declares that the duel will proceed according to ancient laws in a cleared snowy plain. Darrow meets with Augustus and secures a blood blessing for the fight by cutting his own face. He reveals to Cassius that he was trained in the Willow Way by the legendary Lorn au Arcos. Darrow uses a razor that can shift between a solid sword and a flexible whip. He dominates the fight by using circular movements and precise strikes to mutilate Cassius’s limbs.

Darrow severes Cassius’s right arm with his razor just as the Sovereign attempts to stop the duel. A massive brawl erupts among the noble families as the Bellona lancers charge into the circle. Tactus kills Cagney au Bellona by cutting her in half after she tries to attack Darrow. The Sovereign’s Praetorians, including the dark-skinned Fury Aja, intervene to evacuate Octavia au Lune from the chaos. The Jackal uses a silver stylus to poison Leto, allowing Karnus to decapitate the ArchGovernor’s favorite ward.



### 13: Mad Dogs
Darrow flees the ruined gala with the Augustus entourage while carrying a wounded Praetor. Seven members of their group are confirmed dead, including Leto and several other lancers. They board a lift and witness the Falthe family lancers slaughtering the Thorne family in the gardens below. Daxo au Telemanus uses a data jammer to prevent the families from contacting their ships in orbit. The group discovers that all family ships have been removed from the hangars on the Sovereign's orders.

A squad of ninety-nine Obsidian Praetorians in purple-and-black armor lands around the Augustus group. They are led by the new Rage Knight, who is revealed to be Fitchner au Barca. Fitchner wears a gold cape and a helmet shaped like a laughing wolfskull. He uses a "stunFist," a non-lethal energy weapon, to incapacitate both Augustus and the Jackal. Fitchner informs the group that he has already killed Augustus’s Stained bodyguards, Alfrún and Jopho.

Kavax au Telemanus and his son Daxo agree to follow Fitchner’s instructions to avoid immediate execution. Fitchner places the ArchGovernor and his family under house arrest in a nearby villa. He demands that Darrow accompany him separately to see the Sovereign for an audience. Darrow surrenders his weapon and agrees to follow Fitchner to protect his remaining friends. The Olympic Knight promises that the families will not be harmed if they do not resist.



### 14: The Sovereign
Darrow enters a Spartan suite to meet with Octavia au Lune as rain falls outside. The Sovereign sits on a couch while her granddaughter's guardian, Aja, watches from a nearby chair. Aja is the Protean Knight and wears gold and midnight blue armor decorated with sea serpents. A young boy named Lysander, the Sovereign’s eight-year-old grandson, enters and sits with Aja. Darrow and the Sovereign discuss the legal consequences of his actions and his refusal to obey her orders.

The Sovereign offers Darrow a position as an Olympic Knight if he agrees to serve her. Darrow refuses the offer and demands the head of Cassius au Bellona to fulfill the duel's terms. He reveals that Lorn au Arcos is Lysander’s grandfather and retired because of the Sovereign's actions. Three Stained warriors in bone masks and purple armor block the door with pulseAxes. Lysander pleads with Darrow to join them and avoid being killed by the grandmother’s guards.

Darrow relaxes his blade as the Sovereign proposes a game of naked truths instead of combat. Lysander retrieves a small wooden box from another room and brings it to the table. The Sovereign explains that if Darrow wins the game, he can request anything of her. She promises to personally execute Cassius if Darrow succeeds in their upcoming match. Darrow opens the wooden box as Fitchner and Aja watch the proceedings in silence.
