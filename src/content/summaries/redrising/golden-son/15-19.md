### Chapter 15: Truth
Darrow undergoes a truth-telling game called the Oracle, which involves two genetically engineered creatures that sense physiological changes and deliver a agonizing poison if a lie is detected. The Sovereign, Octavia au Lune, uses a gold needle ring to draw blood from Darrow and herself to activate the Oracles, which are part scorpion, pitviper, and centipede. During the interrogation, Darrow reveals the location of ArchGovernor Augustus's unregistered electromagnetic weapons and learns that the Sovereign aided Karnus in his academy attack. Fitchner is revealed as the Rage Knight, one of the Sovereign's twelve Olympic Knights who serve as her elite protectors and enforcers. Darrow discovers that Fitchner provided evidence of the Mars Institute's corruption to Octavia as leverage against the Proctors. The game concludes when Darrow asks if Octavia planned to let the Bellona family assassinate Augustus at the gala, and the Oracle strikes her when she lies and says no.

Mustang enters the room in House Lune colors and reports the casualty counts from the gala skirmish while confirming that Cassius survived and is receiving medical care. Octavia admits she lied about the assassination plan because she views the Augustus family as a threat to Society peace and her control over helium-3 quotas. The Sovereign explains that she destroyed the moon Rhea sixty years ago to end a rebellion, resulting in fifty million deaths. She shows Mustang holos of civil unrest on Venus, claiming the Sons of Ares are spreading and that Augustus allowed them to thrive on Mars. Order Zero is mentioned as a total media blackout used to contain information about the uprising. Mustang ultimately agrees to let the Sovereign move against her father, stating that the man she once loved is dead.

Darrow is escorted to his new quarters in the Citadel, where Fitchner encourages him to embrace his new life as the Sovereign's personal emissary. He is given a fifty-million-credit stipend and ten million for a harem, though he feels trapped by the opulence and his betrayal of his friends. Darrow observes a Pink, a slave class bred for beauty and physical pleasure, waiting in his bed but ignores her. He reflects on his feelings for Eo and Mustang while waiting for his door to open, hoping for an escape. A male Pink delivers an onyx box containing a holoCube from Mustang that instructs Darrow to take cover. Sevro and nine Howlers, a group of elite soldiers wearing wolf-pelt cloaks, shatter the window and infiltrate the room during a lunar storm.


### Chapter 16: The Game
Octavia justifies her plan to eliminate the Augustus family by citing the need to maintain the "figment of imagination" that is her power through fear. She explains that a full rebellion on Mars would be too costly for the Society to sustain, making a swift assassination more efficient. Mustang watches images of riots on Venus and learns that Order Zero has been implemented to hide the spread of the Sons of Ares. The Sovereign argues that removing Augustus and installing the Bellona family will bring peace to Mars and snuff out the rebellion. Octavia reminds Mustang that her father hanged the woman whose death sparked the Sons of Ares movement. Mustang denounces her father, telling the Sovereign to do with him as she likes.

Darrow's internal monologue reveals his struggle to reconcile his mission with the potential deaths of his friends like Roque and Victra. He receives a stipend and a Pink servant, which he views as the price of his betrayal. The Citadel is described as a massive fortress on Luna, punctured by buildings like glittering spears. Darrow recognizes that Pinks are used as a distraction to make Golds forget the suffering of others. He waits in his room without his razor, the signature weapon of the Gold class, while Obsidians guard the hallway. Mustang's holoCube message triggers a power outage that locks the door and plunges the room into darkness.

Sevro appears at the window wearing a wolfshead helmet and a wolfpelt, accompanied by eight other Howlers including Quinn. They use a pulseFist, a gauntlet that fires concussive energy blasts, to break the reinforced glass after setting up a jamField to block sound. The Howlers weld the door shut with plasma torches to prevent the Obsidian guards from entering. Sevro reveals that Mustang brought the Howlers back from the Rim, the outer edge of the Solar System, to serve as an insurance policy. He presents Darrow with a large bag that contains a mysterious, moving "package" meant to be used as leverage. The group prepares to leave the Citadel and head toward the ArchGovernor's villa to rescue their allies.


### Chapter 17: What the Storm Brings
Darrow and the Howlers dive out of the high-rise window and freefall through the lunar clouds before activating their gravBoots. They maneuver through the Citadel, avoiding a ripWing, a fast and agile Gold fighter craft, by hiding behind a spire. The group lands on the wall of the Augustus villa, noting that the lights are out and no fighting is visible. Darrow realizes the Sovereign wants the massacre to look like a Bellona honor killing to avoid direct implication. Mustang is scheduled to arrive with a shuttle, specifically a stork transport ship, in ten minutes. The Howlers infiltrate the seventh level of the villa, finding it empty of Augustan family members.

They descend through the villa, finding the first signs of slaughter on the sixth level where dozens of low-color servants have been executed. Darrow sees a female Augustan lancer killed by a Gold Praetorian, the Sovereign's elite guards who wear black armor with purple fringe. The Howlers find more dead Gold knights near the stairwells and elevators as they move toward the lower floors. Darrow jumps seven stories down the center of the spiral staircase, entering a jamField that hides the sounds of active combat. He lands on the first floor and kills four Praetorian Grays using his razor in a violent loop. The Howlers follow him down, using their aegis shields to block magnetic railRifle fire from the attacking squads.

Karnus au Bellona and three Bellona Obsidians arrive, prompting Darrow to call for a tactical retreat up the stairwell. Sevro uses a pulseFist to blast a hole in the ceiling, allowing the group to fly back into the rainy sky above the villa. Darrow shouts for Aja au Grimmus, the Protean Knight, to draw her out into the garden near the hot springs. A squad of Praetorians, including a Stained Obsidian named Varga with a skull tattoo, uncloaks and confronts the Howlers. Darrow demands to speak with Aja, using his status as a Peerless Scarred to avoid immediate execution. Aja emerges from the steam, revealing that the remaining Augustans are hiding underwater in the lagoon to mask their infrared signatures.


### Chapter 18: Bloodstains
Aja tells Darrow that the Augustans in the pool will drown or die of oxygen deprivation within minutes. She informs him that Victra has been spared due to her mother's cooperation, but two Augustan ships were shot down in orbit. Darrow insults Aja's honor for slaughtering families in the night, provoking her to draw her razor. Sevro signals Weed to fly down from the wall carrying the large bag containing the "present." Darrow opens the bag to reveal Lysander au Lune, the Sovereign's young grandson and heir to the throne. He holds a razor to the boy's throat, forcing Aja to command her men to stand down.

The Howlers rescue the surviving Augustans from the lagoon, including the ArchGovernor, the Jackal, and the Telemanus family. Victra is pulled from the water wounded and shouting threats at Aja, while Roque and Theodora are also recovered. Tactus is brought out of the villa bleeding from multiple wounds after a heavy fight against the Praetorians. Darrow bargains for their safe passage to a hovering stork transport ship by using Lysander as a human shield. The Sovereign speaks through Aja's comm-link, telling Darrow that the moon Rhea was turned to ash for its defiance. She warns Darrow that her legions will follow him to Mars and that Lysander is his only bargaining chip.

As the Howlers and Augustans board the ship, Aja executes a sudden, lethal strike against Quinn. She grabs Quinn by the hair and slams her head into the ground before punching her temple multiple times with an armored fist. Quinn suffers a seizure and collapses as Aja backs away with a smile, knowing Darrow cannot retaliate without risking Lysander. The Jackal, Adrius au Augustus, dives down from the ship to scoop the unconscious Quinn from the ground and bring her aboard. The stork's bay doors close, sealing the survivors inside as the ship begins its ascent from Luna. Darrow is left with the agonizing realization that his bargaining chip could only be spent once.


### Chapter 19: Stork
The Jackal uses a silver magnetic resonance imager and Darrow's datapad to diagnose Quinn with severe brain swelling and hemorrhaging. Roque remains by Quinn's side, singing a story about messenger pigeons from Old Earth as she continues to seize. The Jackal determines that Quinn has bone fragments in her brain and will die without immediate access to a professional medBay. Darrow speaks with the Jackal, who reveals that he believes Mustang's infiltration of the Sovereign's court was her own idea. Victra confirms her loyalty to House Augustus, despite her mother Agrippina's alliance with the Sovereign. Sevro watches Roque and Quinn from a distance, hiding his own grief behind his hawkish features.

Darrow enters the flight cabin where Mustang is piloting the stork alongside a Blue pilot who is jacked into the ship's systems. Kavax au Telemanus mourns the loss of his son Pax while Augustus prepares for a full-scale war against the Sovereign on Mars. Mustang ignores Darrow and removes the horse ring he gave her, indicating a shift in their relationship. Pliny, the ArchGovernor's Politico, attempts to argue for peace and reconciliation, but Augustus firmly rejects the idea. The ArchGovernor acknowledges Darrow's loyalty and promises to reward him once they reach the safety of the Mars fleet. The ship begins to vibrate as it enters the low atmosphere in preparation for its escape from Luna.

The Blue pilot reports that the cargo bay door has been opened from the inside, indicating a security breach. Darrow realizes that Tactus has betrayed them and is attempting to escape with Lysander to earn the Sovereign's favor. He sprints to the cargo hold with Sevro and Victra, finding Clown and another lancer unconscious and bleeding. Tactus has already jumped from the ship using gravBoots, taking the Sovereign's grandson with him into the darkness. Sevro is enraged and wants to jump after him, but the hatch is closed and Tactus is already a distant dot. Darrow calculates they have only two minutes before the Sovereign's ripWings arrive to board the ship.
