### 5: Supper
Darrow sits in a shuttle among ArchGovernor Augustus’s lancers as they travel through the Scepter Armada toward Luna for the Summit. He feels isolated and vulnerable because his contract with Augustus is ending in three days, leaving him without political protection. Darrow recalls how Julia au Bellona, mother of the boy he killed at the Institute, hungers for his death and maintains a ritual of mourning to shame her family into seeking vengeance. The lancers mock Darrow's lack of fencing skill, suggesting that Cassius au Bellona will surely challenge him to a duel once his immunity expires. Roque attempts to comfort Darrow, promising that other families will bid for his contract despite the risk of going against Augustus.

Mustang’s final words to Darrow before he left for the Academy haunt him, as she had begged him to choose a path of peace over becoming her father's warlord. Darrow reflects on his loneliness and the distance he has maintained from those he loves to protect his secrets as a Red agent. He notes that Sevro and the Howlers have been reassigned to Pluto, a move he blames on the ArchGovernor’s advisor, Pliny. Roque explains that violence and bloodfeuds are illegal within the Citadel unless sanctioned by the Sovereign, offering Darrow a sliver of hope for safety. The shuttle approaches Luna, a city-moon surrounded by a massive halo of satellites and orbital installations.

As the shuttle lands, Darrow is filled with dread at the prospect of being a discarded commodity in the high-stakes political environment of the moon. He realizes that while he was enough for Mustang, he was never enough for his late wife Eo, which deepens his internal conflict. Roque promises to support Darrow as much as possible, though his family's loyalty to Augustus prevents them from bidding on Darrow's contract directly. The group prepares to disembark into the heart of the Society’s power, where Darrow’s survival depends on his ability to find a new master. Darrow stares at the amber glow of Luna, feeling like a slave despite his Golden appearance and Sigils.





### 6: Icarus
The group lands at the Citadel on Luna, where the low gravity of one-sixth Earth makes Darrow feel clumsy and claustrophobic. Darrow observes the Luneborn cityscape, which features massive towers linked by a network of RungPaths used by citizens to traverse the air. The household of Augustus is assigned a luxury villa surrounded by thirty acres of pine forest and stone fountains. Roque declines Darrow's invitation to train in kravat, a form of martial arts, because he must attend a lecture by the billionaire Regulus ag Sun, known as Quicksilver. They discuss the legendary Rage Knight, Lorn au Arcos, who famously insulted the Sovereign at her coronation.

A Pink steward attempts to assign Darrow a small, cramped room, but Darrow’s servant Theodora intervenes by asserting her status as a former Rose of the Gardens. Theodora warns Darrow that the holoNet is filled with rumors speculating about his impending death and where he will be buried. Victra invites Darrow to her private terrace, where she mocks his lack of status and his choice of friends like Tactus. Darrow explains that Tactus sold a valuable violin Darrow gave him to buy drugs, proving he is not a true friend. Victra warns Darrow that Pliny has been working to destroy his reputation since he first joined Augustus.

Victra offers Darrow a dataSlip containing the location of a tavern in the low-districts, suggesting he find an alternative to waiting for death in the Citadel. Darrow is hesitant to leave the safety of the Citadel grounds, but Victra convinces him that staying makes him a target for the Bellona family. Darrow and Victra use ghostCloaks to slip away from the villa and meet a team of Gray mercenaries known as lurchers. Colonel Valentin, the leader of the lurchers, scans them for bugs before they board a shuttle bound for the Lost City. As the ship departs, Valentin announces that "Icarus is on the move" to his hidden support teams.





### 7: The Afterbirth
The shuttle descends from the high Gold districts through the Silver, Copper, and mid-Color sectors to the gutters of the city. Darrow observes the extreme poverty and pollution of the low-districts, where thousands of low-Colors live in tiny, windowless apartments. He sees a ten-story digital graffiti image of his dead wife, Eo, which serves as a symbol of hope and rebellion across the worlds. The lurchers escort Darrow and Victra into a dirty garage in a lawless area filled with gangs and drug trade. They walk through humid streets thick with the smell of rot toward a massive, fifteen-level tavern called the Lost Wee Den.

Darrow wears an Obsidian disguise with white hair and black eyes to blend into the rough environment. Inside the tavern, he finds the Jackal, Augustus’s son Adrius, lounging behind a group of mercenaries and a Stained Obsidian bodyguard. Victra leaves Darrow with the Jackal, who reveals that Pliny has emptied Darrow's bank accounts and completely shattered his reputation. The Jackal explains that he has spent his exile building a business empire and acquiring thirty percent of the Society’s media infrastructure. He claims that by controlling the flow of information, he can contaminate the public's perception and manipulate entire populations.

The Jackal proposes an alliance, stating that he needs Darrow to be his "sword" and charismatic figurehead while he acts as the "sceptre." He reveals that the Sovereign and her Furies secretly control the crime syndicates in the Lost City to perform their dirty work. The Jackal intends to help ambitious low-Colors overthrow the current syndicate leadership in exchange for their help in hunting the Sons of Ares. He tells Darrow that the Sons are a "termite colony" gnawing at the Society's foundation and must be eliminated to gain his father's favor. Darrow realizes the Jackal is a sociopath but feels he has no other options for survival.





### 8: Alliance
Darrow reacts with internal shock to the Jackal's plan to destroy the Sons of Ares but maintains his Golden persona. The Jackal explains that Cassius au Bellona is being groomed to become an Olympic Knight, filling one of the vacancies left by retiring legends. Darrow agrees to the alliance on the condition that the Howlers and Sevro are brought back under his command. The Jackal tasks Darrow with his first mission: to kill a man named Fencor au Drusilla to gain favor with a powerful cousin of the Sovereign. Darrow is surprised when Evey, a Pink girl from his past who once had prosthetic wings, appears at the tavern as a syndicate ambassador.

Evey recognizes Darrow through his Obsidian disguise, but the Jackal mistakes Darrow's reaction for lust and offers her to him for the night. The Jackal assaults Evey for hesitating, and Darrow intervenes by taking her upstairs to a private room. Above the bar, holoCans begin broadcasting news of multiple terrorist bombings targeting Gold establishments across Luna and Mars. Evey reveals that she is working with a cell of the Sons of Ares and that her mission was to kill the Jackal. She informs Darrow that Adrius has murdered fifteen of their comrades and that the room is rigged to explode.

Darrow seizes a datapad from a Red insurgent and uses his gravBoots to fly back toward the Jackal’s table to save him. On the tavern's screens, a figure in a red helm identifies himself as Ares and declares, "Reap what you sow." Evey’s bomb detonates, but the Jackal’s Stained bodyguard throws his body over the device to shield his master. The Stained is instantly vaporized as a black-hole-like energy blossom consumes the central drinking hall. Darrow grabs the unconscious Jackal and smashes through a wall just as the entire tavern collapses into a ruin of fire and ash.





### 9: The Darkness
Darrow survives the explosion but suffers a dislocated shoulder, bleeding ears, and severe internal trauma. He rescues the unconscious and badly burned Jackal, leaving him for his support teams before stealing a Gray police craft. Using the hacked coordinates from the Red insurgent's datapad, Darrow tracks the cell to an abandoned factory in the Old Industrial Sector. He is captured by Red insurgents, bound, and taken to a laboratory where he finds Mickey the Carver and Evey. Mickey, who appears aged and beaten, is being forced to carve a new army of Red soldiers for the Sons.

Evey justifies the tavern bombing as a necessary strike against the Golds, but Darrow rebukes her for killing hundreds of low-Colors. Harmony, a scarred leader of the Sons, enters and informs Darrow that his mentor Dancer is dead, killed by a lurcher squad. She explains that the Sons are launching a massive, system-wide offensive involving hundreds of bombings in three nights. Harmony wants Darrow to carry a radium bomb into the Sovereign's gala and commit a suicide attack to decapitate the Gold leadership. Darrow refuses the mission, arguing that a bloodbath will only lead to the total eradication of the Reds.

Harmony reveals that Titus, the brutal Red agent Darrow encountered at the Institute, was her protege and that his death has been avenged. To manipulate Darrow, Harmony plays the unedited footage of his wife Eo’s execution, which he had never seen in full. Darrow watches as his sister-in-law, Dio, climbs the scaffold to speak with Eo in her final moments. He uses Mickey's equipment to amplify the audio and hears Eo’s final secret whispered to Dio. Eo reveals that she was pregnant with Darrow's child, a fact she hid to prevent his heart from breaking.
