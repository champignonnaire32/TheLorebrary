### 30: Gathering Storm
Darrow stands on the command deck of the *Pax* with Orion, observing his fleet which now includes ships captured from Kellan au Bellona. Kavax and Daxo au Telemanus arrive to discuss the division of the captured warships as spoils of war. Darrow negotiates a thirty percent share for House Telemanus to compensate for their losses during the boarding actions. Daxo reveals that Ragnar Volarus took command of a boarding party after the Gold leaders were killed by Bellona blade dancers. Darrow praises Ragnar's performance and speculates to the Telemanuses about the tactical benefits of allowing Obsidians to lead without Gold supervision. Orion warns Darrow that he is overstepping social boundaries before an assault shuttle carrying Virginia au Augustus arrives at the fleet.

Virginia, known as Mustang, storms off her ship onto the hangar deck of the *Pax* while flanked by Obsidian bodyguards and Gray mercenaries. She informs Darrow's assembled council that Pliny has initiated a coup on Mars and captured her father, the ArchGovernor. Mustang explains that Pliny led her fleet into a Bellona ambush at the Academy, resulting in ten thousand deaths and the loss of her forces. She reveals that Pliny attempted to force her into marriage to legitimize his new position as a proxy for the Sovereign. Darrow orders the immediate disposal of Mustang's shuttle to prevent Pliny from using its tracking beacons to locate the hidden fleet. An Orange engineer named Cyther proposes using the shuttle's scanners to mislead Pliny's hunters instead of simply destroying the vessel.

Darrow and Mustang visit the ship's medbay, where Roque au Fabii is keeping a vigil over the body of the deceased Tactus. Roque expresses bitterness toward Darrow, blaming Darrow's grand schemes for the deaths of their friends and the loss of Quinn. The group convenes a war council where Mustang shows holo footage of her father's failed raid on the Ganymede shipyards. The footage reveals that Cassius au Bellona is alive with a mended arm and that the Julii fleet assisted the Sovereign in defeating Augustus. The council members argue over the loyalty of Victra au Julii, with many suggesting she should be imprisoned as a potential spy for her mother. Roque passionately defends Victra's steadfast loyalty since the Academy, prompting Darrow to publicly reaffirm his trust in her.

### 31: Coup
Virginia au Augustus arrives on the *Pax* via a smoking assault shuttle accompanied by Obsidian bodyguards and Gray mercenaries. She reports that Pliny has seized control of Mars through a coup and arrested her brother, the Jackal. Mustang details how Pliny betrayed her fleet to the Bellona, resulting in the destruction of ten thousand men during a failed operation. She recounts Pliny's offer of marriage and his claims of legitimacy granted by the Sovereign following the fall of House Augustus. Darrow meets her with a group including Lorn au Arcos, Sevro, and Victra, while noting Roque's absence from the greeting. Mustang produces a physical eye she claimed to have taken from Pliny as a response to his marriage proposal.

Sevro collects the discarded eye while Darrow orders the hangar crew to prepare for the fleet's move to a new sector. Darrow commands an Orange engineer named Cyther to eject Mustang's shuttle from the airlock to prevent Pliny from tracking it. Cyther suggests a more sophisticated plan to plant the shuttle's tracking equipment on a long-range scout to misdirect their pursuers. Mustang admits she does not know if her escape was genuine or if Pliny allowed it to happen to lead him to Darrow. The Jackal's mercenaries are identified as the ones who facilitated Mustang's flight from the Academy station before her capture. Darrow accepts Cyther's tactical suggestion and promises the engineer a position on the Citadel staff if the ruse succeeds.

The council prepares to move to the warroom to discuss their next steps for the liberation of Mars and the rescue of the ArchGovernor. Mustang reveals that Pliny has already begun persuading her father's bannermen to swear new oaths of fealty to the Sovereign. Lorn expresses concern for Mustang's physical state, but she dismisses his suggestions for rest in favor of immediate planning. Darrow calls for provisions to be brought to the warroom, including enough food and stimulants for the expanded council of twenty. Sevro remains fixated on the morbid trophy of Pliny's eye, adding it to his collection of macabre items. The fleet begins its maneuvers to exit the current sector before any Bellona reinforcements can arrive to engage them.

### 32: Die Young
Mustang insists on visiting the medbay to see Tactus before the commencement of the war council meeting. They find Roque sitting beside Tactus's corpse, where he has remained since the engagement at Europa ended. Roque criticizes Darrow's leadership, suggesting that Darrow values his grand schemes more than the lives of his close companions. Theodora informs Darrow that Roque specifically asked not to be disturbed while he mourned his fallen friend in private. Mustang comforts Roque, and they briefly discuss the tragedy of Tactus's life and his unfulfilled desire to please his friends. Roque reveals that Tactus had kept the Stratovarian violin Darrow gave him and had been practicing a sonata in secret.

In the warroom, Mustang displays holo footage of the ArchGovernor's disastrous attempt to seize ships from the Ganymede shipyards. The recording shows Augustus being overwhelmed by Praetorians and Olympic Knights, including the Morning Knight, Cassius au Bellona. Cassius and Fitchner, the Rage Knight, are seen standing over the defeated and bleeding ArchGovernor on the deck of a ship. The council members debate the involvement of the Julii fleet, which provided the final support needed to crush Augustus's forces. Kavax and Daxo express deep suspicion toward Victra, arguing that she should be confined to her quarters for the duration of the war. They believe she is a spy for her mother, Agrippina, who has aligned with the Sovereign for financial gain.

Roque stands and delivers a firm defense of Victra, highlighting her loyalty during the Academy and the assassination attempt on Luna. He threatens to leave the fellowship if the council continues to bicker and question the loyalty of those who have remained steadfast. Darrow breaks his silence to support Roque's position, stating that they must choose to trust their friends regardless of their origins. Victra expresses her gratitude to Roque and Darrow, asserting that her ships and her men belong to her alone. Mustang shifts the focus back to the immediate threat of Pliny's fleet and the impending execution of her father in Agea. She warns that if they do not rescue the ArchGovernor, they will be left alone to face the entire Society's wrath.

### 33: A Dance
Darrow wakes from a dream of his past life with Eo to find Mustang waiting outside his suite's door. He initially hesitates to let her in to avoid further emotional complications, but ultimately opens the door for her. Mustang enters the room wearing a black kimono and carrying food she intended for Ragnar, who was guarding the hall. She inspects Darrow's living quarters, which are decorated with wood and stone carvings meant to evoke ancient human history. They discuss Mozart, whom Mustang describes as a rebel genius, while Darrow finds the classical music to be stuffy. Mustang recounts stories of her childhood, including her father's irrational fear of robots and her mother's laughter at his paranoia.

The two move into the circular training room where Mustang examines the friezes depicting the history of the House of Grimmus. Mustang challenges Darrow to a sparring match using practice razors, which are hard and painful but have no cutting edges. During the fight, she accuses Darrow of being a hypocrite for making himself into a simple warrior while preaching about choice. She hits Darrow's kneecap and forces him to defend his favorite dissertation, "The Insects of Specialization," which critiques the limitations of Gold Society. Mustang argues that Darrow is becoming exactly like her father by following a path of solitary warfare and emotional isolation. Darrow's thoughts drift to Eo, causing a visible change in his expression that Mustang notices and interprets as a rejection.

Mustang confronts Darrow about the girl he lost, realizing that her memory still stands between them and prevents any deep connection. She expresses frustration at Darrow's tendency to pull her close only to push her away when she seeks emotional honesty. Darrow remains silent, unable to explain that his entire identity is a lie built upon the memory of his dead wife. Mustang tells Darrow that he will eventually become a mirror of her father if he continues to shut people out. She stands by the door and asks Darrow to request that she stay with him for the night. Darrow looks at his hands and fails to answer, and by the time he looks up, Mustang has already left.

### 34: Blood Brothers
Lorn's scouts intercept a supply vessel carrying foodstuffs to Pliny's fleet at Hildas Station to use as a Trojan horse. Darrow and his inner circle hide inside crates of protofiber meals within the cargo bay for a fifteen-hour journey. Sevro and the Howlers eat the rations while Roque and Victra converse, and Mustang avoids Darrow's gaze by speaking with the Telemanuses. Lorn advises Darrow that he must use tact and valid arguments to win over the bannermen currently aligned with Pliny. Darrow defends Ragnar's recent leadership during ship boarding actions, but Lorn insists that Obsidians are merely biological weapons bred for service. Lorn warns Darrow that men do not change and that trusting a bad man will eventually lead to betrayal.

Darrow finds Ragnar in a freezer unit and attempts to engage him in a conversation about the nature of his service. Ragnar asserts that as a Stained Obsidian, he is bound to obey Darrow's commands without question or personal desire. Darrow challenges this by cutting his own finger and smearing Red blood over Ragnar's Obsidian sigils while smearing Obsidian blood on himself. He declares that they are brothers of the same flesh and dirt, rejecting the notion that Gold is divine. Ragnar expresses deep fear at the concept of freedom, stating that the history of his kind is one of fire and sky-iron. Darrow reveals his true origins, telling Ragnar about his wife's execution and his own death and resurrection by the Sons of Ares.

Darrow explains that the Golds have deceived the Obsidians for centuries to keep them enslaved through fabricated myths and technological terrors. He formally releases Ragnar from his service, demanding that the Obsidian choose his own path rather than following orders. Darrow tells Ragnar that he must decide whether to be a friend and rise with him or continue living as a slave. He leaves the freezer without demanding an oath of silence, allowing Ragnar the space to process the revelation of Darrow's humanity. Darrow reflects on the necessity of winning over the Obsidians to ensure the success of the coming revolution against the Society. He acknowledges that the truth is the only weapon capable of dismantling the kingdom of lies built by the Golds.
