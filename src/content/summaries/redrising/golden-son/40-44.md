### 40: Mud
The EMP detonation shuts down Darrow’s starShell and sends him sinking to the bottom of a river. He remains trapped in the mud under a ton of metal while his oxygen recycler fails. Two armored bodies sink near him as he realizes Cassius anticipated the attack. Darrow uses his chemical-switched razor to slice through his own suit's arm and gauntlet. He skins himself out of the exoskeleton and helmet, nearly nicking his own jugular in the process.

Darrow swims through the dark water and finds Ragnar weeping inside a paralyzed starShell. He cuts Ragnar free of the armor before both kick toward the surface for air. Ragnar drags a drowning Darrow to the riverbank among a field of dead Obsidians and Golds. Bellona Golds and Grays walk the bank using forcePikes and digger bullets to execute the survivors. Darrow and Ragnar cake themselves in mud to hide their tattoos and skin while scavenging razors from corpses.

Ragnar snaps a Gray's neck while Darrow kills two Obsidians from the shadows. Darrow takes a bullet to the bicep before slaughtering twelve enemies using Lorn’s kravat techniques. Ragnar slam-kills a Gold and holds a razor to the throat of the Peerless Legate Gaius au Carthus. Gaius refuses to yield to an Obsidian and dies when Ragnar pushes the blade through his throat. Darrow and Ragnar use stolen gravBoots to rescue Sevro and a handful of surviving Howlers from the mud.

### 41: Achilles
Darrow divides his remaining force and sends Ragnar to open the gates of the Agea wall. He gives Ragnar a razor and tells him to choose his own destiny as a leader of men. Thistle attempts to strip the weapon from Ragnar, but Sevro threatens to cut her down in response. Darrow tosses more scavenged razors to the other ten surviving Obsidians before they depart for the wall. The Howlers strip off their heavy armor to move faster toward the Citadel across fifteen kilometers of terrain.

The group sprints through commercial districts and lowDistricts while avoiding Gray platoons and shuttles. Darrow’s arm wound focuses his mind as they navigate the bazaar and graffiti-stained streets. Sevro scouts ahead and reports that Ragnar successfully opened twelve gates in the wall’s face. He also reveals that Ragnar killed the Wind Knight and nearly defeated Cassius in front of the army. The group reaches the Citadel grounds but finds their only pair of gravBoots has lost all mechanical power.

Mustang detonates the shield generators, causing the prismatic mist of the city's defense to shatter. Darrow abandons his friends and jumps over the Citadel wall to prevent the Sovereign from escaping. He sprints through rosebushes and past fountains toward a landing courtyard holding four black shuttles. Praetorians and Aja lead Octavia au Lune up the ramp of the primary armored ship. Darrow jumps and grips the edge of the rising ramp, pulling himself into the bay as the door pressurizes.

**



### 42: Death of a Gold
Darrow faces Octavia, Aja, Karnus, and Fitchner inside the rising shuttle's bay. He threatens them with a scavenged pulseGrenade, but the pilot performs a barrel roll to dislodge him. Aja kicks the grenade out of the open hatch where it explodes harmlessly in the night sky. Octavia activates a ghostCloak on the ship and orders Aja to decapitate Darrow. Karnus interrupts and demands the right to kill Darrow for the honor of the Bellona family.

Karnus charges Darrow with a razor, but Darrow side-flips and drives his blade through the giant's stomach. Darrow pulls the razor out of Karnus’s back and decapitates him as he collapses. He then throws his razor into the chest of a charging Praetorian to kill him instantly. Aja rushes forward and beats Darrow severely, slamming him against the ceiling until he sinks into the floor. Octavia orders Fitchner to finish the execution and bring her Darrow’s head for a pike.

Fitchner draws his razor as Aja reopens the drop ramp to discard Darrow’s body into the Martian wind. Darrow hallucinates his Uncle Narol and his wife Eo while fading from blood loss and exhaustion. Fitchner whispers that he is actually Ares and has been in control of the situation all along. He puts on his Rage Knight helmet and uses his pulseFist to scatter the Sovereign's Praetorians. Ares howls like a wolf and carries the unconscious Darrow out of the ship to safety.

**



### 43: The Sea
Darrow wakes up in a whitestone room overlooking the sea with fresh skingrafts covering his wounds. Mustang sleeps beside him on the bed while he remembers falling from the sky with Fitchner. He recalls waking to a Yellow's tools and a Carver's scalpel as they repaired his heart and skin. Darrow reflects on his childhood in Lykos and the dread he felt hearing his father leave for the mines. He kisses Mustang's forehead, causing her to wake and explain that he has been unconscious for a week.

Mustang informs Darrow that the Jackal's agents rescued Augustus from the Citadel after the shields fell. Roque has captured eighty percent of the Bellona fleet and earned the nickname "Nelson reincarnate" from the media. Darrow learns that Lorn and Victra have formed a triumvirate with Augustus against the Sovereign. The Governors of the Gas Giants and the Reformers are traveling to Agea for a summit. Cassius and his mother are missing following the death of the rest of the Bellona family.

Darrow breaks down in tears over the guilt of the war and the friends he has lost. Mustang holds him until his heart rate slows and he eventually stops shaking from the trauma. He abandons his restraint and his guilt regarding Eo to embrace Mustang as his lover. They peel away their clothing and engage in physical intimacy for the first time since the Institute. Darrow decides he will no longer run from the warmth of the only woman he loves.

**



### 44: The Poet
Darrow and Mustang walk through the estate hall and listen to Daxo and Roque joking about carpenters. Daxo identifies Darrow’s presence by the "scent of a suicidal madman" before pulling him into a hug. The group shares a large breakfast of bacon and coffee while enjoying a rare moment of peace. Darrow notices that Roque’s hug is mechanical and perfunctory, indicating their friendship remains damaged. He follows Roque onto the lawn where the poet is watching two deer eat from a feeder.

Roque explains that he values the tranquility of the countryside over the business of the cities. Darrow apologizes for taking Roque for granted and keeping him at arm’s length since the Academy. Roque rejects the apology, stating that Darrow used him like a horse rather than a true friend. He reminds Darrow that he sat by the bedsides of Quinn, Tactus, and Darrow during their injuries. Roque claims he would have switched places with any of them but doubts Darrow would do the same.

Sevro arrives in uniform and interrupts the conversation to deliver urgent news to Darrow. Roque walks away toward the deer, stating that friendships take years to repair after they are broken. Sevro ignores Darrow’s attempt to apologize for his own behavior and directs him to Ares. He reveals that the Jackal has captured Darrow’s friends: the Red, the Pink, and the Violet. The capture of Mickey, Evey, and Harmony forces Darrow to immediately pivot back to the war.
