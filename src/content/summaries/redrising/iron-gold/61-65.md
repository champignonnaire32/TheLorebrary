### Chapter 61: Lysander
Romulus au Raa enters the Hall of Justice in Sungrave to face a hearing for gross negligence during wartime. The Hall of Justice is a pyramid-shaped structure faced with shiny black stone and contains twelve Olympic Knights on an elevated podium. Dido au Raa acts as the accuser and presents holographic evidence of the destruction of the Ganymede docks. The evidence includes communication records between Romulus and Darrow that prove Romulus knew the docks were vulnerable. A final clip shows Darrow mocking Romulus and implying the nuclear threat used to justify their alliance was a lie.

Dido calls her daughter Seraphina to testify about her arrest and the nature of the evidence she carried from the Core. Seraphina confirms Romulus acted out of a father’s love to protect her but does not judge his negligence. Dido requests only the impeachment of her husband from his position as Sovereign of the Rim. Romulus stands and admits his guilt to the charges of negligence without offering any mitigating evidence. The Olympic Knights and the crowd appear satisfied with this honorable admission, which would have ended the trial with impeachment.

A young White girl named Chance interrupts the proceedings by ringing an iron bell to invoke an archaic legal power. The Olympic Council confers and adds a charge of arch-treason against Romulus on behalf of the State. Helios au Lux asks Romulus if he knew Darrow destroyed the docks and if he conspired to conceal the truth. Romulus admits he knew the truth because he had intercepted and killed the original brokers of the information. He explains he lied to prevent a war he believes will destroy the Society and its culture.



### Chapter 62: Lysander
Romulus says farewell to his children on a frozen sulfur dune while wearing a kryll and a scorosuit. He urges his son Marius to honor his memory and tells Diomedes to stay true to his own heart. Romulus speaks to Seraphina, explaining that his death is necessary to unify the Rim and prevent internal division. He warns her not to let the same spirit that consumed his brother destroy her own conscience. Lysander watches the scene from the edge of the ranks and is eventually approached by Romulus for a private conversation.

Romulus tells Lysander that there is still a chance to stop the impending war from destroying everyone. He reminds Lysander of the ancient unity between Silenius au Lune and Akari au Raa that created the Pax Solaris. Romulus asks Lysander to choose between running and becoming an echo of the great men of his bloodline. He sets his hand on Lysander’s shoulder and asks if the young heir can save the worlds. Romulus then walks to his wife Dido to share a final, toxic-air-exposed kiss before his execution ritual begins.

Romulus strips naked in the negative one hundred degree Celsius cold to perform his final walk toward the Dragon Tomb. The Dragon Tomb is a black obelisk shaped like a winged beast at the top of a rock crag. He roars his lineage and begins the eighty-step journey across the frozen sulfur waste as his skin turns purple. Romulus collapses at step fifty but forces himself up to continue until his legs finally fail ten steps from the monument. He dies in the cold, and Seraphina whispers a Latin phrase as the Raa family departs the site.



### Chapter 63: Lysander
Dido sits in a state of deep regret and loss by a window overlooking the sulfur plains. Lysander and Seraphina enter the room, and Lysander offers his condolences for the death of Romulus. Dido dismisses his pity and mocks Lysander for being a king without a kingdom who lacks military experience. She questions what value a Lune could possibly bring to the Rim’s upcoming war against the Rising. Lysander reflects on his own identity and decides he no longer fears the burden of his heritage or his bloodline.

He tells Dido that he is not a tyrant like his grandmother but an Iron Gold dedicated to order. Lysander explains that the Colors were created to shepherd humanity and prevent the tribal strife that broke Earth. He proposes a plan to join the war by traveling to the Core to find his godfather, Cassius. Lysander intends to act as an envoy to unite the Rim and the Core against the Reaper. He argues that if peace must be brought with a sword, the two Gold factions should hold it together.

Dido listens to his speech while standing imperiously over him in the silent room. She previously compared him to a wretched creature, but his conviction causes her to narrow her eyes in thought. Seraphina remains silent during the exchange, having previously stated there is no place for a Lune in their army. Lysander asserts that he possesses the same birthright and inheritance as the Moon Lords of the Rim. Dido’s initial dour expression eventually shifts into a smile as she considers the strategic value of his proposal.



### Chapter 64: Ephraim
Ephraim guides a stolen Hornet ship over a gray cityscape while suffering from a serious chest wound. Pax and Electra are in the cockpit with him, having used resFlesh to temporarily seal the hole in his chest. A noseless face distorted by a scrambler appears on the holopad, identifying itself as the Syndicate Queen. The Queen demands the return of the children, but Ephraim mocks her and attempts to shut off the communicator. The Syndicate Queen reveals she owns the ship and takes remote control of the vessel’s flight path.

The ship banks hard to port and begins flying toward a Syndicate facility, rendering the manual controls dead. Ephraim realizes the Republic escorts will not reach them in time to prevent their capture by the Syndicate. He swallows three zoladone pills to numb his pain and stumbles toward the back of the ship. Ephraim finds a locker containing pulseRifles, railguns, and a stack of thermal grenades stored in formafoam. He clusters the grenades near the ship’s engines and sets a thirty-second timer on one of the explosives.

Ephraim races back to the cockpit and orders Pax and Electra to buckle into their safety restraints. He seals the cockpit door just before the grenades detonate and cause a deafening roar in the ship. The explosion destroys the engines, causing the ship to pitch sideways and begin a terminal spiral toward the ground. They plummet into a wasted crater landscape located within a skeleton city on the planet’s surface. Ephraim laughs bitterly as the ship careens toward a crash he believes will be a one-way ticket.



### Chapter 65: Darrow
Darrow exits the Ash Lord’s fortress on Tyche as an empty shell after learning of the fleet’s movements. The Howlers wait on a landing pad where the Nessus is prepared for an immediate departure to Luna. Sevro is consumed by rage and sorrow upon hearing that Pax and Electra have likely been captured or killed. Pebble reports that Society ripWings are eight minutes away and the trip back to Luna will take four weeks. Darrow stuns his friends by announcing that he will not return to Luna to find his son.

He believes that Mustang will handle the recovery of the children while he remains focused on the war. Darrow argues that nine million people on Mercury will be exterminated if he abandons his army and the fleet. Sevro accuses Darrow of abandoning his child and "stealing" his daughter by forcing the Howlers into this mission. The two friends argue at the ramp of the ship, with Sevro expressing total contempt for Darrow’s choice. Sevro boards the Nessus with the intention of reaching Victra and Mustang to find the missing children.

Darrow remains on the island and prepares to take the Ash Lord’s personal shuttle to continue the fight. He is joined by Alexandar, Thraxa, Rhonna, Colloway, Tongueless, and several Gold prisoners taken from Deepgrave. Darrow deactivates the bomb in Apollonius’s head but refuses to hand over the promised hostages or the planet. The shuttle lifts off under fire from Society ships, using the Gold hostages as shields to ensure their escape. Darrow removes the key his son gave him and resolves to let his rage as the Reaper take control.
