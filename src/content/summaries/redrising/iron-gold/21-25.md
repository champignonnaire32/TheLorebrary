### Chapter 21: Darrow
Darrow gathers gear from the Den’s armory and agrees to let Sevro visit his children before they leave for Venus. Victra warns Darrow not to take Sevro with him, but Darrow refuses to manipulate or force his friend to stay behind. Darrow visits his son Pax at the Telemanus estate and watches him sleep before waking him for a final conversation. They ride Pax’s handmade hoverbike to a hidden cove on Lake Silene where Pax expresses his anger and sadness over Darrow’s constant absence. Darrow gives Pax his bike key and departs to find Mustang waiting for him in the darkness outside the house.

Mustang informs Darrow that his departure is treason and that she has called the Republic Wardens to arrest him. Wulfgar and ten armored Wardens arrive on the lawn and demand that Darrow surrender himself into custody. Two veteran soldiers who served with Darrow turn against their fellow Wardens to defend the Reaper. Darrow uses a Gold Warden as a shield and engages in a violent skirmish against the remaining Republic forces. He cuts off limbs and disables several Wardens while avoiding lethal blows during the initial seconds of the chaotic melee.

Darrow duels Wulfgar and uses his superior razor skills to drive the larger man back across the grass. An accidental energy round from a veteran soldier pushes Wulfgar into the path of Darrow’s killing thrust. Darrow’s razor enters Wulfgar’s mouth and exits the back of his skull, killing the legendary Obsidian hero instantly. Sevro pulls a shocked Darrow toward their shuttle as more Republic reinforcements begin to arrive at the estate. Mustang and Pax watch from the clearing as Darrow and the Howlers lift off and flee the scene of the murder.

### Chapter 22: Lysander
Lysander and Cassius are transported across the sulfur plains of Io inside a Rim dropship while wearing metal muzzles. Bollov, a Rim guard with a tremor in his hand, refuses to give the dehydrated Lysander an extra cup of water. Diomedes au Raa enters the prisoner section and provides Lysander with water despite the guard’s fear of a woman named Pandora. Lysander tries to convince Diomedes to release them by appealing to his sense of honor and his daughter Seraphina’s safety. Diomedes responds by lifting Lysander by his muzzle chain and dragging him into the loading bay of the ship.

Diomedes attaches a cable to Lysander’s harness and explains that the Core Golds once mistook his family’s grace for weakness. He opens the dropship doors and drops Lysander into the poisonous, high-radiation atmosphere of Io while the ship is in flight. Lysander plummets until the cable snaps taut, causing his head to strike the hull and drawing blood from his forehead. He experiences extreme pain from the heat and lack of oxygen before Diomedes hauls him back into the hangar. Diomedes tells Lysander that the ordeal was a lesson in respect before welcoming him to the moon of Io.

The dropship continues its journey toward a destination controlled by the Raa family while Lysander recovers from his injuries. Cassius remains bound in his seat and warns Lysander to stop talking to their captors to avoid further punishment. Lysander reflects on the speed of the new Rim ships and the potential death or enslavement of their pilot, Pytha. He observes the agricultural domes and skeletal tramways of Io where lowColor slaves produce food for the Jovian cluster. The chapter ends with Lysander being forced to stand as they prepare to disembark onto the surface of the moon.

### Chapter 23: Lyria
Lyria works as a valet for House Telemanus on Luna and spends her mornings collecting fox droppings for Dr. Liago. She lives in the Citadel of Light and cares for Sophocles the fox, who has become aggressive toward the local birds. Kavax Telemanus provides Lyria with a good wage, clean uniforms, and reading lessons in his private stateroom. Liam attends a school for employees’ children within the Citadel walls, though he is one of the only Reds present. Lyria struggles to adapt to the low gravity of the moon and feels judged by the other servants on the estate.

The moon is in a state of unrest following Darrow’s murder of Wulfgar and his subsequent disappearance from Republic space. Protests and violent clashes between Darrow’s supporters and detractors clog the boulevards of Hyperion and the Citadel. Lyria encounters a cold-eyed Gray woman who is rumored to be a Howler and a former Son of Ares. She also observes a secret meeting between a Gray warden and a Copper official near the base of a Telemanus estate. Dr. Liago shows Lyria a genetically modified flower called the Night Lily that kills organic tissue upon aggressive contact.

Lyria visits a warehouse to collect supplies for Sophocles and smokes with other Martian Reds who work as dockers and stockers. An old woman named Garla explains that Sophocles is a clone currently in his twenty-first life of service to the family. Garla shows off a golden griffin medal given to her father by Kavax’s father for killing a wild beast in the mountains. The Reds express their loyalty to House Telemanus and their duty to protect Kavax in the political environment of Luna. Sophocles pounces on a bird and destroys Liago’s lab equipment, leading the doctor to banish the fox from the greenhouse.

### Chapter 24: Ephraim
Ephraim, Volga, and Cyra walk through a dilapidated level of Hyperion where the Vox Populi has painted graffiti on the buildings. Newscasts blare information about the hunt for the Reaper following the murder of the ArchWarden at the Telemanus estate. Ephraim meets with Gorgo, a Syndicate liaison, and demands a military-grade gravWell for the upcoming heist. He encourages Volga to focus on her own survival rather than worrying about the potential harm their job might cause the Rising. They arrive at Kobachi’s Tech Emporium, a shop that displays signs explicitly banning Obsidians and Reds from the premises.

The shop interior is filled with secondhand gadgets, biomodifications, and a holoExperiential station where kids watch a simulated pirate mission. A Green employee tries to kick Volga out of the store, but Ephraim threatens the man and demands to see the owner. Kobachi emerges from the back and greets Ephraim while complaining about the extortion and taxes imposed by the Republic Wardens. Ephraim reveals an iron rose from his pocket to signal his Syndicate affiliation and force Kobachi into cooperation. The small man pales at the sight of the rose and leads Ephraim into a secret back room filled with illegal military technology.

Kobachi offers various weapons for sale, but Ephraim displays his own rare Omnivore-540 railgun which he refuses to sell. The shop owner shows off a collection of hunter-killer drones equipped with silent engines and neurotoxin delivery systems. Ephraim asks Kobachi how small he can make an assassin drone for the specific requirements of their Syndicate mission. Volga remains in the front of the shop watching the children play with the tech while Cyra gathers items from a shopping list. The group prepares to use these high-tech tools to execute the heist commissioned by the Duke of Hands.

### Chapter 25: Lysander
The dropship lands at a remote mountain fortress on Io where masked legionnaires and Vela au Raa greet the passengers. Seraphina appears transformed from a grime-streaked prisoner into a fierce woman who walks with an unsmiling, buzzed-head appearance. Cassius and Lysander are led through bare rock halls filled with protection glyphs and robed Obsidians. They enter a warroom where Romulus au Raa, the Sovereign of the Rim, sits on a cushion at the foot of a stone throne. Romulus is missing his right arm and his left eye is replaced by a smooth globe of blue marble.

Romulus greets his children and dismisses his daughter’s complaints about the torture of her companion, Hjornir. He identifies Cassius and Lysander as razor-wielding warriors by inspecting the calluses on their palms. Seraphina confesses that she went to the Interior to find the truth about the destruction of the Ganymede Docks. Romulus claims there is no conspiracy and that the docks were destroyed by the Society during the earlier war. He sentences Seraphina to life imprisonment in the remote fortress to protect her from the legal consequences of her treason.

Romulus orders Pandora to behead Cassius and Lysander and then cast their ship into the atmosphere of Jupiter. Lysander attempts to appeal to Diomedes while Cassius prepares to launch a desperate physical attack against the Rim Sovereign. The ground suddenly rumbles from a missile strike that disables the fortress's escort vessels and orbital defenses. Vela reports that ten warhawks and multiple chimeras are attacking the installation from the surface of the moon. Romulus laughs and sits back down on his cushion, stating that he is waiting for his wife to arrive.
