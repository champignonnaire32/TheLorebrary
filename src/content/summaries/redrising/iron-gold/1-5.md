### 1: Part I: Wind - Chapter 1: Darrow
Darrow walks at the head of the Seventh Legion along the Via Triumphia on Luna as children throw flower petals from steel towers. Hyperion City Watchmen in gray uniforms and cyan berets guard the route against crowds of various Colors. Darrow observes holosigns of himself wearing a bloody crown and hears jeers from the Vox Populi, a lowColor political faction. He reflects on the ten-year Solar War and the two hundred million lives lost since the rising began. Darrow climbs the stairs of the New Forum, a massive marble structure where the Republic's government meets.

Darrow stands thirty-three years old with the Gold physical enhancements Mickey the Carver gave him. He looks back at Thraxa au Telemanus, a stout Gold woman with a metal arm prototype who holds the pegasus standard of the Seventh Legion. Sevro au Barca, the pinched and thin-lipped Imperator of the Republic, mocks Darrow's cologne as they ascend the stairs. Sefi the Quiet, a giant Obsidian woman, follows them in silence alongside a pack of Howlers, Darrow's elite shock troops. ArchWarden Wulfgar, a gnarled Obsidian man in pale blue armor, meets them midway up the stairs to greet the returning soldiers.

Wulfgar informs Darrow that the Senate had summoned him during the Iron Rain over Mercury, an orbital assault Darrow led against their orders. Sevro hands over a long blade from his boot to the Republic Wardens after Darrow and Sefi relinquish their razors, which are sword-like weapons made of form-shifting metal. One hundred and forty Senators in white togas wait at the top of the stairs, though the Red leader Dancer is absent. Darrow reaches the summit and finds Virginia au Augustus, the Sovereign of the Republic and his wife, holding the Dawn Scepter baton. He kneels before her and she welcomes him home after his year-long campaign.





### Chapter 2
Darrow and Sevro arrive via shuttle at Silene Manor, a stone retreat located on a lake at the base of the Atlas Mountains on Luna. They are greeted by Cedric cu Platuu, a small Copper steward with a thin mustache and graying hair. Cedric informs Darrow that his son Pax and Sevro's daughter Electra are returning from an adventure in the mountains. Sevro complains that his wife Victra missed the Triumph parade due to labor strikes in her shipping warehouses. Darrow asks for his son's location and proceeds toward the dueling grotto, a training area adorned with granite fountains and gargoyles.

In the grotto, ten-year-old Pax and nine-year-old Electra spar with plastic razors inside a chalk circle under the supervision of Niobe au Telemanus. Electra, a narrow-faced girl, aggressively attacks Pax while Sevro offers to bet his trophies on her victory. Pax uses a defensive strategy to score a point, but Electra eventually disarms him and attempts to strike his head. Thraxa au Telemanus intervenes by catching the plastic blade with her mechanical hand and pouring beer on Electra's head. Pax greets his father with a formal bow instead of a hug, showing a coldness that troubles Darrow.

Darrow visits his mother, Deanna, who is planting bulbs in a garden alongside Red servants and a man with a dragging leg. Dancer, Darrow's old mentor and now a Senator, joins them wearing laborer's clothes instead of his official robes. Dancer and Darrow walk toward the lake where Dancer criticizes Darrow for disobeying the Senate's orders regarding the Mercury campaign. He accuses Darrow of acting like a Gold tyrant and warns that the Vox Populi will no longer tolerate his unilateral military actions. Darrow denies having secrets from Dancer, even though he and Sevro are hiding information about secret emissaries.





### Chapter 3
Daxo au Telemanus, a giant Gold man, arrives for dinner at Silene Manor with Darrow's brother Kieran and niece Rhonna. Victra au Barca joins the meal late and pregnant, reporting that her mechanics union in the docks is on strike for higher wages. She mocks the Vox Populi for inciting the workers and expresses disdain for the lowColor influence in the Republic. During the meal, Sevro returns from a brief absence with a split lip and missing buttons after a private encounter with Victra. The group eats Martian dishes seasoned with curry and cardamom at a long wooden table.

After dinner, the adults retire to a back grotto while the children play in the vineyards. Daxo sketches pictures for Sevro's youngest daughter while the Telemanuses sing "The Fox of Summerfall." Darrow notices the House Augustus Lionguards, elite soldiers in high-tech armor, patrolling the dark forest around the manor. He eventually follows Virginia into the main house, which is filled with relics from the previous Gold regime of the Lunes. Darrow finds her on a veranda reading a book of poetry and begins to massage her shoulders.

Virginia warns Darrow that the Senate is hostile and that the Vox Populi can no longer be ignored by the executive branch. She admits she might not run for Sovereign again when her term ends in two years, dreaming of a quiet life on Mars. Darrow realizes they are both trapped by the responsibilities of empire and the ongoing war against the Ash Lord. Their conversation is interrupted by Daxo, who brings news that Dancer has called an emergency Senate session. The hearing for Darrow's unauthorized Iron Rain has been moved up to the following night.





### Chapter 4
Lyria, a Red woman from the Lagalos mine on Mars, recalls her life before the Republic freed her clan from a Gray warlord. She remembers the arrival of the Rising soldiers who wore spiked red helmets and carried a white flag with a fourteen-pointed star. These liberators provided the Red slaves with goggles to protect their eyes from the Martian sun during their first ascent to the surface. A hologram of Virginia the Lionheart greeted the refugees, promising them a share in the bounty of the new Solar Republic. Lyria now views these promises as lies after living for two years in Assimilation Camp 121 on the Cimmerian Plains.

Lyria hunches over a river to wash clothes while mosquitoes and bugs bite her dark-baked skin. She belongs to the Gamma clan, which is marginalized in the camp because they were formerly favored by the Golds. The camp is a crowded town of plastic and tin housing fifty thousand refugees, though it was designed for twenty thousand. Red Hand marauders, a terrorist group, frequently raid nearby camps to kill or kidnap members of the Gamma clan. Lyria's father has lost his legs and her mother is dead, leaving her to help support her remaining family.

Lyria walks back to the camp with a basket of laundry on her head as a storm approaches. She observes burner boys, laborers who use engine oil to set fire to heaps of trash infected with malaria. Her brother Tiran works as a burner for one token an hour instead of pursuing his dream of becoming a Helldiver, an elite mine pilot. The Republic has replaced human miners with robots, leaving the refugees without the jobs or profits they were promised. Lyria enters the camp gates, navigating through streets filled with mud and the smell of dog waste.





### Chapter 5
Lyria enters her family's small plastic hut as a heavy rainstorm begins to hammer the roof. Her sister Ava is cooking a stew and shows off a pair of blue leather shoes given to her by a soldier. Lyria changes her father's diaper while he stares at a dead channel on a stolen holo-television box. Her brother Tiran and Ava's sons return home and they all eat soup while discussing the merits of Republic starships. They watch a news program featuring the Reaper's Triumph on Luna, which highlights the disconnect between the highColors' glory and the refugees' squalor.

In the middle of the night, Lyria wakes to the sound of distant engines and heavy machinery. She leaves the hut against Tiran's advice to investigate the noise, carrying a frying pan as a makeshift weapon. Lyria reaches the landing pads where two rusty transport ships have landed, illuminating a group of armed Red men. These outsiders are members of the Red Hand and are demanding that the camp residents identify the members of the Gamma clan. A man from the camp points toward the Gammas, and the Red Hand soldiers immediately open fire with plasma rifles, which fire orbs of acid-green light.

Lyria watches from behind a cargo container as Tiran attempts to flee from the gunmen. A volley of gunfire hits Tiran in the chest and head, killing him instantly in the mud. The lead attacker rakes Tiran's corpse with a plasma weapon that glows with acid-green light. This leader is a Red woman with extensive scarring across half of her face who shouts for "Justice to Gamma." She bellows her declaration of death to collaborators through the ships' speakers as the massacre begins.
