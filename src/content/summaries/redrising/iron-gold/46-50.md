### Chapter 46
Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius secure Tharsus in his home while he sits in a robe surrounded by bodies. Apollonius asserts his status as the heir of Valii-Rath and slaps Tharsus for his petulance. Tharsus claims he tried to rescue Apollonius from Deepgrave, but Darrow reveals that only one assault occurred and it was not for him. Sevro cuts off Tharsus's left ear with a boot-knife after the Gold continues to insult the group. Apollonius watches the mutilation with dispassion and commands his brother to mind his manners.

Apollonius accuses Tharsus of being complicit in his incarceration to maintain his own survival and luxury. Tharsus eventually admits his betrayal and explains he acted under the Ash Lord's threat of death. Apollonius forgives his brother and kisses the tears from his face, emphasizing that only the two of them remain in their house. Darrow watches the display with disgust and feels internal conflict about bringing Sevro into this environment. Apollonius declares that their "pound of flesh" will be taken from the Ash Lord for turning the brothers against each other.

Tharsus informs the group that the Ash Lord has reigned in complete solitude for three years following an assassination attempt. He states that any audience must go through the Ash Lord's daughter, Atalantia, but she has recently vanished. The Ash Lord resides in a fortress on Gorgon Isle, a darkzone protected by the Ash Legions. Tharsus explains that the air around the island is restricted to House Grimmus aircraft for two hundred kilometers. Apollonius smiles and suggests they will need their own army to penetrate the island's defenses.



### Chapter 47
Lysander tends to a dying Cassius on the marble floor and lies to him, calling himself Julian. Diomedes orders the Olympic Order to take custody of Cassius to ensure he receives medical treatment from their surgeons. Dido demands Lysander open the safe to prove his identity and worthiness to the assembled Moon Lords. Lysander turns the dial and unlocks the safe, revealing a House Lune ring, a book of poetry, and Karnus's razor. Seraphina removes the holodrop hidden within the razor’s hilt and places it into a holoprojector for the crowd to witness.

Dido addresses the assembly, admitting to a coup against her husband, Romulus, to reveal the truth about the Dockyards of Ganymede. The holoprojector displays graphic footage of an Obsidian woman mutilating the tongue of a dead Gold woman on a starship bridge. The recording shows Sefi the Obsidian, Victra au Julii, and Darrow watching the Dockyards through a viewport. Victra orders the helmsman to open fire with all port batteries and launch tubes at the center line of the station. The Moon Lords watch as tungsten iron rounds shear through the metal bulkheads, destroying the pride of their civilization.

Lysander realizes that Darrow staged the destruction of the docks to win a war against the Rim before it officially began. He concludes that the Reaper must die to protect mankind from his savage intellect and capacity for violence. Dido lifts her razor high and declares that the Pax Ilium is broken because of the Slave King's betrayal. Helios, the ArchKnight of the Olympics, stands and extends his razor to officially call for war. The assembled Moon Lords unsheathe their weapons and thunder the word "War" in a collective fever of vengeance.



### Chapter 48
Diomedes and his men escort Lysander back to his room and lock the door from the outside. Lysander retreats into a deep meditative state called the Willow Way to manage the stress of the unfolding events. He recalls his first meeting with Cassius Bellona at the Citadel when he was a small child holding a mathematics book. In the memory, a young and arrogant Cassius mocks Lysander’s appearance while the boy criticizes Cassius's lack of humility. Virginia au Augustus enters the office and interacts with both Lysander and Cassius before taking Cassius to meet the Sovereign.

Lysander wakes from his memories to the sound of his door opening and finds the Pink woman, Aurae, standing there. Aurae informs Lysander that Cassius has died of exsanguination while the young man was sleeping. She explains that she has the trust of Diomedes, who has gone to pursue schoolmates of the deceased Bellerephon. These schoolmates took Cassius’s body into the Waste with the intent to desecrate his remains. Lysander asks to see the body, but Dido’s guards prevent him from leaving the room.

Lysander feels the weight of his choices and mourns the loss of the man he hoped to redeem. He realizes that Cassius died believing Lysander had betrayed him and stole his chance for redemption. The loss of his mentor leaves Lysander feeling weightless and crushed by the finality of the void. He breaks down in a single sob and rocks on the floor with his knees hugged to his chest. With Cassius gone, Lysander recognizes that the child within him is dead and the life of the man must begin.



### Chapter 49
Soldiers drag Lyria from an assault ship and escort her deep into the Citadel of Light during a rainstorm. She is placed in a chair before Daxo au Telemanus, his mother Niobe, and an elderly Pink woman named Theodora. Niobe enters the room in rain-slicked armor and roars at Lyria to remain silent when she asks about Kavax’s condition. Daxo removes Lyria's muzzle and warns her that her life depends on her honesty during the following minutes. Next to Daxo sits an aquarium containing an Oracle, a gelatinous, maggot-colored creature with spindly legs used for invasive interrogation.

Lyria explains that a man named Philippe delivered Pax and Electra to a group of Obsidians, Grays, and a Pink. Daxo and Theodora accuse Lyria of being a Society agent or a member of the Red Hand working to discredit the Sovereign. Theodora, who was once Darrow's steward, suggests that Lyria's memories of her dead family might be a fiction created by Society Intelligence. Lyria erupts in rage, blaming the Sovereign for promising freedom but leaving the Reds to rot in camps where her family was murdered. Despite her outburst, the council decides to use the Oracle to verify her claims and find the missing children.

Holiday, a Gray soldier, holds Lyria down while Daxo cuts a shallow wound into her forearm with a knife. Daxo places the Oracle onto the wound, where the creature begins to suck her blood with its cold, slippery legs. Lyria begs them to stop, but the council remains focused on finding Pax and Electra before the trail grows cold. Virginia au Augustus suddenly enters the room and demands to know if Daxo has her permission to torture the girl. The Sovereign kills the Oracle with her razor and orders her council to leave so she can speak with Lyria alone.



### Chapter 50
The Sovereign sits with Lyria and asks why she chose to return to the Citadel despite the personal danger. Lyria explains that she came back for her nephew, Liam, to ensure he grows up proud of his family instead of feeling shame. Virginia accepts this motivation and coordinates with Holiday to trace the man Lyria knows as Philippe. They review bodycam footage from Officer Stefano, which reveals that Philippe used a Blighter to mask his face from recognition. A Blighter is a black-market technology that uses high-frequency light waves to create an invisible mask around the user.

Holiday discovers that Philippe used a ghost debit card to purchase various items, including a toy lion intended to mock the Sovereign. Lyria describes a Pink man in charge of the kidnappers who carried a white cane topped with a black, multi-limbed monster. Virginia identifies the monster as a cephalopod and realizes that the Syndicate, a powerful criminal organization, has her son. She explains that the Syndicate is ruled by a woman known as the Queen and her prince of thieves, the Duke of Hands. Virginia orders Theodora to contact Darrow and summons the Sovereign Council for an emergency meeting.

Lyria informs the Sovereign that she stole Philippe’s gun and left it at the Alpha City checkpoint. Lionguard teams search the checkpoint and find a Vulcan Omnivore pistol hidden inside a Watchman's locker. Forensics analysts identify a fingerprint on the gun's battery pack belonging to a man named Ephraim ti Horn. Ephraim is a swarthy claims investigator for Piraeus Insurance who matches the build of the man Lyria described. Holiday recognizes the name and informs the Sovereign that Ephraim is her own brother-in-law.
