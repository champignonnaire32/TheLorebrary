### Chapter 31: Ephraim
Ephraim collects a custom-made metal drone disguised as a Bacchus pendant from a technician named Kobachi in Hyperion. He disguises himself as his persona Philippe using makeup, retinal forges, and a prosthetic cane to meet Lyria at Aristotle Park. Security in the city has increased due to an undisclosed event on Earth, forcing Ephraim to adjust his infiltration plan. He meets Lyria under a sycamore tree and uses his feigned leg injury to manipulate her into having a picnic instead of visiting the wharf. They share a meal of fried cod, cheese, and jasmine ice cream while watching children fly kites in the park.

Lyria confesses her feelings of isolation and guilt over being a survivor of the Red Hand's massacre at the Lagalos camps. Ephraim experiences internal conflict and a sense of detachment as he listens to her story, feeling unworthy of her trust. He provides her with the drone-laden Bacchus pendant, claiming it was a gift from his fiancé and will offer her strength. Lyria accepts the gift and kisses him on the cheek, expressing her belief that they are both less alone. Ephraim watches her depart for the Citadel, knowing he has successfully planted the Syndicate's tracking and sabotage device on her.



### Chapter 32: Lysander
Lysander and Cassius fly toward Sungrave, a city of black stone spires carved into Io's highest mountain, Boösaule. The city utilizes tidal heating from Jupiter's gravity for power, allowing it to maintain shields without relying on helium-3. Upon arrival, Lysander observes that the Rim has built a new fleet of advanced warships despite their previous losses to the Sovereign. He is taken to Spartan quarters where he receives a massage from Pink slaves, Aurae and a male companion. Lysander feels a deep intellectual revulsion toward the existence of Pinks, viewing their biological engineering for lechery as a failure of Gold leadership.

He dismisses the slaves after noticing a flash of relief in Aurae's eyes when he stops the physical contact. Cassius joins him in the room and warns that they are likely being monitored by Dido’s security systems. They discuss the political instability on Io, with Cassius predicting that Dido’s coup against Romulus may face resistance from other Rim moons. Cassius suspects that Seraphina’s evidence of the Republic's betrayal might be a bluff or well-hidden. Lysander realizes that his relationship with Cassius is fracturing as he begins to outgrow his mentor's cynical worldview.



### Chapter 33: Lysander
Lysander stands naked in his room at Sungrave, observing the frozen landscape of Io through a small window. He witnesses a group of pursuers on hoverbikes destroy two leading lights with orange flames on the plain below. Seraphina uses a secret door to enter his room and activates a jammer to block the city's surveillance cameras. She mocks Lysander's modesty and brings him a package of clean Ionian clothing to replace his soiled garments. Lysander demands to see their pilot, Pytha, but Seraphina flatly refuses the request without offering any room for negotiation.

Seraphina notices a thin scar around Lysander’s neck and questions him about the man who inflicted the injury. Lysander recalls the trauma caused by the Reaper during his childhood but tells Seraphina the past does not define him. Seraphina argues that scars are stories of subjugation that only liberation through killing the oppressor can resolve. She admits she came to his room to decide if his life was worth saving after he rescued her on the Vindabona. Before departing through the wall, she warns Lysander to give her mother whatever she wants to ensure his survival.



### Chapter 34: Darrow
Darrow performs swimming laps on the Nessus to rehabilitate his arm after the battle with the Republic Wardens. The ship's sensor officer, Colloway, discovers a twenty-fifth thermal signature indicating a stowaway is hiding near the engines. Darrow, Sevro, and a squad of Howlers track the intruder to the engine room and discover Darrow's niece, Rhonna. Alexandar shoots Rhonna with a paralytic spider round to punish her for disobeying orders to stay on Mars. Darrow and Sevro then visit the brig to negotiate with the imprisoned Gold warrior, Apollonius au Valii-Rath.

Apollonius reveals he has spent his years in Deepgrave remaking his body and mind through rigorous study and exercise. Darrow offers Apollonius revenge against the Ash Lord, who betrayed him and abandoned his century during the invasion of Luna. To ensure cooperation, Darrow reveals that Winkle has implanted a high-grade explosive and a neural chip in Apollonius’s brain. Apollonius accepts the mission to help Darrow's team infiltrate Venus and land on his ancestral island to reclaim his house. Darrow explains that Apollonius's brother, Tharsus, has pillaged their family's wealth and assets to serve the Ash Lord's war effort.

The plan involves using Tharsus's greed for blackmarket Earth luxuries to secure a landing on Venus's surface. Darrow intends for Apollonius to launch a joint attack on the Ash Lord’s fortress, Gorgon Isle, with his former legionnaires. Once the Ash Lord is dead, Apollonius is expected to crown himself Tyrant and issue a conditional surrender to the Republic. Sevro expresses doubt about the plan, but Darrow believes setting Apollonius loose will tear the Society apart from the inside. Darrow apologizes to Sevro for the cost of their mission and the death of Wulfgar as they prepare for the invasion.



### Chapter 35: Lyria
Lyria attends Quicksilver's birthday celebration in Hyperion, where she receives a gift basket and watches automated robots massage House Telemanus staff. She accompanies Kavax and the fox Sophocles to a private council meeting involving the Sovereign, Victra, and Daxo. The leaders discuss the political threat posed by Dancer and the Vox Populi, who are expected to win the Senate majority. Virginia the Lionheart expresses concern over a potential impasse if she is forced to veto the peace accord ratified by the Senate. Lyria is spotted eavesdropping and quickly exits the room with Sophocles to take him to the garden.

In the garden, Lyria meets Pax Augustus, who is watching a high-speed ship race on his datapad. Pax attempts to befriend Lyria and asks her about life in the Martian mines to better understand the lowColors. Lyria becomes enraged by his curiosity, viewing it as a privileged voyeurism into her family's suffering and the horrors of the camps. She insults Pax, calling him a Gold, and retreats to a balcony to send a frantic message to Philippe. Lyria boards a shuttle with the Telemanuses and the children to travel to Lake Silene for the week.

During the flight, the Bacchus pendant Ephraim gave her begins to vibrate and glow red before splitting open. A small metal disk emerges from the locket and releases a paralytic gas that incapacitates every passenger and guard on the shuttle. The drone emits a high-pitched pulse that disables the ship's engines and electronics, causing the vessel to plummet toward the city. The shuttle crashes into a hidden facility where a retracting door closes over them, sealing the survivors in darkness. A teardrop-shaped section of the door begins to glow as someone prepares to board the downed ship.
