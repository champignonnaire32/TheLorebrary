### Chapter 56: Lysander

Lysander, Pytha, and the Obsidian Goroth navigate the pitch-black subterranean tunnels beneath Sungrave. Goroth, a massive Stained with large eyes adapted for the dark, guides them past hidden laboratories and botanical gardens. Lysander uses a razor—a whip-like sword made of memory-metal—to impale Goroth's knee in a sudden betrayal. Despite his injury, Goroth lunges at Lysander and crushes the bones in the young man's left hand. Pytha kicks Goroth in the head, allowing Lysander to sever the Obsidian's hand and flee into the Dust Cells prison.

Lysander and Pytha surrender to Dido au Fabii and present her with Gaia’s transponder as evidence of a conspiracy. Dido identifies the device as a way to summon the Bellona legions under Vela au Bellona to interfere with the trial. Seraphina au Raa confirms the severed hand belongs to Goroth and warns that the Stained is still loose in the tunnels. Dido orders a hundred Obsidian bloodstalkers and kuon hounds—large, predatory tracking canines—to hunt Goroth. The tunnel maps were previously lost during a data purge by House Fabii's hacker battalions, leaving the city vulnerable.

Dido plans to use the transponder to lure Vela’s legion into the Waste and destroy them to solidify her control. Lysander claims he betrayed his grandmother because Cassius died for honor and he wishes to end the cycle of blood. He argues that the Republic has fallen into anarchy and that he seeks a return to order and justice. Dido reveals that Helios will conduct a sham trial to impeach Romulus for negligence before the couple rules as equal Sovereigns. Seraphina escorts Lysander back to his cell while questioning if he has ever told the truth.



### Chapter 57: Ephraim

Ephraim meets the Obsidian Gorgo at a restaurant and is subsequently taken to a trophy room via a sensory-deprivation hood. The room contains an immense ant colony filled with acid-yellow ants and the severed hand of an Obsidian named Belog. Gorgo expresses his desire to kill Ephraim while the Duke of Hands, a Pink crime lord, enters wearing a black silk robe. The Duke dismisses Gorgo and invites Ephraim to a dinner of charred octopus and wine. Ephraim notices the Duke wears the keys to a CR-17 Hornet yacht around his neck and keeps two Obsidian guards nearby.

To get the guards to leave, Ephraim feigns sexual interest and slides his foot up the Duke's leg. Once alone, Ephraim strikes the Duke in the face, shattering his nose and stunning him. Ephraim retrieves his Omnivore pistol and uses the Duke's bloody face to unlock a biometric-protected datapad. He contacts Holiday and discovers he is in Endymion on the far side of the moon, several hours away from reinforcements. Ephraim activates a bonesaw—a surgical tool with a vibrating blade and a cauterizing laser—to extract information.

He lowers the saw into the Duke’s wrist, severing the hand while the crime lord screams into the loud music. The Duke confesses that the children are held in a vault two floors down in the East Wing. During the interrogation, the Duke stabs Ephraim through the cheek with a hidden subdermal blade in his remaining hand. Ephraim retaliates by smashing a wine bottle against the Duke's face, collapsing his cheekbone. He drags the mangled Pink out of the room and toward the elevators to find the vault.



### Chapter 58: Ephraim

Ephraim uses the Duke as a human shield to approach the vault, where he encounters three armed Syndicate guards. A firefight erupts, and Ephraim uses his Omnivore to blow off an Obsidian’s head and mow down the remaining men. The Duke opens the biometric vault using a voice-password and a retinal scanner, revealing a hoard of stolen treasures. Inside, Ephraim finds Pax Augustus and Electra Barca held in a small cage on a single mattress. Electra immediately arms herself with a solid razor—a sword that can transition between whip and blade forms—and threatens Ephraim.

Ephraim loots a bag of gems and carefully cuts Salvador Dalí’s "The Persistence of Memory" from its frame. He forces the Duke into the hallway as a shield, successfully intimidating a line of guards into dropping their weapons. During the escape, Pax accidentally fires a stolen plasma pistol into the ceiling, nearly causing a panic. As they reach the landing pad, the Obsidian Gorgo fires a sniper rifle from a window three stories up. The high-caliber round passes through the Duke's body and embeds itself in Ephraim’s chest.

The children drag the wounded Ephraim onto the Hornet yacht while the Duke is left dying on the ramp. Ephraim launches the ship and engages the twin ion engines to fly toward the manufacturing districts of Endymion. He contacts Sovereign Virginia au Augustus via holopad, showing her that the children are safe but wounded. Ephraim refuses to surrender and demands the release of his companion Volga in exchange for the children. Pax places the tip of a razor against Ephraim's eye and orders him to fly directly to the Citadel.



### Chapter 59: Lyria

Lyria watches from a balcony as RipWings—fast, triangular atmospheric fighters—launch from the Palatine to retrieve the children. Holiday explains that Ephraim was once a recruiter for the Sons of Ares who watched his team get skinned alive. The Sovereign pardoned the Gold responsible for that torture, causing Ephraim to spiral into a decade of self-destruction. Lyria joins Virginia, Theodora, and Daxo au Telemanus as they track the progress of the rescue squadron. Virginia suspects the Ash Lord hired the Syndicate to kidnap her son to destabilize the Republic's peace process.

In a sudden display of humility, the Sovereign drops to one knee and asks Lyria to forgive the Republic’s failures. Lyria accepts the apology and agrees to testify as a witness before the Senate to help expose the conspirators. In exchange for her cooperation, Virginia promises to pay for a medical procedure to restore Liam’s eyesight. Holiday offers to bring chocolate candy to Liam on Lyria’s behalf to reassure him of her safety. Lyria returns to her private quarters and takes a hot shower to wash away the stress of the day.

A Brown woman dressed in white maid shoes enters the bathroom holding a gun equipped with a hypodermic needle. The assassin injects Lyria in the chest with a sedative, causing her to lose consciousness and collapse on the tile. The woman identifies herself as an agent of House Barca and calls Lyria a traitor. She claims the attack is a form of retribution for Lyria's involvement in the kidnapping of Victra’s daughter. The chapter ends with Lyria falling into a deep, drug-induced darkness.



### Chapter 60: Darrow

Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius breach the Ash Lord’s island fortress and find him guarded by medical machines. Magnus au Grimmus is revealed to be a skeletal husk, his body ravaged by a slow-acting poison and rotting from within. Apollonius admits he used a biological agent to infect his rival three years prior as revenge for his own imprisonment. The Ash Lord mocks Darrow, calling him a slave whose victories have cost him his humanity and his family. He explains that his daughter Atalantia has been secretly leading the Core legions and managing the war effort.

The peace negotiations were a strategic ruse designed to lure Darrow away from the Senate and fracture the Republic. Atalantia is currently leading a fleet to destroy the weakened Republic forces stationed in orbit over Mercury. Magnus reveals that Pax Augustus and Electra Barca have been kidnapped by a Syndicate agent working for the Core. Sevro loses control and brutally beats the Ash Lord’s face with his metal-covered fists until the old man's lips are mangled. The Ash Lord bites a poison tooth and begins to foam at the mouth as his life finally fails.

Apollonius uses an antibacterial spray—a flammable chemical aerosol—to coat the dying warlord’s body. He drops a candle onto the bed, igniting a blue inferno that consumes the Ash Lord in a horrific blaze. Darrow stands beside Sevro and watches the blue flames consume the last of his long-time adversary. He turns away from the execution and likens his despair to the moment he faced the gallows as a young man. The chapter concludes as Darrow realizes he has lost his son and his family in the pursuit of the Ash Lord.
