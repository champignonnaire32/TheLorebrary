### Chapter 56: Lysander
Lysander, Pytha, and their Obsidian guide Goroth navigate the dark, freezing tunnels beneath the city of Sungrave. Lysander identifies Goroth’s intentions to lead them to their death and preemptively strikes him with a razor, severing the Obsidian’s hand in a brutal struggle. Escaping through a storage room into the Dust Cells, Lysander surrenders to the guards to choose a side against his grandmother Gaia. He presents Gaia's razor and Goroth's severed hand to Dido au Saud as proof of Gaia’s conspiracy to free Romulus. Dido believes his story and decides to use the transponder he surrendered to lure Gaia's ally, Vela, into a trap in the Waste. Lysander justifies his betrayal by stating his desire for order and justice over the anarchy he witnessed on Luna.


### Chapter 57: Ephraim
Ephraim meets the Syndicate Duke in a lavish trophy room filled with stolen treasures and an ant colony feeding on a severed Obsidian hand. During a tense dinner, the Duke attempts to recruit Ephraim as an architect for new unconventional streams of criminal income. Ephraim plays along with the Duke's advances to get him alone and then brutally shatters the Pink's nose with a palm strike. He steals the Duke's ship keys and datapad, using the latter to signal Holiday for a rescue mission. Realizing he is on the far side of the moon in Endymion, Ephraim knows the cavalry is two hours away and he must act immediately. He uses a bonesaw to sever the Duke's hand to coerce him into revealing the location of the kidnapped children.


### Chapter 58: Ephraim
Ephraim enters the vault to find the children, Pax and Electra, held in a cage amidst mountains of stolen currency and art. The children arm themselves with razors from the Duke's collection, and Electra immediately displays a feral, violent temperament. Ephraim uses the Duke as a human shield to navigate past a dozen Syndicate thorns who initially surrender at the Duke's command. During the escape, Gorgo snipes from a window, killing the Duke and hitting Ephraim in the chest. Pax and Electra help the wounded Ephraim into the Duke's Hornet ship and they rocket away from the tower. Ephraim contacts the Sovereign to confirm the children are safe but refuses to surrender until his partner Volga is released.


### Chapter 59: Lyria
Lyria watches from the Citadel as rescue ships depart to rendezvous with Ephraim and the recovered children. Holiday explains Ephraim's tragic history as a former Sons of Ares recruiter who was forced to watch his men be skinned alive by a Gold. The Sovereign later meets with Lyria and, in a surprising display of humility, kneels to ask for her forgiveness for the Republic's failures. Lyria agrees to testify before the Senate about the Syndicate's crimes in exchange for medical care for her nephew Liam. After the meeting, Lyria returns to her room to find a Brown woman dressed as a maid waiting for her. The woman stabs Lyria with a hypodermic needle, claiming that House Barca sends its regards as Lyria loses consciousness.


### Chapter 60: Darrow
Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius breach the Ash Lord’s fortress on Tyche to find the warlord emaciated and dying in a medical bed. The Ash Lord reveals that his daughter Atalantia has actually been commanding the Society's forces for the last three years while he wasted away from poison. He mocks Darrow and Sevro, informing them that their children have been kidnapped by a traitor within their own ranks. The revelation that the entire mission was a distraction and that their families are in danger sends Sevro into a violent rage. Darrow gives Apollonius permission to execute the Ash Lord, who is subsequently burned alive with antibacterial spray and a candle. Darrow stands in the smoke-filled room, overwhelmed by the realization that his pursuit of vengeance has cost him his son.
