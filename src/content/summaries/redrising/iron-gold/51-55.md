### Chapter 51: Ephraim
Ephraim drinks vodka and zoladone in a rented Luna hotel suite while planning to flee to Earth with Volga. He feels guilty over the death of the Red girl and his colleagues Cyra and Dano. Volga enters the balcony and throws Ephraim’s drug dispenser over the edge to force him to feel his emotions. Ephraim insults Volga and she physically attacks him, nearly throwing him off the balcony in a rage. They argue about the kidnapping of the children before Volga leaves the suite, effectively ending their partnership.

Ephraim visits Cyra’s apartment building but cannot enter due to security scanners and wanders the rainy streets alone. He collects his ready bags from a storage unit containing currency, IDs, DNA sleeves, and pistols. At a private skyhook, he checks in using a false ID and waits for Volga to arrive for their flight. After several hours, he realizes she is not coming and boards the private ship alone to depart for Echo City. Upon entering the ship’s lounge, he finds Holiday Ti Nakamura and Lyria waiting for him with guns drawn.

Holiday reveals that they have captured Volga and will turn her over to the Telemanuses unless Ephraim cooperates. Lyria confronts Ephraim about his cynicism while Holiday explains that the Syndicate is holding the Sovereign's children. Ephraim identifies the Duke of Hands and the Obsidian Gorgo as his contacts for the kidnapping. The Sovereign, Virginia au Augustus, appears via holo and demands Ephraim's help in locating the children in exchange for a pardon. Ephraim agrees to the deal on the condition that Volga is pardoned and protected from the Telemanuses.





### Chapter 52: Darrow
Darrow observes Apollonius au Valii-Rath as he addresses the 911 remaining soldiers of his once-massive legion on a Venusian island. Apollonius shames the men for their idleness and calls for them to reclaim their glory by hunting the Ash Lord. Sevro, Thraxa, and Darrow watch the speech via holo, skeptical of the small and unrefined force. Sevro expresses deep frustration with Darrow’s leadership and the dangerous shortcuts Darrow takes that have resulted in many deaths. Darrow insists on proceeding with the mission to kill the Ash Lord despite Sevro’s desire to return to his family.

Darrow orders the Howlers to prepare the Nessus and their starShells for a stealth assault on the Ash Lord’s darkzone. Two hours later, Apollonius’s fleet of forty ripWings and five assault frigates lifts off to provide a diversionary attack. Apollonius mocks Darrow before joining his men, wearing his baroque Minotaur armor with long twisted horns. Darrow reminds Apollonius that the bomb embedded in his chest is on a three-hour timer once he enters the darkzone. Sevro remains sullen as the Howlers load their gear and prepare the Nessus for the high-speed atmospheric insertion.

Darrow assigns Rhonna and Winkle to the gunner chambers of the Nessus since Colloway will be piloting a ripWing. Rhonna kisses Darrow on the cheek and promises not to let him down before she heads to her station. Darrow reflects on Sevro’s doubts and wonders why the Ash Lord has not been seen in public for three years. He suspects the old warlord is hiding something behind the curtain of the massive darkzone covering the islands. The Nessus prepares to launch its starShells into the heart of the enemy stronghold as the diversionary battle begins.





### Chapter 53: Darrow
Apollonius’s fleet engages the Ash Lord’s patrols and enters the darkzone to draw fire away from the Nessus. Darrow is launched from the Nessus in a twelve-foot-tall starShell mechanized suit aimed at the island’s back door. He punches through the darkzone and immediately faces a barrage of particle beams and anti-aircraft fire from atoll batteries. Darrow destroys a central particle cannon installation by firing his own shoulder-mounted cannon into its power generator. The Howlers land on a rugged escarpment and witness the massive scale of the Ash Lord’s volcanic island chain.

The Howlers observe a humped central island with a white spire protected by a large shield generator. Darrow sees nearly two hundred enemy ripWings and hoverboats preparing to launch from a nearby airfield to reinforce the defenders. To prevent being overwhelmed, Darrow leads the Howlers into the water to intercept the pilots before they reach their ships. They navigate a deep-sea minefield with the help of the Nessus's belly turrets providing cover from above. Darrow boards several hovercraft and massacres the Blue pilots and crew inside their command cabins using his railgun.

The Howlers destroy the hovercraft fleet, but Darrow notes that two of his men, Grana and Vandros, are missing. Apollonius’s legions storm the main island’s beachhead under heavy fire from the Ash Legions and subterranean bunkers. Darrow and Sevro climb a ridgeline to plan their next move as Apollonius fights his way toward the bunkers. A sniper round pierces Darrow’s starShell and dents his pulseArmor, forcing him to eject from the disabled mechanized suit. Sevro fires a half-megaton nuclear missile at the coastline, destroying the sniper nests and collapsing the island’s shield generator.





### Chapter 54: Darrow
Darrow and the Howlers fly toward the Ash Lord’s tower into the wake of the nuclear explosion. They are met by twenty elite Peerless dragoons of Legio XIII Dracones who engage them in a high-speed aerial dogfight. Darrow kills a Gold by slamming into him and firing his pulseFist at point-blank range after falling onto a rock ledge. Sevro uses his starShell to crush the heads and limbs of the more maneuverable Golden knights in the air. The Howlers suffer heavy losses as the dragoons begin to overwhelm the slower mechanized starShells.

Milia is killed when a Gold knight cuts her in two during a mid-air collision. Darrow orders the remaining Howlers to rally to the landing pad on the roof of the Ash Lord's tower. They land on the roof and fight through squads of Gray snipers and Obsidian reinforcements hidden behind a shuttle. Darrow is hit by a pulseHammer and nearly scalped by a giant Obsidian who pins him to the concrete. Just as the Obsidian prepares to kill Darrow, Apollonius au Valii-Rath arrives and hacks the Obsidian in half.

Apollonius and his knights slaughter the remaining Ash Guard and Grays defending the entrance to the fortress. Apollonius hugs a blood-covered Darrow and celebrates their victory on the beach and the tower roof. Sevro limps toward them with a broken arm and charred armor while Tongueless treats a wounded Thraxa. Darrow realizes that Pebble and Clown are missing and likely dead or captured during the chaotic ascent. Darrow, Sevro, and Apollonius prepare to enter the tower to find the Ash Lord while Alexandar holds the roof.





### Chapter 55: Lysander
Lysander wakes in his cell on Io and is led through hidden tunnels by an old Brown steward named Aruka. He is brought to a traditional Rim sitting room where he meets Gaia, the mother of Romulus Raa. Gaia is an ancient, frail woman who uses a wooden cane and is escorted by a massive Obsidian named Goroth. Lysander plays a mournful song on a heartwood piano, which triggers a vivid suppressed memory of his mother’s face. Gaia reveals she is not senile and challenges Lysander's loyalty to the Core and the legacies of his grandmother.

Gaia informs Lysander that Dido intends to have him and Pytha executed quietly before Romulus’s trial. She offers to help them survive if they agree to free Romulus from his imprisonment in the Dust Cells. Pytha is brought into the room, having been tortured by Pandora but recently cared for by Gaia’s servants. Gaia explains that her daughter Vela is waiting in the desert with legions loyal to Romulus to stop Dido’s war. Lysander agrees to the mission and accepts Gaia’s ancestral razor, a long glass-bladed hasta named Shizuka.

Goroth prepares to lead Lysander and Pytha through the mountain's secret passages to reach the hoverbikes hidden in a garage. Gaia expresses her desire to preserve the peace Romulus built and stop the Rim from joining the Ash Lord's war. Pytha decides to accompany Lysander despite her physical weakness and the danger of the mission. Goroth kisses Gaia on the brow in a rare display of affection before leading the escapees into the dark. Gaia closes the hidden door and tells Lysander to save her son as they depart for the Waste.
