### Chapter 40: Chapter 40: Paradigm
Darrow and Mustang discuss trust in the Apollo warroom after Fitchner’s departure. Mustang wears a wolfcloak, a pelt fashioned into a garment, and carries a standard, which is a long pole topped with a metal icon. Darrow orders his army to move through a blizzard to surprise House Jupiter. The army marches through snow and spring thaws to reach Jupiter’s triple-walled castle. Pax and Sevro compete to stay near Darrow during the rugged four-day trek.

Darrow's forces besiege Jupiter's castle, which is sandwiched between two large rock faces in a mountain pass. The defenders surrender after three days because they have run out of food. A boy named Lucian, who wears a golden Jupiter ring, surrenders the fortress on the condition that they are not enslaved. Darrow brings his army inside the castle as a heavy rainstorm begins. He orders his men to pretend to be drunk on scavenged wine to set a trap.

Darrow stages a fake argument with Pax to demonstrate discipline to Lucian. Mustang returns from scouting and Darrow dismissively sends her and Pax away from the warroom. Lucian relaxes and tells Darrow about his demanding father while they sit at the map table. Darrow produces a bag of golden sigil rings taken from fallen students of various Houses. He looks up and reveals that he has finally found the missing Pluto ring.





### Chapter 41: Chapter 41: The Jackal
Darrow stabs a dagger through Lucian’s hand, pinning him to the table. Lucian reveals he is the Jackal and recovers his composure despite the injury. Darrow reveals he knew the surrender was a trap coordinated by the Proctors. Fifty sober soldiers rise from the floor to surround the Jackal and his ten Howlers. The Jackal offers Darrow a patronage from his father in exchange for his freedom.

Pax returns to report that he captured fifty of the Jackal's men hiding in a well. Darrow challenges the Jackal to cut off his own hand to escape the dagger. The Jackal uses an ionBlade, a weapon with a super-heated cutting edge, to saw through his own wrist. As the Jackal finishes, a sonic detonator clatters onto the table through an open window. The Jackal stabs Pax in the throat with an ionBlade and activates the sonic device.

A sonic blast throws Darrow across the room and temporarily deafens the Howlers. Pax throws his body over Darrow to protect him from the Jackal’s repeated stabbing attacks. Pax dies with a smile on his face while the Jackal flees into the rainy mountains. Darrow and eleven Howlers track the Jackal through the snowy peaks by following his blood trail. Darrow kills Proctor Apollo by stabbing a knifeRing, a ring with a small retractable blade, through his visor.





### Chapter 42: Chapter 42: War on Heaven
Darrow dons Apollo's golden recoilArmor, which is a liquid-metal suit that hardens upon impact. He gives his stolen gravBoots, footwear that enables flight, to Sevro and takes Apollo's larger pair for himself. Darrow uses the gravBoots to ferry his Howlers up to Olympus, a floating mountain citadel one mile above the ground. The group lands on a balcony and Darrow streaks ash across his face for camouflage. Darrow uses a pulseFist, a gauntlet that fires kinetic force blasts, to blow open the doors of the pale marble citadel.

The Howlers capture Proctor Venus in her bedroom and tie her to her bedposts. They find Proctor Juno in the baths and Proctor Vulcan in a holoImmersion room watching the Jackal. Darrow’s army arrives via reinforcements brought by Thistle using gravBoots. The students swarm through Olympus, attacking Grays, who are mid-level soldiers in gray uniforms, and highReds. Darrow encounters Proctor Mercury, a small man who fights with a razor, a whip-like sword that can stiffen into a blade.

Darrow breaks Mercury's arm and stuns him with a pulseFist before tying him up. Proctor Jupiter, a massive man in full armor, attacks with a pulseShaft and a razor. Darrow and Jupiter fall through a window and plummet a hundred feet down a snowy mountain slope. Sevro uses a ghostCloak, a cloak that provides invisibility, to turn invisible and hamstrings Jupiter from behind. Darrow takes Jupiter’s weapons and armor before learning that his army has secured the Olympus armory.





### Chapter 43: Chapter 43: The Last Test
Darrow finds Mustang asleep in a suite on Olympus after she was drugged with sedatives. He wakes her and they share a kiss before she leaves to capture the Jackal's standard. Sevro edits the holo-records of the Institute to frame the narrative for the Drafters. Fitchner is released from the dungeons and reveals that Mustang is the Jackal’s twin sister. Darrow realizes he has given half his army and advanced technology to the Jackal's sibling.

Darrow flies to House Mars and finds the castle in ruins after months of siege. He discovers Antonia and Vixus nailed to crosses at the bottom of the hill and orders them cut down. The defenders of Mars raise a bedsheet painted with a slingBlade to welcome Darrow's return. Darrow reunites with a malnourished Roque and learns that Quinn has stayed by the poet's side. The Howlers enslave the besieging forces from Houses Jupiter, Apollo, and Pluto using the Mars standard.

Darrow enters the Mars warroom and finds Cassius sitting in his carved chair. Cassius spits in Darrow's face and declares a formal blood feud between their families. Darrow accepts the challenge and watches Cassius walk away from the castle forever. He pins the Primus badge to his chest and looks at the map showing his banner over nearly every House. Darrow realizes his slingBlade symbol represents the clan of his Red family, the lowest laboring caste, in the mines.





### Chapter 44: Chapter 44: The Beginning
Tactus takes command of three hundred soldiers on Olympus as they wait for the final assault. Mustang arrives in a flier and delivers the Jackal, who is bound, gagged, and naked. She explains that the Jackal is her twin brother but she remains loyal to Darrow. Sevro reveals he deleted audio of Darrow using Red slang during his fight with Apollo. Darrow leaves the Proctors bound and gagged as the dignitaries arrive via shuttles for the closing ceremony.

Director Clintus officially names Darrow the winner and refers to him as the Reaper. ArchGovernor Nero au Augustus arrives and offers Darrow a patronage in exchange for his silence about the cheating. Darrow learns that the Jackal was Nero's son and that the ArchGovernor values power over blood purity. Nero promises to send Darrow to the Academy to learn how to command space fleets. Darrow reflects on his secret mission for the Sons of Ares while standing among the Gold elites.

Darrow kneels before Nero au Augustus and swears an oath to become his lancer. He forsakes his false name Andromedus and pledges his purpose to the ArchGovernor's glory. Nero sets his hands on Darrow's head and formally claims him as a son of House Augustus. The other dignitaries watch in shock as the most powerful man on Mars adopts the winner of the Institute. Darrow resolves to use this position to eventually destroy the Society from within.
