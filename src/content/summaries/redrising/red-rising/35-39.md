### 35: Oathbreakers
Darrow and Mustang recruit ten "Oathbreakers" who refuse to be slaves after Darrow treats Mustang's fever and they travel north. Milia, daughter of a Martian Justiciar, and Dax are among the first to join the new army of free soldiers. Darrow offers his followers purpose and food without the requirement of subservience or beatings. The group observes a spectacular but foolish armored battle between House Mars and House Jupiter near Phobos Tower. Darrow uses white and gray wolfcloaks—smelly garments made from hunted wolves—to camouflage his squad in the snow near a bridge.

Darrow's squad ambushes a House Mars work party led by Cipio at a bridge blocked by felled trees. Mustang uses her standard to convert Minerva slaves while the rest of the group subdues the Mars soldiers. Pax is captured after seven soldiers pile onto him and Mustang clears his slave mark, leading to his joyful recruitment. Darrow infiltrates Castle Mars at night by climbing the stone walls while Mustang lights a diversionary fire. He avoids the guards and carves the Reaper's slingBlade symbol into every door and the main warroom table.

Darrow leaves a knife driven into the back of Cassius's chair and carves a skull into it to create fear. He encounters a former Minerva slave guarding the ramparts and convinces her to let him pass by using his Mars ring. The infiltration is intended to replace the image of a flawed leader with a supernatural, superhuman myth. Darrow reunites with his group in the woods after completing the psychological operation against his former House. He decides to target the ovens of Ceres next to ensure his growing army remains fed.

### 36: A Second Test
Darrow plans a daytime assault on the fortress of House Ceres to secure its bread ovens for the winter. The army practices balancing on a long wooden beam in the Northwoods to prepare for the specific siege tactic. Darrow and Mustang huddle together under the snow for warmth while Tactus and Pax pair off in another tunnel. The twenty-four soldiers carry a long tree trunk across an open plain during a morning snowstorm toward the fortress walls. Archers from Ceres lean over the battlements and fire arrows, wounding Dax as the group reaches the stone fortification.

Pax and five others shove the wooden beam against the wall at an angle to create a makeshift ramp. Darrow sprints up the wobbly beam and hurls Mustang and Milia over the battlements to clear the archers. Mustang and Darrow fight their way into the courtyard and open the main gates for the rest of the army. Pax charges into the Ceres students shirtless with his hair spiked into horns, wielding a massive wooden club. The army captures the citadel and ovens in five minutes, leading to a celebration involving fresh bread.

Tactus attempts to rape a Ceres slave named Nyla, prompting Milia and Mustang to intervene and notify Darrow. Darrow chooses to take twenty-five lashes from Pax in front of the army instead of just punishing Tactus. He explains that as the leader, the crimes of his soldiers are his own, fostering a sense of shared responsibility. Darrow hugs a confused and bloody Tactus afterward, declaring them blood brothers to secure the soldier's loyalty through shared pain. Mustang applies salve to Darrow's shredded back while mocking his use of philosophical quotes to justify his martyrdom.

### 37: South
Mustang organizes eighteen scouts into six squads equipped with primitive commUnits found in the Ceres warroom to provide a communication advantage. Darrow leads his army of fifty-six soldiers south toward the House of Apollo to fulfill a promise to Fitchner. They encounter black-clad Pluto scouts led by a girl named Lilath who wears a mantle of unbleached bones. A high-ranking Apollo emissary named Novas meets Darrow and mocks him for his army's ragged and pathetic appearance. Darrow fakes a limp and acts meek to lure Novas into underestimating his strength and strategic intent.

Novas offers to let Darrow's group join Apollo as mercenaries but slaps Darrow across the face when he refuses. Apollo cavalry attempts a night raid on Darrow's camp but fails when their horses plunge through softened river ice. Mustang walks with Darrow through deep snow, noticing his lack of sleep and the fact that he previously cried out in his dreams. Tactus observes the interaction and mocks Darrow for being smitten while also noting mysterious linear impressions in the snow. These tracks reveal that Proctors are following the group using gravBoots—boots that allow flight—and ghostCloaks for invisibility.

Proctors sabotage Darrow's group by pulling horse ropes apart, cracking soup pots, and filling their bread supplies with weevils. Mustang questions Darrow about his betrayal of Cassius and learns that Darrow killed Julian in the Passage. Darrow enters the woods at night to urinate and hears a voice mimicking Mustang's to lure him into a trap. He realizes the deception, sprints back toward camp, and climbs a tree to observe his surroundings with knives drawn. Proctor Apollo appears in the tree, mocks Darrow's refusal to return north, and shoves him into the path of a massive bear.

### 38: The Fall of Apollo
A genetically engineered bloodback bear with eight-fingered paws and black teeth pursues Darrow through the dark woods. Darrow lures the beast into a pit trap filled with wooden spikes, but he is immediately caught in a rope snare. Sevro and the Howlers—Thistle, Screwface, Clown, Weed, and Pebble—rescue Darrow and reveal they have been operating independently. Sevro lost an eye during a confrontation with the Jackal in the mountains and now seeks revenge against the Pluto leader. Tactus and the Howlers bond over their shared experiences hiding inside horse carcasses during previous military operations.

Sevro returns Darrow's slingBlade and informs him that the Jackal used cannibalism to survive a tunnel collapse at House Vulcan. Darrow takes off his ring to speak freely with his lieutenants about a plan to destroy House Apollo. He splits his army into six squads of ten to conduct a campaign of fragmentation and sabotage against Apollo's holdings. The army burns supply stores, fouls water supplies, and murders livestock to spread terror throughout the southern territories. Darrow integrate Ceres slaves into his units and encourages the use of stained wolfpelts as standards to build a new legend.

Proctor Apollo enters Darrow's camp inside a jamField—a technology that prevents sound from entering or leaving—to threaten the students. He offers wine and warns that the ArchGovernor's son is destined to win, urging Darrow's lieutenants to defect. Darrow reveals he knows about the Proctors' cheating and threatens to expose the rigged game to the other Drafters. While Apollo is distracted, Sevro and Tactus infiltrate the Apollo castle through the latrine holes to seize control of the ramparts. Darrow captures Novas, hurls a pulseSpear at the fleeing Proctors, and hears his army chant the name "Reaper" in defiance.

### 39: The Proctor's Bounty
Darrow's army sleeps in the conquered Apollo castle while Sevro frees five Mercury students from the remaining slave groups. Mustang observes that the soldiers view Darrow as a figure out of time, similar to the ancient Gold conquerors of Earth. Fitchner arrives at the castle to warn Darrow that Proctor Apollo has refused to leave Olympus despite losing his House. He reveals that several Proctors were promised high-ranking positions on Luna if the Jackal won the Institute game. Darrow asserts that his victory is about merit and proving he can overcome the ArchGovernor's political influence and cheating.

Fitchner returns Darrow's father's knifeRing—a weapon that extends an eight-inch blade from a finger joint—as a formal bounty. He warns Darrow that continuing to push the Proctors will lead to his death and expresses concern for Sevro's safety. Darrow realizes that Fitchner is Sevro's father based on their shared physical traits and frequent arguments. Fitchner admits he cannot help Darrow directly without risking his career but warns that a trap has been set by "the girl." Darrow dismisses the warning of duplicity, claiming his army is completely loyal and won in both heart and soul.

Darrow thanks Fitchner for the bounty and the warning before gripping the Proctor's hand in a powerful Helldiver's grasp. He suddenly breaks Fitchner's nose and strikes his temple with an elbow to knock him unconscious in the warroom. This act of violence against a Proctor marks Darrow's final transition into a leader who will no longer follow the rigged rules. He prepares to face the coming traps while holding a high-ranking official as a hostage or incapacitated witness. The chapter ends with Darrow standing over the fallen Proctor as his army maintains control of the southern fortress.
