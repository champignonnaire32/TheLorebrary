### Prologue
Darrow watches twelve hundred Gold students listen to a speech between marble pillars. A tall, imperious Gold man declares that all men are not created equal and calls democracy a cancer. He tells the students they are Gold, the end of the evolutionary line, and must claim power through blood. The speaker warns that only those fit for power among the "scarless children" will survive the coming trials. Darrow reveals in his internal monologue that he is not a Gold but a Red miner from the bowels of the world.

Darrow observes the Gold leader, who views Reds as weak, subhuman, and feebleminded. He notes that while Golds were raised in palaces, he was forged in the harsh underground and sharpened by hate. The Gold man explains that the strong should rule the "lesser Colors" as shepherds of the human race. Darrow reflects on his upbringing, which involved hard labor rather than riding horses or eating hummingbird tongues. He concludes that the Gold man is wrong about the strength of the Reds.

Darrow asserts that none of the Gold students gathered in the assembly will survive his coming actions. The setting is a grand assembly area characterized by massive marble pillars and a pitiless atmosphere. Gold is described as the highest caste, characterized by physical stature and inherited dominion over others. Red is identified as the lower caste to which Darrow belongs, characterized by manual labor and underground living. Darrow's intent is established as a lethal opposition to the Gold hierarchy and its current leaders.





### Part I: Slave - Chapter 1: Helldiver
Darrow recalls the public execution of his father, who was hanged by Grays after a trial by Golds. His brother Kieran cried during the event, but Darrow remained stoic as his sister Leanna sang a lament. A young girl named Eo placed a red haemanthus flower, a hardy "blood blossom," into his father's boot before the hanging. Because of low gravity on Mars, family members must pull the legs of the condemned to break their necks. Darrow now works as a Helldiver, the highest-earning role in his mining clan, operating a massive clawDrill.

A clawDrill is a titanic metal hand designed to melt and grasp rock to extract precious helium-3. Darrow sits in a holster seat at the drill's elbow joint, controlling rock-melting digits with his fingers. He wears a frysuit, a nanoplastic garment that insulates him from extreme heat but traps sweat and urine. While drilling, Darrow scans the dark tunnels for pitvipers, black oil-like beasts that burrow into suits to lay eggs. Darrow is sixteen years old and married to Eo, who is also sixteen and from the same clan of Red diggers.

Uncle Narol, the headTalk for the Lambda clan, orders Darrow to stop drilling due to a potential gas pocket. Darrow argues with his crew, including Old Barlow and his brother Kieran, wanting to take risks to win the Laurel. The Laurel is a quarterly prize awarded to the most productive clan, providing extra food, alcohol, and luxury goods. Darrow insults Uncle Narol by calling him a coward for preferring a slow safety scan over continued drilling. To avoid the delay, Darrow jumps down a hundred-meter shaft to manually scan the gas pocket near the molten drills.





### Chapter 2: The Township
Darrow's frysuit begins to melt as he nears the intense heat of the gas pocket and the glowing drills. He successfully scans the area, but his foot becomes jammed in a gear near a melting drill finger. Using a curved slingBlade, a weapon used to amputate limbs caught in machinery, Darrow slices his suit to free his leg. He sustains a severe burn on his hand while escaping the heat but manages to climb back to his holster seat. Darrow lies to his uncle about the danger, reporting minimal gas and resuming the drilling process immediately.

After his shift, Darrow joins his clan on a gravLift that shoots upward six vertical kilometers to a staging depot. He sees Gray guards, known as Tinpots, who wear simple duroArmor and carry weapons called thumpers. Ugly Dan, a Gray captain with a prosthetic arm, mocks Darrow and makes a crude comment about his wife. Darrow bows deeply to the Gray captain to avoid conflict, though he secretly contemplates killing the man. He confronts Dago, the legendary Helldiver of the rival Gamma clan, boasting that Lambda will win the Laurel.

Darrow cleans himself in an air shower tube that uses antibacterial molecules to blast filth from his skin. He returns to his township, a hivelike cluster of homes carved into the rock walls of the old mines. A holoCan displays propaganda from Octavia au Lune, the Sovereign of the Society, who praises the Reds as pioneers. Gold is the ruling Color, while Grays serve as police and Coppers handle the bureaucracy of the mining colonies. Darrow finds Eo at home, and she treats his burned hand with antibiotic and nervenucleic medicine.





### Chapter 3: The Laurel
Darrow and Eo walk to the Common, where the residents of Lykos gather for the Laureltide festival. The holoCan broadcasts news about a terrorist group called the Sons of Ares, who use a spiked helmet symbol. Obsidians are described as colossal warriors twice the size of Reds, while Yellows act as the Society's doctors. Uncle Narol plays a zither, a stringed instrument with a plastic soundboard, while the children of the township dance. Darrow notes the central gallows in the square, where a single skeleton hangs as a reminder of Society law.

The people of Lykos drink a distilled fungus called grendel and dance to resist the monotony of their labor. Darrow describes the forbidden dance and song that his father died for, which mentions a reaper and loss. At the Lambda feast table, Darrow sits with his brother Kieran, his mother, and his cousins Loran and Dio. He reflects on the Sigils on his hands, which are bonelike textures representing his status as a Red miner. A physical fight breaks out between Darrow and Uncle Narol, ending when the other men pull them apart.

A group of Grays in gravBoots descends from the Pot along with Magistrate Podginus, a Copper-caste official. Podginus announces that the Mu and Chi clans will receive no extra rations due to failing their production quotas. He mocks the "rustic" nature of the Reds before presenting the gold-painted Laurel branch to the crowd. Despite Darrow's record-breaking helium-3 production, the Magistrate awards the Laurel to the rival Gamma clan. Darrow realizes the Laurel is a tool of control used to keep the clans divided and subservient to the Golds.





### Chapter 4: The Gift
Eo calms Darrow’s rage after the Laurel loss by insisting they dance to the fast zither music. She leads him through the Webbery, a factory where giant spiderworms with skeletal legs produce bioSilk. They crawl through a hidden ventilation duct to reach a secret, pressurized room containing a lush forest. Darrow experiences grass, trees, and flying insects with green abdomens for the first time in his life. Through a transparent ceiling, he views the stars and the Andromeda Galaxy while lying in the grass with Eo.

Eo reveals that the Grays use this hidden garden for themselves and do not share it with the Red workers. She argues that the Reds are actually slaves to the Society, which takes the resources they mine for other Colors. Darrow defends the hierarchy, claiming there is nobility in obedience and in preparing Mars for humanity's future. Eo counters that Darrow’s father was right to dream of freedom but was too weak to fight for it effectively. She challenges Darrow to use his talents as a Helldiver to lead their people against the Gold oppressors.

Darrow rejects the idea of a rebellion, fearing that any attempt at resistance will lead to their execution. He argues that his father's death brought only sadness and that family is more important than a dream of freedom. Eo tells him that living without freedom is emptiness and that she wants their children to be born free. They watch a sunrise through the glass dome, observing the landscape of Mars turning into a tide of fire. As they prepare to leave, an oily voice belonging to a Gray guard discovers them in the forbidden garden.
