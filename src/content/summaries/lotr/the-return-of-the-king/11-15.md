### 11: The Tower of Cirith Ungol
Samwise awakens outside the closed gates of the Orc-hold and resolves to rescue Frodo despite the odds. He returns to Shelob's tunnel and finds the path to the main entrance of the tower under the red glow of Mount Doom. Sam puts on the Ring and experiences a vision of himself as a great hero turning Mordor into a garden. He rejects this temptation, realizing his own humble nature and the Ring's deceptive malice. Upon reaching the gate, Sam encounters the Two Watchers, which are stone statues with three heads that block his path with an invisible force.

Sam uses the Phial of Galadriel to break the will of the Watchers and enters the courtyard filled with Orc corpses. He discovers that the companies of Shagrat and Gorbag have slaughtered each other in a dispute over Frodo's mithril coat. Sam climbs the tower, scaring off a surviving Orc who perceives him as a great Elven-warrior due to the Ring's influence. He eventually reaches the highest turret but finds the final door locked and initially feels defeated in the silence. Sam begins to sing a song of the Shire, which prompts a faint response from Frodo and alerts a lurking Orc named Snaga.

The Orc Snaga enters Frodo's cell with a ladder and a whip to torment the prisoner further. Sam follows Snaga up the ladder and attacks the Orc, causing the creature to fall through a trapdoor to its death. He finds Frodo naked on a heap of rags and restores his master's hope by revealing he has kept the Ring safe. Frodo briefly attacks Sam in a Ring-induced frenzy before apologizing and reclaiming the heavy burden. The two Hobbits disguise themselves in Orc-mail and rags to begin their final trek across the plains of Mordor.



### 12: The Land of Shadow
Sam and Frodo escape the Tower of Cirith Ungol and narrowly avoid a searching Nazgûl on its winged steed. They leap from a stone bridge into a thicket of thorns to hide from a company of Orcs marching toward the pass. Frodo discards his heavy Orc-mail, finding the weight unbearable alongside the increasing burden of the Ring. Sam gives Frodo his Elven-cloak to provide warmth and concealment while they navigate the rocky ravine. The Hobbits notice a change in the wind and see the darkness of Sauron beginning to break in the distance.

A Nazgûl flies overhead toward the Dark Tower, emitting a cry of woe that Sam hears as a sign of Sauron's defeat elsewhere. The pair reaches a trickling stream of bitter, oily water and drinks their fill to survive the parched land. They continue northward along an Orc-path to avoid the difficult terrain of the Morgai ridges. After resting under a curtain of brambles, Sam sees a single star twinkle through the clouds and feels a renewed sense of hope. He realizes that the Shadow is a passing thing and that light exists forever beyond its reach.

The Hobbits climb to a high cleft and look out over the vast, ruinous plain of Gorgoroth toward Mount Doom. They observe massive camps of Men and Orcs and realize the plain is heavily guarded by the forts of the Isenmouthe. While hiding, they overhear a tracker Orc and a soldier Orc quarreling about the "great Elf" and the escaped prisoner. The tracker shoots the soldier in the eye and flees, confirming that Gollum is still following them and has stolen a cast-off mail-shirt. Sam and Frodo are eventually captured by an Orc company and forced to march in line toward the Isenmouthe.



### 13: Mount Doom
Frodo and Sam collapse into a shallow pit after escaping the Orc column during a chaotic collision of military units. They hide under the grey robes of Lórien while many companies of Orcs and Men march past toward the Black Gate. Sam realizes that their supplies are only sufficient to reach the Mountain and that there is no hope for a return journey. He hardens his will to complete the task even if it means dying alongside his master in the wilderness. The Hobbits crawl through a broken landscape of pocked holes and fissures to avoid detection by the watchful eye of the Dark Tower.

The weight of the Ring becomes a physical torture for Frodo, causing him to stagger and see a wheel of fire in his mind. Sam encourages Frodo to discard all unnecessary gear, including their Orc-shields, helmets, and Sam's beloved cooking pans. They trudge across the ash-ridden land of Gorgoroth as the Nazgûl gather far away to face the Captains of the West. Sam gives the last of their water to Frodo and struggles with his own visions of the cool streams of the Shire. He resolves to carry Frodo up the slopes of Mount Doom if his master's strength fails completely.

At the foot of the Mountain, Sam lifts Frodo onto his back and begins the grueling ascent through the choking fumes. He discovers Sauron's Road, a path leading from Barad-dûr to the Sammath Naur, also called the Chambers of Fire. Gollum suddenly attacks the pair on the road, attempting to reclaim the "Precious" in a desperate, haggard state. Frodo fends off Gollum with a sudden burst of fury and commands the creature to begone under the threat of the Fire. Sam spares Gollum's life out of pity and watches as Frodo enters the Sammath Naur to fulfill his quest.



### 14: The Field of Cormallen
Inside the Sammath Naur, Frodo stands at the edge of the abyss and suddenly claims the Ring as his own. He puts on the Ring and vanishes, alerting Sauron and drawing the Nazgûl back toward the Mountain in a frantic race. Gollum attacks the invisible Frodo and bites the Ring-finger off his hand in a violent struggle. While gloating over his prize, Gollum loses his footing and falls into the fires of the Crack of Doom. The Ring is destroyed, causing Mount Doom to erupt and the foundations of Barad-dûr to crumble into ruin.

Aragorn and the Captains of the West witness the fall of the Black Gate and the dispersal of Sauron's mindless armies. Gandalf summons the Eagles to rescue Frodo and Sam from the literal island of ash where they await their deaths. The Eagles Gwaihir, Landroval, and Meneldor swoop down and carry the unconscious Hobbits away from the rivers of fire. Sam awakens in the fragrant woods of Ithilien to find Gandalf alive and Frodo resting nearby with a missing finger. He learns that the Shadow has departed and that a new age of the world has begun.

Frodo and Sam are honored as heroes by the gathered host of Gondor and Rohan on the Field of Cormallen. Aragorn bows his knee to them and sets them upon thrones as the army praises the Ring-bearers with great songs. A minstrel of Gondor sings the lay of Nine-fingered Frodo and the Ring of Doom to the weeping and laughing assembly. The Hobbits reunite with Merry and Pippin and learn of the great deeds performed by their friends during the war. Aragorn is crowned King Elessar outside the gates of Minas Tirith as the Sun rises on a new day.



### 15: The Steward and the King
Faramir and Éowyn meet in the Houses of Healing while the army is away at the Black Gate. Éowyn asks for raiment and states her desire for a warrior's death, but Faramir treats her with grave tenderness. They walk together in the gardens and wait for news from the East as the shadow of the Dark Tower looms. When Sauron falls, they witness the departure of the Shadow and feel a sudden surge of joy and hope. Faramir confesses his love for Éowyn, and she realizes her own heart has changed from a desire for glory to a desire for healing.

Aragorn returns to the City and receives the crown from Faramir and the scepter from Elrond. He is crowned King of Gondor and Arnor, and his reign begins with acts of mercy and the restoration of the City. Aragorn finds a sapling of the White Tree, a three-foot-tall plant with dark leaves and white petals, on the slopes of Mount Mindolluin. He plants the new tree in the Court of the Fountain, and it blooms as a sign of the new age. The King rewards those who served him faithfully, including Beregond, who is appointed captain of Faramir's guard.

On the Eve of Midsummer, the Elven-lords of Rivendell and Lothlórien arrive at the gates of Minas Tirith. Master Elrond brings his daughter Arwen Undómiel, who is described as the Evenstar of her people, to the City to fulfill her long-awaited wedding to Aragorn. The King wedded Arwen on the day of Midsummer, marking the end of their long labors and the fulfillment of their hopes. The Fellowship remains together for a time in the City as the world is healed and the scars of war are removed. Frodo and Sam eventually prepare for their journey back to the Shire as the Third Age comes to a close.
