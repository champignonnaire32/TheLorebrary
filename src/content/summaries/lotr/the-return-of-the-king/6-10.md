### Chapter 6: The Battle of the Pelennor Fields
Théoden leads the Rohirrim across the Pelennor Fields and slays the Haradrim chieftain. The Lord of the Nazgûl descends from the sky on a winged beast to challenge him. This featherless creature has vast pinions made of hide stretched between horned fingers and emits a foul stench. A black dart strikes the king's horse, Snowmane, which falls and crushes Théoden. The winged creature settles on the horse's carcass while its rider wields a black mace.

Merry Brandybuck crawls forward in fear as the Nazgûl threatens to devour the mind of the soldier Dernhelm. Dernhelm reveals her true identity as Éowyn, the niece of the king and daughter of Éomund. She beheads the winged beast with a single sword stroke as it lunges at her. The Ringwraith shatters her shield and breaks her arm with a heavy mace blow. Merry stabs the Ringwraith behind the knee with his enchanted blade from the Barrow-downs.

Éowyn drives her sword into the empty space between the crown and the mantle. The Witch-king vanishes with a thin wailing cry as his armor falls empty to the ground. Théoden dies after naming Éomer his successor and bidding farewell to the hobbit. Aragorn arrives at the Harlond quays aboard the captured black ships of the Corsairs. The forces of Gondor and Rohan unite to destroy the remaining Orcs and Haradrim.



### Chapter 7: The Pyre of Denethor
Pippin finds Gandalf at the Great Gate and reports Denethor’s intent to burn Faramir. They ride Shadowfax to the Citadel and find the door to the tombs guarded by the soldier Beregond. Beregond kills two of the Steward's servants to prevent them from entering the Hallows. Gandalf stops the remaining men and enters the House of the Stewards to find a pyre prepared. Faramir lies unconscious on a table surrounded by wood and oil while Denethor holds a torch.

Gandalf lifts Faramir from the fuel and carries him toward the door of the tomb. Denethor reveals he has looked into the palantír, a crystal Seeing Stone that reveals distant events. He believes the West has failed and refuses to serve a returning King of the North. The Steward asserts that the Dark Power has already stretched out its finger to destroy the City. Denethor breaks his white staff of office and casts the pieces into the growing flames.

The Steward leaps onto the pyre and holds the palantír as the fire consumes him. Gandalf closes the doors as the dome of the tomb collapses into a flurry of fire. Beregond is tasked with guarding the key to the tomb until the City is restored. Faramir is carried to the Houses of Healing to be treated for his fever and wounds. Gandalf explains that Sauron used the Stone to feed Denethor’s despair and cloud his mind.



### Chapter 8: The Houses of Healing
The dead and wounded are carried from the battlefield into the upper circles of Minas Tirith. Pippin finds Merry wandering dazed in the streets and leads him to the Houses of Healing. Merry suffers from a cold, numbed arm and the effects of the Black Breath. Faramir and Éowyn also lie in the houses afflicted by the same deadly Nazgûl malaise. The eldest healer, Ioreth, mentions an old lore stating that a true king has healing hands.

Gandalf summons Aragorn to the Houses of Healing to treat the dying captains. Aragorn refuses to enter the City as a ruler and wears a grey cloak over his mail. He calls for athelas, a long-leaved healing herb also known as kingsfoil or asëa aranion. The herb-master describes it as a common weed used by country folk for headaches. Aragorn crushes the leaves in steaming water to release a scent of dewy mornings.

Faramir awakens first and recognizes Aragorn as his lord and rightful king. Aragorn kisses Éowyn's brow and calls her back from the darkness of the shadow. Merry is revived last and immediately asks for a meal and his smoking pipe. Word spreads through the City that the King has returned to bring healing to his people. Aragorn slips out of the City before dawn to sleep in his tent upon the field.



### Chapter 9: The Last Debate
Legolas and Gimli enter Minas Tirith the morning after the battle to find their friends. They meet Prince Imrahil and observe the elven blood in his ancestral lineage. The Elf and Dwarf find Merry and Pippin resting in the gardens of the healers. Legolas sees gulls flying up the River Anduin and feels a sudden longing for the Sea. Gimli expresses his distaste for the Paths of the Dead and the horror of the mountain road.

Legolas recounts the journey of the Grey Company through the dark tunnels under the mountain. Aragorn summoned the Shadow Host, spectral wraiths cursed for breaking an oath, at the Black Stone of Erech. The Dead followed the Heir of Isildur across Lamedon and Lebennin to the port of Pelargir. The wraiths used fear to drive the Haradrim and Corsairs from their fifty black ships. Aragorn declared their oath fulfilled and released the spirits to be at rest forever.

The Captains of the West hold a council in Aragorn’s tents to plan their next move. Gandalf explains that Sauron is in doubt and must be drawn out into a trap. The lords decide to lead a diversionary force of seven thousand men to the Morannon. This army will act as bait to keep the Eye of Sauron away from the Ring-bearer. Aragorn draws the sword Andúril and vows not to sheath it until the final battle.



### Chapter 10: The Black Gate Opens
The Army of the West marches from the Pelennor Fields toward the Black Gate. Merry remains in the City to recover while Pippin marches as a soldier of Gondor. The host crosses the River Anduin at Osgiliath and reaches the Cross-roads of Ithilien. They replace the defiled orc-head on the stone king's statue with its original head. Scouts led by Mablung defeat an ambush of Orcs and Easterlings along the road.

The army reaches the slag-heaps before the Morannon and sounds a trumpet challenge. The Mouth of Sauron, a living Black Númenórean sorcerer and lieutenant, rides out to parley. He displays Sam's sword, an elven-cloak, and Frodo's mithril-mail coat as proof of capture. The Messenger demands the retreat of the West and the tribute of all lands east of the Misty Mountains. Gandalf rejects the terms and seizes the tokens from the Mouth of Sauron.

Sauron springs his trap by releasing a massive army of Orcs, Trolls, and Easterlings. The men of the West are surrounded on two hills of stone and blasted earth. Hill-trolls from Gorgoroth, creatures with scaled hides and heavy hammers, charge the front ranks. Pippin stabs the troll-chief with his Númenórean blade before being pinned beneath its body. He loses consciousness just as he hears a cry that the Great Eagles are arriving.
