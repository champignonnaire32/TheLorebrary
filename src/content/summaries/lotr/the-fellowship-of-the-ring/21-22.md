### Book II - Chapter 9: The Great River
The Company travels south down the Anduin in three small boats provided by the Elves of Lothlórien. Sam notices a log with eyes following them and suspects that Gollum is tracking the group. Frodo confirms seeing the same creature earlier in the journey and during their stay in Moria. Aragorn admits he has seen the creature paddling behind them on a log but has failed to capture it. The group decides to paddle faster and travel mostly by night to avoid detection by enemies.

On the eighth night, the boats reach the rapids of Sarn Gebir and are nearly swept into sharp rocks. Orcs on the eastern bank attack the Company with bows, and an arrow strikes Frodo's hidden mithril mail coat. The travelers struggle against the current and manage to paddle back upstream to a small bay on the western shore. A large winged creature, described as blacker than the pits in the night, flies over them from the south. Legolas shoots the creature out of the air with a single arrow from his Elven bow.

The next day, the Company carries their boats and baggage over a rocky portage-way to bypass the rapids. They return to the water and drift toward the hills of Emyn Muil as the fog lifts. Two massive stone statues, the Argonath or Pillars of the Kings, tower over the river on either side of the channel. Each king holds an axe and wears a crown while raising a palm in a gesture of warning. The boats pass through the narrow chasm between the statues and enter the oval lake of Nen Hithoel.



### Book II - Chapter 10: The Breaking of the Fellowship
Aragorn leads the boats to the lawn of Parth Galen at the base of Amon Hen to camp. Frodo draws his sword, Sting, and observes a faint blue glow indicating that Orcs are nearby. The Company gathers the next morning to decide whether to travel to Minas Tirith or continue toward Mordor. Aragorn tells Frodo that the choice rests solely on him as the appointed Ring-bearer. Frodo requests one hour of solitude to make his decision and walks up the slopes of the hill.

Boromir follows Frodo into the woods and attempts to convince him to bring the Ring to Minas Tirith. He argues that the Ring is a gift that should be used by a great leader to defeat Sauron. When Frodo refuses to hand over the Ring, Boromir grows angry and attempts to take it by force. Frodo puts the Ring on his finger to become invisible and escapes from the charging Man. Boromir falls, recovers from his madness, and shouts for Frodo to return, but the hobbit does not answer.

Frodo climbs to the summit of Amon Hen and sits upon the Seat of Seeing to view the world. He sees visions of war across Middle-earth and feels the Eye of Sauron searching for him. After removing the Ring, Frodo decides he must travel to Mordor alone to protect the other members of the Company. Sam realizes Frodo is attempting to leave by boat and jumps into the river to stop him. Frodo rescues the drowning Sam, and the two hobbits set off together toward the eastern shore.
