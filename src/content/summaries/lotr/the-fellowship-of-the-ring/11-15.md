### Chapter 11: Book I - Chapter 11: A Knife in the Dark
Black Riders enter the house at Crickhollow and find it empty of hobbits. Fatty Bolger escapes the residence and blows the Horn-call of Buckland to alert the neighborhood. The Riders flee from the growing alarm and gallop toward the North-gate of the Shire. In Bree, Strider wakes the hobbits and shows them their room has been ransacked and their bolsters slashed. The travelers discover that all the horses and ponies at the inn have been driven off or stolen.

Strider purchases a half-starved pony from Bill Ferny for twelve silver pennies to carry their supplies. The group departs Bree on foot while a crowd of villagers observes their exit. Sam strikes Bill Ferny in the face with an apple as they pass the last house in the village. Strider leads the hobbits off the main road and into the wilderness to avoid pursuit. They spend several days crossing the Midgewater Marshes while enduring constant attacks from insects.

The travelers reach the foot of Weathertop and find a message from Gandalf scratched into a stone. Strider identifies five Black Riders approaching the hill from the Road below. The group retreats to a sheltered dell and builds a fire to defend themselves. Five Ringwraiths - tall figures in grey robes with silver helms - attack the camp after nightfall. Frodo puts on the Ring and is stabbed in the shoulder by a glowing knife before Strider drives the attackers away with flaming brands.



### Chapter 12: Book I - Chapter 12: Flight to the Ford
Frodo regains consciousness by the fire and describes the pale king who attacked him. Strider finds a slashed black cloak and a notched morgul-knife - a cursed blade that melts away - on the ground. The Ranger uses the crushed leaves of the athelas plant to bathe and soothe Frodo's wound. The company places Frodo on the pony and flees south toward the Road to find better cover. They cross the Last Bridge over the River Mitheithel and find a green beryl left as a sign.

The travelers enter a region of steep hills and ancient stone ruins once inhabited by men of the North. Constant rain soaks the group as they scramble through pathless dales and rocky ridges. Frodo grows weaker and experiences a deadly chill that spreads from his shoulder through his side. The group discovers a cave containing old bones and large empty jars belonging to three stone trolls. They recognize these statues as the specific trolls turned to stone during Bilbo's earlier adventure.

Glorfindel, an Elf-lord with golden hair and a white horse, finds the company on the Road. He reports that the Nine Riders are searching the wilderness and that the Ford of Bruinen may be held against them. Frodo mounts Glorfindel's horse, Asfaloth, while the rest of the group marches at high speed through the night. Five Black Riders intercept the travelers near the river and begin a high-speed pursuit. Frodo crosses the Ford just before the river rises in a flood that sweeps away the pursuing horses and riders.



### Chapter 1: Book II - Chapter 1: Many Meetings
Frodo wakes in a carved bed within the House of Elrond on the morning of October twenty-fourth. Gandalf sits by the window and explains that Elrond removed a morgul-knife splinter from Frodo's shoulder. The wizard reveals that the Black Riders are the Ringwraiths, the nine mortal kings who were ensnared by Sauron. Sam has remained by Frodo's bedside for days during the hobbit's long recovery. Frodo discovers his arm has regained its movement and his health is returning rapidly.

The hobbits attend a feast in the great hall where Frodo sits next to the dwarf Glóin. Glóin describes the prosperity of the Lonely Mountain and the disappearance of Balin in the mines of Moria. Frodo sees Arwen Undómiel, the daughter of Elrond, who possesses a likeness to the elf-maiden Lúthien. After the meal, the company gathers in the Hall of Fire to listen to elvish music and stories. Frodo finds Bilbo Baggins sitting alone in a corner of the hall.

Bilbo asks to see the Ring one last time, but Frodo experiences a dark vision of his friend as a wrinkled creature. The two hobbits discuss the Shire until Strider, now called the Dúnadan, joins them to help Bilbo finish a song. Bilbo recites a long poem about the mariner Eärendil to a circle of attentive listeners. The Elf Lindir jokes about the difficulty of distinguishing between the poetry of two different mortals. Frodo eventually falls asleep to the sound of elvish singing before being led back to his room.



### Chapter 2: Book II - Chapter 2: The Council of Elrond
Elrond convenes a formal council attended by representatives of Elves, Dwarves, Men, and Hobbits. Boromir of Gondor describes a prophetic dream about the Halfling and the Sword that was Broken. Elrond recounts the history of the Second Age and the cutting of the Ring from Sauron's hand by Isildur. Glóin explains that a messenger from Mordor offered rings to Dáin in exchange for news of the thief Bilbo. Frodo stands and displays the One Ring to the entire assembly to confirm its discovery.

Gandalf details his search for Gollum and his discovery of Isildur's scroll in the archives of Minas Tirith. The wizard places the Ring in a fire to reveal the black speech of Mordor inscribed upon its surface. Legolas reports that Gollum escaped from the Elves of Mirkwood during an orc raid on his guards. Gandalf describes his imprisonment by the traitor Saruman the White on the pinnacle of the tower of Orthanc. The Eagle Gwaihir rescued Gandalf from the tower and carried him to the land of Rohan.

The Council debates whether to send the Ring to Tom Bombadil or cast it into the Sea. Elrond rejects these options and declares that the Ring must be taken to Mordor to be destroyed in Mount Doom. Boromir suggests using the Ring as a weapon against Sauron, but the Wise refuse the temptation of its power. Frodo volunteers to be the Ring-bearer and carry the object to the Fire. Sam Gamgee reveals himself from a corner and insists on accompanying his master on the quest.



### Chapter 3: Book II - Chapter 3: The Ring Goes South
Merry and Pippin protest being left out of the quest until Gandalf suggests they may be included later. Elrond sends out scouts to search the lands for any remaining trace of the Black Riders. The messengers find eight of the nine horses drowned in the river and a tattered black cloak. Elrond selects nine companions to form the Fellowship of the Ring to oppose the nine Ringwraiths. The group includes Frodo, Sam, Gandalf, Legolas, Gimli, Aragorn, Boromir, Merry, and Pippin.

The elven-smiths reforge the shards of Narsil into a new sword named Andúril for Aragorn. Bilbo gives Frodo his elven-blade, Sting, and a hidden coat of mithril mail for protection. The company departs Rivendell at dusk on a cold day in late December with a laden pony named Bill. They journey south through the desolate land of Hollin while avoiding the eyes of flying spies. Regiments of black crows called crebain fly over the group to observe their movements.

The travelers attempt to cross the Misty Mountains by climbing the high pass of the Redhorn Gate. A violent blizzard strikes the company and buries the hobbits in deep drifts of snow. Stones fall from the heights of Caradhras as the mountain itself seems to resist their passage. Gandalf uses magic to light a fire to save the group from freezing to death in the storm. Boromir and Aragorn force a path back down the mountain through the snow to lead the company to safety.
