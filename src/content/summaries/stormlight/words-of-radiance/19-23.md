### Chapter 19: Safe Things
Five and a half years ago, Shallan Davar sits in her family's dining chamber after months of silence following her mother's death. Her father, Brightlord Davar, has recently killed his wife and her lover, leaving the household in a state of repressed terror. Helaran, Shallan’s oldest brother, returns to the manor and presents her with a satchel of expensive charcoal pencils and drawing paper. He encourages her to practice drawing as a way to find strength for her younger siblings. Shallan begins to sketch, but her initial drawings depict the traumatic image of bodies lying facedown on the floor.

Helaran crumples the drawing and instructs her to focus on safe subjects like plants and animals instead of dwelling on the past. He warns her that he must be away frequently and tasks her with watching over their brother Balat, who has shown cruelty to animals. Their father enters the room wearing traditional Veden clothing and accompanied by three parshmen with marbled skin. He bellows at Helaran for preparing a carriage to leave again, leading to a heated confrontation between father and son. Helaran calls his father a murderer and refuses to listen to his defensive lies regarding his mother.

Helaran suddenly summons a Shardblade, a six-foot-long silvery steel sword with a gemstone set in the pommel, which coalesces from mist. He levels the Blade at his father’s chest, threatening to kill him as a mercy to the household. Shallan breaks her long silence to plead for her father’s life, prompting Helaran to lower the weapon. Helaran promises to tell Shallan about his mysterious new friends and their important work before departing the manor. Brightlord Davar reacts to the departure by brutally smashing furniture in a fit of rage while Shallan watches in horror.



### Chapter 20: The Coldness of Clarity
Shallan and Tvlakv’s caravan approach a smoldering site where three wagons have been destroyed and corpses litter the ground. They find a surviving caravan led by Macob and guarded by a woman named Tyn, who is currently fending off bandit attacks. Bluth scouts the area and confirms that a group of thirty well-equipped deserters is following Shallan’s tracks from behind. Tyn explains that the bandits ahead used fire to weaken their defenses and are expected to return before the coming highstorm. Facing threats from both directions, Shallan ignores Tvlakv’s pleas to flee and decides to intervene using the Stormlight she carries.

Shallan commands Bluth to drive her wagon south directly toward the approaching deserters while she feels the "coldness of clarity" guiding her. Upon reaching the deserters, she stands on her seat and calls out to them, claiming that bandits are slaughtering soldiers nearby. She confronts their leader, Vathah, a tall bearded man with long arms, and challenges him to erase his criminal past by becoming a hero. Shallan uses a Lightweaving illusion to make herself appear as a stately queen in a golden gown rather than a dirty refugee. She promises the men that she will see their crimes erased and their futures changed if they defend the caravan.

Vathah initially scoffs at her offer and orders her capture, but his men are moved by the distant screams of the dying workers. The deserters, longing for their former lives as soldiers, ignore Vathah’s orders and charge toward the bandit attack to join the fight. Bluth joins the charge with his cudgel, leaving Shallan alone with Vathah and a few remaining men who eventually follow. Shallan slumps against her wagon, exhausted after depleting her Stormlight and watching the violet moon Salas rise in the sky. She sketches glyphs for a prayer of hope while Pattern reveals that she possesses the powers of Lightweaving and Transformation.



### Chapter 21: Ashes
The battle concludes with the deserters defeating the bandits, though Bluth and seven of Vathah’s soldiers are killed in the fighting. Shallan finds her drawing of Bluth as a hero in his pocket and keeps it as a memento of his sacrifice. She approaches Macob, the caravan owner, and Tyn, the head of guards, to negotiate their travel to the Shattered Plains. Macob agrees to provide Shallan with fine clothing, food, and luxury in exchange for the protection of her new mercenary force. Shallan asserts her authority over the combined caravan, insisting that her "soldiers" will ensure their collective safety for the duration of the trip.

Vathah confronts Shallan, pointing out that she no longer looks like the queen he saw in the darkness and expressing fear of being hanged. He tenses his grip on her arm, but Pattern intervenes by speaking from the back of his uniform, prompting Vathah to retreat in confusion. Shallan turns her attention to Tvlakv and demands the five slaves he is transporting as payment for her protection. She forces Tvlakv to hand over the writs of slavery and opens the cage to offer the men paid work as her servants. She also purchases a third wagon from him using sapphire broams, though Tvlakv refuses to sell his parshmen workers.

The former deserters gather around a bonfire to burn a prayer of thanks given to them by the darkeyed caravan workers. Shallan observes the men standing taller as they receive the silent appreciation of the families they saved from the bandits. She prepares for the approaching highstorm, noting that several wagons were lost and shelter is limited for the survivors. Tvlakv decides to head south as originally intended while Shallan prepares to move toward the Alethi warcamps with her expanded retinue. Pattern watches the transitions, noting that Shallan’s lies have effectively transformed the criminals into men of honor.



### Chapter 22: Lights in the Storm
During a highstorm at the Shattered Plains, Kaladin observes red lights resembling eyes outside the palace windows before King Elhokar interrupts his trance. Inside the palace, a heating fabrial—a technology using a large ruby to generate warmth—keeps the room comfortable without a traditional fire. Adolin and Renarin wait out the storm with their father, Dalinar, while Kaladin maintains a strict guard rotation using men from Bridge Four. Kaladin inspects the ruby in the hearth, noticing that the Stormlight within shifts with the chaotic patterns of the tempest outside. Dalinar and Navani retire to a windowless side room where the highprince experiences his recurring visions of the past.

After the storm passes, Adolin admits that highprinces are refusing his dueling challenges because they fear his skill with Shardplate and Blade. Dalinar discusses the possibility of Highlord Amaram seceding from Sadeas’s princedom to join the Kholin house, bringing his territory and Shards with him. Kaladin feels a surge of anger at the mention of Amaram but remains silent while the lighteyes plan their political maneuvers. Dalinar instructs Adolin to focus his duels on undermining Sadeas’s influence rather than fighting every Shardbearer in the warcamps. Kaladin dismisses several exhausted Bridge Four guards, including Moash, who has been pulling double shifts to train with the King’s Guard.

Kaladin accompanies Dalinar toward the palace doors and decides to reveal the truth about Highlord Amaram’s rise to power. He tells Dalinar that he was the soldier who killed a Shardbearer, only for Amaram to steal the Shards and slaughter his squad. Kaladin explains that he was branded a slave and sold to keep the theft a secret, naming a stormwarden who witnessed the act. Dalinar reacts with skepticism, stating that Amaram is one of the most honorable men he knows and asking for concrete proof. As Kaladin returns to the Bridge Four barracks, he meets Punio, a new Herdazian recruit, and is confronted by Shen about his status as a slave.



### Chapter 23: Assassin
Natam arrives at the Bridge Four barracks in a panic, reporting an assassination attempt on King Elhokar at the palace. Kaladin rushes back to the royal chambers and finds the king alive but suffering from a minor gash on his forehead. The attempt occurred on a stone balcony where a section of the iron railing had been tampered with and pulled free of its mountings. Elhokar had leaned against the rail, causing it to bend outward and leaving him hanging a hundred feet above the rocks. Guards rescued the king after he clung to a single bar of the twisted ironwork that remained attached.

Kaladin and Moash inspect the damaged railing and discover that the mortar in the stone mountings has been deliberately chipped away. They notice that the metal joints were not just broken but were sheared with the smooth, clean edges of a Shardblade. Dalinar joins Kaladin on the balcony and agrees that the sabotage was a deliberate attempt to make the king’s death look like an accident. He notes that the assassins targeted the king's favorite spot for viewing the Shattered Plains, suggesting they have intimate knowledge of his habits. Kaladin observes that the methods used are nonconfrontational, leading him to label the conspirators as cowards rather than bold assassins.

Dalinar decides to hide the evidence of the Shardblade cut to maintain a tactical advantage against the unknown attackers. He reveals that the king has requested Kaladin to take over his personal security, moving up the timetable for Bridge Four’s new duties. Dalinar admits his growing distrust of the King’s Guard and the palace servants, fearing that someone on the inside is responsible. He promotes Idrin to a military command and places his best men under Kaladin’s leadership for the duration of the transition. Elhokar continues to rant about seeing mysterious things over his shoulder in mirrors, while Kaladin prepares to protect a king he finds difficult to respect.
