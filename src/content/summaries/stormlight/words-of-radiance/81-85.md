### Chapter 81: The Last Day
Dalinar walks into the rain with Navani and Shallan as the Alethi army approaches the center of the Shattered Plains. Thousands of Parshendi gather on the surrounding plateaus with glowing red eyes. Dalinar encourages Aladar and Roion before giving a public speech to his soldiers about his visions and the Voidbringers. The Parshendi begin a staccato song that causes red lightning to spark around their bodies. Rlain warns Dalinar that the rhythm is one of destruction and begs him to stop the performance.

Dalinar changes his strategy and orders an immediate attack on the singing Parshendi. Kaladin searches the warcamp training grounds for Wit but finds only the ardent Zahel. Zahel explains that the training grounds are flooded with water to wash the sand of the sparring floor. Kaladin practices spear forms in the water and realizes that King Elhokar is like Dalinar's brother Tien. Adolin prepares his battalions for a charge across the bridges to the southern plateau.

He tells his bridgeman guards Skar and Drehy to keep up as they cross the chasm. A blue gemstone signal is raised at the command tent to initiate the assault. Adolin rides Sureblood into the Parshendi lines while his infantry follows with hammers and axes. Shallan works on a massive map of the Shattered Plains to identify the location of the Oathgate. Pattern identifies Stormspren, a variety of Voidspren with spindly arms and bone-like features, hovering near the plateau.



### Chapter 82: For Glory Lit
Kaladin limps to the king's palace and finds it unguarded as he searches for Elhokar. He encounters and defeats two of Graves's men who are disguised as Bridge Four guards. Kaladin enters the king's chambers and discovers Elhokar is drunk and lying on a couch. Adolin’s horse Sureblood is killed by a bolt of red lightning during the charge. Adolin charges into the Parshendi lines and observes that small red spren emerge from the corpses.

Shallan completes her map and notices a discrepancy in the shape of a specific plateau. A scout informs Shallan that the central plateau is a perfect circle with large gaps around it. Dalinar sends Renarin and a squad of soldiers to protect Shallan as she investigates the circle. Aladar's army struggles against the red lightning, but Dalinar reinforces the eastern flank with light infantry. Navani activates Attractors, which are fabrials consisting of a glowing garnet in wire lacework that pull moisture from the air.

Dalinar hears a voice he recognizes as the Almighty speaking to him out of the air. Kaladin tries to evacuate Elhokar, but a hidden assassin stabs the king in the side. Cleft-chin, the attacker, dies after Kaladin thrusts a spear into his stomach. Moash and Graves arrive at the palace hallway in Shardplate to finish the assassination. Kaladin stands between the king and the Shardbearers despite his reopened wounds and broken leg.



### Chapter 83: Time’s Illusion
Shallan and the scholars arrive at the circular plateau and identify it as the dais for the Oathgate. Renarin observes the sky and declares that the Everstorm, a magical storm coming from the west, is approaching. Adolin leads a battalion into a hollow crem-covered building to ambush the singing Parshendi. He cuts a hole in the wall and leads his men into the ranks of the singing Parshendi. The singing Parshendi are disoriented and killed easily as their song is interrupted.

Eshonai arrives in her own Shardplate and engages Adolin in a duel. Dalinar rescues Roion’s routed army by using Navani’s archer pavilions and moisture-collecting fabrials. The voice in the air tells Dalinar it is the Stormfather and that the Almighty is dead. Dalinar pleads with the Stormfather not to abandon humanity as the Everstorm approaches. Moash tries to convince Kaladin to step aside so they can kill the king.

Kaladin refuses and raises a broken spear to defend the unconscious Elhokar. Graves urges Moash to kill Kaladin, claiming the king's death is necessary for the kingdom. Kaladin argues that they must be better men and offers to help Moash get justice through legal means. Moash rejects the offer, stating it is too late for legal recourse. Moash seals his faceplate and prepares to attack his former captain.



### Chapter 84: The One Who Saves
Renarin uses his Shardblade to cut a hole into the central building on the circular plateau. Shallan enters the chamber and discovers a massive mosaic mural depicting the Knights Radiant and ten kingdoms. Pattern warns Shallan that the Everstorm and a highstorm are about to collide over the plains. Renarin inserts his Shardblade into a ten-pointed star keyhole to activate the Oathgate. Adolin duels Eshonai near a chasm and manages to push her over the edge to her death.

Adolin nearly falls into the void himself but is caught by Skar and Drehy. Moash strikes Kaladin in the gut with an armored fist, breaking his ribs. Graves prepares to kill the king with a Shardblade to frame the Assassin in White. Kaladin struggles to his feet and swears the Third Ideal of the Windrunners. He vows to protect even those he hates as long as it is right.

Syl returns and transforms into a glowing Shardblade in Kaladin's hand. Kaladin explodes with Stormlight and heals his injuries as a glyph of wings forms in the frost beneath him. Graves flees in terror after realizing the Diagram, a coded set of prophecies on a ceiling, failed him. Moash runs away down the palace corridor as Kaladin manifests his new powers. Kaladin looks eastward and realizes that Dalinar is in grave danger from the coming storms.



### Chapter 85: Swallowed by the Sky
The Alethi command tent is ripped away by the wind as the storms begin to collide. Szeth-son-son-Vallano arrives at the camp and begins his attack on the Kholin forces. In the Oathgate chamber, Renarin huddles against a wall and writes a countdown of zeros in his distress. Dalinar orders his men to evacuate toward the circular plateau as the stormwall approaches. Adolin is thrown through a tent by Szeth and suffers heavy damage to his Shardplate.

Dalinar challenges Szeth and asks why he murdered King Gavilar. Szeth identifies himself as Truthless of Shinovar and claims he must obey his masters. Dalinar bonds Szeth's old Blade and engages the assassin in a sword duel. Szeth uses his Lashings, which are gravitational surges that allow him to fly or change weight, to break Dalinar's ribs. Highprince Roion charges Szeth but is Lashed into the sky to fall to his death.

Adolin and a group of bridgemen attempt to rush the assassin, but they are easily repelled. Szeth Lashes Dalinar into the air, sending the highprince upward to die in the storm. Kaladin arrives from the sky and uses his powers to lower Dalinar safely to the ground. Kaladin crashes onto the plateau, creating a ring of Stormlight and a glowing glyph. He identifies himself as the one who claims the winds and challenges Szeth for his life.
