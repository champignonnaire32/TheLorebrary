### 86: Patterns of Light
Kaladin saves Dalinar from Szeth's sky-lashing and sends him back to the plateau. He encounters Bridge Four and absorbs Stormlight from their lanterns. Dalinar recognizes Kaladin as a Knight Radiant. Kaladin's Shardblade, Syl, manifests as a silver sword. He engages Szeth in a dual of Blades.

Shallan identifies the circular chamber as a massive fabrial powered by lamps. Adolin joins the retreat as the everstorm and highstorm begin to collide. Dalinar organizes the armies of Sebarial, Aladar, and Roion on the circular plateau. Kaladin and Szeth take their fight into the sky above the storm clouds. Szeth reveals he uses an Honorblade, a weapon that grants Windrunner powers without a spren bond.

Shallan uses her Shardblade, Pattern, to unlock the Oathgate fabrial and transport the army. Kaladin and Szeth duel atop the storms until Kaladin severs Szeth's spine with a Blade strike. Szeth falls into the stormwall, and Kaladin retrieves his Honorblade. The Alethi army arrives at the mountain city of Urithiru via the teleportation plateau. Kaladin lands safely and celebrates his victory with Syl.





### 87: The Riddens
Kaladin surveys the destruction on the Shattered Plains after the storm collision. Syl explains that the Everstorm is a new phenomenon created by the enemy. Kaladin carries Szeth’s Honorblade, which Syl identifies as a Herald’s weapon that feeds heavily on Stormlight. He discovers the Alethi army has disappeared from the circular plateau. He encounters Bridge Four members who remained behind to wait for his return.

The bridgemen reveal that Shallan taught them how to operate the plateau’s teleportation mechanism. Rlain, the Bridge Four parshman, reveals he was a spy for the Parshendi but has been pardoned by Dalinar. Teft reports that some Bridge Four members have begun to glow with Stormlight. Kaladin learns that his eyes have turned pale blue due to his Radiant bond. He uses Syl as a key to activate the fabrial and rejoin the army.

Shallan explores the tower city of Urithiru, which sits high in a remote mountain range. The Oathgate teleportation drained most of the army's gemstones and left them isolated. Dalinar and Navani receive reports that King Elhokar is missing and Kholinar is in riot. The Lopen, guarding the King in a secret location, successfully breathes in Stormlight and begins regrowing his arm. Moash and Graves flee the warcamps, revealing their membership in the Diagram and their plan to return Voidbringers.





### 88: The Man Who Owned the Winds
The Alethi army moves into the tower of Urithiru to seek shelter from the cold and storms. Shallan finds a Ghostblood card with her true name on a wall. Mraize confronts her, revealing he knows her identity and her Radiant powers. He claims the Ghostbloods saved her brothers from Jah Keved's chaos and are bringing them to her. Shallan summons Pattern as a Shardblade to threaten him, but he leaves her with an invitation to join them.

Shallan later embraces Adolin, who acknowledges her status as a Radiant and shows her to a room. Pattern forces Shallan to confront a memory of her childhood home and her mother's death. She recalls that her mother tried to kill her after discovering her Surgebinding abilities. Shallan killed her mother and her mother's friend in self-defense using Pattern as a Blade. Her father took the blame for the murders to protect her, which ultimately destroyed their family.

Highlord Amaram writes to the leader of the Sons of Honor about the success of returning the Voidbringers. He attempts to kidnap the Herald Talenelat'Elin from the Alethi monastery. A Ghostblood assassin tries to kill Amaram with poisoned darts, but Talenelat catches them with superhuman speed. The Herald then relapses into his catatonic state, and Amaram takes him to a coach. Szeth is resurrected by the Herald Nale using a specialized fabrial and receives a black, smoking Shardblade.





### 89: The Four
Dalinar experiences a dream of his childhood home bathed in a warm, piercing light. He awakens in Urithiru and prepares to scout the tower's roof. Adolin encounters Sadeas in a dark hallway and confronts him about his plans to undermine Dalinar. Sadeas admits he will continue to oppose Dalinar and try to take control of the city. Adolin snaps, attacks Sadeas, and kills him by driving a knife through his eye into his brain.

Adolin discards Sadeas's Shardblade out a window and conceals his involvement in the murder. Dalinar reaches the roof of the tower and speaks directly to the Stormfather. The Stormfather explains he is a spren of the Almighty and that the Everstorm will transform all parshmen into Voidbringers. Dalinar swears the second oath of the Bondsmiths, promising to unite instead of divide. The Stormfather accepts the words but warns Dalinar he will have no Shardblade and must lead a dying people.

Dalinar returns to find Kaladin and Shallan, demonstrating his new ability to infuse gemstones. Renarin reveals he is also a Radiant of the Truthwatcher order and has healed his own eyes. Dalinar explains the threat of the Everstorm and the need to find more Radiants. Kaladin decides to fly to his hometown of Hearthstone to protect his parents from the parshmen. Dalinar provides him with Stormlight, and the four Radiants commit to saving whom they can.





### Epilogue: Art and Expectation
Hoid, known as Wit, sits in the Frostlands and philosophizes about the nature of art to a small crab-like creature. He plays his pipes and discusses how art depends entirely on the audience's expectations. He notes that a performer is only as good as what the audience expects to see. Wit strikes a pose as a ring of light appears on the ground nearby. Jasnah Kholin emerges from the light, having escaped the Cognitive Realm.

Jasnah appears ragged and burned, having survived the assassination attempt at sea. She immediately summons a long, thin Shardblade and points it at Wit's neck. Wit remains unimpressed and informs her that Urithiru has been found and the Radiants are refounded. He mentions that her apprentice, Shallan, is significantly more agreeable than she is. Jasnah dismisses her Blade and prepares to walk toward the nearest civilization.

Wit joins Jasnah on her journey and confirms that the Everstorm has arrived. Jasnah worries that her knowledge of the past may be inaccurate if the desolation is happening differently this time. Wit suggests that they might have synergy if they share their different knowledge. He tells her she will find salvation inside the hearts of men rather than in the heavens. They walk together under the stars as the world enters its era of endings.
