### 53: Perfection
Adolin Kholin duels Elit Ruthar in the arena using precise Windstance maneuvers. He focuses on creating numerous small cracks in Elit's Shardplate to drain its Stormlight. The crowd reacts with dissatisfaction to Adolin's clinical and unexciting combat style. Adolin systematically deflects Elit's strikes without launching a significant counterattack. He eventually wins the duel by simply pushing the immobilized Elit over once his armor fails.

Following the victory, Adolin meets with Shallan Davar in the preparation chamber. Shallan praises his skill but suggests he needs a more spectacular performance to achieve his goals. She mentions discovering a historical precedent in King Gavilar's biography that could help his cause. Adolin expresses concern that his aunt Navani is still avoiding Shallan. Their conversation is interrupted when Relis Ruthar enters to complain about the previous duel's lack of honor.

Relis demands the duel be invalidated, but Adolin challenges him to a new match. Adolin wagers all his family's Shards, totaling six sets, on the outcome of a single bout. He offers to fight Relis and any number of companions simultaneously to entice him into the agreement. Relis accepts the terms and schedules the fight for two days later in the arena. Adolin then asks Shallan for more details about her plan involving Highprince Yenev.



### 54: Veil’s Lesson
Shallan Davar travels to the Unclaimed Hills dressed in the white coat and trousers of her persona, Veil. She meets Mraize at a temporary pavilion located near a rock fracture. Mraize demonstrates the use of a Parshendi blowgun, which is a short tube used to launch small darts at prey. He uses the weapon to pin a cremling to a stem to illustrate his point about cultural weapons. Shallan observes that the blowgun is primarily a tool for hunters seeking food rather than trophies.

Shallan presents Mraize with detailed sketches of Meridas Amaram’s private room and maps. Mraize expresses surprise at the quality of the drawings and the information she gathered. He correctly deduces that Shallan killed her previous mentor, Tyn, and praises her for surpassing her superior. Shallan admits to the act but claims it was done in self-defense. Mraize offers her a potential role in the Ghostbloods if she proves reliable through future assignments.

After leaving the pavilion, Shallan uses Stormlight to create an illusion of a boulder and hides inside it. She overhears Mraize discussing Master Thaidakar and the activities of other secret factions. Once Mraize departs, Shallan discovers her hired carriage has been burned and its occupants murdered. The coachman’s throat is slit and the parshman porters are left in a pile near the wreckage. Shallan walks back to the warcamps alone, realizing the lethal nature of the organization she is infiltrating.



### 55: The Rules of the Game
Kaladin serves as a bodyguard for Adolin Kholin during a scheduled date with Shallan Davar. They find Hoid, the King’s Wit, acting as their carriage driver for the journey. Shallan surprises both men by embracing Wit and thanking him for his past assistance. During the ride, Shallan presents research about the historical Right of Challenge and the King’s Boon. She explains how Sadeas previously used these traditions to legally execute Highprince Yenev in a duel.

The group visits a menagerie in the Outer Market to observe various exotic animals. They view a sarpenthyn, which is a translucent, many-tentacled creature that is warm to the touch. Shallan sketches a colorful chicken with vibrant red, blue, and green feathers. They also observe a whitespine, a predatory beast with large tusks and armored spikes along its back. Adolin and Shallan continue to bond while Kaladin remains suspicious of Shallan's true motives and calculating nature.

Dalinar Kholin and Meridas Amaram arrive at the menagerie to address the public. Amaram wears a golden cloak emblazoned with the double eye symbol of the Knights Radiant. Dalinar officially announces the reestablishment of the Knights Radiant under the king’s direction. He names Amaram as the first member and leader of this newly reformed order. Kaladin watches in silent fury as the man who enslaved him is elevated to a position of holy honor.



### 56: Whitespine Uncaged
Kaladin arrives at the dueling arena with forty guards to protect the royal family and Dalinar. Adolin prepares for his duel against Relis Ruthar by praying with a glyphward. To Adolin’s shock, four Shardbearers emerge from the preparation room instead of the expected two. Sadeas reveals he manipulated the challenge's wording to subject Adolin to a full disadvantaged duel. Adolin chooses to proceed with the fight despite the overwhelming odds and the risk of losing his family's Shards.

Adolin initiates a furious assault to keep the four opponents from surrounding him. He manages to crack the armor of both Elit and Relis during the initial stages of the combat. However, the numbers eventually overwhelm him as Jakamav and Abrobadar join the attack from his flanks. Adolin is struck by a Shardhammer and begins to leak significant amounts of Stormlight from his Plate. He realizes the match is a trap intended to cripple him rather than a fair competition.

Renarin Kholin enters the arena without Shardplate to assist his brother but is quickly neutralized by Abrobadar. The attackers ignore Adolin’s attempts to surrender and continue to strike his weakened armor. Dalinar fruitlessly appeals to the gathered lighteyes for someone to help his sons in the arena. Highprince Amaram refuses to intervene, looking away when Dalinar meets his eyes. Kaladin declares that honor is dead and leaps onto the sands with only his spear to aid Adolin.



### 57: To Kill the Wind
Kaladin enters the arena and uses his spear to distract Relis and the other Shardbearers. He uses Stormlight to heal his legs after performing a powerful Lashing-enhanced kick that cracks Relis's armor. Adolin manages to break Elit’s breastplate, forcing the man to yield to Kaladin's knife. The prince then focuses on the remaining opponents while Kaladin uses a discarded Shardplate helm as an improvised shield. Adolin eventually defeats Abrobadar, leaving only Jakamav and Relis active in the match.

Adolin grapples Jakamav in a wrestling move that causes both of them to crash to the sand. Relis attempts to attack the defenseless Renarin, but Kaladin intervenes just in time. Kaladin catches Relis’s Shardblade between his palms, an act that triggers a psychic scream from the weapon. The scream drives Relis into a state of temporary madness, causing him to flee the arena and forfeit. Jakamav yields to Kaladin’s knife, officially ending the duel in a victory for the Kholin brothers.

King Elhokar offers Adolin a boon for his spectacular victory in the arena. Adolin demands a Right of Challenge against Highprince Sadeas for his crimes against House Kholin. Kaladin immediately steps forward to demand his own boon, challenging Meridas Amaram for murder and theft. He publicly accuses Amaram of slaughtering his friends and branding him a slave. The King reacts with fury at the darkeyed man's insolence and orders his immediate arrest.
