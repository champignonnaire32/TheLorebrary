### 10. Red Carpet Once White
Six years ago, young Shallan stands in a room with two corpses on a red carpet. Her father, Lin Davar, whispers to her and wipes blood from her cheek. He sings a lullaby to comfort her while she trembles in shock. Lin carries Shallan past her mother’s body, which lies facedown on the floor. They pass a glowing strongbox in the wall before Lin closes the door on the scene.

Shallan feels like a monster for the murders she has committed. She notes the man in the room bled significantly while the woman in white did not. Lin tells her he will protect her and to pretend the events never happened. The strongbox set into the wall glows with an intense light from the cracks. Shallan remains frozen and unable to move as her father holds her.

The lullaby mentions crystals that glow sublime and sleeping in deep chasms. Shallan imagines the song might end her nightmare despite the reality of the bodies. Her skin squirms at her father's touch because she believes she is a murderer. Lin's thumb is stained red from the moisture on Shallan's face. The chapter concludes as Lin carries his daughter away from the site of the killings.





### 11. An Illusion of Perception
Shallan wakes up on a rock in the ocean after the shipwreck of the Wind's Pleasure. She sees a santhid, a massive sea creature with a large brown shell, swimming away. Pattern, a Cryptic spren appearing as a shifting geometric pattern, hums near her. Shallan realizes Jasnah Kholin is dead and the ship's crew is gone. She swims through frigid water to reach the sandy shore of the Frostlands, a chilly and uninhabited coastal region.

Shallan searches the coastline for survivors but finds only debris and coarse sand. She attempts to Soulcast a pile of sticks into fire using a garnet sphere. During her attempt, she enters Shadesmar, a realm with a bead-filled ocean and a black sky. The stick in the cognitive realm refuses to change, repeatedly stating that it is a stick. Shallan fails to create fire, exhausts her Stormlight, and collapses from the cold.

She walks toward a distant light and stumbles into a camp of slavers. The lead slaver, a Thaylen man named Tvlakv, wakes her the following morning. Shallan assumes a commanding persona to convince Tvlakv she is a powerful lighteyes. She discovers her trunk filled with Jasnah’s notes has washed ashore and commands the men to retrieve it. Tvlakv agrees to transport Shallan and her trunk toward the Shattered Plains in his chull wagons.





### 12. Hero
Kaladin practices his Surgebinding abilities with Bridge Four members in the chasms. He uses Stormlight to stick Rock, a large Horneater with four rows of molars, to a wall. Sigzil records the duration of the Full Lashing, noting it lasts eighty-seven seconds with a diamond chip. Lopen, a one-armed Herdazian man, jokes while Kaladin tests his ability to stick people to surfaces. Syl, Kaladin’s honorspren, watches as Kaladin learns to manipulate Stormlight like luminescent paint.

Kaladin spars against Rock, Sigzil, and Lopen to test his new combat powers. He sticks practice spears together and adheres Rock to the ground during a grapple. Rock uses a small tree log as a weapon but cannot overcome Kaladin's Lashings. Teft arrives to report that the new bridge recruits are adjusting well to training. Kaladin decides he must practice until his abilities are as natural as his spear work.

The group returns to the surface and learns that a new hero has arrived at the warcamp. Kaladin runs to Dalinar's complex and sees Highprince Dalinar Kholin greeting a guest. The newcomer is Highlord Amaram, the man who branded Kaladin and stole his Shardblade. Amaram reveals a new Shardblade he claims to have taken from an assassin. Kaladin stands frozen in hatred as he watches the man who sold him into slavery.





### Interludes
Eshonai, a Parshendi Shardbearer in warform, harvests a gemheart on the Shattered Plains. She returns to Narak, the listener capital, and discusses her desire for peace with her sister Venli. Venli, a scholar in nimbleform, reveals she has discovered the secret to stormform, an ancient power. In Iri, an old cobbler named Ym heals an urchin's foot using a glowing sphere. A dark-skinned man with a crescent scar executes Ym with a Shardblade for a past murder.

Rysn, a Thaylen apprentice trader, travels to a Reshi island that is actually a massive greatshell. She falls from the creature's head while trying to prove her boldness to the Reshi king. A larkin, a small winged cremling that drains Stormlight, is given to Rysn as she recovers. Eshonai visits her mother, who is losing her memory and singing songs of the Last Legion. She learns the listeners fled their gods by adopting dullform, a form of low intelligence.

Eshonai meets with the Five, the listener leaders, to discuss the discovery of stormform. The leaders worry that Surgebinders have returned to humans and decide they need power. Eshonai volunteers to be the first to test the transformation into stormform during a highstorm. Venli expresses resentment toward Eshonai for taking the opportunity to be the first to change. The interlude ends with Eshonai hoping she can still find Dalinar Kholin to negotiate for peace.





### 13. The Day’s Masterpiece
Shallan travels toward the Shattered Plains in a slave wagon driven by the mercenary Bluth. She observes the stunted flora of the Frostlands, noting rockbuds and brittle, tubular shrubs. Bluth remains sullen and suspicious of Shallan’s high-born vocabulary and refined manners. Shallan commands Tvlakv to convert a slave cage into a private carriage for her journey. She locks herself inside the cage with Jasnah’s trunk to ensure the security of the research.

Inside the wagon, Shallan cleans her wounded feet and speaks with Pattern. Pattern explains that he was sent to her because of the lies in her past. He warns her that his kind, the Cryptics, are worried about the return of the Voidbringers. Shallan looks through Jasnah's notes to find a way to continue the search for Urithiru. She discovers a sketch she drew of Jasnah which the scholar had kept in her trunk.

Shallan mourns the loss of her original sketchpads and all her previous artwork. She realizes that the people who killed Jasnah are likely trying to profit from the apocalypse. Pattern encourages her to keep drawing, asserting that her nature cannot be stopped. Shallan resolves to reach the Shattered Plains and warn the Alethi about the parshmen. She wraps her feet in cloths and prepares to face the long journey through the wilderness.
