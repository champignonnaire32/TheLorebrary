### 38: The Silent Storm
Shallan Davar meets Dalinar and Navani Kholin to deliver the news of Jasnah Kholin’s death on the Wind’s Pleasure. She recounts watching assassins stab Jasnah through the heart and setting the ship on fire to create a diversion during the attack. Dalinar agrees to provide royal pardons for the deserters who accompanied Shallan through the Frostlands. During a meeting of highprinces, Shallan encounters Sebarial, a plump man wearing a takama, which is an open-fronted robe over a warrior's skirt. Shallan publicly claims Sebarial has hired her as a clerk to avoid the restrictive oversight of Dalinar’s household.

Dalinar announces his intention to lead an army to the center of the Shattered Plains to find the Parshendi camp and end the war. Highprince Sadeas mocks the plan, reminding the room of the military disaster on the Tower plateau. Dalinar reveals that the Parshendi sent a messenger to discuss peace shortly before the Assassin in White attacked the palace. He declares that Adolin will win Shards from the other highprinces to provide more Shardbearers for the upcoming expedition. Shallan meets Adolin Kholin, who wears a blue Alethi uniform and offers her a cup of orange wine.

She notes that Highprinces Aladar, Ruthar, and Vamah are skeptical of the king’s authority and Dalinar’s leadership. Sebarial reluctantly accepts Shallan’s claim of employment despite her demand for a stipend of three broams per week. Shallan describes Sebarial’s banner as a gold skyeel on a black field. After the meeting, Shallan visits King Elhokar to personally deliver the news regarding his sister’s fate. She delivers the writ of pardon to Vathah and Gaz before preparing to move into Sebarial’s warcamp.

### 39: Heterochromatic
Lin Davar hosts a feast for Brightlord Tavinar and his family despite the failing finances of House Davar. He announces his upcoming marriage to the youngest daughter of Brightlord Gevelmar to provide a new mother for his children. Redin, the bastard son of Highprince Valam who possesses one blue eye and one brown eye, interrupts the feast. The messenger explains he is investigating rumors that Lin Davar murdered his wife and her alleged lover. Shallan sits with her brothers Balat, Wikim, and Jushu near the hearth as the investigation unfolds.

She tells her brothers fabricated stories about the trees in the garden to make them laugh and lighten the atmosphere. Redin asks if any lighteyed witness will testify against Lin Davar regarding the crimes committed on the estate. Balat grabs his chair as if to stand but remains seated after seeing the cold rage in his father’s eyes. Jushu curses and turns back to his wine while Wikim continues to stare silently at the fire. Redin expresses his disgust for the family’s cowardice and leaves the manor to return to Vedenar.

Lin Davar screams at his children to leave the room after the highprince’s bastard departs. The gift Lin gave Shallan, a necklace of fat silvery links, sits abandoned in its box on the table. Shallan notes that her father has previously beaten a maid named Isan until she walked with a limp. The brothers receive daggers engraved like Shardblades as gifts to mark the betrothal announcement. Shallan and her brothers flee the room, leaving their father alone with his anger.

### 40: Palona
Shallan rides in a carriage with Highprince Sebarial toward his warcamp on the Shattered Plains. Sebarial explains that he has built an internal economy with textile mills, glassblowers, and farms instead of hunting gemhearts. They arrive at a modest manor house protected by taller tenement buildings that serve as windbreaks. Shallan meets Palona, a Herdazian woman with rock-like fingernails who serves as Sebarial’s mistress. Palona assigns Shallan a suite of white rooms furnished with silk hangings and thick rugs.

The manor features a storm cistern, which is a structure that collects and filters crem from highstorm water to provide indoor plumbing. Sebarial admits he pays fines to Dalinar to avoid sending his soldiers on plateau runs. Shallan informs Palona that she has eighteen soldiers and five slaves who need housing. Palona promises to find a lady’s maid for Shallan and provide food at specific bells during the day. Shallan observes that Sebarial’s camp has more civilians, artisans, and manufactories than typical military warcamps.

She learns that Sebarial grows lavis and tallew grain and maintains hog farms within the camp. Sebarial complains about the social requirements of being a highprince, such as owning three sets of dining furniture. Shallan finds herself blushing when Adolin Kholin is mentioned as her potential husband. She reflects on the luxury of her new surroundings compared to her survival efforts in the Frostlands. Exhausted from her travels and the day’s political maneuvers, Shallan falls asleep on her new bed.

### 41: Scars
Kaladin leads Bridge Seventeen into the chasms for training, noting that the men still handle spears awkwardly. He discusses the Assassin in White with Sigzil, Rock, and Lopen while sucking in Stormlight from spheres. Sigzil questions how a Shin assassin, a member of a xenophobic race, ended up working for the Parshendi. Kaladin successfully Lashes his perspective to the chasm wall and runs horizontally along the stone. He concludes that the sabotage on the king’s balcony railing was a clean cut made by a Shardblade.

Kaladin suspects the railing was cut after the highstorm to prevent the wind from exposing the damage prematurely. Upon returning to the barracks, Kaladin finds Renarin Kholin rocking back and forth near the firepit. Renarin reveals he is epileptic and suffers from jerking fits that cause physical weakness on one side of his body. He asks to join Bridge Four to experience the life of a soldier despite his blood weakness. Kaladin assigns Renarin to wash dirty stew bowls and clean Rock’s cooking cauldron as his first duty.

Moash expresses deep distrust of Renarin, arguing that lighteyes can never be trusted by the darkeyed men. Kaladin observes deep gouges in the chasm walls, indicating a massive chasmfiend recently passed through the area. Moash encourages Kaladin to kill Highlord Amaram in a duel to avenge the deaths of his former squad. The men of Bridge Four leave the barracks to visit taverns, but Kaladin declines to join them. Kaladin sits alone with a bowl of stew, reflecting on his inability to live a normal life anymore.

### 42: Mere Vapors
Shallan wakes in her room at Sebarial’s manor and notices Tyn’s spanreed blinking with a red light. Pattern helps Shallan identify a quintet-based code used by the Ghostbloods to authenticate messages. Shallan uses Lightweaving, the ability to create illusions with Stormlight, to transform herself into a darkeyed woman with tan skin and black hair. She adds a deliberate flaw to her illusion—a small gap on the side of the nose—due to a sketching error. The Ghostblood contact demands an immediate meeting with Tyn to discuss Jasnah’s ward.

Shallan writes back using Tyn’s forged handwriting, claiming she is unwell but will send an apprentice. The messenger provides a map to a meeting location in a tenement within Sebarial’s warcamp. Shallan dresses in Tyn’s clothing, including loose trousers, a white coat, and a single glove on her safehand. She puts on Bluth’s white hat to shade her face and help conceal the imperfections of her Lightweaving. Pattern notes that the Lightweaving feeds on Stormlight and will eventually consume the energy in her spheres.

Shallan realizes she must maintain a stock of infused spheres to keep the illusion from fading. She finds the trousers and glove uncomfortable compared to the traditional Alethi safehand sleeve. The meeting is scheduled for Salas’s moonheight, giving Shallan less than half an hour to arrive. She reflects on Jasnah’s teaching that authority is an illusion, or mere vapors, created by those in power. Shallan climbs out her ground-floor window and heads into the night to meet the secret organization.
