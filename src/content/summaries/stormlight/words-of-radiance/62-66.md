### Chapter 62: The One Who Killed Promises
Kaladin remains in a dungeon cell where guards replace his lamp spheres daily. He performs push-ups to maintain his strength while trapped in the small stone room. Syl explains that she saw a Cryptic, a type of spren appearing like crossing wires, during the recent duel. Dalinar Kholin visits the cell and informs Kaladin that King Elhokar will soon grant a formal pardon. Kaladin voices his distrust of the king, citing the historical imprisonment of silversmiths in Kholinar.

Dalinar admits the rumors are true and identifies the man responsible as a minor lighteyes named Roshone. Kaladin realizes Roshone is the same man whose petty jealousy led to the death of his brother, Tien. Dalinar explains that he allowed Roshone to be exiled rather than executed to avoid alienating powerful political allies. Kaladin argues that mercy from the throne usually only benefits the relatives of lighteyed lords. Dalinar urges Kaladin to distinguish himself as a darkeyed leader to help change the kingdom's structure.

Kaladin concludes that the king’s decision to exile Roshone to his home village caused his family’s suffering. He recalls the poverty his family endured after Roshone arrived to be the new citylord. Kaladin decides that Elhokar is a hindrance to Dalinar and the safety of the realm. He compares the king to a festering limb that must be removed for the health of the body. Kaladin resolves that for the good of the kingdom, King Elhokar must be killed.





### Chapter 63: A Burning World
Shallan hides in a tenement shed to observe a tree where Mraize leaves secret instructions. She uses a spanreed to exchange idle messages with Adolin while she waits for hours. Pattern identifies a masked woman named Iyatil who is spying on Shallan from a window below. Shallan creates an illusory double of herself to fetch the instructions while she sneaks into the hallway. She confronts Iyatil and learns the mission is to investigate a madman Dalinar is hiding.

Shallan and Iyatil travel to Dalinar’s warcamp inside a palanquin carried by parshmen porters. Shallan reveals her true face to the guards to gain quick entry as Adolin's betrothed. She then uses Stormlight to transform into a portly ardent to infiltrate the monastery grounds. Pattern helps her pick the lock to a dark room where the madman is kept. The man, a dark-skinned Makabaki with hulking arms, repeatedly whispers a recitation about the Return.

He identifies himself as Talenel’Elin, the Herald known as Stonesinew. Shallan dismisses the claim as a delusion common among the broken-minded. When Shallan draws Stormlight to renew her disguise, the madman attacks her with incredible speed. He pins her to the wall and calls her one of Ishar’s Knights before retreating to his bed. Highlord Amaram arrives at the door with a servant named Bordin as Shallan hides.





### Chapter 64: Treasures
Shallan uses an illusion of pure darkness to hide in the corner of the madman's cell. Amaram enters with Bordin and addresses the madman as "Great Prince," referencing the late King Gavilar. Bordin reports that the man has spoken of a hidden cache of Shardblades. Amaram identifies these weapons as the Honorblades, ancient swords once wielded by the Heralds. He vows to recover the treasures and put the Blades to use before others can find them.

Amaram and Bordin leave the room to search for the specific location mentioned by the madman. Shallan escapes the monastery and returns to her palanquin to write a full report. She sends the ramblings of the madman and sketches of the men to Mraize via spanreed. Mraize responds by officially welcoming Shallan into the Ghostbloods organization. He informs her that the other members have been ordered not to harm her.

Mraize requires her to get a specific tattoo as a permanent symbol of her loyalty. In the dungeon, Kaladin grows despondent until he draws Stormlight from a wall sphere. He savors the power in his veins to push back the encroaching psychological darkness. Syl watches him and expresses concern that his spirit is growing too dark. Kaladin insists he only needs to escape his cage to be all right.





### Chapter 65: The One Who Deserves It
In a flashback, Shallan reads a book by Jasnah Kholin regarding the infinite variety of womanhood. She hears her father order the assassination of her eldest brother, Helaran. Shallan brings her father spiced wine in the feast hall to calm his cold anger. Her father complains about the disobedience of his children and his wife, Malise. Shallan finds her brother Balat in the garden pulling the claws off live cremlings, which are small crustaceans.

She warns Balat about the assassins and urges him to flee to Helaran for protection. Shallan suggests that Balat take Malise and Eylita with him when Father travels to Vedenar. Balat admits his cowardice and agrees to the plan to contact Helaran’s associates. Shallan returns to the manor and tricks the guards to enter her father's sitting room. She finds Malise in an easy chair with a broken arm and a bleeding lip.

Shallan uses gauze and cloth from the kitchen to treat her stepmother's physical injuries. Malise describes the darkness she sees behind the eyes of Shallan’s father. Shallan explains that Helaran is a Shardbearer and can provide them all with safety. Malise questions why Father only spares Shallan from his physical violence. Malise suggests that Shallan is actually the one who deserves to be hated.





### Chapter 66: Stormblessings
A jailer releases Kaladin from his cell after the king signs a formal pardon. Kaladin discovers Adolin has spent the last several weeks in the adjacent cell by choice. Adolin explains he refused to leave prison while Kaladin remained incarcerated for his actions. The two men shake hands, and Adolin thanks Kaladin for saving Renarin’s life. Bridge Four gathers in the jail's front room to cheer for their captain’s return.

Adolin reveals he won two Shardblades and three sets of Plate from the four-on-two duel. He offers a full set of Shards to Kaladin as a reward for his bravery. Kaladin refuses the gift, stating that the Blades have killed too many people he loved. He gives the Shardblade and Shardplate to Moash, making him a Shardbearer of the fourth dahn. Moash summons the silvery Blade with vine patterns and dons the orange Plate.

Rock leads the men to a celebratory feast featuring a stew he simmered for weeks. Kaladin pulls Moash aside to discuss the secret plan to assassinate King Elhokar. He tells Moash that he now agrees the king must be removed for the good of the realm. Moash promises that after the king is dead, they will target Amaram for revenge. Kaladin rejoins his men in the sunlight, feeling free for the first time in weeks.
