### 107: The First Step
Dalinar wakes from his drinking binge and discusses his dream of Nohadon with the Stormfather. He joins a strategy meeting where Alethi, Veden, Azish, and Thaylen leaders discuss defending Jah Keved. Dalinar realizes the enemy actually intends to strike Thaylen City to control the Southern Depths and its Oathgate. He attempts to apologize to the ardent Kadash for the burning of the Rift but is rebuffed. Taravangian and his conspirators prepare to leak damaging information to break Dalinar's coalition.

Dalinar reflects on his flawed memories and the ritual of shaving to prepare for the day. Taravangian expresses concern about his own deteriorating health while walking with Dalinar to the Map Gallery. During the meeting, Queen Fen criticizes Dalinar for his week-long absence and lack of leadership. Dalinar suggests that an enemy fleet will likely launch from Marat to blockade the coast. The ardent Kadash informs Dalinar that the plants in the tower gardens are failing to grow.

Dalinar releases the ardents from his command to spare them the stigma of his heresy. Taravangian meets with the Radiant Malata, a Dustbringer capable of destroying objects with the Surge of Division. Malata uses her powers to etch black marks into a wooden table while discussing her ashspren’s desire for vengeance. Adrotagia prepares a series of anonymous essays to expose Dalinar’s secrets to the coalition. Taravangian laments the necessity of crushing Dalinar now that the highprince has recovered his resolve.





### 108: Honor’s Path
Shallan, Adolin, and Kaladin travel toward the honorspren fortress on a ship called *Honor's Path*. Captain Notum allows the humans on deck but keeps Syl imprisoned in a cabin. Azure reveals she negotiated for her freedom by offering information on a Shardblade that bleeds black smoke. Adolin admits to Shallan that he murdered Torol Sadeas in the corridors of Urithiru. Kaladin attracts rare windspren—small pinpricks of light—while eight Fused begin chasing the ship.

Shallan sits on the main deck practicing her ability to visualize the souls of various glass beads. Captain Notum explains that honorspren were created by Honor before the Stormfather took over the task. Azure tells Shallan she is hunting a criminal who brought a smoke-bleeding weapon to Roshar. Adolin confesses his crime because he feels like a liar while presenting an honorable face to the world. Shallan accepts the news of Sadeas's death and admits that her own personality is fracturing into multiple personas.

Notum warns Kaladin that humans are too changeable and their bonds with spren are inherently dangerous. Sylphrena speaks to Kaladin’s mind and tells him he is close to swearing the Fourth Ideal. Windspren leave glowing spiral trails through the air, surprising the mistspren sailors who rarely see them in Shadesmar. Mistspren are gaseous beings with porcelain-mask faces who wear vests and trousers that float on their fog bodies. The chapter ends as a scout spots a flight of eight Fused rapidly approaching the ship from the horizon.





### 109: Neshua Kadal
Dalinar and Navani investigate veins of red garnet crystal embedded in the walls of Urithiru. During a highstorm, Dalinar enters a vision to attempt a peace negotiation with the singer Venli. Odium invades the vision, causing the environment to disintegrate into a void of yellow light. Dalinar glows with Stormlight and stabilizes the stone palace, which Venli interprets as the mark of a Radiant Knight. After the vision, Odium claims Dalinar will be the one to eventually destroy Roshar as his champion.

Navani explains that garnets are associated with blood and the tower appears to be a sleeping organism. In the vision, Dalinar takes the place of a guard and approaches Venli to offer terms for a truce. Venli refuses his offer, arguing that humans were the ones who originally enslaved her people. Odium shatters the sky of the vision, pulling debris and souls into a hole of brilliant golden light. Venli saves her spren Timbre, a small pulsing light who communicates via rhythms, as the world collapses around them during the divine intervention.

Dalinar catches Venli’s hand as she falls into a chasm, slowing her descent before the vision terminates. Odium appears on a plain of white stone and mocks Dalinar’s attempts to find answers from the dead. The deity insists that he is the only god who truly cares about human passion and pain. Dalinar returns to the Physical Realm in a state of collapse as the Stormfather weeps in his mind. The Stormfather admits he is terrified of Odium’s strength and barely managed to hold the entity back.





### 110: A Million Stars
Shallan and her companions jump off *Honor's Path* into the sea of glass beads near the coast. Azure remains on the ship to distract the pursuing Fused using Awakened cloth bales. Shallan creates a temporary air pocket within the bead ocean by manifesting the soul of a single bead. The group reaches the shore, which consists of a forest filled with translucent glass plants and moss. Kaladin uses the remaining Stormlight to Lash the group toward the lake representing Thaylen City.

The group carries several large gemstones to provide the Stormlight necessary for their survival and travel. Shallan creates the small room beneath the beads to prevent Adolin and Kaladin from drowning. Pattern and Syl rejoin the group inside the air pocket as they wait for the Fused to pass. The shore of Longbrow's Straits is home to trees with glass trunks and multi-colored glass leaves. Syl glows with a healthy blue-white light as the effects of a highstorm reach them through the canopy.

Shallan struggles with her internal identities as she sketches the strange glass plants in her book. Adolin helps the group navigate the incline of the bead ocean to reach solid ground. Kaladin decides to Lash the group inland to put distance between themselves and the coastline. The deadeye spren of Adolin's Shardblade follows the group without expression or reaction to the environment. They fly toward the island of Thaylenah, which appears as a lake in the Cognitive Realm.





### 111: Eila Stele
Dalinar arrives at the defense of Thaylen City and confronts Meridas Amaram regarding his army's conduct. Jasnah shares a translation of the Eila Stele, an ancient stone record written by the original parshman inhabitants. The text identifies humans as the original Voidbringers who destroyed their own world before invading Roshar. This revelation is leaked to the other monarchs alongside details of Dalinar’s private meetings with Odium. Queen Fen and the Azish viziers confront Dalinar, effectively shattering the unified coalition as an Everstorm approaches.

Amaram argues that his troops are losing morale while being used for construction rather than combat. Bridge Four members joke about stabbing Amaram while they wait for the coalition meeting to begin. Jasnah explains that the Eila Stele was written by Dawnsingers who described humans as betrayal-prone "otherworlders." The record states that humans brought a "new god" to Roshar that sucks in emotion and songs. Dalinar realizes that the Knights Radiant originally disbanded because they learned their ancestors were the invaders.

Taravangian reveals that an anonymous essay has exposed Dalinar’s intent to become the Highking of Roshar. The Azish vizier Noura produces documents detailing Dalinar’s interactions with Odium during his visions. Queen Fen demands an explanation for the "highking business" and the news of humanity’s dark origins. Dalinar admits to meeting the enemy, which causes immediate panic and distrust among his allies. The coalition meeting collapses into accusations just as reports arrive of a faster, more violent Everstorm approaching.
