### 68: Aim for the Sun
Wit leads Shallan to a crem-covered tavern where he is recognized by a disgruntled Alethi innkeeper. Shallan questions Wit's identity, and he denies being a Herald but claims to have lived dozens of lifetimes. Wit explains that his presence is determined by where he is needed, though he often arrives at random locations. Shallan reveals that Torol Sadeas was recently murdered, and Wit responds that he would have applauded the act. The innkeeper serves a meal of skrip cremlings, which are small crustaceans with flat tails and two large claws.

Wit explains that the Cult of Moments has seized control of the Kholinar Oathgate platform. He identifies the "Heart of the Revel," one of the Unmade, as the entity driving the cult's celebrations. The common cult members roam the streets moaning like spren, while elite members party on the platform. Wit warns Shallan that hedonism in the cult is not enjoyment but the carnal indulgence of beautiful things. He advises Shallan to bring fine food to the cult's leaders to secure an invitation to their revel.

Shallan realizes she needs to gather a team and specific resources to acquire the necessary food. Wit refuses to join her mission, stating that Shallan is not the reason he is currently in the city. He instructs Shallan to tell the innkeeper that he disappeared in a puff of smoke to annoy the man. Shallan notices that Wit has stolen several spheres from her pouch before he slips out of the tavern. She returns to the tailor's shop to plan how her team will obtain the food for the infiltration.





### 69: Free Meal, No Strings
Kaladin infuses a stone with Stormlight in a public plaza to attract the attention of the screaming spren. Two yellow-white spheres of energy appear and screech, followed by a flying Voidbringer in red and black clothing. The Voidbringer carries a long, slender spear designed for aerial combat and a triangular shield. After the creature departs, the group returns to the tailor's shop where Adolin tries on a new high-fashion suit. Shallan uses Lightweaving to disguise Elhokar as General Khal and Adolin as Captainlord Meleran Khal.

Adolin and Elhokar plan to infiltrate lighteyed parties to find allies among the city’s noble families. Kaladin refuses a full facial disguise but allows Shallan to hide his slave brands with an illusion. The group travels through the stressed city streets toward a modest four-story mansion for a party. Adolin and Elhokar present their invitations while Drehy and Skar head to the darkeyed guard tent. Kaladin walks toward the city wall to survey the guard posts and the Wall Guard's numbers.

A squad of five soldiers from the Wall Guard approaches Kaladin near the foot of the wall. The soldiers notice Kaladin's slave brands because Shallan's illusion has unexpectedly worn off. Lieutenant Noro mistakes Kaladin for a deserter and offers him a free meal at their barracks. Kaladin agrees to follow them to gather information about their leader, Highmarshal Azure. He carries Skar’s spear as he is led away to the Wall Guard's home within the city fortifications.





### 70: Highmarshal Azure
Kaladin enters the Wall Guard barracks, noting the scents of leather, stew, and weapon oil. He eats a bowl of stew while observing armorers repairing gear and an ardent scribing glyphwards. A soldier named Beard claims to have performed secret missions for Amaram and shares a Horneater flatbread recipe. Kaladin realizes that every soldier in the room is a lighteyes, despite the lack of typical noble posturing. Highmarshal Azure arrives at the barracks wearing an azure cloak and carrying a silver basket-hilted sword.

Azure is revealed to be a woman with short hair and orange eyes, which is unusual for Alethi military ranks. The soldiers maintain a "secret" regarding her gender and treat her with immense respect and loyalty. Azure recognizes Kaladin's shash brand and correctly guesses he was in the northern recruitment armies. She takes Kaladin to the top of the sixty-foot-tall city walls to survey the enemy camp. In the distance, the Voidbringer army maintains a glowing encampment that advances after every storm.

Azure describes the Wall Guard as a place for redemption where past crimes do not matter. She explains that the monsters outside will enslave or kill the entire population if the wall falls. Kaladin searches for his former parshman companions, Sah and Khen, among the lights of the enemy camp. Azure invites Kaladin to return the next day to join the guard rather than pressing him into service. Kaladin returns to the group and informs Elhokar that he may have found another Radiant.





### 71: A Sign of Humanity
Dalinar marches his army toward the Rift to suppress the rebellion led by Brightlord Tanalan eleven years ago. Evi travels in a carriage, burning incense and weeping over the impending violence of the assault. Dalinar enters the carriage to speak with her, noting the scent of crem dust and plush cushions. Evi begs Dalinar to hold back the "monster" within him and show mercy to the rebels. Dalinar dons his Shardplate and rides alone to the gates of Rathalas to confront the enemy leaders.

Young Brightlord Tanalan, whose father Dalinar previously killed, emerges from the city on a white horse. Dalinar proposes a duel to settle the conflict, but Tanalan refuses, knowing he cannot win. Tanalan claims that a traitor among Dalinar's highprinces has been supplying the rebels with weapons. He identifies Torol Sadeas as the man whose caravan just left the city heading east. Dalinar and Tanalan agree to a ruse where the rebellion is framed as a trap for the traitors.

Tanalan agrees to open the gates and stand down his soldiers in exchange for safety and legitimacy. Dalinar orders Teleb to occupy the fortifications while keeping Alethi officers out of the city itself. He gathers a company of one hundred elite soldiers to pursue the suspicious caravan. Teleb reports that scouts saw someone in Shardplate among the supply caravan's guards. Evi expresses relief at the lack of bloodshed, and Dalinar sends a warning message to Gavilar.





### 72: Rockfall
Veil, Vathah, and Red infiltrate the Rockfall mansion to steal food for their cult infiltration mission. The mansion contains waterfalls that were Soulcast into columns of crystal, dark wood, and marble. Veil uses Lightweaving to impersonate Brightness Nananav, a woman with a tall bun of hair. She tricks a master-servant into opening the locked larder by threatening to cut off his legs. Vathah and Veil fill a dumbwaiter with dried sausages, sacks of grain, and tubers called longroots.

The team hides the stolen food inside rolled-up carpets in the loading bay of the mansion. The real Nananav discovers the theft and alerts the guards as the team attempts to flee. Shallan creates illusions of blood pools and tentacles while wearing Nananav's face to terrify the guards. A guard fires a crossbow bolt that strikes Shallan in the temple, but she remains conscious. Vathah opens the gates, allowing the chull-drawn wagon to escape into the city streets.

Vathah pulls the crossbow bolt from Shallan's head, and her Stormlight heals the wound immediately. Veil decides to distribute the stolen food to refugees to gain the attention of the Cult of Moments. She visits a grimy urchin named Grund to gather a list of deserving people who are starving. The team delivers sacks of food to a seamstress named Muri and a group of children in a park. By evening, the wagon is empty, and Veil hopes her public display of charity will draw out the cult.
