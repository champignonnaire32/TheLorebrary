### Chapter 112: For the Living
Kaladin remembers his past lover Tarah, a darkeyed quartermaster's daughter who favored Thaylen-style dresses with apron fronts. They sat together in the cold evening while she inspected the balance of his long spear. Tarah informed him that she was transferring to a scribe's position in Mourn’s Vault to care for her aging father. Kaladin refused to leave his military post because he was consumed by the memory of his brother's death and a white and black stone. Tarah kissed him goodbye and urged him to learn how to care for the living instead of the dead.

In Shadesmar, Kaladin leads Adolin and Shallan through a forest of crimson trunks and crystal limbs. Shallan struggles with exhaustion as several exhaustionspren, which look like large chickens, circle above her. They reach the top of a hill and see Thaylen City as a sea of thousands of candle flames. Two enormous spren stand as sentries over the Oathgate platform, which manifests as a stone platform with a white bridge. A massive army of enemy spren guards the bridge over the ocean of glass beads.

Kaladin worries that the group is moving too slowly as they conserve their remaining Stormlight. Shallan collapses to the ground to rest after the difficult climb through the obsidian landscape. The group observes that the sentry spren do not appear to be corrupted like the ones in Kholinar. Kaladin vows not to fail his companions as he did during the siege of the Alethi capital. They remain hidden while surveying the thousands of hostile entities blocking their path to the portal.



### Chapter 113: The Thing Men do Best
Dalinar stands in his villa and contemplates the fact that humans are the original Voidbringers. The other monarchs refuse to trust him after learning that humans destroyed their previous world with Surges. The Stormfather explains that Shinovar was a preserve for humans and their alien plants like chickens and horses. He reveals that the ancient Knights Radiant abandoned their oaths to prevent the destruction of Roshar. Dalinar insists that he will not allow his new Radiants to repeat the failure of their predecessors.

Bridge Four soldiers decide to return to Urithiru because they feel conflicted about fighting the displaced singers. The Azish emperor and Vizier Noura depart via ships, calling Dalinar's visions and powers dangerous. Taravangian apologizes to Dalinar but states he can no longer fight beside the Alethi coalition. Queen Fen remains Dalinar's only ally because she needs his infantry to defend the city walls. Dalinar admits to the Stormfather that he cannot find a way to argue against the bridgemen's logic.

The Stormfather reminds Dalinar that he is a monster who killed his own wife. Dalinar winces at the verbal blow but finds no physical enemy to strike in his rooms. Highprinces and scribes offer various excuses to leave as the coalition officially dissolves. Dalinar watches the temple go quiet as the last of the world's leaders abandon him. He retreats to his villa to wait for the arrival of the enemy army and the Everstorm.



### Interludes: Venli • Rysn • Teft
Venli lives in a cave in Marat and preaches about the rhythms of Passion to former slaves. A Fused named Rine orders her to join a fleet of hundreds of ships sailing for a grand assault. Red lightning flashes on the western horizon as Odium’s storm arrives to guide the vessels. Rysn works as a clerk in the Gemstone Reserve while managing her paralyzed legs. Her former babsk Vstim visits her and gives her the Wandersail, a three-masted frigate equipped with fabrial stabilizers.

During a vault audit, a guard disguised as a queen's soldier kills Tlik and stabs Vstim. The traitor attempts to steal the King’s Drop, an enormous ruby that never loses its Stormlight. Rysn’s larkin, Chiri-Chiri, a winged beast with an axehound snout, flies out and drains the violet light from the thief's body. Rysn uses her captain's cord to cock a crossbow and shoots the thief in the chin. The thief is revealed to be a Voidbringer who can no longer heal without his stolen energy.

Teft finds the Bridge Four barracks in Urithiru covered in blood and discovers Rock and Bisig are wounded. Eth is dead and the Honorblade has been stolen by an attacker wearing a Bridge Four coat. Teft realizes the thief used the officer's coat he sold to fund his addiction. He flees in shame into the dark hallways and encounters a glowing woman made of light and air. Voidbringers begin flooding through the Kholinar Oathgate and attacking the soldiers in the tower.



### Chapter 114: The Cost
Five years ago, Dalinar travels to the Valley to seek a boon from the Nightwatcher. He enters a dense forest where the ground is covered in wrinkles and the trees are bent leeward. Inside the jungle, Dalinar hears the screams of children and sees visions of himself burning the Rift. He drops his Shardblade and asks the Nightwatcher for forgiveness for his past atrocities. The Nightwatcher appears as a green mist with too-long arms and offers him wealth or a dark Blade.

A woman named Cultivation, who has skin like darkwood bark and wears a brown dress, appears and stops the Nightwatcher. She explains that she will prune Dalinar’s memories to allow him to grow into a weapon. Cultivation warns that the cost will be high and that his memories will eventually return. She sends vines into Dalinar’s pores and veins to begin the excision process. Dalinar wakes up the next morning outside the forest with no memory of his wife.

He attempts to recall his wife's face but finds only a blur of sounds and a lack of images. Dalinar decides to stop drinking and orders his men to find a copy of The Way of Kings. He feels a sense of peace despite the loss of his memories and the continuing guilt over his brother. His soldiers prepare the camp for departure as Dalinar requests battle maps for the coming war. He straightens his uniform and vows to follow the Codes and never fail a king again.



### Chapter 115: The Wrong Passion
Dalinar watches the Everstorm stop over the bay as enemy ships ram onto the shore. Fused launch into the air while Amaram’s troops deploy onto the battlefield between the city and the bay. Dalinar asks the Stormfather about the most important step a man can take before leaving his villa. In Shadesmar, Kaladin and his group watch the enemy spren army vanish from the glass bead ocean. A red mist surges across the obsidian ground and enters the physical world to affect the battle.

Jasnah finds Renarin in a temple and sees a red, snowflake-shaped spren on his back. She summons Ivory as a Shardblade to execute her cousin for his apparent corruption. Venli watches as thirty-foot-tall stone monsters called thunderclasts, with rock skeletons and wedge heads, pull themselves out of the ground. Odium appears in a marbled gold and white form to personally supervise the assault on the city. He releases the Thrill, a roiling mass of red light, into the human ranks of Amaram’s army.

The dark spren and red mist bond with ten thousand Alethi soldiers in green uniforms. The soldiers' eyes glow red as they turn their weapons against the defenders of Thaylen City. Odium tells his Fused that he has prepared this human army for decades to manifest their anger. Teft watches from a balcony as Voidbringers flood into Urithiru through the supposedly locked Oathgate. Navani and Queen Fen watch from the city wall as the battlefield erupts into a slaughter.
