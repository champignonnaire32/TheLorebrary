### 15: Brightness Radiant
Shallan and Adolin discuss the murders of Highprince Sadeas and Brightlord Perel. Both victims were found in the same area of Urithiru and killed using the same method. Adolin suggests that two different people might be responsible for the crimes. Shallan notes that nothing connects the men except their rank and the location of their bodies. She considers that the second murder might be a decoy to mask a specific target.

Adolin changes the subject and encourages Shallan to practice using her Shardblade. He believes that since she and Jasnah are Radiants, they must ignore traditional gender roles regarding weapons. Shallan experiences intense anxiety because holding the sword reminds her of killing her parents. To cope with the trauma, she draws a new persona named Brightness Radiant. This version of herself is modeled after Jasnah and possesses extreme poise and self-confidence.

Shallan uses Lightweaving to transform into Radiant and summons Pattern as a thin Shardblade. Pattern modulates his shape to dull his edge for a safe sparring session. Adolin spends an hour teaching Radiant basic sword stances, focuses on footing, and center of balance. He explains that a duelist must respect their weapon as if it were a part of their own life. Shallan switches back and forth between her personas to manage the emotional pain of the training.





### 16: Wrapped Three Times
Dalinar examines the Honorblade used by the Assassin in White on a stone slab. The Stormfather explains that Honorblades are forged from Honor's soul and do not scream because they are not dead spren corpses. Dalinar decides to hide the weapon in a stone sewage tube within a vacant section of the tower. He believes the Blade is too dangerous to keep in his personal quarters without a vault. The Stormfather warns Dalinar that he only sees what is touched by highstorms and is not a true god.

Dalinar goes to the training grounds where he meets Sergeant Rial, a Bridge Thirteen soldier with a Koron accent. He spars with Aratin Khal in a wrestling match and loses to the younger, more practiced man. Navani interrupts the session to inform Dalinar that Queen Fen of Iri is ready to speak via spanreed. Fen refuses to join Dalinar’s coalition and accuses him of stealing Shardplate belonging to the kingdom of Rira. She suggests that the Iriali Triumvirate might side with the Voidbringers because their terms are gracious.

Dalinar debates religious tradition with the ardent Kadash, who was once one of Dalinar's elite soldiers. Kadash demands that Dalinar recant his claim that the Almighty is dead to maintain Vorin support. Dalinar refuses to lie and uses Stormlight to bind Kadash to the stone floor during a sparring match. This display of Radiance is intended to show the soldiers and ardents that the Knights Radiant have truly returned. When Navani mentions the name of Dalinar's late wife, Evi, he suddenly remembers her face for the first time.





### 17: Trapped in Shadows
Kaladin remains bound to a tree as a prisoner of a group of parshman refugees. The group includes parshman children who possess orange-pink carapace (organic armor) on their heads and forearms. Kaladin observes the parshmen discussing whether to kill him to prevent him from revealing their location. He realizes they are not soldiers but runaway slaves who are moving under the cover of night. The parshmen use simple Alethi names and speak with familiar local accents.

Sah, the parshman captor, tells Kaladin about the pain of his family being torn apart by human slave traders. He describes living "in a fog" for years until the Everstorm restored his mind and identity. Syl reveals that a voidspren—a higher spren serving Odium—is actively guiding the parshman group. She explains that the Everstorm healed the parshmen's souls by bridging the gaps in their Connection. Syl remains invisible to the voidspren by masquerading as a common windspren during the march.

Kaladin cuts his ropes using Syl in knife form but chooses not to escape or attack the group. He notices their grain supplies are rotting and offers to help them survive their journey. Kaladin demonstrates how to use the dry inner layers of rockbud shells as fuel for a fire. He instructs the parshmen on how to boil their grain into cakes and hide their smoke in the trees. The parshmen return his knife and allow him to assist with their camp chores.





### 18: Double Vision
Shallan transforms into Veil, a persona with black hair and an angular face, wearing trousers and a long white coat. She enters the Breakaway, a chaotic and unregulated market that has formed within a large cavern in Urithiru. Veil visits a low-end tavern and drinks harsh Veden wine to prove her toughness to the patrons. A bouncer named Jor flirts with her and eventually mentions a recent murder in a nearby area called All’s Alley. He describes a man named Ned who was executed for killing his wife.

Veil moves to a disreputable bar called All’s Alley and drinks Horneater white, a potent and dangerous spirit. She approaches a table of thugs led by a woman named Betha to ask about the murders. To gain their respect, Veil stabs her own hand through the hand of a large Horneater named Ur. She then scratches the Ghostblood symbol—three interlocking diamonds—into the tabletop to intimidate the group. The thugs reveal that they threw a man named Ned off the plateau for murdering a barmaid.

Betha explains that Ned's wife was strangled, and the barmaid was found the next night killed in the exact same manner. Ned claimed he was innocent of the second murder, despite the identical marks left on the victims. Shallan realizes that this pattern of double murders matches the deaths of Sadeas and Perel. The Stormlight in her system automatically heals the knife wound in her hand and cures her drunkenness. She leaves the tavern with a jug of Horneater white, contemplating the existence of a serial killer.





### 19: The Subtle art of Diplomacy
Thirty-one years ago, Dalinar attends a feast at a highstorm waystop during a military campaign. He is joined by his brother Gavilar, Highprince Sadeas, and a Westerner named Toh who is seeking asylum. Toh’s sister, Evi, is also present and brings with her a set of Shardplate as a political offering. Dalinar feels restless and bored by the polite atmosphere and the lack of women in the main hall. He walks out into the raging highstorm to find a missing knife, shocking the foreigners with his disregard for the storm's danger.

Dalinar returns to the feast hall dripping with rainwater and attempts to eat his pork with a tiny dining knife. Navani Kholin, his brother’s wife, enters the room wearing rubies and a stunning red-lipped smile. Dalinar struggles with his intense attraction to her while trying to maintain his military composure. Gavilar discusses the importance of legacy and the failure of the Sunmaker to maintain a lasting empire. Sadeas and his wife Ialai discuss the possibility of Dalinar marrying Evi to secure the Shardplate for House Kholin.

An assassin disguised as a servant attacks Gavilar with a knife while Dalinar is distracted by his steak. Dalinar reacts instantly by kicking Gavilar's chair over and slamming the killer to the stone floor. He kills the assassin with the man's own weapon and then uses it to finish eating his dinner. Gavilar thanks his brother for the rescue and notes that Dalinar's brutality is often a persuasive political tool. Dalinar agrees to marry Evi to fulfill his duty and finally end his pursuit of Navani.
