### Chapter 73: Telling which Stories
Kaladin patrols Kholinar’s wall with Lieutenant Noro’s squad of low-ranking lighteyed tenners. The guardsmen mock a disguised Adolin Kholin for his flamboyant yellow suit without realizing his identity. Kaladin and the squad escort a grain wagon through a crowd of hostile refugees to Velalant’s distribution troops. Members of the Cult of Moments appear and draw corrupted painspren, which look like skinless hands of an unnatural color, by whipping their forearms. Kaladin maintains his lighteyed disguise by summoning Syl to change his eye color several times a day.

A Fused, which is a powerful flying Voidbringer, triggers a strike alarm and Kaladin briefly attempts to take command before deferring to Noro. The Fused test the wall's defenses but retreat before engaging Kaladin’s specific platoon. Noro praises Kaladin’s enthusiasm and suggests he will soon be promoted to a squadleader position. Kaladin observes the palace remains dominated by gloom and is avoided by the regular Wall Guard. The squad carries pikes and rectangular shields to form a defensive block against potential aerial assaults.

Kaladin discusses Azure’s Shardblade with Beard, noting it lacks a gemstone on the pommel like an Honorblade. Beard reveals the Wall Guard seized a Soulcaster, a device used to transform matter into food, from a local monastery. Syl questions Kaladin about the existence of God after Beard expresses his own lack of faith. The soldiers express distrust of the highlords while remaining focused on defending the city wall. Kaladin realizes the Wall Guard’s survival depends on maintaining order within the starving, rioting city.



### Chapter 74: Swiftspren
Shallan visits the Kholinar market as Veil to provide food packages to Grund the urchin. She learns the beggars have named her "The Swiftspren" because of her white coat and elusive nature. Corrupted exhaustionspren, which appear as red whirlwinds, spin around her as she moves through the suffering crowds. Shallan encounters the Cult of Moments and uses Lightweaving to manifest as a glowing arrowhead-shaped spren. She shouts at the cultists to abandon their costumes and return to their families, causing them to flee.

Inside the tailor shop, Shallan finds Elhokar drinking wine and reviewing lists of potential lighteyed allies. The king expresses his desire to be a hero and his grief over his son being locked in the palace. Shallan sketches a portrait of Elhokar that depicts him as a noble and resilient leader rather than a failure. Elhokar becomes emotional upon seeing the drawing and asks to keep the lacquered page. Shallan realizes her different personas are beginning to bleed into one another as she interacts with her companions.

Wit leads a group of refugees in boisterous songs beneath the azure light of the moon Nomon. Shallan observes the laughter of the people and feels like a creature merely wearing human skin. She finds a note from the Cult of Moments inviting her to a revel on the Oathgate platform during the next Everstorm. Ishnah and Vathah continue to use illusions to distribute food and build Veil's reputation in the streets. Radiant reminds Shallan of her obligations to Adolin and her distaste for her own deceptive behavior.



### Chapter 75: Only Red
Flashback to eleven years ago shows Dalinar leading a company of elite runners toward the Rift. He suspects Highprince Sadeas has betrayed Gavilar and intends to find the truth personally. Tanalan’s Rifters spring a trap by triggering a massive landslide that buries Dalinar beneath tons of rock. Dalinar survives the impact because of his Shardplate but loses his helmet and suffers a head wound. The Thrill, a supernatural battle lust that grants energy and focus in combat, ignites within him.

Dalinar summons his Shardblade, Oathbringer, and slaughters the Rifters who come to scavenge his armor. He loses himself to a red haze of violence and kills the enemy soldiers with his bare hands after his Blade sticks in a rock. The gemstone on the pommel of Oathbringer cracks, preventing Dalinar from dismissing the weapon as he walks back to camp. He discards pieces of his broken Shardplate along the way to reduce his weight while losing a dangerous amount of blood. Dalinar reaches the Kholin camp at night and walks straight into the command tent while trailing blood.

Dalinar informs Sadeas and his generals that the scouts were traitors working for the Rift’s highlord. He orders the army to Soulcast massive quantities of oil instead of food to prepare for a total assault. His wife Evi pleads with him to show mercy and reconsider his plan to destroy the city. Dalinar ignores her and vows to make Rathalas an example by burning it so thoroughly that it will be haunted for ten generations. He commands the archers to shoot any envoys who attempt to leave the city under a flag of truce.



### Chapter 76: An Animal
Dalinar launches a night assault on the Rift’s walls while his armorers replace the cracked gemstone on his Blade. Sadeas and Teleb join the fight to rout the defenders while the army prepares barrels of oil and pitch. The Kholin soldiers drop the flammable liquids into the city and ignite them with flaming brands. Dalinar leads a squad of elites to a hidden metal door and uses Oathbringer to cut the hinges. He rolls burning barrels of oil into the tunnel to kill anyone seeking shelter in the underground safehouse.

Tanalan is captured near a white palace and begs Dalinar to save his family from the spreading fires. Dalinar declares himself an animal that cannot be whistled back once it has gone feral. The palace collapses into the burning chasm, killing Tanalan’s wife and children before Dalinar can intervene. Tanalan laughs hysterically and reveals that Evi had entered the city to plead for peace on Dalinar’s behalf. Dalinar realizes he has burned his own wife alive in the safehouse he targeted with oil and fire.

The Kholin army watches in silence as the entire city of Rathalas is consumed by a firestorm. Dalinar strangles Tanalan to death before collapsing from the emotional and physical intensity of his realization. He recovers Evi’s scorched body and orders the scribes to lie about the circumstances of her death. The official narrative states that Evi was killed by an assassin, providing the soldiers with a justification for the city's destruction. Dalinar begins to hear the constant, phantom screams of the innocent people he murdered in the Rift.



### Chapter 77: Stormshelter
Kaladin, Shallan, and Adolin take shelter in a high-end winehouse during the arrival of an Everstorm. Kaladin reports that Highmarshal Azure’s Shardblade lacks a gemstone and might be an Honorblade. Shallan presents research from Hessi’s Mythica identifying Sja-anat and Ashertmarn as the Unmade, ancient and primal spren entities, that are corrupting Kholinar. Ashertmarn, known as the Heart of the Revel, is believed to be inciting the hedonistic behavior at the palace. Adolin examines Kaladin’s side sword and notes it is a well-maintained infantry weapon.

Shallan and Adolin walk through the city after the storm passes to observe the damage caused by red lightning. Adolin praises Shallan’s sketches of refugees and encourages her by suggesting the Radiants were artists as well as soldiers. Shallan feels conflicted about her identities and wonders if she can craft a perfect version of herself for Adolin. She kisses Adolin on the cheek before departing to prepare for her meeting with the Cult of Moments. Veil takes control of Shallan's body and retrieves her trousers and hat from a hidden drop point.

Vathah and Veil meet at an inn to review maps of a manor called the Mausoleum. Veil plans to steal food from the manor’s larder to pay her way into the cult’s revel. Vathah accidentally manifests a Lightweaving illusion of a man from Shallan’s sketchbook while in a state of panic. Veil realizes Vathah has become a squire, a trainee of a Radiant order, and instructs him to begin practicing with Stormlight. The team prepares to execute the heist while the Cult of Moments waits for Veil at the Oathgate platform.
