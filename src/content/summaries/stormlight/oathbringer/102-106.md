### 102: Celebrant
Kaladin, Adolin, and Syl explore the spren city of Celebrant to find passage to Dalinar. They observe diverse spren populations, including Cryptics, Reachers, and ash-colored spren that frequently regrow their skin. Adolin buys clothing and harpoons while Syl explains that spren reproduction involves power coalescing rather than physical birth. Shallan and Azure learn from a local registrar that Voidspren have seized the perpendicularity and are demanding tribute. A Fused inspector named Kyril arrives at the docks and burns Ico’s ship after finding evidence of human stowaways.

The registrar identifies Shallan as a Radiant and urges the group to board a ship before the Fused returns. Kaladin observes a haunting painting of a figure with nine shadows before Adolin warns him of the approaching danger. Syl admits she has a bounty on her head and is considered a "spren lighteyes" by her honorspren siblings. The Fused searches the market for the humans, forcing the group to hide in an alley near the warehouses. Azure notes that ships are fleeing the city to avoid the enemy holdings nearby.

Syl discards her disguise and runs toward a majestic white wood vessel belonging to the honorspren. She shouts to the crew, identifying herself as the Ancient Daughter to lure them into capturing her. The honorspren lower a gangway, allowing Kaladin and the others to board the ship under their protection. Crew members made of fog and mist bind Syl’s arms and confiscate the humans’ harpoons and infused gemstones. The ship sails away from Celebrant as the Fused watches from the docks beside the burning wreckage of Ico’s vessel.



### 103: Hypocrite
Dalinar wakes up on a cut stone floor and enters a vision where he meets an older Nohadon. Nohadon is busy writing a shopping list for ingredients to bake fluffy Shin loaf bread. The king invites Dalinar to accompany him to a vibrant market filled with people wearing colorful, ancient clothing. Nohadon explains that his path to writing The Way of Kings began after a senseless war in the west. Dalinar complains about the burdens of political unity and expresses a desire to force his allies into compliance.

A massive shadow passes over the city, and the ground shakes with the impact of unseen, heavy footfalls. Nohadon negotiates for golden grain while Dalinar warns him of the impending danger from the shadow. The king argues that principles are defined by what a person gives up rather than what they gain. Dalinar hears the screams of the dead from his past and confesses his feelings of hypocrisy. A stone thunderclast with red glowing eyes appears and reaches down to touch Nohadon’s hand.

Nohadon explains that all things exist in three realms: the Physical, the Cognitive, and the Spiritual. Hundreds of stone monsters emerge from the city rooftops and surround Dalinar as he begins to panic. The king asks Dalinar what the most important step a man can take before the vision collapses. Dalinar awakens in his bed in Urithiru huddled in his clothing with a bottle of wine nearby. He experiences a crisp recollection of the night of Gavilar’s funeral six years prior.



### 104: Strength
Navani Kholin leads a meeting with Highprinces Aladar, Sebarial, Hatham, and Brightness Bethab to address Urithiru’s logistics. The highprinces report that the tower’s plumbing is clogged and the Soulcasters are exhausted from processing waste. Sebarial mentions a lack of parshmen labor for cutting lumber and requests a favorable supply deal from Azir. Navani defends Dalinar’s public mourning and insists he will eventually push through his pain to lead the coalition. She promises to fix the tower’s ancient gardens and discover the secrets of the fabrials that once provided water.

Navani notes that the highking proclamation has been delayed until Elhokar and Adolin return from Kholinar. She leaves the meeting and heads to the library where Jasnah has organized a massive translation project. Scholars listen to and record information from gemstones while others attempt to crack the ancient Dawnchant script. Jasnah reports that the gemstones mostly contain personal histories rather than technical details about the tower’s fabrials. Renarin works at a side table, searching for patterns in the gemstone drawers and joking with Bridge Four veterans.

Navani notices that Renarin appears genuinely happy while interacting with the soldiers instead of remaining distant. Jasnah expresses concern about the history of human behavior being the best indicator for future actions. Navani discovers records describing how Renarin scrawled numbers on palace walls before the Everstorm arrived. Another report mentions a Radiant named Lift who was found in Yeddaw and is now visiting Azimir. Navani collects translated gemstone records and leaves the library to check on Dalinar’s condition.



### 105: Spirit, Mind, and Body
Six years ago, Dalinar stands at the front of a crowd in the Kholinar catacombs for Gavilar’s interment. The ardent Jevena preaches that the king’s war has moved to the Tranquiline Halls to serve the Almighty. Dalinar clenches his fists to hide his trembling and struggles with the urge to drink during the ceremony. A Soulcaster with granite-colored skin touches Gavilar’s forehead and instantly transforms the corpse into a stone statue. Ardents move the statue into an alcove where it joins the line of previous Kholin monarchs.

Elhokar stands before his father’s statue and demands that the highprinces swear a Vengeance Pact against the Parshendi. Torol Sadeas is the first to publicly swear his support, followed by the other gathered lighteyes. Dalinar flees the catacombs and returns to his chambers, feeling overwhelmed by the memory of his crimes at the Rift. He finds Jasnah in the royal reading room where she is crying over a book associated with the Lost Radiants. Jasnah identifies the text as the source of Gavilar’s final words and begins to read it aloud to Dalinar.

Dalinar listens for eight hours as Jasnah reads the stories of a king on a pilgrimage. The vignettes describe the importance of choosing a path and taking a journey rather than focusing on the destination. Inspired by the book, Dalinar decides to go on a pilgrimage to visit the Old Magic. He reconciles with Adolin and explains his pride in the boy’s mastery of the sword and earnest character. Dalinar plans an advance force to the Shattered Plains via Dumadari and the Ocean of Origins to secure the region.



### 106: Law is Light
Szeth flies with the Herald Nin and several Skybreakers toward the southern kingdom of Marat. They stop at hidden stockpiles in mountain peaks to recover gemstones that have been charged by highstorms. Nin takes Szeth to a ruined courthouse in a small coastal town that was recently captured by the singers. The Herald points to blood on the floor and discarded civilian possessions inside the cavernous main room. Nin argues that the law exists to prevent human sentimentality and chaos from affecting justice.

Szeth observes ships gathered in a nearby bay while Nin reveals he visited the Herald Ishar for guidance. Nin admits he failed to prevent the Desolation despite thousands of years of working to stop it. He summons a glistening Shardblade that is one of the two missing Honorblades originally held by the Heralds. Nin instructs Szeth to choose a Third Ideal, which for most Skybreakers involves swearing to follow local laws. The sword at Szeth’s back suggests fighting Nin, claiming the Herald might be evil despite his divine status.

Nin notes that the singers allowed the local people to return to the town to burn their dead. Szeth questions Nin for pursuing Surgebinders while ignoring other lawbreakers to achieve his own ends. Nin explains that he obtained legal permission for his executions but admits he is not passionless. The Herald summons a second Shardblade and reveals he is a Skybreaker of the Fifth Ideal. Nin begins to explain the decision the Heralds made at Aharietiam to sacrifice one of their own to end the cycle.
