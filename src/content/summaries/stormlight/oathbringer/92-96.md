### 92: Water Warm as Blood
Szeth arrives in a Purelake town to undergo a Skybreaker test alongside other hopefuls and squires. Master Ki explains that a group of dangerous murderers has escaped from a nearby cliffside prison. The local minister, Kwati, has granted the Skybreakers legal authority to execute these convicts on sight. Szeth questions why the men weren't executed previously and learns the Reshi people hold nonviolent ideals. He steps onto the warm, shallow water of the Purelake to begin his search for the fugitives.

Szeth encounters a scrawny convict hiding in a twisted tree and engages him in a struggle. He paralyzes the man with a strike from his sheathed sword, Nightblood, which is a powerful weapon that creates dark smoke. After questioning the man, Szeth discovers the prisoners were starving and neglected by their guards. He visits the dark, foul-smelling prison and finds a single dead guard in the guard post. Szeth concludes that the prison administrator was corrupt and had misappropriated funds meant for security.

Returning to the town, Szeth confronts the beefy nobleman who managed the prison and accuses him of mistreating the inmates. He draws Nightblood, which consumes Stormlight from his spheres and causes intense physical pain to his arm. The sword releases liquid darkness that turns the nobleman’s body into black smoke, consuming his very soul. Szeth sheathes the weapon, leaving his hand bleached grey-white from the effort. Master Ki accepts Szeth as her squire after he swears the Second Ideal to seek justice.



### 93: Kata
Adolin wakes on the glass-bead ocean of Shadesmar, the Cognitive Realm, feeling insignificant compared to the world’s new monsters and Radiants. He performs a morning kata to calm himself, and he is soon joined by Azure and Kaladin. Shallan inventories their meager supplies, noting they have one bag of gemstones and only one day of water. Azure explains that she is hunting a criminal and previously entered Roshar through Cultivation’s Perpendicularity, a portal in the Horneater Peaks. She clarifies that her Shardblade is a flawed copy and she is not a Herald or a Knight Radiant.

Azure suggests they travel to a port city to hire a ship sailed by lightspren, who are traveling traders. She notes that humans living in the Cognitive Realm require supplies that can be traded for Stormlight. Shallan explains that Soulcasting here would only change an object's soul without providing physical sustenance. Pattern, a Cryptic spren made of shifting geometric patterns, warns that the Oathgate spren have been corrupted. The group realizes they are hundreds of miles from the Horneater Peaks and must find an alternative escape.

Syl, a glowing windspren in a rippling dress, describes the three realms as Physical, Cognitive, and Spiritual. She explains that the Spiritual Realm is a place where all time and space are unified as one. Azure warns that they must move quickly to avoid attracting dangerous "people spren" who inhabit this realm. Suddenly, a haunting screech like grinding steel echoes across the glass landscape. The group hurriedly packs their remaining items as the intensity of the sounds increases.



### 94: A Small Bottle
Seven years ago, Dalinar destroys his room in a drunken rage while searching for hidden bottles of wine. He hears the constant weeping of children and the voice of his late wife, Evi, begging for her life. After finding a small flask in his trunk, he consumes the dregs and struggles with his self-loathing. He recalls a recent hunt where he found parshmen in the wilderness while pretending to be a sober commander. Dalinar realizes he is only a few days’ march from the ruins of the city of Rathalas.

Adolin and Renarin enter the room after hearing the commotion of their father overturning his furniture. Dalinar roars at his sons to get out, fueled by angerspren and a belief that Evi turned them against him. Adolin stands his ground at seventeen years old, but the younger Renarin cringes in fear. After they flee, Dalinar falls to his knees in despair, overwhelmed by the volume of the internal screaming. He pounds his fists on his bedside table and topples his sphere lamp in a fit of agony.

Renarin returns to the room alone and offers Dalinar a small bottle of wine purchased with his own spheres. The boy hugs his father tightly and refuses to let go despite Dalinar's initial flinch. Renarin tells his father that Evi described him as the most honorable soldier and the greatest man in Alethkar. Dalinar weeps as he realizes his sons do not hate him despite his self-destructive behavior and failures. He clings to Renarin like the boy is the only real thing left in a world of shadows.



### 95: Inescapable Void
The group marches south through Shadesmar along a narrow peninsula of obsidian that represents a river in the Physical Realm. Kaladin struggles with a simmering dissatisfaction and a fear of the internal darkness that leads to his depression. Pattern warns that human emotions are inconvenient in this realm because they attract hostile spren. Azure explains that the screeches come from angerspren, which appear as pools of boiling blood in the Physical Realm. Syl notes that the angerspren are dangerous even to other spren and likely drawn toward the chaos in Kholinar.

Kaladin feels a sense of failure for abandoning the people of Kholinar to the invading Voidbringers. He worries that his internal numbness will eventually claim him and make him betray his oaths again. The group passes small, brittle plants that resemble ferns and occasionally display vibrant, stained-glass colors. The sun remains fixed in the sky, and the spaces between clouds reveal an eternal, endless blackness. They camp for a "night" and continue their trek, with Adolin and Kaladin rotating through the rearguard position.

Syl recounts her past, explaining that she was born in a grand city of honorspren far to the west. She describes her first bond with an elderly man who died in battle a thousand years ago. This loss caused her to lose her mind and sleep for centuries until the Stormfather woke her. Syl reveals she snuck away from her home to join the Physical Realm by hiding among mindless windspren. She hops with excitement and points toward a bright yellow light marking a lighthouse located at the end of the long obsidian peninsula.



### 96: Pieces of a Fabrial
Navani requires every monarch at the Urithiru meeting to carry their own chair as a symbol of shared wisdom. She uses a Soulcast wooden chair while Dalinar selects a sturdy, unpadded seat of stumpweight wood. The Azish Prime arrives with an ornate throne and a large contingent of advisors from Tashikk, Emul, and Yezier. Ialai Sadeas ignores the chair tradition, having a guardsman place a sleek, dark maroon chair for her. Navani opens the conference by stating that no such gathering has occurred since the ancient Recreance.

The monarchs immediately argue over tariffs, the use of Oathgates, and the reclaiming of lost nations like Emul. Navani manages the chaos by assigning the Azish bureaucracy the task of creating unified legal codes for the coalition. She grants Queen Fen of Thaylenah the authority to administrate trade and supplies through the magical portals. Jasnah explains that the ancients did not have superior technology but rather the ability to bond spren. Navani reveals the failure of the Kholinar expedition and confirms that the Alethi capital has fallen to the enemy.

The coalition agrees to send five battalions to Jah Keved to support King Taravangian against a potential invasion. Dalinar vows that the new Knights Radiant will not abandon the world as their predecessors once did. Ialai Sadeas suggests invading Shinovar for its resources, but Jasnah insists on entrenching their current defensive positions. Navani observes that the monarchs are like pieces of a fabrial that she must now assemble into a functioning machine. After the meeting, she suggests that Dalinar tour Vedenar to gain a new perspective and escape his haunting memories.
