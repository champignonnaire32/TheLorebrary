### 44: The Bright Side
Veil sits in a tavern tent in Urithiru with Red, Gaz, and Vathah to train them in espionage under the darkeyed spy Ishnah. Ishnah instructs the men on the importance of being unmemorable and observing details like clothing and occupants without looking. Veil impresses Ishnah by perfectly recounting the number of customers and their specific physical traits, though she initially misses one hidden woman. Ishnah identifies the hidden woman as a rival spy gathering information on troop morale for one of the highprinces. Vathah shows surprising enthusiasm for the memory drills while Red and Gaz struggle with the mental exhaustion of the training.

Shallan returns to her persona as a scholar and attends a meeting in the Urithiru library with Navani, Jasnah, and various scribes. The scholars discuss their inability to activate the tower’s gemstone pillar and the function of copper metalworks embedded in the walls. Renarin Kholin enters the meeting and sits with the scribes while constantly pulling a metal chain through his fingers. A scribe named Janala mocks Renarin’s presence at the meeting by asking if he is secretly reading women's script. Shallan publicly insults Janala's intelligence in retaliation, which causes Jasnah to dismiss the meeting for a short break.

During the break, Renarin thanks Shallan for defending his honor and explains his theory that Urithiru is not a collection of fabrials but one singular, massive fabrial. Shallan realizes the Unmade they fought previously was likely controlling the tower's functions through the central gemstone pillar. Renarin advises Shallan that the best way to avoid Jasnah’s strict orders is to be absent when she is looking for someone to command. Shallan decides to use the upcoming mission to Kholinar as an escape from her apprenticeship under Jasnah. Dalinar Kholin joins the meeting to provide social cover for Renarin so the prince does not feel out of place in a feminine setting.

### 45: A Revelation
Fused warriors drop Moash outside the city of Revolar in central Alethkar and force him into a group of human refugees. Moash observes that the Voidbringers have taken control of the city and exiled the human population to the surrounding outskirts. Parshmen who speak perfect Alethi herd the refugees into large stone storm bunkers and inform them they are now slaves. Moash explores the bunker and finds that the Voidbringers are organizing work crews for farming, cooking, and army support. He encounters a lighteyed man with green eyes who has volunteered to perform common labor such as carrying water.

Moash searches for an old acquaintance named Guff, a wheelwright he knew from his days working caravan runs. Guff leads Moash into a hidden tent area where several lighteyes, including the regent Paladar, are hiding with extra food and supplies. Paladar demands information about when the highprinces will arrive to rescue him and restore his rank in the city. Moash realizes the lighteyes are still hoarding resources and abusing their authority despite being conquered by the Voidbringers. He concludes that both Alethi social classes are fundamentally broken and that mankind has squandered its previous victory over the Void.

Paladar orders his guards to throw Moash out of the tent after deciding he is mentally unstable for remaining silent. The guards seize Guff to beat him for bringing a deserter into the presence of the regent. Moash walks past starving women and children to reach the work station handled by the parshmen overseers. He volunteers for the most difficult available task, which involves pulling heavy supply wagons across the landscape. This work places him directly within the support structure of the Voidbringer army as it prepares to march.

### 46: When the Dream Dies
Skar runs up the ramps of Urithiru in full marching gear but fails to breathe Stormlight despite his physical exertion. Sigzil uses a small mechanical clock to time the runs of Bridge Four members while recording the results in his notebook. Drehy completes the run in under six minutes by using a quarter-Lashing—a surge of Gravitation—to reduce his physical weight by half. Lopen demonstrates his abilities by walking on the ceiling of the Oathgate control building while the others wait for Kaladin. Teft, who shows physical signs of moss addiction, encourages Skar to eat chouta while they discuss Skar's failure to glow.

Kaladin arrives at the control building with thirty new recruits, including lighteyed officers and scouts from other bridge crews. He uses his Shardblade as a key to activate the Oathgate mechanism, which transports the entire stone platform to the city of Narak. The team passes through Narak, a former Parshendi stronghold built from crem and greatshell carapace, which now serves as a human waystop. Skar notices Rlain, the only parshman in Bridge Four, carrying a spear and wonders if the former slave preferred art over war. Kaladin leads the group to a training plateau on the Shattered Plains to drill the recruits in spear formations.

Skar observes Lyn, a female scout, sitting on a rock in frustration because she cannot breathe Stormlight. He tells Lyn about the first time he saw Kaladin use a Lashing to swerve Parshendi arrows away from the bridge crew. Skar encourages Lyn to keep trying and hands her an emerald to practice the breathing technique. Lyn successfully inhales the Stormlight, turning the gemstone dun and causing her body to glow with white light. Skar realizes he is also glowing when he notices Stormlight rising from his hands as he holds a cup of Horneater tea.

### 47: So much is Lost
The narrative provides the repetitive speech of Talenel’Elin, the Herald of War, who warns of the approaching Desolation. Taln offers to teach the people how to cast bronze and Soulcast metal blocks because stone tools will not stop the Voidbringers. He mentions that Jezrien will teach leadership and Vedel will train surgeons to prepare humanity for the coming conflict. Jasnah Kholin reads transcripts of these ravings in her secure room at the center of Urithiru. She confirms that the madman who arrived in Kholinar is indeed a Herald based on the specific historical details in his speech.

Jasnah works with Ivory, her inkspren, who appears as a jet-black figure with prismatic oil-like swirls on his skin. She uses three expensive spanreeds to recover her research notes from a scribe in Tashikk after losing her trunks in a shipwreck. Ivory suggests searching the soul-realm of Shadesmar to find the Heralds because their souls shine brightly on the other side. Jasnah reflects on a childhood illness that caused her to scream in a dark room and left her with a fear of losing her sanity. She views her logic as a defense mechanism and worries that the Heralds have suffered the slow destruction of their minds.

Ivory expresses his distrust of Cryptics—a race of spren that feed on lies—describing them as creatures that cluster around those who speak untruths. He notes that Cryptics possess only one city in Shadesmar and generally prefer listening to ruling or warring. Jasnah reviews reports from her informants regarding Shallan’s activities in the warcamps during Jasnah's recent absence. She decides that Shallan needs more challenges rather than more structure to help her gain control over her impulsive nature. Jasnah prepares to bind her research into a book, intending to eventually reveal a secret she learned from the King's Wit.

### 48: Rhythm of Work
Moash pulls a sledge on steel skids across the fertile plains of central Alethkar as part of the Voidbringer army. The Voidbringers provide the human slaves with sturdy boots, gloves, and adequate rations to ensure they can perform the labor. Moash observes thousands of parshmen in warform—a physical transformation providing natural carapace armor—training with axes and spears. Fused warriors zip through the air or walk beside the supply carts while surrounded by visible dark energy. Moash concludes that the fall of Kholinar is deserved because humankind has proven incapable of governing itself over the centuries.

He notices that the Voidbringer army is highly efficient and that the parshmen overseers are trained by unseen spren. One group of parshmen in workform is treated with extreme cruelty and forced to pull a sledge apart from the humans. The overseers frequently whip and beat these specific parshman slaves, leaving them bloodied and lagging behind the main column. Moash’s own overseer, a parshwoman in a havah—a formal Alethi dress—with her safehand covered, explains that these slaves harbored a false god. The army marches through the heat of approaching summer, passing villages that have been burned to prevent flanking maneuvers.

Moash’s sledge catches up to the mistreated parshman crew just as one of the slaves collapses from exhaustion. Two overseers begin whipping the fallen slave, whose feet are bleeding from the long march over uneven ground. Moash drops his rope and intervenes by shoving the overseers away from the fallen parshman. He catches a whip in mid-air and punches one of the overseers in the face, knocking the creature to the ground. Moash helps the injured slave into the sledge and takes his place in the pulling line for the remainder of the march.
