### Chapter 83: Crimson to Break
Adolin prepares for the assault on the palace and reconciles with his Shardblade. Elhokar leads a force of six hundred men from the Wall Guard and loyalist highlords. A Thunderclast, a giant stone monster with glowing red eyes, pulls itself onto the city walls. Kaladin flies into the air to deflect a massive boulder thrown by the creature. The army reaches the palace grounds and begins cutting through the main doors.

Adolin and Elhokar use their Shardblades to breach multiple entrances into the building. The soldiers engage in a brutal melee against the muted Palace Guard defenders. Adolin discovers a group of loyal guardsmen who were imprisoned for refusing the queen’s orders. He recruits these men to serve as reserves during the continued assault. Kaladin and his bridgemen clear the eastern gallery of enemies to secure a path forward.

Elhokar decides to lead a detachment up the stairs to find his wife and son. Adolin, Shallan, and Highmarshal Azure advance toward the Sunwalk and the Oathgate platform. They find the control building covered in a pulsating black mass called the Unmade Ashertmarn. Shallan prepares to use her powers to drive the darkness away from the device. Adolin surveys the city and sees parshman armies flooding through the broken gates.



### Chapter 84: The One you can Save
Kaladin and Elhokar reach the royal chambers and find Queen Aesudan combing her hair. The queen reveals she has bonded with the ancient spren Yelig-nar to gain power. Kaladin discovers the heir Gavinor being tormented by small red-glowing spren behind a screen. He kills one of the spren with a dagger formed from his Sylblade. Elhokar picks up his son while the queen begins to manifest black smoke and red eyes.

At the control building, Shallan touches the dark heart and experiences a flood of conflicting emotions. A voice similar to Wit’s encourages her to remain strong amid the mental assault. The Unmade Ashertmarn flees the building, leaving behind a trail of dark sludge. Shallan enters the structure and sees a shadow with white eyes named Sja-anat in a mirror. This Unmade warns Shallan that the Oathgate has been corrupted and is now a trap.

A massive battle breaks out in the palace between parshmen, Palace Guards, and Azure’s men. Kaladin freezes when he recognizes his former parshman friends Sah and Khen among the attackers. Elhokar is wounded and attempts to speak the First Ideal of the Knights Radiant. Moash emerges from the fighting and stabs Elhokar through the chest and eye. After the king dies, Moash gives the Bridge Four salute and retreats with the parshman army.



### Chapter 85: Grieve Later
Adolin drags a catatonic Kaladin away from the king’s corpse as the palace falls. Skar and Drehy guard the retreat of the surviving Wall Guard onto the Sunwalk. The group reaches the Oathgate platform while Fused and parshman armies seize the rest of Kholinar. Adolin realizes the city is lost and orders an immediate retreat to Urithiru. He instructs the soldiers to gather as many civilians as possible onto the platform.

Inside the control building, Shallan informs the group that the device has been tampered with. She explains that Sja-anat, the Taker of Secrets, warned her of a disaster if activated. Adolin insists they must take the risk because the alternative is certain death or capture. Highmarshal Azure hands over a pack of emeralds to provide the necessary Stormlight. Shallan summons Pattern as a Shardblade and prepares to engage the mechanism.

The figure in the mirror promises to try and keep the humans alive during the transition. Shallan inserts her Blade into the lock and turns it to trigger the transport. A violent tremor shakes the entire platform as the process begins. Adolin watches the city disappear as the Oathgate activates with a blinding flash. Sja-anat’s final words echo in Shallan’s mind as the world dissolves around them.



### Chapter 86: That others may Stand
Dalinar Kholin stands on his balcony at Urithiru while struggling with restored memories of his past. He recalls the screaming of children and the fire he set at the city of the Rift. Navani attempts to comfort him, but he remains standing at stiff military attention. He questions the Stormfather about why a man with his history was chosen for a bond. The Stormfather admits he is only now beginning to understand the depth of human pain.

Dalinar enters his meeting room to wait for news alongside Navani, Taravangian, and various highprinces. He discusses the nature of leadership and moral burdens with the elderly King Taravangian. Taravangian argues that leaders must stain their own souls so that their people may remain clean. This utilitarian philosophy provides Dalinar with little comfort as the hours pass without word. The armies of the coalition wait on the Oathgate platforms for a signal to invade.

A spanreed finally begins to write a message from the palace ardent in Kholinar. The message states that the capital has fallen and the Voidbringer forces have seized the palace. It reports that the mission to secure the Oathgate has failed and the Wall Guard is captured. The communication cuts off abruptly after mentioning there is no word on the king or Radiants. Dalinar sinks into his chair in despair as the realization of the defeat takes hold.



### Chapter 87: This Place
The activation of the Oathgate causes a massive ripping sound and a jarring impact. Adolin tumbles off a stone platform and falls into a sea of small glass beads. Azure pulls him back onto the circular platform before he can sink or suffocate. The group finds themselves under a pitch-black sky filled with clouds that look like roads. A small, distant sun hangs in the air above the endless ocean of beads.

Kaladin and Shallan stand up and realize they have been transported to the Cognitive Realm, also known as Shadesmar. Two enormous sentinels, thirty feet tall and colored black and red, hover in the sky nearby. A Cryptic spren with a head made of shifting geometric patterns stands on the platform. A blue-white cultivationspren and an ashen brown reacher spren also observe the group. These entities represent the true forms of spren within this strange dimension.

Azure expresses her deep distaste for the location and identifies the surroundings. Only the people who were inside the control building have been successfully transported. The rest of the soldiers and civilians on the platform outside are missing. The group stands alone on a small island in a vast, alien landscape. Part Three of the narrative concludes with the team stranded in the realm of thought.
