### Chapter 5: Hearthstone
Kaladin walks through the rockbud fields toward his home town of Hearthstone. He notices the debris from the recently passed Everstorm scattered across the landscape. Syl, an honorspren who appears as a ribbon of light or a small woman, flies alongside him. Kaladin reflects on his journey of over a thousand miles from the Shattered Plains. He observes a broken roof and a churn near the river called Hobble’s Brook.

The narrator describes gloomspren as long, grey streamers of cloth that whip about the air. Kaladin reaches the outskirts of the town and sees that many structures are roofless or destroyed. He decides to approach the citylord's manor instead of his parents' home first. At the manor, he finds boards nailed over the windows to replace glass broken by the storm. A human guard with a rusted helmet and a mace detains him at the servants' entrance.

The guard forces Kaladin inside the building while demanding to know if he is a deserter. Kaladin sees lights flickering inside and hears sounds of many people moving about. He notices that the manor porch was ripped away by the storm winds. He enters the hallway and prepares to summon his Shardblade if necessary. The guard bullies him further into the manor to present him to Brightlord Roshone.



### Chapter 6: Four Lifetimes
Kaladin walks through the manor and recognizes familiar townspeople who have taken shelter inside. He passes the kitchens where darkeyes sit in family groups on the floor. The guard shoves his mace into Kaladin's back and demands an explanation for his presence. Kaladin uses a sergeant’s trick to glare at the guard and make him hesitate. He continues into the parlor which has been converted into a medical triage room.

He finds his father, Lirin, a balding surgeon with spectacles, kneeling on the floor to splint a broken arm. Kaladin speaks to his father, and Lirin recognizes him despite his ragged uniform and beard. His mother, Hesina, enters the room with a tray of bandages and drops them in shock. Kaladin sits on the floor and consumes a bowl of warm vegetable soup. He confesses to his parents that he failed to protect his brother Tien in the army.

Lirin tells the guards that he will buy Kaladin's writ of slavery to prevent his arrest. The guard captain identifies Kaladin as a runaway slave because of the shash brand on his forehead. Kaladin observes that the patients in the room have fractures from the storm rather than battle wounds. He realizes the townspeople do not recognize him as the boy who left five years ago. Lirin and Hesina discuss writing a letter to the highprince to explain Kaladin’s return.



### Chapter 7: A Watcher at the Rim
Brightlord Roshone enters the room and mocks Kaladin for the brands on his forehead. Kaladin responds by punching Roshone in the face and knocking him to the floor. When the guards level their swords at him, Kaladin summons his Shardblade from mist. The Blade appears as a bright, dew-covered weapon that causes his eyes to turn light blue. He stabs the weapon into the floor to demonstrate his status and authority to the room.

Kaladin learns that the parshmen transformed into Voidbringers with bony protrusions and increased height. The guards explain that the creatures broke out of their quarters but fled into the night without attacking. Laral, now Roshone's wife, appears with a firm face and utilitarian clothing. She provides a spanreed, a communication device using paired rubies, to contact the Alethi court. Kaladin meets his infant brother, Oroden, and holds the child while weeping.

He trades his gemstones for infused spheres to obtain the Stormlight he needs for his powers. Kaladin informs the gathered crowd that the Desolation has begun and the Voidbringers have returned. He inhales Stormlight until luminescent smoke rises from his skin and he begins to glow. He Lashes himself to hover above the floor and manifests Syl as a Shardspear. After telling his parents he will return to take them to safety, he flies out into the rain.



### Chapter 8: A Powerful Lie
Shallan sits on an Oathgate platform outside Urithiru and sketches the massive tower. She speaks with Pattern, a Cryptic spren that appears as a moving sequence of geometric lines. Shallan feels the cold and uses Stormlight from her spheres to stay warm while she draws. She struggles to capture the immense scale of the tower and its numerous balconies on her page. A scout named Lyn arrives to summon Shallan to a meeting with Highprince Dalinar.

Shallan walks through the tower and notices the walls are made of solid rock without seams. She enters a meeting room where Dalinar, Navani, Adolin, and Renarin are discussing diplomacy. Dalinar describes the enemy's champion as a figure in black armor who possesses nine shadows. He expresses his frustration with being unable to see the entire world he must unite. Shallan approaches Dalinar and feels a pulling sensation as she draws in Stormlight.

The Stormlight pours from Shallan and creates a massive, three-dimensional map of Roshar in the air. This map shows topographical details like mountains, plains, and cities with incredible accuracy. Dalinar identifies the locations of the ten Oathgate cities, including Azimir, Kholinar, and Vedenar. He explains that they must use the Oathgates to move armies and supplies between these bastions. The scout returns to the room and reports that another person has been murdered in the tower.



### Chapter 9: The Threads of a Screw
Shallan and the Kholin family follow the guards to the scene of the murder. They travel through a hallway where the stone strata spiral around the corridor like the threads of a screw. The body is found at the bottom of a bathing chamber that features horse-head sculptures on the walls. The victim is Vedekar Perel, an officer belonging to Highprince Sebarial’s military force. He has been stabbed through the eye, mirroring the death of Highprince Sadeas.

Navani descends into the pit and observes that the positioning of the corpse is identical to the previous murder. Dalinar states that they are facing a killer who is slinking through the tower tunnels. Adolin appears visibly shaken and mutters that the situation is impossible. Renarin stands on the walkway above the pool and watches his brother with unblinking blue eyes. Dalinar explains that he lacks the necessary investigators since many of his men died in battle.

Dalinar appoints Adolin to lead the investigation into both the Sadeas and Perel murders. He believes putting his son in charge will show the other highprinces that he takes the crimes seriously. Adolin accepts the task but remains distracted and avoids meeting the eyes of his family. Shallan questions Adolin about whether he knew the dead man or the killer. Adolin claims he does not know who committed the crimes but vows to find out.
