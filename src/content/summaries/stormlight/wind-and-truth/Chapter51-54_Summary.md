### Chapter 51: TEST

Young Szeth and his family arrive at a military training camp situated on a ridge near the Stoneward monastery. His sister Elid displays resentment toward their new life while their mother gives Szeth a small wool toy for comfort. They meet Betheth, a burly recruitment captain with dark hair, who notes that the parents are volunteers. Szeth hears a mysterious voice in his head for the first time as he steps onto the hard stone. The voice remains silent after its initial greeting, leaving the boy feeling unnerved and apprehensive about his future.

Szeth surrenders his colorful handkerchief and puts on a dark brown military uniform to begin his training. He stands in a line with older youths while his father, Neturo, argues with Betheth about the use of fear for discipline. Betheth explains that the recruits are considered deviants and problems that require harsh correction to become useful soldiers. Workers push out racks holding dead sheep carcasses to serve as targets for the boys' initial assessment. The other youths attack the carcasses with uncontrolled rage and aggression to release their pent-up frustrations.

The mysterious voice identifies itself as a stone spren and instructs Szeth to show restraint during the test. Following the spren's guidance, Szeth makes a single surgical strike with his spear at the neck of his carcass. He explains to Betheth that a single well-placed stab is sufficient to stop an enemy in a real confrontation. Szeth repeats lines provided by the voice to justify his previous killing of a soldier as a necessary act. Impressed by the boy's apparent discipline and control, Betheth decides to take Szeth to the camp's general for officer training.

### Chapter 52: A PERFECT MOMENT

Adolin Kholin assists Emperor Yanagawn in donning Shardplate for a training session inside a large, cluttered tent in Azimir. The emperor changes into a thick gambeson and padded hose while trusted guards observe the intimate process of his dressing. Adolin interacts with a friendly guard named Gezamal, who discusses various regional board games like Flat Face and Deliverer. Yanagawn experiences the immense power of the Plate for the first time and accidentally stumbles onto his face. The monarch quickly recovers and expresses his delight at the unnatural strength provided by the ancient suit of armor.

Yanagawn practices walking and eventually shatters several training dummies into splinters with his armored fists. Adolin teaches the emperor about the importance of delicate control by throwing wooden eggs for him to catch. The emperor initially crushes several eggs before learning to regulate the force of his grip while wearing the Plate. They transition to playing the card game Flat Face to further hone Yanagawn's tactical instincts and manual dexterity. Adolin explains the strategic value of keeping reserves and knowing when to retreat from a losing skirmish.

Yanagawn asks Adolin to call him by his personal name rather than his imperial title to foster a genuine friendship. Adolin promises to sneak the emperor out for a night of relaxation in Urithiru once the current crisis has passed. The two men discuss the nature of oaths and why Adolin has chosen not to become a Radiant. Their session is interrupted by the arrival of the enemy army, which begins a major assault on the city. Adolin prepares for battle as his armorers finish suiting him up in his own set of Shardplate.

### Chapter 53: MAKARI SIN

Szeth and Kaladin rest near a river while Szeth contemplates the mysterious voice he heard during his childhood. Without warning, Szeth is pulled into the Cognitive Realm of Shadesmar, which he recognizes as the glasslands of Makari Sin. He uses Stormlight to create a small raft from beads to prevent himself from drowning in the endless sea. Pozen-son-Nash, an Elsecaller Honorbearer carrying a hooked Blade, appears on a separate platform to challenge Szeth to a test. A Shin woman bearing a narrow, six-foot-long Edgedancer Honorblade initiates an attack against the weaponless Szeth with her sword.

Szeth’s highspren appears in the beads as a figure of blackness and stars but is initially useless and frightened. Szeth swims through the beads and uses his spren to manifest a wooden stirring spoon as a meager tool. He heals from several Blade wounds using his remaining Stormlight and eventually disarms the Edgedancer in a close struggle. Nightblood manifests in this realm as a brilliant sword of golden light because of its own radiant self-perception. Szeth uses the glowing weapon to strike the Edgedancer's Honorblade, releasing a blast of force that knocks her unconscious.

Pozen refuses to help Szeth and instead commits suicide by falling on his own Honorblade, dissolving into dark mist. Szeth recovers the Willshaper Honorblade and realizes he is trapped in the middle of a vast, airless ocean of beads. He uses the power of the recovered Blade to slice a hole through reality and return to the Physical Realm. Szeth tumbles out onto the grass with two additional Honorblades as Kaladin approaches him in the darkness. The highspren claims his previous uselessness was a deliberate attempt to ensure Szeth did not rely too heavily on him.

### Chapter 54: A FRIEND

Shallan, Renarin, and Rlain explore a woodblock-print-style vision of the Shattered Plains created by their respective spren. Shallan discusses her mental struggles with Pattern and resolves to accept her pain without letting it define her personality. The group discovers the malevolent face of the Unmade Ba-Ado-Mishram manifesting in the clouds and the patterns of the stone. They retreat into a hollow stone building to hide from the entity's gaze and discuss the history of the location. Renarin and Rlain bond over their shared experiences of childhood isolation while they wait for the next vision to trigger.

The group decides to continue their mission to stop the Ghostblood assassins who are infiltrating the vision sequences. They realize that Mishram is watching them because their search for her prison is drawing her malevolent attention. Shallan prepares her warped bronze dagger that distorts the air while Renarin and Rlain plan to find weapons. In Azimir, Adolin walks through the plaza after a long battle and reflects on his past failures in Kholinar. He encounters the head vizier Noura, who demands that he stop influencing Emperor Yanagawn with his Alethi customs.

Adolin argues that the young emperor needs a true friend rather than just isolated guidance from political minders. He compares the weight of leadership to overloading a horse and warns that constant isolation will eventually break the monarch. Noura admits that Yanagawn was originally chosen as a disposable puppet but claims she would now die for him. Adolin asserts his own lack of political ambition as proof that he is a safe and necessary companion for the emperor. The two reach a stalemate as the fourth day of the transition comes to an end in the besieged city.
