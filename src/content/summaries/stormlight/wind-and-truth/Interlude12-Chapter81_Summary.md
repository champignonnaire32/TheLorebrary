### Interlude 12: WHAT MUST BE DONE

Odium discovers Cultivation is moving against him in Kharbranth. Cultivation's forces, equipped with advanced gear and strange armor, infiltrate the city in black boats. Taravangian forms an invisible avatar to watch as his city guards are systematically slaughtered. Cultivation appears beside him, threatening his family who have been captured in the palace. She demands an immediate armistice and peace with the human coalition.

Taravangian realizes Cultivation chose him to hold Odium's power to control him. He argues that her Intent loves growth through conflict, which war provides. Cultivation attempts to force peace to avoid admitting she made a mistake in her choice. Taravangian refuses to back down, stating his path is now clear and requires only strength. A massive wave begins to build in the ocean several miles from the city.

Taravangian identifies specific friends and family members like his daughter Savrahalidem and his friend Maben. Cultivation offers to back down and begs him to stay his hand to save her followers. Taravangian declares that a god must have no weaknesses and refuses her offer. He steeels himself and calls upon his divine power to command the sea. Odium destroys Kharbranth entirely with a hundred-foot wave, killing everyone he once bargained to protect.

### Chapter 78: A TRUE RADIANT

Sigzil plans a diversion to Narak Three to prevent the enemy from overwhelming Narak Prime. He convinces his generals that the enemy will prioritize the supply depot over the Oathgate. Sigzil argues that capturing the Oathgate would only isolate the humans and increase their desperation. He proposes a feint to make the enemy believe their Stormlight cache is stored at Narak Three. A Lightweaver named Stargyle is tasked with creating an illusion of gemstones to bait the trap.

Sigzil and his honorspren Vienta discuss the critically low supply of Stormlight within the camp. He finds Leyten on the wall walk and they discuss their shared history as bridgemen. Leyten expresses doubt about his worthiness as a Radiant after losing two squires in battle. Sigzil uses a calculated gambit to shift the blame to Kaladin’s absence to bolster Leyten's confidence. Venli leads a group of listeners and Fused through the chasms following a strange tone.

The group encounters an old bridge wedged in the chasm walls from the human bridge runs. Thundercloud the chasmfiend picks up Venli to help her inspect the wooden structure. Jasnah confirms via spanreed that the enemy fleet at Thaylen City consists of decoys filled with stone. Queen Fen expresses fear that the empty holds are a ruse to leave the city undefended. Jasnah decides to act on the discovered facts and orders her Radiants to reinforce the Shattered Plains.

### Chapter 79: THE RHYTHM OF LONGING

Shallan experiences a vision of her past as a child hiding from her shouting parents. She sees her mother muttering about not being a soldier and wanting a simple life. Shallan hides under the steps with her brothers Balat, Jushu, and Wikim. She tells them a story about an adventurous axehound to provide joy amidst their fear. Her first Cryptic, Testament, is drawn to the "wonderful lie" of her feigned happiness.

Renarin experiences a vision of a feast in his youth where the Kholins practiced refinement. He encounters a group of older boys in a hallway who are passing around firemoss. The boys surround and taunt him, testing whether Dalinar is ashamed of his youngest son. Renarin uses a hand-to-hand move taught by Zahel to sweep the lead boy's legs. Adolin arrives and threatens the boys with a duel, scaring them into retreating.

Rlain experiences a vision of a strategy meeting with Eshonai and other listeners in old Narak. They discuss sending a spy in dullform to infiltrate the human warcamps. Thude and others volunteer but are rejected because they are deemed too essential for battle. Rlain volunteers and realizes that his friends treat him as invisible and easily replaceable. He accepts the mission to become a spy, feeling a deep longing for genuine connection with his peers.

### Chapter 80: SEEING THE FUTURE

Adolin inspects the city of Azimir from a tower and uses a new fabrial device called binoculars. He describes the binoculars as two joined spyglasses that magnify images and provide depth perception. He observes four Heavenly Ones in the air and orders his scouts to be put on alert. Navani and Dalinar experience a vision of Urithiru during its ancient abandonment by the Radiants. They follow a Windrunner named Garith through a crowd of people wheeling belongings to the Oathgates.

Navani, appearing as Melishi, stands between a group of angry Skybreakers and Garith. The Skybreakers accuse the other Radiants of lying about humankind's arrival on Roshar. They refuse to stay in the tower, claiming the protections are vanishing and the Bondsmith is untrustworthy. The Skybreakers fly away, leaving Garith to plan a retreat to a camp near Cabridar. Garith expresses disappointment in Melishi before soaring away, leaving Navani with many unanswered questions.

Adolin and Commandant Kushkam survey the battle-scarred Oathgate platform at the center of Azimir. Kushkam identifies a corridor where debris has been cleared, signaling a massive impending assault. Adolin promises Kushkam that help is coming based on a telepathic message from his spren Maya. The alarm sounds as a massive thunderclast, a stone behemoth thirty feet tall with glowing red eyes, rises from the ground. Commandant Kushkam prepares his personal guard to face Deepest Ones, Fused who can phase through stone, rising from the plaza.

### Chapter 81: THE SCHOLAR WITH A SPEAR

Sigzil and thirty Windrunners clash with Skybreakers and Focused Ones, Fused with springlike musculature used for launching boulders, in the Everstorm. He uses a stolen enemy lance to drain Stormlight from a Skybreaker, causing them to fall. Sigzil's feint at Narak Three succeeds as the enemy army pivots toward the illusory gemstone cache. Leyten sends a coded spanreed message confirming that he has located the traitor Moash. Sigzil dives toward the north side of the battlefield where Moash has been spotted.

Adolin prepares an aluminum chain, a lightweight metal restraint that can hinder stone monsters, to use against the thunderclast. Gallant the Ryshadium helps pull the heavy wagon carrying the chain while Adolin's soldiers provide support. Adolin and an Azish Shardbearer named Neziham plan to trip the thirty-foot-tall stone beast. They aim to strike the creature's neck with Shardblades to sever its connection to the Physical Realm. Adolin worries about his inability to reach Maya as she is busy with a task he requested.

Sigzil finds Moash using a specialized fabrial that negates Radiant powers within a limited radius to kill one of Leyten's squires. Moash is revealed to have glowing diamonds instead of eyes and juts of crystal growing from his skull like a crown. He uses an anti-Stormlight knife, a blade that warps the air and can permanently kill spren, to murder Ethenia. Moash performs the Bridge Four salute before flying away as Sigzil's reinforcements arrive. Leyten dies in Sigzil's arms while speaking a Death Rattle that prophesies Sigzil's own future death.
