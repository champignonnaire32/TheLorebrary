### Chapter 138

Shallan kills Mraize with an anti-Light knife by using an illusion to distract his attention. Radiant merges with Shallan, stating that she is no longer needed to carry the weight of violence. Shallan admits she is not fully healed but is better than she was before the fight. Mraize’s Cryptic spren, a bone-white creature with geometric patterns, is wounded by the anti-Light and loses an arm. Shallan heads toward the room where Renarin and Rlain prepare to release the ancient spren Ba-Ado-Mishram.

Nale is overwhelmed by the guilt of his murders after Ishar strips away his mental protections. Syl is paralyzed by a deep nothingness as Ishar forces his supernatural pain onto her essence. Szeth curls up in despair, haunted by the whispers of those he has killed throughout his life. Kaladin grapples with the darkness but chooses to look up and reflect on his past versions. He realizes he admires his former selves because they all shared a willingness to protect others.

Kaladin decides he wants to be a man who protects even when a crisis is not demanded. He recognizes the darkness contains the crushing burdens of the nine remaining Heralds of Roshar. He resolves to maintain personal boundaries and refuses to take Szeth’s failings upon himself anymore. Kaladin finds a healthy way to handle his internal darkness and takes a deep, steady breath. He stands up on the barren field of rock despite the weight of Ishar’s power.

### Chapter 139

Kaladin stands up to protect Szeth, Syl, and Ishar, choosing his identity as a protector. Ishar is shocked that a mortal can resist the distilled darkness he emanates from his soul. Kaladin explains that while he feels Ishar’s pain, he knows that Ishar will be warm again. He acknowledges that the Herald’s pain is the same as his own, just significantly more concentrated. Kaladin admits he does not know what a therapist is but claims the title regardless.

Ishar threatens to crush Kaladin and kill everyone he loves for his continued insolence. Kaladin speaks the Fifth Ideal, stating he will protect himself so that he may protect others. Dalinar accepts the viewpoint of the power of Honor and feels lines of light tethering him. The power of Honor demands a vessel that keeps its word rather than one that grows. Dalinar realizes the power is immature and newly aware but speaks the words of understanding.

Dalinar slams his hands together to open a perpendicularity as he speaks to the power. He realizes the destination is about Connection and who a person has truly become. The power of Honor gathers around him like a corona, making Odium’s rumbling sound tiny. Renarin and Rlain hold the gemstone prison of Ba-Ado-Mishram high above the rocky ground. Together, they slam the gemstone down, shattering it completely and releasing a dark, swirling storm.

### Chapter 140

The Wind accepts Kaladin’s Words, causing him to explode with power as he achieves the Fifth Ideal. Power surges through the Bondsmith tether to Ishar, throwing the Herald back and flushing out smoke. This act lifts an oppressive dark cloud from the eight other Heralds across the entire world. Kaladin uses the black sword Nightblood to sever the tethers connecting Ishar to his companions. The six Honorbearers, created from Ishar’s darkness, rise and prepare to attack with masks of rage.

Nightblood connects to Kaladin and begins draining his Stormlight instead of consuming Szeth’s own soul. Nightblood demands to be given to Szeth so they can destroy the rising abominations nearby. Kaladin tosses the black sword to Szeth, and the line of darkness between them stretches thin. Szeth lifts the sword high as Kaladin helps Syl rise to her feet again. Nightblood drips inky black liquid that evaporates into smoke before it can hit the ground.

Szeth faces the six Honorbearers, who represent his family and mentors turned into mindless puppets. He sees that they are being forced to act and feels their intense internal pain. Nightblood restores Szeth’s Lashings, which the sword learned by connecting to the various Honorblades. Szeth feels a chill in his palm but knows Kaladin’s Stormlight will sustain the weapon. He gives his heart to the fight for the first time in years and begins.

### Chapter 141

Szeth fights the six Honorbearers alone, moving with the wind and easily parrying their Shardblades. He uses Nightblood to destroy a stone wall raised by a Stoneward and Lashes her. Kaladin watches and sees thousands of windspren watching from the other side of the veil. He feels the land tremble as a significant event occurs at the tower of Urithiru. The release of Ba-Ado-Mishram rights an ancient wrong and vibrates the very soul of Roshar.

Dalinar becomes the Vessel for the power of Honor as it returns to him through Connection. He realizes that Mishram’s betrayal was what had originally broken the power of Tanavast long ago. Szeth kills the first Honorbearer, Pozen, by thrusting the black sword Nightblood through his chest. He then identifies Moss, his only actual friend among them, and kills him to grant rest. Ba-Ado-Mishram appears as a vengeful shadow before Renarin and Rlain in a billowing black smoke.

Rlain protects Renarin from Mishram, arguing that Renarin did what was right by freeing her today. Mishram screams, causing an explosion of light that ejects the group onto an Oathgate plateau. Szeth kills the Stoneward, leaving only his sister Elid, a Truthwatcher, and his father Neturo. The Truthwatcher summons shadows of everyone Szeth has killed, which accuse him of his many crimes. Szeth ignores the shadows and kills his sister Elid with Nightblood to end her suffering.

### Chapter 142

Dalinar’s mind expands as he becomes a god, seeing the past, present, and potential futures. He considers destroying Odium but realizes the power of Honor is fueled by pride and war. Taravangian challenges Dalinar to a confrontation, hoping to splinter the power of Honor once again. Dalinar is momentarily tempted by the Blackthorn’s rage but stops when he hears Gavinor crying. He recalls a vision of a cataclysm and realizes the destruction is caused by his actions.

Dalinar refuses to fight Odium and enters a final vision where he meets the king Nohadon. Nohadon shares Shin bread, a thick loaf eaten with salted butter, with Dalinar in his kitchen. Dalinar realizes that both peace and war currently serve Taravangian’s goals for total galactic conquest. He speaks with the developing mind of the power of Honor, which appears as a child. Dalinar understands that the powers themselves must learn and change to break the cycle of battle.

Dalinar formulates a plan to buy time for Honor’s power while denying it to Taravangian. He recognizes that Kaladin will preserve a piece of what is needed for the world’s future. Returning to the rooftop, Dalinar tells Taravangian he cannot best him and will not kill Gavinor. The Stormfather agrees to a sacrifice similar to the path of the Heralds from the past. Dalinar speaks the words "I renounce my oaths," effectively breaking his Bondsmith status and connection.
