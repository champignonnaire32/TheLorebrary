### Prelude to the Stormlight Archive
Kalak walks across a battlefield littered with human and non-human corpses and smoldering rocks. He reaches a stone spire to meet the other Heralds but finds only Jezrien waiting for him. Seven masterly swords are driven into the ground, representing the seven Heralds who survived the battle. Talenel is missing because he died holding a passage and has been sent back to the place of torture. Kalak admits to Jezrien that he can no longer endure the cycle of torture and death.

Jezrien reveals that a decision has been made to abandon the Oathpact and leave the cycle of Desolations. He believes that Ishar’s plan will work as long as one person remains bound to the pact. Taln is left alone to suffer the eternal torture while the other nine Heralds stay behind on Roshar. Kalak looks at the primitive humans and the Radiants who must now defend themselves without the Heralds' guidance. Jezrien summons his own glowing Blade and thrusts it into the stone alongside the others.

The Heralds decide to go their separate ways and never seek each other out again. Jezrien instructs Kalak to tell the people that they have finally won the war. Kalak summons his own Blade and drives it into the ground, completing the circle of abandoned weapons. He looks back at the empty spot where Taln’s tenth sword should have been placed. Kalak walks away in the opposite direction from Jezrien, leaving the battlefield and his duties behind.



### Prologue: To Kill
Szeth-son-son-Vallano sits in a white robe at a treaty-signing feast between the Alethi and the Parshendi. He observes King Gavilar’s relatives, including the drunken Dalinar and the heirs Elhokar and Jasnah. The Parshendi musicians play drums while the Alethi lighteyes dance and drink sapphire wine. Szeth leaves the hall and passes nine statues of the Heralds, noting that Shalash's statue is missing. He prepares to assassinate King Gavilar as commanded by his Parshendi masters.

Szeth draws Stormlight from sapphire lamps to power his Lashings and infiltrates the king's hallway. He uses a Basic Lashing to change his gravity, allowing him to walk on walls and drop guards with his feet. After a ten-heartbeat delay, Szeth summons his Shardblade and kills a guard by severing his soul. He uses a Full Lashing to bond a stone block to a doorframe, crushing the pursuing soldiers. Szeth enters the king's chambers and confronts a Shardbearer who he realizes is actually King Gavilar.

The two engage in a duel where Gavilar’s Shardplate blocks Szeth's Blade until the armor begins to leak Stormlight. Szeth Lashes the wooden balcony downward five times, causing it to collapse and send the king falling to the ground. He finds Gavilar dying in the wreckage and harvests Stormlight from the king's broken Shardplate. Gavilar gives Szeth a mysterious dark sphere and a final message for his brother Dalinar. Szeth uses the king's blood to write the message on the wood before escaping into the night.



### Chapter 1: Stormblessed
Fifteen-year-old Cenn waits in the front lines of Brightlord Amaram’s army during a border skirmish. Dallet, a veteran soldier, calms Cenn by explaining that they are in Kaladin Stormblessed’s lucky squad. Kaladin, the youthful squadleader, arrives and confers with Dallet about the best positioning on the field. The Alethi army breaks formation and charges toward the enemy, but Kaladin’s squad runs without raising their shields. Dallet explains that the archers aim for the crowded middle ranks, so the front-runners are safe from the initial volleys.

The squad reaches an incline and forms a tight V-shaped defensive formation to repel the enemy attackers. Kaladin uses rhythmic signals by banging his spear on his shield to command his men through the chaos. Cenn struggles to maintain his training as the battlefield devolves into hundreds of small, brutal skirmishes. As Amaram’s lines begin to buckle, Kaladin leads the squad on a retreat toward a stronger Alethi position. Cenn gets separated from the group and is stabbed in the thigh by an enemy spearman.

Kaladin arrives just in time to deflect the killing blow and kills six enemy soldiers single-handedly. He binds Cenn’s wound with cloth and orders his men to take a high-ranking enemy officer. Dallet reveals that Kaladin uses his own pay to bribe runners into carrying darkeyed wounded off the field. Kaladin leaps upon a mounted battalionlord and stabs him in the eye to ensure the squad's promotion. A Shardbearer on a massive black horse appears and gallops toward the squad, causing Cenn to lose hope and close his eyes.



### Chapter 2: Honor Is Dead
Kaladin rides in a slave cage through the Unclaimed Hills, eight months after being branded a deserter. He eats a bowl of grey tallew slop and resists the paranoia and despair felt by the other nine slaves. A windspren takes the form of a young woman and begins following Kaladin, playing pranks on him. Another slave asks Kaladin about his past, noting his three forehead brands including the dangerous "shash" glyph. Kaladin reveals that he has attempted to escape ten times from five different masters over the last eight months.

The slave caravan stops for the night, and the slaver Tvlakv checks on his investments with his mercenaries. Kaladin fingers a stash of poisonous blackbane leaves he has hidden in his belt as a potential weapon. The windspren speaks to Kaladin, using his name and asking why he has stopped fighting for his life. Kaladin experiences flashbacks of the deaths of Tien and Dallet while the spren questions his exhaustion. A sick slave in the corner of the cage begins coughing up greenish phlegm from the grinding coughs.

Kaladin intervenes and tells Tvlakv that the sick man will live if given extra water and sugar. Bluth, a mercenary, drags the sick slave out of the cage and kills him with a cudgel to prevent infection. Kaladin screams in rage at the slaver, but Tvlakv dismisses him as a hateful deserter. During the confrontation, Kaladin accidentally drops his crumbled blackbane leaves onto the dirty cage floor. He sinks into a state of defeat as the windspren darts around him in confusion.



### Chapter 3: City of Bells
Shallan Davar arrives at the city of Kharbranth on the ship Wind’s Pleasure after a six-month journey. She observes the wedge-shaped city built into a cliffside and notes the bright colors of the buildings. Captain Tozbek informs her that Princess Jasnah Kholin is still in the city and has not yet departed. Shallan reflects on her secret plan to save her family’s house from financial ruin and political collapse. She engages in witty banter with the sailors to hide her nervousness about her upcoming meeting.

Shallan covers her left safehand with an oversized cuff as she prepares to walk through the Vorin city. She hires a porter to pull her in a cart toward the Conclave at the apex of the city. Yalb, a sailor, accompanies her and translates the porter’s explanations about the city's many bells. Shallan sketches the exotic people and creatures she sees, including parshmen, skyeels, and minks. She notices the color-coded buildings of the city, where violet indicates clothing and green indicates food.

The cart reaches the Conclave, a massive building carved directly into the white rock of the cliff. Shallan pays the porter with diamond chips and gives Yalb a ruby mark to wait for her return. She observes lighteyed men in military coats and women with covered safehands entering the structure. Shallan enters the Conclave and follows a hallway lined with white Stormlight lamps powered by diamond broams. She grips her satchel and prepares to locate Jasnah Kholin to begin her desperate apprenticeship.
