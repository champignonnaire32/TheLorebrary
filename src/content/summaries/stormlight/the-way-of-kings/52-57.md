### Chapter 52: A Highway to the Sun
Dalinar Kholin discusses his plan to abdicate the highprincedom with his sons Adolin and Renarin. Adolin is pained by his father's decision, realizing his own doubts about Dalinar's sanity triggered it. Dalinar explains he will return to Alethkar to aid the queen while Renarin remains in the warcamps. The sons tie Dalinar's arms to a chair to prepare him for the coming highstorm vision. Dalinar finds himself in a vision atop the red stone battlements of Feverstone Keep.

Soldiers at the keep watch a massive line of three hundred Knights Radiant, warriors in magical Shardplate, approach across the stone. The Radiants charge toward the fortress but stop and begin driving their Shardblades, weapons of rippling steel, into the ground. Dalinar watches in horror as the knights discard their Plate and walk away from their abandoned weapons. This event is revealed to be the Day of Recreance, the historic betrayal of mankind by the Radiants. One man remains behind to tell Dalinar that the True Desolation and the Everstorm are approaching.

The figure commands Dalinar to unite the highprinces and find guidance within the book *The Way of Kings*. Dalinar wakes from the vision and dictates the details to Navani Kholin to preserve them. Renarin suggests they investigate the history of Feverstone Keep to prove if the visions are truth or madness. Navani attempts to flirt with Dalinar in private, but he refuses her due to his strict moral code. Dalinar struggles with his growing attraction to his brother's widow while maintaining the principles of his house.



### Chapter 53: Dunny
Kaladin leads Bridge Four through a hail of Parshendi arrows, the enemy archers, during a high-stakes plateau assault. He feels a sudden surge of physical strength as the enemy projectiles strangely miss his bridge crew. The bridgemen set their heavy wooden bridge and the Alethi cavalry charges across to engage the singers. A youthful bridgeman named Dunny is struck by multiple arrows and falls dead on the bridge deck. Moash pulls Kaladin back to safety as the charging horses trample Dunny's body beyond all recognition.

Kaladin watches the ensuing battle from the chasm edge to maintain his focus as a soldier. He decides to begin rescuing and treating wounded bridgemen who have been abandoned by other crews. Teft and Rock express concerns about limited resources, but Kaladin insists that the men deserve honor. Bridge Four gathers the survivors and Kaladin uses his surgical skills to sew their wounds. Teft instructs Kaladin to keep his spheres infused with Stormlight, the luminous energy of the storms, claiming they provide luck.

Kaladin notices that his gemstones consistently lose their luminous energy much faster than normal. Teft points out that arrows consistently fail to strike Kaladin even when he is running point. Kaladin is shocked to discover that an arrow wound on his cheek has vanished without leaving a scar. Moash acknowledges Kaladin as an honest leader and apologizes for his previous skepticism. The crew carries four wounded survivors back to the barracks on a makeshift water litter.



### Chapter 54: Gibletish
Dalinar attends a feast on the king's island while wearing a formal uniform to maintain military decorum. The King's Wit, a professional jester named Hoid, joins him at the table and speaks cryptically. Wit mentions the mysterious term Adonalsium and warns Dalinar that Sadeas is planning a revelation this night. The jester explains he is leaving the warcamps and tells Dalinar that life is becoming dangerous. Dalinar orders his officers to prepare for a potential arrest as he approaches the king and Sadeas.

Sadeas reveals the results of his investigation into the cut girth strap from the greatshell hunt. He explains that eight gemstones in the king's Shardplate were also cracked to cause them to fail. A young groom testifies that the king's saddle was switched before it ever reached the Kholin camp. Sadeas publicly declares that Dalinar is innocent and was likely being framed by a third party. The highprinces disperse in disappointment as Dalinar's reputation is restored by his greatest rival.

Sadeas admits he sought the position of Highprince of Information specifically to clear Dalinar's name. Dalinar offers Sadeas a formal alliance to conduct joint plateau assaults using their combined strengths. They agree to split gemhearts and Shardblades, with Dalinar claiming any Plate for his son Renarin. Sadeas is intrigued by the prospect of winning more battles and agrees to review the formal details. Dalinar feels a sense of relief as he begins to rebuild the unity his brother Gavilar desired.



### Chapter 55: An Emerald Broam
Bridge Four watches from a distance as Dalinar and Sadeas execute their first joint plateau assault. Dalinar's army uses massive bridges pulled by chulls, which are bridge-pulling crustaceans that protect the soldiers from arrows. The two armies easily crush the Parshendi by pinning them between their combined forces. Later that day, Hashal sends Kaladin and his crew into the chasms for scavenging duty. Kaladin discovers an emerald broam, a high-value currency sphere, on the body of a wealthy officer.

Moash and Rock discuss the possibility of stealing the fortune to bribe their way to freedom. Kaladin realizes that spending such a large sphere would immediately expose the bridgemen as thieves. He decides to turn in the emerald broam to gain the camp's trust while hiding smaller spheres. Sigzil describes the ancient city of Sesemalex Dar in the kingdom of Emul, which Kaladin recognizes from his dreams. The crew encounters a group of dead Parshendi and finds that Shen is intensely protective of the bodies.

Kaladin steals a Parshendi bow and arrows despite the cultural taboos against bridgemen owning weapons. He plans to use the bow to shoot pouches of stolen spheres into the underside of permanent bridges. Rock admits he is a skilled archer from the peaks but refuses to use the weapon for killing. The Horneater successfully shoots an arrow with a pouch of spheres into the wooden bridge above. Bridge Four hides the rest of their scavenged weapons in the chasms to use for future training.



### Chapter 56: That Storming Book
Dalinar fights with the Thrill, a pulsing battle-lust, through him as he swings his Shardblade Oathbringer. He notices that several beardless Parshendi warriors have feminine features, suggesting women fight in their ranks. A second Parshendi army unexpectedly reinforces the first, threatening to overwhelm the Alethi forces. Sadeas is caught in a vulnerable position at the back of his lines as the new army charges. Dalinar abandons his honor guard and charges alone on his Ryshadium horse, a massive stallion named Gallant, to save his ally.

He carves a path through the enemy army, slaughtering hundreds of Parshendi with his supernatural strength. Dalinar finds Sadeas surrounded and uses a massive fallen hammer to hurl enemies away from the fallen man. He stands over Sadeas and defends him against a thousand predators until his son Adolin arrives. Adolin leads the Cobalt Guard to punch through the enemy lines and rout the remaining Parshendi. The Alethi claim a total victory, and Sadeas is pulled from his shattered Shardplate largely uninjured.

Adolin points out that Dalinar's armor is severely cracked and leaking Stormlight from multiple rifts. Sadeas thanks Dalinar for the rescue and asks why he took such an incredible personal risk. Dalinar explains that he followed the Codes from *The Way of Kings* which forbid abandoning allies. Sadeas expresses a genuine desire to understand Dalinar's principles and the book that inspired them. Dalinar feels a deep sense of revulsion at the slaughter despite the success of the joint assault.



### Chapter 57: Wandersail
Kaladin fails to save the bridgeman Maps, who dies from an arrow wound that pierced his spine. He is overcome by the sorrow of his previous failures as a surgeon in his father's surgery. Bridge Four has lost several members recently, leaving only twenty-six men to carry their heavy bridge. Lopen retrieves a hidden pouch of spheres from the underside of a bridge during the return march. The crew adopts a formal salute of crossing their wrists in fists to show their unity.

Kaladin realizes he can use the Parshendi's reverence for their dead to his advantage in future plans. He treats a minor wound on Teft's arm, but Teft intentionally provokes a physical reaction from him. Kaladin draws in Stormlight from his hidden spheres and begins to glow with luminescent vapors. He discovers he can adhere a stone to a wooden wall by imbuing it with the light's energy. Syl, his companion spren, admits she is responsible for his emerging supernatural abilities and explains that she binds things.

Kaladin wanders onto the Shattered Plains and encounters Hoid, who is playing a wooden flute. Hoid tells the story of the Wandersail, a legendary ship, and a people who murdered each other in a dead emperor's name. The storyteller gives Kaladin the flute and reveals that Sigzil was once his own apprentice. Kaladin realizes he has been using the idea of being cursed to avoid the responsibility of his powers. He returns to the barracks and tells Teft that they will work together to master his new abilities.
