### 20: SCARLET
Thirteen-year-old Kaladin attempts to save a five-year-old girl named Miasal after she suffers a high fall. He identifies a compound fracture in her leg and notes significant blood loss from severed arteries. Kaladin applies a tourniquet using his shirt while the girl’s father, Harl, is restrained by the crowd. He requests fire and a knife to perform an emergency cauterization on the open wound. The young surgeon successfully stops the bleeding using the heated blade while his hands remain steady.

Kaladin realizes Miasal has stopped breathing despite his successful work on her leg injury. He discovers she has succumbed to a head wound that was not visible during his initial focus. Harl falls to his knees and wails over the corpse of his daughter as the crowd falls silent. Kaladin retreats to the steps of the surgery room to process his grief and failure. His father, Lirin, eventually finds him and inspects the medical work performed on the girl.

Lirin tells Kaladin that his technique was correct and that he is proud of his son's actions. Kaladin observes the blood on his clothing has turned from scarlet to a dull reddish brown. He tells his father that he no longer wants to be a surgeon due to this experience. Lirin explains that a surgeon must grow calluses to decide when to care and when to let go. The chapter ends with Kaladin sitting in the distance while Harl continues to mourn his child.





### 21: WHY MEN LIE
Kaladin wakes in the Bridge Four barracks feeling intense physical pain from his previous bridge run. He forces himself to stand and inspects the three wounded men he chose to save. Leyten remains alive with a weak pulse, while Hobber expresses gratitude for Kaladin's medical intervention. Dabbid remains in a catatonic state due to battle shock and does not speak to the others. Kaladin washes his own side wound with water and performs physical stretches to maintain his mobility.

He practices carrying a heavy bridge plank alone in the lumberyard to rebuild his strength. Syl appears and asks Kaladin why men lie and if Dalinar Kholin is also a liar. Kaladin explains his deep mistrust of all lighteyes and specifically mentions Brightlord Amaram's betrayal. Gaz approaches Kaladin to report that Highprince Sadeas has officially denied food and pay to the wounded. The sergeant warns Kaladin that trying to steal extra food for the men will result in punishment.

Kaladin asks the other bridgemen to share their rations, but Moash and most others refuse to help. Rock agrees to share a portion of his food because Kaladin took his place during the last run. The Horneater reveals he can see Syl and believes Kaladin is protected by a spiritual force. Kaladin convinces Gaz to assign Bridge Four to a stone-gathering detail outside the warcamp. He informs Teft and Rock that they must gather a specific plant called knobweed during their work.





### 22: EYES, HANDS, OR SPHERES?
Dalinar Kholin walks with his sons Adolin and Renarin toward the king’s outdoor feasting basin. Adolin reports that leatherworkers found no definitive proof that the king’s saddle girth was intentionally cut. They arrive at a series of artificial islands created by Soulcasters for the royal celebration. Wit sits on a high stool at the entrance to the king's island and insults the arriving guests. Dalinar observes the younger lighteyes drinking violet wine while he adheres to the strict Alethi Codes.

Wit warns Dalinar that rumors about him abandoning the Vengeance Pact are spreading through the camp. Navani Kholin, the king's mother, arrives unexpectedly and interrupts the conversation between Dalinar and Wit. She reveals that Veden artifabrians have developed "half-shards," which are shields capable of blocking Shardblades. Navani explains that she returned because she believes the warcamps are the true political center of Alethkar. She demonstrates a crustacean-like ruby fabrial to her dinner companions while Dalinar watches from a distance.

King Elhokar stands and addresses the highprinces regarding the perceived attempt on his life. He announces the appointment of Brightlord Sadeas to the new position of Highprince of Information. This role grants Sadeas the authority to investigate crimes and question any person within the warcamps. Dalinar realizes that Sadeas has outmaneuvered him by taking control of the assassination investigation. Elhokar tells Dalinar that he hopes the investigation will eventually vindicate his uncle's claim of safety.





### 23: MANY USES
Kaladin supervises the stone-gathering detail while Rock and Teft search the landscape for knobweed reeds. These reeds grow behind boulders and possess delicate fronds that retract when a highstorm approaches. Syl assists the group by flying ahead to locate thick patches of the plants for Rock to harvest. Kaladin ties bundles of the collected reeds to the underside of the stone-gathering wagon to hide them. The other bridgemen like Jaks and Natam remain hostile and refuse to speak with Kaladin during the work.

That night, Kaladin and his two companions sneak into the wagonyard to retrieve their hidden bundles. They collect discarded liquor bottles from behind a tavern to use as storage containers for the sap. The group travels to the edge of a deep chasm near the staging area to work in private. Kaladin demonstrates how to squeeze the knobweed stems to extract a milky white antiseptic liquid. He explains that this liquid is more effective than standard apothecary salves for treating infected wounds.

Rock reveals that his true name is Numuhukumakiaki'aialunamor and that he was once a cook. He explains he became a bridgeman after his master lost all his possessions in a duel with Sadeas. Rock admits he was sent to the bridge crews because he put chull dung in Sadeas's food. Kaladin mentions that he was once a soldier who was betrayed by a lighteyes after killing an enemy. The three men finish milking the reeds and toss the empty husks into the darkness of the chasm.





### 24: THE GALLERY OF MAPS
Dalinar meets Highprince Roion inside the Gallery of Maps to discuss a change in military strategy. The facility contains Soulcast stone walls and gnarled shalebark plants that reach toward the skylights. Dalinar proposes a joint plateau assault where their two armies would coordinate their movements and resources. He offers to let Roion have the first Shardblade won during a successful combined mission. Roion expresses suspicion that Dalinar is attempting to seize his lands and subordinates his authority.

Dalinar studies the Prime Map and focuses on a massive plateau known as The Tower. He believes a concentrated strike at this location could destroy the Parshendi's capacity to wage war. Adolin enters the gallery and reports that Roion has left without agreeing to the alliance. He informs his father that Sadeas is moving to interview the grooms who handled the king's horse. Adolin warns that Sadeas will likely manufacture evidence to link Dalinar to the saddle girth incident.

Dalinar insists on trusting Sadeas based on the visions he receives during highstorms. Adolin confronts his father and claims the visions are symptoms of aging or mental decline. He argues that the king’s saddle girth simply wore out and that there is no grand conspiracy. Dalinar orders Adolin to leave the gallery after his son shouts about the diminishing reputation of their house. The highprince remains alone to contemplate the map and the legacy of the ancient king Nohadon.
