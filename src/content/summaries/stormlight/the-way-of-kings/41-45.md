### 41: OF ALDS AND MILP
Young Kaladin enters his father’s surgery room to find it drenched in blood following a disastrous hunt for a whitespine, a dangerous predatory beast. Lirin works frantically on Brightlord Rillir, who has a whitespine tusk jutting from his gut and a crushed leg. Brightlord Roshone lies nearby with a similar bony spear piercing his leg while Laral screams in the doorway. Lirin orders Kaladin to assist with water and bandages as they attempt to stabilize the two lighteyed patients. Kaladin observes that Rillir’s face is partially flayed and one of his eyes has been sliced open.

Lirin abruptly stops treating Rillir and moves to tend to Roshone, despite the latter's demands to save his son first. The surgeon explains his prioritization guidelines, stating that he must choose patients based on the likelihood of successful treatment. Lirin declares Rillir is beyond help and instructs Kaladin to administer dazewater, a clear liquid anesthetic, to Roshone. Kaladin uses the remaining dazewater on Rillir as a final mercy while the youth dies on the table. Lirin removes the tusk fragments from Roshone’s leg while expressing his disdain for the lighteyes' reckless hunting habits.

Kaladin notices his father’s hand quivering as the surgical knife comes dangerously close to Roshone’s femoral artery. Lirin chooses to save the man’s life rather than letting him bleed out, completing the surgery successfully. That evening, Kaladin admits he realized he was capable of murdering Roshone or letting him die on the table. Lirin insists that a surgeon must never become a killer and must always do what is right simply because it is right. Kaladin recognizes a frightening new truth within himself: some people, like a festering finger, need to be removed.





### 42: BEGGARS AND BARMAIDS
Shallan and Jasnah argue over the morality of Jasnah’s recent execution of four criminals in a Kharbranth alleyway. Jasnah reveals her Soulcaster, a glove-mounted fabrial with three large gemstones, and gives Shallan the rest of the day off. Shallan retreats to her room and attempts to activate the stolen Soulcaster by humming and concentrating on a wooden stick. The stick does not transform, leading Shallan to worry that the fabrial might be a fake or broken. A mysterious, whispered voice asks "What are you?" but Shallan finds only a palace maid entering the room.

Shallan uses the maid's presence as a cover to sneak into Jasnah’s private chambers and search her notebooks. She discovers research on Urithiru, a legendary lost city, and notes describing Voidbringers as ancient enemies made of ash and fire. Brother Kabsal arrives with a basket of bread and truthberry jam, which is rumored to compel speakers to tell the truth. During their picnic in the Conclave gardens, Kabsal admits his superiors originally wanted him to steal Jasnah’s Soulcaster. Kabsal claims that Soulcasting is actually a simple process of pressing a hand against an object and tapping a gemstone.

Shallan realizes her own failures with the device mean there is likely a deeper secret to its activation. They spot King Taravangian walking nearby, forcing Kabsal to hide to avoid being caught shirking his duties. Shallan receives a spanreed message from Captain Tozbek stating that his ship will return to Kharbranth in one week. She decides to use her final seven days to continue her studies before she must flee with the stolen fabrial. Shallan returns to Jasnah's alcove and offers her bread and jam, pretending to be a dedicated ward for a little longer.





### 43: THE WRETCH
Kaladin wakes in a state of deep apathy, feeling that his survival of the highstorm has accomplished nothing for his men. He finds that the bridgemen have used Rock’s razor to shave their faces, though they remain ragged and dirty. Brightness Hashal and Brightlord Matal arrive to take command of the bridge crews, replacing the executed Lamaril. Hashal announces that Highprince Sadeas has forbidden any further gawkers from visiting the man who lived through the storm. A soldier strikes Kaladin with a spear butt after the former bridgeman instinctively blocks a blow aimed at his stomach.

Hashal assigns Bridge Four to permanent chasm duty, forcing them to scavenge for salvage whenever they are not running bridges. Kaladin leads the men into the rifts of the Shattered Plains, where they find a massive pile of rotting corpses. Syl admits to Kaladin that she has a faint memory of helping men kill for a righteous cause in the past. The bridgemen begin stripping armor from the dead and discover the bodies of several fellow bridgemen washed into the recess. Teft and Sigzil discuss the First Ideal of the Knights Radiant: "Life before death, strength before weakness, journey before destination."

While the other men argue about the historical betrayal of the Radiants, Kaladin retreats into the shadows to brood. The phrase "Life before death" resonates with him, sparking a sudden resolve to stop his slide back into apathy. He returns to the group and proposes a plan to train with scavenged spears in preparation for an escape attempt. Kaladin warns that the attempt will be dangerous and likely lead to pursuit by Sadeas’s soldiers. Teft, Moash, and several others agree to the plan, while Rock offers to support the rebellion by serving as their cook.





### 44: THE WEEPING
During the four weeks of constant rain known as the Weeping, Kaladin patches the roof of his home in Hearthstone. Highmarshal Amaram, a renowned military leader and cousin to Roshone, arrives in town to recruit soldiers for border defense. Tien joins Kaladin on the roof and gifts him a beautifully carved wooden horse to lift his spirits. Hesina reveals that Lirin is spending his hidden spheres to trick Roshone into believing the family has finally been broken. Lirin leads his family to the town square for a mandatory meeting where Roshone will announce his latest decrees.

Roshone appears alongside Amaram and Laral, who is wearing a bride’s prayer on her sleeve to signal her engagement to the citylord. Amaram announces a recruitment drive, offering bonuses and potential nahn increases for volunteers willing to serve for four years. Five townspeople, including a boy named Jost, step forward to join the army as volunteers. Roshone exercises his legal right as citylord to conscript additional men to fill Amaram’s ranks from a pre-selected list. Alaxia reads the names of the conscripts, ending with Tien, the thirteen-year-old son of Lirin.

Lirin protests that his family is exempt from conscription as vital medical personnel, but Roshone points out that Tien is a carpenter’s apprentice. Roshone reveals that taking Tien is his specific act of revenge for the death of his own son years prior. Amaram promises that the boy will serve as a messenger rather than a combatant to avoid the front lines. Kaladin immediately volunteers for the army to protect his terrified brother, forcing Amaram to accept him as a soldier. Kaladin promises his father he will bring Tien home safely in four years before they both leave Hearthstone for the war.





### 45: SHADESMAR
Shallan reads King Gavilar’s journals, which describe the Parshendi as a unique parshman race with a complex society and religion. Gavilar notes that the Parshendi sing identical songs across distances and are confused by the silent nature of ordinary servants. Jasnah explains she is a Veristitalian, a scholar who reconstructs the truth of the past to understand the present. Shallan identifies an inconsistency between the Parshendi’s primitive stone huts and their sophisticated, etched steel weapons. Jasnah clarifies that Urithiru was the ancient center of the Silver Kingdoms, though it is now considered a myth by many.

While researching independently in the library, Shallan encounters Kabsal and they discuss the theological nature of the Voidbringers. Kabsal theorizes that Jasnah is trying to prove Vorinism is a fraud by demonstrating that the Voidbringers were a fabrication. Shallan confesses to Kabsal that she must leave Kharbranth immediately, though she realizes she will miss her studies more than him. After taking a Memory, Shallan sketches Kabsal and notices terrifying, symbol-headed figures standing behind him in the drawing. She flees toward her room, seeing these spectral "predators" woven into the crowds of the Palanaeum and the Veil.

In her chamber, Shallan’s drawings show a creature reaching for her, and she feels a cold presence in the room. She is suddenly pulled into a vision of a black sky and an ocean of glass beads where a warm voice demands a trade. She inadvertently Soulcasts a glass goblet into a large quantity of blood and finds herself back in her room. To hide her illegal Soulcasting from Jasnah, Shallan uses a broken pitcher shard to cut her own arm and fake an injury. Jasnah enters to find the room flooded with blood and calls for help while Shallan feigns unconsciousness to protect her secret.
