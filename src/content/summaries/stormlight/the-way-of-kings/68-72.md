### Chapter 68: Eshonai
Adolin and Dalinar lead a desperate charge against the Parshendi to escape the Shattered Plains. They use their Shardblades to carve a path through the enemy ranks for their wounded and exhausted soldiers. Kaladin defends the bridge head alone while infused with Stormlight, displaying inhuman speed and precision. The bridgemen watch in awe as he kills Parshendi with his spear to protect the retreating Alethi. Teft organizes the bridge crews into defensive formations to support Kaladin's stand.

Dalinar breaks through to the chasm and finds the bridgemen fighting to hold the escape route open. He orders Adolin and the Cobalt Guard to reinforce the bridge head before the Parshendi can reclaim it. A Parshendi Shardbearer in silver Plate challenge Dalinar to a duel near a rock formation. Dalinar uses a tactical maneuver to shear the rock under the enemy's feet and drops a boulder on him. The nameless spearman, Kaladin, arrives and uses his spear to damage the Parshendi Shardbearer's Plate through a crack.

Kaladin's intervention allows Dalinar to escape on his horse, Gallant, across the bridge. The Alethi army successfully retreats, though they have suffered massive casualties during the betrayal. Dalinar salutes the remaining Parshendi forces as the bridgemen pull the bridge back across the chasm. Once safe, Dalinar promises to protect Kaladin and his men from Sadeas's retribution. He vows to take them into his own camp and ensure they are not returned to slavery.



### Chapter 69: Justice
Navani paints and burns a massive "Justice" glyph in Sadeas's camp to pray for Dalinar's survival after rumors of his death spread. Dalinar returns with his surviving troops and reconciles with Navani and Renarin at the warcamp gates. He decides to confront Sadeas to settle the betrayal without sparking an immediate civil war. Sadeas admits he abandoned Dalinar to remove him as a political rival and an influence on the King. Dalinar shocks both armies by trading his Shardblade, Oathbringer, to Sadeas in exchange for the freedom of all the bridgemen.

After the trade, Dalinar promises Kaladin and the bridgemen that they will be free soldiers in his own camp. He then visits King Elhokar's palace and orders the guards to stay outside despite any noises they hear. Dalinar physically assaults Elhokar, kicking him across the room and shattering his breastplate to demonstrate his lethality. He forces the terrified King to admit that he cut his own saddle girth to fabricate assassination attempts. Dalinar explains that if he truly wanted to kill Elhokar, he could have done so easily and without witness.

Dalinar demands that Elhokar name him Highprince of War to centralize command and enforce the Codes across the warcamps. He explains that the Alethi must be treated like children and forced to act with honor until it becomes habit. Elhokar reluctantly agrees to the appointment and the new structure of gemheart distribution. Dalinar also informs the King that he is now openly courting Navani regardless of societal disapproval. He leaves the palace feeling resolute, having traded his Blade for honor and secured the authority to reform the kingdom.



### Chapter 70: Sea of Glass
Shallan recovers in a hospital room, mourning Kabsal's death and feeling guilty for betraying Jasnah. She realizes that Jasnah survived the poisoned bread by Soulcasting it into a different substance as she ate. Through her sketches, Shallan deduces that Jasnah's Soulcaster fabrial is actually a fake decoy. She leaves the hospital in her robe and travels to the Conclave to confront Jasnah in her alcove. Shallan presents a drawing of Shadesmar to prove she has discovered Jasnah's secret ability.

Jasnah initially denies the claim but is shocked when Shallan describes her own accidental Soulcasting of the goblet. Shallan reveals that she has been using the fake Soulcaster to hide her inherent power from others. To prove her point, Shallan speaks a truth to the symbol-headed spren and admits she killed her father. The admission pulls both women into Shadesmar, where they stand on a sea of glass beads under a black sky. Jasnah rescues Shallan from drowning in the beads and pulls her back into the physical world.

Jasnah admits that she uses the fake fabrial to distract from her true nature as a potential Radiant. Shallan begs to be Jasnah's ward again, promising to never lie or steal from her mistress. Jasnah agrees to resume her training and officially involve Shallan in her secret research. They sit together as Jasnah begins to share her findings regarding the ancient enemies of mankind. Jasnah reveals that the notes describe the nature and history of the Voidbringers.



### Chapter 71: Recorded in Blood
Szeth-son-son-Vallano arrives in Kharbranth working as a common porter to avoid drawing attention to his Shin features. He feels the weight of his previous assassinations and hears the screams of his victims in his mind. His current mission is to kill King Taravangian, a monarch famous for his humanitarian hospitals. Szeth uses a Lashing to infiltrate the palace through a lighteyes' privy and navigates the corridors in disguise. He reaches the king's study and prepares to deliver a required message before the execution.

Taravangian reveals that he already knows Szeth's identity and is actually the holder of his Oathstone. The king explains that he placed his own name on the assassination list as a deceptive security measure. He admits that he ordered the deaths of his own friends and other world leaders to ensure global stability. Szeth is horrified to learn that his kindly master is the architect of the recent international chaos. Taravangian orders Szeth to spare him and reveals he has a new purpose for the assassin's skills.

Taravangian leads Szeth to a secret hospital ward where terminally ill and forgotten people are being slowly drained of blood. The king listens to the final words of the dying, hoping to record prophetic visions of the coming disaster. He believes that the blood of the innocent is a necessary sacrifice to save the world from destruction. Taravangian adds Dalinar Kholin to the assassination list to prevent him from uniting the Alethi highprinces. Szeth accepts the command to go to the Shattered Plains and end Dalinar's life brutally.



### Chapter 72: Veristitalian
Jasnah explains that she Soulcast Shallan's blood to remove the poison while she was unconscious in the hospital. She clarifies that pure Essences like blood are easier to create than complex organic structures like jam. Jasnah reveals that the symbol-headed creatures Shallan sees are spren tied to the abilities of the Knights Radiant. She confirms that while Soulcasting fabrials exist, her own power comes from an inherent bond with these spren. Shallan listens as Jasnah describes the historical role of the Radiants in defending Roshar.

Shallan questions Jasnah's obsession with the Voidbringers when most modern scholars consider them a total fabrication. Jasnah explains that she seeks the natural, real-world origins behind the supernatural legends of the past. She believes the Voidbringers were physical beings of ash and fire who fought mankind in ancient times. Shallan reviews the notes describing the creatures as having skin like flame and char with pits of blackness for eyes. The descriptions paint a picture of a terrifying enemy that was supposedly defeated and banished forever.

Jasnah reveals her theory that the humans of the past did not actually destroy their ancient enemies. She points out that humans historically prefer to enslave what they cannot kill for their own benefit. Shallan looks at the parshmen working in the palace and notices their skin of black and red marble. She realizes with horror that the docile parshmen are actually the remnants of the Voidbringers. Jasnah confirms that the ancient monsters were never truly gone, but have been serving as slaves for centuries.
