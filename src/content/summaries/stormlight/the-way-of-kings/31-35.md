### 31: BENEATH THE SKIN
Six years ago in Hearthstone, Lirin gets drunk on violet wine while Kaladin studies anatomy folios. Lirin expresses regret for returning to the town and advises Kaladin to stay in Kharbranth once he arrives there. Highlord Roshone has manipulated the townspeople into withholding donations from the surgeon by implying they shouldn't pay if Lirin doesn't charge. Lirin refuses to spend the spheres saved for Kaladin's education, believing Roshone is trying to bully them into poverty. Kaladin recognizes a rock left for him by his brother Tien, who is currently apprenticing with a carpenter.

A group of masked men thumps on the surgery door late at night demanding the hidden spheres. Lirin calmly opens the door and confronts the intruders, who are armed with metal bars. Kaladin's father retrieves the glass goblet of spheres and removes the black cloth covering it. The sudden explosion of Stormlight reveals that the "bandits" are actually familiar townspeople like Luten, Horl, and Balsas. Lirin challenges the men to commit violence against the person who has healed their children for years.

Shamed by the light and the recognition, the masked men retreat into the darkness without a word. Lirin stands in the doorway with the blazing goblet until the stone square is empty. Kaladin watches the men fade away and puts his anatomy folio aside. Lirin returns the glass goblet to the cupboard and covers it with the black cloth. The room returns to silence as Kaladin looks back down at the drawings of dissected bodies.





### 32: SIDE CARRY
Kaladin trains Bridge Four to carry their bridge on its side to use as a shield against arrows. Gaz brings a new batch of recruits to the lumberyard and assigns them to various bridge crews. Kaladin intercepts Gaz and demands one of the new men to fill his depleted ranks. He selects Lopen, a one-armed Herdazian slave who waves at him and smiles despite his brand. Lopen claims that Herdazians are great fighters and explains his name's uniqueness to his new leader.

The army receives the call to march toward a massive, wedge-shaped plateau known as the Tower. Dabbid and Hobber assist Lopen in carrying twenty waterskins on a makeshift litter during the march. As they approach the Tower, the scouts report that the Parshendi are already waiting on the opposing rim. Kaladin orders Bridge Four to perform the side carry during the final assault on the chasm. He zigzags the bridge to intercept incoming volleys, successfully shielding his men from the Parshendi archers.

Bridge Four reaches the chasm without a single casualty, but the other crews suffer heavy losses. The uneven placement of the bridges prevents the Alethi cavalry from executing a coordinated charge. Highlord Sadeas’s forces become surrounded and pushed onto the defensive on the Tower’s surface. Brightlord Lamaril and Gaz approach Bridge Four to punish Kaladin for disrupting the army's tactical formation. Spearmen beat Kaladin to the ground and kick him until his belt pouch breaks and his spheres scatter.





### 33: CYMATICS
Shallan walks through the Palanaeum, an inverted pyramid library carved into the rock of Kharbranth. She studies natural science books and sketches the people she observes in the balconies. Jasnah Kholin has begun allowing Shallan to assist with her bathing, providing a potential window for theft. In the stacks, Shallan reviews her hidden sketches of Jasnah Soulcasting paper and wine. She compares her father's broken Soulcaster to her drawings of Jasnah’s functioning device.

Shallan finds the book Shadows Remembered and discovers it is a collection of darkeyed children's folktales. She reads a story about a child who fools Voidbringers with a wooden decoy on a lake. Ardent Kabsal arrives at the alcove with a basket of bread and simberry jam for Shallan. He demonstrates the concept of cymatics by vibrating a metal plate covered in sand with a music bow. The sand forms symmetrical patterns that match the aerial layouts of holy cities like Kholinar and Vedenar.

Kabsal claims these patterns are proof of the Almighty’s existence and touches Shallan’s hand. Jasnah returns to the alcove and dismisses Kabsal with a sharp remark about his recruitment tactics. She asks the Ardent if his plate can reproduce the pattern for the fabled city of Urithiru. After Kabsal leaves, Jasnah warns Shallan that the Ardent is interested in the Soulcaster, not her soul. Shallan clutches her safepouch as Jasnah turns back to her book.





### 34: STORMWALL
Kaladin wakes up hanging upside down from a rope tied to the roof of Bridge Four's barrack. Highprince Sadeas has sentenced him to be judged by the highstorm for dooming the battle at the Tower. Syl tells Kaladin that Brightlord Lamaril was executed for the failure while Gaz was allowed to keep his rank. Kaladin checks his body for broken ribs and feels flakes of dried blood on his face. Syl mentions that she feels a strange coldness and has been making soldiers trip as a form of revenge.

Rock, Teft, and Moash visit Kaladin outside the barrack as the highstorm approaches. Teft reports that over two hundred bridgemen died in the battle and only eleven bridges survived. The men promise to keep the new traditions of Bridge Four alive even if Kaladin dies. Kaladin refuses their offer to cut him down, as it would result in their own executions. He tells them to come out after the storm and see that he has survived the judgement.

Teft hands Kaladin a single dun skymark sphere that the men recovered from the ground. The bridgemen retreat into the barrack and shut the door as the sky turns black. Kaladin watches the stormwall approach, seeing it as a massive curtain of rain, dirt, and rocks. Thousands of windspren zip through the air ahead of the churning wave of water. The stormwall slams into the lumberyard and hits Kaladin with the force of the wind.





### 35: A LIGHT BY WHICH TO SEE
The force of the highstorm presses Kaladin against the stone wall of the barrack. The wind lifts him into the air, causing him to flap like a kite on his rope tether. He falls onto the slanted roof and grabs the lip with his fingers to stay attached. Debris and boulders crash around the building as the lightning illuminates the chaos. Kaladin eventually reaches for the steel ring set into the roof and clings to it with both hands.

Syl stands before him in the tempest, appearing to split the wind like a stone in a stream. Kaladin hits the roof hard after being tossed by a gust and loses his vision. He stands up in a sudden, unnatural silence and finds the air feels weightless. An enormous face of blackness appears in front of him, smiling and traced in the dark. The sphere in his hand flares with a sapphire glow, illuminating his tattered shirt and lacerated skin.

Lightning flashes again, returning the wind and rain as Kaladin falls back to his knees. He loses consciousness on the roof while clutching the glowing blue sphere. After the storm subsides, Rock and Teft exit the barrack to find Kaladin’s body hanging by the ankles. Kaladin’s eyes snap open and he exhales a spray of bloody spittle as his hand releases a dun sphere. Teft picks up the empty sphere and watches as the bridgemen bring a ladder to cut Kaladin down.
