### Chapter 63: Fear
Leyten completes the first set of chasmfiend carapace armor for Kaladin and Bridge Four. Kaladin orders full sets for the entire crew and uses wounded men to gather more carapace from the chasms. Hashal reduces the crew's scavenging quota after Kaladin presents the armor concept as her own idea. Leyten explains he was made a bridgeman to take the blame for a broken piece of lighteyes armor. Kaladin trains his men with spears in a wide chasm intersection using diamond chips for light.

Kaladin identifies Moash and Skar as the most skilled spearmen in Bridge Four. Moash tells Kaladin that his primary motivation for training is a desire for vengeance. Kaladin reveals he trained with the spear because he failed to save his brother. Moash swears by the blood of his fathers to guard Kaladin with his life. Kaladin instructs Moash to help Hobber and Yake with their thrusts while he supervises the group.

Rock tells Kaladin that a leader's face is always troubled. Kaladin expresses concern that Sadeas will never let Bridge Four go even with their improved survival rate. Rock suggests that Sigzil might be able to help them find a way to disappear or leave a false trail. Teft asks Kaladin to demonstrate spear routines to boost the men's morale. Kaladin refuses to spar because he fears becoming too eager and losing his current caution.



### Chapter 64: A Man of Extremes
Navani and Dalinar walk together through the grounds of Elhokar's hilltop palace. Dalinar explains that he stepped aside from courting Navani because Gavilar expressed interest first. Navani tells Dalinar that she interpreted his past coldness as hatred rather than self-restraint. Dalinar asks Navani for her honest opinion of his late wife, whose name he cannot remember. Navani describes Dalinar's wife as kind and calm but lacking in cleverness.

Dalinar admits to Navani that he binds himself to the Codes to control his internal extremes. Navani argues that there is nothing in Gavilar's book that prohibits a relationship between them. Dalinar expresses concern that Elhokar will read their potential relationship as a conspiracy. Horns sound from the lumberyard to announce a chasmfiend sighting at the Tower plateau. Dalinar bows to Navani and departs to collect his Shardplate for the joint assault with Sadeas.

Bridge Four marches through the lumberyard wearing orange carapace armor and shields reinforced with Parshendi bones. Kaladin carries a full pouch of infused spheres to provide the Stormlight he needs to survive bridge runs. Adolin decides to trust his father's visions after Navani reveals Dalinar is not insane. Sadeas meets Dalinar and Adolin to propose a joint assault involving fifteen thousand Alethi troops. Dalinar agrees to the plan and orders Adolin to mobilize the Fourth through Eighth Divisions for the march.



### Chapter 65: The Tower
Dalinar and Sadeas observe the Parshendi attempting to extract a gemheart from a chrysalis on the Tower. Sadeas proposes a single grand wave of attack across forty bridges to overwhelm the ten thousand Parshendi. Dalinar refuses to force his men to carry bridges while under fire and insists on a staggered landing. Sadeas agrees to clear a landing zone for Dalinar's bridge crews before starting his own assault. Bridge Four arrives at the chasm and Kaladin prepares to act as a decoy for the Parshendi archers.

Kaladin draws the attention of the Parshendi archers by dancing between their arrows at the front of the bridge. Sadeas's forces push onto the plateau while five bridges fall and their crews are killed. Skar takes an arrow to the foot and Teft receives a deep shoulder wound during the crossing. Kaladin uses a heated knife to cauterize Teft's wound on the battlefield to stop the bleeding. Dalinar summons Oathbringer and crashes into the Parshendi line to secure his landing point.

Dalinar and Adolin lead their army up the slope of the Tower to pin the Parshendi against the southeast cliff. Dalinar uses head-sized stones to crush a team of Parshendi using rock slings from a ridge. The Thrill surges through Dalinar as he cuts through the Parshendi ranks and scatters their leaders. Dalinar stops his blade over a young Parshendi boy and suddenly feels sickened by the slaughter. Adolin alerts Dalinar to a second Parshendi army jumping the chasm while Sadeas's forces begin a full retreat.



### Chapter 66: Codes
Kaladin finishes bandaging Skar's foot and realizes that Sadeas's entire army is retreating from the Tower. Matal orders Bridge Four to prepare to carry their bridge back across the chasm. Kaladin observes that Dalinar's force is now surrounded by the second Parshendi army. Sadeas crosses a bridge on his horse while Dalinar and his men are left without a means of retreat. Kaladin hears Sadeas tell his companions that Dalinar's honor has finally gotten him killed.

Dalinar and Adolin take a defensive position on a hillside as their Shardplate begins to leak Stormlight from cracks. Adolin shouts that he warned Dalinar not to trust Sadeas before they were trapped. Dalinar realizes that Sadeas's betrayal was a premeditated maneuver to eliminate him without open challenge. Dalinar and Adolin retreat behind their front line to rest and drink water while the Parshendi surround them. Adolin tells Dalinar that he will not blame him for Sadeas's deceit and affirms his support for the Codes.

Dalinar feels his guilt evaporate and delivers a speech to his remaining four thousand soldiers about honor in death. Dalinar summons Oathbringer and declares he is not ashamed of the man he has become. The Alethi soldiers stand taller and prepare to die fighting with their heads held high. Dalinar and Adolin charge back into the Parshendi ranks to continue the hopeless battle. Dalinar regrets leaving Renarin without Shardplate but finds peace in his final stand.



### Chapter 67: Words
Bridge Four lags behind the retreating army because they are carrying two wounded men and their bridge. Kaladin sees Syl appear as a full-sized woman staring in horror at Dalinar's surrounded army. Matal tells Bridge Four to cross the chasm and leave the staging plateau immediately. Kaladin convinces Matal to let his team rest for a few minutes while the rest of the bridges are pulled up. Kaladin initially proposes that Bridge Four use this opportunity to escape northward and disappear.

Syl tells Kaladin that she is an honorspren who binds things through oaths and nobility. Kaladin remembers his father's words about the necessity of someone stepping forward to do what is right. Bridge Four agrees to follow Kaladin back to the Tower to attempt a rescue of Dalinar's men. Kaladin draws in a massive amount of Stormlight and leads a charge toward the chasm. Dalinar sees the lone bridge crew approaching and orders his remaining men to attempt a breakout toward them.

Kaladin uses his shield to draw a full volley of over one hundred arrows to protect his men. The Parshendi archers flee in terror after witnessing Kaladin erupt with a fountain of light. Bridge Four sets the bridge across the chasm while Kaladin falls into a daze from Stormlight exhaustion. Kaladin experiences a vivid memory of the day his brother Tien was killed in Amaram's army. Kaladin speaks the Second Ideal of the Knights Radiant and draws Stormlight from the gems in the Parshendi beards to renew his strength.
