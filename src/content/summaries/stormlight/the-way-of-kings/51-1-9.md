### 51: Sas Nahn
Kaladin sits in Amaram's warcenter waiting room following a battle where he killed a Shardbearer. He reflects on his decision to give the Shardblade and Plate to his squad leader Coreb. Only four of his twenty-six men survived the engagement. Highmarshal Amaram enters the room accompanied by a stormwarden and several lighteyed officers. Two of Amaram's personal guards close and lock the doors behind the group.

Amaram praises Kaladin for his bravery before questioning why he rejected the Shards. Kaladin explains he does not want the weapons because they represent the slaughter of his men. Amaram looks at Coreb and then signals his guards to draw their swords. The officers immediately move to attack the four surviving members of Kaladin's squad. Kaladin attempts to intervene but is punched and pinned to the floor by multiple men.

Amaram's soldiers systematically execute Reesh, Hab, Alabet, and Coreb while Kaladin watches in horror. Amaram explains that he must take the Shards for himself to serve the interests of Alethkar. He claims the spearmen had to die so they could not contradict the story that Amaram killed the Shardbearer. A stormwarden uses a hot branding iron to mark Kaladin's forehead with the "sas nahn" glyphs. Amaram discharges Kaladin as a deserter and sells him into slavery as he leaves the room.



### 1-7: Baxil
Baxil and his cousin Av infiltrate a palace in Emul belonging to the wealthy Ashno of Sages. They follow a mysterious woman with long black hair and violet eyes who carries a thin-bladed sword. The mistress leads them into the Hallowed Hall, which contains numerous religious images of the Kadasix. Baxil carries a bag of tools but remains nervous about being caught by palace servants. The group ignores the valuable gemstones in the chandeliers because the mistress forbids stealing.

The mistress begins a systematic destruction of the palace's artwork using a knife and tool belt. She slashes a painting of Epan, Lady of Dreams, and gouges the eyes from a fine bust. Baxil and Av watch as she shatters a vase and crushes the remaining pieces on the floor. While they wait, Baxil discusses his desire to visit the Valley and seek a boon from the Nightwatcher. He hopes to ask for courage through the Old Magic to improve his station in life.

Av warns Baxil about the risks of the Old Magic by recounting his father’s experience with the Nightwatcher. His father received a supply of cloth but was cursed to see the world upside down for the rest of his life. The mistress returns to demand a padded mallet so she can break the arms off a large statue. She mentions she could use a Shardblade for the task but decides it would make the work too easy. The three thieves eventually finish their work and prepare to exit the palace without taking any loot.



### 1-8: Geranid
Geranid works on complex mathematical equations in a stone room on a remote Reshi island. Her partner Ashir, an elderly ardent, experiments with a new recipe involving caramelized Shin fruit and minnows. They live in seclusion to provide religious services for travelers while pursuing their private scientific interests. Ashir cooks on a modern fabrial hotplate while Geranid focuses her study on the flamespren dancing in the hearth. The room smells strongly of spices and the nearby sea as they discuss the nature of the Spiritual Realm.

Geranid uses calipers to take physical measurements of the flamespren as they flicker over the burning logs. She notices that the spren behave erratically and change shape constantly until she records their dimensions. Once a specific measurement is written down on her board, the measured spren immediately freezes in that state. The spren remains locked in its current luminosity and size as long as the notation exists. Ashir leaves the room and calls out measurements for Geranid to record as a test of this phenomenon.

The experiment confirms that the spren only stabilize when Geranid actually writes down a measurement she intends to keep. Erasing the notation from the board causes the flamespren to immediately return to its variable, pulsing form. Ashir realizes that the act of observation and recording somehow traps the spren by defining its physical form. He suggests that this breakthrough could fundamentally alter their understanding of spren and fabrial technology. Geranid returns to her calculations while Ashir prepares a sugary snack to celebrate her discovery.



### 1-9: Death Wears White
Szeth-son-son-Vallano kills two palace guards and bursts into the feast hall of King Hanavanar in Jah Keved. He wears white clothing and absorbs Stormlight from gemstones to power his supernatural abilities. Szeth wields a Shardblade to slaughter the guests and guards, ignoring their screams while mourning his own actions. He uses a Basic Lashing to toss a dining table across the room, crushing those in its path. Szeth follows his masters' commands to cause a public massacre that leaves the lighteyes of Jah Keved in terror.

King Hanavanar attempts to escape but is blocked by furniture Szeth has Lashed to the doorways. The king reveals a trap, summoning his own Shardblade as two other Shardbearers and soldiers with half-shard shields enter. These fabrials allow common soldiers to block Szeth's Shardblade, forcing him to change his tactics. Szeth dismisses his Blade and uses his hands to Lash dozens of soldiers toward the ceiling. He moves through the room with inhuman speed, using the martial art of kammar to incapacitate his opponents.

Szeth infuses a massive stone from the palace wall with multiple Lashings and hurls it into one of the Shardbearers. The impact shatters the man's Plate and kills him instantly, while the second Shardbearer is tossed aside by a weightless tabletop. Szeth summons his Shardblade to finish the second armored warrior before cornering the king. Hanavanar begs for his life, but Szeth declares himself to be death and drives his Blade through the king's head. The assassin leaves the blood-soaked hall, having successfully eliminated his target and many innocent witnesses.
