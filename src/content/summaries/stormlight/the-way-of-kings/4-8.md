### 4: THE SHATTERED PLAINS
Kaladin remains in a slave cage as Tvlakv’s caravan travels toward the Shattered Plains. The windspren Sylphrena, appearing as a tiny mist-like feminine figure, questions Kaladin about why he does not cry like the other slaves. When Tvlakv gets lost, he asks Kaladin for directions, but Kaladin responds by snatching and shredding the slaver’s map into confetti. A highstorm strikes the caravan, forcing the slaves to huddle in the leaking wagon while Tvlakv hides in a reinforced shelter. After the storm passes, the caravan continues until they reach a vantage point overlooking the massive Alethi warcamps.

Kaladin observes ten distinct circular army encampments bivouacked within large, crater-like rock formations. He identifies the deep blue banners of House Kholin, featuring a stylized sword standing before a crown. Beyond the camps lies the Shattered Plains, an enormous expanse of rock split by a network of deep, jagged chasms. The other slaves express hope that they will find better treatment or wages under Alethi law in the warcamps. Kaladin remains skeptical, recalling how every lighteyed officer he has known proved to be corrupt.

He reflects on his past in Amaram’s army and the four years that have passed since he joined the military. Despite his cynicism, Kaladin feels a lingering desire to hold a spear and fight as a soldier again. Sylphrena points toward the horizon, sensing the presence of many people nearby. The wagons descend the final incline toward the warcamp of Highprince Sadeas. Kaladin prepares to face his new life as a slave in the heart of the Vengeance Pact’s war.





### 5: HERETIC
Shallan Davar meets Jasnah Kholin in Kharbranth and finds the heretic princess to be a stately, beautiful woman in her thirties. Jasnah is accompanied by King Taravangian of Kharbranth, a kind-looking elderly man in orange and white robes. During an introductory interview, Jasnah rigorously evaluates Shallan’s knowledge of music, logic, and history. Shallan admits to major deficiencies in her education, particularly in history, which Jasnah considers the most important literary subart. Despite Shallan’s persistence, Jasnah initially expresses disappointment and notes that Shallan is the twelfth prospective ward she has interviewed this year.

The group arrives at a hallway where a massive stone boulder has fallen from the ceiling, trapping the king’s granddaughter in a room. Jasnah uses her Soulcaster, a fabrial jewelry piece consisting of three large gemstones on a hand-mounting, to address the obstruction. She presses her hand against the rock, and the smokestone gem glows fiercely as she transforms the entire boulder into a burst of dense black smoke. The explosive transformation clears the doorway, allowing the king to rescue the trapped child and nursemaids. Shallan observes that Jasnah’s Soulcaster is exceptionally powerful, capable of any transformation unlike more common, limited fabrials.

After the rescue, Jasnah informs Shallan that she cannot accept her as a ward due to her lack of academic foundation. Shallan attempts to show Jasnah her artistic portfolio, but the princess dismisses visual arts as mere frivolity. Jasnah departs for the Palanaeum, leaving a frustrated and desperate Shallan behind in the palace corridor. Shallan resolves not to leave, as her family’s survival depends on her gaining access to Jasnah’s inner circle. She reveals her true mission is to steal Jasnah’s functional Soulcaster to replace the broken one that formerly sustained House Davar’s wealth.





### 6: BRIDGE FOUR
Tvlakv sells Kaladin to Highprince Sadeas’s army, where a lighteyed woman named Brightness Hashal inspects the slaves. Kaladin tries to lie and claim he is a murderer to get into the infantry, but Tvlakv reveals he is a branded deserter. He is assigned to Bridge Four under Sergeant Gaz, a scarred man with one eye who commands the bridge-carrying crews. Bridge crews are low-status laborers who manually carry massive wooden bridges across the plateaus of the Shattered Plains. Kaladin is forced to run barefoot and without a protective vest as his crew hauls a forty-foot bridge during an army march.

The bridge run is a grueling ordeal that leaves Kaladin’s shoulders bleeding and his side aching from extreme physical exertion. They reach the battle zone where the Alethi army prepares to engage the Parshendi, a race of muscular soldiers with marbled red and black skin. The bridge crews are ordered to charge the final chasm while Parshendi archers fire volleys of arrows directly at them. These archers use short, recurve bows to slaughter the exposed bridgemen before they can set their bridges in place. Many bridgemen die in the initial volleys, and Kaladin’s leathery-faced friend is killed by three arrows to the neck.

Kaladin survives the charge and helps push the bridge into position despite the carnage and his own exhaustion. After the battle, he finds his windspren Sylphrena, who reveals that her full name is actually Sylphrena. Kaladin salvages a leather vest and sandals from a dead comrade to protect himself for the return journey. He realizes that Gaz intentionally placed him in the most dangerous front row of the bridge to ensure his death. The remnants of the crew lift the bridge to return to camp, and Kaladin identifies Bridge Four as his final, most terrible torment.





### 7: ANYTHING REASONABLE
Shallan waits for Jasnah in the Veil, a grand chamber serving as the entrance to the massive Palanaeum library. A master-servant informs her that a chit of admittance to the library costs a staggering one thousand sapphire broams. While waiting in a reading alcove, Shallan uses charcoal pencils to draw a detailed Memory of the Kharbranth docks and the sailor Yalb. Her act of creation attracts several creationspren, small silvery spren that take the shapes of nearby objects like urns or wheels. She completes a masterpiece sketch of Jasnah Soulcasting the boulder, capturing the princess’s proud stance and the glowing fabrial.

Shallan writes a persuasive letter to Jasnah, arguing that her self-taught nature proves her tenacity and potential as a student. A young, handsome ardent named Brother Kabsal enters the alcove while looking for Jasnah. Kabsal discovers Shallan’s drawings and expresses genuine admiration for her artistic talent and skill with charcoal. He offers to help her by applying a protective lacquer to the sketches using a specialized bulb sprayer. The two share a friendly conversation where Kabsal jokes about dancing on tables to put Shallan at ease.

Kabsal explains that he has been trying to gain an audience with Jasnah to discuss her religious heresy. Before leaving, he asks Shallan what she is fond of, and she honestly replies that she likes jam. He promises to bring her jam during their next meeting and departs into the dark corridors of the Conclave. Shallan finishes lacquering her pictures and packs them away as Jasnah finally returns to the alcove. Jasnah appears exhausted and displeased, her high-necked dress still dusty from the day’s work in the palace.





### 8: NEARER THE FLAME
Jasnah finds Shallan in her alcove and initially orders her to leave the Conclave and never return. Shallan retreats in tears, but a servant soon catches her and says that Jasnah wishes to speak with her again. Jasnah apologizes for her earlier anger, admitting that she has been under significant strain and should not have punished Shallan’s persistence. She discovers Shallan’s letter and reads the argument regarding Shallan’s self-taught education and personal struggle. Jasnah finds the logic and rhetoric of the letter impressive and reevaluates her decision to dismiss the girl.

The princess inspects Shallan’s satchel and examines her detailed notebook of natural history sketches. She is impressed that Shallan pursues scholarship for its own sake rather than just for social status or wealth. Jasnah finally accepts Shallan as her ward, instructing her to begin her education and assist with research starting the next morning. Shallan sends Yalb to fetch her trunk from the ship while she prepares for her new life in the Conclave. She meets Yalb on the city streets, where he reveals he has been winning spheres by cheating guards at cards.

Yalb helps Shallan purchase a collection of history and philosophy books from a Thaylen merchant named Artmyrn. Through a clever ruse, Yalb forces the merchant to lower the price of the books from ten emerald broams to less than three. Shallan settles into her new reading alcove and begins an intensive period of study to catch up on her education. She reflects on the gravity of her situation and the secret she carries, which she identifies as her father’s Soulcaster. Despite the successful first step of her plan, Shallan feels the mounting pressure of her mission to steal Jasnah’s working fabrial.
