### 1-2: Nan Balat
Nan Balat sits on his family's porch in Jah Keved pulling the legs off small crabs to soothe his internal aches. He walks with a cane due to a badly broken leg that a surgeon nearly amputated following years of his father’s abuse. The Davar estate is covered in thick vines and greenery that retreat whenever anyone steps near them. Balat interacts with his axehound Scrak, a six-legged, smooth-skinned creature with a hard carapace and dual voices. Scrak plays with a songling, a peaked disc-shaped animal with four arms used to scrape rhythms on its shell.

Balat reflects on his sister Shallan, who recently messaged via spanreed to confirm she is now Jasnah Kholin’s ward. Shallan intends to steal a Soulcaster, a legendary fabrial device capable of transforming materials, from the highprincess. Balat feels like a coward for staying behind while his shy sister carries out a dangerous heist against a powerful woman. He recalls his deceased eldest brother Helaran, who was the only one to stand up to their brutal father. His father is also dead, leaving the siblings to manage a fractured household of emotional and physical cripples.

Balat pulls the shell off a crab and ponders how creatures seem dead once they stop moving their limbs. His younger brother Wikim approaches him on the porch, having emerged from a recent bout of deep despair. Wikim informs Balat that a significant new problem has arisen for the family to deal with. The brothers walk toward the mansion to address the unspecified crisis mentioned by Wikim. Balat abandons his animal-killing habit for the moment to focus on the urgent news from his sibling.





### 1-3: The Glory of Ignorance
Szeth-son-son-Vallano sits in a grimy tavern in Ironsway, Bavland, with beer soaking into his fraying trousers. He is currently owned by Took, a transient miner who purchased Szeth for a sack of seeds from a farmer. Took orders Szeth to jump and then to cut his own arm with a dirty boot knife to impress local miners. Szeth explains he is Truthless and forbidden from taking his own life, though he must obey all other commands. The miners view Szeth as a Shin slave, noting his large round eyes and lack of facial hair.

Szeth is a Surgebinder and a Windrunner who possesses a Shardblade, a magical weapon that turns his eyes pale green. He hides his identity as the "Assassin in White" who killed King Gavilar Kholin five years ago in Alethkar. After the assassination, the Parshendi discarded Szeth’s Oathstone, a simple rock that dictates who owns him. A merchant named Avado found Szeth and eventually traded him away into a cycle of menial labor. Szeth keeps a mysterious black sphere given to him by Gavilar hidden away in a secret location in Jah Keved.

Took leaves the tavern and collapses in the street from intoxication, prompting Szeth to attempt to carry him. A group of footpads ambushes them and kills Took by stabbing a knife into his neck. The footpads discover Szeth's Oathstone in Took's pouch and realize it is a significant object. Szeth informs the thieves that whoever holds the stone is his new master and must be obeyed in all things. The lead footpad takes possession of the stone and realizes he now controls a powerful and submissive Shin servant.





### 12: Unity
King Elhokar Kholin leads a grand procession onto the Shattered Plains to hunt a massive greatshell called a chasmfiend. Highprince Dalinar Kholin rides beside the king on Gallant, a massive and intelligent black Ryshadium stallion. Adolin and Renarin Kholin follow closely, with Adolin wearing blue-painted Shardplate while Renarin remains unarmored. Highprince Sadeas joins the hunt in his red Shardplate, boasting about his recent successes in capturing gemhearts from Parshendi plateaus. Dalinar experiences a haunting vision during a highstorm, leading to rumors of madness among the Alethi armies.

Elhokar challenges Dalinar to a race to the top of a forty-foot-high rock formation overlooking the Plains. Dalinar uses the enhanced strength of his slate-grey Shardplate to leap and climb the stone face with extreme speed. He briefly hesitates near the summit after hearing a mysterious voice command him to "unite them." Elhokar wins the race and reaches the top first, feeling a surge of triumph that attracts golden gloryspren. From the vantage point, they see the hunting pavilion and the permanent bridges used for troop crossings.

The king expresses constant paranoia about assassins, though he remains eager to prove his courage in battle. Adolin manages scout reports and orders extra outriders to prevent a Parshendi ambush from the rear. Dalinar explains to Adolin that the highprinces are currently divided and treat the war as a competitive game. He believes the Alethi must leave the Shattered Plains and return home to stabilize their fractured kingdom. The procession crosses the final chasm and arrives at the designated hunting site to prepare for the beast’s arrival.





### 13: Ten Heartbeats
A massive chasmfiend with violet-ink carapace and four wicked foreclaws unexpectedly climbs onto the viewing plateau instead of the bait plateau. The creature smashes a permanent bridge, sending attendants and scribes falling into the depths of the chasm. Dalinar summons his Shardblade, Oathbringer, which takes exactly ten heartbeats to coalesce from mist into his hand. King Elhokar summons his own Blade, Sunraiser, and charges the monster despite the danger to the unarmed guests. Dalinar and Adolin mount their Ryshadium horses and begin circling the beast to strike at its fourteen legs.

Adolin shears through multiple legs while Elhokar recklessly charges the chasmfiend's chest to seek a kill. The king’s saddle girth snaps during a sharp turn, throwing him to the ground and causing his Blade to vanish. Highprince Sadeas arrives and fires massive steel bolts from a grandbow, a fabrial-enhanced weapon, into the monster's head. Dalinar’s horse Gallant is struck by the creature's tail, tossing Dalinar onto the rocky ground and forcing his Blade to puff to mist. Renarin rides toward the danger to help his father, but Dalinar bellows at him to retreat to safety.

As the chasmfiend prepares to crush the grounded king with a massive claw, Dalinar charges forward without a weapon. Dalinar slides under the falling limb and catches the enormous claw with his bare hands, holding it back with Plate-enhanced strength. The weight causes Dalinar's armor to crack and leak Stormlight, but he keeps the king from being flattened. Adolin and Elhokar deliver final strikes that kill the beast, causing its many green eyes to blacken and shrivel. Elhokar reaches into the monster's chest and rips out a lumpy emerald gemheart the size of a man's head.





### 14: Payday
Kaladin wakes Bridge Four early and uses his authority as bridgeleader to force the men out of their barrack. He punches Moash in the stomach and carries him outside to demonstrate that he will no longer tolerate insubordination. Kaladin announces that the crew will begin morning training runs with the bridge to build endurance for future battles. The bridgemen, including a seven-foot-tall Horneater named Rock and a Makabaki man named Sigzil, mock Kaladin’s plan. Sergeant Gaz informs the men that bridgeleaders only have authority in the field, leading the crew to ignore Kaladin.

Kaladin tracks Gaz into an alley and bribes him with a weekly diamond mark to stay out of his way. He tells Gaz that he will continue to pay the bribe as long as he is allowed to manage the crew without interference. Returning to the lumberyard, Kaladin takes a heavy wooden plank and begins running laps in front of the barracks. He pushes himself to exhaustion for hours, attracting a crowd of stunned workers and silent Bridge Four members. Kaladin assigns the men to clean pots after their afternoon bridge duty, stating that the last one to arrive must do the work.

Syl, the windspren, tells Kaladin that she is changing and now understands complex concepts like lying and death. She reveals that she remembers bringing Kaladin a poisonous leaf when he was considering suicide at the Honor Chasm. Syl admits she cannot remember much of her life from before she met Kaladin a year ago. Kaladin asks Syl to watch over him at night to ensure Gaz does not attempt to have him killed while he sleeps. He reflects that true authority comes from the men who choose to follow him rather than from a military rank.
