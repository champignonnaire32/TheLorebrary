### Chapter 15: The Decoy
Adolin oversees the cleanup after the chasmfiend attack while the king brushes off the heavy casualties. Dalinar reflects on the war's stagnation and how the focus has shifted from vengeance to competing for gemhearts. He notes that the Parshendi are being depleted, but the Alethi are distracted by greed and sport. In the pavilion, Dalinar and Sadeas manipulate Highprince Vamah to ensure he continues relying on the king's Soulcasters for lumber. This joint effort shows a lingering, albeit strained, partnership between the two powerful highprinces.

Adolin questions his father about the king’s paranoia regarding a broken saddle girth that threw Elhokar during the hunt. Dalinar inspects the leather strap and admits that it appears to have been sliced to weaken it. He orders Adolin to investigate the grooms and the leather's history while doubling the king's guard. Adolin expresses his deep distrust of Sadeas, but Dalinar insists that Sadeas is one of the few who truly cares for the king's safety. Dalinar then takes Adolin aside to reveal the deeper history of their family and the origin of their current conflict.

Dalinar explains that Sadeas acted as Gavilar's decoy during the night of the assassination, nearly sacrificing his life. He admits that he and Sadeas both feel a shared guilt for failing to protect the king. Dalinar then reveals a secret blood-written message from Gavilar that urges him to find the most important words a man can say. This message led Dalinar to the ancient text The Way of Kings, which he now listens to in secret. Finally, the group crosses back over the chasm using a mobile bridge carried by a weary crew of slaves and criminals.



### Chapter 16: Cocoons
Seven and a half years ago in Hearthstone, young Kaladin and Laral discuss his father's plan to send him to Kharbranth. Lirin wants his son to become a master surgeon, but Kaladin is captivated by stories of heroic soldiers and Shardblades. Laral, the citylord's daughter, encourages his dreams of martial glory while the two play on the rocky ridges. Kaladin's younger brother Tien finds a uniquely patterned stone and gives it to Kaladin to cheer him up. The group spends the afternoon hunting lurgs, which are small amphibians that spin protective cocoons in rock crevices.

Later, they encounter a group of local boys who are taking a break from worming the lavis fields. The boys mock Kaladin for his clean clothes and high nahn status, as his family does not labor in the sun. Jost, the leader of the boys, challenges Kaladin to a quarterstaff fight to prove his father's military honor. Kaladin initially refuses but is goaded into the duel when Laral watches him with expectation. During the fight, Kaladin feels a sudden and unnatural surge of martial clarity before a moment of hesitation leads to his defeat.

Laral walks away in disappointment, leaving a bruised and shamed Kaladin on the ground. Tien gives Kaladin another stone to comfort him, this one featuring a pattern that resembles a smiling face. Upon returning home, Kaladin finds his father sitting in the dark surgery room looking exhausted and somber. Lirin reveals that Citylord Wistiow has died, leaving behind a collection of spheres to pay for Kaladin’s medical education. This news effectively seals Kaladin's fate, forcing him to accept the life of a surgeon despite his brief taste of combat.



### Chapter 17: A Bloody, Red Sunset
Kaladin visits an old apothecary in the warcamp to purchase medical supplies like bandages and antiseptic for his bridge crew. He uses his meager wages to buy bleached cloth and knobweed sap, though he must settle for cheaper larmic mucus. The apothecary recognizes Kaladin's training and notes that he is far more knowledgeable than a typical bridgeman. Kaladin observes the bustling camp life and reflects on the discord between the ten rival Alethi highprinces. He returns to the barracks just as the horns sound, signaling a new chasmfiend pupating on a nearby plateau.

Bridge Four charges onto the Shattered Plains, carrying their heavy mobile bridge through the heat and dust. Kaladin decides to take the dangerous deathpoint position at the very front to protect his men and set an example. During the final assault, he experiences a strange surge of energy and survives a volley of Parshendi arrows that kills those around him. The bridge is placed, and the Alethi cavalry charges across to engage the Parshendi warriors in a brutal skirmish. Kaladin immediately begins pulling wounded bridgemen from the battlefield, ignoring the standard orders to leave them behind to die.

He sets up a makeshift triage in a rock cleft and uses his surgeon's skills to treat severe wounds. Kaladin cauterizes a man's side with a heated knife and sews a leg wound with needle and gut. He manages to save Leyten and Hobber, though Gadol dies after whispering a haunting prophecy about the land's destruction. To get the survivors back to camp, Kaladin bribes Gaz with a sapphire mark and threatens to kill him if he interferes. The chapter ends with a bloody sunset as the victorious army returns, and Kaladin realizes he must rely on human greed to keep his men alive.



### Chapter 18: Highprince of War
Adolin consults with leatherworkers who confirm that the king's saddle girth was intentionally sliced to cause a fall. Janala, his current courting interest, expresses her frustration with the drab uniforms Dalinar forces his officers to wear. Adolin is distracted by the war horns and feels frustrated when his father chooses not to race Sadeas for a gemheart. He then visits the temple to speak with the ardent Kadash, a former soldier who knew Dalinar in his youth. Kadash suggests that Dalinar's visions are likely a psychological manifestation of war trauma and survivor's guilt.

Meanwhile, Dalinar visits the king's palace and reviews the financial ledgers with his scribe, Teshav. He learns that several highprinces are failing to pay their Soulcasting fees, indicating a decline in royal authority. Dalinar speaks with the king on a balcony, warning him that the Alethi must unify or face internal collapse. He proposes that Elhokar name him Highprince of War to give him legal authority over all ten armies. Elhokar is skeptical and reminds Dalinar that such a move could trigger a rebellion among the other highprinces.

The king challenges Dalinar to prove that the others are willing to follow his lead before granting the title. Dalinar promises to protect Elhokar and admits his own failures in protecting Gavilar six years ago. As Dalinar and Renarin ride back to their camp, they notice a massive highstorm approaching much faster than predicted. They are forced to take shelter in a common barrack filled with nervous soldiers and servants. The storm hits with full force, and Dalinar immediately slips into a powerful and public trance.



### Chapter 19: Starfalls
Dalinar enters a vision where he is a simple man named Heb living in a primitive farmhouse in Natanatan. He finds a young girl named Seeli and her mother Taffa hiding in the dark from invisible monsters. These creatures, known as Midnight Essence, are smooth-skinned predators that hunt by smell and bleed black smoke. Dalinar uses a fire poker to fight two of the beasts, experiencing the Thrill of battle with incredible precision. He manages to slay the monsters, but he realizes that the entire village is under attack by a larger pack.

He leads the woman and child toward a river to hide their scent, but they are cornered against a cliff. Just as the creatures are about to overwhelm them, two figures in glowing Shardplate fall from the sky. These are Knights Radiant, and their armor glows with blue and amber light while trailing wisps of Stormlight. The male knight effortlessly slaughters the monsters with a Shardblade that shears through them like paper. The female knight uses a glowing gemstone to magically heal Dalinar’s wounds and regrow the woman’s torn flesh.

The knights explain that they are the watchers of Alethela, preparing the world for an impending Desolation. The male knight invites Dalinar to join them at Urithiru before flying off into the night sky. As the vision fades, the voice of the Almighty speaks through the form of Taffa, urging Dalinar to unite the highprinces. The voice explicitly tells Dalinar to trust Sadeas and to act with honor to ensure the kingdom's survival. Dalinar wakes up in the barrack, surrounded by his men, and resolves to follow the cryptic instructions of the vision.
