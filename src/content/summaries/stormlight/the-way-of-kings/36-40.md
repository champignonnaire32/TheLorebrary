### 36: THE LESSON
Shallan sits in a luxuriously heated bathing chamber in Kharbranth while Jasnah Kholin soaks in the water. The young ward reads King Gavilar’s personal account regarding his first meeting with the self-sufficient Parshendi people. Shallan considers swapping her broken Soulcaster, an ancient fabrial used for transmutation, for the working one Jasnah keeps in a nearby pouch. Jasnah wakes and explains that her father Gavilar may have sought a treaty with the Parshendi to secure their Shardblades. Despite the opportunity, Shallan fails to make the exchange in the bathing room and instead massages soap into Jasnah’s hair.

Jasnah takes Shallan into the dangerous streets of Kharbranth at night to conduct a practical philosophy exercise. They walk down a dark side street known for recent murders and Jasnah briefly reveals her glowing Soulcaster gemstones. Four footpads armed with knives corner the two women in a narrow, muddy alleyway. Jasnah removes her glove to reveal the blaring light of her Soulcaster and touches the lead attacker. The man instantly transforms into a column of fire, while a second attacker is turned into a kneeling statue of quartz.

Jasnah releases twin bolts of Stormlight that strike the remaining two fleeing men, transmuting them into grease-scented smoke. The effort cracks the smokestone gemstone on the Soulcaster, rendering that specific transmutation impossible for now. Upon returning to their rooms, Shallan finally swaps her nonfunctional device for the princess’s working Soulcaster. Shallan brushes Jasnah’s hair for fifty strokes while concealing the stolen artifact in her safehand sleeve. Jasnah locks the broken fake in her jewelry case without noticing the theft before going to sleep.





### 37: SIDES
Thirteen-year-old Tien shows his brother Kaladin a rock that changes colors from red to green depending on the angle of the light. Lirin prepares to dine with Brightlord Roshone at the manor house and allows fifteen-year-old Kaladin to accompany him. During the carriage ride, Lirin reveals he considers moving the family to Kharbranth or Kholinar to escape Roshone’s local influence. Lirin enters the manor and sits at Roshone’s table, asserting his rights as a member of the second nahn. Roshone demands the return of the spheres allegedly stolen from the late Brightlord Wistiow, but Lirin refuses to capitulate.

Roshone threatens to ruin Lirin’s reputation with any future employers, yet Lirin counters that the townspeople still secretly support his family. Kaladin is sent to the kitchens where he encounters Laral, who has dyed her hair black and wears a traditional lighteyed dress. Roshone’s son Rillir insults Kaladin and Laral orders her former friend to fetch them a meal like a common servant. Kaladin refuses to serve them and instead denounces Rillir’s arrogance before returning to the dining nook. After the meal, Kaladin tells his father that he has decided to become a surgeon instead of a soldier.

Lirin admits to Kaladin that he did indeed take the spheres from Wistiow while the man was dying and mentally incapacitated. He explains that he committed the theft to ensure Kaladin would have the funds necessary for a proper medical education. Kaladin is shocked by the revelation but chooses to keep the secret from his younger brother Tien. He informs his father that he will use the stolen spheres to travel to Kholinar and study the ways of the lighteyes. The boy decides to start using his full name, Kaladin, as he prepares for his future as a man.





### 38: ENVISAGER
Kaladin suffers through a delirious fever within the Bridge Four barracks following his survival of the highstorm. He experiences hallucinations of his childhood and sees himself as a fallen spearman among piles of corpses. Deathspren, which are small black creatures with glowing red eyes, cluster around his body in the dark room. Syl stands on Kaladin’s chest as a tiny figure of pure white light and wards the deathspren off with a glowing sword. Kaladin briefly awakens to see lines of wounded men and fears he is being sold as a corpse to Highprince Sadeas.

Teft enters the barracks at midday and finds Kaladin covered in bloody bandages and sweating from the fever. The older bridgeman observes that the rest of the army views Kaladin’s survival as a miraculous judgment from the Stormfather. Teft worries that a lighteyes might poison Kaladin to end the political embarrassment of his survival. After Skar leaves to eat, Teft pulls three small diamond spheres from his pocket to test a theory. He presses the glowing spheres into Kaladin’s limp hand and watches for a reaction.

Kaladin gasps and draws the Stormlight out of the spheres, causing the glow in his hand to vanish. Faint wisps of white light rise from Kaladin’s skin like steam and move toward his wounds. The light knits several cuts together on his chest and the redness of his infection begins to fade. Teft realizes that the ancient legends of the Knights Radiant are true and weeps upon the stone floor. He retrieves the now-dim spheres and decides to keep Kaladin’s ability a secret while providing more Stormlight for healing.





### 39: BURNED INTO HER
Shallan sits in her windowless stone chamber and sketches the violent deaths of the footpads using charcoal. She recreates the image of the man becoming fire and the others turning into crystal and smoke. The young woman feels immense guilt for witnessing the killings and for stealing Jasnah’s Soulcaster. She reviews various philosophical frameworks, including the Philosophy of Aspiration, to determine if Jasnah’s actions were moral. Shallan realizes she has accidentally drawn a dead man in fine clothing lying on a rug, which causes her to panic and crumple the paper.

A master-servant delivers a flashing spanreed to Shallan, indicating an incoming communication from her home in Jah Keved. Shallan uses a secret code involving a physical complaint to verify she is speaking with her brother Nan Balat. She informs him that she has successfully stolen the Soulcaster but cannot leave Kharbranth immediately without raising suspicion. Nan Balat warns her that the dangerous men who provided their father with the original fabrial have returned to ask for her. Shallan burns the transcript of the conversation in the hearth to prevent anyone from discovering her treachery.

A palace maid enters to clean the rooms and brings Shallan a basket of bread and bluebar jam from Kabsal. Shallan secures the stolen Soulcaster inside her safepouch to keep it on her person while the maid works. She retreats to the palace gardens and finds a secluded nook within a maze of "plated stone" shalebark. Shallan dons the Soulcaster and commands a piece of shalebark to become smoke, crystal, and fire. The device fails to activate, leaving Shallan frustrated and determined to find instructions within the Palanaeum.





### 40: EYES OF RED AND BLUE
Kaladin walks out of the barracks into the sunlight ten days after being hung in the highstorm. His wounds have mostly healed into scabs or disappeared entirely, and he feels a strange residual strength. He finds the men of Bridge Four practicing and Moash nearly causes a pile-up when he notices Kaladin is alive. The bridgemen crowd around him in celebration, calling him "Captain" while Lopen and Rock assist him. Kaladin counts thirty-three men and learns that only two were lost during the runs he missed while unconscious.

A horn sounds and Bridge Four is called to duty for a new plateau assault against the Parshendi. Kaladin joins Lopen and Dabbid to carry water, as he is still too weak to carry the bridge itself. From the edge of a cliff, Kaladin watches the Alethi spearmen fight Parshendi warriors who have red-orange armor growing from their skin. He observes that the Parshendi fight in independent pairs and sing rhythmic melodies throughout the combat. Kaladin deduces that Sadeas uses unarmored bridgemen as bait to distract Parshendi archers from his trained soldiers.

Back at the camp, the men gather around a fire and wait for Rock to finish cooking a large pot of stew. Maps presents Rock with a gift of a straight razor, a steel mirror, and beard soap to thank him for his service. Rock is moved to tears by the gesture and retreats into the barracks while the men serve themselves the meal. Syl appears as a blue flame on Kaladin’s shoulder but offers no explanation for her absence during his recovery. Kaladin feels a deep melancholy as he realizes that his survival does not change the fact that bridgemen are destined to die.
