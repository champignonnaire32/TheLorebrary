### 25: THE BUTCHER
In a flashback to seven years ago, thirteen-year-old Kaladin walks through the snowy town of Hearthstone. He overhears townspeople gossiping that his father, the surgeon Lirin, stole spheres from the recently deceased Citylord Wistiow. Kaladin confronts his mother Hesina about the rumors, but she explains that people fear Lirin's medical knowledge. While helping his mother chip crem from buildings, Kaladin admits he is tempted by the life of a soldier. Lirin interrupts them to announce the sudden arrival of the new Citylord, Brightlord Roshone.

The town gathers to witness a grand procession featuring a black carriage and armed soldiers. Roshone exits the carriage wearing a traditional warrior's takama, which is a long straight skirt, and looks upon the crowd with visible distaste. Lirin steps forward to welcome the new ruler, but Roshone immediately insults him. Roshone blames the surgeon for Wistiow's death and calls Hearthstone a miserable pit before retreating to his carriage. The Citylord's immediate departure leaves the townspeople anxious and whispering about the future.

Kaladin sits on a rain barrel and watches the carriage turn back toward the manor. Lirin returns to his family with a troubled expression, comparing the encounter to a game of breakneck. He explains that they have thrown "the butcher," a result where winning depends on the actions of others. Tien, Kaladin's younger brother, joins them and tries to find wonder in his collection of rocks despite the tension. The chapter ends with Kaladin feeling the weight of the uncertain conflict beginning with the new lighteyes.

### 26: STILLNESS
Dalinar Kholin listens to his scribe Litima read excerpts from the ancient text The Way of Kings. He stands before maps of the Shattered Plains while struggling with self-doubt regarding his recent visions. His son Renarin stands nearby, but his eldest son Adolin remains absent following their public argument. When horns sound signaling a chasmfiend sighting, Dalinar decides to lead a plateau run to improve troop morale. He dons his Shardplate, a suit of interlocking magical armor that enhances his physical strength and speed.

Highprince Sadeas arrives at the staging grounds and insists on accompanying the army to conduct his investigation. During the long march, Sadeas mocks Dalinar for following the Codes and reading philosophical books. Upon reaching the contested plateau, Dalinar and Adolin summon their Shardblades and leap across the chasm. They land amidst the Parshendi archers and begin slaughtering the enemy to secure the landing zone. Dalinar experiences the Thrill of battle but is suddenly overcome by a wave of intense revulsion for the killing.

Despite his sickness, Dalinar continues fighting until the Alethi cavalry crosses the bridges to finish the assault. Adolin kills the chasmfiend pupating in its rocky chrysalis and extracts the valuable gemstone from its heart. Dalinar removes his helmet and wanders the battlefield, feeling exhausted and spiritually disconnected from the victory. He spots a Parshendi Shardbearer in glittering armor watching the Alethi from a distant plateau before retreating. The chapter concludes with Dalinar questioning his own sanity and the purpose of the ongoing war for vengeance.

### 27: CHASM DUTY
Kaladin visits an apothecary in the warcamps to sell the knobweed sap he harvested from the chasms. He discovers the apothecary is lying about the sap's value and threatens to expose the price-gouging scheme. The merchant pays Kaladin a sapphire mark, a high-value blue gemstone sphere, to keep the secret of the local harvest. Returning to the lumberyard, Kaladin learns that Bridge Four has been assigned to chasm duty as punishment. The crew descends fifty feet into the damp, mossy rifts to scavenge salvage and equipment from fallen soldiers.

While searching for gear, the men find the broken body of a bridgeman who committed suicide by jumping. Kaladin identifies a well-made spear in a pile of weapons and impulsively performs a complex fighting kata. The bridge crew watches in stunned silence as the slave displays the speed and grace of a master soldier. Deep in the rifts, they discover Parshendi corpses and realize the enemies have natural red carapace armor grown from their skin. Kaladin takes gemstones from the Parshendi beards and gathers Alethi boots and breastplates to meet his quota.

After the search, Kaladin leads the crew to the camp market and uses his sapphire mark to buy a cauldron and food. He organizes a fire and a meal of stew outside the barracks to encourage camaraderie among the men. Most of Bridge Four joins the circle to eat, share songs, and listen to Rock tell stories of the Horneater Peaks. Even the most cynical bridgemen, like Skar, eventually accept a bowl of stew and join the group. The next morning, the majority of the crew rises willingly to practice bridge maneuvers under Kaladin's command.

### 28: DECISION
Adolin watches a failed demonstration of a mobile siege bridge as the wooden supports collapse under the weight. Dalinar questions why Shardplate is used only for warfare instead of helping common laborers with their work. They interview the grooms Sadeas questioned about the king's saddle, but the men's defensive loyalty seems suspicious. Highprince Thanadal sends a messenger to cancel his meeting with Dalinar, officially refusing any alliance or joint assault. Frustrated and seeking clarity, Dalinar takes a warhammer and begins digging a latrine trench himself on the staging field.

Navani Kholin finds Dalinar working in the ditch and reminds him that he missed their scheduled appointment. Their conversation is interrupted by a spanreed signal from Jasnah Kholin, who is currently researching in Kharbranth. Jasnah asks Dalinar to recount his first encounter with the Parshendi and inquires about their early behavior. She sends a drawing of a chasmfiend via the conjoined reeds and reveals that ancient texts label it a Voidbringer. Jasnah warns that her mother Navani "bites" and declines to return to the Shattered Plains immediately.

After the communication ends, Dalinar admits to Navani that he has decided to abdicate his position as Highprince. He believes his potential madness makes him unfit to lead and wants Adolin to take over the Kholin house. Navani protests the decision, but Dalinar remains firm and orders her to leave his presence for propriety's sake. Alone and physically exhausted from the day's labor, he collapses to the floor while still wearing his Shardplate. The second part of the narrative concludes with Dalinar resolved to announce his retirement to the army within the week.
