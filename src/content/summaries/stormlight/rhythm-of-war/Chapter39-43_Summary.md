### Chapter 39: Invasion

Teft collapses during withdrawal-like symptoms, and Kaladin attempts to stabilize him before realizing other Radiants are also falling unconscious. Kaladin finds that his Gravitation lashings fail while his Adhesion powers remain functional, suggesting a partial suppression of his abilities. Navani receives a message from the Sibling via spanreed warning of an enemy invasion already inside the tower. She mobilizes runners and guards to secure the lower levels and alert the remaining military forces. Hundreds of Fused and Regals descend upon Urithiru, utilizing the Oathgates to bring in reinforcements and seizing control of the pillar room.

Kaladin carries Teft to his father’s clinic, where he finds several other unconscious Radiants being treated for mysterious seizures. He experiences severe anxiety and a near-breakdown before Syl helps him regain focus and alerts him that the enemy has captured the heart of the tower. Lirin attempts to dissuade Kaladin from fighting, arguing that any resistance would result in the family's execution and that the tower has already fallen. Kaladin realizes that all fabrials and spanreeds have stopped working, leaving the defenders isolated and without communication. He chooses to put down his surgeon's knife for the moment as he observes the massive invasion force occupying the city.

Navani meets with Dabbid, who reveals that the Sibling communicates through garnet veins in the tower walls. She releases Dabbid from his manacles and decides to move her operations to the map room on the second floor. The Sibling expresses fear of a Fused known as the Lady of Pains, who is attempting to corrupt the tower's heart. Navani learns that the enemy is using their own Light to activate specific tower defenses that were previously dormant. The Sibling instructs Navani that she must provide a great deal of Stormlight to activate a fail-safe defense. Navani prepares to lead her scholars in a desperate attempt to protect the Sibling from further corruption.

### Chapter 40: In for All

Navani arrives at the map room to find Roion and a group of officers, including the darkeyed Battalionlord Teofil, organizing a defense. Teofil reports that enemy stormform Regals are pouring in from the basement while Heavenly Ones secure the lower balconies. The officers confirm that every Radiant in the tower, except for Kaladin, has fallen into a coma-like state. Teofil explains that the singers have seized the Oathgates and are using them to flood the tower with reinforcements. Navani decides that holding the central rooms is impossible because Fused can move through stone and Regals can unleash lightning.

Navani orders an "in-for-all" maneuver to reclaim the crystal pillar in the basement and restore the Radiants' powers. Teofil accepts the command and begins organizing a withdrawal of troops from the upper floors to muster for a massive push. He informs Navani that the enemy targeted and killed the only non-Radiant Shardbearer in the tower to secure his Plate. Navani speaks privately with the Sibling through a garnet vein, learning that the spren has been hiding and pretending to be dead. The Sibling identifies the Lady of Pains as the Fused Raboniel, who is currently corrupting the tower's suppression field to target Radiants.

The Sibling explains that only a Bondsmith or a massive infusion of Stormlight can restart the tower's primary functions. Navani learns that the Lady of Pains intends to change the Sibling's mind and essence to serve the enemy's purposes. The Sibling mentions a fail-safe created by a past Bondsmith designed to protect the tower from human or singer interference. This protection requires a significant amount of Stormlight to activate a barrier around the pillar room. Navani promises to help and begins coordinating her scholars to gather as many gemstones as possible. She realizes the survival of the tower depends entirely on the success of Teofil’s bloody assault and her own technical intervention.

### Chapter 41: The Most Dangerous

Venli stays in the basement with Raboniel, who is inspecting murals and critiquing human artistic methods as inferior to singer traditions. Raboniel, known as the Lady of Wishes, displays an individual rhythm granted by Odium that implies quiet and deadly destruction. The Fused known as the Pursuer arrives, demanding permission to find and kill the Windrunner Kaladin Stormblessed. Raboniel grants him leave to claim his prize but orders him to show restraint toward other humans and capture the fallen Radiants. The Pursuer transforms into a ribbon of red light and departs for the upper levels, leaving his physical husk behind.

Raboniel examines a human fabrial and expresses admiration for the audacity and imagination of human inventions. She explains to Venli that the Fused have become stagnant and lack the ability to create anything new after millennia of immortality. Raboniel believes that the eternal war can only be ended through a partnership that exploits human creativity. Venli observes that Raboniel seems more interested in the tower's technological secrets than the actual military conquest. The Lady of Pains suggests that humans are prone to self-destruction if provided with the necessary tools or "swords."

Navani enters the ground floor of Urithiru and encounters the smell of burned human corpses and the sounds of unnatural thunder. She observes soldiers using metal pillars as lightning rods to attract the electric attacks of the stormform Regals. Teofil leads a fake rout to bait the enemy into an ambush, which successfully clears a path toward the basement steps. The human troops use crossbows to cut down the Regals before beginning a grueling push down the circular stairwell. Kaladin continues delivering blankets and food to refugees in the upper levels while avoiding the patrolling Fused and Voidspren. He encounters a singer who mocks his status as a surgeon but allows him to continue his rounds after a brief physical altercation.

### Chapter 42: Armor and Teeth

Navani follows the guidance of a spark of light from the Sibling to find a hidden topaz gemstone embedded in a wall. She leads her scholars in an attempt to infuse the gemstone with Stormlight using large bags of gemstones. The Thaylen scholars Vrandl and Ulvlk reveal a secret method of using tuning forks to create vibrations that siphoned Stormlight more efficiently. This technique allows Navani to quickly infuse the wall gem, which causes the stone to part and reveal a hidden circular chamber. Inside, they find a pedestal holding a large uninfused sapphire that serves as the control for the tower’s fail-safe.

The Sibling reports that the Lady of Pains is currently infusing the crystal pillar heart with Voidlight to corrupt the tower's essence. Fresh enemy troops continue to arrive through the Oathgate, which is being operated by the human traitor Moash using an Honorblade. Navani’s team works desperately to transfer Stormlight into the central sapphire, which begins to drink in massive amounts of power. The Sibling warns that if the enemy reaches this control gemstone, they could use it to corrupt the tower even further. Once the fail-safe is primed, the Sibling tells Navani she must destroy the fabrial and the glass globe attached to it.

Venli watches from the basement as the human soldiers, led by Teofil, fight their way down the central stairwell behind a canopy of shields. The humans maintain a relentless three-man-wide front, successfully repelling Heavenly Ones and Deepest Ones as they descend. The Pursuer suddenly materializes in the center of the human formation, shattering their lines with his rapid teleportation and carapace strikes. Regals unleash a massive bolt of lightning that destroys the staircase, causing Teofil and his lead men to fall to their deaths. Raboniel expresses relief as the slaughter concludes and returns to her work at the crystal pillar. Navani activates the shield, which expands as a solid blue barrier to protect the Sibling, before surrendering to the Fused.

### Chapter 43: Men and Monsters

Kaladin and his family spend twenty hours treating patients in the clinic as the news of Navani’s surrender reaches the upper floors. The messenger Alili confirms that the final push to restore the Radiants failed and that the tower is now under singer law. Kaladin realizes that without the Oathgates and spanreeds, the human armies are isolated across Roshar. Lirin argues that life under singer rule will be manageable for darkeyes and that they must focus on their duties as surgeons. Hesina worries that the lack of heating and water fabrials will soon make the tower unlivable for its thousands of inhabitants.

Kaladin feels a sense of impending doom and struggles with the realization that he is the only Radiant in the tower who remains awake. He visits Teft, who is shifting and muttering in his sleep but shows signs of responding more than the other comatose Radiants. Kaladin notes that Teft takes broth eagerly, leading him to believe that Windrunners might have a higher resistance to the tower's suppression. Lirin urges Kaladin to use his status as a surgeon to visit other fallen Radiants and check their vitals instead of resisting. Kaladin agrees to trust his father’s judgment for now, acknowledging that his own mind is often clouded by trauma and exhaustion.

Syl alerts Kaladin that a group of soldiers led by a Regal is methodically searching each residence on their floor. Kaladin suspects they are hunting for him specifically after hearing reports that "Stormblessed" is still active. Lirin decides to confront the search party himself to protect Kaladin and maintain the facade of a simple medical practice. Kaladin remains in the back rooms, observing the unconscious Stoneward and Teft as the enemy approaches. The occupation of Urithiru enters its first full day, with the human population disarmed and forced to submit to the new singer authorities. Kaladin prepares for the possibility of capture as the sound of marching boots draws closer to the clinic.
