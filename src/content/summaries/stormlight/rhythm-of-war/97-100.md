### Chapter 97: Freedom

Navani enters a feverish state of study, ignoring sleep and food to find the opposite of Voidlight. She applies mathematics and sound theory to identify the concept of destructive interference as a means to negate Light using opposing sound waves. After filing metal plates to match specific resonant frequencies, she creates a tone that successfully ejects Voidlight from gemstones. She discovers that producing this anti-sound requires specific Intent to align the hummed tones with the desired outcome. Raboniel observes the breakthrough and experiences a physical cringing in her gemheart when hearing the tone.

Navani uses a Thaylen vacuum tube to isolate Voidlight from the ambient rhythms of Roshar. She allows the Light to drift through the vacuum so it cannot hear Odium’s rhythm before touching a new diamond. By playing her anti-tone while the Light enters the gem, she creates anti-Voidlight, which appears as a violet-black energy. Raboniel screams in pain when touching the new Light, describing it as a wrongness that should not exist. Navani attempts to kill Raboniel by causing an explosion when the Fused tries to mix the opposing Lights.

Raboniel survives the blast and realizes Navani intended for the two substances to annihilate each other under pressure. The Fused uses a raysium dagger to channel the anti-Voidlight into her own brain-damaged daughter. This process permanently kills the younger Fused, ending her cycle of eternal rebirth and allowing her mother to grieve. Raboniel explains that she captured the tower specifically to find a way to grant her daughter this final mercy. She seizes Navani’s research notebook, intending to develop anti-Stormlight to permanently destroy all Radiant spren.



### Chapter: Interludes

Hesina studies maps of Tomat and realizes the singer invaders are actually repairing the city's ancient walls. Lirin joins her in their surgical chamber and expresses skepticism about whether Hesina’s ornery father is still alive. The couple argues over Lirin’s refusal to appreciate Kaladin’s role as a soldier defending the people. Hesina challenges Lirin to look at the common people in the infirmary who have painted shash glyphs on their foreheads. They encounter Noril, a man suffering from battle shock who previously attempted suicide.

Noril explains that he chooses to get up every day because he sees Kaladin continuing to fight despite his own depression. This revelation forces Lirin to acknowledge that Kaladin’s actions provide a type of healing his surgery cannot offer. Nearby, a young boy named Adin helps his father in a pottery shop while dreaming of becoming a Windrunner. Adin observes singer warforms marching through the market and maintains a secret stance of rebellion. His father encourages him to take pride in making plates, arguing that sloppy craftsmen make for sloppy soldiers.

Taravangian sits in his prison, struggling with his declining intelligence and his inability to manipulate Dalinar. He researches the soul-consuming sword Nightblood and notes its ability to feast on Investiture like a larkin. The Unmade Sja-anat appears in a mirror, revealing herself as a shadow with white holes for eyes. She warns Taravangian that Odium is vulnerable because his god-like power and human mind seek different goals. Sja-anat promises to lure Odium to Taravangian by providing gemstones containing her corrupted spren.



### Chapter 98: An Unwholesome Shade

Dalinar walks across a fresh battlefield in Emul, observing the mixed blood of singers and humans in the stone rifts. He analyzes the positions of the dead to reconstruct how the singer lines broke under the coalition's pressure. The scent of burning corpses reminds him of his greatest failure at the Rift in Alethkar. He realizes that while the coalition won the battle, the enemy strategically withdrew to maintain their hold on Jah Keved. Dalinar acknowledges his growing loathing for slaughter, a secret he hides from his Azish and Emuli allies.

The Mink informs Dalinar that the war in Emul is essentially finished, leaving only minor cleanup operations. Dalinar promises to prioritize the liberation of Alethkar and Herdaz once Urithiru is retaken from the Fused. Sigzil the Windrunner arrives with Stargyle, a Lightweaver who specializes in using color as a mnemonic for illusions. Stargyle projects a moving map of a shoreline camp belonging to the Tukari army. The illusion centers on a large pavilion where a single figure stands waiting for the coalition's approach.

The figure is identified as Ishar, an older Shin man with a white beard wearing simple blue robes. Ishar uses his powers to crystallize frost on the ground, forming a glyph that represents a question. Szeth becomes transfixed by the image, realizing for the first time that one of the Heralds appears as his own race. Dalinar confirms the man's identity using descriptions provided by Wit and prepares for a meeting. He orders further scouting missions while planning to consult Jasnah about the potential dangers of facing a Herald.



### Chapter 99: Not Bound

Jasnah works in a fortified command camp after receiving word of the coalition’s victory in Emul. She stays in a second-floor room equipped with fabrial traps and a pre-planned escape route to Shadesmar. Her interviews with the Heralds Ash and Taln reveal that much of her life's historical research was based on inaccuracies. She finds it difficult to reconcile the first-hand accounts of the Heralds with the biased records of ancient scholars. Jasnah realizes that relying on the past is insufficient when Odium can see the potential outcomes of the future.

Wit joins Jasnah in her room and shares a story about a gambler who lost a rigged game. He explains that even a god who sees the future can be defeated if the game is rigged to force a tie. Wit presents a formal contract designed for Dalinar’s upcoming contest of champions against Odium. The agreement dictates that Odium will retreat to Damnation for a millennium if he loses the duel. If Odium wins, he retains control of Roshar while the monarchs and Radiants must submit to his divine rule.

Wit ensures the contract includes a clause naming himself as a representative of Honor to protect him from Odium’s wrath. He explains that Rayse’s arrogance and desire for a meaningful victory make him vulnerable to this specific gamble. Jasnah remains paranoid about the high stakes, questioning if the fate of the world should rest on a single fight. Wit reassures her that the contest is truly about the hearts of the mortals who choose to fight. Jasnah admits her deep-seated lack of trust in herself but feels electrified by the mystery of Wit’s true nature.



### Chapter 100: Watchers at the Rim

Kaladin wakes in a dark chamber and realizes he tied his own hands to prevent himself from attacking his allies during nightmares. He hears the Sibling’s voice, which sounds incredibly frail and desperate as the Fused close in. The tower spren warns that the final node has been found near the large architectural model in the infirmary. The Sibling reveals that the Fused are planning to execute all of the captive Radiants. Kaladin wakes Teft and explains that they must move immediately to stop the impending massacre.

Teft agrees that their hand has been forced and notes that they can no longer follow Lirin’s path of non-violence. Dabbid arrives and reports that Rlain is shaken by a recent interaction with Venli on the fourth floor. Kaladin instructs Dabbid to tell Rlain to contact Queen Navani while he and Teft focus on the rescue. The group prepares to use Bridge Four uniforms that Dabbid scavenged from the tower. Kaladin orders Dabbid and Rlain to hide once the message is delivered to ensure their safety.

The rescue plan involves Teft and Lift infiltrating the infirmary to wake the comatose Radiants. Kaladin intends to create a loud distraction outside to draw the guards away and lure out the Pursuer. He instructs Teft to escape through the windows and return to Dalinar even if the mission fails. Teft demands that Kaladin promise to follow him rather than making a hopeless final stand against the trap. Kaladin swears the oath and prepares to face the Pursuer as the Sibling’s light continues to fade.
