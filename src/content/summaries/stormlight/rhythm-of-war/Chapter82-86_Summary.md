### Chapter 82: Knife

Veil confronts Mraize through a communication cube regarding his withholding of Restares's true identity as the Herald Kelek. Mraize instructs her to open a hidden compartment in the cube containing an intricate metal dagger with a gemstone on its pommel. He explains that the weapon is designed to trap the soul of a Herald within the gemstone rather than killing them. Veil initially resists the mission but Shallan takes control when she realizes replacing Kelek with an illusion could win Adolin's trial. Mraize promises to release Shallan from her apprenticeship and make her a full Ghostblood if she completes the task.

Adolin and Blended approach Kelek, who is acting as the High Judge in Lasting Integrity. Kelek appears as a short man with thinning hair and expresses deep cowardice regarding the war with Odium. He shies away from Adolin upon learning he is the son of the Bondsmith Dalinar Kholin. The Herald openly states that Honor is dead and that the only remaining option is to escape the world. Adolin petitions for a trial by witness, hoping to move the proceedings along quickly.

Kelek grants the request for a trial by witness despite objections from the honorspren leader Sekeir. The Herald schedules the trial to begin in two days at the public forum to provide entertainment for the city. Blended warns Adolin that a public trial is dangerous because he must now persuade an entire audience. Adolin realizes that Kelek has already decided on a guilty verdict before hearing any evidence. Blended leads Adolin away to begin intense preparations for the upcoming sham trial.

### Chapter 83: The Games of Men and Singers

Venli practices her Surgebinding on the fifteenth floor of Urithiru by sinking her hands into the stone floor. She accesses the combined tones of Cultivation and Odium to reshape the rock into a sculpture of her ancestors. The stones whisper impressions of the tower's history as she visualizes the listeners who chose to walk away from the ancient war. Venli realizes that her mother’s songs preserved the history of her people despite the listeners' transformation into dull forms. She finishes her practice and leaves the miniature stone trail of people as a testament to her progress.

Venli meets with Leshwi in the atrium of the tower to report that the Pursuer has located Kaladin's parents. Two nightform Regals, Urialin and Nistar, have been posted as guards at the Radiant infirmary as a warning. Leshwi explains that Raboniel is manipulating the Pursuer to exert authority over the other Fused. The Heavenly One admits that she fears Raboniel's grand plots more than the standard games of war between men and singers. Venli departs to check on the human refugees after feeling the ominous weight of Leshwi's words.

In the infirmary, Lirin and Rlain argue about Kaladin’s fate while the unconscious soldier suffers from a spreading infection. Rlain and a mute bridgeman named Dabbid refuse to let Lirin treat Kaladin unless he promises not to surrender the Radiant to the Fused. Venli intervenes and proposes a plan to rescue an Edgedancer who is being held in a nearby cell. She uses her stone-shaping powers to breach the prison wall and frees the girl, who is named Lift. Lift agrees to help heal Kaladin after Dabbid reveals he can speak and leads her to the hidden room.

### Chapter 84: Scholar

Navani is confined to her research and begins building defensive traps disguised as harmless scholarship. She constructs alarms and spring-loaded spike traps using technology discovered from ancient gemstones within the tower. These devices are armed by magnets and stored in the hallway among other boxes to avoid suspicion from the singer guards. Navani also masters the rhythm required to create Warlight by humming in harmony with Raboniel. She focuses her primary efforts on understanding how to create a negative form of Voidlight to use as a weapon.

Raboniel visits the lab and provides Navani with an ornate dagger containing a vein of raysium, a white-gold metal that conducts Investiture. The Fused explains that the weapon is intended to harvest the souls of Heralds and trap them in gemstones. Navani uses the dagger to experiment with a split flamespren that is trapped within two halves of a conjoined ruby. She discovers that the raysium blade can touch the soul of the spren and pull it into a new gemstone. Raboniel notes that Jezerezeh has already been captured and permanently removed from the cycle of rebirth using this method.

During her testing, Navani accidentally discovers a phenomenon involving force multiplication between conjoined gemstones. When she moves the dagger's large gemstone, the smaller half of the split ruby moves three times the distance. Raboniel is startled by the discovery and orders Navani to record her observations immediately. The Fused promises to provide specialized sand that changes color in the presence of Investiture to help measure power levels. Navani realizes she has found a way to create a weapon capable of killing a god through these scientific breakthroughs.

### Chapter 85: Dabbid

Dabbid reflects on his life and how people have always treated him as different due to his slow mental processing. He survived the bridge runs with Sadeas's army before being rescued and integrated into Bridge Four by Kaladin. Dabbid chose to remain mute among his new family to hide the fact that he was born with a cognitive disability. He eventually began communicating with the Sibling, the tower spren, who promised that becoming a Radiant would heal his mind. This belief drives his loyalty to the tower and his desire to help the unconscious Kaladin.

Dabbid leads Rlain and Lift through hidden stairwells and tunnels to avoid the Fused patrols in the tower. Lift pauses to eat curry stolen from the singers while questioning Dabbid about when he regained his speech. They reach the hidden room on the eleventh floor where the door's gemstone has run out of Stormlight. Lift uses her power to infuse the stone and open the door to access the patients. She finds Kaladin suffering from a violent infection and internal bleeding that has turned his skin red.

Lift presses her hands to Kaladin’s chest and side, flooding his body with Stormlight until his wounds fully close. The healing process causes Kaladin to glow and forces the rotspren—small spren that appear around infected wounds—to flee. Lift also heals Teft, who wakes up confused and demands to know the location of his clothing. Kaladin eventually stirs and opens his eyes, realizing he is no longer in a coma and is surrounded by friends. Dabbid feels a sense of accomplishment for returning Kaladin's previous heroism with a successful rescue mission.

### Chapter 86: The Song of Mornings

Eighteen months in the past, Venli watches her sister Eshonai return from a raid with a massive gemheart. She secretly collaborates with the Voidspren Ulim to undermine the listener leadership and introduce stormform. Venli orders the head farmer, Denshil, to skim small portions of gemstones from the harvests to build a secret stockpile. She justifies her actions as a quest for true freedom and personal power over others. Ulim explains that a storm is building in Shadesmar that will allow the listeners to transform into powerful forms of royalty.

Venli encounters Eshonai, who is struggling with the physical changes of warform and the burden of leadership in Shardplate. They visit their mother, who has begun to suffer from a mental ailment that leaves her wandering and confused. Eshonai expresses deep regret for the war and the decision to kill Gavilar, wishing for a return to childhood innocence. Venli rejects this sentiment, viewing innocence as a false god that leads only to enslavement. The sisters sit together in a rare moment of peace as the sun sets over the plateau.

Venli and Eshonai sing the Song of Mornings together, a simple teaching song from their childhood. Eshonai asks Venli to find a new form that would allow her to speak more diplomatically with humans like Dalinar Kholin. Venli promises to help but secretly continues her plan to summon the Everstorm and capture stormspren. She feels a brief moment of guilt about her deceptions but ultimately chooses to pursue her own ambitions. The chapter ends with Venli heading into a highstorm to begin her experiments with the new forms of power.
