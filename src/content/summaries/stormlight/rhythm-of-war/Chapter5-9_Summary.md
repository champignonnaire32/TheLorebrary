### Chapter 5: Broken Spears

The Windrunners engage a force of Heavenly Ones led by Leshwi above the town of Hearthstone. Kaladin identifies Leshwi among the Fused and accepts her challenge for a one-on-one duel. Dalinar opens a perpendicularity on the Fourth Bridge to provide the Radiants with an infinite supply of Stormlight during the engagement. The Heavenly Ones use spears tipped with aluminum and gemstones designed to drain a Radiant's Stormlight upon contact. Leshwi and Kaladin pursue each other through the town and around the massive airship while the evacuation of refugees continues.

Kaladin attempts to use a Shardspear thrown by Rock to gain an advantage but Leshwi manages to evade his strike. He eventually loses his concentration and lands roughly on the ground, allowing Leshwi to temporarily escape into the broader melee. Teft checks on Kaladin's mental state while the Fused continue to test the defenses of the Fourth Bridge without launching a full assault. Navani’s secret fabrials remain hidden within the hold as the Heavenly Ones survey the technology of the flying vessel. Kaladin notices a strange new Fused appearing as a ribbon of red light that moves with incredible speed.

In the chasms of the Shattered Plains, Shallan uses her Veil and Radiant personas to infiltrate a group of Sons of Honor cultists. She tricks the cultists into believing she possesses stolen Kholin fabrial schematics by using a Lightweaving in her notebook. The cultists decide to take her to Ialai Sadeas to negotiate a deal for the military secrets. A man places a sack over Shallan's head just before Kholin troops under Adolin's command launch an ambush in the chasm. Shallan realizes Adolin has attacked early and fears the distraction might compromise the chance to capture Ialai.

### Chapter 6: A Loose Thread

The cultists drag the hooded Shallan through a secret tunnel system that connects the chasms to the Sadeas warcamp buildings. She is eventually brought into a fortress-like chamber where Ialai Sadeas is hiding and attempting to maintain power. Ialai has been left as the sole remaining force in the warcamps after the deaths and departures of other highprinces. Shallan reveals her presence to Ialai and prepares to carry out a plan to assassinate the woman to protect Dalinar's interests. Adolin has followed the group and prepares to strike the fortress to rescue Shallan and capture the traitorous highprincess.

Kaladin continues his duel with Leshwi and notices that the Heavenly Ones are strictly adhering to a code of one-on-one combat. He watches as Leshwi impales Sigzil through the chest and begins to drain his Stormlight with her specialized spear. Overcome by memories of past failures, Kaladin nearly breaks the dueling protocol to intervene before Leshwi releases the wounded Windrunner. Sigzil survives the encounter and retreats to the Fourth Bridge to recover while Kaladin and Leshwi resume their fight. The two combatants maintain a mutual respect as they attempt to outmaneuver each other in the skies above Hearthstone.

Navani manages the evacuation of the townspeople while Renarin uses his unusual light-based powers to comfort frightened children. Ardent Falilar expresses concerns about the technical limitations and weight capacity of the Fourth Bridge during the flight. Navani observes Dalinar opening the perpendicularity again and attempts to record the unique tones and visual phenomena associated with the event. She believes the tower of Urithiru and the airship share a common power source located beyond the physical realm of Shadesmar. Navani suddenly spots Moash standing on a nearby hill wearing a black Bridge Four uniform and warns the Radiants of his presence.

### Chapter 7: The Rarest Vintage

Shallan confronts a weathered and hollowed-out Ialai Sadeas who is drinking clear simberry wine in her darkened chambers. Ialai reveals she is aware that Shallan is an operative and expects to be killed for her opposition to Dalinar. Shallan uses various personas to engage Ialai in a verbal sparring match involving metaphors about wine and power. During the conversation, Shallan realizes that Ialai believes she is working for the Ghostbloods rather than the Kholin family. Ialai claims that the Ghostbloods killed the other highprinces and are targeting the Sons of Honor for their secrets.

The highprincess invites Shallan to drink a final vintage while Adolin and his soldiers breach the room to take the conspirators into custody. Shallan decides not to kill Ialai and instead uses a Lightweaving to disguise the woman for a secret extraction from the warcamp. As they prepare to leave, Ialai warns Shallan to search her rooms for a "rare vintage" that contains important answers. The soldiers lead the disguised Ialai away, but she suddenly collapses and dies from blackbane poisoning before they can escape. Shallan suspects that a Ghostblood assassin hidden among her own agents or Adolin's guards carried out the killing.

At Hearthstone, Kaladin enters the burning manor to rescue Godeke and the former citylord Roshone from a strange new Fused. He encounters a singer using a Voidlight-powered fabrial that suppresses the powers of any Radiant within its immediate range. Kaladin's Stormlight and Syl's ability to form a Shardblade are both neutralized, forcing him to fight the singer guards with mundane weapons. He manages to kill the Fused and several guards using his physical combat training before Lift disables the suppression device. Kaladin finds Roshone's body in the stormcellar just as Moash slits the man's throat and surrenders to the Windrunner.

### Chapter 8: Surrender

Kaladin stands over the dying Roshone in the stormcellar and confronts Moash, who refuses to fight back. Moash justifies his actions by claiming he is seeking justice for the crimes Roshone committed against his and Kaladin’s families. He tells Kaladin that everyone he loves will eventually die and that there is no way to protect them from suffering. Moash offers to take away Kaladin's pain and encourages him to give up on life and find peace in non-existence. Kaladin falls into a state of paralyzed despair as his Stormlight runs out and the weight of his failures consumes him.

A sudden burst of brilliant white light fills the cellar as Renarin Kholin arrives to find the two men. The light reveals an afterimage of Moash dressed in a noble uniform and acting as a protector of the people. This vision of a better path causes the real Moash to scream in agony and flee from the cellar into the burning building. Renarin helps the numb and exhausted Kaladin to his feet and escorts him back to the Fourth Bridge as the Fused retreat. The Radiants recover the suppression fabrial and bring it aboard the ship to be studied by Navani and her technicians.

In the Sadeas warcamp, Shallan searches Ialai’s rooms for the secret information the highprincess mentioned before her death. She discovers a hidden compartment in the wall by identifying ancient glyphs and maps representing the Ten Kingdoms. Inside the compartment, she finds a secret notebook that she hides from her own agents and Adolin to investigate later. Adolin and Shallan prepare for a quick extraction from the warcamp as they realize Ialai's death will complicate the political situation. Shallan remains deeply troubled by the possibility of a traitor in her inner circle and the true goals of the Ghostbloods.

### Chapter 9: Contradictions

The Fourth Bridge departs from Hearthstone as Kaladin sits on the deck and watches his birthplace disappear into the distance. He feels a deep sense of fatigue and failure despite the successful evacuation of the town and the Radiants' victory. Kaladin struggles to process the words Moash spoke to him in the cellar and the numbness that followed his Stormlight depletion. Syl expresses her concern and tells him that his mental darkness is becoming more severe and harder for her to understand. Kaladin attempts to hide his condition from the other Windrunners who are celebrating a new knight's Third Ideal.

Teft joins Kaladin on the deck and encourages him to perform his duties as Highmarshal by congratulating the new Radiant. They discuss the news that Moash is still alive and has been granted an Honorblade by the Fused. Teft notices that Kaladin froze during the battle and warns him that the enemy might try to exploit his emotional vulnerabilities again. Kaladin realizes that he is losing the ability to function without the constant infusion of Stormlight to mask his pain. He forces himself to stand and interact with his soldiers to maintain the appearance of a strong and capable leader.

Laran, the newest Windrunner to bond a spren, summons her Shardblade for Kaladin to inspect and acknowledge during the flight. The ship carries hundreds of Windrunners and refugees back toward Urithiru while the leaders analyze the new singer technology. Navani prepares to investigate the suppression fabrial and the implications of the enemy's ability to neutralize Radiant powers. Kaladin's internal struggle continues as he faces the reality that he cannot protect everyone and that his own mind is failing. The chapter ends with Kaladin performing his official duties while the Fourth Bridge soars through the sky away from the ruined manor.
