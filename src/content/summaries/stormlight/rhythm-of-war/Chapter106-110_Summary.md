### Chapter 106: A Hundred Discordant Rhythms

Kaladin collapses into a state of shock while holding Teft’s lifeless body. Navani triggers a painrial trap—a fabrial device designed to inflict debilitating nerve pain—that disables Raboniel. This allows Navani to stab the Fused with a dagger containing anti-Voidlight. Although wounded, Raboniel survives long enough to warn Navani that the assassin Moash is returning. Navani seizes her research notebook and attempts to flee before Moash can reach her.

In the tower atrium, the Pursuer seizes Lirin and attempts to provoke the catatonic Kaladin. Kaladin enters a state of cold, glowing-eyed fury and attacks the Pursuer with supernatural speed. He pins the Fused against a large window overlooking the highstorm outside. Kaladin uses his Lashings to infuse the ground while the Pursuer’s body remains stuck to the glass. The pressure from the conflicting gravitational forces rips the Pursuer’s head from his shoulders.

A panicked Heavenly One grabs Lirin and flies toward the roof of the tower to escape Kaladin. Kaladin uses Navani’s gravitational fabrial to launch himself into the air and give chase through the lift shafts. He bursts onto the roof just as the Fused throws Lirin into the blackness of the storm. Kaladin looks down the edge of the pinnacle and recognizes the ledge where he once considered suicide. He chooses to jump after his father into the swirling tempest below.

### Chapter 107: Uniting

Navani stumbles through the hallways while bleeding from the knife wound Moash inflicted. Raboniel launches a final desperate attack on Moash, using a dagger to drain his Stormlight. This distraction allows Navani to reach the chamber containing the crystal pillar, which serves as the tower’s heart. She collapses against the pillar and hears the Sibling producing the Rhythm of War. Moash eventually overcomes Raboniel and continues his slow, relentless pursuit of the Queen.

High above the battlefield, Dalinar Kholin feels a disturbance in the storm through his connection to the Stormfather. The Stormfather informs Dalinar that Kaladin has entered the storm for what appears to be the last time. Dalinar watches from a spiritual perspective as Kaladin tumbles through the air without using any Stormlight. Kaladin whispers that he is not strong enough and lets go of Syl’s hand during the fall. The Stormfather insists that they should only witness the end, but Dalinar demands they intervene.

Dalinar uses his powers as a Bondsmith to manipulate the Connection between Kaladin and the Spiritual Realm. He manages to slow the passage of time for Kaladin within the "moment between" the storm’s beats. Venli stands in the atrium and watches as the Pursuer’s soldiers begin slaughtering human civilians. She decides to reveal her secret and begins drawing Stormlight from her spheres in front of Leshwi. Leshwi reacts with shock and immediately seizes Venli to demand an explanation for her glowing form.

### Chapter 108: Moments

Kaladin awakens within a vision of a past battlefield where he meets his deceased brother, Tien. He finds himself dressed in the antiquated leather armor of his youth while a battle rages nearby. Kaladin watches as his younger self and Tien are ordered to the front lines by a squadleader named Varth. He initially tries to turn away, unable to bear the thought of witnessing Tien’s death again. Tien takes Kaladin’s hand and explains that he chose to stay to help others feel brave.

Tien comforts Kaladin and tells him that the moments they shared are more important than the circumstances of their deaths. He encourages Kaladin to view his time with Teft as a series of successes rather than a final failure. Kaladin realizes that he has been looking at his burden of protection in the wrong way. Tien transforms into a full-grown man and embraces the child-version of Kaladin as they weep together. Before the vision ends, Tien presses a small wooden horse into Kaladin’s hand as a tangible memory.

Kaladin returns to the reality of the falling storm and reaches out to seize Syl’s hand once more. He finally understands the truth and swears the Fourth Ideal of the Windrunners. He speaks the Words: "I accept it, Stormfather! I accept that there will be those I cannot protect!" The Stormfather’s voice booms in acceptance, and a massive explosion of light surrounds the falling pair. Kaladin is fully infused with Stormlight and prepares to save his father before they hit the rocks.

### Chapter 109: Emulsifier

Leshwi falls to her knees in awe after learning that Venli has bonded a spren and become a Radiant. She realizes that the spren have begun to forgive the singers for the ancient betrayals of the past. Leshwi and her loyal troops turn against the Pursuer’s army to protect the human civilians and unconscious Radiants. A brutal civil war breaks out in the atrium as singer fights against singer for the first time. The combined force of rebellious singers and humans eventually drives off the Pursuer’s remaining soldiers.

Navani remains at the crystal pillar and witnesses a vision of the humans and singers fighting together. She describes this unification of purpose as an "emulsifier" that blends the influences of Honor and Odium. The Sibling continues to resist bonding Navani, claiming she is unworthy because she captures spren in fabrials. Moash reaches the chamber and continues to mutilate the dying Raboniel before turning his attention to Navani. Navani attempts to hum the song of anti-Voidlight to repel the assassin, but her strength begins to fail.

Rlain and Dabbid arrive at the atrium and find the aftermath of the battle among the dead. They discover Teft’s body and perform a traditional singer song for a fallen hero in his honor. Rlain takes command of the situation, using the authority he learned from Bridge Four to lead the survivors. He organizes an evacuation of the wounded and the unconscious Radiants toward the tower's Oathgates. Venli confesses to Rlain that she does not actually possess a Shardblade to activate the transportation platform.

### Chapter 110: Reborn

Kaladin bursts through the black clouds of the storm as a pillar of radiant light. Thousands of windspren swirl around him and transform into a set of living Shardplate—armor that appears as a glowing outline around his body. This armor, which bears Bridge Four glyphs, protects him during his descent. He accelerates through the tempest and catches Lirin just twenty feet above the jagged rocks. Kaladin hoists his father into a tight embrace, and Lirin notices that the Plate feels transparent to his touch.

In the basement, Navani argues with the Sibling that their differences are exactly why they should bond. She explains that a Bondsmith must be able to unite disparate forces through compromise and understanding. As Moash raises his Blade to kill her, Navani hears the pure song of the Tower’s rhythm. She modulates her voice to harmonize the vibrations of Honor and Cultivation into a single powerful tone. The energy of the tower floods her body, healing her wounds and pushing back the darkness Moash represents.

Navani speaks the Bondsmith Ideals and officially bonds the Sibling, becoming a Radiant queen of the tower. Moash is blinded by the sudden surge of light and is forced to flee into the shadows. Outside, Kaladin and Lirin reconcile as they hover safely above the ground in the aftermath of the storm. Lirin points out that the slave brands on Kaladin’s forehead have finally scabbed and fallen away. Kaladin realizes he has finally forgiven himself and prepares to return to the tower to finish the fight.
