### Chapter 58: Spanreeds

Kaladin infiltrates a laundry facility in Urithiru to steal a Voidlight spanreed for Navani. He uses Syl to scout ahead while he hides in a darkened room from passing singer guards. Syl discovers a spanreed hub where dozens of communication devices are centrally managed and monitored. Kaladin observes a new type of red lightning Voidspren that leaves visible trails for the guards. He successfully lures the guards away by having Syl impersonate a Voidspren and point out an infused rope on a wall.

Kaladin squeezes through a tight ventilation shaft to reach the ceiling above the spanreed hub room. He uses a Reverse Lashing on a brush attached to a rope to snatch a leather case containing twenty spanreeds. After retreating safely, he notices a blinking garnet light on a wall that speaks with the voice of the Sibling. The tower spren pleads for Kaladin to defend one of the nodes that protects its mind. He learns that a Fused known as the Lady of Pains has been sent to capture the node.

The Sibling leads Kaladin toward the second level near the central atrium to intercept the enemy force. Kaladin recognizes the danger but suppresses his exhaustion and mental fatigue to resume his role as a soldier. He tells Syl they must find a better weapon quickly before engaging the Fused. The urgency of the plea overrides his previous plan to remain hidden and avoid direct confrontation. He prepares to face the Lady of Pains to prevent the total corruption of the tower's systems.



### Chapter 59: The Lattice of a Growing Crystal

Kaladin bursts into a guard post with Stormlight to surprise and disable four singer guards using Lashings. He sticks their spears to the ceiling and their bodies together, leaving them immobilized without killing them. After reclaiming a spear, he follows a trail of garnet light through the tower's perimeter to avoid reinforcements. He uses back pathways and a shortcut through a woodworker's shop to reach the atrium ahead of his pursuers. The woodworker recognizes him as Stormblessed and promises to hide his trail from the searching singer patrols.

Kaladin reaches a secret tunnel on the eastern wall where the Pursuer and Raboniel are inspecting a large sapphire node. The Pursuer abandons his physical body and attacks Kaladin as a ribbon of red light. Kaladin attempts to trap the Fused using Full Lashings on the floor, but the creature repeatedly jumps to new bodies. He realizes the Pursuer is immortal and will hunt him forever until one of them is permanently destroyed. The Fused known as Raboniel watches the fight with detached interest while she prepares to corrupt the gemstone node.

The Sibling guides Kaladin to a hidden room with a fast-closing door to trap the Pursuer inside. Kaladin lures the creature into the room and manages to shut the stone door, locking the Fused away. He returns to the node and smashes the sapphire with rubble, sending the cracked gemstone falling down the mountainside. Raboniel stabs Kaladin in the chest with a knife, but she does not pursue a killing blow as he heals slowly. She explains that Windrunner traditions arise naturally like a growing crystal and warns him that reinforcements are arriving.



### Chapter 60: Essai

Venli discovers Rlain imprisoned in a cell and realizes she is no longer the last of the listeners. Rlain treats her with suspicion and human-like sneers because he does not recognize her in her Regal form. Venli notices a Voidspren hiding in a cremling body watching the prison area and shooes it away. She speaks to Rlain in the listener language to explain that she has maintained her original personality despite her form. Rlain reveals he was never executed but instead survived as a bridgeboy and eventually joined the Radiants.

Rlain demands that Venli use her influence as Raboniel's Voice to free him from the prison. Venli hesitates because she fears drawing attention to her own secrets and her attempts to rebuild a listener group. Rlain calls her insufferably selfish and urges her to act for the greater good for once in her life. Venli visits Raboniel's quarters where Fused are meticulously cataloguing Dalinar Kholin's personal belongings. She witnesses an envoy from the Ghostbloods deliver a caged teenage girl who is a Lift-like Edgedancer fueled by Lifelight.

Venli successfully petitions Raboniel for custody of Rlain by claiming him as her precious kin. Raboniel grants the request but warns Venli that she will be held responsible for any trouble he causes. Timbre pulses with the need for a new oath related to seeking freedom for those in bondage. Venli attempts to speak the new words in private, but a distant voice rejects the oath. She realizes her motives are still too selfish and she must truly care for others to progress as a Radiant.



### Chapter 61: Oil and Water

Navani communicates with Kaladin through the Sibling after he wakes up from a deep sleep following the node battle. She reassures him that he did not fail and that destroying the node bought her essential time. Kaladin reports that he stole twelve spanreeds and that their spren appear to be corrupted like Renarin's. He explains that he cannot fly due to the tower's suppression and must climb the exterior using Adhesion. Navani directs a junior engineer named Tomor to smuggle out a single-person lift fabrial for Kaladin to use.

Raboniel forces Navani to work in isolation to prevent her from coordinating delays with her other scholars. She shows Navani a glass globe fabrial that creates the blue shield around the crystal pillar via a Soulcasting-like effect. Raboniel also presents a diamond filled with a teal-colored mixing of Stormlight and Lifelight called Towerlight. She believes Stormlight and Voidlight are opposites like oil and water and cannot be combined into a new energy. Navani demonstrates that an emulsifier can mix oil and water, suggesting that a binding agent might unite the Lights.

The Sibling speaks to Navani and expresses a deep fear of both humans and singers as its death approaches. Navani argues that humans are the lesser evil because they do not intentionally corrupt spren like the Fused do. She tries to convince the Sibling to reveal the locations of the remaining two nodes to protect them. The Sibling refuses to share the locations until Navani proves she has a viable plan to mislead the Fused. Navani begins documenting everything she knows about Light to find a way to reverse the tower's corruption.



### Chapter 62: Keeper of Forms

Eight years ago, Eshonai inspects an orange gemstone containing a trapped painspren that Venli has produced. Venli explains she learned the art of trapping spren from the humans during their recent trade encounters. The elders of the Five discuss using trapped lifespren to regulate when listeners adopt mateform for convenience. Eshonai expresses concern that the humans are treating the listeners as a united nation when they are actually fractured families. She proposes visiting other families with her maps to encourage unity, but Venli mocks her idealistic plan.

Eshonai finds her mother Jaxlim wandering near the city wall and realizes her mother's memory is failing. Jaxlim recognizes the onset of her mental decline and asks for help returning to their home. She tells Eshonai that she must ensure Venli learns the songs before her mind completely gives out. Eshonai feels a deep sadness that her mother is regressing just as the listeners are making social progress. She avoids sharing her travel stories to prevent upsetting Jaxlim during her moments of lucidity and confusion.

Venli returns home and announces that the elders are calling for volunteers to test new forms of power. She suggests that changing to a new form might save Jaxlim's mind by providing a different way of thinking. Eshonai is excited by the possibility and encourages her mother to volunteer for the experiment. Jaxlim agrees to try the form and praises Venli as a potential "keeper of forms" for her discovery. Eshonai notices that Venli's rhythms seem faster and more violent, but she tries to suppress her jealousy.
