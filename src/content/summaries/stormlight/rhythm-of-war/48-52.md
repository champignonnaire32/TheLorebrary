### Chapter 48: Scent of Death, Scent of Life

Eshonai meets humans for the first time nine years ago. She observes their metallic armor and lack of rhythms. She notes they brought "dullform" creatures who look like listeners but do not speak. Eshonai learns the human language faster than other listeners. She leads a group of humans, including King Gavilar and his brother Dalinar, onto the Shattered Plains.

A human woman, a surgeon's assistant named Axindweth, asks Eshonai about spren. Eshonai describes windspren as long white lines or pinpricks in the sky. Gavilar shows little interest in the listeners initially but his scholars are fascinated. Gavilar's brother, who appears to have a "warform" equivalent, asks about chasm monsters. A chasmfiend—a massive crustacean predator that lives in the plateaus' rifts—emerges from a chasm.

The humans are frightened by the chasmfiend, but the beast ignores them. Gavilar notices Eshonai's ancient hunting knife and asks where it came from. Eshonai mentions the ruins and the old songs of her people. Gavilar becomes extremely interested when Eshonai mentions the Neshua Kadal and Radiants. Gavilar gives the listeners steel weapons and asks them to move into the ancient cities.



### Chapter 49: Soul of Discovery

Navani organizes her scholars to perform busywork to hide their real intentions from singer guards. She interacts with the Sibling, the tower's sentient spren, through a mental link. The Sibling reveals that Raboniel thinks the tower spren is dead because of information from the Midnight Mother. Navani identifies her goals: maintain the shield, contact Dalinar, negate the suppression fabrial, and counterattack. The Sibling expresses distrust of humans and calls Navani a slaver for using fabrials.

The Sibling explains that the blue shield solidifies air into glass using Soulcasting and external Stormlight. Raboniel can circumvent the shield by corrupting one of the three remaining nodes connected to the Sibling's heart. The Sibling mentions that a suppression defense was turned against the Radiants. The tower's rhythm was lost centuries ago during the binding of the Unmade Bo-Ado-Mishram. The Sibling reveals that two Radiants are awake in the tower: Lift and Kaladin.

Kaladin sneaks through the darkened tower with Syl to find Stormlight. He discovers that the enemy is pretending the tower has not been conquered to deceive scouts. Kaladin uses a Reverse Lashing—a power that makes an object attract others—to unlock gemstone lanterns. He retrieves several amethysts and travels to a fourth-floor monastery for supplies. Navani contacts Kaladin through the Sibling's bond and instructs him to investigate how the enemy operates the Oathgates.



### Chapter 50: Queen

Dalinar coordinates the military offensive in Emul from a war tent in the town of Laqqi. He learns that the coalition suffered minimal losses during the Veden betrayal due to his early warnings. Taravangian is held captive but remains silent during interrogations. Dalinar receives reports from Navani via spanreed, though he does not know she is captive. He worries about the suppression device at Urithiru that renders Radiants unconscious.

Jasnah confronts Dalinar about his decision to step back from direct military command. Dalinar explains that he must investigate his Bondsmith powers to find a way to win the war. Wit advises that Dalinar must threaten Odium to force a contest of champions. Dalinar discusses reforging the Oathpact to stop the Fused from being reborn. Highprince Ruthar insults Jasnah's authority and mocks Dalinar's scholarship during a drunken outburst.

Wit baits Ruthar into a duel and Jasnah exercises her right to be Wit's champion. Jasnah stabs Ruthar through the throat but allows Renarin to heal the wound immediately. She strips Ruthar of his title and passes it to his son as punishment for the insult. Jasnah uses the event to propose a law banning trials by sword. Dalinar observes the efficiency of Jasnah's plan and recognizes its political necessity.



### Chapter 51: To Sing Hopeless Songs

Raboniel supervises singer scholars as they sort through Navani's notes in the library hallway. Venli's singer staff arrives at the tower to assist with the research. Raboniel suspects Navani is purposefully creating chaos to stall for time. Venli hums to Indifference while Raboniel questions her loyalty to Odium. The Fused scholar mentions she is waiting for reports on the search for awake Radiants.

Venli meets with Dul, a member of her inner circle of singers who wish to escape. Dul reports that they are preparing supply packs for a future desertion. Venli plans to lead her group through the mountains to hide from the Heavenly Ones. They discuss a new recruit named Shumin who is making dangerous remarks about rebellion. Venli instructs Dul to isolate Shumin to prevent the Fused from discovering their plot.

Venli feels the weight of her past failures and doubts her ability to lead her people to safety. She reflects on her history of bringing destruction to the listeners. Timbre, her Reacher spren—a sentient light creature that bonds with singers—pulses with curiosity about Venli's hidden memories. Venli decides it is time to share the story of how the betrayal began. She recalls the second visit of the humans years ago.



### Chapter 52: A Path Toward Saving

Eight and a half years ago, Venli's mother Jaxlim begins forgetting the lyrics to the listeners' sacred songs. Venli observes her mother's mental decline with growing anxiety. Eshonai is obsessed with the humans and waits on a tower for their return. Venli tries to get Eshonai to help with the songs, but Eshonai leaves to lead a scout group. The humans return to the listener city and demand constant recitations.

Venli grows frustrated with the humans' lack of rhythms and their constant interruptions during her performances. She encounters "parshmen" servants—unintelligent human-owned singers—and feels uncomfortable with the humans' perception of her kind. Listeners from many families gather to see the humans and their metallic equipment. Venli boasts about her status as a songkeeper to impress other listeners. She avoids Eshonai, who remains focused on exploring the human world.

A human woman named Axindweth approaches Venli while she is alone on the plateaus. Axindweth reveals she can speak the listener tongue perfectly and offers a way to restore the listeners' glory. She criticizes the listeners' ancestors and mentions forms of power that can heal the sick. Axindweth gives Venli a blood-red gemstone containing a Voidspren—a malevolent spirit that grants dark abilities. She tells Venli to break it in a storm to save those she loves.
