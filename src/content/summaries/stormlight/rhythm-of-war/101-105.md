### Chapter 101: Undertext

Dalinar enters the Azish Prime's warcamp home and observes the excessive, ornate decorations and furniture. He meets with Yanagawn, Jasnah, and the vizier Noura to discuss the military situation at Urithiru. Jasnah reports that the tower is inaccessible because fabrials fail near it and the enemy has blocked the entrance tunnels. She argues that the war effort must continue without Urithiru, despite Dalinar's insistence on a rescue mission. Dalinar reveals that Kaladin's powers still function and suggests a infiltration team to disable the suppression fabrial.

Dalinar proposes recruiting Ishar, a Herald and master of Bondsmithing, to help save Urithiru. He informs the coalition leaders that he intends to meet Ishar personally in Tukar to seek his assistance. Jasnah shares her progress on a legal contract for a contest of champions with Odium. The meeting concludes with Dalinar promising Yanagawn to look for Lift, who remains in the occupied tower. Dalinar gives Jasnah the completed original manuscript of his book, *Oathbringer*.

Jasnah accepts the book and agrees to write the undertext, which is a traditionally female commentary hidden beneath a male's text. Dalinar encourages her to be honest and include her atheistic viewpoints to show that diverse people can unite. He admits he is effectively revising the Vorin religion and expects his book to cause a major schism. Jasnah warns that her critique will be thorough and may take years to complete. Dalinar expresses hope that their combined perspectives will create a greater work than his individual effort.



### Chapter 102: Highstorm Coming

Venli waits in a long line at the Urithiru Oathgates with fifteen other singers, planning to escape to Kholinar. She intends to use Raboniel’s writ to travel to the Shattered Plains and search for her mother. The transfer is delayed because the human Oathgate operator, Moash, has disappeared. Venli organizes the waiting singers to move inside the tower as a massive highstorm approaches. Timbre, her Lightspren companion, encourages Venli to act as a hero rather than a coward.

Navani sits in her smoking office, feeling defeated after discovering that Raboniel has found the tone to kill spren. She paints prayer glyphs for guidance while Raboniel’s guards monitor the hallway. Moash enters the area and informs Raboniel that he is there to kill Navani on Odium's orders. Raboniel redirects him to kill Kaladin first, claiming the Windrunner is a "worm" disrupting the tower's occupation. Moash agrees to ruin Kaladin and destroy the final defensive node in exchange for being allowed to execute Navani later.

Moash confronts Navani and blames her for the death of Kaladin’s brother, Tien, before leaving to find the final node. Navani discovers a discarded dagger containing a small charge of anti-Voidlight among the wreckage of her desk. Kaladin walks openly through the tower's ground floor, ignoring guards as he heads toward the atrium. He reunites with Syl, who has recovered from the suppression fabrial's initial strike but remains cold and sad. Kaladin challenges the Fused known as the Pursuer to a final duel in the center of the tower.



### Chapter 103: The Legend You Live

Teft and Lift disguise themselves as civilians to sneak toward the tower's infirmary during the distraction of Kaladin’s duel. They observe Kaladin brandishing his spear against the Pursuer in the central atrium. Teft uses his Bondsmith apprentice skills to stay unnoticed while his honorspren, Phendorana, rides on his shoulder. They encounter a Stormform Regal guarding the infirmary door and pretend to be frightened workers seeking shelter. Teft tackles the guard into the infirmary and restrains him while Lift heals the electrical burns Teft sustained during the struggle.

Kaladin engages the Pursuer in a grapple, intentionally letting the Fused exhaust his supply of husks. He uses Navani’s experimental flight gauntlet to slide across the floor and escape the Pursuer’s grip. Kaladin mocks the Pursuer, claiming that every defeat kills the Fused's legendary reputation. The Pursuer grows increasingly agitated as Kaladin reveals he is no longer afraid of death or the Fused. Syl mimics Kaladin's defiant posture while they prepare to use a surgical scalpel as a weapon.

Moash ambushes Teft and Lift inside the infirmary after emerging from a hiding spot beneath a sheet. He uses an Honorblade to slice through Lift's legs, leaving her unable to walk. Teft tries to defend himself with a knife, but Moash is a far superior warrior trained by Kaladin. Moash reveals he is seeking the "peace" of having no emotions and kills an unconscious Radiant to goad Teft. Teft successfully manifests Phendorana as a solid spear shaft for a moment, surprising Moash and blocking a killing blow.



### Chapter 104: Full of Hope

Rlain and Dabbid attempt to deliver a barrel of water to the tower's basement while disguised with makeup. They are intercepted by singer guards who recognize Rlain as the listener formerly imprisoned by the humans. The guards try to recruit Rlain into their squad, but he insists on continuing his assigned duties. Rlain is eventually detained for questioning, preventing him from reaching the Sibling's core as planned. He hopes the Sibling will provide a way for him to escape and complete his mission.

Kaladin continues his duel with the Pursuer in the atrium as the highstorm wall hits the tower's windows. He uses the flight device to stay out of reach and forces the Pursuer to use his fourth and final body. Kaladin loudly proclaims that he is the storm and that he will chase the Pursuer eternally. The Pursuer panics when Kaladin draws a boot knife and flees toward his own soldiers. The watching humans begin chanting "Stormblessed" as the Fused's reputation is publicly destroyed.

Moash kills Phendorana by stabbing her with a special anti-spren dagger, causing her to explode into white sand. The bond-snapping strike leaves Teft in physical and spiritual agony as he collapses on the floor. Moash taunts Teft, claiming that killing him will be the ultimate way to break Kaladin's spirit. Teft remains defiant, stating that he dies knowing he is loved, unlike the emotionless Moash. Moash stabs Teft through the neck with his Shardblade, ending the Windrunner's life.



### Chapter 105: Children of Passions

Kaladin stalks the defeated Pursuer, who has broken and run from the duel in front of hundreds of witnesses. He attaches Navani's flight device to the Pursuer's chest and launches him into the glass window. The Pursuer survives but is left bleeding and humiliated as the humans celebrate Kaladin's victory. Kaladin notices the infirmary door is open and walks toward it, expecting to find his friends. He is met with the sight of Teft's dead body being dropped from above onto the atrium floor.

Moash watches from a distance as Kaladin collapses in grief over Teft's corpse, predicting the Windrunner will now be useless. He enters the infirmary and locates the final defensive node, which is disguised as a miniature model of the tower. Moash destroys the fabrial, fully exposing the Sibling to Raboniel’s corruption. He hands Kaladin’s bound father, Lirin, over to the Pursuer as insurance against Kaladin recovering his senses. Moash leaves to find and kill Queen Navani while Kaladin remains catatonic in the center of the atrium.

Navani arms a pain-inducing fabrial trap in the hallway after noticing the Sibling's defensive field has vanished. She encounters Raboniel, who is alone in the crystal pillar chamber finishing the corruption process. Raboniel offers Navani a chance to escape, but Navani refuses to abandon the Sibling. Navani feigns a surrender and offers Raboniel the anti-Voidlight dagger as a gesture of peace. Raboniel accepts the weapon and walks into the hallway, where she immediately triggers Navani’s hidden trap.
