### Chapter 34: A Flame Never Extinguished

Adolin holds counsel with the Radiants in Shadesmar to discuss their plea to the honorspren. The Stump and Godeke reject Adolin's initial proposals, arguing that honorspren will not be moved by guilt or threats. Notum, an honorspren on a white patrol steed with glowing ribbons for hair, warns the group that he is forbidden from entering Lasting Integrity. He salutes the party and rides south to continue his patrol along the coast. Adolin remains anxious about the success of the mission as they approach the end of their journey.

Adolin and Shallan walk through a grove of obsidian trees and glass plants to observe a starspren. The starspren is an ashen greatshell with seven sets of insect wings and glowing constellation-like points across its joints. Shallan takes Memories of the beast and sketches it as it poses atop the clouds. Adolin expresses his frustration with Dalinar, who views him as a pristine statue rather than a man capable of making mistakes. Shallan consoles him, and they share a moment of intimacy on the ridge before returning to camp.

They return to find the soldiers packing up for the final day's march toward the honorspren stronghold. Adolin notes that the Tukari humans trailing them have not made any aggressive moves yet. Shallan remains suspicious of her own agents, fearing one of them is a Ghostblood spy. Veil continues to monitor the communication cube hidden in Shallan's trunk for any signs of tampering. Radiant maintains a stoic presence to bolster Adolin's morale as they prepare for the final leg of the journey.

### Chapter 35: The Strength of a Soldier

Felt reports that the Tukari humans trailing the caravan have suddenly turned south. Adolin realizes they are not following the caravan but are hunting Notum instead. He mounts his Ryshadium, Gallant, and rides at a full gallop to intercept the attackers. Maya, his deadeye companion, clings to the horse's mane as they charge toward the Tukari. Adolin arrives to find twenty men repeatedly stabbing Notum and his Reacher attendants with spears and cutlasses.

Adolin dismounts and engages the twenty Tukari alone with a heavy greatsword. He uses Flamestance, a fast and brutal sword form, to kill several men and decapitate a spearman. The Tukari surround Adolin and wound him in the side with a spear thrust. Maya suddenly dismounts and places her back against Adolin's to protect his rear. She holds Adolin’s shortsword in a defensive stance and mimics the morning katas he taught her.

Adolin feels a surge of confidence and continues the fight with his back to his deadeye. He kills several more men, including one whose fingers he hacks off and another he spears through the stomach. The Tukari leader is wounded in the shoulder and eventually orders his men to retreat. Gallant assists by stomping and screaming to intimidate the remaining attackers. Adolin’s soldiers arrive minutes later to find him bloodied but standing over the wounded Notum.

### Chapter 36: The Price of Honor

Notum survives the attack but is confused by the dishonorable human assault on a spren. Shallan discovers that the powder on her communication cube has been disturbed while Pattern was guarding the camp. She tests Pattern's loyalty by fabricating a lie about a childhood memory involving her brother Balat and a masked figure. Pattern claims to remember the event, confirming to Shallan that he is lying to her. This revelation leaves Shallan emotionally devastated and she retreats deep within her mind.

The caravan arrives at Lasting Integrity, a massive blue stone fortress built on an obsidian outcrop. Three honorspren riders in high-collared field uniforms meet Adolin outside the gates. They take Dalinar's letters of peace and immediately shred them in front of the delegation. The honorspren commander rejects the offer of bonds and orders the group to leave with the exiled Notum. She points a sword at Adolin and accuses all humans of being murderers and traitors.

Adolin demands a trial to face the honorspren's judgment and defend the integrity of the Kholin house. The honorspren agree to the trial and permit Adolin to enter the fortress with two companions and his deadeye. Adolin chooses Veil and Pattern to accompany him while the rest of the caravan remains outside. As they pass through the gates, honorspren guards seize Adolin and place his wrists in chains. The massive gates boom shut, trapping Adolin and his companions inside the stronghold.

### Chapter 37: Silence from the Dead

Navani inspects a new lifting fabrial designed as a leather gauntlet by her scholar Tomor. The device uses conjoined rubies and falling weights to pull the wearer into the air. Navani's team captures a spy who is a servant of the Radiants planting spanreed rubies in her workstation. Navani receives a report of an explosion in the tower just as she begins the interrogation. She prepares to investigate the laboratory while the captured man remains silent in custody.

Venli and Raboniel lead a strike force of five hundred Fused and singers into the basement of Urithiru. They utilize Deepest Ones, Fused who can phase through solid stone, to bypass the tower's external defenses. Venli kills a human soldier during a frantic skirmish at the cavern entrance and feels deep regret. Raboniel executes a human woman attempting to use a spanreed to alert the tower guards. The invasion force moves silently through the tunnels toward the crystalline heart of the city.

Kaladin and Lirin discuss the need for a medical revolution to better treat battle shock and mental illness. They realize that current treatments involving ardents and isolation are insufficient for broken minds. Teft informs Kaladin that Dabbid, a nonverbal veteran, has been missing for three days. Kaladin feels a sense of failure for not supervising the troubled man more closely. The Everstorm begins to gather outside the tower as the Fused strike force approaches the basement chambers.

### Chapter 38: Rhythm of the Terrors

Navani arrives at the laboratory to find that an explosion has killed two expert scholars, Nem and Talnah. The blast destroyed the Voidlight sphere that Gavilar Kholin had entrusted to her years ago. Navani orders Ardent Rushu to preserve the room and catalogue every piece of broken glass for investigation. She suspects that the explosion was not a typical fabrial accident and fears the tower's security is compromised. Navani returns to the prisoner to seek answers about the mysterious communications she has received.

Venli and Raboniel reach the basement chamber containing the Sibling’s crystalline pillar, a massive monolith of mixed gemstones. The Deepest Ones silently slaughter the human scholars and guards stationed in the room. Raboniel places her hand on the pillar and begins to flood it with violet-black Voidlight. She explains that she intends to corrupt the Sibling and turn the tower's defenses against the Radiants. Venli watches the massacre in horror, realizing the true scale of Raboniel’s ambition.

Kaladin and Teft visit a winehouse in the tower and notice how empty it has become with the army away. They see sketches Shallan carved into the tables, including a drawing of Kaladin in oversized boots. Teft expresses his struggle with his addiction and his fear of returning to firemoss. Kaladin tries to comfort him, but they are interrupted by the arrival of Rlain and Syl. The atmosphere in the room remains tense as the tower's residents sense the approaching storm.
