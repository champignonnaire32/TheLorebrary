### 36: The Broken Arrow
The dæmons Pantalaimon and Kirjava visit the sleeping Will and Lyra before meeting Serafina Pekkala in a wheel tree grove. Serafina explains that the children have become like witches because they can separate from their dæmons without being severed. She names Will’s dæmon Kirjava, which is the first time the cat-shaped entity has received a name. Serafina informs the dæmons that they must eventually tell their humans a difficult truth about the fate of the worlds. She then flies to the village to wake Mary Malone using a dream-resonance spell to avoid startling her.

Mary and Serafina walk to the river where Serafina reveals she can see Mary's dæmon as an Alpine chough. Serafina uses Mary’s spyglass to observe the Dust and confirms that the children's love has diverted the flow of Dust from the abyss. The witch reports that Lord Asriel and Mrs. Coulter died together while pulling the Regent Metatron into the chasm. To signify the end of her vengeance, Serafina breaks the arrow she had intended for Mrs. Coulter’s throat. She tells Mary that a ship carrying the gyptians will arrive within a day to take them home.

A large blue lizard discovers the corpse of Father Gomez, who was killed by Balthamos, and drags it to its nest. The priest's rifle is left behind in the grass where it begins to rust and disappear from the narrative. Serafina Pekkala departs on her cloud-pine branch to fly over the marshes and the sea toward the approaching ship. Mary remains in the village to await the arrival of the gyptian fleet and the children's return. The chapter concludes with the scavenger lizards feasting on the remains of the fallen priest in the darkness.





### 37: The Dunes
Will and Lyra spend the day alone in their gold-and-silver grove before walking to the beach at night. They encounter their dæmons, Pantalaimon and Kirjava, who have taken the forms of a nightingale and an owl before landing as doves. Kirjava reveals that every window cut by the subtle knife creates a Specter and allows Dust to leak into the abyss. The dæmons explain that all windows must be closed to save the worlds from fading away and dying. This realization means Will and Lyra must live in their own separate worlds or one will eventually sicken and die.

Lyra wails in grief upon learning they cannot travel between worlds or leave a secret window open. Will remembers his father's warning that humans can only survive for ten years in a world that is not their own. Lyra attempts to consult the alethiometer for a loophole but finds she has lost the ability to read the symbols. She sobs as she realizes the intuitive grace that allowed her to understand the instrument has vanished forever. The angel Xaphania arrives on the sand and confirms that the children must submit to their separate fates.

Xaphania promises to leave one window open between the world of the dead and the mulefa world so ghosts can escape. Will demonstrates how to close a window by pinching the edges of the air together after opening a final gate to a factory. He then shatters the subtle knife by thinking of his love for Lyra, causing the blade to fall into pieces. The two dæmons settle into their final forms as a red-gold pine marten for Pantalaimon and a lustrous cat for Kirjava. Will and Lyra stroke each other's dæmons, sealing their bond as the moon and stars move above the dunes.





### 38: The Botanic Garden
The gyptians arrive in a launch and are greeted on the beach by a crowd of mulefa and Mary Malone. John Faa and Farder Coram embrace Lyra and meet Will, acknowledging the boy's strength and the children's weary appearance. The three groups share a feast where the gyptians trade silver cups and silk tapestries for mulefa lacquer and rope. The mulefa show their guests the grove they are planting around the opening to the world of the dead. Mary receives a gift of wheel tree oil and seeds from Atal before boarding the ship for the voyage home.

The ship sails for two weeks until it reaches a point where Will's Oxford and Lyra's Oxford overlap. Will, Lyra, Mary, and Serafina walk through the empty city of Cittàgazze to reach the final crossing points. Serafina teaches Mary how to use double-vision to see her Alpine chough dæmon and the dæmons of others. Will cuts a window into his Oxford, and Lyra leads him to a specific wooden bench in the Botanic Garden. They agree to meet at this bench every Midsummer Day at midday in their respective worlds to remain close.

Will and Mary step through the final window into their Oxford, and Will closes the gap forever. He breaks the subtle knife into fragments on the wet stones of the garden after thinking of Lyra. Mary offers to help Will care for his mother and provides him with a place to live to avoid social services. Lyra returns to Jordan College where she meets with the Master and Dame Hannah Relf to discuss her future. She decides to attend St. Sophia’s school and begins the lifelong work of learning to read the alethiometer through study.
