### 1: The Enchanted Sleeper
Mrs. Coulter hides in a mountain cave with her daughter, Lyra Belacqua, whom she keeps in a drugged, unconscious state. A local girl named Ama, who believes Mrs. Coulter is a holy woman, brings food and supplies to the cave. Mrs. Coulter explains to Ama that Lyra is under an enchanter's spell and needs protection while she recovers. The golden monkey, Mrs. Coulter’s dæmon (a physical manifestation of a person's soul in animal form), carefully monitors Ama’s butterfly dæmon during their interactions. Mrs. Coulter prepares a sleeping draught using dried leaves and yellow oil to maintain Lyra’s induced slumber.

Mrs. Coulter lifts Lyra’s head and forces her to drink the bitter decoction whenever the girl begins to stir. Lyra briefly wakes and calls for Roger, a friend who died previously, but she is quickly submerged back into unconsciousness. The golden monkey watches over Pantalaimon, Lyra’s dæmon, to ensure he does not wake up and interfere. Mrs. Coulter washes Lyra’s body and combs her hair while reflecting on the danger of the Church discovering their location. She relies on matches and a naphtha stove (a portable burner using liquid fuel) to survive the freezing mountain nights.

Lyra’s consciousness drifts into a dark, foggy plain where she encounters the ghost of Roger. Roger is trapped in the world of the dead, a desolate realm where the deceased are imprisoned without knowing their crimes. He pleads for Lyra's help, describing his fear and the faceless, silent company of other ghosts surrounding him. Lyra promises Roger that she will find a way to rescue him from this final destination of all souls. She mentions that other allies, including the bear Iorek Byrnison and the witch Serafina Pekkala, will assist in their escape.





### 2: Balthamos and Baruch
Will Parry uses a tin lantern to examine Lyra’s rucksack after she is kidnapped following his father's death. Two angels, Balthamos and Baruch, appear as shifting shadows and demand that Will travel with them to Lord Asriel. Balthamos is an older angel, while Baruch was once a human who became an angel four thousand years ago. Will refuses to leave until he finds Lyra and uses the subtle knife (a blade that can cut through any material and between worlds) to command the angels' assistance. The angels fly off to search the mountainside for traces of Mrs. Coulter and the missing girl.

The angels return at dawn and report finding the corpse of Sir Charles Latrom at a blue lake camp. Will travels to the lake, slakes his thirst, and scavenges essential survival gear from the abandoned tents. He loots a magnifying glass, binoculars, gold coins, and a first-aid kit from Sir Charles’s belongings. Will experiments with the subtle knife and discovers he can feel different "snags" in the air representing various universes. He successfully opens a window to a world with blue-furred bison and another to an industrial city before closing them.

Baruch follows Mrs. Coulter’s trail south while Balthamos stays with Will to act as a guide and dæmon-mimic. The group is attacked at night by Metatron, a powerful angel and the Regent of the Authority, who descends from the sky. Will uses the subtle knife to fight Metatron, slashing at the regent’s wings until the air is thick with white feathers. Baruch helps Will pin the attacker down, but the regent eventually dissolves into mist and escapes. The trio flees through a window to a tropical world where the angels reveal that the Authority is not a creator but the first angel to condense from Dust (conscious matter).





### 3: Scavengers
Serafina Pekkala flies through the warming Arctic and observes the devastating melting of the polar ice caps. She locates Iorek Byrnison, the king of the armored bears, as he hunts a walrus in the open sea. Serafina confesses to Iorek that she failed to save their friend Lee Scoresby, who died in a battle against Muscovite soldiers. She explains that she placed a preservation spell on Lee’s body so Iorek could pay his respects. Iorek decides to travel to the site of Lee's death before heading south to find new, colder lands for his people.

Iorek swims to a new world and identifies the narrow gully where Lee Scoresby made his final stand. He finds Lee’s body perfectly preserved by the witch’s magic behind a bullet-pitted boulder. Iorek honors his friend by consuming Lee's flesh and blood to gain strength for his coming vengeance. He gathers the remaining fragments of the body and places a purple saxifrage flower on the heap as a final tribute. The bear-king resolves to find the high, snow-capped mountains of the south that Lee once described to him.

Cliff-ghasts (large, winged carrion-eaters with human-like faces) scavenge the remains of an Arctic fox near the bear's path. The dying fox tells the ghouls that the bear-king is moving south and that angels are carrying crystal treasures. The cliff-ghasts celebrate the news of Lee Scoresby’s death and the potential for new plunder. They mock the fox’s babble about witches being troubled and the world's ice disappearing. The lead cliff-ghast kills the fox and shares the entrails with its brothers while laughing at the chaos.





### 4: Ama and the Bats
Ama travels to a monastery at Cho-Lung-Se to seek a cure for Lyra’s induced sleeping sickness. She meets the healer Pagdzin tulku, whose bat dæmon swoops through the dim cell during their meeting. The healer provides Ama with a packet of powder and instructions to brush it into the sleeper’s nostrils. Ama returns to the cave and hides when she hears Mrs. Coulter and the golden monkey returning. She watches as Mrs. Coulter forces another dose of drugged tea into Lyra’s mouth during a brief waking moment.

Lyra fights her mother, calling out for Will and Iorek Byrnison to save her from the cave. The golden monkey attacks Pantalaimon, who shifts through multiple animal forms before becoming a porcupine to defend himself. Three quills lodge in the monkey's paw, causing Mrs. Coulter to slap Lyra and force the medicine down her throat. Mrs. Coulter then trims a curl of Lyra's hair with scissors and places it inside a gold locket. Ama witnesses the golden monkey cruelly dismembering a bat while Mrs. Coulter indifferently eats a bar of chocolate.

Ama realizes that Mrs. Coulter has lied about her intentions and is actually poisoning the girl. She consults with her dæmon, Kulang, and decides to use the healer’s powder to wake Lyra in secret. In the world of the dead, Lyra continues her conversation with the ghost of Roger. She expresses her fear of dying without ever truly being awake and alive. Roger tells her that he maintains hope because he believes she will eventually find a way to reach him.





### 5: The Adamant Tower
An angel named Baruch evades enemy scouts near a lake of molten sulphur to deliver a message. He is wounded in a midair struggle and eventually reaches Lord Asriel’s basalt fortress in a saw-toothed mountain range. Sentry guards discover the injured angel and transport him to a guardroom before alerting their commanders. Lord Asriel resides in a tower of adamant (a legendary indestructible stone) where he coordinates a massive rebellion. He meets with Lord Roke, a Gallivespian (a tiny human-like being with poisonous heel spurs) who leads a network of spies.

Lord Roke reports that the Church believes Lyra is the most important child in existence and is actively searching for her. Baruch is brought before Lord Asriel and reveals that the Authority has retired, leaving the regent Metatron in control. He explains that Metatron was once a human named Enoch who now intends to establish a permanent inquisition across all worlds. Baruch also mentions the existence of the subtle knife and its bearer, a boy named Will who is the son of Stanislaus Grumman. The angel provides the location of Lyra’s cave in the Himalaya before his physical form dissolves into light and vanishes.

Lord Asriel immediately orders his commanders to prepare for a military expedition to rescue his daughter. He summons Teukros Basilides, an alethiometrist (an expert who reads the truth-telling alethiometer), to pinpoint the cave's coordinates. Asriel commands the mobilization of gyropters (small, agile flying machines) and a tanker zeppelin to support the mission. He realizes he made a mistake by ignoring Lyra on Svalbard and now recognizes her central role in the coming conflict. The fortress hums with the sound of hammers and the preparation of new titanium alloys for the war against the Kingdom.
