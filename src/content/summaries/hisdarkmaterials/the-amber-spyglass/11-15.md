# HDM: Chapter 11 to Chapter 15 Summaries


### Chapter 11: The Dragonflies
Ama finds Will and Iorek Byrnison near a waterfall while she is bringing food to the cave. The girl and her dæmon Kulang communicate through Balthamos to explain Lyra’s location and Mrs. Coulter’s use of a sleeping draught. Will leaves his rucksack and travels with Ama to scout the cave where Mrs. Coulter is hiding. They observe Mrs. Coulter and her golden monkey dæmon from behind a large cedar tree. Will instructs Ama to bring her waking medicine when she returns with food at sunset.

Will enters the cave and speaks with Mrs. Coulter while she sits in a canvas chair. He verifies that Lyra is alive and notices the golden monkey is ready to attack. Mrs. Coulter offers Will a drink and claims she is hiding Lyra to protect her from the Church's intent to kill her. She asks Will to use the subtle knife to help them escape, but he refuses and leaves the cave. Will returns to Iorek and Balthamos to report that the woman is lying about her motives.

Iorek Byrnison spots eight zeppelins approaching from the north and predicts they will arrive shortly after nightfall. Will plans to open a window to another world to rescue Lyra while Ama uses her powder to wake her. Balthamos flies over the valley to keep watch for any movement from the golden monkey. Inside a leading zeppelin, the Gallivespians Tialys and Salmakia assist in the hatching of their giant dragonflies. They receive orders from Lord Roke to cooperate with Will and follow him into any other world he enters.



### Chapter 12: The Break
Lord Asriel and King Ogunwe monitor reports as their forces move toward the valley to intercept the Consistorial Court. Mrs. Coulter remains in the cave while Lyra experiences a recurring dream involving the ghost of her friend Roger. Will and Ama approach the cave under the cover of windy moonlight. Will cuts a window into a silent, bone-white world to use as a path for their escape. Ama refuses to hand over the waking powder and insists on entering the cave to apply it herself.

Will and Ama enter the cave through a small window and find Mrs. Coulter and her monkey already awake. The roar of zeppelin engines and the clatter of Lord Asriel’s gyropters fill the air as soldiers begin landing. Will attempts to cut a new window next to Lyra but sees Mrs. Coulter’s face and momentarily mistakes her for his mother. This lapse in concentration causes the subtle knife to snap into several pieces on the cave floor. Ama sprinkles the powder on Lyra’s lip while Will gathers the seven broken fragments of the knife.

A battle erupts outside between the Swiss Guard and King Ogunwe’s African riflemen. Mrs. Coulter fires a pistol at the combatants while Lyra slowly regains consciousness and recognizes Will. Balthamos cowers in fear against the cave wall as the violence intensifies around them. The Lady Salmakia and the Chevalier Tialys attack Mrs. Coulter and her dæmon with their poisonous spurs. The golden monkey catches Salmakia in his paw, leading to a stalemate as Tialys threatens Mrs. Coulter’s jugular vein.



### Chapter 13: Tialys and Salmakia
Will knocks the golden monkey aside with his hand, allowing the Lady Salmakia to escape his grip. Tialys and Salmakia identify themselves as Gallivespian spies for Lord Asriel and command the children to follow them. Mrs. Coulter begs Lyra not to leave as the group flees the cave into the burning valley. Will uses Mrs. Coulter’s dropped pistol to kill a Swiss Guard blocking their path. Balthamos flees in terror when wolf-dog dæmons attack him, soaring away into the forest canopy.

The children and the Gallivespians jump through the window into the bone-white desert world. Will experiences a fit of vomiting after the horror of killing the soldiers during the escape. Lyra retrieves the alethiometer from Will's rucksack and confirms it is safe. They move to a rocky ledge where Will admits the subtle knife is broken and useless. Lyra consults the alethiometer and learns that Iorek Byrnison is nearby and capable of mending the blade.

Lyra shares her dream about Roger and her plan to enter the land of the dead to apologize to him. Tialys and Salmakia demand that the children accompany them to Lord Asriel’s fortress after meeting the bear. Will threatens the spies when Salmakia violates Pantalaimon by touching him with a spur. The group agrees to a temporary alliance to find Iorek before making further moves. Will and Lyra fall into a deep sleep while the Gallivespians maintain a watchful guard over the camp.



### Chapter 14: Know What It Is
Will and Lyra wake at sunrise and learn from Tialys that Mrs. Coulter is now a captive of King Ogunwe. The group eats a small meal of dried peaches and rye bread before returning to Lyra’s world. Will closes the window behind them, and they walk past the wreckage of zeppelins and gyropters in the valley. Lyra suffers from blisters and exhaustion but refuses to admit her weakness to Will. Tialys uses his lodestone resonator to report their progress to Lord Roke at Asriel’s fortress.

The travelers reach the mountainside and reunite with Iorek Byrnison near his cave. Iorek informs Lyra that Lee Scoresby died bravely while fighting a company of Muscovites. Will and Lyra eat roasted goat meat while Iorek kindles a fire inside the dark cavern. Will confronts the Gallivespians for eavesdropping on their private conversations and forces them to apologize. He reveals to Iorek that the subtle knife is broken and asks the bear to repair it.

Iorek examines the seven fragments of the blade and expresses a deep fear of the knife's destructive power. He agrees to mend the weapon only if Lyra uses the alethiometer to understand the consequences of its repair. Lyra’s reading indicates that the knife is essential for the survival of Dust but is also extremely dangerous. She also learns that their planned journey to the land of the dead may result in their deaths. Will and Lyra gather resinous wood from a nearby bush to provide fuel for Iorek's forge.



### Chapter 15: The Forge
The Gallivespians discuss their plan to remain close to Will once the knife is restored. Iorek Byrnison constructs a primitive forge using an open fire and his own armor as an anvil. Will and Lyra provide fuel while Ama’s stones produce a protective gas to prevent the metal from oxidizing. Iorek shapes a stone hammer and begins heating the first two pieces of the broken blade. Will must use his mind to help forge the connection between the atoms of the metal.

The bear and the children work through the night to join the seven fragments together. Will experiences extreme mental exhaustion as he holds each piece in precise alignment during the hammering process. Iorek finishes the final join and takes Will outside into the cold air to discuss the knife's failure. Will explains that the image of his mother caused his mind to falter and snap the blade. Iorek warns Will that he must put aside thoughts of his mother to prevent the knife from breaking again.

Will plunges the heated blade into a snowbank to complete the tempering process. The mended knife appears shorter and scarred with dull silver marks at every join. Iorek says farewell to Lyra and departs to lead his people back to the northern regions. Will and Lyra use the restored knife to open a window into a warm, sandy world with fernlike trees. They fall asleep on the dunes while Tialys sends a message to Lord Asriel through the lodestone resonator.
