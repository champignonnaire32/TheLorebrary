### 11: The Belvedere
Will wakes in the villa with a loose bandage and a crimson, blood-soaked bed. He meets Lyra in the kitchen, where she dresses his wound with tea towels and finds him canvas trousers. Lyra confesses that she saw Tullio attacked by Specters and that Angelica spotted her at the tower window. Will suggests Specters might exist in his own world, linking them to the behavior of the mentally ill. They discuss Mrs. Coulter’s soldiers who have had their dæmons cut away and might be immune to Specter attacks.

Lyra uses the alethiometer to confirm Will's mother is safe with a kind friend who will not give her away. A mob of forty or fifty children, led by Angelica and a boy with a pistol, approaches the villa to kill the "murderers." Will and Lyra flee to a circular, temple-like viewing structure to avoid the angry crowd. Will uses the subtle knife to cut the iron staircase, sending the first wave of children crashing to the floor. The remaining children swarm the roof, throwing broken tiles and stamping on the ridges to reach the windowsills.

Will and Lyra defend themselves with pieces of iron railing as the mob tries to bludgeon and kill them. Kaisa, the snow goose dæmon of Serafina Pekkala, arrives and dives at the children to distract the attackers. Witches sweep out of the sky and fire arrows at the roof, causing the terrified children to jump off and flee. Serafina Pekkala explains the area is surrounded by a hundred invisible Specters and leads the children toward the woods. They reach a cave where Serafina prepares a spell to heal Will's hand while he falls into a drugged sleep.



### 12: Screen Language
Mary Malone explains her findings on Shadow particles, which Lyra calls Dust, to her skeptical colleague Oliver Payne. She theorizes that human consciousness was amplified by these particles thirty-five thousand years ago during a specific evolutionary leap. Sir Charles Latrom visits the laboratory and informs the scientists that their research funding will not be renewed by the committee. Latrom offers to intervene if they focus on manipulating consciousness and investigating the many-worlds hypothesis for defense purposes. He also warns Mary to report any contact with Lyra or the "murderer" boy to him privately for national security.

Oliver Payne agrees to Latrom's terms, prompting a disgusted Mary to quit her job and leave the building. Mary returns to the lab at night, evading a new security guard to access the Cave computer equipment. She uses a program to communicate with the Shadows through text on the computer screen. The Shadows identify themselves as angels, which are complex structures of spirit and matter that have always known humans. They reveal they intervened in human evolution for vengeance against the Authority following the war in Heaven.

The angels command Mary to play the serpent and find the girl and the boy to prevent their enemies from taking control. Mary is instructed to find a red and white nylon tent on Sunderland Avenue and go through a window there. She destroys the laboratory equipment and formats the computer hard disks to protect her research before leaving. At Sunderland Avenue, Mary uses a fake ID to deceive a policeman guarding the van and the tent. She steps through the window inside the tent and arrives in the silent, sunlit city of Cittàgazze.



### 13: ÆSAHÆTTR
Serafina Pekkala leads a healing ritual for Will’s hand by singing to the knife and the blood under the moonlight. The witches demonstrate the medicine's power by splitting and healing an alder sapling and a live, struggling hare. Will's fingers are soaked in a bitter herbal decoction and bound tightly with a strip of silk. The group travels toward the mountains, guided by the alethiometer and the witches flying as scouts above. Will tells Lyra about his mother’s mental illness and how he fought the boys who tormented her in his world.

Will recounts killing the man who broke into his house searching for his father’s letters before he found the window. They pass through a village where children flee from the sight of Will and Lyra’s tattered, bloodstained appearance. Will’s wound begins to bleed again, indicating that the witches' healing spell has failed to close the blood-gates. Lyra hopes Lee Scoresby is nearby when a witch reports seeing a balloon over the sea ahead of a storm. Ruta Skadi, a fierce-eyed Latvian witch queen, arrives after a battle with cliff-ghasts in the dark sky.

Will kills a surviving cliff-ghast, a beast with leathery skin and matted fur, using the subtle knife. Ruta Skadi describes Lord Asriel’s basalt fortress and his gathering army of diverse warriors from many worlds. She recounts hearing cliff-ghasts speak of Æsahættr, a "god destroyer" weapon that Asriel needs to defeat the Authority. Angels appear as pilgrims of light around the sleeping children before flying toward the north in a circle of radiance. Ruta Skadi leaves to rally all witch clans for war while Serafina remains to guard Lyra and Will.



### 14: Alamo Gulch
Lee Scoresby and Stanislaus Grumman drift over Cittàgazze in a balloon and observe the Specter-ridden city from above. Grumman explains that Specters feast on the attention of adults, leaving children alone but vulnerable as they reach adolescence. Lee spots a pursuing balloon and four Imperial zeppelins signaling to each other with red flares in the distance. Grumman enters a trance and summons a massive storm that destroys one of the pursuing zeppelins with a lightning strike. Lee lands the balloon in a mountain forest during the storm, nearly crashing into a massive oak tree.

They hide the balloon's gasbag and basket under the tree canopy to avoid detection from the remaining three airships. Lee dreams of Grumman commanding Specters and birds to attack and down the other enemy zeppelins during the night. The shaman warns that the Imperial soldiers will burn the forest with naphtha and potash to force them out. Lee and Grumman flee the flaming woods, clambering toward a narrow defile in the cliffs to escape the heat. A remaining zeppelin lands and deploys twenty-five soldiers of the Imperial Guard to capture the two men alive.

Lee makes a stand at a gulch to hold off the soldiers while Grumman escapes into the mountains. He shoots out the zeppelin's engine and begins a lethal firefight with the climbing soldiers and their wolf dæmons. Lee is repeatedly wounded, taking bullets to his scalp, shoulder, and leg during the prolonged battle. He uses a scarlet flower to call for help from Serafina Pekkala as the soldiers close in on his position. Lee and his dæmon Hester die together after Lee fires his final bullet to ignite the zeppelin’s gasbag.



### 15: Bloodmoss
Will and Lyra continue their climb toward the mountains, guided by the alethiometer and the remaining witches. Serafina Pekkala senses Lee Scoresby’s call and flies away to find him, leaving the children with fewer guardians. Will suffers from intense heat and a worsening hand wound that refuses to stop bleeding despite the silk bandages. Lyra observes Will's internal longing for his father as they rest by an icy blue lake among the rocks. Lena Feldt, a flying witch scout, discovers a troop of soldiers without dæmons camping by the lake on the woman's orders.

Lena Feldt finds Mrs. Coulter in a tent with Sir Charles Latrom and his serpent dæmon. Mrs. Coulter uses her golden monkey dæmon to seduce Latrom’s dæmon and gain access to his secrets. Latrom reveals that the boy has the subtle knife, also known as Æsahættr or the god destroyer weapon. Mrs. Coulter secretly poisons Latrom’s wine while he is distracted by the monkey’s caress of his dæmon. Lena Feldt is captured by Mrs. Coulter, who has neutralized the witch's invisibility spell through her own superior will.

A Specter attacks Lena Feldt’s dæmon, a snow bunting, leaving the witch in a state of despairing agony. Under duress, Lena Feldt reveals the location of Lyra and the twenty guarding witches on the mountain. The witch also confesses that Lyra is destined to be the new Eve, mother of all mankind. Mrs. Coulter realizes her mission is to destroy her daughter to prevent another Fall from grace. The Specter continues its feeding on Lena Feldt, filling her with a soul-crushing melancholy and disgust for life.
