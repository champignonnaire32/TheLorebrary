### Chapter 1: The Decanter of Tokay
Lyra and her dæmon Pantalaimon, currently in moth form, sneak into the forbidden Retiring Room of Jordan College. They hide behind a chair when the Master enters and prepares a decanter of special 1898 Tokay for Lord Asriel. Lyra watches through a crack as the Master stirs a white powder into the wine before leaving. She is trapped in the room when the Steward enters, forcing her to hide inside an oak wardrobe. Lord Asriel arrives soon after and orders coffee while waiting for the Scholars to finish their dinner. Lyra witnesses her uncle's snow leopard dæmon, Stelmaria, standing guard as he prepares for a presentation.

When Lord Asriel prepares to drink the poisoned wine, Lyra leaps from the wardrobe and knocks the glass from his hand. She informs him that the Master poisoned the drink, and he forces her back into the wardrobe to spy on the meeting. Lord Asriel tricks the Porter into thinking the decanter was dropped accidentally to hide the evidence of the attempt. He then orders his manservant Thorold to set up a projecting lantern and a screen for a lecture. The Scholars enter the room, and the Master is visibly unsettled to see the spilled wine on the floor. Lyra remains hidden, tasked by her uncle to observe the Master's reactions during the upcoming demonstration.

Lord Asriel begins his presentation by showing slides of the North taken with a special silver nitrate emulsion. He explains that a mysterious substance called Dust, which consists of glowing particles from the sky, is visible around adults but not children. The Scholars are shocked by an image of a child standing near a man, where the child is not affected by the Dust. Asriel then reveals the City in the Sky, a solid metropolis visible within the Aurora Borealis. The chapter ends with Asriel presenting a vacuum container containing the severed, trepanned head of the explorer Stanislaus Grumman. Scholars argue about the implications of the find and the involvement of the Tartars.





### Chapter 2: The Idea of North
The Scholars debate the evidence of the City in the Sky and the fate of the Grumman expedition. Lord Asriel explains that Grumman was murdered and his head trepanned, a practice common among certain northern tribes. The Palmerian Professor suggests that Iofur Raknison, the king of the panserbjørne—a race of armored bears who live on Svalbard—might be responsible. He notes that the bear king is obsessed with obtaining a dæmon, which are the external physical manifestations of human souls. The Scholars eventually vote to fund a new expedition for Lord Asriel despite the Master’s initial reservations. After the meeting, Asriel finds Lyra in the wardrobe and tells her she cannot accompany him to the North.

Lord Asriel departs Jordan College immediately, leaving Lyra frustrated and curious about the mysterious nature of Dust. The Master and the Librarian meet privately to discuss the "appalling consequences" predicted by the alethiometer if Asriel continues his research. They discuss the Consistorial Court of Discipline and the rising power of the Magisterium, the ruling body of the Holy Church. The Master expresses fear regarding the Oblation Board, a secretive agency that has begun operating under Church protection. He reveals that Lyra has a major role to play in the coming events, though she must remain unaware of her destiny. The Librarian doubts Lyra’s interest in theological riddles, but the Master insists she will eventually face a great betrayal.

The Master explains the Barnard-Stokes business, a heresy involving the existence of multiple material worlds that the Church suppressed. He fears that Jordan College will be seen as a hotbed of heresy for funding Lord Asriel’s search for these worlds. The alethiometer, a truth-telling device, indicates that Lyra must make a journey she was originally meant to be spared from. The Master laments that he cannot explain the danger of Dust to her without putting her at further risk. He concludes that it is the duty of the old to be anxious for the young, while the young scorn such worries. The two old men part for the night, feeling the weight of the political and spiritual storm approaching.





### Chapter 3: Lyra's Jordan
Lyra lives a wild life at Jordan College, spending her time climbing roofs and fighting with other children. Her best friend is Roger the kitchen boy, with whom she explores the vast underground network of tunnels and crypts. They engage in mock warfare against town children, brickburners, and the nomadic gyptians who travel by canal boat. Lyra is largely ignored by the Scholars except for formal visits from her uncle, Lord Asriel, who occasionally gives her gold. She is known for her stubbornness, dirty fingernails, and a complete lack of interest in her formal studies. The college itself is a wealthy and ancient institution that serves as a center for experimental theology.

Rumors begin to spread through Oxford and the rest of England about the "Gobblers," a mysterious group kidnapping children. In London, a beautiful lady with a golden monkey dæmon entices a boy named Tony Makarios away with the promise of chocolatl. She takes him to a warehouse in Limehouse where other children are being held before being shipped to the North. The lady burns the letters the children write to their parents, ensuring they will never be traced. Back in Oxford, Lyra and Roger play a game of "kids and Gobblers" in the dusty attics of the college. They eventually break into the wine cellars and get drunk on ancient wine, further cementing their bond.

The disappearances reach a crisis point when Billy Costa, the son of a prominent gyptian family, vanishes during the horse fair. Lyra joins the search for Billy but finds no trace of the boy or the rumored white truck used by the kidnappers. When she returns to the college, she discovers that Roger has also disappeared and was last seen in the morning. Lyra is devastated and blames the adults for not doing enough to find her friend. She retreats to the roof of the college to scream her frustration as the evening zeppelin for London departs. Mrs. Lonsdale, the Housekeeper, eventually finds her and forces her to wash and dress for a formal dinner.





### Chapter 4: The Alethiometer
Lyra attends a formal dinner at the Master’s lodging where she meets the glamorous Mrs. Coulter. The woman possesses a golden monkey dæmon and charms Lyra with stories of her arctic explorations and northern adventures. Mrs. Coulter is a member of the Royal Arctic Institute and offers to take Lyra to London as her personal assistant. The Master explains to Lyra that her time at Jordan College is ending and she needs female guidance for her future. Lyra is thrilled by the prospect of traveling to the North and learning navigation and celestial geography. She spends her final night in Oxford packed and ready for a dawn departure via zeppelin.

Before Lyra leaves, the Master calls her to his study for a final, secret meeting. He presents her with an alethiometer, a rare golden instrument that resembles a large watch with thirty-six symbols on its dial. He explains that it is one of only six in existence and has the power to tell the absolute truth. The Master instructs her to keep the device hidden from everyone, including Mrs. Coulter. He warns her that the powers of the world are fierce and that she must keep her own counsel. Lyra hides the heavy gold device in her coat pocket as she joins Mrs. Coulter for the journey.

Lyra and Mrs. Coulter arrive in London and settle into a beautiful, sun-filled flat on the Embankment. The flat is filled with porcelain figures and soft carpets, a sharp contrast to the stony grandeur of Jordan College. Mrs. Coulter takes Lyra shopping for a new wardrobe of pretty clothes and treats her to lunch at the Royal Arctic Institute. Lyra is enchanted by the luxury but feels a growing sense of confusion about her loyalty to the Master. She spends her evening examining the alethiometer, trying to understand how the three moving hands and the swinging needle function. Pantalaimon suggests that the Master wanted them to keep the device safe from Mrs. Coulter specifically.





### Chapter 5: The Cocktail Party
Lyra becomes an fixture in Mrs. Coulter's social life, attending meetings, restaurants, and the theater. Mrs. Coulter begins teaching her mathematics and geography, though she reacts sharply when Lyra mentions Dust. The golden monkey dæmon bristles with hostility at the mention of the substance, signaling a hidden tension. Lyra learns feminine arts like hair care and makeup while observing Mrs. Coulter’s elegant interactions with powerful Londoners. Despite the luxury, Lyra feels increasingly confined and longs for her old life of adventure in Oxford. She continues to keep the alethiometer hidden in a white leather shoulder bag she carries everywhere.

Mrs. Coulter hosts a grand cocktail party filled with politicians, bishops, and scholars to celebrate her upcoming expedition. She forces Lyra to remove her shoulder bag, and her golden monkey dæmon physically attacks Pantalaimon to enforce obedience. Lyra is horrified by the cold cruelty of the monkey but puts on a charming facade for the guests. She encounters a journalist named Adèle Starminster who tries to interview her about Mrs. Coulter’s private life. Mrs. Coulter discovers the interloper and threatens to destroy her career, revealing a terrifying, metallic aura of power. Lyra notices that Mrs. Coulter can seemingly distance herself from her dæmon, a feat that should be impossible.

During the party, Lyra overhears guests discussing the General Oblation Board, which they mockingly refer to as the "Gobblers." A man named Lord Boreal explains that Dust is attracted to adults but not to children who have not reached puberty. He reveals that Mrs. Coulter is the head of the Oblation Board and that the kidnapped children are "sacrifices" for her research. Lyra realizes with horror that her benefactor is the very person responsible for Roger's disappearance. She also learns that the golden monkey has been spying on her and likely knows about the alethiometer. The chapter ends with Lyra understanding the true, predatory nature of the woman she once admired.
