### 11: Armor
Lyra consults the alethiometer and learns the location of Iorek Byrnison’s stolen armor. Kaisa, the dæmon of the witch queen Serafina Pekkala, arrives at the ship as a large gray goose. Kaisa explains that the Oblation Board is operating at a station called Bolvangar, meaning "fields of evil." The goose dæmon reveals that Lord Asriel is imprisoned at Svalbard by the armored bears. Lyra informs Iorek Byrnison that his armor is hidden in the cellar of the priest’s house.

Iorek Byrnison stops his labor at the sledge depot and rushes into the town to reclaim his property. The bear smashes into the priest’s brick house and retrieves his rust-red iron plates. Armed police and the sysselman, or town governor, surround the house as Iorek emerges in his armor. Iorek nearly kills a sentry before Lyra intervenes and convinces the bear to leave peacefully. The aeronaut Lee Scoresby guards the bear's armor with a long pistol while Iorek cleans it with seal blubber.

John Faa and Farder Coram negotiate with the sysselman to allow the bear to depart with the gyptian expedition. Iorek Byrnison dons his sky iron armor and joins the group as they head north. Lee Scoresby packs his balloon into sledges and joins the journey. Lyra falls asleep in Farder Coram's sledge as the Aurora glows in the sky. Pantalaimon notices a mysterious monkey-like spirit following the sledges through the trees.





### 12: The Lost Boy
John Faa asks Lyra to use the alethiometer to learn about the defenses at Bolvangar. The device reveals that sixty Tartar riflemen with fire throwers and wolf dæmons guard the station. Lyra discovers a second message about a ghost haunting a nearby village. Iorek Byrnison agrees to take Lyra to the village on his back while the gyptians continue their march. Lyra witnesses hundreds of witches flying through the night sky toward the north.

The bear and Lyra reach a cluster of wooden buildings by a frozen lake. A fearful villager directs them to a narrow fish house near the jetty. Lyra enters the dark shed with a borrowed lantern and finds a boy named Tony Makarios. Tony Makarios is huddled against fish racks and clutches a piece of dried fish. Lyra realizes the boy has undergone intercision, a process that has severed his dæmon away.

The villager demands payment for the fish Tony is holding before they leave. Lyra helps the dæmonless boy onto Iorek’s back and they ride back to the gyptians. The boy repeatedly asks for his dæmon, Ratter, whom the Gobblers have taken. Lyra experiences intense physical and emotional pain when she imagines being separated from Pantalaimon. The pair catches up with the gyptian sledges as the boy grows increasingly inert and cold.





### 13: Fencing
Tony Makarios dies an hour after reaching the gyptian camp while searching for his missing dæmon. The gyptians build a fire to cremate the boy's body because the frozen ground is too hard for burial. Lyra discovers that a man took Tony's dried fish and fed it to his dogs. She carves the name "Ratter" onto a gold coin and places it in the dead boy’s mouth. Iorek Byrnison constructs two lead-lined tin boxes for Lyra using his opposable thumb claws.

Lyra places the clockwork spy-fly into one of the tins to prevent its escape. Iorek explains that he was exiled from Svalbard for killing another bear in a fit of anger. He identifies the current king of Svalbard as Iofur Raknison, a name Lyra remembers from Jordan College. Iorek demonstrates his fighting skill by parrying Lyra's stick attacks with perfect precision. The bear claims he can see through tricks and deceit because he is not human.

Lee Scoresby prepares to inflate his balloon using ground-gas vents near fire mines. Scoresby mentions an explorer named Stanislaus Grumman who lived among Tartar tribes. Lyra tells Scoresby that Lord Asriel showed the Scholars a decapitated head he claimed was Grumman’s. Scoresby suggests that Lord Asriel may have been misleading the Scholars to obtain funding. The expedition continues north through heavy snow as they approach Bolvangar.





### 14: Bolvangar Lights
A thick fog descends on the gyptian camp as they prepare their weapons for the assault. Samoyed hunters launch a surprise attack with a rain of arrows, killing several gyptians. Iorek Byrnison leaps into the mist to fight the attackers while arrows pin down the men. A Samoyed dæmon attacks Pantalaimon, allowing hunters to capture and tie up Lyra. The kidnappers throw Lyra onto a sledge and drive away into the snowy darkness.

Lyra identifies herself as "Lizzie Brooks" to the Samoyed hunter to hide her true identity. The hunters deliver Lyra to a fenced compound with high poles and bright anbaric lights. A doctor pays the Samoyeds a dozen coins for the capture of the child. Lyra is taken inside a heated building that smells of bacon and medical supplies. Sister Clara, a nurse with a white dog dæmon, takes Lyra to a reception area.

Pantalaimon becomes a falcon and circles the room to test the doctor’s marmot dæmon. The nurse makes Lyra take a shower and provides her with clean secondhand pajamas. Lyra hides the alethiometer and the spy-fly tin in an oilskin pouch around her waist. A doctor questions Lyra about her father and her journey while she pretends to be dim-witted. Lyra is led to a dormitory where other children inform her that those who disappear never return.





### 15: The Daemon Cages
Lyra finds Roger in the canteen and communicates with his dæmon, Salcilia, using Pantalaimon. The children discuss a girl named Bridget McGinn who is taken away for a "little operation." A nurse explains that the procedure involves a cut that makes children grow up. Roger reveals a hiding place behind a ceiling panel in the dormitory. Doctors test Lyra for Dust by making her stand on a metal plate and hold a brass globe.

A fire alarm bell rings, and the staff leads the children outside into the arena. Lyra, Roger, and Billy Costa escape the crowd and hide behind a square building. Kaisa, the goose dæmon, arrives and opens a locked door marked "ENTRY STRICTLY FORBIDDEN." Inside the building, Lyra finds dozens of glass cases containing the severed dæmons of the children. Lyra blows snow on the cases to unlock them and releases the pale, ghostlike creatures.

The lost dæmons cluster around Lyra before Kaisa herds them into the sky to find their humans. Lyra instructs Roger and Billy to tell the other children to be ready for an escape. She plans to use the fire bell as a signal for the children to seize their outdoor clothes. A silver zeppelin arrives at the station and moors to a large metal mast. Mrs. Coulter and her golden monkey dæmon look out from the cabin window as the airship lands.
