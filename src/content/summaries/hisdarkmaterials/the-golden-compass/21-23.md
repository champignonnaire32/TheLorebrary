### 21: Lord Asriel’s Welcome
Lyra and Roger ride armored bears through the mountainous interior of Svalbard toward Lord Asriel’s residence. The bear Søren Eisarson explains that Asriel’s haughty nature allowed him to dominate the bear-king and build a luxurious laboratory on a northern headland. They reach a gaunt crag topped by a spacious building illuminated by naphtha lamps and featuring expensive glass windows. The manservant Thorold greets them at the door and is shocked to see Lyra. Lord Asriel appears and reacts with immediate horror and shouting upon recognizing his daughter.

Asriel staggers back in distress and orders Lyra to leave before noticing Roger standing beside her. He calms down instantly after seeing the boy and orders Thorold to provide the children with baths and food. While Roger bathes, he expresses deep fear of Lord Asriel, comparing him to a wolf. Lyra later meets Asriel in his library, which overlooks the frozen sea, and admits she knows he is her father. She presents the alethiometer, but Asriel shows no gratitude and refuses to take the device from her.

Asriel explains that Dust is an elementary particle that clusters around adults and is linked to the concept of original sin. He reads from the Bible to show that dæmons becoming fixed is synonymous with the loss of innocence and the entry of sin into the world. Asriel reveals that Mrs. Coulter founded the Oblation Board to experiment on children by separating them from their dæmons to prevent Dust from settling. He states that the energy released during this separation is immense and that he intends to harness it to cross into another universe. Lord Asriel tells Lyra he does not need the alethiometer and dismisses her to bed.





### 22: Betrayal
Thorold wakes Lyra in a state of panic to inform her that Lord Asriel has left with Roger. Asriel packed a sledge with instruments and batteries before harnessing the dogs and departing into the night. Lyra realizes with horror that Asriel’s distress at seeing her was because he needed a child for his bridge-building experiment. She understands that she has inadvertently betrayed Roger by bringing him directly to the person who intends to kill him. Lyra weeps in despair before ordering Thorold to help her dress in heavy furs for the pursuit.

Lyra calls for Iorek Byrnison, who immediately emerges from the snow to carry her toward the mountains. They follow the sledge tracks across a silver, moonlit plain while the Aurora Borealis begins to shimmer intensely in the sky. Iorek suddenly stops when he detects the presence of a hostile witch clan in the air. A green-feathered arrow narrowly misses Lyra, and the bears form a defensive square to repel the swooping attackers. Iorek’s bears use a fire hurler to launch flaming sulfur at the witches and an approaching Magisterium zeppelin.

The flaming sulfur strikes the zeppelin’s gas bag, causing the hydrogen to ignite and the aircraft to crash in a massive inferno. Mrs. Coulter’s troops and the witches continue to fight the bears on the ground with machine guns. Iorek leaves his subjects to handle the battle and continues to carry Lyra up the steep mountain slopes. They reach a deep chasm where the only path forward is a fragile bridge of compacted snow that cannot support a bear's weight. Lyra crosses the bridge alone just as it collapses into the abyss, leaving her separated from Iorek.





### 23: The Bridge to the Stars
Lyra clings to Pantalaimon in the snow and weeps over her fear and the perceived cruelty of adults toward children. She eventually composes herself and continues the steep climb under the brilliant, dancing light of the Aurora. Pantalaimon flies ahead in owl form and reports that Lord Asriel has reached the summit and is preparing his instruments. Lyra hears Roger calling her name and stumbles through the deep snow to reach the peak. She finds Asriel at the edge of a thousand-foot cliff, twisting wires together on his sledge.

Lord Asriel’s snow leopard dæmon holds Roger’s dæmon in her mouth to keep the boy from escaping. Roger struggles and begs for mercy, but Asriel ignores his pleas and knocks him to the ground. Asriel connects a wire to Roger’s dæmon, causing a jet of pure energy to shoot upward and tear a hole in the sky. The release of energy kills Roger instantly, and his body falls limp in Lyra’s arms as a bridge to another world opens. Mrs. Coulter arrives at the summit and embraces Lord Asriel amidst the pulsing light of the new portal.

Asriel invites Mrs. Coulter to join him in the other world to find the source of Dust and destroy the Church's power. Mrs. Coulter hesitates and ultimately refuses, choosing to stay in her own world despite her obvious passion for Asriel. Lord Asriel walks into the sunlit city in the sky with his snow leopard, leaving Mrs. Coulter weeping on the mountain. Lyra realizes that if the Church and her parents hate Dust, it must actually be a good and necessary thing. She decides to follow her father into the new universe to find and protect Dust herself.
